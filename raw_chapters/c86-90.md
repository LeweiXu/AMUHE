# Chapter 86: The Balloon Princess and the Seamstress Servant

*Several weeks earlier.*

From Fort Belgash to the Empire.

Heading north along the highway across the plains were several carriages.

Guarding them front and rear, twenty cavalrymen rode escort, and ten soldiers clad in black-painted armor accompanied them as well.

The number was modest for a royal escort, but it was more than sufficient.

Increasing the number of guards did not necessarily make the defense more solid.

The larger the body, the more agility was lost, and reflexive responses became slower.

That was precisely why the escort was a select few.

The cavalrymen were all of noble birth as well, every last one a mana-user.

What was required this time was an escort capable of fleeing even from an army if it came to the worst.

Should the Arna Empire prove hostile, no number of guards would make a difference.

Only another army could contend with an army.

Thus, mobility mattered more than collective fighting power.

They were individuals possessed of the ability to spirit the two charges safely away, the finest soldiers in the kingdom, handpicked by Selene, Krische, and the corps commanders.

Safety upon safety.

Among the carriages forming a line at the center, the two charges were not riding in the particularly ornate one.

They had boarded a different box carriage instead.

"……I am famished."

Golden hair tinged with red.

Though the face adorned beneath the elegant long tresses was young, her beauty was like a fairy from a picture book.

Large violet eyes wrapped in long lashes, and perfectly balanced features that seemed crafted to pursue absolute beauty carried a bewitching, almost otherworldly charm.

The white-and-gold dress she wore was embellished with exquisite lace, and the girl wearing it was the first princess of the kingdom.

Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan. (T/N: The source uses クレシェンタ＝ファーナ＝ヴェラ＝アルベラン, a variant of her established full name. Maintained established romanization.)

Truly a figure worthy of being called a child of the gods, or so it should have been.

"Here you are, say ahh."

"……Ahh."

Yet sitting on the lap of a red-haired servant, she looked every bit as young as her appearance suggested.

Her beauty had settled into a pout, her large eyes narrowed with something like dissatisfaction.

The child of the gods, Kreschenta.

But the way she stuffed the cookie brought to her mouth into her cheeks was, by any measure, a spoiled child.

"Excuse me, please do not treat me like a child. Who do you think I am?"

"You are Kreschenta-sama. Krische-sama's little sister."

The servant, her youthful face wearing a gentle smile, spoke coolly and stroked the head of the princess on her lap.

Paying no mind to an increasingly displeased Kreschenta, she squeezed her body close.

Feeling the swells pressing against her back, Kreschenta grew even more irritable and squirmed, but the servant, Bery, held the thrashing Kreschenta firmly.

"You mustn't thrash about. Kreschenta-sama, you were the one who said your bottom was sore."

"……That does not mean I gave you permission to pat my head."

"A good servant acts before being commanded."

"A day will never come in my life when I command you to pat my head."

"My, how difficult you are. When you were playing puppy with Krische-sama, you were ever so obedient."

When Kreschenta tried to turn around to say "why, you," Bery's hand gently pressed her head back into place.

"Come now, Kreschenta-sama, cookies. Say ahh."

"Uuu……mng……"

The complaints forming on her lips were sealed away by a cookie.

Having grasped the weakness of her slight hunger, Bery was doing as she pleased.

*This servant must be punished someday.*

She thought so, but nutritional replenishment took priority.

Regardless, the cookies were extremely delicious.

Honey sweetness seeped from the moist cookie, and inside were nuts, most likely.

They were fragrant in the mouth, and the crisp, crunchy texture was a delight against the teeth.

A master of cooking, a master of sweets.

In that respect, Kreschenta would not deny she valued Bery.

Setting aside the matter of her being the object of her somewhat troublesome sister's dependence, she was willing to acknowledge that Bery was incomparably more capable than Anne, and useful as a servant.

But the chief problem was that this servant held not a shred of respect toward Kreschenta.

The awe and reverence toward Kreschenta that had been there when they first met had now completely vanished.

Kreschenta assigned a ranking to all things.

The same applied to people.

Having grown up in the court society of the royal palace, Kreschenta had clear standards in that regard, adjusting her own attitude and conduct accordingly.

She did not mind if the royalty of other nations or those connected to the royal house were rude toward her.

She had a disposition that found memorizing ornamental words tiresome, so she did not concern herself with such rudeness either.

But this servant's attitude was a different matter.

That her somewhat silly sister Krische, who was careless in many regards, behaved as a superior toward Kreschenta was something she could still accept, but.

Bery and Selene were clearly positioned in Kreschenta's mind as beings who should be beneath her.

After all, she was a princess, a genius whose abilities surpassed everyone else's.

Excluding Krische, who was her sole equal in bloodline and ability, every person in the kingdom ought to fear and submit to her superior, excellent self. In other words, it was their duty.

And yet here she was, treated like a child.

Kreschenta felt an extremely strong indignation at the current state of being treated like any ordinary child.

She was great. The greatest in the kingdom. In terms of her excellence, the greatest in the world.

Because Kreschenta naturally assumed she was superior to everyone else, she felt an intense displeasure at being treated so carelessly.

She recognized this as a truly unforgivable state of affairs.

Yet her current treatment was, at best, that of a child. At worst, she was treated like a dog being fed treats. No, not "like" a dog. She was a dog.

Kreschenta's dissatisfaction swelled ever larger.

Her rising discontent puffed out her soft cheeks just the same.

"Kreschenta-sama, you mustn't go all pouty. It ruins your lovely face."

Bery's hand squished Kreschenta's cheeks.

With a pathetic "pfhew" sound, air leaked out, and Kreschenta glared at her.

"That is what I mean by disrespectful! Wh-what do you think I am?"

"Fufu, no, it's just that when I see you puffing your cheeks like that, somehow it's……a reflex. Please forgive me."

"There is absolutely no sincerity in that!"

"You mustn't raise your voice, Kreschenta-sama. Those outside might hear……Come now, there there, good girl."

"Uuu……!"

Her body was squeezed close, her head stroked.

Dissatisfaction mounted inside Kreschenta, but she could not resist.

Kreschenta was a creature who cared deeply about appearances.

A lady, a perfect princess. She placed great importance on that image of herself, and no matter how dissatisfied she felt, she could not openly voice complaints in front of others.

Yet Bery, understanding this aspect of Kreschenta's character, turned it against her.

It was wicked.

*Someday, she must be punished.*

Reaching for the cookie bag in Bery's hand to distract herself, her hand was caught midway.

"You mustn't, Kreschenta-sama. If I hand them over to you, you'll eat them without end. Come now, stay. Good girl."

"……Do not treat me like a dog."

"Ufufu, you're bored, aren't you? Don't worry, if you're a good girl, I'll give them to you as a proper reward……Oh, I know, Kreschenta-sama, shall we play puppy?"

"I am absolutely never doing that again……!"

"Well now, is that so?"

Bery held Kreschenta in place and produced a cookie, dangling it before her nose.

She was giggling with great amusement.

"Come now, Kreschenta-sama, a cookie. A very sweet and delicious cookie. Ah, this one was made by Krische-sama. Lots and lots of honey."

"Uu……"

The rich scent of honey tickled her nose.

It was plainly a moist, pure honey cookie.

Unlike Bery, who preferred mix-ins like nuts and dried fruit, Krische's cookies tended to be simple, almost cloyingly sweet, and they were Kreschenta's absolute favorite.

It was cowardly.

"Well? Don't you feel like eating one?"

"……I will not fall for that."

"My, what a pity."

Bery broke the cookie in two before Kreschenta's eyes and brought one half to her own mouth.

Kreschenta stared at this in disbelief.

"……Delicious. I really can't compete with this sort of simple thing. The honey seems to spread through your whole mouth……Perhaps I should eat all of Krische-sama's cookies myself?"

"Th-that is unfair. Those were made by onee-sama for me……"

"Krische-sama said to share them with Kreschenta-sama, so I am keeping that promise. They won't keep very long, either, so if Kreschenta-sama says she won't eat them, then I really must eat them all myself."

The half-cookie waggled before her eyes.

Uuu, groaned Kreschenta, and her gaze swayed left and right in time with its motion.

"Well? You truly won't eat them? Then, what a shame. I'll just——"

"W-wait a moment……"

"My. Whatever is the matter? I had thought these cookies would not suit the palate of so magnificent and great a princess……"

"Uuu……"

Bery giggled and waved the cookie.

"Perhaps I was mistaken. The one here is not Kreschenta the Royal Princess at all, but Kreschenta-sama who simply loves sweet cookies?"

Inside Kreschenta, the scales swung wildly from side to side.

Pride and cookie.

The tilt that should have been decisively one-sided gradually leveled, then reversed, and——

"W-woof……"

"My, it was a misunderstanding after all. I am sorry, fufu, I nearly made a mistake. Kreschenta-sama, would you like a cookie?"

"Uuu……woof."

Bery pressed the cookie to her lips with amusement, and cherry-blossom lips clasped the cookie and swallowed it down.

*Good girl*, as her head was stroked, the honey sweetness seemed to seep from the cookie she tasted.

Kreschenta, her desire liberated from the oppression of pride, released from the powerful stress.

Everything that had been suppressed flooded through her mind with overwhelming euphoria.

It overflowed, filling her body, and her cheeks relaxed.

"Fufu, what a truly adorable person you are. Here, Kreschenta-sama, your next cookie."

"W-woof……!"

And so, Kreschenta became a dog.

Lately, Bery and Kreschenta had been repeating the same sort of exchange, spending roughly half of every day that way.

The journey from the fortress to the northern border took about seven days.

From there, another four days to Naut Arna, the capital of the Arna Empire.

Since they would be using the highway connecting the two nations, there were reasonably large towns along the way, but naturally there were also days when they had to camp outdoors.

The sixth day of their journey happened to be one such day.

The only nearby settlement was a small one that looked every bit the mere posting town, with no suitable manor or inn for the first princess of the kingdom.

In such cases, the normal practice was to make camp.

There was the matter of maintaining dignity, but also security reasons.

A princess could not stay at a place where people of unknown origin came and went, and even a single cup of tea or a single dish served there had to be scrutinized.

Unless the servants' backgrounds were also clearly established, she was not permitted to stay freely.

In that sense, camping was the safer option, and once they had purchased travel provisions and horse feed in the town, the group pitched their tents a short distance away and began preparing the evening meal at dusk.

The tedium of pitching tents. Tending the fire and standing watch.

Camping imposed a heavy burden on the soldiers.

A journey already fraying their nerves as escorts of a distinguished personage, the least they wanted was a soft bed to rest in. That was only natural to wish for.

Yet on this particular journey, the soldiers actually welcomed the camping.

"Kreschenta-sama, shall we add a bit more salt?"

"Mm……this much?"

"Yes, that should be fine. Fufu, and now this leaf——"

In charge of cooking was always the culinary researcher, the dilettante chef, Bery Argan.

And assisting her was none other than Her Highness Princess Kreschenta.

The chance to eat a meal prepared by those two made a little fatigue easy to bear.

And when those meals turned out to be far too fine for a campsite, no one could fail to be pleased. The reaction was entirely reasonable.

The cavalrymen, in particular, had been chosen not only for ability but for deep loyalty to the kingdom, and just the other day, one of them had trembled and shed tears upon receiving a meal because it was the princess's own cooking.

There had naturally been those who protested that the two should not be made to cook, but——

"I am in the middle of learning to cook from Argan-sama. I hardly ever get to hold a knife at the palace otherwise……Would it not be all right?"

Kreschenta's upturned gaze had utterly defeated them.

Eyes moist, both hands to her chest.

A slightly sorrowful upward glance at a 45-degree angle.

Kreschenta had completely mastered the art of using her loveliness and beauty to their fullest advantage in order to get her way.

In that regard, Kreschenta possessed an ability that overwhelmingly surpassed Krische, who held comparable potential.

By the second time, no one questioned the two cooking any longer.

As the surrounding soldiers helped pitch tents and carry ingredients, they watched the two work through their tasks briskly, taking in the sight as a treat for the eyes.

"…………"

Kreschenta was always in deadly earnest here.

Her violet eyes tracked and studied Bery's hand movements.

It was merely dicing an onion, and yet the years accumulated there, the devotion poured into it, were staggering, and not easily imitated.

Size, grain, shape. She discerned each one's individual character, making minute adjustments to the angle of her cuts at the fingertips.

The onions that stung Kreschenta's eyes and drew tears when she cut them became docile and obedient the moment Bery took them in hand, as though they were different things entirely. Her skill was that of a veritable onion tamer.

Nor was that all.

When she peeled a daikon, the moon showed through the peel; when she put a blade to meat, there was no resistance; and all the while, Bery was simultaneously checking on the soup and roast and adjusting their seasoning.

From past experience, Kreschenta knew that Bery was capable of processing seven tasks in parallel.

Simply imitating a single technique was meaningless if one could not replicate the thinking that combined them fluidly.

That was why Kreschenta's excellent mind was turning at a rare pace, observing and analyzing every last one of Bery's movements.

One of the reasons Krische regarded Bery with absolute reverence was this level of culinary skill.

Kreschenta had concluded that conquering this area was essential to her "Operation: Make Onee-sama Cling Entirely to Me."

But Bery's culinary skill was an impregnable fortress. Kreschenta had no choice but to acknowledge that in this domain, Bery stood far, far above her.

A vast body of knowledge and experience concerning food, cooking implements, and ingredients, combined with a genius sense and creativity that built on all of it, meant she was always growing.

Today's Bery had already left yesterday's Bery behind.

No matter how hard Kreschenta tried to catch up, the distance would not close, and Bery's back seemed to shimmer in the haze ahead.

Unlike Krische, who had been accumulating experience since childhood, Kreschenta had been a complete novice until coming here.

Though her palate, cultivated on court cuisine, was excellent, that did not give her a significant advantage over Bery.

The handling of knives, the art of seasoning.

In each individual technique, Kreschenta possessed an almost pathological mimicry ability identical to Krische's.

She had already shown growth that would make a professional's jaw drop, but her opponent was Bery Argan, an artist who permitted no compromise in cooking whatsoever.

Even Kreschenta was at a disadvantage if she tried to take her on half-heartedly.

"The meat is ready now."

"……I don't think the inside is even close to cooked yet."

"It's good to let beef rest a little."

Bery swiftly transferred the meat from the grill over the campfire to a plate.

The color of the meat showed barely any searing. Bery placed it on the plate and covered it with a cloth.

"Harmful agents and insects are weak to heat, so meat is fundamentally cooked by grilling, stewing, frying, and so on. However, if you overcook it, you end up ruining its deliciousness, so every ingredient has its ideal heat. You see?"

She tasted the soup and continued.

"With beef, after you sear the surface, you let it rest a while and let the residual heat cook the inside. Since beef has few harmful agents and insects, many people eat it with almost no heat in the center. But personally, I feel more at ease giving it a little rest like this and gently letting the heat penetrate. It becomes more tender too."

"Tender……"

"Yes. Meat tastes better tender, don't you think?"

Bery pointed to the minced onion she had rubbed onto the meat earlier.

"Like I showed you, you can soak it a bit in minced or grated onion, or marinate it in milk, and there are various other methods to tenderize it……Well, we can try those another time. Ingredients are fundamentally obedient, you see. The more care you give them, the more delicious they become."

Bery explained with evident enjoyment.

That, too, was a point that irked Kreschenta.

Bery freely revealed everything in her repertoire.

This composure, against the genius Kreschenta, no less.

The knowledge in her head was bottomless; no matter how much Kreschenta memorized, new methods and concepts were presented.

*Surely she must hit a limit eventually.* With that thought Kreschenta kept drawing out knowledge, but Bery simply kept pouring forth her vast store with a smile.

This composure before a serious Kreschenta was truly unforgivable.

When Kreschenta unknowingly pursed her lips, Bery smiled wryly and told her not to make such a face.

"Relaxing and enjoying yourself is important in cooking. Head and heart both soft, thinking about how to make it delicious. Krische-sama always looks like she's having fun, doesn't she?"

"……Hmph, I know that."

"There's no need to rush. You pick things up incredibly fast, Kreschenta-sama. Next time you cook together with Krische-sama, she might be quite surprised at how skilled you've become. She'll be very happy."

Kreschenta imagined it, just a little, and her cheeks relaxed.

Noticing she was being watched with amusement, she shook her head vigorously.

"Wh-what shall I do next?"

"Fufu, truly an adorable person. Well then——"

The grilled meat was tender beyond anything one would expect from a campsite, and the sauce made from onion, red wine, and grated fruit was worthy of court cuisine.

On top of that, a sweet pumpkin-based soup and a pumpkin pie made in a frying pan.

Before anyone realized it, Bery had even prepared a platter of cut fruit.

The dish had been made under Bery's complete control, directed and guided, yet it was beautiful and delicious, and Kreschenta could hardly complain.

Trying to suppress her relaxing cheeks as she ate, Kreschenta watched Bery.

For a mana-user, Bery ate rather little; instead, she seemed happier watching the people around her and Kreschenta eat with relish.

"This pumpkin soup reminds me of the one the Corps Commander made. ……Truly, to eat something this fine at a campsite is a luxury."

The one who said this was one of the soldiers in black-painted armor.

Kirik, the leader of Squad Nineteen and the escort for this journey. A man in his prime with one eye.

Along with Squad Twenty, the two squads accompanied this journey, and he was their de facto leader.

"Did Argan-sama teach her?"

"No. When it comes to pumpkin dishes, I learned more from her than the other way around. ……It's Krische-sama's favorite."

Bery spoke happily and gently traced the pumpkin pie.

Her profile, lit by the campfire.

That face, which usually looked youthful, appeared somehow mature, and the way she gazed lovingly at the pie was as beautiful as the maiden sung of by bards.

An elegant flower blooming in a garden.

Not the vivid, unmistakable beauty of Selene.

A single blossom shining deep within the forest.

Not the fantastical beauty of Krische or Kreschenta, either.

A flower beside a shaded tree you noticed with a start upon sitting down.

Her beauty held no splendor or brilliance, yet it undeniably drew the heart.

That was the kind of charm she possessed.

"When I see a pumpkin, I can't help but think of her. I wonder if Krische-sama is on the battlefield right now."

"Most likely, if it's gone quickly. ……Please rest assured. There is no one in this world who can match the Corps Commander. Unless they brought a dragon, perhaps……haha, well, that's the kind of person she is."

"Yes. ……But she pushes herself so hard, and that worries me a little."

Bery took a small bite of pie and swallowed it.

Then she traced her lips with her fingertips. It was a gesture that carried a certain heat.

Kirik gazed at it, half-spellbound, and nodded in agreement.

Recalling Krische's actions during the recent delaying operation, he understood what she meant.

"That is……certainly true."

Like a mother worrying about her daughter.

Like a maiden awaiting her beloved's return.

Like a faithful wife thinking of her husband at the front.

Kirik wondered what words could properly describe her.

The anxiety proportional to her deep love only made her all the more captivating.

Almost as a matter of course, he offered words to ease that worry.

"As far as our unit is concerned, however, compared to before, the company's cohesion has grown stronger, and everyone respects the Corps Commander from the heart. Captain Dagra foremost among them, we all strive to minimize the burden on the Corps Commander, so there should be no repetition of what happened before."

"……Thank you."

Bery smiled and bowed her head, and Kirik hastily shook his.

Every gesture of hers was refined; Bery was the very picture of a proper noblewoman.

Yet there was no pretension in it, and everything was so natural that when faced with her directly, she unwittingly made earnest men like Kirik straighten up.

Kreschenta, meanwhile, continued eating in silence, observing every one of Bery's movements, the reactions of others, each and every detail.

Even for Kreschenta, who had grown up in the palace observing the conduct of nobles, the aristocratic air Bery wore was unique and worthy of study.

The tone of her voice, her expressions, the movements of her hands and lips, the direction of her gaze.

There was much to learn from Bery's manner, which unconsciously drew the goodwill of others.

Even though Princess Kreschenta was right here, the atmosphere in this space belonged to Bery.

She attracted the attention and goodwill of everyone around her and stirred their desire to protect.

How to freely command such an atmosphere was something Kreschenta always thought about, and what she should be seeking lay right there.

And yet, what she felt there was, as always, dissatisfaction.

As things stood on this journey, it was plainly Bery rather than Kreschenta who was gathering the interest and goodwill of those around them, and that was deeply irksome.

A princess, one who should become the queen of this kingdom, being outdone by a servant in popularity and attention was a truly dire state of affairs.

Her dissatisfaction unconsciously puffed out those soft cheeks.

Bery noticed, smiled wryly, and stroked them.

A hissing sound of deflating air.

"Fufu, worrying won't change anything, will it?"

"……That is so, Argan-sama. What I am worried about is that you might make yourself ill worrying about onee-sama too much. If that happened, I don't know what onee-sama would say to me."

"……Yes, I am sorry."

Bery wiped Kreschenta's mouth with a handkerchief, saying "your mouth is dirty," and Kreschenta's cheeks reddened.

The men who saw this exchanged glances.

Kreschenta, who displayed dignified composure beyond her years, became like a girl her own age around Bery, just a little bit willful.

That made the heavy burden forced upon her slender shoulders feel closer to the men, and today, too, she collected their respect.

But Kreschenta did not notice that what she sought was right at her feet.

And so today, once again, she merely stoked her rivalry, filled with petty dissatisfaction and the sting of defeat.

Small frustrations and small joys.

The journey of the young princess and the servant who supported her continued along in just such a manner, uneventfully, today as always.

# Chapter 87: The Innocent Princess and the Trainer

The Arna Empire was flanked by mountains to east and west, with a broad coastline facing the sea to the north.

Its territory was not particularly vast, roughly the size of the kingdom's northern region, but trade with the island nation to the north was thriving and prosperous. By nature, it was best described as a maritime state.

Where the mountains on either side blocked the kingdom's northern region formed the border between the two nations, and customs posts for each country stood at the frontier.

Between the two posts lay a distance of roughly three ri, and there a market had been built where merchants traded in countless goods. (T/N: 三里; per author's scale from C.78, approximately 1.2 km.)

Mercenaries guarding caravans and ruffians of all sorts, great merchants known to everyone.

Given the commerce flowing back and forth between the nations, tariffs were naturally levied on imports and exports, but trade between the two closely connected nations remained vigorous.

There was no way to oversee every last transaction.

Goods that had received a wink and a nod from customs officials, or hidden cash and valuables, were commonly traded here.

Distinguishing between untaxed legitimate goods and illegal transactions was an enormously difficult task, and to a large extent both nations tacitly turned a blind eye.

In the grand scheme, these were trifling sums, and those who profited from them were, in most cases, merchants and immensely wealthy nobles.

Such deeply rooted corruption was difficult to uproot, and the matter had been largely left alone.

Considering that the result was a more active commerce between the two nations, it was not all bad.

The checkpoint could be passed with nothing more than Princess Kreschenta's seal and signature, without the cargo even being inspected.

Impersonating royalty was punishable by summary execution with all personal assets seized.

Few would dare to question it, and advance notice had been sent, so there were no complications.

"……This is my first time leaving the country. It's lively, isn't it."

Bery peered out the small window with interest, and Kreschenta, on her lap as always, sighed in displeasure at the sight.

"I would like to do something about this eventually. It is practically lawlessness."

Kreschenta did not tolerate misconduct by anyone other than herself.

Unregulated chaos, in most cases, produced outcomes beyond Kreschenta's ability to predict.

The rooting out of corrupt nobles. The confiscation of their assets.

Her eyes were already drawn to plans for after the civil war's conclusion.

In her mind, this country was already hers.

Bery smiled wryly and sandwiched the puffed cheeks between both hands.

*Pfhew*, she enjoyed the sound of escaping air and smiled.

"What sort of country would you like to build, Kreschenta-sama?"

"One where I hold everything in my grasp. ……Please stop that."

She gave Bery's hand a pat.

But for all its sound, it was a remarkably gentle one.

"I shall purge the rotten nobles and clean out the stagnant, congested parts. Uncle will surely squander a great deal in this civil war, so I will confiscate their assets to compensate for that as well and direct it to state administration. The kingdom's laws have too many loopholes as they stand, so things will be quite busy."

Bery listened with something like admiration and stroked Kreschenta's head.

This time, the hand was not batted away.

"I will fatten the populace and increase national power. The nobles hold too much power as things are, so I wish to address that as well. A system is needed to raise the intellectual level of commoners and appoint the talented. At present, advancement comes almost exclusively through military service or through the incompetent inheriting by birth. Fools heaped upon greater fools are all that gather……"

"Fufu, to Kreschenta-sama, everyone is a gathering of fools, no?"

"There is a difference between fools you can hold a conversation with and fools you cannot."

*Do not interrupt me*, she said, displeased, pressing the back of her head against Bery's chin and grinding it in. Bery let out an amused laugh.

"But it is a lovely idea. Schools for elementary education of the common people, was it? I have heard that some country has them."

"The Republic of Nerka, far south of Galshan. The Christand army assigns those who can do arithmetic to logistics, but that level of calculation could be grasped by any fool if lightly taught, I should think. If funds were allocated and facilities and laws put in place, that is. If the general intelligence of the common populace improves, one can expect an increase in national power across all sectors, beginning with commerce."

*Though it must be viewed on a ten-year scale*, she said, and her cheeks puffed again.

"Vases and paintings and art and such, spending money on nothing but frivolities. I have always thought it wasteful. Perhaps I should clear the rubbish out of the palace entirely."

Bery squished the puffy cheeks of the petulant Kreschenta and told her:

"I do think excessively drastic measures might be unwise……"

"One need only cite financial hardship as the reason and it will all work out. If the royal house practices austerity, the merchants may not care, but one can capture the public's interest. On top of that, if we bestow gifts upon the populace, it becomes easier to maintain control, does it not? 'Ever since I became the head of the royal house, the crown has practiced frugality,' and so forth."

*Stop that*, she batted the hand again, then kicked her feet happily back and forth.

Bery stroked the girl's cheek and smiled.

She did not delight in luxury, nor did she delight in causing others pain. Though there were many problems, it was certain that she was trying to steer the kingdom in a good direction.

Whether one could say she was virtuous as an individual was not easy to assert, but her goals were legitimate, and like Krische, her wishes were purer and more beautiful than anyone's.

"……That may be so."

"That is how it will be. Of course there will be various problems, so as Argan-sama says, I will take things gradually to some extent……But first, the confiscation of assets from the corrupt nobles. I already have my eye on them."

Kreschenta continued happily.

"Once we build up national strength that way, onee-sama won't have so much work to do eventually, and then I could make her something like my personal guard. ……Fufu, Argan-sama, you can just stay holed up at the estate, pining for onee-sama's and my return."

"Fufu, once there is no more work, I think Krische-sama will want to cook together with me, though."

"Muu……"

Kreschenta shifted position on Bery's lap and glared at her head-on.

Bery smiled.

"……Don't think things will always go your way, Argan-sama. By that time, I shall be addressed as Her Majesty the Queen."

"Yes. But I am Kreschenta-sama's subject and at the same time, one who serves Krische-sama. ……If Krische-sama were to wish it, I would have no choice but to give in, but otherwise, Krische-sama stays by my side. And it is a servant's duty to follow her mistress's wishes."

A declaration, all but saying outright that she herself would always come first.

Bery beaming with a smile.

Krische, Kreschenta, Selene, Lazura, Bogan.

Everyone around Bery had been someone who tried their hardest.

The little pats against the cheek were very gentle.

# Chapter 90: The Princess Who Ensnares

(T/N: 一尺＝約30cm, per author's note at chapter opening.)

"Well then, Kreschenta-sama."

"Yes."

Carved from the cliff face, something that could be called neither castle nor temple.

After entering, Kreschenta parted from Bery and was accompanied only by a few escorts, those with standing as nobles.

The structure was hollowed from the cliff, but the floor and the walls up to a height of about six shaku were coated smooth with white Forged Stone. (T/N: 六尺 ≈ approximately 180cm / about 6 feet.)

The white light of the eternal magic lamps illuminated the surroundings, while the ceiling and the upper walls returned a faint blue reflection.

It was the sheen of magic crystals themselves.

This palace had originally been a mine that produced many fine magic crystals, and they had repurposed it into its present form.

Because of that, exposed magic crystals could be seen here and there in the bare rock.

The crystallized form of mana flowing through the ley lines.

That was how magic crystals were described, and perhaps because of it, the ambient floating mana in the air felt considerably denser than in the kingdom.

Inside the palace, it was especially so.

*Couldn't this be put to some use?* Thinking such things, she followed the escorting guard.

Down long corridors, deeper and deeper in, until the sight before her was a great black door, both its height and width a full jou. (T/N: 一丈 = 10 shaku ≈ approximately 3m / about 10 feet.)

The Chamber of the Dragon Priestess, the place of audience.

The gate guards slowly pushed open the heavy doors, and beyond lay a vast space.

A ceiling so high one might strain to see it, and an orderly forest of pillars.

The walls shimmered faintly with the phosphorescence of magic crystals, and the soldiers following Kreschenta held their breath for a moment at the fantastical sight.

Down a path of Forged Stone laid in the center, at the far end was a waterfall fed by a spring and an altar carved from magic crystal in the form of a great dragon.

And a single woman, veiled in pale blue.

Those who worshipped the dragon also held reverence for nature, and it was common for them not to use chairs, simply placing a cushion underneath instead.

The one who stood at the pinnacle of the nation was no different.

She sat without a chair, legs folded beneath her, watching Kreschenta.

A welcoming flute sounded, but unlike the stirring music of the kingdom, it was somehow quiet and wistful.

The soldiers listened, entranced, while Kreschenta, paying it no mind, strode forward with a dignified gait and stood alone before the woman.

Flanking the chamber beyond the pillars were the Archbishop who served as the Priestess Princess's chief minister, the four Grand Priests.

And the musicians, and several dozen priestly ladies-in-waiting.

A row of white figures. Only Kreschenta, at the center, wore the vivid red dress adorned with roses.

Her elegant red, with her golden hair ornamented by a silver tiara bearing a crescent-moon ornament on the left, stood out sharply in the space of white and blue.

"It has been some time, Priestess Princess. I am delighted beyond measure to be granted the honor of this audience."

She did not bow her head.

Resolute, poised, she stood and faced her directly.

Kreschenta had adopted not the attitude of a princess, but of one who was the rightful successor to the royal house.

Some of the priests lined up on either side exchanged glances, but she did not care.

By any means necessary, she would first capture attention, draw consciousness toward herself.

Bind those gazes, gather them, and dominate the room.

That was the art of a sovereign who commands the hearts of men.

"It has been a while, Princess Kreschenta. You have grown considerably since last I saw you."

A smile played beneath the pale-blue veil.

Long black hair, a beauty mark. Though her manner carried something beyond youth, her composed features, the long, sharp eyes, and the tall, slender figure visible even while seated lent her a dignified allure.

The pale-blue and gold robe she wore was simple, by no means extravagant, yet the elegance of the thin fabric, clinging to and partially revealing the skin, wreathed her in mystique.

She had ascended to the nation's highest seat at fifteen, and her reign now spanned forty years. She was the Priestess Princess.

"I have heard of the kingdom's plight. The death of your father, the late king……I have grieved deeply. I have been considering what words might suffice, but even now, upon this occasion, I find I have none."

She had no name.

When she became the Priestess Princess, her name was lost, and she was bound solely to her role.

Human and yet not human; that was how she was treated, and in the Empire, she was the one closest to the divine.

Though she was merely speaking, the men at Kreschenta's side felt a pressure, and their bodies tensed.

The air possessed by one born and raised to be a ruler was like that.

Such a being, merely by existing, cowed ordinary people into submission and compelled their obedience.

"Thank you. Though it was an unwanted death, I am sure my father the king is pleased by your sentiments, wherever his blood has mingled with the earth."

In that space, the only one who stood as an equal was Kreschenta alone.

Rote exchanges of pleasantries. These were the sort of tedious formalities Kreschenta despised most, yet she could not simply ignore them.

Betraying nothing of her true feelings, she spoke impassively.

"The reason I have come before you today is related to that matter. I believe Zarnalibea-sama has conveyed the general situation."

"Yes, I have been informed. That you seek the Empire's aid, I am told."

Kreschenta smiled and said nothing.

A brief silence spread. The Priestess Princess wondered at her silence but continued.

"……That said, the circumstances are what they are. In the case of an invasion from a foreign power, as with the recent aggression by the Holy Elsren Empire, the Empire would gladly lend its hand, in keeping with our longstanding friendship and alliance treaty. That I will pledge, in the name of the Sacred Spirits."

Kreschenta kept silent, waiting for more.

The Priestess Princess went on.

"However, this war is an internal matter of your nation. Regardless of which side holds the greater legitimacy, we are outsiders. Out of respect for your storied kingdom, we have no choice but to decline participation in this civil war. We are both independent, sovereign nations, each with our own politics and convictions. Interference would leave seeds of discord in the years to come."

Sadly, pityingly.

The Priestess Princess regarded the young princess.

"Princess Kreschenta. As an individual, I do not claim to feel nothing about your situation. But I, too, am charged with stewardship of this Empire……I am not permitted to make decisions from the sentiment of my heart. ……I understand that such an answer, given to Princess Kreschenta who has come all this way knowing the dangers of the road, is cruel."

Even if you appeal to emotion, I cannot respond in kind.

That was the Priestess Princess's position.

Looking at the information available, the Priestess Princess felt that legitimacy lay on Kreschenta's side.

She did feel genuine sympathy. But that did not mean she could take Kreschenta's side.

The princess's faction had lost Christand, their hero, and was being driven into a corner.

If the situation were in Kreschenta's favor, that might be another matter, but lending a hand now and somehow suffering a defeat could endanger the stability of the Empire itself.

She could not side with this girl.

At present, the advantage belonged to the prince.

Having stated her position through formalities, the Priestess Princess looked to Kreschenta for her reaction and frowned.

Because Kreschenta was smiling even after hearing those words.

"……Hearing that puts my mind at ease."

"……?"

*What I desire, Priestess Princess, is the Empire's neutrality. I am not seeking aid with this civil war.*

*How troublesome*, Kreschenta said, feigning innocence.

Without a hint of shame, smiling brightly.

Evade the other party's assumptions, seize the initiative.

Read their thoughts and logic, expose their defenses.

Mislead, confound, and always be the one in control.

The cursed child of Alberan.

Visiting another country alone, stirring this much turbulence in her counterpart's heart, and still smiling as though nothing had happened.

A leader acts not on personal feeling but on practical benefit.

The cursed child of Alberan, Kreschenta.
