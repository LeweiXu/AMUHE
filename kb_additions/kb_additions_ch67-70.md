## CORE CHARACTERS

### Elrouga Faren
- **Nickname (Krische-given):** "Gaikotsu" (Skeleton) — bestowed by Krische in C.68, following the same logic as "Hagawashi" for Dagra. Krische knew "Gaikotsu" was an existing unflattering nickname but offered it as a genuine term of affection. Elrouga could not refuse.
- **Personality (expanded, C.68–69):** Privately a deeply kind man who loves peace, flowers, and watching children grow. His fearsome appearance has meant a lifetime of being avoided — even his son cries at the sight of him; even stroking his son's head has been stopped by his wife. He had never been given a candy until Krische offered one. Was moved to genuine emotion by the gesture and accepted Krische's head-stroking in return. Effectively won over completely by one candy.
- **Relationship with Nozan:** Long-term battlefield comrades and mutual mentors. Elrouga considers their bond that of true war-comrades despite an age gap.
- **Adjutant:** Kyunez Karza — plump, youthful-looking, mid-thirties noble and mana-user. More competent than he looks. Frequently alarmed by Krische's behavior.
- **Tactical advice to Krische (C.68):** Counseled Krische that giving subordinates responsibility fosters both loyalty and growth. Demonstrated the principle via Dagra. Pointed out that entrusting command *to* subordinates signals confidence rather than dismissal. Echoed Bogan's own philosophy.
- **Performance at Battle of the Central Plain (C.70):** Led the Fourth Corps in a lance-formation central breakthrough, splitting three thousand enemy reserves left and right and opening the gap through which Krische and the black century struck. Earned Krische's genuine praise for "opening the path."

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **Departure scene (C.67):** Extended farewell kissing session with Bery before departure, requiring Selene to physically intervene. Exchanged a private moment with Selene — admitted she felt frightened by the separation for the first time, likening it to her fear of being disliked. Selene comforted her, affirmed that fear is proof of caring. Both wept slightly.
- **Tactical deployment (C.68–70):** Led the First Corps plus black century in a flanking maneuver over the Kureil Range, seized enemy supply train, then waited in forest near Nozan's position. At battle, Keith delegated rear command; Begil given vanguard cleanup role; Krische personally led the black century breakthrough. Killed Klare Markelus personally — stepped up the shaft of his spear, landed on his horse, kicked him clear, and severed his neck in the same motion.
- **Nickname explanation to Elrouga (C.68):** Explained "Hagawashi" to Elrouga as an "affectionate nickname" to foster closeness. Genuinely believed this framing. Elrouga and Dagra shared a silent look of mutual commiseration.
- **Logistics-awareness (C.68):** Demonstrated clear understanding of mountain logistics constraints — specifically that wagons are necessary for supply lines, limiting mountain crossings to troop-only movements. Chose the crossing point specifically to reach the enemy's supply train.

### Selene Christand
- **Farewell scene (C.67):** Private moment with Krische before departure. Cried quietly, apologized for not being able to keep her promise of peaceful estate life sooner. Was comforted in turn by Krische, who reaffirmed their shared dream.

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Departure speech (C.67):** Delivered a speech to fifteen thousand soldiers before departing for the Arna Empire. Falsely claimed to have received a reply from the Arna Empire granting permission to visit (the letter had in fact just been sent the previous day, one-way, with no reply). Purpose: to spread the rumor of Arna Empire support as a deterrent to fence-sitters considering Gildanstein. Also included a fabricated personal account of pleading with Gildanstein to stop his cruelties. Dagra served as the pre-arranged signal-raiser for the soldiers' cheer.
- **Continued dog-mode (C.67):** Competed with Bery for Krische's kisses before departure, and was tapped on the forehead by Selene.

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Granmeld Vakus (グランメルド＝ヴァーカス)
- **Expanded role (C.69–70):** Present as a corps commander in Nozan's eastern army. Commanded the left flank at the Battle of the Central Plain, pressing the enemy right flank with his great war mace. Described as: wolf-themed armor, deep scar across the cheek, a great war mace "longer than he is tall" (six feet seven inches). Leads by Nozan's permission to "do as you like." - **Note:** Knowledge base previously listed this character as グランメルド＝ヴァクス (Vakus). Chapter 69 uses ヴァーカス (Vaakus). Author variation; using established "Vakus."

### Nozan Velraigh
- **Battle of the Central Plain (C.69–70):** Commanded fifteen thousand against Klare Markelus's twenty-seven thousand. Correctly deduced Krische and Elrouga's position in the nearby forest from enemy camp behavior (supply train not arriving, camp too quiet). Chose to use an echelon formation on day four once Krische arrived — a deliberate signal to Krische that he had understood. Deliberately showed apparent disorder in the echelon's rear lines to bait Markelus into committing his left-flank cavalry early. Seething with cold fury at Bogan's death; frames the battle as Bogan's memorial.
- **Assessment of Elrouga:** Considers him a long-term comrade and mentor, trusts him completely.

### Klare Markelus (クラレ＝マルケルス)
- **Background:** Sixty years old; scion of the Markelus Ducal house connected to royal bloodline. Inherited his general's rank rather than earning it. Virtues: decisiveness, personal courage, willing to lead from the front. Flaw: cannot think deeply; expects adversaries to fight honorably and directly as he does; makes conclusions that flatter his own self-image. Assessment: would have been an excellent battalion or centurion commander.
- **Civil war role:** Commanded the Gildanstein central army tasked with blocking Nozan's eastern advance.
- **Death (C.70):** Killed by Krische personally. Made a final stand calling out for someone to take his head — Krische stepped up his spear shaft, kicked him off his horse, and split his neck. His adjutant Dora, who attempted to shield him, was bisected by Kalua a moment before.

### Dora (ドーラ)
- Klare Markelus's adjutant. Female. Attempted to interpose herself to protect Klare in his final moments. Bisected by Kalua before Krische reached Klare. Deceased (C.70).

### Kyunez Karza (クイネズ＝カーザ)
- Elrouga Faren's adjutant. Slightly plump young man, mid-thirties, noble, mana-user. Gentle, soft-featured face with a calming effect on others. Competent despite his appearance. Frequently goes pale from Krische's behavior. Present throughout the Kureil flanking maneuver.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Mountain Logistics
- Armies can send soldiers over moderate mountains, but supply wagons cannot follow. Without wagons, individual soldiers can carry approximately 10 days' provisions at maximum (less with full equipment). This makes wagon-impassable mountain terrain effectively an advance-barrier.
- Krische's flanking force used pack horses (expendable, provisions when killed) and sheep (mobile provisions) instead of wagons for the crossing.

### Echelon Formation (斜線陣) — Expanded
- Advancing wings in staircase intervals from one side, concentrating force on the leading wing. Primary advantages: breakthrough power at the main-effort wing, time gained on the delayed wing. Primary risks: recovery difficulty after breakdown; cadence gaps between staircase intervals. Requires extensively drilled troops.
- Used by Nozan Velraigh at the Battle of the Central Plain as a signal to Krische that she had been located, and to bait Markelus into committing his cavalry prematurely.

### Battle of the Central Plain (C.69–70)
- **Forces:** Nozan Velraigh (15,000) vs. Klare Markelus (27,000). Krische + Elrouga (approx. 10,000, including First Corps and Fourth Corps) striking the rear.
- **Outcome:** Markelus killed; army surrendered after less than one *koku* following Christand's rear entry. Decisive Christand victory.
- **Key sequence:** Krische and Elrouga crossed the Kureil Range early, seized Markelus's supply train on day one, hid in forest near Nozan's position for three days, waited for Nozan to shape the battle. On day four, Nozan used an echelon formation as a coordinated signal; Elrouga's Fourth Corps used a lance breakthrough to open the rear; Krische and the black century drove through to kill Markelus directly.
