## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Vinsreel
- **Role:** Warrior Leader (戦士長) of Kreisharana. Chieftain Alkirens's son; Lila's older brother.
- **Age:** Early thirties. Became a warrior at age thirteen.
- **Combat:** Kreisharana's strongest warrior. Has personally slain more than ten magical beasts. Won the Warrior Leader title from Velvas after a full koku (~2 hour) aerial battle. Since taking office, has not allowed a single magical beast near the village.
- **Status (C.147):** Tracking the intruder (Krische) by following hoofprints and signs of magical beast kills.

### Velvas
- **Role:** Guardian of the forbidden domain (禁域の守手). Ranked equally with the Warrior Leader.
- **Appearance:** Large man with jade tiger claw scars on his cheek.
- **Combat:** Former Warrior Leader; lost the title to Vinsreel. Still a formidable fighter. Uses an eight-shaku spear.
- **Status (C.149):** Defeated by Krische; slammed into a cliff face and temporarily incapacitated. Refused to yield despite recognizing Krische's overwhelming superiority.

### Alkirens
- **Role:** Chieftain (族長) of Kreisharana. Father of Vinsreel and Lila.
- **Appearance:** White-bearded elder with strong bearing despite his age.
- **Status (C.150):** Mobilized all warriors and personally sortied toward the forbidden domain upon learning of Krische's breach.

### Bikirles
- **Role:** Elder (長老); previous chieftain of Kreisharana.
- **Appearance:** Bald, chest-length beard, thin and frail but sharp-eyed. Still radiates authority.

### Milkires
- **Role:** Marquis (侯爵); formerly aligned with Duke Arcasakos.
- **Status (C.147):** Entrusted by Kreschenta with the "investigation" into the purge — effectively ordered to fabricate a cover story. Chose submission.

### Lila Sharana (update)
- **Title confirmed:** Priestess of the divine being (聖霊の巫女). Full introduction: daughter of Chieftain Alkirens.
- **Mother:** Died of illness when Lila was young. Lila once tried to sneak to Yagelnaus to save her; was caught and returned.
- **Relationship with Krische (C.148):** Despite being coerced, developed genuine empathy. Cannot bring herself to hate Krische. Offered to facilitate an "audience" with the dragon rather than a fight. Compared Krische to the moon — beautiful without knowing it.
- **Status (C.149-150):** Accompanied Krische past Velvas's guard into the forbidden domain. Attempted to address Yagelnaus but was overcome by terror. Thrown clear by Krische when the dragon attacked.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Kreisharana — Expanded (C.146–150)
- **Population:** ~3,000.
- **Settlement pattern:** Small scattered settlements across Albyagel rather than centralized towns. The largest is called "the village" (里).
- **Griffin cavalry:** Fewer than 100 (down from ~1,000 historically).
- **Social structure:** Warrior society. Chieftain chosen by duel. Women expected to gather plentiful harvests; the chieftain's daughter is no exception. Warriors are honored above all. Men who are not griffin cavalry are "levied warriors" (従士) who devote leisure time to training.
- **Decline:** Gradual depopulation as people leave for the lowlands. No entertainment; only warriors are honored. The decline is slow but unmistakable.
- **Alarm system:** "Tiger-entry bell" (虎入りの鐘) — cascading bell signals across settlements trigger mobilization. All non-cavalry men take up spears; women gather in pre-designated storehouses.
- **Cultural teachings:** "Adornment brings arrogance and a blade's edge. Beauty of the body reaches not even the divine." — A teaching from the Sacred Convention warning against materialism and vanity, rooted in the lesson of Kreisharana's historical defeat. Despite this, women still adorn themselves lavishly at festivals.
- **Clothing:** Men wear iron breastplate, gauntlets, greaves over riding trousers; nothing under the breastplate (bare torso). Women wear wrapped cloth around waist and chest. No ornamental accessories normally worn. The Warrior Leader is distinguished only by red cloth tied to the griffin's tail.
- **Language:** The Kreisharana language is what the kingdom calls "Old Tongue" (古語) — the language once used in the kingdom's north. Krische can speak it (learned from books), though with imperfect pronunciation.
- **Forbidden domain guardians:** Three permanent sentinels at the road entrance, commanded by Velvas (a warrior ranked equal to the Warrior Leader).

### Kreisharana — Historical War (expanded, C.149-150)
- **Opponent:** The Vagusarido (ヴァーグサリド) tribal confederation — the kingdom of Alberan's predecessor.
- **Key figures:** Great Chieftain Bazarishe (already established); his lieutenants Mitskronia and Bernaich (the Twin Mountains are named after them).
- **Strategy:** Bazarishe's forces built fortifications on barren ground, equipped all soldiers with armor and shields, used siege engines. When frontal assault at the Dragon's Maw stalled, Bernaich infiltrated the north to turn Kreisharana's vassal tribes and target women and children.
- **Outcome:** Kreisharana lost the north, its great chieftain was slain, and the tribe was driven into the Albyagel mountains (the griffin breeding grounds).
- **Legacy:** The defeat became a foundational cautionary tale — Kreisharana fell because it honored only martial strength, not harmony.

### Yagelnaus — Direct Observation (C.150)
- **Appearance:** Enormous — body the size of a small hill; head reaches above the massive cliff behind it when standing. Grey, rock-like scales. Bat-like wings large enough to swallow a small village. Red-purple eyes, gem-like and inorganic.
- **Mana:** Overwhelmingly vast — estimated to exceed a thousand or even ten thousand Krisches combined. The mana spilling from its body distorts the ambient mana of the entire basin merely by existing.
- **Aura:** Its gaze alone warps air and disrupts mana. Causes instinctive terror in all living things. Even Krische experienced fear for the first time.
- **Communication:** Telepathic — speaks without opening its mouth; mana carries the "voice" directly into the listener's skull. The voice itself causes surrounding mana to quiver. Described as intensely uncomfortable, like having a foreign presence inside one's body.
- **Personality:** Aware, intelligent, amused by Krische's audacity. Not hostile toward harmless visitors but reacted with force to Krische's challenge.
- **Wingbeat:** A single beat creates a devastating windstorm that carves the earth, peels up grass, and turns pebbles into projectiles across the entire 2-ri basin.
- **Vulnerability demonstrated (C.150):** Krische's thrown spear penetrated its scales at the base of the left wing, drawing blood. The spear continued through to shatter the magic crystal cliff behind the dragon. This confirms the scales, while ore-like, can be pierced by Krische's attacks.

### Krische's Spear Technique (expanded, C.150)
- Against Yagelnaus, Krische threw an 8-shaku Kreisharana spear approximately one ri (~400 m) with sufficient force to pierce the dragon's scales, bore through flesh at the wing base, and shatter the magic crystal cliff behind it. The throw was executed during the dragon's windstorm, from a standing position with minimal run-up, by channeling force through her entire body in a whip-like motion.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Moon as identity** — Krische's name means "crescent moon." In C.148, the waning crescent moon appears as she articulates her nature — beautiful but cold, giving no warmth. Lila responds: "The moon knows not itself." The moon becomes a symbol of Krische's unconscious beauty and her inability to perceive her own worth.

**Beauty of the body / beauty of the heart** — The Kreisharana teaching that outward beauty cannot reach the divine, but inner purity can. Lila applies this to Krische, hoping the dragon will perceive her sincerity. Contrasted with Krische's own belief that her beauty is merely ornamental.

**Fear as growth** — C.150 marks the first time Krische experiences genuine fear. She connects this to understanding her mother Grace's sacrifice — Grace feared losing Krische more than dying. This realization makes Krische feel "a little more normal," suggesting her emotional development continues.
