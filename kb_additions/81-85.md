## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Christand
- **Dragon's Maw final assault (C.81–82):** Led the black century in a night assault on the mountain ridge fortress while Nozan's forces created a smoke screen from Bernaich timber soaked in oil. Personally cleared the fortress, killing all defenders including Adjutant Rubens. Raised the crescent-skull banner over the fortress. Intercepted Geltz Wirring and forced his surrender through a combination of demonstration of force and ultimatum: surrender or she would slaughter every remaining soldier on the mountain.
- **Post-Hilkintos execution strategy (C.81):** Articulated a deliberate doctrine of terror: using brutal public executions and overwhelming force to make enemies surrender before fighting. Framed this as the fundamental purpose of an army — "to force the other side into submission through fear backed by violence." Proposed to assume the role of feared figure deliberately, arguing this would ultimately save lives and protect Selene. Received Elrouga's reluctant support and Nozan's logical approval.
- **Prisoner policy shift (C.82):** Refused to accept Rubens's surrender on the grounds that Geltz was still alive and could revoke it under Sacred Convention rules. Explicitly stated no prisoners would be taken in the fortress. Killed Rubens by crushing his neck underfoot.
- **"Role" concept (C.81, C.83):** Articulated a self-assigned role as the feared figure, separate from Selene's role as the respected leader. Acknowledged that she is already disliked and proposed to weaponize that. Told Selene she doesn't care about others' opinions as long as those she loves still love her.
- **Future war strategy (C.83):** Outlined a post-civil-war defensive plan: contract the kingdom's strategic perimeter to the forests in the west and Wulfenite in the east, defend the capital with Selene, and personally conduct offensive operations against hostile neighbors. Estimated "a few years" to eliminate all threats. Expressed this in the same flat, matter-of-fact tone as cooking plans.
- **Character profile alignment (C.84):** "Law / Evil" — values rules and discipline but is fundamentally self-interested. Approximate height: 147 cm.
- **Self-assessment (C.84):** Considers herself very clever and capable but with many areas still needing improvement. Thinks she is "a bit" spoiled and a glutton.

### Selene Christand
- **Response to Krische's terror doctrine (C.83):** Expressed discomfort and fear at Krische's escalating ruthlessness but could not bring herself to condemn it outright. Acknowledged she was powerless to offer a better alternative. Told Krische she wanted her to be happy above all else. Called Krische a "fool" with deep affection.
- **Strategic assessment (C.83):** Correctly identified that foreign nations would likely exploit the kingdom's post-civil-war weakness. Recognized that Krische's involvement in future wars was inevitable. Wondered when she would be able to fulfill her promise of peaceful life.
- **Commander maturity (C.83):** Now views Bogan's death through the lens of a pure commander — identifying his insufficient escort as the tactical cause of defeat. Always travels with thirty cavalry escort.
- **Character profile alignment (C.84):** "Neutral / Good." Approximate height: 159 cm.

### Nozan Velraigh
- **Full name:** Nozan Nelre Wulferinea Velraigh (ノーザン＝ネルレ＝ウルフェリネア＝ヴェルライヒ). Revealed in C.84.
- **Banner:** A pair of elegant wings and a howling wolf — wings represent Christand (without depicting the hawk directly, out of reverence), wolf represents Nozan's role as Christand's guardian. Motto: "All who menace the hawk, the wolf pack shall devour."
- **Dragon's Maw final battle (C.82):** Commanded five thousand against Geltz's roughly two thousand holdouts plus three hundred sortie force. Offered Geltz a last chance to surrender before battle. Personally killed Gikal, Takilsu, and Alva in the melee. Fought at the forefront using Rollka-style shield-and-sword, described as moving "as though walking through an empty field." Full plate armor with steel greatshield; total weight exceeds normal human capacity but his movements remained as if unarmored.
- **Advice to Selene (C.83):** Counseled Selene to keep Krische's forces separate from the main Christand army — maintaining the "carrot and stick" division. Argued this was Krische's own wish and that undermining it would only trouble her. Pointed out that even after the war, Krische would be placed in the same role.
- **Combat description (C.82):** Surpasses even Bogan in swordsmanship. Shield used offensively (crushing skulls, launching enemies airborne) and defensively. His armor — silver with hawk-wing engravings — remained unscratched throughout the battle.
- **Character profile (C.84):** "Law / Neutral." Baron's youngest son. Bogan's earliest subordinate. Described as a "dangerous fanatic" type. Secretly curious about what the nickname "Skeleton" means. Mastered Rollka-style swordsmanship; unmatched with greatshield and longsword. Approximate height: tall and slim. Armor: full plate with silver-wing visor.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Nozan Velraigh's Heraldry
- Banner: A pair of elegant wings and a howling wolf on a field.
- The wings represent Christand without directly depicting the hawk (out of reverence); the wolf represents Nozan's role as guardian.
- Informal motto: "All who menace the hawk, the wolf pack shall devour."

### Smoke Warfare Tactics (C.81–82)
- Timber from Bernaich was hidden behind arrow screens at Mitskronia's base, placed in shallow trenches and soaked in oil. Green wood with high moisture content produced massive smoke when ignited. The prevailing south-to-north wind (blocked by the Dragon's Maw) carried smoke across the mountain. Purpose: not to cover an assault but to blind the defenders and trigger psychological collapse. Combined with the prior execution of Hilkintos and the reputation of the black century, the smoke alone caused mass hysteria and desertion.

### Sacred Convention — Surrender Rules (C.82)
- When accepting a surrender, if there is a risk of casualties from chain-of-command confusion, the receiving side may refuse. This prevents scenarios where a subordinate's surrender is accepted but a higher-ranking officer revokes it and launches a counterattack.
- When a general is still alive, the general's orders take priority; an adjutant's surrender can be legitimately revoked.
- A self-initiated surrender (not from the highest authority) can be refused if the opposing side considers hostilities ongoing.

### Rollka-Style Swordsmanship — Shield Combat (expanded)
- The Rollka style reaches its full potential when used with a shield. The left hand carries a steel greatshield used offensively (crushing skulls, launching opponents airborne, deflecting weapons) and defensively (creating an impassable wall). The right hand wields a longsword for precision strikes. Nozan Velraigh is described as the supreme practitioner — surpassing even Bogan in pure swordsmanship technique.

### Alberan Noble Name Structure (C.84)
- Detailed structure confirmed by author: (1) Given name, (2) Largest administered territory (abbreviated from full list), (3) Royal succession marker (Vel for male, Viera/Vera for female), (4) Honorary title (military valor; not heritable), (5) Surname. Military ranks and palace positions formally appear between 4 and 5; peerage titles follow the surname. In practice, the text uses simplified Japanese suffixes for readability.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Krische as deliberate "monster"** — Krische consciously adopts the role of feared figure to complement Selene's role as respected leader. This is a strategic choice, not an emotional one, and represents a new phase in her self-conception: she no longer cares about external evaluation except from her inner circle.

**The carrot and the stick** — Nozan articulates the principle that Krische and Selene must be kept separate: one feared, one loved. This mirrors the broader theme of Krische's disconnection from normal human relationships being weaponized for military utility.
