## TRANSLATION DECISIONS

- ゲイルビーグ = "Geilbiig" (Mekrea warrior; katakana ゲイルビーグ rendered with double-i for long vowel).
- バズラー＝ルーカザーン = "Bazlar Lukazan" (Grand Duke; ルーカザーン rendered without long vowel for readability).
- ビーナル＝サルシェンカ = "Biinal Sarshenka" (Duke; double-i preserves long vowel; サルシェンカ matches Aleha's existing family name).
- カルメダ = "Kalmeda" (historical Alberan general; minimal context, straightforward katakana rendering).
- クーリベ平原 = "Kuribe Plains" (katakana rendering).
- バゥムジェ＝イラ = "Baumje Ira" (kept as transliteration; the ancient-tongue meaning "expanding impact" is given in narration).
- 五大国戦争 = "Five-Nations War" (五大国 = five great nations; the name for the multi-front war).
- 投岩機 (narrator's emphatic variant) = rendered contextually as emphasis within "trebuchet" description ("a rock-thrower, not a stone-thrower") rather than as a separate term.
- アルベナリア工廠 = "Albernaria Arsenal" (工廠 = arsenal/factory; アルベナリア likely variant of アルベルナリア/Albernaria).
- ガーレン in C.201 = confirmed as the same Gahren (ガーレン) from C.177/C.193, not Gallen (ガレン). C.201 establishes him as Bogan and Nozan's former centurion during their early military careers. The rank百人隊長 (centurion) and the timeline (Bogan as corporal, Nozan as lance corporal) are consistent with a younger Gahren long before the current story events.
- コーズ = "Kouz" (squad leader; katakana コーズ).
- モーラズ = "Moraz" (squad leader; katakana モーラズ).

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

**Geilbiig** — Mekrea warrior chief encountered in C.201 flashback. Fights with a great spear; formidable mana-wielder who overpowered both young Bogan and Nozan in two-on-one combat. Killed by Gahren's arrow (shot to the temple). Speech: bold, taunting.
**Kouz** — Squad leader under Gahren (C.201 flashback). Large build. Loyal to Gahren; assisted Bogan after the ambush. Minor role.
**Moraz** — Squad leader under Gahren (C.201 flashback). Commanded rear-guard element during supply raid. Minor role.
**Bazlar Lukazan** — Grand Duke of the Elsren Holy Empire; supreme commander of the eastern invasion force (C.205). Head of House Lukazan, one of three grand-ducal houses with succession rights. Well-groomed, masculine beard; ornate gold-embroidered uniform. Manipulative, self-serving; deflected blame for the previous war's failures onto the Sarshenka family while claiming credit for himself. Assigned Biinal as sacrificial vanguard for the Wulfenite siege. Speech: lordly, condescending beneficence.
**Biinal Sarshenka** — Duke; Aleha Sarshenka's elder brother (C.205). Commands siege operations (trebuchets, siege towers) under Bazlar. Handsome, tea-gold hair showing early signs of age. House Sarshenka was financially ruined by Bazlar's political maneuvering after the previous war; Biinal took on massive debts to prepare siege equipment in hopes of restoring his family's standing. Internally furious at Bazlar but forced to comply. Speech: controlled deference masking resentment.
**Gahren** — (Existing entry update) C.201 flashback establishes him as Bogan and Nozan's former centurion in a special light infantry squad during the Elderant campaign, years before the main story. A commoner who rose from private; unable to use mana but a master archer and shrewd tactician. Defeated both Bogan (corporal) and Nozan (lance corporal) in a two-on-one sparring match using only a short sword and battlefield cunning. Earned their loyalty through a combination of harsh discipline and genuine care. The left-breast salute scene establishes the depth of Bogan and Nozan's devotion to him. Also appeared in the domestic flashback telling war stories to young Selene, frightening her to tears. Lazura scolded Bogan; Bery comforted Selene with cookies.
**Saldan Galkalon** — (Existing entry update) Second Corps Commander, Velraigh army (full name: サルダン＝ガルカロン). Bald, bespectacled, wiry, stern-faced. Despite his severe appearance, has a soft spot for children. Previously assisted Krische at Kirzalan. Now targeted by Krische's nickname instinct; offered the choice between "Baldy Four-Eyes" (ハゲメガネ) and "Smoothie" (つるりん). Outcome unresolved. Present at the Wulfenite war council (C.205).
**Kalmeda** — Former Alberan general at Wulfenite; killed by Aleha Sarshenka in the previous Elsren war. His death caused the Alberan army's total collapse and the fall of Wulfenite. Referenced historically only (C.205). Romanization tentative.
