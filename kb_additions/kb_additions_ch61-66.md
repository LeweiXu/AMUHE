## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **Post-Bernaich collapse (C.61):** Returned after a week-long guerrilla campaign in a state of near-total exhaustion — high fever, swollen shoulder, hands covered in burst blood blisters, sword chipped beyond recognition. Carried back by Mia wrapped in a blood-soaked blanket. Remained unconscious for two days.
- **First words upon waking (C.61):** Apologized to Selene, believing Selene hated her. Could not eat despite hunger; asked to sleep. Requested only that Selene keep liking her.
- **'Spoiled Krische' mode (C.62):** Created a self-rationalizing persona during recovery to justify indulgence — 'Spoiled Krische' (甘えクリシェ). Consolidated the "best qualities" of Working Krische, Lady Krische, Glutton Krische, and Clingy Krische into an "invincible" state that feels no shame. Result: childlike regression, constant kissing, clinging, and being fed.
- **Dog and owner game (C.62–63):** Invented a 'dog and owner' game after Bery taught her to say "woof." Became obsessed with making Kreschenta play the dog — including 'shake,' 'fetch,' and rewarding with cookies and head pats. Also made Kreschenta perform for Bery.
- **Candy return (C.62):** Used the candy Bery had given her before the Bernaich campaign. Fed it to Anne, then passed it mouth-to-mouth to Bery as a returned gift. Also shared one with Selene via kiss.
- **Promotion (C.64):** Appointed First Corps Commander after Selene's promotion to General. Accepted without opposition from other commanders.
- **Black century rewards (C.64):** Granted the black century permission for self-furnished weapons and armor, unit insignia, and two days' leave as a reward for their service during the Bernaich campaign.
- **Sword condition (C.65):** Curved sword was so heavily chipped that resharpening reduced it to a fraction of its original size — light as a feather. Counted 593 kills with this sword (784 total during the campaign). Mourned the damage in pumpkin-equivalent terms.
- **Swordsmanship lecture (C.64):** Gave an impromptu lecture to the First Battalion's light infantry, unaware that the soldiers' "enthusiasm" was largely directed at watching her move in a skirt. Selene intervened and sent the battalion on a punishment forced march.
- **Views on honor and death (C.65):** Expressed to Bery that she cannot understand choosing death for abstract values like honor or duty. Wished Christand had been a merchant family instead. What she wants is modest: safety, proximity to loved ones, freedom from risk.
- **Dream concept (C.65):** After Bery's explanation, adopted the concept of a "dream" — specifically, a shared dream with Bery of returning to peaceful daily life at the estate. Promised Bery they would achieve it together.
- **Flanking strategy (C.66):** Planned to lead a wide flanking sweep around Bernaich, cross the mountains, coordinate with Nozan Velraigh for simultaneous east-west attack to take the Dragon's Maw from behind while Selene holds the front.

### Bery Argan
- **During Krische's absence (C.61):** Spent nearly every day in a chair beside the empty bed. Maintained composure to avoid worrying Selene, but anxiety grew daily after learning Krische had a fever. Broke down crying with relief when Krische finally woke.
- **'Spoiled Krische' enabler (C.62):** Actively facilitated Krische's recovery by providing unlimited sweets, kisses, and physical affection. Admitted to Selene she had "given up" on correcting Krische's kissing behavior and was now recruiting Selene as a fellow sufferer.
- **Dog game participation (C.63):** Went along with Krische's dog-and-owner game, serving as secondary "owner" for Kreschenta. Found the situation genuinely funny, particularly Kreschenta's instant transformation when Gallen appeared.
- **Volunteer for Arna mission (C.66):** Volunteered to accompany Kreschenta to the Arna Empire as attendant. Argued that danger exists everywhere and that she wants to contribute. Krische assigned Korintz's squad (10 soldiers) as escort.
- **Dream speech (C.65):** Explained the concept of dreams to Krische — that a "what-if" about the future, unlike one about the past, can be made real through effort. Compared dreams to a moon lighting a dark night. Her own dream: returning to everyday life with Krische at the estate.

### Selene Christand
- **Full name (C.64):** Selene Argalitte Rinea Christand — inherited the Argalitte name via abbreviated investiture from Kreschenta. Now holds the title denoting authority over the entire northern region.
- **Promotion to General (C.64):** Replaced Bogan as General of Christand. Appointment was smooth — unanimous support from the three remaining corps commanders. Now the highest-ranking officer in the Christand army.
- **Reconciliation with Krische (C.61):** When Krische woke and apologized, Selene embraced her and swore upon the name Selene never to betray her trust again. Exchanged kisses.
- **Growing attachment (C.62):** During the recovery period, began kissing Krische freely and reflecting on whether she was "becoming like Bery." Recognized she might be filling her own loneliness.
- **Thoughts of escape (C.63):** Briefly entertained the fantasy of running away with Krische, Bery, and Kreschenta to live quietly in another country. Rejected it — as a noblewoman, as Bogan's daughter, she cannot betray those who pledged their lives to Christand.
- **Dancer Krische intervention (C.64):** Found Krische giving a swordsmanship lecture in a skirt to an audience of men more interested in watching her move than learning technique. Punished the First Battalion with a forced march drill until sundown.

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Dog game (C.62–63):** Became fully invested in the 'dog and owner' game despite initial reluctance. Displayed instant transformation between "dog mode" and "princess mode" when visitors appeared. Brain described as "liquefying with pleasure." Found emptying her head and being spoiled a revelation.
- **Ceasefire council (C.66):** Proposed volunteering as envoy to the Arna Empire to stall their potential entry into the war. Delivered a calculated emotional speech — tears, trembling voice, invocation of Bogan's death — that eliminated the ceasefire option from the council's consideration. Afterward, immediately reverted to being Krische's dog in private.
- **Diplomatic strategy (C.66):** Plans to delay negotiations with the Arna Empire until at least spring, and to announce the Empire's support (even if not secured) as a deterrent to the western and southern factions.

### Gallen
- **Visit to Krische (C.63):** Visited during recovery. Gently reminded Krische that others worry about her just as she worries about them. Told her to let herself be properly spoiled. Drained scalding hot tea in one gulp, astonishing the two cat-tongued girls.
- **Comforting Anne (C.66):** Received Anne (sent away from the main group) and lavished her with praise, calling her a fine servant. Anne was delighted.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Mythology/Religion
- **Arshe (アルセー):** Goddess of fortune. Invoked in blessings by merchants — "May the guidance of Arshe, goddess of fortune, be with you" (C.65).

### Merchant Guilds
- Guild membership (商会所属) is the aspiration of all merchants. Requires an established shop in a town and a reputation for trustworthiness. The guild vouches for members' identities and provides capital and credit for large transactions. Members wear a silver scales pendant with the guild name inscribed. Going from traveling peddler to guild merchant is considered a significant achievement (C.65).

### Heraldry and Insignia
- Certain designs are restricted: dragons (聖霊 / sacred spirits) and crowns may only be used by royalty or high-ranking nobles. Herald officials (紋章官) exist but are rare; typically someone with adequate heraldic knowledge serves in the role. Unit insignia require approval through a designated coordinator (C.64).

### Weapon Self-Furnishing (武器自弁)
- Normally a privilege reserved for battalion commanders. Being granted this right as an ordinary soldier is considered the highest honor. The black century received this privilege as a reward for their Bernaich campaign service (C.64).

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**'Spoiled Krische' / persona compartmentalization** — Krische creates a named internal persona to rationalize indulgence, revealing her unusual psychology: she manages emotion through logical frameworks rather than feeling. The humor masks the strangeness (C.62).
**Dog and owner** — The dog game serves multiple functions: comedy, Kreschenta's rare vulnerability, the contrast between her public princess persona and private craving for affection. Also echoed by Kokys calling himself Christand's "hound" (C.63).
**Dreams vs. what-ifs** — Bery introduces a key distinction: past what-ifs are regret; future what-ifs are dreams worth pursuing. The moon metaphor (lighting a dark night) becomes a shared image for Krische and Bery's hope (C.65).
**Dancer Krische** — Krische's obliviousness to her own physical appeal, played for comedy but also underscoring her fundamental disconnect from normal social awareness (C.64).
