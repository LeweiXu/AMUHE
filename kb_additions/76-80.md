## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **Daglis encounter (C.76):** Personally intercepted Roland's spy Daglis at night in Kirzalan. Used the black century to narrow escape routes, then ambushed him solo. Demonstrated ability to completely suppress a veteran assassin in an instant — pinning his arm, inserting blade between his teeth. Recruited Daglis by offering him control of Kirzalan's underworld in exchange for cooperation.
- **Roland's execution plan (C.76):** Proposed and received unanimous approval to eliminate Roland as a threat to logistics security. Justified the killing as necessary removal of an uncooperative element from a future logistics hub. Was in high spirits about the prospect.
- **Concern for Elvena (C.76):** Expressed desire to help Elvena specifically, prompted by finding her kind. Elrouga counseled caution about overextending help; Krische acknowledged the wisdom and resolved to think further.
- **Hilkintos strategy (C.77–79):** Designed the entire battle plan five days in advance. Key elements: (1) deliberately destroyed a bridge to create the appearance of being cornered; (2) concealed archers across the river under Gainz; (3) concealed Begil's cavalry and infantry in the northern forest; (4) tied grass in the path of the enemy charge to disrupt momentum; (5) used the black century to cross the river and strike the enemy right wing from behind, personally killing the right wing corps commander. Result: complete annihilation of the Hilkintos right wing via river encirclement, followed by rear attack from Begil to collapse the center.
- **Grass-tying sabotage (C.79):** Had grass tied together in the expected path of the Hilkintos center charge, causing mass tripping during the assault. This nullified the momentum of the heavy infantry charge against the long-spear line.
- **Woof-woof nickname (C.80):** Accidentally called Granmeld "Woof-woof" (わんわん) during the post-battle conversation, based on his dog-like grin. Attempted to formally request permission after the slip. Granmeld was unable to refuse. Mental process: wolf → dog → woof → wufwuf → grrr → woof-woof.
- **Reaction to Dagra's wounding (C.80):** Severed Aulgorn's right arm and crushed his left arm after Aulgorn stabbed Dagra. Showed completely cold, emotionless demeanor. Insisted on Sacred Convention compliance for Aulgorn's treatment despite her anger.
- **Healing magic (C.80):** First use of mana for internal medical treatment. Used mana to perceive internal wounds, physically manipulated torn intestinal tissue back into place, then inscribed a sustaining formula inside Dagra's body. Caused significant mana depletion and dizziness. Resolved to practice this technique further, reflecting that earlier complacency after Grace's death had cost her the chance to develop this skill sooner.
- **Philosophical shift (C.80):** Concluded that she should make enemies more afraid — executing resisters publicly as examples to deter future attacks on her people. Viewed this as a logical extension of what armies exist for.
- **Javelin throw (C.79):** Demonstrated devastating throwing power with a standard six-foot infantry spear — shattered a centurion's skull through his helm and killed several behind him. A second throw tore away half a centurion's body despite his attempt to deflect. Described as exceeding siege ballista power.

### Elrouga Faren
- **Hilkintos battle role (C.78–79):** Commanded the overall Christand forces in the decisive battle against Hilkintos. Conducted the pre-battle parley with Aulgorn. Exchanged threats with dark humor.
- **Granmeld incident (C.76):** Angry about Granmeld staying behind at Roland's estate with a provided woman, but refused to explain the details in front of Krische out of grandfatherly protectiveness.
- **Elvena discussion (C.76):** Counseled Krische about the limits of charity, but then reversed himself, saying it was improper to impose a jaded old man's cynicism on someone with her talent and future. Promised to assist whatever she decided.

### Granmeld Varkus
- **Hilkintos battle performance (C.78–79):** Led the left wing main assault ("Wolf Pack"). Broke through the Hilkintos right wing with devastating effect — personally caving in armored soldiers with his great war mace. Split the enemy right from center, enabling the complete encirclement and annihilation of the right wing.
- **Nickname: Woof-woof (C.80):** Accidentally named by Krische based on his dog-like grin. Unable to refuse. Following the pattern of "Vulture" (Dagra) and "Skeleton" (Elrouga).
- **Aulgorn's treatment (C.80):** The only person who could speak to Krische in her cold fury after Dagra was stabbed. Pragmatically argued for keeping Aulgorn alive. Performed rough tourniquet on Aulgorn's severed arm.

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Daglis (ダグリス)
- Roland's spy and former assassin. Age sixty. Mana-user. Twenty years in Roland's employ. Skills: blending into crowds, night movement, forest traversal, bypassing security. Operates with mechanical precision during assignments — no emotional responses. Recruited by Krische in Kirzalan (C.76) after she ambushed and completely overpowered him. Offered control of the Kirzalan underworld in exchange for cooperation. Delivered Roland's letter to Aulgorn as a double agent (C.77), maintaining his cover. Aulgorn offered to hire him; Daglis declined politely. His body and instincts told him Gildanstein would not win the war.

### Beruse (ベルーゼ)
- Aulgorn Hilkintos's adjutant. Bald-headed. Long-time subordinate. Serves as sounding board for strategic discussions. Present throughout the Hilkintos campaign (C.77–79).

### Aulgorn Hilkintos (expanded)
- **Full name:** Aulgorn Sakizren Nilkrinea Hilkintos (middle names revealed C.77).
- **Appearance:** Short-cropped tea-brown hair streaked with white; carefully shaved, sunken cheeks; strikingly large, reptilian eyes giving a neurotic impression. Tall and thin. Wears armor engraved with a goddess-faced visor.
- **Military philosophy:** Believes in building terrain and numerical superiority before engaging. Unhesitatingly burns cities to retreat if necessary. Lures enemies into favorable conditions. His strength is cold-blooded commitment to victory over sentiment.
- **Hilkintos campaign (C.77–79):** Received intelligence from Roland (via Daglis) about Christand force composition and objectives. Correctly deduced that Christand intended to ambush him at the western bridge, and force-marched to cross it first. Pursued the retreating Christand army for two days. Fought the decisive battle with 22,000 against Christand's estimated 10,000+ visible forces. Despite sound tactical judgment throughout, was outmaneuvered by Krische's multi-layered trap: hidden archers across the river, concealed infantry in the northern forest, grass-tying sabotage, and the black century's river-crossing assault.
- **Post-battle breakdown (C.80):** After being forced to surrender by Begil, was enraged by Krische's indifference. Drew his sword, killed one black century soldier, and stabbed Dagra while charging at Krische. Krische severed his right arm and crushed his left. To be executed at a later date. His attack was motivated by pride — he could not accept being treated as worthless by the girl who had defeated him.

### Begil (expanded)
- **Full name:** Begil Rinea Sandika (revealed C.80).
- **Hilkintos battle role (C.79):** Commanded 300 cavalry and 1,000 light infantry concealed in the northern forest. Emerged after Aulgorn withdrew his forest watchers, striking the Hilkintos center from behind. Accepted Aulgorn's surrender and formally invoked the Sacred Convention.

### Barga (expanded)
- **Hilkintos battle (C.78–80):** Volunteered for the most dangerous position — front line of the right wing, facing enemy arrows without archer support. Fourth Battalion sustained over 60% casualties and was effectively annihilated. Barga himself received light wounds. Lost seven centurions.

### Keith (expanded)
- **Family name:** Kiltins (キルティンス), revealed C.77.
- **De facto adjutant role (C.77):** Serves as relay commander for the First Corps, handling routine orders and inter-battalion communication. This arrangement, proposed by Elrouga, establishes Keith's command authority and prepares him for battlefield command.
- **Hilkintos battle (C.78):** Commanded the right wing defense. Tasked with minimizing casualties while holding the line.

### Gainz (expanded)
- **Hilkintos battle (C.78–79):** Led the Fifth Battalion as the concealed archer force across the river from the battlefield. His men felled trees deep in the forest to create a firing field, then delivered devastating flanking volleys into the Hilkintos right wing. Later transitioned to direct fire from the riverbank to support the black century's river-crossing assault.

### Bilza (ビルザ)
- Seventeenth Squad leader, black century. Slim young man from a herbalist family. Not a doctor but performed quasi-medical duties in his village. His squad is experienced in emergency treatment. Assigned to Dagra's care after the battle (C.80).

### Dagra (expanded)
- **Hilkintos battle wounding (C.80):** Attempted to restrain the surrendered Aulgorn, who drew his sword, killed one black century soldier, and stabbed Dagra through the flank while charging at Krische. Wound was deep — field surgeon said survival depended on getting through the night. Krische secretly used healing magic to seal the internal intestinal tear and inscribed a sustaining formula. Mia assumed temporary command of the black century in his absence.

### Kalua (expanded)
- **Hilkintos battle (C.79):** Led the left-hand assault during the black century's attack on the archers, demonstrating beast-like low-stance combat learned from Krische. Described as the one who best understood Krische's combat philosophy through natural talent.
- **Post-battle (C.80):** Noticed mana fluctuation from Krische's healing magic but accepted Krische's denial. Comforted Krische about Dagra's condition, stroking her head and putting an arm around her shoulders.

### Mia (expanded)
- **Temporary command (C.80):** Assumed acting command of the black century after Dagra's wounding. Appointment supported by Korintz and Tagel. Krische noted she was "a bit dim" but adequate.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Underworld Organizations
- **Night's Claw (夜の爪)** and **Dark Wing (闇夜の羽):** Assassin/spy guilds mentioned by Krische when interrogating Daglis (C.76). No further details given.

### Medical Knowledge (In-World)
- In-world medical theory operates on the concept of 邪気 (jaki, "harmful agents/malign influences") rather than germ theory. Wounds must be cleaned with alcohol or salt and kept sterile; lacerations must be sealed. Otherwise, festering and inflammation occur, allowing jaki to enter the body. A healthy body can resist jaki, but a wounded body cannot (C.80).

### Krische's Healing Magic
- First demonstrated in C.80. Uses mana to perceive internal wounds, physically manipulates damaged tissue back into position, then inscribes a sustaining formula (same principle as magic crystal circuits) inside the patient's body to maintain the sealed state. Costs significant mana — more than operating imaginary muscles, since the mana is being used externally. Classified as secret (Krische denied it when Kalua noticed the mana fluctuation).

### Shield Wall Dynamics
- Soldiers carry shields on the left arm, protecting themselves on the left half and the comrade to their left with the right half. This makes formations inherently vulnerable to attacks from the right. Fear of death causes soldiers to drift rightward toward the shield of the man beside them, requiring veteran soldiers to be placed on the exposed right side of each centurion's formation to hold the line. A flanking volley from the right disproportionately kills these veterans, causing cascading formation collapse (C.78).

### Grass-Tying Battlefield Sabotage
- Krische's forces tied grass blades together along the expected path of the enemy charge (C.79). This caused mass tripping among charging heavy infantry, nullifying their momentum before they reached the long-spear line. The sabotage was too subtle to be detected from the enemy command position; Aulgorn attributed the disruption to fatigue.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Nickname bestowing** — Krische's compulsion to give "affectionate nicknames" continues with "Woof-woof" for Granmeld, following "Vulture" (Dagra) and "Skeleton" (Elrouga). The pattern: Krische sees a physical resemblance to an animal/thing, feels an irresistible urge, and the recipient cannot refuse. Dagra and Elrouga now share knowing looks when they see it happening.

**Hunting metaphor** — The entire Hilkintos campaign is framed as a hunt. Krische explicitly uses Gainz's hunting knowledge (bait → bite → kill) as the tactical framework. The "hunting ground without hope" (C.79 title) inverts the predator-prey relationship.

**Kalua-Elvena resemblance** — Krische notices that Kalua's almond eyes and slender features are strikingly similar to Elvena's, adding to the web of unidentified connections surrounding Elvena/Bery/Lazura.

**Medical reflection** — Krische connects her failure to develop healing skills to her earlier habit of discarding regret as "a shame / it can't be helped." Recognizes that cutting off the emotion of loss also cuts off the motivation to learn from it. Marks continued emotional growth.
