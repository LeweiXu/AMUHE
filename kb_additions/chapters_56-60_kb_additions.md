## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **Bernaich guerrilla campaign (C.57–60):** After the failed decisive battle, Krische refused to withdraw and led the black century in a week-long guerrilla harassment campaign on Bernaich. Personally led every assault, sleeping minimally (only light naps), eating only hardtack and stolen provisions. Burned the ridgeline fortress twice, killed a visiting corps commander and adjutant, massacred engineers, and annihilated seven enemy hundred-man squads across multiple sweeps. Developed a high fever and physical tremors by the end.
- **Nickname among enemy:** "Head-Hunter Krische" (首狩人のクリシェ) — used by Gildanstein's soldiers to describe her during the Bernaich campaign.
- **Encounter with Gildanstein (C.57):** After the decisive battle, scaled the cliff and launched a solo ambush on Gildanstein's ~20-man elite guard. Killed several including Walter Zargan in seconds. Pinned Gildanstein to the ground and nearly killed him, but was interrupted by Salva. Gildanstein subsequently offered a deal — his sword (Bogan's) in exchange for Krische withdrawing. Krische, tormented by anxiety over Bery, Selene, and Kreschenta's safety, accepted and withdrew. This marks the first time Krische explicitly chose emotional security over the tactically optimal action.
- **Bernaich withdrawal battle (C.58):** Took command of First Corps from Selene. Ordered the Third Battalion used as deliberate bait in a mobile defense, threatened to kill Barga for questioning orders, and personally led the Fourth Battalion's counterattack. Result: Christand losses 1,500 vs. Gildanstein losses ~4,000 including deserters. No one praised her afterward; soldiers feared her.
- **Reaction to Bogan's death (C.59):** Attempted to comfort Selene but was struck on the cheek (by Selene's gauntlet) and told to leave. Deeply shaken; interpreted this as potential rejection. Refused to rest, instead departing immediately for the guerrilla campaign, driven partly by desire to prove useful to Selene.
- **Forced rest (C.60):** Dagra, with concurrence from Korintz and Tagel, invoked the military regulation for temporary assumption of command, stripping Krische of authority and ordering her to rest. Kalua physically caught her when she collapsed, and Mia held her while she slept. Krische fell asleep almost instantly upon accepting the order.
- **Bery's candy (C.59–60):** Carried a small pouch with a single candy on a cord around her neck throughout the campaign — a keepsake from Bery with a promise to save the last one for Bery to eat.

### Bery Argan
- **Farewell scene (C.59):** Gave Krische a pouch of candies before departure, invoking the same promise as before a previous battle: "save the last one for me to eat." Kissed Krische's forehead. Asked Mia and Kalua to look after Krische, bowing deeply.
- **Consoling Selene (C.59):** Comforted Selene after Bogan's death and the incident with Krische. Told Selene she would not scold her but would not forgive her either — Selene must apologize properly. Drew parallels between Selene and Bogan (both retreated to grieve alone with the same words). Reminded Selene that Krische is "very delicate and very timid, honest like a child, and easily hurt."

### Selene Christand
- **Bogan's death (C.58):** Ran to Bogan at Mitskronia after Krische took over First Corps command. Bogan regained consciousness briefly — stroked Selene's cheek, patted her head, mouthed "I leave the rest to you" (任せた), and died. Selene broke down completely, clinging to his body.
- **Incident with Krische (C.59):** When Krische entered her tent to comfort her, Selene — overwhelmed by grief and unable to accept comfort — swung her arm and cut Krische's cheek with her gauntlet. Immediately regretted it, but Krische fled before she could apologize. Described her own action as "the worst."

### Bogan Argalitte Vezrinea Christand
- **Death (C.56, C.58):** Fought a warrior's duel against Gildanstein after his escort was killed. Displayed both the Rollka-style military swordsmanship and elements adapted from Krische's sword. Lost his right arm to Gildanstein but threw Gildanstein to the ground even as his arm was severed. Enemy reinforcements forced Gildanstein to withdraw without taking Bogan's head. Bogan was evacuated to Mitskronia, where he briefly regained consciousness to see Selene one last time — patted her head, mouthed "I leave the rest to you," and died.
- **Swordsmanship legacy (C.56):** In his final duel, incorporated elements of Krische's sword — specifically the ground-level strike and fluid, stance-free movement. Acknowledged it as crude imitation but effective as a surprise element.
- **Epithet origin (C.56):** "Lightning Quick" (迅雷) was not merely a reference to his generalship but to his ferocious combat presence at the front lines — a berserker state that inspired those around him to madness and courage. Described as "a storm in human form."

### Gildanstein Kalnaros Vel Sarcarinea Alberan
- **Duel with Bogan (C.56):** Offered Bogan a one-on-one duel as a mark of respect, using the "king's sword" (正眼 / middle guard) — a two-handed greatsword style embodying noble tradition. Despite sustaining damage from Bogan's berserker assault, ultimately severed Bogan's right arm. Refused to take Bogan's head, taking only the arm and sword. Declared the fight a draw when enemy reinforcements arrived.
- **Encounter with Krische (C.57):** Correctly assessed Krische as an opponent he could not defeat even with his elite guard. Negotiated a withdrawal by threatening harm to Kreschenta and exploiting Krische's emotional attachments. Showed both genuine respect for Bogan and calculated manipulation of Krische.
- **Assessment of Krische (C.57):** Recognized her as not a thrill-killer but someone who kills with the detachment of "swatting an annoying insect." Identified her as a twisted child who does not stand on the battlefield by choice. Declared she is "no warrior."

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Reaction to battle outcome (C.58):** Present in the main camp tent when Krische arrived blood-soaked. Krische told her "whatever kind of child Kreschenta is, you are Krische's little sister," kissed her forehead, and asked her to watch over Bery.

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Nakilus Ferizar (ナキルス＝フェリザー)
- One of Gildanstein's elite warriors present at the ambush of Bogan. Wields twin great-axes with extraordinary strength. Mentioned but not given individual scene.

### Walter Zargan (ウォルター＝ザーガン)
- Successor to the Zain school of swordsmanship. One of Gildanstein's elite guards. A veteran of great skill and experience — described as capable of easily cutting down any ordinary opponent in a single counter-stroke. Killed by Krische in seconds during her solo ambush on Gildanstein's guard (C.57). Not to be confused with Walter Del Grizlandy (the Empire general's aide from earlier chapters).

### Bargra (バーグラ)
- Young soldier in Gildanstein's army. Hastily mobilized, promoted from common soldier to corporal to captain through battlefield attrition. Assigned to the third Bernaich mountain sweep. Witnessed Krische's attack firsthand — saw Arond and multiple comrades beheaded in seconds. Lost consciousness from terror; permanently traumatized and unable to serve as a soldier afterward (C.60).

### Arond (アロンド)
- Centurion in Gildanstein's army. Over thirty years of military service. Deeply wrinkled old man who did not look the part but was a battle-hardened veteran. Killed by Krische during the third Bernaich sweep — beheaded so quickly that his subordinate standing next to him did not understand what had happened (C.60).

### Kalua (カルア)
- **Blood-lust in combat (C.57):** Displayed dark joy in killing during the withdrawal battle — moist eyes and a twisted grin. Had killed more enemies than anyone in the black century aside from Krische. Completely unrestrained.
- **Care for Krische (C.60):** Initiated the chain of events leading to Krische being forced to rest. Caught Krische when she collapsed, spoke to her directly and frankly (calling her "bunny" and scolding her stubbornness), and prompted Dagra to invoke the command-assumption regulation. Received a punch from Dagra for disrespectful language to a superior.
- **Promise to Bery (C.59):** Along with Mia, received Bery's request to watch over Krische. Gave a ladylike, formal pledge to bring Krische home safely.

### Zarka (ザーカ)
- Black century soldier. Wounded during the Bernaich guerrilla campaign (C.60). Minimal detail.

### Barga (バーガ)
- **Conflict with Krische (C.58):** Objected to Krische's order to use the Third Battalion as bait, calling it too cruel to those who had volunteered for the rearguard. Krische threatened to kill him on the spot for insubordination. Barga submitted, recognizing Krische as fundamentally different from Selene — she viewed soldiers purely as numbers with no emotional attachment.

### Geltz Willing (ゲルツ＝ヴィリング)
- **Post-battle decisions (C.58, C.60):** Correctly ordered pursuit halted when Christand's withdrawal counterattack devastated his forces. Later organized three increasingly large mountain sweeps of Bernaich to deal with Krische's guerrilla campaign, culminating in a 2-battalion (2,000-man) sweep with four-squad mutual-support groups. Pulled troops off Bernaich for a day to give the black century a window to escape, judging that eliminating them was less important than stabilizing the Dragon's Maw.

### Keys (キース)
- **Wounded (C.58):** Third Battalion commander. Suffered a broken arm and critical blood loss during the mobile defense where his battalion was used as bait. His adjutant was killed. The Third Battalion lost half its number dead.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Swordsmanship Styles
- **Rollka style (ロールカ式):** Traditional military swordsmanship passed down in the Alberan army. Uses a left-side forward stance with the left hand extended (originally holding a small shield) as a lure — to invite, deflect, or counter the opponent's sword, or to grapple. Specialized in breaking the stance of armored opponents and delivering killing blows. Requires tall stature and long reach. Bogan's primary style.
- **King's Sword / Middle Guard (正眼 / 王者の剣):** Two-handed greatsword style embodying noble tradition. Sword held in center position, facing the opponent squarely. The right hand represents heaven (versatility), the left represents earth (stability). Founded on the principle of encompassing all angles within the blade's arc. Considered the foundational noble sword style. Gildanstein's primary style.
- **Zain style (ザイン式):** A distinct sword school; Walter Zargan was its inheritor. No further details given.
- **Krische's influence on others:** Bogan incorporated elements of Krische's sword (ground-level strikes, fluid stance-free movement) into his own fighting. Selene learned from Krische a low-stance style suited to her smaller frame — keeping posture low, striking upward, using the blade to cut in and break the opponent's balance. Both acknowledged their versions as crude compared to the original.

### Military Regulations
- **Temporary assumption of command (指揮代行):** A regulation allowing a commanding officer's direct subordinates to forcibly strip them of authority when judged unfit for command (e.g., madness, incapacitation). Requires unanimous consent of all direct subordinate unit leaders. Exercised by the next-ranking officer (adjutant-equivalent). Carries severe penalties if misused but is a legitimate right to protect subordinates from abusive or incapacitated commanders. Used by Dagra (with Korintz and Tagel's consent) to relieve Krische during the Bernaich campaign (C.60).

### Mobile Defense (機動防御)
- A defensive tactic that deliberately opens holes in the defensive line to lure pursuing enemies into a killing zone, then counterattacks from the flank or rear. High-risk (miscalculation leads to complete breakthrough and collapse) but high-reward. Used by Krische during the Bernaich withdrawal (C.58): Third Battalion served as deliberate bait on the right flank while the Fourth Battalion struck from the center-right, driving enemies off the cliff. Result: ~3x enemy casualties vs. friendly losses.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Krische's imperfection** — Krische explicitly fails to make the optimal choice for the first time, choosing emotional security (checking on Bery) over killing Gildanstein. "Before she had realized it, Krische was no longer a perfect being." Her growing attachments create vulnerability.

**Warrior's duel** — The Bogan-Gildanstein duel as a mutual acknowledgment of worth between enemies. Gildanstein grants Bogan the honor of a one-on-one fight and refuses to take his head. The concept of "warrior" as a title Gildanstein explicitly denies Krische.

**Fear of the cursed child** — After the withdrawal battle, no one praises Krische; soldiers watch from a distance in fear. On Bernaich, enemy soldiers are driven mad by terror. The contrast between her effectiveness and her isolation deepens.

**Bery's candy** — A tangible symbol of Krische's connection to home, carried physically around her neck through the entire guerrilla campaign. The promise to "save the last one" as an implicit promise to return alive.

**Small rebellion** — Dagra, Kalua, Mia, Korintz, and Tagel collectively override Krische's authority to force her to rest — an act of care disguised as military regulation. The first time subordinates have pushed back against Krische out of concern rather than self-interest.
