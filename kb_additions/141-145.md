## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Christand
- **Post-conference actions (C.141):** Encountered Galshan Vice Chairman Olgan in the courtyard; wiped her hand immediately after a handshake (major diplomatic faux pas she was oblivious to). Named Kokys "Nyan-nyan" after seeing his tiger-shaped helm.
- **Bery's stabbing response (C.143):** Froze Bery in a time-stasis sphere (空間切断 + 停止術式) using an enormous mana expenditure — literally stopping time within a sealed space. Spent several days in the royal archives, reading thousands of volumes at inhuman speed. Identified two potential remedies; chose to pursue the dragon's heart first. Left the capital at night.
- **Kreschenta's Great Purge (C.143):** In a single night, dismembered and killed 17 nobles who had refused allegiance to Kreschenta. Targets included Fahre Arcasakos and Earl Narkes. Applied the quartering penalty for treason uniformly. Historical records attribute the purge to Kreschenta; private memoirs hint at Krische.
- **Dragon quest (C.144):** Traveled to Albyagel mountains seeking Yagelnaus's heart as a remedy for Bery. Acquired a jade tiger companion (Gururun) en route. Encountered and coerced Lila Sharana into guiding her.
- **New nickname given:** "Nyan-nyan" (にゃんにゃん) for Kokys.

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Conference performance (C.141):** Skillfully deflected Olgan's territorial demands and Krolus's accusations. Assessed all three rival nations (Galshan, Elderant, Elsren) as likely to attack; gave up on war avoidance early.
- **Response to Bery's stabbing (C.143):** Personally administered emergency antidote for night's drop poison. Interrogated the assassin woman in the castle dungeons — crushed all fingers and toes with a hammer, cut off fingers with shears. Discussed political implications with Daglis; recognized Krische would go on a rampage.
- **Political assessment:** Predicted Krische's purge was inevitable once Bery was harmed. Concerned about public perception and the need for a scapegoat.

### Bery Argan
- **Stabbing (C.142):** Discovered poison in Kreschenta's tea set using the poison-Piririn. Attempted to persuade the assassin woman to surrender peacefully. Stabbed in the abdomen with a knife coated in night's drop poison. Managed to knock the woman unconscious before collapsing.
- **Status (C.143):** Frozen in Krische's time-stasis sphere; effectively in suspended animation. Condition critical — survival uncertain without extraordinary intervention.

### Selene Christand

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Kreisharana (クレィシャラナ)
- Mountain-dwelling warrior tribe in Albyagel. Descendants of northern tribes defeated by Alberan ~500 years ago.
- Extremely strict warrior society; men train in combat from childhood. Even farmers become formidable warriors when armed.
- Bond with griffins: breed and ride them as aerial cavalry (~100 griffin cavalry, total military ~1,500). Juvenile griffins assigned to handlers for rearing.
- Faith: worship the dragon Yagelnaus as a holy spirit. Offerings made periodically; approach otherwise forbidden.
- Relations: non-aggression pact with Alberan via Sacred Convention. Religious ties with the Arna Empire. Hostile to kingdom citizens due to historical grievances.
- Currently in gradual decline due to small population and mountain isolation.

### Griffins (獅子鷲)
- Eagle head, lion body, great wings. Shoulder height ~5 shaku (~150 cm) at maturity. Omnivorous, prefer meat. Smarter than horses. Ridden with saddles. Juveniles can carry one rider by ~1.5 years.
- Used exclusively by Kreisharana as aerial cavalry.

### Magical Beasts — New Varieties
- **Indigo deer (藍鹿, ranka):** House-sized deer magical beast. Running alone uproots trees. Can be more dangerous than jade tigers due to sheer mass.
- **Storm-wing (嵐翼, ranyoku):** Mentioned only; details unknown. One of the dangerous magical beasts inhabiting Albyagel.

### Sacred Convention — Origin (C.144/145)
- Originated from the peace accord between Alberan and Kreisharana, mediated by the dragon Yagelnaus (~500 years ago).
- Founding king Bazarishe then convened a multi-nation council with the dragon to establish it as international law.
- Spread continent-wide by Grabareine's conquests.
- No confirmed instance of Yagelnaus enforcing the Convention by destroying a nation.

### Dragons — Expanded Lore (C.144/145)
- Also called ancient dragons or holy spirits. Fortress-sized bodies with mineral-like scales. Mana vastly exceeds magical beasts.
- Dragon War: ancient territorial disputes among dragons destroyed many civilizations. Lusheran Empire famously destroyed after provoking a dragon.
- Dragon blood/flesh: believed to grant power; treated as a spiritual elixir. This is what Krische seeks as a remedy for Bery.
- No confirmed dragon sighting in centuries; existence sometimes doubted.

### Mana-Users — Expanded Physiology (C.145, author's note)
- Mana-altered bodies contain spiritual-substance internal organs that convert food and waste into mana → no excretion needed.
- Aging slows: growth stops in young adulthood, gradual aging begins past 30. Some unchanged past 50.
- Disease/poison/alcohol resistant.
- Higher infertility rates, especially when both parents are mana-users.
- Nobles (mostly mana-users) often feel physiological revulsion toward non-users who age faster and excrete normally. Mixed marriages disfavored.
- Sudden mana-user births in non-mana families occur; possibly linked to ambient mana density.

### Galshan Republic — Expanded (C.141/145)
- Former Alberan vassal; achieved independence during the Elsren split.
- Republic established ~20 years ago after Olgan's revolution.
- Southern territory: mostly arid, but river zones enriched by periodic highland rainfall yield unrivaled wheat.
- Name means "strong rain"; possesses rain-worship religion.
- Major trading power; multi-ethnic. Internally stable post-republic.
- Military specialty: beast soldiers — domesticated war animals of various species.
- Currently planning joint attack on Alberan with the Empire.

### Elderant Kingdom — Expanded (C.141/145)
- Tribal confederation; territory mostly mountains and forests. Former Alberan subjects.
- Current king from eastern Elderant; anti-Alberan.
- Frequent civil wars during succession; Alberan exploits these to seize territory.
- Military specialty: light archers with exceptional forest marksmanship.

### Arna Empire — Military Detail (C.145)
- Crossbow-focused military; citizens of all ages trained in crossbow use.
- Quality repeating ballistae and catapults from skilled engineers.
- High magic crystal processing technology; major export.

### Night's Drop (一夜の雫)
- Lethal poison. Fairly well-known; widely available, making supply chain tracing difficult. Kreschenta carried an antidote. Even with treatment, survival is uncertain.

### Krische's Time-Stasis Magic (C.143)
- Enormous mana expenditure. Creates a sealed sphere that "cuts out" a section of space and stops all processes within it — including breathing, dust movement, and presumably biological decay. Appears as a translucent blue-gray sphere. Hard and smooth to the touch from outside. Requires nearly all of Krische's mana reserves. Used to preserve Bery indefinitely while seeking a cure.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Dragonslaying** — Krische's willingness to kill a dragon (a god-figure) for Bery's sake represents the ultimate expression of her "pierce all obstacles" nature. The narration frames her as "thrusting a blade into heaven."

**Kreschenta's Great Purge** — The first major instance of Krische's violence being attributed to Kreschenta in historical record, establishing the pattern of Kreschenta bearing public responsibility for Krische's actions.

**Nyan-nyan naming** — Krische's nickname affliction continues to escalate with Kokys as the latest victim; Gallen enables it.
