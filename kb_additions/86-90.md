## CORE CHARACTERS

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Arna Empire journey (C.86–90):** Traveled from Fort Belgash to the Arna Empire with Bery as attendant and Kirik's squads (Nineteen and Twenty) plus twenty cavalrymen under Captain Ehran as escort. Journey: ~7 days to the border, ~4 more to Naut Arna.
- **Dog-mode continuation (C.86–90):** The dog game with Bery has become a daily routine during the journey, occupying roughly half of every day. Kreschenta rationalizes it through elaborate internal justifications: e.g., "I am already being forced to play dog, so resuming costs no additional humiliation." Bery uses cookies (especially Krische's honey cookies) as both bait and reward.
- **Cooking apprenticeship (C.86):** Actively studying under Bery during camp cooking. Shows prodigious mimicry ability identical to Krische's but acknowledges Bery's skill as an insurmountable fortress. Motivating strategy: "Operation Make Onee-sama Cling Entirely to Me" requires surpassing Bery's cooking.
- **Post-war governance plans (C.87):** Outlined ambitious reform agenda: purge corrupt nobles and confiscate assets, reform kingdom law, establish elementary education for commoners (modeled on Republic of Nerka), reduce noble power, recruit talented commoners, practice royal austerity as a populist strategy. Views the kingdom as already hers.
- **Diplomatic mission (C.88, C.90):** Successfully manipulated Grand Priest Zarnalibea into granting audience via a wager on the outcome of the Battle of the Central Plain. At the audience with the Priestess Princess, deliberately allowed the misunderstanding that she sought military aid, then revealed her actual goal was post-war alliance coordination. Extracted a commitment of aid against foreign aggression before clarifying. Proposed pre-positioning Imperial troops under the pretext of defending a weakened kingdom, which in substance constitutes participation in the civil war under a different name.
- **Assessment by others (C.88, C.90):** Zarnalibea initially uncertain, gradually impressed by her intelligence and the genuine warmth of her relationship with Bery. The Priestess Princess recognized her as a "cursed child of Alberan" — abnormal, dangerous, possessing an aura incompatible with her age.
- **Crescent moon motif (C.89):** Dislikes her name (Kreschenta = crescent moon) as an ill omen, seeing it as a disposable name given to a cursed child. Bery reframes it as a symbol of aspiration and future radiance. Wears a crescent-moon hair ornament chosen by Bery for the audience.
- **Internal hierarchy (C.86):** Places everyone below herself except Krische. Finds Bery's complete lack of deference unforgivable. Deeply competitive with Bery for attention and influence.

### Bery Argan
- **Arna Empire journey (C.86–90):** Served as Kreschenta's sole attendant throughout the journey. Managed all meals (camp cooking including court-level dishes), comfort arrangements, and emotional care. Maintained Kreschenta's routine and physical comfort to such a degree that Kreschenta's intellect self-reportedly "deteriorated to a dismal degree."
- **Dog-training mastery (C.86–90):** Has fully developed the cookie-based system for managing Kreschenta's mood. Uses Krische's honey cookies as the ultimate tool. Deliberately withholds comfort (warmth, food, head-patting) to trigger Kreschenta's voluntary dog-mode. Finds the dynamic genuinely enjoyable.
- **Culinary observation (C.86):** Described as capable of processing seven tasks simultaneously. Her onion-cutting technique adapts angle minutely to each onion's individual characteristics. Explained meat resting and tenderizing techniques to Kreschenta.
- **Diplomatic support (C.88):** Anticipated Zarnalibea's visit and pre-arranged the tent for a meeting. Served Aseram milk tea. Her composure and the quality of her service impressed Zarnalibea, who used the Bery-Kreschenta relationship as a lens to evaluate the princess.
- **Crescent moon speech (C.89):** Told Kreschenta that no human is without flaws; what exists are only those who strive to become so. Said Kreschenta's flaws and her striving to fill them are "splendid, dear, and beautiful." Advised against rushing. Offered herself as a resting place "like a snack between meals."
- **Cooking philosophy addition (C.86):** Prefers cookies with nuts and dried fruit; contrasted with Krische's simple, sweet-dominant style.
- **Cheek-deflating habit (C.86–90):** Has developed a consistent habit of squishing Kreschenta's puffed cheeks to produce a "pfhew" / "puhyuu" sound. Described as a conditioned reflex.

### Selene Christand
- **Brief appearance (C.89):** Shown in a morning scene with Krische at the front. Confirmed that Kreschenta has been granted an audience. Teased Krische for playing the older sister. Dynamic remains warm and domestic.

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Zarnalibea
- **Title:** Grand Priest (大神官), third-ranking in the Arna Empire. One of four Grand Priests.
- **Appearance:** Elderly man with wrinkled face and gentle smile; sharp, observant eyes.
- **Role:** Holds strong connections to the Alberan Kingdom; knows more about the kingdom's internal affairs than most of its own people. Aware of the "cursed child" rumors about Kreschenta. Sent by the Priestess Princess to evaluate Kreschenta and potentially prevent her entry to the capital.
- **Political stance (C.88):** Claims neutrality; actually inclined toward Kreschenta's faction as a moderate who opposes precipitous action. Recognizes that the Empire is internally divided between prince-faction and princess-faction supporters.
- **Assessment of Kreschenta (C.88):** Initially uncertain whether she matched the historical profile of a cursed child. Observed her relationship with Bery and concluded she was not the cold tyrant of the chronicles. Accepted her wager proposal and was effectively maneuvered into granting the audience.

### Priestess Princess (巫女姫)
- **Title:** Supreme ruler of the Arna Empire; nameless after assuming the role.
- **Appearance:** Long black hair, beauty mark, sharp narrow eyes, tall and slender. Wears pale-blue and gold robes. Sits on a cushion without a chair, per tradition.
- **Background:** Ascended at age fifteen; reign of forty years. Commands authority that physically affects those in her presence.
- **Political stance (C.90):** Wishes to avoid entanglement in the Alberan civil war. Privately sympathizes with Kreschenta but is bound by political calculation. Recognized Kreschenta as a "cursed child of Alberan" after being outmaneuvered in the audience. Agreed to consider pre-positioning troops.

### Ehran (エーラン)
- **Role:** Cavalry captain commanding the twenty-rider escort for Kreschenta's journey.
- **Incident (C.89):** Accidentally saw Kreschenta sleeping in Bery's arms through a jostled carriage window. Was moved to tears by her vulnerability rather than scandalized. Deeply loyal to the royal house.

### Harbis (ハルビス)
- **Title:** Grand Priest (大神官), Arna Empire.
- **Stance:** Hawkish; objected to Kreschenta's maneuvering during the audience. Rebuked by the Priestess Princess.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Arna Empire — Political Structure
- Matrilineal theocracy governed by the Priestess Princess (巫女姫), who serves as priestess of the Sacred Spirits.
- Chief deity: the ancient dragon Yagelnaus (ヤゲルナウス).
- Priests surrender their family names upon ordination. Hierarchy: Priestess Princess → Archbishop (大司教, chief minister) → Grand Priests (大神官, four total) → priests/priestesses.
- No civil strife for several centuries, owing to the authority guaranteed by faith in the actually-existing ancient dragons.
- Internally divided over the Alberan civil war: prince-faction supporters vs. princess-faction supporters. This division threatens to split the Empire itself.
- Blue is a sacred color in the Arna Empire; avoided by visiting dignitaries.
- Nature-worshipping tendency: chairs are not commonly used by high-ranking priests; they sit on the floor with cushions.

### Arna Empire — Geography and Defense
- Flanked by mountains east and west; northern coastline faces the sea. Territory roughly equivalent to the Alberan Kingdom's northern region.
- Prosperous maritime trade with a northern island nation.
- Border with Alberan: customs posts on each side separated by ~3 ri, with an interstitial market zone. Smuggling and tax evasion are widespread but tacitly tolerated.
- Capital Naut Arna: cliff-carved fortress city; palace originally a magic crystal mine; high ambient mana density inside. Chamber of the Dragon Priestess (竜巫女の間) serves as the audience hall, featuring a waterfall, magic crystal altar in dragon form, and phosphorescent walls.

### Arna Empire — Religion
- Sacred Spirit faith (聖霊信仰): worship of ancient dragons, principally Yagelnaus.
- The Priestess Princess loses her name upon assuming the role and is considered the being closest to the divine.
- Contrast with Holy Elsren Empire: the Elsren faith involves "Reine's Paradise" (レイネの天国), a heavenly realm of perfect beings (angels) created by a creator god. Religious paintings of this paradise are noted for their beauty.

### Tea Varieties (expanded)
- **Aseram (アセラム):** Tea leaf with subtle sweetness and body; particularly suited to milk tea. Used by Bery when serving guests in cold weather.

### Cursed Child — Historical Context (expanded)
- The Arna Empire's archives contain more records about Alberan's cursed children than the kingdom's own, since the kingdom banned such records as a royal shame.
- Historical example: Queen Grabareine (グラバレイネ) and Princess Elsreine (エルスレイネ), two cursed children whose conflict caused the split of the original Alberan into the current kingdom and the Holy Elsren Empire. Grabareine's reign was characterized by severe terror politics.

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

**Dog-mode as psychological relief** — Kreschenta's elaborate internal rationalizations for playing dog reveal the depth of her need for comfort and affection. The contrast between her ruthless diplomatic performance and immediate reversion to dog-mode is a recurring structural device in C.86–90.

**Bery as kingmaker** — Bery's influence extends beyond Krische; she now shapes Kreschenta's emotional development and serves as the visible marker of Kreschenta's character that outside observers (Zarnalibea, the Priestess Princess) use to evaluate the princess.

**Crescent moon** — Kreschenta's name meaning (crescent moon) explored as a symbol of incompleteness and disposability (from the royal perspective) versus aspiration and future potential (Bery's reframing).
