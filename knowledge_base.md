## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **True identity:** First princess of Alberan; a "cursed child" (did not cry at birth) who was supposed to be killed. Nora abandoned her in the forest instead. Found at ~age 3 by Gorka.
- **Raised by:** Gorka and Grace in Karka Village until the bandit attack (C.4–5)
- **Adopted by:** Bogan Christand (C.7 onward), on Gallen's arrangement
- **Appearance:** Silver hair to the waist, large violet/amethyst eyes, long lashes, snow-white skin, petite frame. Fairy-like, ephemeral. Wears simple one-piece dresses; favors black cloak with silver embroidery (falcon-and-lightning crest). Carries a custom curved nata-like sword (made by Koz, worth ~3 gold coins) and two knives.
- **Psychology:** No empathy, no guilt. Thinks entirely in gain/loss. Kills as easily as cleaning a room — it is the most efficient solution to an unpleasant problem. Hides this behind social rules when observed. Genuinely affectionate toward those who give her gains (Bery, Selene, Bogan, Gallen). Gradually develops a closer approximation of normal emotion through Bery's patient teaching.
- **Loves:** Cooking (especially pumpkin), being patted on the head, sleeping while hugging someone, sweets, Bery specifically
- **Embarrassments:** Her appetite (known village-wide as "little glutton Krische"); her dependency on Bery; her childlike habits generally
- **Speech:** Third-person self-reference ("Krische thinks…"); formal register (desu/masu); literal interpretation of words; frequent "Nn……" and "Err……"; "Ehehe" laugh
- **Combat abilities:** Elite swordswoman. Uses mana as "imaginary muscles" (physical enhancement) perfected since ~age 3 — her version is uniquely efficient, no stiffening or tension. Discovered magic-without-crystals (secret). Exceptional tactical/strategic mind. Counts "kill opportunities" during sparring as a training metric.
- **Sword:** Small curved nata-like sword; heavy at the tip like a naginata; one-sided; excellent cutting ability. Made by Koz the blacksmith.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea) — granted directly by the king at the victory ceremony (C.34)
- **Working title:** "Corps Commander's Adjutant" (軍団長副官) — used by soldiers when addressing her during civil war operations (C.41–45).
- **Kiss habit:** Bery inadvertently taught her that kissing = expression of love between close people. She now kisses Bery and Selene freely without self-consciousness. This is innocent on her part.
- **Cooking philosophy:** Approaches a single ingredient from every possible angle, wasting nothing. Uses all parts (skin, leaves, etc.). Excels at simple country cooking with limited ingredients. Contrasts with Bery's lavish, combinatorial approach (C.42).
- **Leadership style:** States objectives and policy first, delegates execution entirely to capable subordinates. Never doubts her own correctness. Fills in shortfalls or replaces personnel if execution proves infeasible. Spares no effort providing resources so that the unreasonable becomes reasonable. Earns respect from capable subordinates through results and wide discretion; can be feared by others for demanding the unreasonable. Explicitly contrasted with Selene's approach (C.45).
- **"Anne Category":** Krische's mental classification for well-meaning but clumsy people. Anne is the prototype; Mia was filed here as "a slightly cleverer Anne" (C.45).
- **Nickname among black century:** "Bunny" (うさちゃん / うさぎちゃん) — soldiers liken her silver hair, white skin, and small stature to a winter white rabbit. Carries both affection and mild derision; Dagra strictly forbids it.
- **Memory:** Demonstrated ability to recall individual soldiers' names, selection numbers, squad assignments, and arrival dates from a single encounter during selection (C.48).
- **Training approach (C.46):** Uses five-on-one mock combat against herself to humble the black century after they grew arrogant. Demands a 1:10 loss ratio in ambush scenarios against regular troops. States her goal is to turn every member of the black century into a copy of Dagra.
- **Nickname for Dagra:** "Bald Eagle" (ハゲワシ) — bestowed as an "affectionate nickname" based on his bald head and hooked nose. Krische genuinely believes this is a term of endearment (C.46).
- **On rumors:** Does not care about negative rumors about herself; would use them if advantageous. However, deeply dislikes that such rumors cause Bery to be spoken of negatively (C.48).
- **Dragon's Maw strategy (C.51):** Proposes burning Mitskronia mountain to trap enemy forces, combined with a full-army breakthrough from Bernaich. Dismisses moral and environmental concerns with characteristic pragmatism. Argues victory should be the sole consideration.
- **Pre-battle speech (C.53):** Delivers a speech to the black century that is simultaneously pragmatic and chilling: "killing someone doesn't hurt you one bit," "kill them before they kill you," and expresses her own anxiety only in terms of Selene's safety. Falls asleep standing against a tree with hand on sword hilt.
- **Cliff descent assault (C.54):** Devised a tactic using the black century's mana-enhanced abilities to descend near-vertical cliffs at the Dragon's Maw. Led the descent personally, moving faster than freefall, and systematically eliminated enemy officers to break cohesion before the main force engaged.
- **Bernaich guerrilla campaign (C.57–60):** After the failed decisive battle, Krische refused to withdraw and led the black century in a week-long guerrilla harassment campaign on Bernaich. Personally led every assault, sleeping minimally (only light naps), eating only hardtack and stolen provisions. Burned the ridgeline fortress twice, killed a visiting corps commander and adjutant, massacred engineers, and annihilated seven enemy hundred-man squads across multiple sweeps. Developed a high fever and physical tremors by the end.
- **Nickname among enemy:** "Head-Hunter Krische" (首狩人のクリシェ) — used by Gildanstein's soldiers to describe her during the Bernaich campaign.
- **Encounter with Gildanstein (C.57):** After the decisive battle, scaled the cliff and launched a solo ambush on Gildanstein's ~20-man elite guard. Killed several including Walter Zargan in seconds. Pinned Gildanstein to the ground and nearly killed him, but was interrupted by Salva. Gildanstein subsequently offered a deal — his sword (Bogan's) in exchange for Krische withdrawing. Krische, tormented by anxiety over Bery, Selene, and Kreschenta's safety, accepted and withdrew. This marks the first time Krische explicitly chose emotional security over the tactically optimal action.
- **Bernaich withdrawal battle (C.58):** Took command of First Corps from Selene. Ordered the Third Battalion used as deliberate bait in a mobile defense, threatened to kill Barga for questioning orders, and personally led the Fourth Battalion's counterattack. Result: Christand losses 1,500 vs. Gildanstein losses ~4,000 including deserters. No one praised her afterward; soldiers feared her.
- **Reaction to Bogan's death (C.59):** Attempted to comfort Selene but was struck on the cheek (by Selene's gauntlet) and told to leave. Deeply shaken; interpreted this as potential rejection. Refused to rest, instead departing immediately for the guerrilla campaign, driven partly by desire to prove useful to Selene.
- **Forced rest (C.60):** Dagra, with concurrence from Korintz and Tagel, invoked the military regulation for temporary assumption of command, stripping Krische of authority and ordering her to rest. Kalua physically caught her when she collapsed, and Mia held her while she slept. Krische fell asleep almost instantly upon accepting the order.
- **Bery's candy (C.59–60):** Carried a small pouch with a single candy on a cord around her neck throughout the campaign — a keepsake from Bery with a promise to save the last one for Bery to eat.
- **Post-Bernaich collapse (C.61):** Returned after a week-long guerrilla campaign in a state of near-total exhaustion — high fever, swollen shoulder, hands covered in burst blood blisters, sword chipped beyond recognition. Carried back by Mia wrapped in a blood-soaked blanket. Remained unconscious for two days.
- **First words upon waking (C.61):** Apologized to Selene, believing Selene hated her. Could not eat despite hunger; asked to sleep. Requested only that Selene keep liking her.
- **'Spoiled Krische' mode (C.62):** Created a self-rationalizing persona during recovery to justify indulgence — 'Spoiled Krische' (甘えクリシェ). Consolidated the "best qualities" of Working Krische, Lady Krische, Glutton Krische, and Clingy Krische into an "invincible" state that feels no shame. Result: childlike regression, constant kissing, clinging, and being fed.
- **Dog and owner game (C.62–63):** Invented a 'dog and owner' game after Bery taught her to say "woof." Became obsessed with making Kreschenta play the dog — including 'shake,' 'fetch,' and rewarding with cookies and head pats. Also made Kreschenta perform for Bery.
- **Candy return (C.62):** Used the candy Bery had given her before the Bernaich campaign. Fed it to Anne, then passed it mouth-to-mouth to Bery as a returned gift. Also shared one with Selene via kiss.
- **Promotion (C.64):** Appointed First Corps Commander after Selene's promotion to General. Accepted without opposition from other commanders.
- **Black century rewards (C.64):** Granted the black century permission for self-furnished weapons and armor, unit insignia, and two days' leave as a reward for their service during the Bernaich campaign.
- **Sword condition (C.65):** Curved sword was so heavily chipped that resharpening reduced it to a fraction of its original size — light as a feather. Counted 593 kills with this sword (784 total during the campaign). Mourned the damage in pumpkin-equivalent terms.
- **Swordsmanship lecture (C.64):** Gave an impromptu lecture to the First Battalion's light infantry, unaware that the soldiers' "enthusiasm" was largely directed at watching her move in a skirt. Selene intervened and sent the battalion on a punishment forced march.
- **Views on honor and death (C.65):** Expressed to Bery that she cannot understand choosing death for abstract values like honor or duty. Wished Christand had been a merchant family instead. What she wants is modest: safety, proximity to loved ones, freedom from risk.
- **Dream concept (C.65):** After Bery's explanation, adopted the concept of a "dream" — specifically, a shared dream with Bery of returning to peaceful daily life at the estate. Promised Bery they would achieve it together.
- **Flanking strategy (C.66):** Planned to lead a wide flanking sweep around Bernaich, cross the mountains, coordinate with Nozan Velraigh for simultaneous east-west attack to take the Dragon's Maw from behind while Selene holds the front.
- **Departure scene (C.67):** Extended farewell kissing session with Bery before departure, requiring Selene to physically intervene. Exchanged a private moment with Selene — admitted she felt frightened by the separation for the first time, likening it to her fear of being disliked. Selene comforted her, affirmed that fear is proof of caring. Both wept slightly.
- **Tactical deployment (C.68–70):** Led the First Corps plus black century in a flanking maneuver over the Kureil Range, seized enemy supply train, then waited in forest near Nozan's position. At battle, Keith delegated rear command; Begil given vanguard cleanup role; Krische personally led the black century breakthrough. Killed Klare Markelus personally — stepped up the shaft of his spear, landed on his horse, kicked him clear, and severed his neck in the same motion.
- **Nickname explanation to Elrouga (C.68):** Explained "Hagawashi" to Elrouga as an "affectionate nickname" to foster closeness. Genuinely believed this framing. Elrouga and Dagra shared a silent look of mutual commiseration.
- **Logistics-awareness (C.68):** Demonstrated clear understanding of mountain logistics constraints — specifically that wagons are necessary for supply lines, limiting mountain crossings to troop-only movements. Chose the crossing point specifically to reach the enemy's supply train.
- **Food preservation magic (C.71):** Devised a spell to suspend food in stasis — adapted from a barrier spell originally designed to keep pot contents warm by insulating from outside air. Reverses the process to restore the food. Costs significant mana (causes dizziness and exhaustion). Used to preserve a rakura pie baked with Bery across a week-long campaign.
- **Emotional state during campaign (C.71):** Experienced deepening hollowness when eating Bery's pie alone — realized the food was delicious because of Bery's presence, not just the cooking. Reflected on the growing disconnect between her improving killing ability and Bery's values. Expressed desire to become "the Krische who would never make Bery wear a sad face."
- **Soundproofing magic (C.71):** Created a soundproofing spell by adapting the food-insulation formula. Deliberately made fragile so it breaks when someone enters the tent. Justified under Bery's rule as private use.
- **Feast behavior (C.71–72):** Carried to the victory feast by Kalua; sat on her lap passively. Peeled fruit for dozens of soldiers without complaint — an activity that reminded her of peeling fruit for village children and of learning techniques from Bery. Became mildly drunk on fruit-diluted wine (three cups). Spoke at length about her dream of domestic life, repeating herself as drowsiness set in.
- **Self-assessment on swords (C.72):** Stated plainly that she does not like swords — practices and improves because it's necessary, not because she enjoys it. Finds the optimization process satisfying but not the act itself.
- **Hilkintos strategy (C.75):** Proposed personally leading the smaller force against Hilkintos rather than the Dragon's Maw, citing time advantage and the ability to choose better terrain further west. Plans to bait Hilkintos into pursuit by deliberately failing to secure a river crossing, then ambush when the enemy overextends. Dismissed frontal engagement as wasteful.
- **Hilkintos execution (C.74):** Publicly executed Aulgorn Hilkintos before the Dragon's Maw garrison as a demonstration. Presented it as legal punishment for high treason (drawing a sword against royalty) and Sacred Convention violation (feigned surrender). Systematically dismembered him while delivering a speech combining legal argument, threat, and an ultimatum to the garrison. Announced she would personally assault the fortress that night.
- **Black century unit flag (C.74):** First description — black-painted flag with a crescent moon sharp as a scythe and a skull, symbolizing heads reaped by a blade.
- **Reaction to Roland (C.75):** Narrowed her eyes upon hearing Roland's name; toyed with her sword hilt beneath her cloak during their meeting. Ignored Roland throughout the dinner, focusing on food research with Elvena instead.
- **Elvena encounter (C.75):** Was kissed by Elvena in the bath; responded with wide-eyed bewilderment rather than any romantic reaction. Lectured Elvena that kisses are only for people you really like. Found Elvena's manner reminiscent of Bery but noticed her facial features reminded her of someone else she couldn't identify.

### Bery Argan
- **Full name:** Bery Argan; House Argan (ruined noble family, baron rank)
- **Role:** Sole servant of the Christand household; Selene's aunt (younger half-sister of Bogan's late wife Lazura)
- **Appearance:** Red hair, shoulder-length; large kind brown eyes; youthful but mature beauty; womanly figure. Wears white-and-black apron dress habitually.
- **Personality:** Highly intelligent; skilled in cooking, domestic arts, and gentle manipulation. Extremely patient. Acts as Krische's primary teacher, surrogate mother/older sister, and emotional anchor. Secretly fearful of high society and men due to her past.
- **Background:** Family went bankrupt; she and Lazura were sent to the capital essentially as slaves/mistresses. Bogan purchased Lazura and married her, rescuing both sisters. Bery has a weak constitution and cannot bear children. Has despised Roland the merchant since childhood — he was involved in their bondage situation.
- **Cooking:** Master-level; Krische's forerunner and teacher. Spends most of her salary on kitchen knives. Philosophy: pursues the ultimate flavor through combination, using ingredients lavishly — the finest parts of the finest ingredients. Uses ingredients for subtle hints and hidden accents, tolerating waste in service of artistry. Contrasts with Krische's waste-nothing approach (C.42).
- **Magic:** Can use physical enhancement. Krische taught her the magic-without-crystals technique.
- **Relationship with Krische:** Deeply affectionate and complex. She is too soft on Krische and knows it. Reciprocates Krische's kisses (after initial resistance) but is flustered and conflicted about what this means. Selene complains about this regularly.
- **Administrative skills:** Faster than Selene at calculations and management. Handles provisions/equipment allocation documents and administrative paperwork when not cooking or tending Kreschenta (C.43).
- **Terms of address:** Calls Selene "ojou-sama"; calls Krische "Krische-sama"
- **Hidden name (真名):** Lips. Full noble name: Bery Lips Argan. Given by her parents at birth per noble tradition; never spoken aloud until she shared it with Krische. Performed an old-form oath of devotion using this name (C.49).
- **Relationship with Lazura (flashback, C.47):** As a child, Bery was withdrawn, self-loathing, and physically frail, believing herself worthless. Lazura was bright, beautiful, and fiercely loving — insisted on being Bery's personal caretaker, overriding Bery's attempts to isolate herself. Lazura declared Bery her "treasure" and refused to let her diminish her own worth. This memory is triggered by hearing soldiers' rumor-mongering about Krische.
- **Self-assessment (C.49):** Recognizes her own feelings as potentially impure — suspects she may have cultivated Krische's dependency partly out of a desire for possessive control and to be someone's sole indispensable person. Questions whether her resignation about Krische's abnormality is genuine acceptance or self-serving. Considers herself "warped" and an "imitation" compared to Krische's authentic nature.
- **Changed demeanor (C.50):** After the oath scene, Bery no longer flushes or resists Krische's kisses — she accepts them happily and even subtly invites them. This shift is noticed by Kreschenta (irritated), Selene (amused but unconcerned), and the assembled officers.
- **Full moon motif:** The flashback links Bery's love of the full moon to her admiration for Lazura — "like a full moon that never waned, her sister was perfect." The waning moon at the end of C.47 mirrors Bery's sense of her own imperfection.
- **Oath revelation to Selene (C.52):** Confessed to Selene that she offered her hidden name to Krische (as described in C.49). Gave extended account of her childhood — self-loathing, twisted, envious of Lazura, cold and intellectual. Described finding meaning at Krische's side. Selene teased about a possible ceremony; Bery's reaction confirmed she had imagined it.
- **Farewell scene (C.59):** Gave Krische a pouch of candies before departure, invoking the same promise as before a previous battle: "save the last one for me to eat." Kissed Krische's forehead. Asked Mia and Kalua to look after Krische, bowing deeply.
- **Consoling Selene (C.59):** Comforted Selene after Bogan's death and the incident with Krische. Told Selene she would not scold her but would not forgive her either — Selene must apologize properly. Drew parallels between Selene and Bogan (both retreated to grieve alone with the same words). Reminded Selene that Krische is "very delicate and very timid, honest like a child, and easily hurt."
- **During Krische's absence (C.61):** Spent nearly every day in a chair beside the empty bed. Maintained composure to avoid worrying Selene, but anxiety grew daily after learning Krische had a fever. Broke down crying with relief when Krische finally woke.
- **'Spoiled Krische' enabler (C.62):** Actively facilitated Krische's recovery by providing unlimited sweets, kisses, and physical affection. Admitted to Selene she had "given up" on correcting Krische's kissing behavior and was now recruiting Selene as a fellow sufferer.
- **Dog game participation (C.63):** Went along with Krische's dog-and-owner game, serving as secondary "owner" for Kreschenta. Found the situation genuinely funny, particularly Kreschenta's instant transformation when Gallen appeared.
- **Volunteer for Arna mission (C.66):** Volunteered to accompany Kreschenta to the Arna Empire as attendant. Argued that danger exists everywhere and that she wants to contribute. Krische assigned Korintz's squad (10 soldiers) as escort.
- **Dream speech (C.65):** Explained the concept of dreams to Krische — that a "what-if" about the future, unlike one about the past, can be made real through effort. Compared dreams to a moon lighting a dark night. Her own dream: returning to everyday life with Krische at the estate.

- **Dragon's Maw siege (C.74):** Commands fifteen thousand Christand troops from the north side of the Dragon's Maw, conducting slow-pressure attacks with overhead shields and arrow screens. Deployed four trebuchets against the now-bare Mitskronia.

### Bogan Argalitte Vezrinea Christand
- **Title:** Margrave; Northern General of the Alberan Kingdom
- **Nickname:** "Lightning Quick" (迅雷, Jinrai)
- **Appearance:** Blonde hair with white streaks combed back; beard; muscular; countless scars; imposing presence that softens with family
- **Personality:** Pragmatic, humble for his rank. Invests personal wealth in his army rather than personal luxury. Treats commoners with unusual fairness. Honest and loyal to a fault.
- **Crest:** Falcon and lightning bolt
- **Background:** Born low-ranking noble; rose entirely through merit. Gildanstein promoted him to general. Widower (Lazura died). Adopted Krische on Gallen's recommendation after seeing her potential.

- **Civil war:** Declared for Kreschenta after she fled to his estate (C.37–38). Leads northern army against Gildanstein. Commands the Dragon's Maw front; forces reduced from ~20,000 to 14,000 after initial engagements (C.43).
- **Dragon's Maw campaign (C.50):** Has been fighting at the Dragon's Maw for approximately two months. Situation is overall favorable but deadlocked. Seeks a decisive result — primary objective is Gildanstein himself, secondary is complete control of the Dragon's Maw. Shows significant fatigue.
- **Exchange with Gildanstein (C.50):** A chivalrous exchange before battle. Gildanstein offers Bogan the chance to kneel and be spared; Bogan refuses, stating he has already committed as Kreschenta's blade. Both acknowledge the other as a worthy warrior. Gildanstein asks for a personal duel; Bogan declines, saying he cannot match the prince.

His fate — Gildanstein later displays a severed arm and sword, declaring Bogan slain.

Lost his right arm to Gildanstein but threw Gildanstein to the ground even as his arm was severed. Enemy reinforcements forced Gildanstein to withdraw without taking Bogan's head. Bogan was evacuated to Mitskronia, where he briefly regained consciousness to see Selene one last time — patted her head, mouthed "I leave the rest to you," and died.
- **Swordsmanship legacy (C.56):** In his final duel, incorporated elements of Krische's sword — specifically the ground-level strike and fluid, stance-free movement. Acknowledged it as crude imitation but effective as a surprise element.
- **Epithet origin (C.56):** "Lightning Quick" (迅雷) was not merely a reference to his generalship but to his ferocious combat presence at the front lines — a berserker state that inspired those around him to madness and courage. Described as "a storm in human form."

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Magic System
- **Mana (魔力):** Internal energy. Most nobles have it; rare commoners do too. Users tend to age slower and live longer (Bery, who looks late teens, is in her twenties).
- **Physical Enhancement (身体強化):** Using mana as "imaginary muscles" — the standard combat magic. Replaces real muscle tension; users must be completely relaxed to use it properly. Krische's version is uniquely perfect, learned from age ~3.
- **Mana training methodology (C.45):** Trainees are more receptive to learning mana control when at the peak of physical exhaustion. Training involves extreme physical conditioning followed by mana manipulation exercises. Those who grasp the knack help teach others.
- **Magic Crystals (魔石):** Crystallized underground mana. Circuits carved into them produce effects (heat, cold, light, etc.). Break down over time from residue mana buildup.
- **Magic Technicians:** Carve circuits into crystals.
- **Magicians:** Research and develop those circuits. Generally low-status despite their work.
- **Eternal Magic Lamps (常魔灯):** Mana-powered lighting; used in upper-class homes and the capital.
- **Krische's Secret Technique:** Forming magic circuits directly within one's own mana — eliminates the need for crystals entirely. Can produce fire, water, heat, etc. Revolutionary; kept secret by Bery's request because it would transform warfare (C.25).

### Military Structure
- **General:** Highest field rank. Commands an army. Bogan holds this for the north.
- **Corps Commander:** Commands ~5,000 men (5 battalions)
- **Battalion Commander:** Commands ~1,000 men (10 centurions)
- **Centurion:** Commands 100 soldiers (2 captains)
- **Captain:** Commands ~50 men (10 corporals)
- **Corporal:** Commands a squad of 5
- **Sergeant (伍長):** Rank below captain; used for Mia's appointment as Dagra's adjutant. In the special century, adjutant role is attached despite this normally being a battalion-level position (C.45).
- **Strategic Command:** A unit Bogan is developing — dedicated strategists separate from field commanders. Krische is being trained for this role. Uses magnetic boards with troop-type and terrain symbols for tactical instruction (C.45).
- **The Wolf Pack:** Nickname for the first battalion of the first corps; all career soldiers; most elite unit in the Christand army.
- **Black Century (黒の百人隊):** Special unit of ~98 mana-capable soldiers under Dagra's command, with Krische as operational commander. Structure: Dagra (centurion), Tagel (captain), one captain TBD, Mia (sergeant/adjutant). Operational role: battlefield reserve/strike force deployed at decisive moments for flanking, breakthrough, and enemy commander elimination. Trains in indoor storerooms to avoid resentment from regular troops (C.45).
- **Training Century (教導百人隊):** Dagra's former men from the mountain infiltration, reorganized as instruction cadre for selection and training duties (C.45).

### Noble Ranks
- **Archduke:** King's brothers and close relatives
- **Duke:** More distant royals; Dagren Garhka holds this
- **Margrave:** Highest non-royal noble rank; typically granted to border generals. Bogan holds this.
- **Earl, Baron:** Mid and lower noble ranks. Bery's family (Argan) was baron before going bankrupt.
- **Senior Knight (Rinea):** Honorary title for battlefield achievement. Not heritable; no land granted. The word "Rinea" means "warrior" in the old language. Granted by the king directly for exceptional service.
- **Junior Knight:** Lower tier; can be granted by generals with royal permission.

**Alberan Kingdom** — Absolute monarchy. Women can inherit the crown (rare but legal). Currently split by civil war.

- Narrative: Gildanstein murdered the king and usurped the throne

**Prince Faction (Gildanstein):**
- Controls the capital and central territories
- Legitimate by succession rules
- Narrative: Kreschenta murdered the king to seize power
- Forces: ~20,000 (down from ~30,000). Many hastily mobilized but being trained through ongoing skirmishes. Capital not fully consolidated; fence-sitters remain.

**Situation (C.43):** Deadlocked at the Dragon's Maw. Combat front too narrow for decisive engagement. Fighting has shifted to the mountains (Mitskronia and Bernaich). Both sides scraping together reservists and veterans.

**Holy Elsren Empire:** Eastern major power. Invaded during the succession crisis. Used "Holy War" declarations backed by the Church (Curia). Strong cavalry; crude logistics (pillaging-based). Defeated at the Battle of Arzlen (C.24). Not currently an active threat but not destroyed.

**Arna Empire:** Northern neighbor and ally of Alberan. Has offered reinforcements; unlikely to move against Alberan regardless of which faction wins.

**Galshan Republic:** Southern neighbor. Historically hostile to Alberan. Did not intervene during the Empire invasion (was at war with Elderant at the time). Status in civil war uncertain.

**Elderant Kingdom:** Western neighbor; was at war with Galshan during the Empire invasion events.

### Social/Cultural Details
- **Cursed Child (呪われた子):** Royal tradition — an infant who does not cry at birth is considered cursed and killed. Both Krische and Kreschenta were designated this.
- **King's Road (王道):** Red carpet in the throne room representing all blood shed for the Kingdom. Normally only royalty walks it; those invited to do so during ceremonies are symbolically granted a share of that legacy.
- **King's Fingers (王の指):** Five main roads to the capital. Towns along them receive greater tax revenue in exchange for hosting traveling nobles.
- **Forged Stone (鍛石):** Building material made from volcanic ash that hardens when compressed. Used throughout the capital; extremely durable. The Alberan Kingdom's foundational competitive advantage historically.
- **Sacred Convention (聖典協定):** International agreement governing treatment of prisoners of war, town protections, etc. Has real force; violations create diplomatic consequences.
- **Military Salute:** Right palm to left chest = "I offer my life to you." Superior replies with right thumb pointing at heart = "Your loyalty will be my flesh and blood."
- **Servants:** Upper-class households hire daughters of lower nobles as live-in apprentices; common path for lower nobles to gain connections. Bery is unusual in being the sole servant of a margrave's household.
- **Military logistics (C.43):** Primarily carried out by hired civilians and merchants. Military logistics units handle management and paperwork — require literate, numerate elites. Merchants also provide comfort houses (brothels) and sell luxury goods to soldiers outside fortresses. Selene pays particular attention to managing sexual violence risks.

### Food and Cooking Terms
- **Pumpkin (カボチャ):** Krische's favorite ingredient; a motif for her simple domestic happiness. When pumpkin plans are foiled, chaos is encroaching.
- **Pumpkin gratin (C.42):** Bery's idea — scoop out a pumpkin, fill with gratin. The scooped flesh is mashed into soup for a milky variation. Planned for the next day.
- **Rakura (ラクーラ):** Sweet red crunchy fruit (strawberry-apple hybrid); very juicy. Explodes when bitten — Krische got juice on her dress the first time.
- **Nirkana (ニルカナ):** Small sweet strawberry-like fruit.
- **Black soy tea (黒豆茶):** Bitter drink; Krische adds excessive honey and milk.
- **Graberas / Almarei (グラベラス / アルマレイ):** Tea leaf varieties. Graberas is refreshing, recommended straight or with lemon.

### Noble Hidden Names (真名)
- Noble families give their children a secret name (隠し名 / 真名) at birth. This name is never spoken aloud under normal circumstances. Sharing it constitutes an intimate act of trust. An old tradition exists of pledging one's true name as an oath of devotion — "this true name, together with all that I am, I give entirely to you" — described as "a rather old way of declaring one's love" (C.49).

### Village Rules Referenced
- **"If you miss with two arrows, you may not hunt that quarry" (二射を外せば狩ってはならぬ):** A Karka Village hunting code. Originally a caution against chasing prey too far; extended to promises. A promise broken twice is assumed it will be broken a third time. After two violations, a penalty is enforced. Krische applies this to Kreschenta's repeated complaints about bathing arrangements (C.48).

### Military Tactics (expanded)
- **Flank breakthrough vs. envelopment (C.51):** A flank breakthrough is a purely tactical maneuver — breaking through the enemy's flank position and swinging behind them. An envelopment is a wider swing that avoids combat to reach the enemy's rear, approaching a strategic-level action that can threaten rear lines of communication. In kingdom military terminology, "strategy" refers to army-level movement and battlefield selection; "tactics" refers to methods of engagement after contact.
- **Cliff descent assault (C.54):** A tactic devised by Krische exploiting the black century's mana-enhanced physical abilities. Soldiers descend near-vertical cliffs using protrusions as footholds, preceded by logs dropped to clear a landing zone and create confusion. Requires specialized training (three injuries during practice). Only feasible with an all-mana-user unit.
- **False intelligence operation (C.53, C.55):** Gildanstein's operation to assassinate Bogan: turned a trusted subordinate (Salva) using intelligence gathered through coercion of soldiers' capital-based kin. The traitor fed false reports through a compromised messenger (Gran), luring the target into an ambush with a small elite unit. Salva created the false report of Terious's death to draw Bogan personally.
- **Free standby (自由待機, C.53):** An informal order created by Krische. Unlike combat-ready alert standby, it signals full rest until the appointed time with no alertness required. Intended to prevent unnecessary fatigue before action.

### Gildanstein's Personal History (C.55)
- Childhood: began as an idealistic prince, studying governance to support his father and brother. Gradually discovered that courtiers were universally corrupt, using false faces to advance personal ambitions. The royal house was a system, not a family, and he had been dancing within a cage.
- Breaking point: killed a pet cat on a whim; recognized that all beings are desire-driven beasts. Decided to become the strongest and ugliest beast himself. Pursued hedonistic cruelty thereafter.
- Battlefield redemption: discovered genuine nobility among soldiers — people who swore upon their names and died fulfilling their duties. Found the battlefield's simplicity refreshing after court's complexity. Developed particular respect for Bogan Christand.
- Encounter with Lazura: Before Bogan rescued her, Lazura was brought to one of Gildanstein's gatherings. Her composure when threatened with death — asking only that Bery be given genuine love — impressed him deeply. He released her unharmed.

### Swordsmanship Styles
- **Rollka style (ロールカ式):** Traditional military swordsmanship passed down in the Alberan army. Uses a left-side forward stance with the left hand extended (originally holding a small shield) as a lure — to invite, deflect, or counter the opponent's sword, or to grapple. Specialized in breaking the stance of armored opponents and delivering killing blows. Requires tall stature and long reach. Bogan's primary style.
- **King's Sword / Middle Guard (正眼 / 王者の剣):** Two-handed greatsword style embodying noble tradition. Sword held in center position, facing the opponent squarely. The right hand represents heaven (versatility), the left represents earth (stability). Founded on the principle of encompassing all angles within the blade's arc. Considered the foundational noble sword style. Gildanstein's primary style.
- **Zain style (ザイン式):** A distinct sword school; Walter Zargan was its inheritor. No further details given.
- **Krische's influence on others:** Bogan incorporated elements of Krische's sword (ground-level strikes, fluid stance-free movement) into his own fighting. Selene learned from Krische a low-stance style suited to her smaller frame — keeping posture low, striking upward, using the blade to cut in and break the opponent's balance. Both acknowledged their versions as crude compared to the original.

### Military Regulations
- **Temporary assumption of command (指揮代行):** A regulation allowing a commanding officer's direct subordinates to forcibly strip them of authority when judged unfit for command (e.g., madness, incapacitation). Requires unanimous consent of all direct subordinate unit leaders. Exercised by the next-ranking officer (adjutant-equivalent). Carries severe penalties if misused but is a legitimate right to protect subordinates from abusive or incapacitated commanders. Used by Dagra (with Korintz and Tagel's consent) to relieve Krische during the Bernaich campaign (C.60).

### Mobile Defense (機動防御)
- A defensive tactic that deliberately opens holes in the defensive line to lure pursuing enemies into a killing zone, then counterattacks from the flank or rear. High-risk (miscalculation leads to complete breakthrough and collapse) but high-reward. Used by Krische during the Bernaich withdrawal (C.58): Third Battalion served as deliberate bait on the right flank while the Fourth Battalion struck from the center-right, driving enemies off the cliff. Result: ~3x enemy casualties vs. friendly losses.

### Mythology/Religion
- **Arshe (アルセー):** Goddess of fortune. Invoked in blessings by merchants — "May the guidance of Arshe, goddess of fortune, be with you" (C.65).

### Merchant Guilds
- Guild membership (商会所属) is the aspiration of all merchants. Requires an established shop in a town and a reputation for trustworthiness. The guild vouches for members' identities and provides capital and credit for large transactions. Members wear a silver scales pendant with the guild name inscribed. Going from traveling peddler to guild merchant is considered a significant achievement (C.65).

### Heraldry and Insignia
- Certain designs are restricted: dragons (聖霊 / sacred spirits) and crowns may only be used by royalty or high-ranking nobles. Herald officials (紋章官) exist but are rare; typically someone with adequate heraldic knowledge serves in the role. Unit insignia require approval through a designated coordinator (C.64).

### Weapon Self-Furnishing (武器自弁)
- Normally a privilege reserved for battalion commanders. Being granted this right as an ordinary soldier is considered the highest honor. The black century received this privilege as a reward for their Bernaich campaign service (C.64).

### Mountain Logistics
- Armies can send soldiers over moderate mountains, but supply wagons cannot follow. Without wagons, individual soldiers can carry approximately 10 days' provisions at maximum (less with full equipment). This makes wagon-impassable mountain terrain effectively an advance-barrier.
- Krische's flanking force used pack horses (expendable, provisions when killed) and sheep (mobile provisions) instead of wagons for the crossing.

### Echelon Formation (斜線陣) — Expanded
- Advancing wings in staircase intervals from one side, concentrating force on the leading wing. Primary advantages: breakthrough power at the main-effort wing, time gained on the delayed wing. Primary risks: recovery difficulty after breakdown; cadence gaps between staircase intervals. Requires extensively drilled troops.
- Used by Nozan Velraigh at the Battle of the Central Plain as a signal to Krische that she had been located, and to bait Markelus into committing his cavalry prematurely.

### Battle of the Central Plain (C.69–70)
- **Forces:** Nozan Velraigh (15,000) vs. Klare Markelus (27,000). Krische + Elrouga (approx. 10,000, including First Corps and Fourth Corps) striking the rear.
- **Outcome:** Markelus killed; army surrendered after less than one *koku* following Christand's rear entry. Decisive Christand victory.
- **Key sequence:** Krische and Elrouga crossed the Kureil Range early, seized Markelus's supply train on day one, hid in forest near Nozan's position for three days, waited for Nozan to shape the battle. On day four, Nozan used an echelon formation as a coordinated signal; Elrouga's Fourth Corps used a lance breakthrough to open the rear; Krische and the black century drove through to kill Markelus directly.

### Black Century Unit Flag
- Described for the first time in C.74: a black-painted flag bearing a crescent moon sharp as a scythe and a skull — symbolizing heads reaped by a blade. Carried alongside Krische during the Hilkintos execution.

### Food Preservation Magic
- Krische developed a stasis spell adapted from her food-insulation barrier. Suspends food in a frozen/model-like state; reversing the spell restores freshness and heat. Costs substantial mana. Used to preserve battlefield food supplies made at the estate (C.71).

### Soundproofing Magic
- Adapted from the food-insulation spell. Coats interior surfaces with a thin mana barrier that dampens external sound. Deliberately made fragile — any person stepping inside breaks the formula. Classified as private use under Bery's rule against public magic display (C.71).

### Debt-Bondage Slavery
- Although not publicly sanctioned in the kingdom, debt-bondage slavery exists in practice. The system operates through separated entities: abductors, debt-creators, and debt-buyers. Debts are passed between intermediaries to obscure the trail. Only abductors and debt-creators can be legally prosecuted; buyers are treated as innocent third parties. The state generally lacks the resources to investigate individual cases and looks the other way. Victims bear a misshapen star tattoo from the slave traders. Roland holds debt contracts and profits from this system (C.75).

### Prisoner Absorption Protocol
- After the Battle of the Central Plain, prisoners were given the choice to serve under Christand on equivalent pay, receive guaranteed food and escort to the nearest town, or simply leave. Of ~23,000 captured, ~3,000 fled overnight; of the remaining ~20,000, 15,000 accepted employment. Distribution: Krische and Elrouga each received ~5,000. Krische integrated prisoners into existing battalions (to maintain command simplicity given her front-line leadership style); Elrouga created two new battalions (feasible given his rear-command preference). Battalion commanders and above were interrogated/recruited individually; centurions were kept in command of their own men for organizational continuity (C.71, C.73).

### Military Cuisine System (Black Century)
- The black century operates its own cooking rotation independent of the battalion system. Squads rotate cooking duty; those producing especially good meals receive rewards (duty exemption, alcohol, fruit). Krische personally conducts cooking instruction. The competitive system has notably improved overall culinary skill. Meals prioritize breakfast > lunch > dinner; field camp breakfasts are generous because lunch may be skipped (C.73).

### Trebuchets in Siege Warfare
- Components prepared in workshops, disassembled for transport, reassembled on site. Require calibration time and skilled artisans. Effective against exposed positions like the deforested Mitskronia. Selene deployed four against the Dragon's Maw from the north (C.74).

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

1. **Pumpkin** — Krische's simple contentment; domestic peace. Foiled pumpkin plans = chaos.
2. **Kissing as love** — Bery's accidental teaching; comedy and tenderness.
3. **Cheek-pinching** — Selene's expression of affection/exasperation with Krische. Consistent.
4. **Head-patting** — Universal comfort gesture. Krische loves receiving it; instinctively extends it to Kreschenta. Selene now uses it with Kreschenta as well (C.44).
5. **"A shame" (残念)** — Krische's substitute for "sad." Bery corrects her. Kreschenta uses the same word (C.38–39), showing their identical nature.
6. **Sleeping together** — Safety and trust. Krische needs warmth; Kreschenta finds peace for the first time.
7. **Rules vs. true nature** — Krische follows rules when observed; breaks them privately. Tension throughout.
8. **Gains and losses (損得)** — Krische's entire worldview until Bery teaches her what love actually is.
9. **The birdcage** — Kreschenta's metaphor for their household: a protected place where neither has to perform or fear.
10. **"It was a shame" (残念でした)** — Used in the prologue framing to describe Krische's nature. A signature that bookends her characterization.
11. **Kitchen as sanctuary** — Cooking with Bery remains Krische's "supreme bliss," now transported to the fortress kitchen. The fortress kitchen crew's competition adds comic relief (C.42).
12. **Selene's burden** — The weight of command on Selene; her youth (not yet 16), tendency to overwork, and the isolation of responsibility (C.43–44).
13. **Krische vs. Selene leadership contrast** — Explicitly articulated: Selene aims for "better" and earns devotion; Krische demands "the best" and earns respect through results and resource provision. Two opposite but equally effective poles (C.45).
14. **Seating wars** — Kreschenta's persistent, valiant, futile attempts to sit beside Krische at meals vs. Krische's ironclad defense of the seat beside Bery (C.43).
15. **Anne Category** — Krische's mental classification for well-meaning but clumsy people (C.45).
