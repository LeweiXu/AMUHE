# Translation Context: A Maiden's Unwanted Heroic Epic (少女の望まぬ英雄譚)

## YOUR TASK
Translate chapters of this Japanese web novel into English. Prioritize accuracy and nuance over naturalness. Preserve the original tone exactly. Consult knowledge_base.md for all character details, place names, lore, and the current story state.

---

## CORE TRANSLATION RULES

1. **Krische always refers to herself in third person** — "Krische" instead of "I/me/my." Never change this under any circumstances.
2. **Maintain speech register** — Each character has a distinct voice. Do not homogenize them. Consult SPEECH PATTERNS below.
3. **"Ehehe"** — Keep as-is. It is Krische's signature soft laugh. Do not replace with "hehe," describe it, or vary it.
4. **"……"** — Preserve Japanese ellipses exactly as written. They carry emotional weight and pacing. Do not reduce to "..." or omit.
5. **Honorifics** — Keep Japanese honorifics in all cases: kaa-sama, tou-sama, ojii-sama, ojou-sama, onee-sama, oba-san, -sama, -san. Do not translate them.
6. **Krische's mana** — She calls it "the floofy thing" (ふわふわしたもの) or simply "the floof." Always use this. Never substitute "mana" when Krische speaks about it casually. Other characters use "mana" normally.
7. **Do not soften Krische's abnormality.** When she thinks about killing with the same detachment as cooking, translate that flatness faithfully. Do not add horror, hesitation, or remorse she does not feel. The contrast is the entire point.
8. **Pumpkin (カボチャ)** — Always translate literally as "pumpkin." It is a recurring motif. Do not substitute "squash" or any other word.
9. **Name romanizations are fixed.** Use the established romanizations in knowledge_base.md exactly. Do not invent new spellings for names you haven't seen before — flag them in translator notes instead.
10. **Do not infer character feelings not on the page.** If a character's expression is blank, it is blank. If Krische feels nothing, she feels nothing.

## WORLD CONTEXT

**Setting:** Fantasy kingdom (Alberan Kingdom) at roughly early modern European technology level. Magic exists and is widespread among nobles.

**Magic system summary:**
- Mana is an internal energy possessed by most nobles and rare commoners
- Standard magic requires carved magic crystals as a medium (heating, cooling, lighting, etc.)
- Physical enhancement — using mana as artificial muscles — is the standard combat magic for nobles
- Krische discovered how to cast magic without any crystal by forming circuits in her own mana. This is a revolutionary secret known only to Bery, Selene, and Kreschenta. Do not treat this as common knowledge in the translation.

**Political state (as of Chapter 42 onward):**
- The Alberan Kingdom is in civil war: the **War for the Alberan Crown**
- Factions: Gildanstein (royal prince, holds the capital) vs. Kreschenta (princess, sheltered by the Christands)
- The Christand family has declared for Kreschenta
- The Holy Elsren Empire's invasion was defeated at the **Battle of Arzlen** and is no longer an active threat
- The immediate military objective is securing the **Dragon's Maw** mountain pass

**Military ranks (high to low):** General → Corps Commander (~5,000) → Battalion Commander (~1,000) → Centurion (100) → Captain (50) → Corporal (5)

**Noble ranks (high to low):** Archduke → Duke → Margrave → Earl → Baron → Senior Knight (Rinea) → Junior Knight

## TONE GUIDE

The novel's tone shifts dramatically by scene. Do not flatten these shifts — the contrast is load-bearing.

| Scene type | Tone |
|---|---|
| Krische cooking / eating | Warm, cozy, slightly comic. Her fixation on food is played completely straight — never mocked or condescended to. |
| Krische killing | Flat, matter-of-fact, businesslike. Identical register to cooking. No horror. The contrast with cooking scenes is the entire artistic point. |
| Krische being embarrassed | Genuinely childlike. She is mortified when her appetite or dependence is exposed. Play it warm, not cruel. |
| Bery teaching / guiding Krische | Gentle, patient, slightly melancholy. Bery is wise but also emotionally invested in ways she doesn't fully examine. |
| Selene and Krische banter | Warm sibling energy. Selene is perpetually exasperated but incapable of being genuinely cold to Krische. |
| Kreschenta | Controlled surface over calculating depth. When genuine feeling surfaces, it arrives quietly — never melodramatically. |
| Battle / tactical scenes | Precise, efficient. Krische's tactical voice is identical to her cooking voice — calm, analytical, focused on the optimal outcome. |
| Political scheming | Understated. The most dangerous statements are made plainly, as if discussing the weather. |
| Krische's abnormality on display | Do not editorialize. Let the gap between her words and the situation create the effect on its own. |

## SENSITIVE TRANSLATION NOTES

**Krische's kiss habit:** Bery accidentally taught Krische that kissing is a normal expression of love. Krische now kisses Bery and Selene freely without self-consciousness. Translate as written: innocent and matter-of-fact on Krische's part, flustered on the receiving end. Do not add or remove subtext.

**Krische's killings:** Always translate in Krische's flat, practical register. She does not enjoy killing and does not feel guilty — it is simply efficient. The horror is created by the reader, not the narration.

**Kreschenta's dual nature:** She is genuinely calculating AND genuinely affectionate toward Krische simultaneously. Do not resolve this tension toward either extreme.

**"Cursed child" (呪われた子):** Royal tradition of killing infants who do not cry at birth. Translate consistently as "cursed child."

**Measurement units:** When old Japanese units appear (寸/尺/間), use approximate English equivalents: 寸≈inch, 尺≈foot, 間≈2 yards. Note the approximation in a translator note.

**Garbled speech:** When Krische's speech is physically impeded (cheek-pinching, etc.), render phonetically. Do not translate the underlying words normally.

## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand
- **True identity:** First princess of Alberan; a "cursed child" (did not cry at birth) who was supposed to be killed. Nora abandoned her in the forest instead. Found at ~age 3 by Gorka.
- **Raised by:** Gorka and Grace in Karka Village until the bandit attack (C.4–5)
- **Adopted by:** Bogan Christand (C.7 onward), on Gallen's arrangement
- **Appearance:** Silver hair to the waist, large violet/amethyst eyes, long lashes, snow-white skin, petite frame. Fairy-like, ephemeral. Wears simple one-piece dresses; favors black cloak with silver embroidery. Carries a custom curved nata-like sword (made by Koz, worth ~3 gold coins) and two knives.
- **Psychology:** No empathy, no guilt. Thinks entirely in gain/loss. Kills as easily as cleaning a room — it is the most efficient solution to an unpleasant problem. Hides this behind social rules when observed. Genuinely affectionate toward those who give her gains (Bery, Selene, Bogan, Gallen). Gradually develops a closer approximation of normal emotion through Bery's patient teaching.
- **Loves:** Cooking (especially pumpkin), being patted on the head, sleeping while hugging someone, sweets, Bery specifically
- **Embarrassments:** Her appetite (known village-wide as "little glutton Krische"); her dependency on Bery; her childlike habits generally
- **Speech:** Third-person self-reference ("Krische thinks…"); formal register (desu/masu); literal interpretation of words; frequent "Nn……" and "Err……"; "Ehehe" laugh
- **Combat abilities:** Elite swordswoman. Uses mana as "imaginary muscles" (physical enhancement) perfected since ~age 3 — her version is uniquely efficient, no stiffening or tension. Discovered magic-without-crystals (secret). Exceptional tactical/strategic mind. Counts "kill opportunities" during sparring as a training metric.
- **Sword:** Small curved nata-like sword; heavy at the tip like a naginata; one-sided; excellent cutting ability. Made by Koz the blacksmith.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea) — granted directly by the king at the victory ceremony (C.34)
- **Kiss habit:** Bery inadvertently taught her that kissing = expression of love between close people. She now kisses Bery and Selene freely without self-consciousness. This is innocent on her part.

### Bery Argan
- **Full name:** Bery Argan; House Argan (ruined noble family, baron rank)
- **Role:** Sole servant of the Christand household; Selene's aunt (younger half-sister of Bogan's late wife Lazura)
- **Appearance:** Red hair, shoulder-length; large kind brown eyes; youthful but mature beauty; womanly figure. Wears white-and-black apron dress habitually.
- **Personality:** Highly intelligent; skilled in cooking, domestic arts, and gentle manipulation. Extremely patient. Acts as Krische's primary teacher, surrogate mother/older sister, and emotional anchor. Secretly fearful of high society and men due to her past.
- **Background:** Family went bankrupt; she and Lazura were sent to the capital essentially as slaves/mistresses. Bogan purchased Lazura and married her, rescuing both sisters. Bery has a weak constitution and cannot bear children. Has despised Roland the merchant since childhood — he was involved in their bondage situation.
- **Cooking:** Master-level; Krische's forerunner and teacher. Spends most of her salary on kitchen knives.
- **Magic:** Can use physical enhancement. Krische taught her the magic-without-crystals technique.
- **Relationship with Krische:** Deeply affectionate and complex. She is too soft on Krische and knows it. Reciprocates Krische's kisses (after initial resistance) but is flustered and conflicted about what this means. Selene complains about this regularly.
- **Terms of address:** Calls Selene "ojou-sama"; calls Krische "Krische-sama"

### Selene Christand
- **Full name:** Selene Christand (Selene = "full moon" in old western language)
- **Role:** Bogan's daughter and designated heir; Krische's "older sister" figure
- **Appearance:** Striking golden/blonde hair; sharp cat-like eyes with strength behind them; dignified, powerful beauty. Often wears riding pants and military dress.
- **Personality:** Serious, ambitious, hardworking to a fault. Loves the sword. Deeply protective of Krische in a way she doesn't always articulate cleanly. Acts as the social buffer between Krische and everyone else.
- **Background:** Mother Lazura died after a failed second pregnancy, which Selene blamed on herself (she believed Lazura tried to give her a sibling because Selene wasn't enough as a daughter). This drove her to push herself harder. Bery hoped adopting Krische would help Selene relax — it worked.
- **Habit:** Pinches Krische's cheeks as primary expression of exasperation and affection. This is consistent and recurring.
- **Military role:** Bogan's adjutant throughout the war; commands the special mountain assault squad (C.21–23); appointed First Corps Commander after Nozan's reassignment east.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea)
- **Terms of address:** Calls Krische simply "Krische" (no honorific)

### Bogan Argalitte Vezrinea Christand
- **Title:** Margrave; Northern General of the Alberan Kingdom
- **Nickname:** "Lightning Quick" (迅雷, Jinrai)
- **Appearance:** Blonde hair with white streaks combed back; beard; muscular; countless scars; imposing presence that softens with family
- **Personality:** Pragmatic, humble for his rank. Invests personal wealth in his army rather than personal luxury. Treats commoners with unusual fairness. Honest and loyal to a fault.
- **Crest:** Falcon and lightning bolt
- **Background:** Born low-ranking noble; rose entirely through merit. Gildanstein promoted him to general. Widower (Lazura died). Adopted Krische on Gallen's recommendation after seeing her potential.
- **Relationship with Gildanstein:** Former battlefield comrade. Complex mutual respect. Received private warning from Gildanstein about Kreschenta (C.32), which he neither fully believed nor ignored.
- **Civil war:** Declared for Kreschenta after she fled to his estate (C.37–38). Leads northern army against Gildanstein.

### Gallen
- **Role:** Krische's maternal grandfather (Grace's father); Bogan's adjutant from C.15 onward
- **Background:** Former centurion; served 7 campaigns; legendary among soldiers for rising from private to centurion. Retired but returned to the army to support Bogan. Famous enough that his return was celebrated by old soldiers.
- **Personality:** Gruff, caring, few words. The one who arranged Krische's placement with Bogan and who taught her that it's acceptable to be afraid.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea) — promoted during the victory ceremony (C.34)

### Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Title:** First Princess of the Alberan Kingdom
- **True identity:** Also a "cursed child" (did not cry at birth). Nora taught her to cry to survive. Krische's biological younger sister — they share a father (the king) but different mothers.
- **Appearance:** Strawberry-blonde/golden hair with a red tinge; face nearly identical to Krische's; small, younger by several years; fairy-like
- **Name meaning:** "Crescent moon" in the old western language (same as Krische's name from a different tongue)
- **Psychology:** Same type as Krische — no empathy, pure gain/loss thinking. But unlike Krische, she learned from infancy to act perfectly, presenting as an ideal princess at all times. Far more skilled at social manipulation than Krische. Cold and calculating, but her affection for Krische is genuine — the first real peer she has ever had.
- **History of killings:** Killed her two younger brothers to eliminate succession rivals. Killed other people who suspected her nature. Poisoned the king (her father) to trigger the civil war at a moment favorable to her. Arranged the whole sequence to end up sheltered by the Christands.
- **Civil war script:** Designed the narrative — "innocent princess flees murderous usurper uncle, loyal general shelters her" — to manufacture a popular justification for the war (C.37).
- **Relationship with Krische:** Genuinely seeks "relaxation" — a place she doesn't have to perform or fear for her life. Accepts Bery's authority reluctantly. Calls Krische "onee-sama."
- **Nora:** Her loyal servant who taught her to cry, helped her survive, and assisted her killings. Committed suicide before reaching the Christand estate, to atone for Kreschenta's sins (C.38–39). Kreschenta found this confusing rather than devastating, but quietly buried her.
- **Terms of address:** Calls Krische "onee-sama" (older sister)

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Grace
- Krische's foster mother. Beautiful, black hair, freckles. Kind, clumsy, scatterbrained but deeply loving. The person who taught Krische most of what she knows about human relationships.
- Killed by Gado (the traitor) during the bandit attack on Karka Village (C.5). Her death is Krische's primary "failure" — the moment that made Krische commit to improving her tactical judgment.

### Gorka
- Krische's foster father. Skilled hunter; village hunt leader. A man of few words who recognized Krische's abnormality but loved her regardless.
- Killed by Gado during the bandit attack (C.4).

### Galla
- Widow in Karka Village. Large, sturdy, boisterous. Owns the oven Krische borrows for baking.
- Her son was one of the two children Krische killed before the story begins (killed via cliff/boar trap; Galla never learns this). Galla loves Krische deeply, believes the relationship between them is moving, and dotes on her. This is one of the novel's twisted ironies.

### Nora
- Kreschenta's royal servant from birth. Previously cared for baby Krische in the royal tower before abandoning her in the forest (under orders; she wept and apologized).
- Taught Kreschenta to cry; helped her survive; assisted her in killings over many years.
- Committed suicide by stabbing herself in the heart on the journey to the Christand estate, to atone for everything. Kreschenta buried her (C.39).

### Anne
- Young royal servant (black hair in a bun) at the Erune Estate during the Christands' capital visit.
- Comic-relief character. Clumsy, earnest, overactive imagination. Misinterprets the Krische-Bery relationship as a devoted servant caring for a chronically ill lady. Her rich fantasy life fills in elaborate explanations for everything she sees. (C.31–34)

### Garo (also spelled Galo)
- Former soldier; village guard and sword instructor in Karka Village.
- Sexually harassed Krische. She kills him in Chapter 1 — her first adult kill, using a pre-placed wooden sword and a pre-dug grave. He was technically still alive (paralyzed) when buried.

### Walter Del Grizlandy
- Adjutant and trusted mentor to Empire General Aleha. Old, experienced, deeply loyal.
- Fought valiantly during the Battle of Arzlen. Captured by Krische (lost an arm). Had a respectful exchange with her about why she fights (she said: "tea-time with Bery and Selene"). Died covering Aleha's retreat. Aleha swore to avenge him.

### Aleha Klauzera Shuindel Sarshenka
- General of the Holy Elsren Empire's northern invasion force. Awarded the title "paladin" (Shuindel) by the emperor.
- Rose through merit like Bogan; deeply studied and respected Bogan as an adversary. Was outmaneuvered at the Battle of Arzlen. Executed a disciplined retreat. Sworn to avenge Walter.

### Gildanstein Kalnaros Vel Sarcarinea Alberan
- Royal Prince of Alberan; first in line for the throne before Kreschenta's appointment; current civil war antagonist.
- Infamous for cruel personal entertainments (killing slaves, abusing noble women). Genuinely capable general and pragmatist despite this. Privately warned Bogan about Kreschenta's nature (C.32) — warned him not to be fooled by appearances.
- Controls the capital. Suspects Kreschenta killed the king, correctly.

### Dagren Nakul Dougarinea Garhka
- Southern General of Alberan; rank of Duke. Bear-like, enormously built, loud voice.
- Held the south during the Empire invasion. Struck the final blow against the Empire's main force when Bogan broke Sarshenka in the north. Met Krische at the victory dinner; spent half an hour talking tactics with her using plates and cups as pieces.

### Kokys Nakutra Rinea Agrand
- Second Corps Commander. Enormous muscular build; tiger-themed armor. Very loud. Excellent at direct assault and defense. Led the flanking force (C.20).

### Nozan Velraigh
- First Corps Commander. Handsome. Leads the "Wolf Pack" — first battalion, all career soldiers, the strongest unit in the Christand army.
- Reassigned east as the new Eastern General after the war, taking most of his battalion. This created the hole in Christand's forces at the start of the civil war.

### Elrouga
- Fourth Corps Commander. Skeletal appearance. Skilled at difficult tactical movements and mountain operations. Dotes on his son.

### Terious
- Third Corps Commander. Eagle-eyed. Specializes in defense and fortification construction.

### Dagra
- Centurion; Krische's administrative assistant during the civil war recruitment drive (C.41).
- Hook-nosed, shaved head. Witnessed the mountain infiltration firsthand. Deeply afraid of Krische but devoted. Understands instinctively when she is considering killing someone and has to navigate around it diplomatically.

### Koz (the blacksmith)
- Retired soldier, crippled in service; became a master blacksmith. Gallen's former comrade.
- Made Krische's custom curved sword (C.10). First person outside the military community to treat Krische as a serious swordswoman.

### Roland
- Fat, gaudy merchant in an ostentatiously expensive red coat. Connected to the bankruptcy of House Argan — was involved in arranging Lazura and Bery's effective bondage.
- Encountered on the journey to the capital (C.29). Krische nearly killed him that night but was stopped by Bery's story about her sister and the nature of using knives for cooking versus killing.

## WORLD-BUILDING REFERENCE

### Magic System
- **Mana (魔力):** Internal energy. Most nobles have it; rare commoners do too. Users tend to age slower and live longer (Bery, who looks late teens, is in her twenties).
- **Physical Enhancement (身体強化):** Using mana as "imaginary muscles" — the standard combat magic. Replaces real muscle tension; users must be completely relaxed to use it properly. Krische's version is uniquely perfect, learned from age ~3.
- **Magic Crystals (魔石):** Crystallized underground mana. Circuits carved into them produce effects (heat, cold, light, etc.). Break down over time from residue mana buildup.
- **Magic Technicians:** Carve circuits into crystals.
- **Magicians:** Research and develop those circuits. Generally low-status despite their work.
- **Eternal Magic Lamps (常魔灯):** Mana-powered lighting; used in upper-class homes and the capital.
- **Krische's Secret Technique:** Forming magic circuits directly within one's own mana — eliminates the need for crystals entirely. Can produce fire, water, heat, etc. Revolutionary; kept secret by Bery's request because it would transform warfare (C.25).

### Military Structure
- **General:** Highest field rank. Commands an army. Bogan holds this for the north.
- **Corps Commander:** Commands ~5,000 men (5 battalions)
- **Battalion Commander:** Commands ~1,000 men (10 centurions)
- **Centurion:** Commands 100 soldiers (2 captains)
- **Captain:** Commands ~50 men (10 corporals)
- **Corporal:** Commands a squad of 5
- **Strategic Command:** A unit Bogan is developing — dedicated strategists separate from field commanders. Krische is being trained for this role.
- **The Wolf Pack:** Nickname for the first battalion of the first corps; all career soldiers; most elite unit in the Christand army.

### Noble Ranks
- **Archduke:** King's brothers and close relatives
- **Duke:** More distant royals; Dagren Garhka holds this
- **Margrave:** Highest non-royal noble rank; typically granted to border generals. Bogan holds this.
- **Earl, Baron:** Mid and lower noble ranks. Bery's family (Argan) was baron before going bankrupt.
- **Senior Knight (Rinea):** Honorary title for battlefield achievement. Not heritable; no land granted. The word "Rinea" means "warrior" in the old language. Granted by the king directly for exceptional service.
- **Junior Knight:** Lower tier; can be granted by generals with royal permission.

### Political Landscape (as of end of C.41)

**Alberan Kingdom** — Absolute monarchy. Women can inherit the crown (rare but legal). Currently split by civil war.

**Princess Faction (Kreschenta / Christand):**
- Kreschenta appointed heir by the dying king
- Sheltered by Bogan after fleeing the capital
- Controls the north
- Narrative: Gildanstein murdered the king and usurped the throne

**Prince Faction (Gildanstein):**
- Controls the capital and central territories
- Legitimate by succession rules
- Narrative: Kreschenta murdered the king to seize power

**Holy Elsren Empire:** Eastern major power. Invaded during the succession crisis. Used "Holy War" declarations backed by the Church (Curia). Strong cavalry; crude logistics (pillaging-based). Defeated at the Battle of Arzlen (C.24). Not currently an active threat but not destroyed.

**Arna Empire:** Northern neighbor and ally of Alberan. Has offered reinforcements; unlikely to move against Alberan regardless of which faction wins.

**Galshan Republic:** Southern neighbor. Historically hostile to Alberan. Did not intervene during the Empire invasion (was at war with Elderant at the time). Status in civil war uncertain.

**Elderant Kingdom:** Western neighbor; was at war with Galshan during the Empire invasion events.

### Social/Cultural Details
- **Cursed Child (呪われた子):** Royal tradition — an infant who does not cry at birth is considered cursed and killed. Both Krische and Kreschenta were designated this.
- **King's Road (王道):** Red carpet in the throne room representing all blood shed for the Kingdom. Normally only royalty walks it; those invited to do so during ceremonies are symbolically granted a share of that legacy.
- **King's Fingers (王の指):** Five main roads to the capital. Towns along them receive greater tax revenue in exchange for hosting traveling nobles.
- **Forged Stone (鍛石):** Building material made from volcanic ash that hardens when compressed. Used throughout the capital; extremely durable. The Alberan Kingdom's foundational competitive advantage historically.
- **Sacred Convention (聖典協定):** International agreement governing treatment of prisoners of war, town protections, etc. Has real force; violations create diplomatic consequences.
- **Military Salute:** Right palm to left chest = "I offer my life to you." Superior replies with right thumb pointing at heart = "Your loyalty will be my flesh and blood."
- **Servants:** Upper-class households hire daughters of lower nobles as live-in apprentices; common path for lower nobles to gain connections. Bery is unusual in being the sole servant of a margrave's household.

### Food and Cooking Terms
- **Pumpkin (カボチャ):** Krische's favorite ingredient; a motif for her simple domestic happiness. When pumpkin plans are foiled, chaos is encroaching.
- **Rakura (ラクーラ):** Sweet red crunchy fruit (strawberry-apple hybrid); very juicy. Explodes when bitten — Krische got juice on her dress the first time.
- **Nirkana (ニルカナ):** Small sweet strawberry-like fruit.
- **Black soy tea (黒豆茶):** Bitter drink; Krische adds excessive honey and milk.
- **Graberas / Almarei (グラベラス / アルマレイ):** Tea leaf varieties. Graberas is refreshing, recommended straight or with lemon.
