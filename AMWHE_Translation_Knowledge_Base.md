## TITLE

**Japanese:** 少女の望まぬ英雄譚  
**English:** A Maiden's Unwanted Heroic Epic (also rendered as "The Hero's Tale the Girl Never Wanted")

## CORE CHARACTERS

### Krische Alberinea Krishtand (クリシェ＝アルベリネア＝クリシュタンド)
- **Pronunciation/Romanization:** Krische (not "Cliché" — intentional character name)
- **Also known as:** "Krische-sama" by Bery and others; "onee-sama" (older sister) by Kreschenta
- **Origin:** Abandoned at ~age 3, found in a forest near a village; later discovered to be the first princess of Alberan, a "cursed child" (baby that did not cry) who was supposed to be killed and was instead abandoned by Nora
- **Raised by:** Gorka (foster father, hunter) and Grace (foster mother) in Karka Village
- **Later adopted by:** Bogan Christand
- **Appearance:** Silver hair to the waist, large violet/amethyst eyes, long lashes, snow-white skin, petite frame; described as fairy-like, ephemeral beauty. Wears simple one-piece dresses; favors black cloak with silver embroidery. Carries a small curved nata-like sword and two knives.
- **Speech pattern:** Refers to herself in third person ("Krische" instead of "I/me"). Uses polite, formal Japanese (desu/masu form). Tends to be blunt and literal.
- **Personality:** Abnormal — no empathy, no guilt about killing. Thinks in terms of pure gain/loss. Obsessively efficient. Loves cooking and food above all else, especially pumpkins. Childlike in domestic matters; deeply dependent on Bery. Has a strong aesthetic sense and holds herself to extreme standards. Gradually learns empathy through Bery's teachings.
- **Abilities:** Elite swordsmanship; uses mana as "imaginary muscles" to control her body (physical enhancement), learned naturally from early childhood (~age 3). Can also use magic without magic crystals — creates magic circuits in her own mana (a revolutionary discovery kept secret per Bery's request). Exceptional tactical/strategic mind.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea) — granted directly by the king

### Bery Argan (ベリー＝アルガン / ベリ)
- **Full name:** Bery Argan (House Argan, a ruined noble family of baron rank)
- **Role:** Servant of the Christand household; Selene's aunt (younger half-sister of Bogan's deceased wife, Lazura)
- **Appearance:** Red hair, shoulder-length; large kind brown eyes, youthful and mature beauty, womanly figure
- **Personality:** Highly intelligent, skilled in cooking and all domestic arts. Extremely kind and patient. Uses gentle manipulation to guide Krische. Acts as Krische's primary teacher, surrogate mother/older sister figure. Secretly fearful of high society due to traumatic past.
- **Background:** As a child, her noble family went bankrupt. She and her sister were sent to the capital to become mistresses. Bogan bought her sister Lazura and married her. Has a weak constitution (cannot bear children). Has hated Roland (the merchant) since childhood.
- **Relationship with Krische:** Deeply affectionate, complex; Krische treats kissing as a normal expression of love (taught this by Bery inadvertently). Bery is "soft" on Krische and cannot bring herself to correct the habit fully.
- **Magic:** Can use physical enhancement; Krische teaches her the new magic-without-crystals technique

### Selene Christand (セレーヌ)
- **Full name:** Selene (name means "full moon" in old western language)
- **Role:** Bogan's daughter, Christand family heir; Krische's "older sister" figure
- **Appearance:** Golden/blonde hair, sharp cat-like eyes, dignified powerful beauty
- **Personality:** Serious, hardworking, ambitious. Loves the sword. Deeply protective of Krische. Called "sword maiden" by soldiers. Rivals Krische but dotes on her like a sister. Acts as a social buffer between Krische and the world.
- **Background:** Mother (Lazura) died after a failed second pregnancy. This caused Selene to drive herself harder. Bogan took Krische in partly because Bery hoped it would help Selene relax — it worked.
- **Military role:** Adjutant to Bogan; later First Corps Commander after Nozan's reassignment; commands the special assault squad that includes Krische
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (Rinea)

### Bogan Christand (ボーガン＝クリシュタンド)
- **Full name:** Bogan Argalitte Vezrinea Christand
- **Title:** Margrave; Northern General of the Alberan Kingdom
- **Nickname:** "Lightning Quick" (迅雷, Jinrai)
- **Appearance:** Blonde hair with white streaks, combed back; beard; muscular; countless scars; imposing but eyes soften with family
- **Personality:** Pragmatic, humble for a general. Invests personal wealth in his army. Unusual among nobles for his fair treatment of commoners. Honest and loyal.
- **Crest:** Falcon and lightning bolt
- **Background:** Born low-ranking noble; rose through merit. Gildanstein promoted him to general. Widower (wife Lazura died). Adopted Krische on Gallen's recommendation.
- **Relationship with Gildanstein:** Former comrade, complex respect; received warning from Gildanstein about Kreschenta

### Gallen (ガレン)
- **Role:** Krische's maternal grandfather (Grace's father); later Bogan's adjutant
- **Background:** Former centurion; legendary hero who served 7 campaigns. Retired but returned to army to support Bogan. Famous among soldiers.
- **Personality:** Gruff but caring. The one who arranged Krische's placement with Bogan.
- **Rank:** Senior Knight (promoted during decorations ceremony)

### Kreschenta Alberan (クレシェンタ＝ファルナ＝ヴィエラ＝アルベラン)
- **Full name:** Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan
- **Title:** First Princess of the Alberan Kingdom
- **Appearance:** Strawberry-blonde/golden hair with red tinge; face nearly identical to Krische's; small, younger by several years; fairy-like
- **Name meaning:** "Crescent moon" in old language
- **Personality:** Same type as Krische — no empathy, thinks in pure gain/loss, socially abnormal but has learned to act perfectly. Far better at talking and social manipulation than Krische. Cold and calculating but seeks the same "relaxation" Krische found with Bery/Selene. Calls Krische "onee-sama."
- **Background:** Also a "cursed child" (did not cry at birth). Nora taught her to cry and saved her life. She has killed multiple people (younger princes, others) to secure her position. Poisoned the king (her father). Arranged to flee to Christand to start the civil war in her favor.
- **Relationship with Krische:** Genuine sisterly affection, rare in her life. Accepts Bery's authority over her reluctantly.

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS

### Grace (グレイス)
- Krische's foster mother. Beautiful with black hair and freckles. Kind, clumsy, scatterbrain but deeply loving. Killed by Gado during the bandit attack. Her death is Krische's greatest "failure."

### Gorka (ゴルカ)
- Krische's foster father. Skilled young hunter, village leader. Killed by Gado (traitor) during bandit attack.

### Galla (ガラ)
- Widow in Karka Village; her son was one of the two children Krische killed (Krische killed him with a cliff/boar trap). Galla never learns this. She loves Krische deeply and owns the oven Krische borrows for cooking. Key figure in Krische's village life.

### Nora (ノーラ)
- Kreschenta's royal servant. Previously cared for baby Krische in the tower before abandoning her in the forest. Taught Kreschenta to cry and kept her alive. Helped Kreschenta with her killings. Died by suicide (stabbed herself) on the journey to Christand estate, to atone for her sins.

### Anne (アン)
- Young royal servant with black hair in a bun assigned to Krische/Bery during their capital visit. Clumsy, earnest, easily distracted by her imagination. Comic-relief character. Misinterprets the Krische-Bery relationship as a devoted servant caring for a sickly mistress.

### Garo/Galo (ガロ)
- Former soldier, village guard in Karka. Sexually harassed Krische. She kills him in Chapter 1 — her first adult kill, using a wooden sword and a pre-dug grave.

### Walter Del Grizlandy (ウォルター＝デル＝グリズランディ)
- Empire general Aleha's aide. Old, experienced warrior. Fights valiantly on the battlefield but is defeated and captured by Krische (loses an arm). Has respectful final exchange with Krische. Died in the rear (implied) covering Aleha's retreat. Aleha swears to avenge him.

### Aleha Sarshenka (アレハ＝クラウゼラ＝シュインデル＝サルシェンカ)
- General of the Holy Elsren Empire's northern invasion force. Talented, rose through merit like Bogan. Deeply respects Bogan as an adversary. Defeated at the Battle of Arzlen. Awarded title of "paladin" (Shuindel) by the emperor.

### Gildanstein (ギルダンシュタイン)
- **Full name:** Gildanstein Kalnaros Vel Sarcarinea Alberan
- Royal Prince of Alberan, first in line before Kreschenta's appointment. Infamous for cruel entertainment with slaves and abusing his authority. Extremely pragmatic and capable on the battlefield. Warned Bogan about Kreschenta. Antagonist of the civil war arc.

### Dagren Garhka (ダグレン＝ガーフカ)
- Southern General of Alberan. Duke. Bear-like, loud, battle-hungry but competent. Held the south during the Empire invasion.

### Kokys Agrand (コキス＝ナクトラ＝リネア＝アグランド)
- Second Corps Commander. Enormous, muscular, tiger-themed armor. Loud voice. Excellent at direct assault and defense.

### Nozan Velraigh (ノザン＝ヴェルレイ)
- First Corps Commander; leads the "Wolf Pack" (first battalion, all career soldiers). Reassigned east as new general after the war.

### Elrouga (エルルガ)
- Fourth Corps Commander. Skeletal appearance. Skilled at difficult tactical movements. Dotes on his son.

### Terious (テリアス)
- Third Corps Commander. Eagle-eyed. Specializes in defense and fortification.

### Dagra (ダグラ)
- Centurion; Krische's assistant during recruitment. Hook-nosed, shaved head. Witnessed Krische's mountain infiltration firsthand. Deeply afraid of her but devoted.

### Pell (ペル)
- Young boy in Karka Village whom Krische trained in swordsmanship.

### Zaal (ザール)
- Former soldier, sword instructor in Karka Village. Second-best swordsman in the village after Gallen. Krische's sparring partner; she secretly counts how many times she could "kill" him per session. Killed by the peddler-bandit leader during the village attack.

### Roland (ローランド)
- Fat, gaudy merchant. Connected to Bery's traumatic past — was involved in arranging Lazura and Bery's bondage situation. Krische nearly kills him during their capital journey but is stopped by Bery.

## FACTIONS AND POLITICAL LANDSCAPE

**Alberan Kingdom** — Absolute monarchy with strong noble class. Women can inherit the crown. King has absolute power but is constrained by noble consensus.

**Royal Prince Faction (Gildanstein)** — Gildanstein claims legitimate right to throne by succession rules; controls the capital.

**Princess Faction (Kreschenta)** — Kreschenta appointed heir by the dying king; sheltered by Bogan. Claims Gildanstein assassinated the king.

**Holy Elsren Empire** — Eastern major power. Invaded Alberan during succession crisis. Uses "Holy War" declarations to mobilize nobles. Strong cavalry (nomadic cavalry included). Crude logistics (pillaging-based). Defeated at Battle of Arzlen.

**Arna Empire** — Northern ally of Alberan; provided offers of reinforcement during Empire invasion.

**Galshan Republic** — Southern neighbor; historically at war with Alberan. Did not intervene during Empire invasion (was at war with Elderant).

**Elderant Kingdom** — Western neighbor; at war with Galshan during events of the story.

## KEY TERMS AND CONCEPTS

### Magic System
- **Mana (魔力)** — Called "the floofy thing" (ふわふわしたもの) by Krische. An internal energy that nobles and some commoners possess.
- **Physical Enhancement (身体強化)** — Using mana as "imaginary muscles" to control the body. The standard combat magic for nobles. Krische perfected it naturally from early childhood; her version is uniquely efficient.
- **Magic Crystals (魔石, Maseki)** — Crystallized mana from underground; used as medium for all standard magic (heating, cooling, lighting etc.). Circuits are carved into them.
- **Magic Technicians** — Carve circuits into magic crystals.
- **Magicians** — Research and develop those circuits.
- **Eternal Magic Lamps (常魔灯, Joumatou)** — Mana-powered lights used in estates; alternative to candles.
- **Krische's Revolutionary Technique** — Creating magic circuits within one's own mana, enabling magic without crystals. Can be used for fire, water, heat. Kept secret at Bery's request due to potential for war.

### Military Structure
- **General (将軍)** — Highest field rank. Bogan holds this for the north.
- **Corps Commander** — Commands 5 battalions (~5,000 men)
- **Battalion Commander** — Commands 10 centurions (~1,000 men)
- **Centurion (百人長)** — Commands 100 soldiers
- **Captain** — Commands 10 corporals (~50 men)
- **Corporal** — Commands a squad of 5
- **Strategic Command** — Unit Bogan is developing; Krische is being trained for this

### Titles/Ranks (Nobility)
- **Archduke** — Royal family (king's brothers/close relatives)
- **Duke** — Distant royals; Dagren is a duke
- **Margrave** — Highest normal noble rank; typically border generals. Bogan holds this.
- **Earl, Baron** — Lower noble ranks. Bery's family (Argan) was baron.
- **Senior Knight (上騎士, Rinea)** — Honorary title for battlefield achievement; not inheritable, no land. The title includes the honorific "Rinea" (meaning "warrior" in old language). Krische, Selene, and Gallen receive this.
- **Junior Knight (下騎士)** — Lower knight tier; can be granted by generals with royal permission. Gallen had this before.

### Social/Cultural Terms
- **Cursed Child (呪われた子)** — Royal family tradition: a baby who does not cry at birth is considered cursed and killed. Both Krische and Kreschenta were designated this.
- **King's Road (王道)** — Red carpet in throne room; only royals normally walk it
- **King's Fingers (王の指)** — Five main roads to the capital; towns along them receive tax benefits in exchange for hosting traveling nobles
- **Forged Stone (鍛石)** — Durable construction material made from volcanic ash; used throughout the capital
- **Sacred Convention (聖典協定)** — International agreement governing treatment of prisoners of war, acceptable conduct, etc.
- **Holy War (聖戦)** — Empire's Curia-declared wars; mobilizes nobles by promising plunder and land
- **Salute** — Right palm to left chest = "I offer my life to you"; Superior replies with right thumb pointing at heart = "Your loyalty will be my flesh and blood"

### Food/Cooking Terms
- **Pumpkin (カボチャ)** — Krische's favorite ingredient. A recurring motif representing her simple happiness and desires.
- **Rakura (ラクーラ)** — Sweet red fruit, crunchy, very juicy (like strawberry/apple hybrid). Mentioned in shopping scene.
- **Nirkana fruit (ニルカナ)** — Small sweet fruit like strawberries; Krische feeds some to Bery in the market scene.
- **Black soy tea (黒豆茶)** — Bitter drink on the battlefield; Krische adds excessive honey and milk.
- **Graberas / Almarei** — Types of tea leaves; Graberas is refreshing and best straight or with lemon.

## TRANSLATION CONVENTIONS (Established in Chapters 1–41)

### Character Speech Patterns
- **Krische** — Always refers to herself as "Krische" not "I/me." Speaks in complete, formal sentences. Tends toward literal interpretation. When confused, says things like "Nn…" or "Err…" Use of ellipses (……) common.
- **Bery** — Gentle, eloquent. Uses "Krische-sama," "ojou-sama" for Selene. Occasionally formal even with close people.
- **Selene** — Confident, occasionally curt. Uses "Krische" without honorific. In private can be warm; in public is formal.
- **Kreschenta** — Extremely composed and articulate for her age. Cold eyes described. Refers to Krische as "onee-sama." In private, shows more genuine emotion.
- **Bogan** — Even, measured. Military directness.
- **Gallen** — Gruff, few words, authoritative.

### Recurring Phrases/Expressions
- **"Ehehe"** — Krische's signature soft laugh/giggle. Keep as "Ehehe" consistently.
- **"Floofy thing" / "floof"** — Translation for Krische's term for mana (ふわふわしたもの). Keep this as an established quirk.
- **"Taste-testing" (味見)** — Krische's justification for eating while cooking. Should always feel like a self-aware excuse.
- **"Kaa-sama / Tou-sama / Ojii-sama"** — Mother / Father / Grandfather. The English translation keeps these as "mother/father/grandfather" or uses the Japanese titles with a note where needed.
- **"Oba-san"** — Auntie; used for Galla and sometimes Bery's predecessor figure. Translated as "Auntie" or "oba-san."
- **"Onee-sama"** — Older sister (honorific). Kreschenta uses this for Krische.
- **"Ojou-sama"** — Young lady of the house; Bery's term for Selene.
- **Cheek-pinching** — Selene's habit with Krische; when Krische's cheeks are being pinched mid-speech, the translator rendered garbled speech as phonetics (e.g., "Fue, fuerere, ihyai……"). Keep this convention.

### Notes on Names
- **Krische** — Means "incomplete/crescent moon" in an old barbarian language. Grace named her after the moon on the night Gorka proposed to her.
- **Selene** — Old western word for "full moon"
- **Kreschenta** — Old western word meaning "crescent moon" (same concept as Krische, different language)
- **Bery (ベリー)** — Short for Alberinea? No — Bery is her given name. "Beri" is Krische's nickname for Bery; the established English translation uses "Bery" for the full name.
- **Alberan (アルベラン)** — The Kingdom's name; derived from the god/founder "Alberan"
- **Rinea (リネア)** — Old language word meaning "warrior"; the honorific title granted to Senior Knights

## RECURRING THEMES AND MOTIFS

1. **Pumpkin** — Symbol of Krische's simple contentment. Whenever pumpkin plans are foiled it represents the chaos intruding on her domestic peace.
2. **Kissing as expression of love** — Bery inadvertently taught Krische this; becomes a running source of comedy and tenderness.
3. **Cheek-pinching** — Selene's primary expression of affection/exasperation with Krische.
4. **Head-patting** — Universal comfort gesture. Krische loves receiving it. She instinctively extends it to Kreschenta.
5. **"A shame" (残念)** — Krische's substitute for "sad." Bery corrects her on this. Kreschenta uses the same word by C.38–39, showing their similarity.
6. **Sleeping together** — Represents safety and trust; Krische needs it for warmth and comfort; Kreschenta finds peace in it for the first time.
7. **Rules vs. True Nature** — Krische follows rules when observed; breaks them privately. The tension between her abnormal inner logic and social requirements runs throughout.
8. **"Gains and losses" (損得)** — Krische's entire worldview. She expresses love through this framework until Bery slowly teaches her what love actually is.
9. **The birdcage metaphor** — Introduced by Kreschenta: the estate/household = a safe "birdcage" they protect together.

## TRANSLATOR NOTES FROM ORIGINAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

- The original English translation (by two translators, with permission from author ひふみしごろ) notes that **Krische speaks in very polite Japanese (desu/masu)** consistently.
- The cheek-pinching garbled speech was deliberately not translated phonetically in the original (noted: "The original Japanese is cuter than any garbled English I could come up with").
- The translator confirmed that **Garo was technically still alive (paralyzed) when buried** — not dead at the time of burial.
- "Krische" pronunciation note from translator: "Like Cliché. But r. I'm assuming no one pronounces the second c in cliche as a c."
- The corp commander Salva in C.12 is confirmed as a **corps commander in charge of 500,000 (five hundred thousand)** — translator noted this was corrected from earlier version.
- Curved swords changed from "sabre" to "curved sword" by translator in edit — the original Japanese uses 湾曲した剣 (curved sword), not a specific type.
- Ancient Japanese measurement units used in C.41 (寸/尺/間) were translated as approximate English equivalents (inch/feet/2 yards) by translator for readability.
