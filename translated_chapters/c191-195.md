# Chapter 191: Light and Shadow

A phased retreat——it was clear the enemy's objective was to delay their advance.

The forested western Alberan favored the defenders.

And yet, even so, Alberan's soldiers were strong.

The Alberan army maneuvered broadly across the battlefield corps by corps, weathering their offensive with small numbers while exploiting any opening to devour vulnerable points.

Against their combined force of 120,000, the enemy numbered just over 60,000——yet the invaders were inferior in coordination.

This must be what a fully organized army looked like.

The old general clad in dragon-wrought armor——a man said to be over a hundred years of age——was, however, still razor-sharp in his reactions.

He deflected Red's full-power blow from horseback with his lance, and did not so much as flinch afterward.

"Tch……!"

"Ha ha ha, you've got good eyes for a youngster. Good form, too. But……that level of skill won't take this old man's head."

The venerable general, Felwors Keesliton.

A renowned commander who had ravaged countless battlefields decades ago, one of Alberan's proudest.

His glittering war record was known even to Red, born in the Elderant Kingdom.

This masterful phased retreat was exactly what one would expect from such a man.

Making it appear as though he was falling back, yet should one advance carelessly, the trap would spring into an encirclement of annihilation——Red had only reached this man by abandoning his comrades as decoys.

And despite his age, with the technique and raw strength to deflect Red's greatsword, the venerable general stood before Red like a blade that had never rusted.

"Red, time's up! At this rate they'll encircle us from behind!!"

"Damn……!"

A forced breakthrough to slay the enemy commander.

His window had been brief——but since he had failed to finish the job, this round was Red's loss.

War-lust bleeding through his still-youthful face, Red glared at Felwors.

"I will take your head——this I swear, Red of the Lani! Don't you forget it!"

"Spirited, aren't you. ……That said, Faren is a far more fearsome man than I am. If you have time for parting threats, you'd best be running."

"You bastard……Bez! We're pulling back! Going to pick up Zren!"

"Ou!"

The young man shouted to his subordinate and wheeled around——turning his back, and Felwors's adjutant, the old soldier Tolka, shouted as well, ordering cavalry to pursue them.

Then Tolka looked at Felwors.

"Quite the promising youngster, sir. He'll become a general of note someday. I'd like to dispose of him here, but……"

"The men he leads are rather formidable, too. That young man will survive."

Saying this, Felwors broke into a coughing fit, and Tolka reflexively steadied him.

Felwors smiled wryly and said he was fine.

"I went to the trouble of beating this rusted body back into shape, but……age catches up. To be winded by that much. The lance feels terribly heavy, too."

"Felwors-sama……"

"Don't make that face. It's nothing serious. Faren is giving it his all out there——as his predecessor, I must set a proper example."

He laughed with evident enjoyment, listening to the music of the battlefield, and narrowed his eyes.

A world in which he had spent most of his youth.

The sparks born when life clashed with life, and what rose from those collisions.

What came to mind were the countless battlefields he had fought through long ago.

"And surely, His Majesty is watching me even now."

Recalling the face of the Warrior-King Albaza, whom he had served, Felwors let a deep smile settle into his wrinkled features.

* * *

The Elderant Kingdom had divided its army broadly into three.

North, west, south——a three-pronged exterior operation. While there was strategic intent behind it, the reason for this arrangement was not solely the strategic goal of destroying Alberan's western army.

The Elderant Kingdom was a nation composed of twenty-three tribes.

Its structure was something close to what one might call feudalism.

Each tribe governed its own recognized territory, and the chieftain of the strongest tribe in a given era became the next king, binding them all together.

Any of the twenty-three tribes could, in principle, claim the right to the throne if they demonstrated sufficient martial prowess, but in practice, the power dynamics between tribes created a hierarchy, and at present, only three tribes had any real prospect of ruling Elderant.

The great tribe Veze, led by the current King Gorkista.

The great tribe Mekrea, which had held the throne for five successive generations before Gorkista seized power.

And the third, Akaz, which had seen rapid growth in recent times.

In a sense, this war could be called a battle to determine the next king, and through the distribution of military merit and conquered land, the king would be chosen from among these three tribes.

It was inevitable that they would never march in step——splitting the army into three was a product of such circumstances.

West——the center: Veze.

South——the right wing: Mekrea.

North——the left wing: Akaz.

And Lani, Red's tribe, fell under Veze's central command.

Having concluded the day's battle, Red was drenched in sweat, swinging his greatsword while everyone else reveled in celebration.

"Kh……"

"Your movements are slow, Red. That's why you came back without taking the enemy commander's head."

"I know, dammit!!"

Facing him was a woman of striking beauty with flaxen hair.

Trousers tapered below the knee with a shirt and cloak.

Nothing particularly ornate, yet the beautiful woman deftly manipulated an elegant, slender straight sword, deflecting Red's blade——one that could splinter a great tree——again and again.

"……Got y——geh!?"

Seeing an opening, Red released his greatsword and launched a side kick——but the woman read it cleanly, dodged, seized his arm, and slammed him into the earth.

"There, Red loses. Stop trying to rush to a finish like that."

"Dammit……how are you that strong……"

"It's not 'you,' it's 'Shelna-sama,' loser Red."

Puffing out her modest chest with a grin, the petite woman——Shelna Veze——extended her hand.

Red grasped it with a sour expression, pulled himself up, and scratched vigorously at his short black hair.

"You rely too much on raw power. You lack composure, there's too much wasted motion, and your footwork is hopeless. Be grateful Keesliton didn't kill you."

"Gh……"

"Ha ha ha, Red can't win against Shelna-sama no matter what, huh."

The rugged-faced large man laughed, and Red glared at him.

"Shut it, Zren. Who do you think saved your ass?"

"Oi oi, and you think you remember that we played decoy for you? Coming back as a loser, pathetic. Should've left it to me, I'd have taken his head."

"Hah, as if you could beat someone I couldn't."

"Oh, you said it now, you bastard? Right then, I'll take you on——"

"Stop it. Don't waste your energy."

Shelna said with an exasperated look and a wry smile.

"But, well, if Red gets down on his hands and knees and begs, maybe I'll go with you next time?"

"Don't be an ass. Next time I'll get it done for sure. And what's the supreme commander doing going to the front line?"

Shelna Veze was the supreme commander of this army, in place of her father the king, who had fallen ill.

Going to the front line was madness.

"Mm……I do think that'd be the quickest way, though."

"Don't say that seriously, it pisses me off."

But Shelna's deep, indigo eyes.

Her words held no lie; she truly believed it.

The girl was only twenty, yet she was an anomaly——known as Veze's Sword Princess, possessed of genius-level swordsmanship.

In the recent battle against Galshan, she had effortlessly slain a war-lion commander.

Red, whose skill was exceptional for his twenty-two years, had never won even a single bout against her in sparring.

Her depths remained unplumbed——she was, truly, what one would call a genius.

Unlike Red, who was devoted to training, she would go an entire month without picking up a sword if there was no need.

Yet once she did, she never lost——Felwors Keesliton was certainly a formidable warrior, but Red could not imagine him defeating her.

"You're kind of unfair, you know that?"

"What, are you sulking?"

Shelna brought her beautiful face close and, smiling, ruffled Red's hair.

"You are getting stronger, Red. Properly. Still a long way to go, though."

"Shut up."

He grasped her hand with a sigh and looked east.

"More importantly, what's the plan from tomorrow? They've pulled back quite a bit."

"……Right. I suppose we tighten the distance while staying alert. Ideally Mekrea or Akaz will jump the gun before things get bogged down. Well, I suppose Veze's Hunting Party gets a little break."

"A break, huh……doesn't feel right."

"Breaks are important. ……We took fair losses, so I think we need time to settle down."

Shelna said quietly, looking at the men making merry around them.

A forced breakthrough, the enemy's counterattack——of the 3,000 soldiers in Shelna's personal guard, the Hunting Party of Veze led by Red, one-fifth had been killed or wounded.

Thanks to Red having slain an enemy corps commander in the opening battle, they maintained high spirits while drinking, but the failure today had surely taken some toll.

Red nodded at Shelna's words and answered, yeah.

"Zren, sorry but……"

"Yeah, I'll keep an eye on things. ……Shelna-sama, with you here the men won't be able to complain. Best call it a night."

"……Understood. I'll leave it to you."

Saying this, Shelna gestured to Red with her eyes and began walking toward her own tent, a short distance away.

Red followed in silence and entered her tent.

"……Haah."

Shelna collapsed face-first onto the bed as if leaping onto it, and sighed.

Red scratched his head and, without a word, brewed tea.

"Did the elders say something to you?"

"A bit, yes. They insisted I push harder to take the enemy commander's head, even if it means taking risks. Jiegret-sama did cover for me, saying Veze already holds the top merit, but."

"War merits……huh. Sorry about that."

"I know you're working harder than anyone. For the sake of making me your wife, right?"

"……Give me a break."

Shelna laughed with evident enjoyment and rolled onto her side.

Like this, when it was just the two of them, she was a little childlike——that had never changed since long ago.

Princess Shelna of Veze and Red had been something like childhood friends, owing to the relationship between their parents.

And though there was a gap in status between Veze and Lani——born of the power dynamics between tribes——if Red earned enough merit in this war, he would be permitted to marry Shelna as her groom.

In other words, they were betrothed.

"……If Veze wins a great victory in this war, unification of Elderant comes into view. I understand how the high priests feel."

"Unification of Elderant……can it really be done?"

"It can. ……You'll help too, won't you?"

Sitting up to accept the tea from Red, Shelna smiled.

The strong shall be king——Elderant, bound by that ancient custom.

Every time a king died, fighting erupted among kin, and the nation never stabilized——even Veze and Lani, who now held a pact, had been enemies two generations prior.

Elderant needed to be reborn.

To destroy everything with overwhelming force and rebuild as one great nation.

"Once we've defeated Alberan, I'll make the next civil war the last. ……Many people will die, I'm sure, but I don't care what anyone says. If it means peace decades from now."

"Civil war……huh."

"Yes. Having second thoughts?"

"……No. Just remembering."

Both Shelna and Red had experienced two civil wars.

The first, in childhood——the war in which Shelna's father Gorkista won the throne.

Many of the adults around them died. Villages were burned in the crossfire, and Elderant was engulfed in voices of grief.

Five years ago had been more of a skirmish.

Shelna had been nearly abducted during an inspection of another tribe's territory, and though it was called minor, much blood was spilled.

Red's first battle was that war, serving as Shelna's escort——he had killed dozens.

Seeing for the first time the reality of villages put to the torch, he vomited again and again.

The dark shadow, like filthy sludge, that accompanied war's glory dressed up as valor.

The sight of what human malice had wrought was still deeply engraved in Red's memory.

In this war, too, he had witnessed the burning of an Alberan village whose residents had failed to evacuate in time.

He had the commanding officer punished, and yet——the eyes of the women and children, the village's scant survivors, he could not forget.

There had been pushback within the army over Shelna's decision.

*What good are pretty words on the battlefield*, they said.

Voices claiming that a woman like Shelna was unfit to serve as supreme commander.

"……Which is it, I wonder. Humans, I mean."

"What's this, all of a sudden?"

"……Clashing pride against pride, staking everything you've built, betting the one life each of us has——I'll be honest, there's something captivating about it. But in the end, it's just murder. You're forcing someone to submit through violence, taking from them."

As a child he had admired it innocently, and that feeling still lingered.

"Killing, trampling others underfoot……sometimes I think that's what humans are. Dressing it up in pretty words, imposing rules that make yourself feel good, looking down on whoever you beat. How am I any different from the scum who take pleasure in burning villages?"

No matter what words one used, killing was an atrocity.

Unreasonable, absurd, beyond all debate.

After the elation of the battlefield, once sanity returned, those were the thoughts that came.

"Who knows. Lofty questions like whether humans are good or evil——I couldn't tell you. What trivial things you worry about."

"Oh come on, I'm being serious here."

"The thing I consider most useless in this world is probably the thinking Red's idiot brain produces."

"……Listen here."

Shelna smiled, stood up.

Then she stroked Red's cheek.

"What's 'right'? What's 'good and evil'? In the end, it's all just word games. Everyone's different."

Deep indigo eyes framed by long lashes.

Shelna was always beautiful.

"For example——I'd be perfectly satisfied if I could make one stupid, slovenly Red happy. But Red's an idiot, so he wouldn't be happy being the only one, and so, as an extra, I'll build a country where other people can be happy too."

"…………"

"When your mother died, Red, you told me you'd make me happy. So I'll make you happy, too. I think that's what humans are, and that's what a good society is, don't you?"

Red scratched vigorously at his head and looked away.

"……You know, you look smart but you're actually an idiot."

"You're the idiot. It's precisely because I'm smart that I don't waste time on pointless thinking. ……But, hehe, I don't dislike that stupid side of you."

Shelna pressed her lips to his and pulled his head into an embrace.

"Once the war is over, once the civil war is over, I'll listen to all of it for as long as you want. So before you start thinking about things like that, think about yourself first——about coming back alive. You're still sooo weak, I worry."

"……Shut up."

"Hehe, you're blushing, aren't you."

"I'm not blushing."

Shelna laughed with evident enjoyment and pulled away, looking happy.

Red averted his eyes, cheeks tinged red.

"……Just a little more. Let's keep at it, shall we?"

"……Yeah."

Still, they bumped fists together.

——In the mottled forests of the kingdom's southwest.

With one central general and reinforcements from Elrouga, Felwors's phased retreat appeared to be proceeding well.

Having eliminated scouts and based on reports from the griffin cavalry sent ahead for reconnaissance, their movements had not deviated significantly from expectations, and Krische found this satisfactory.

"……Here it is."

"A recently established village, perhaps……it's not in any royal records."

The sun was still high in the sky. Guided by Vinsreel, Krische surveyed the burned village and narrowed her eyes.

The objective was a strategic surprise attack.

Krische, the griffin cavalry, and the Black Flag Special Service alone had advanced ahead——the purpose was route selection to ensure the 15,000 trailing behind would not be caught by enemy reconnaissance.

They were at a considerable distance from the Elderant right wing deployed to the south, and so far things had gone smoothly.

However, what the griffin cavalry, sent ahead of the Black Flag Special Service, had discovered was a small village not shown on any map.

It had already been burned in places, with houses and fences collapsed——it no longer functioned as a village.

"The evacuation wagons must not have reached here. The smell is terrible."

"……Shall we check?"

"Yes. Vulture, have some of our men watch the perimeter."

"Yes, ma'am. Korintz, cover north and east. Tagel, watch the west."

Dagra barked orders with a grim face.

Kalua furrowed her brow at the stench, and Mia was already letting her gaze wander, likely having imagined what awaited.

Even from outside the village, bodies hung from pillars and trees were visible.

Advancing through what had been the entrance, the situation only worsened.

Heads tossed about carelessly, heads arranged in rows.

Children were mixed among the corpses without exception, and the clothing had been stripped from the bodies of young women.

Scattering the wolves and crows that were feeding on the dead as they advanced, Krische leapt down from the jade tiger——Gururun.

In the center of the square, the body of an old man impaled from buttocks to mouth on a wooden stake, displayed as if crucified.

Krische stared at it, folded her arms with utter composure.

The sight was so horrifying that nearly everyone else looked away.

"They don't appear to have been dead for very long."

"Indeed. Perhaps two days. The decomposition is minimal……it may have been cold. ……Gururun, you're not allowed to eat these, understood?"

She addressed Gururun, who was eyeing the bodies, while nodding at Aleha's words beside her.

There was some odor of decay, but the flesh of the corpses could still be called fresh.

"Vulture, the bodies?"

"So far……approximately sixty. Given the village's size, the population was likely no more than just under two hundred. We've also found three Elderant soldiers' bodies."

"That makes it rather one-sided, then. Judging by the signs of combat."

There were corpses of what appeared to be a simply armed militia.

This had not been a complete surprise——they must have been on guard.

Several bodies around a crude watchtower indicated they had at least put up some resistance.

The attackers had been a reasonably organized group.

"The enemy right wing's main camp is far from here, so this was likely the work of an independent detachment?"

"There's a possibility they're not far off. ……Whether they're merely deserters or on a scouting mission, I can't say."

"Either way, it's a nuisance, so we'll kill them all."

Krische declared coolly.

Aleha turned his gaze to Krische.

"Once the militia was lost, the village would have had no will to fight. Given the state of things, what happened here was a massacre. How convenient——if we find them, we can torture them for information without worrying about the Sacred Convention."

Ultimately, the allowance for pillaging and violation of occupied villages existed only as an unspoken understanding.

No law anywhere sanctioned it, and harming unarmed people was, at least on paper, a violation of the Sacred Convention, which professed humanitarian principles.

Armies were, in principle, meant to fight other armies.

The moment they burned a village and committed massacre, there was no obligation to treat them as prisoners of war; their handling would be the same as for brigands——no restraint necessary.

There was no righteous indignation in Krische's face; if anything, a slight smile.

Aleha felt a shiver run through his own spine, smiled wryly, and shook his head.

The others were much the same, saying nothing.

From the start, this girl was that sort of creature.

"Vinsreel, have the griffin cavalry take to the skies once night falls. Given this state of things, they'll probably be making generous use of fire not too far from here."

"……Understood."

"Vulture, it smells here, so let everyone rest upwind. If they're close, we'll deal with them during the night."

"Understood. ……Krische-sama."

"……?"

Krische turned her gaze to Dagra.

"If possible, I would like permission to bury them. ……That is, corpses are a breeding ground for illness——I would like to dispose of them while decomposition is still minimal, so as not to adversely affect the army following behind."

"Mm……Krische doesn't really want to tire the soldiers, but."

Krische pondered with a troubled look, and Kalua approached and hugged her from behind.

"Bunny, the enemy's a small force, right? It's fine, it's fine. Everyone's just been walking lately and getting stiff, we need to move a bit before combat."

"Muu……I see."

Understanding Dagra's intent, Kalua spoke up, and Krische turned her gaze to Mia, who looked on the verge of tears.

"……Does Mia agree?"

"……Yes. Leaving them like this, in such a terrible state, exposed to the elements——it's just too cruel."

"Aleha?"

"I share Dagra-taichou's opinion. Besides, this carnage may lower the morale of the troops coming up behind us. Mia-fukukan's reaction is an entirely human one……the battlefield may be one thing, but not everyone can regard facts as mere facts the way a career soldier might."

Krische worried a little more, pursed her lips, then pulled away from Kalua and patted Mia on the head.

"Can't be helped, then. You're the adjutant, Mia——you should carry yourself with more composure."

"……I'm sorry."

"Vulture, permission granted. However, no raising great plumes of smoke. Burial only."

"Yes, ma'am, much obliged. ……Koza, inform the men that by Krische-sama's order, burial will be carried out. Tell Tagel's group to dispatch several squads as well."

It was a matter of morale, not practical necessity.

Few soldiers would welcome the task of handling corpses.

Krische's opinion was sound, and simply leaving them was entirely viable.

But Dagra understood Krische, and precisely because he was devoted to her, he gave the order.

The glory of the battlefield, and the shadow's filth.

Logic and emotion.

"Mm, well then, Beltz, go ahead and prepare lunch. It's already midday, and everyone will be getting hungry soon."

"Y-yes, ma'am. I was thinking of focusing on vegetables for today's lunch, if that's……"

"……? That's fine with Krische, but……give Gururun some meat as well. If Krische doesn't fill her up, she'll start eating the bodies lying around. Hopefully there are some dead livestock about……"

Smoothing out the distortions that rose within her——that was, more than anything, their role.

# Chapter 192: Stillness of the Night

The targets were found quickly.

Some two hundred Elderant soldiers had taken over a dozen women from the village and were making a raucous spectacle of themselves.

Annihilating them with a surprise attack as they caroused in their encampment was a simple matter.

They appeared to have been a unit tasked with monitoring the south, but had apparently assumed there was no chance of Alberan, which was being pressed by Galshan's great army, approaching from the south.

Entirely without vigilance, they had evidently been amusing themselves by raiding travelers and villages——that village was their second.

Western Alberan had long been subject to the ravages of Aulgorn Hilkintos's strategy——essentially, luring the enemy in and counterattacking, which meant the land was routinely devastated.

For years, the situation had continued where people paid taxes yet were abandoned by the military, and there were a fair number who paid no taxes and lived quietly deep in the forests.

That village had likely been one such settlement.

The soldiers hailed from a southeastern tribe within Elderant, and were surprisingly well-informed about conditions in western Alberan——enough to impress even Krische.

Inside a tent, the silver-haired girl held an Elderant soldier's longsword, nodding with an expression of interest as she listened to their words. *I see, I see.*

The language spoken in Elderant carried a strong accent but was fundamentally Western Common.

Going deeper into the west would change matters, but the invasion force was the same, and there was no difficulty in this area.

Krische had studied most languages that existed in written form, but listening comprehension and speaking were difficult, and she was not especially skilled at conversation in the first place.

Western Common being the standard here suited her well, with less chance of misunderstanding.

She had kept roughly ten survivors, selecting those who appeared to be of relatively high rank.

One of them she made the remaining nine watch as she severed every finger, gouged out his eyes, shaved off his nose, and amputated both hands and feet——she did not ask the man, who answered that he would tell them everything, a single question, killing him as an example, and only then began interrogating each one individually.

The individual interrogations were for the purpose of verifying the information.

Military men turned looters and rapists ignoring their duties——it seemed unlikely they would lie under these circumstances, but then again, such reasoning did not apply to Krische.

Krische did not especially understand people who enjoyed killing.

Perhaps they had their own aesthetic, she thought, and in that regard, she was thoroughly *earnest* to the end.

"Please……please, spare me……I've told you everything, everything I know……"

The interrogation finished, the unit's commander——the last one Krische had kept——pleaded.

He had been a warrior chief of a minor tribe, but a proper sort of villain.

At the very least, he was not *fundamentally deranged* in the way Krische was, and he could not comprehend the kind of aberration who would dismantle a human being without so much as a change of expression, savoring a candy all the while.

He had known many a pleasure-killer, but what stood before him now was plainly something else.

"Mm, Krische has heard what she wanted to hear, so you're no longer needed. Krische keeps her promises properly. You answered honestly, so Krische won't do things like cut off your fingers or shave your nose."

"Then……"

Relief washed over the man's face, hope surfacing.

"Vulture, we're done."

"Understood. Take him away."

"Ngh——!?"

Once the gag and blindfold were forced back on the man, two soldiers led him out of the tent.

After that, it was simply a matter of killing him.

Like the men outside digging their own graves, there had never been any intention to let them live.

It was a choice between dying in pain or talking——Krische did not recall promising to spare and release anyone.

Only Dagra, Aleha, and Daglis had stayed to witness the entire process from start to finish.

The others had left partway through Krische's human-dismemberment show, feeling ill.

"Daglis, send the information we've gathered to General Keesliton and Skeleton. Also, tell them that on the night of the new moon, three days from now, Krische will crush the right wing and end this. Unless they tell you otherwise, you can head back to the capital."

"Understood. Then if you'll excuse me."

Daglis——who had become Krische's *torture instructor*——left the tent.

Watching him go, Krische traced her lips with a fingertip.

"The quality of Elderant soldiers is poor. They seem like a gathering of brigands. Are they all like this?"

"Not all of them, I would imagine, but……compared to Alberan's army, the quality is undeniably poor. Roughnecks running wild on animal instinct, using war as license for their desires——they have many such men."

Dagra answered.

The nausea from the earlier display of torture was finally subsiding.

"This, too, is one face of war. In the extreme conditions where death looms near, the true nature usually hidden beneath pretense is laid bare."

"……Is that how it is. Krische knew it in theory, but she's a little surprised. That people can truly enjoy doing such things. If things had gone a little differently, Krische's village might have ended up like that, too……"

Recalling the bandits who had come to Karka, Krische narrowed her eyes.

The gruesome killing of one man had been purely a matter of business.

People who genuinely took pleasure in mutilating human beings——that lay beyond Krische's comprehension.

She knew as a matter of knowledge that war produced pillaging.

The Sacred Convention could be interpreted as conveniently as one wished——if one were so inclined, it was easy enough to massacre an entire village on the pretext that they had shown signs of resistance.

The prohibition on village pillaging was not explicitly stated; what was written was merely the clause that unarmed civilians must not be intentionally harmed or killed.

The argument that villagers resisted during requisition of supplies and were unavoidably killed or executed was one that could hold up, as long as one could logically demonstrate it did not violate the Sacred Convention.

But these men had been caught in the act of abducting and brutalizing women——there was no talking their way out of it, and for Krische, that was nothing but convenient.

Yet what Krische felt upon hearing their stories was bewilderment.

She understood that pillaging of occupied villages would naturally be violent, but even so——their actions were clearly something different.

The killing and violence were not a byproduct of military necessity; they were the objective itself.

She had asked them repeatedly what purpose lay behind such acts, and their answer was entertainment.

The polar opposite of what Krische considered decent——they were an entirely different breed.

"Letting people like that run loose is no good. Thinking about the future, this is a good opportunity——perhaps Krische should clean them out thoroughly after all."

Plainly.

Both men did not mistake the meaning of her words.

They knew that her statement was no figure of speech.

Outside, screams could be heard. The men from earlier were likely being killed.

"A painful thing to hear. In the Holy Empire, this sort of thing was done deliberately."

"Deliberately?"

"When people are satisfied under the rule of law, few will commit crimes. That said, the socially vulnerable still exist in reality. To relieve the frustrations of those who cannot find satisfaction domestically, pillaging in other countries is convenient——that is the reasoning."

Aleha smiled wryly but his eyes did not.

"It also serves to cull malcontents. The promise that invading an enemy nation means free rein on their soil is a good pretext for raising the morale of soldiers who have no legitimate cause to fight. The logic is no different from theirs, really."

He said, turning his gaze toward the tent's exterior.

"……I see."

"The reason Alberan's soldiers appear disciplined and well-controlled is likely due in large part to logistics. Merchants travel with the army, and fulfilling the soldiers' desires is always a priority. ……The presence of many female soldiers may be another factor, but it's a good system."

However one looked at it, Alberan was wealthy and stable.

And precisely because there was wealth, people could remain human.

The monetary cost involved was enormous, but that was surely where Alberan's soldiers found their greatest strength.

By satisfying base, animal desires, they turned people into military components willing to throw their lives away for shapeless dreams and ideals.

Soldiers cradled in shapeless dreams and ideals——that was why they were known as disciplined, powerful troops.

A difference in methodology.

One could not say which was correct, and the ways of Elderant and Elsren were not entirely wrong either, but as for which he preferred——

At the very least, Aleha's preference lay here as well.

"Mm……Selene said something about being careful with that sort of thing, too. To make sure Krische listens to Ojii-sama and Keith and the others' opinions. Krische didn't really understand, so she's left it all to them."

Inference piled upon inference——it was, arguably, nothing more than her delusion.

And this was a war in which defeat had never been contemplated.

"Shelna-sama, this is all so sudden——"

"Be quiet, Karan. ……Shelna, you have some basis for this?"

"Yes. The center alone cannot manage. We must merge with Akaz and, even temporarily, commit to full cooperation. I will take responsibility."

Shelna fixed Jiegret with her indigo gaze.

Jiegret met it and nodded.

He had known her since she was a child.

A genius of rare caliber, possessing an intellect that defied comparison with ordinary people.

Those indigo eyes were the greatest treasure Veze possessed.

He did not understand the reasoning behind her conclusion, but at the very least, she had never been wrong.

There were times when something like divine revelation——a supernatural intuition——seized her.

"As High Priest, I concur. Follow Shelna's orders. I will bear the full responsibility."

"……Yes, sir!"

"Thank you, Jiegret-sama——"

Yet even that brilliance, that divine inspiration, could be called one step too late.

What filled Shelna's eyes was the west——shadows rising from the forest behind them.

"……Griffin cavalry?"

What she saw was thirty griffins.

——In a sky that should have held no moon, the crescent of the Black Flag floated.
