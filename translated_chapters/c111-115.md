# Chapter 111: The Feel of an Ending

---

――Gallen was breathing heavily, and across from him, Salva was doing the same.

Cheers could be heard. Krische must have slain Gildanstein.

Gallen softened his expression and lowered his sword.

"……Any further fighting is pointless. Surrender."

Mia remained on guard, holding the perimeter.

Salva's forces were few, and the outcome was already decided.

There was no means to turn things around, and no reason to.

"……So it would seem."

At those words, Salva lowered his sword.

He removed his helmet, knelt, and placed both hands on his thighs.

"――Regardless, I am not merely a rebel but a traitor. My sentence will be death. ……If it can be granted, I wish to be killed here on the battlefield, as a warrior, by you."

Gallen heard his words and answered simply, "I see."

Then he walked closer.

"To have faced you as my final opponent in my last battle……I consider it an honor. I understand well now why General Christand called you 'captain.'"

Gallen thought of the departed Bogan, then looked at Salva before him, and sighed.

"……Enemy, ally. Differences in conviction aside, good men continue to be lost. Year after year, it piles higher onto my shoulders until I can barely breathe. ……This is why I cannot abide war."

"……I apologize for the trouble."

"No, this too is duty……I suppose this is the fate I was made for."

He took Salva's sword. Gallen's short sword was too light to take a head.

"Do you have any last words?"

"……If possible, let the punishment fall on me alone. My family was merely dragged into this by a foolish father."

"I will do what I can. I will report that your end was one no warrior need be ashamed of."

Salva bowed his head, baring his neck.

"Thank you."

"……Aye."

Gallen raised the sword and brought it down.

The blade passed through the gap between vertebrae, and the head rolled.

A soldier nearby offered a cloth, and Gallen wrapped the head in it.

"……Raise the victory banner. The war is over."

"Sir!"

"The battlefield is settling. Those who are free, begin tending to the wounded. The war is over — we are all subjects of the same kingdom. Friend or foe, save those who can be saved."

Gallen walked over to Mia and placed a hand on her shoulder.

"No matter what anyone says, the greatest distinction in this battle belongs to you and your people. ……You did well. You may rest."

"……Yes, sir."

Mia trembled, but she clenched her fists and endured, then began issuing orders.

* * *

Evening ―― the Keesliton army headquarters.

"Push through, Carlis!! Leave the right flank to Keith's corps — you're behind me!"

"Sir!!"

At Granmeld's voice, the First Battalion carved open a breach.

The enemy headquarters — the final line of defense.

There was nothing left to stop Granmeld after he had broken through the enemy vanguard.

He swung his great iron mace, smashing aside everything in his path.

The wolf pack followed in his wake.

――And then.

"I suppose one should say 'as expected'……against Velraigh's forces, this army was simply at too great a disadvantage. I certainly didn't expect it to fall in less than a day."

"Well, I do feel some pity for you. To have held out this long against my corps with these troops — you have my respect. But……this is war. No excuses. You'll die here."

Facing the wolves was a silver-armored figure bearing a dragon crest.

The old general Felwors Keesliton stroked his long white beard and nodded with a satisfied, composed air.

"Haha, that is indeed the way of a warrior. This contest — your victory."

"Ahh……? ……Does that mean you're surrendering?"

"At the very least, it seems our reason to fight has vanished. Look."

Granmeld furrowed his brow and turned to look at the hill.

The headquarters on the hill — there, three blue flags requesting a cessation of hostilities were raised.

These were used when requesting negotiations under the Sacred Convention — and in this case,

"It seems the Royal Prince has been slain. ……Even with Bogan's loss, Christand's strength endures. It was a precarious battle from the start, but……I see."

The reason for fighting had been extinguished.

The flags were a signal that matters had been settled on Selene's side.

"……Flag-bearer, raise our blue flags as well. We accept the ceasefire."

"Sir!"

Granmeld let out an exasperated sigh and dropped his shoulders.

"Tch, I finally break through to their headquarters and this is how it ends……can't say it feels satisfying."

"Kuku, it seems you were a step too late. This old man's neck must have the goddess's protection."

"Haah……I don't even know what this was all for, honestly. Carlis, make sure none of my men get killed. Things won't settle down for a while."

"Sir. ……What a disaster this turned out to be."

"Yeah, I could just about blame Krische-sama for this one."

And so, the fighting on the Velraigh army's side also came to an end.

The War for the Alberan Crown — with tens of thousands of casualties and many generals lost.

That war concluded with the death of the Royal Prince Gildanstein — a victory for the First Princess, Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan.

The Christand side served wine even to the soldiers of the defeated army, hosting a grand feast.

Enemy or ally, all were subjects of the same kingdom.

Different banners, but no guilt attached — and thus, confusion was kept to a minimum.

The treatment of enemy commanders followed the same principle, with punishment for rebels kept remarkably lenient.

This was presented as the mercy of the Princess — but in truth, the kingdom had already lost too much in the civil war.

It could not afford further losses.

There was not enough strength left to impose proper punishment.

The decision to minimize punishments had been agreed upon in advance, and this followed accordingly.

* * *

"I was wondering where you'd disappeared to……what are you doing, Kalua?"

"Ahh, Mia, well, I kinda wanted to drink quietly, y'know?"

When Mia entered the tent, Kalua was sitting on a barrel serving as a makeshift chair, drinking alone.

She had apparently been at it for quite some time — Kalua's face was red.

Unusually, she was thoroughly drunk.

A feast to celebrate victory and see off the dead.

Tomorrow, the collected bodies would be gathered and burned.

Krische had also attended, and the feast was surprisingly bright — everyone forcing themselves to smile.

The main topics of conversation were the fallen and Krische.

Perhaps out of consideration to keep the mood from growing too somber, everyone praised Krische's exploits.

Having suffered heavy losses, been toyed with by Nakilus, and failed to slay Gildanstein themselves — perhaps it was their own helplessness that made them do so.

Despite having fought through such desperate combat, none among them boasted of their own achievements.

But,

*'Krische only helped out a little at the end. In that situation, at that moment — even without Krische, the situation was in our favor, and that's because of this unit's efforts. You worked hard to fulfill Krische's order to protect Selene.'*

Krische had said such things, and,

*'It's a shame Krische can't say this to the ones who died. They violated Krische's orders by dying, so Krische wanted to both praise and scold them. ……Dummies.'*

She had continued.

Tilting a glass of something closer to juice than wine, her eyes downcast.

The same expressionless face as always — but no one misunderstood the meaning packed into the word "dummies."

Mia, too, only then realized the true meaning of the word "dummy" that Krische had used about her.

Krische had apparently not been feeling well.

Kalua had noticed at some point, looking exasperated as she led Krische away, announcing she would take her to the general's tent — and that had been a while ago.

While Mia was being pressed from all sides by people demanding their reward kisses, Kalua hadn't returned.

Mia had eventually noticed her absence and escaped by going to search for her, returning to the tent.

Kalua poured another drink and downed it.

Several empty bottles were lined up.

She was clearly drinking too much. Mia grabbed a bottle and placed it out of Kalua's reach.

"Hey, what're you doing?"

"You'll ruin your health drinking that much. So I'm confiscating it."

"One day's fine. I just wanna sleep like a log."

"……It's already more than enough."

"It's not enough."

Mia glared at Kalua, then grabbed a bottle and tipped it straight back.

Spilling just a little, she drained the whole thing and slammed the empty bottle down on the barrel serving as a table, right in front of Kalua.

"……That's the last one."

"Haah……what are you getting all worked up about?"

Kalua spoke with exasperation, almost mockingly, and Mia met her gaze.

"Because I'm worried about you, Kalua. That's why I'm worked up."

Kalua froze for a moment, then looked away.

Like a child who'd been scolded.

The usual dynamic, reversed.

"……Talk to me."

She said it on her knees. Kalua said nothing for a while.

The report she had given Mia after the battle had been simple — just a routine status update.

Mia hadn't asked what had happened.

After a while, Kalua gave in and began to speak.

"Adol and Kels went with Keenitz and the others, threw their lives away to buy time against Gildanstein. Told the archers to shoot through them and all. Pretty cool way to go, don't you think?"

She said it in a tone that didn't believe a word of it.

They had been together ever since the squads were formed.

Losing comrades you'd spent all that time with — there was no way anyone could be fine with that.

"It seemed to work, but they couldn't finish him off. He came after us all torn up, and Bagu shielded me when I wasn't watching my surroundings — cut clean in two. ……If he was going to die for me, I wish he'd at least survived and shown me something cool."

A bitter smile, eyes downcast.

"After that, Skeleton Corps Commander showed up, then Agrand Corps Commander……and then bunny killed Gildanstein like it was nothing, and it was over. ……That's everything, I guess."

"……That's not what I want to hear."

"What more is there to hear?"

"What you're holding in, Kalua."

She said it and pulled Kalua's head against her.

A solid swordsman's body. Yet right now it felt somehow slight.

"What? Payback for all those times? You wanna make me cry?"

"I wanna make you cry. Is that weird?"

"……Yeah, it's weird."

With a soft laugh, Kalua wrapped her arms around Mia's back.

"If I'm gonna be comforted, I'd prefer a bit more cushion on the chest. It's kinda flat, so my face hurts a little."

"……Shut up. No complaining."

"……Heh, well, it's Mia, so I guess it can't be helped."

Kalua said it cheerfully, then let out a breath.

"Honestly, I thought I could've done more, so it's frustrating. ……I never imagined we'd end up running from one guy, fifteen against one."

"……I see."

"I knew he was strong. Figured I couldn't beat him alone. ……But we had the general too, and the best of the century……and me. I can't beat bunny, but I thought I could at least do more than that."

She stroked that slightly damaged — but still beautiful — black hair.

Kalua's hands gripped tighter at Mia's back.

"Then bunny showed up, killed Gildanstein like it was nothing. And when it was over, she just smiled like a kid, same as always, and threw her arms around the general. ……She said 'helping out,' but for bunny, it really was that easy."

*She's in bed with a fever now, though*, Kalua added with a laugh.

"We couldn't do a thing, sacrificed our friends just to run, and the guy we ran from — she beat him like it was nothing. Against a monster like that, bunny wins that easily. ……If bunny had been there instead of me, I know nobody would've died."

"……Yeah."

"I kept thinking, what was I even doing? ……Even if I couldn't match bunny, if I'd at least had the strength to stand up to Gildanstein, nobody there would've had to die."

A face pressed against her, and something seeped into her chest.

The trembling shoulders looked small, so Mia held on tight.

"……It's not your fault, Kalua."

"Don't try to comfort——"

"I'm not. It was my mistake."

Mia's voice was hard.

"If I'd sent one more squad to your position, things would've been fine. But I couldn't. ……At the very least, before that big guy — Nakilus, was it — arrived, I should've been able to. If Dagra-san were the captain, he definitely could have. Then even without the corps commander arriving, it might've been over."

"That's just hindsight, isn't it?"

"Yes, hindsight. But the moment things bogged down, I could've made that call. I had some room on my side at the time. My priorities were wrong. In that moment, I should've prioritized the general and you above everything else."

*Got it?* Mia said, and continued.

"I'm the captain. The responsibility for the dead falls entirely on me — the one who gave the orders. ……It is absolutely not the responsibility of a regular soldier like you."

"……Don't say things that aren't like you."

"You're the one who always dumps responsibility on me, Kalua. Because of you, I can't even count how many times I've been smacked on the head by the captain and the corps commander."

Mia laughed and tightened her embrace.

"Only at times like this, you play unfair. You're the one being unlike yourself, Kalua. ……Cause me trouble, dump responsibility on me — that's fine with you, Kalua."

"……Are you some kind of pervert, Mia?"

"Shut up……urgh."

And then the strength left Mia's hands, and she slumped where she knelt.

Her face was bright red, her eyes glistening.

"……This is what happens when a lightweight chugs like that."

"But……"

"Good grief, you."

Kalua wiped her eyes and lifted Mia's body.

She carried her to the simple bed — just a blanket laid on the ground — and set her down gently.

"Who's really the one causing trouble here?"

"……Ehehe, want me to sleep with you?"

Mia said it as she pulled Kalua toward her.

Kalua laughed wryly and stroked her soft chestnut hair.

"Really, Mia, you always get clingy when you're drunk. Onee-san is worried."

"That's not true. ……You sleeping or not?"

"……Fine, fine, I get it."

When Kalua lay down, Mia's cheeks softened into a contented smile.

"……Kalua, making fun of me like that is just right."

"Haah……do you even know what you're saying?"

"Maybe not……hmm……anyway, that's how it should be."

Kalua laughed wryly and pressed a finger to Mia's lips.

*Go to sleep already*, she said with exasperation, and Mia nodded, leaned her face closer, and——

"——!"

"……Goodnight."

She murmured it all slurred and closed her eyes.

Kalua's face went bright red, her hand clasped over her mouth, frozen stiff, glaring at Mia as she began to snore.

She stared at that sleeping face for a while, then sighed.

"……I'll have to make sure Mia never gets alcohol again."

She poked the forehead of the carefree, sleeping Mia, and then Kalua, too, closed her eyes.

――Two days later, Selene's tent.

"Honestly, really. Your fever just broke and you're already at it. ……They're on their way here, so just wait a little longer."

"……But it's been so long since Krische has seen them."

"You're exaggerating. ……It's only been one month."

"One month is a long time."

Krische pouted, and Selene pinched her cheek and pulled.

"……Hmm, you're really going? Leaving poor, busy, overworked me behind, just because you want Bery to spoil you?"

"Ugh……"

"That's right, isn't it. Despite everything you say, you'd rather be with Bery than with me. You actually hate me, don't you, Krische?"

"Th-that's not……"

Her gaze immediately began to wander, and she started to waver.

Selene watched and giggled, then said *just kidding* and patted her head.

"I'm joking. Krische, you've done more than enough. Take care of yourself and go."

"……Selene."

"We'll meet again in the capital. Say hello to Bery and Kreschenta for me — tell them I'm doing well."

"……Yes."

Krische stretched up on her toes and kissed her, then smiled, her cheeks soft.

Selene, too, smiled in return and ran her fingers through that silver hair as if reluctant to let it go.

In truth, she was lonely — but Selene had mountains of work.

She needed to get the palace into some kind of order before Kreschenta arrived.

"Krische has left corps matters with Keith. If anything comes up, Keith can handle most things."

"I see. And what about Elvena?"

"She's preparing the luggage. Krische is planning to give Elvena time off to match the black century's — Kalua's — leave, so please make use of her until then."

The black century had lost nearly half of the forces deployed here.

This had been anticipated from the start, but Krische wanted to give them some leave.

"Once things settle down a bit on my end, I'll go thank them properly. They helped enormously — or rather, without that unit, we would have lost."

"Yes. Even though it was the worst-case scenario Krische had planned for, Krische pushed them too hard. ……Krische wishes she could have done better."

Considering the situation she'd heard about, the black century had performed beyond expectations.

Even so, lives had been lost — familiar faces were gone, and there was a feeling she couldn't quite name.

She could think endlessly about what should have been done differently, but that was all hindsight — and in that situation, such choices would have been gambles.

In the end, the lack of options had forced safe choices, which produced the inevitable casualties.

"Everyone did what they could. At some point, you just have to accept it. The loss of Corps Commander Melkikos hurts as well, though……"

Selene sighed.

The Third Corps had continued its resistance — Krische had believed Terrius was still alive, but he had already been killed.

A corps commander who had long served Christand.

The impact of his death was by no means small.

"……Krische is glad ojii-sama didn't die."

"Truly. Gallen-sama helped enormously too. I just keep being saved by everyone……Have you been to check on him?"

"Yes. Anne is doing her best, more or less."

Gallen had pushed himself too hard, and like Krische, had fallen somewhat ill.

Anne, who had been fired up with unusual enthusiasm, had declared *"Leave it to me!"* and was tending to his every need, stationed by his side ever since.

"She said 'Leave it to me,' but……Krische is a bit worried she might be causing trouble for ojii-sama."

"……Gallen-sama seems to have taken quite a liking to Anne, so I think it's fine."

Gallen saw only the best in Anne — hardworking and earnest in everything — and praised her lavishly.

Rather than Anne being especially capable, it was more that having grown up watching Krische and Bery — who were both impossibly exceptional — the normalcy of a girl like Anne felt endearing by contrast.

Gallen had never been particular about food or drink beyond alcohol, and since Krische's mother Grace had been rather clumsy, his standards for "normal" were quite low.

Naturally, Anne had become very attached to Gallen.

Having grown up watching the servant that was Bery, Selene's eyes were discerning, and she found herself at a loss over Anne in various ways — but if the person in question was satisfied, then that was fine.

"Well, I'm sure it's fine. ……More importantly, you're not planning to walk there, are you?"

"Krische plans to borrow one horse for the luggage, but……Krische has had quite enough of riding horses. It would also be a problem to use a military carriage for personal travel, so……"

"That's exactly why you never get used to it, because you keep avoiding it. I found it painful at first too, but you get used to it."

"……Krische doesn't want to endure the pain even while getting used to it."

"Stubborn child."

She pinched her cheek. *Uuu*, Krische groaned.

For someone entering the military, it was normal to get injured repeatedly during training and develop a tolerance for pain — but Krische never got hurt in training.

That was probably part of it.

Krische never grew accustomed to pain and, despite her endurance, was in fact extremely sensitive to it.

If there was no necessity, she simply refused to tolerate it — stubborn, too — and that was perhaps one of her charming flaws.

"Krische-sama, preparations are complete."

"Ah, yes."

Elvena's voice came from outside the tent.

Along with the snort of a horse, and together with Selene, Krische stepped outside.

And stopped in her tracks.

Leading the horse and saluting was a man she recognized.

The messenger she had rescued the other day.

"…………"

And the well-built horse looked familiar too, somehow.

"Thank you for saving me the other day. I heard you were looking for a horse, so I insisted on this one from the other day. Well, turns out it was famous as an unmanageable wild horse……bringing it back was a real ordeal."

"I-I see……"

"The way you rode it so brilliantly the other day, your dashing entrance……it still plays before my eyes. It was quite agitated just a moment ago, but as expected, once it lays eyes on its master, it calms right down……"

While the man spoke earnestly, Krische averted her eyes from the horse.

"……Krische is not that horse's master."

"Haha, no need to be modest. Being recognized by a horse like this — that's a gift one is born with."

The horse moved in front of the reluctant-looking Krische, presenting its flank.

Then, with a *brrrn* and a huff, it indicated the saddle on its back with its neck.

"Just in case, I've put on a cushion to match the one from last time. I hear you only needed a pack horse, but that would be a waste — this horse would be perfect for the journey."

The reins were thrust toward her.

She would have preferred a different horse, but with the luggage already strapped on, she couldn't exactly refuse.

She accepted them reluctantly, and Selene asked:

"The size is a bit different, but want me to lend you some trousers?"

"No thank you. Krische won't be riding."

As if it understood the words, the horse huffed, *brrrn*, indicating the saddle.

And pressed closer.

"……Krische is not riding."

Krische glared at the horse and told it so.

――After that, Krische surrendered to the horse's relentless demonstrations that she was walking too slowly.

Krische ended up traveling sidesaddle after all.

――Several more days later.

Passing alongside Fort Belgash was a column of marching troops that seemed to stretch to the horizon.

The imperial army — forty thousand strong.

Among them, inside a box carriage surrounded by soldiers in black-lacquered armor and cavalry――

"……I'm bored."

"It's almost lunchtime, so perhaps you should rest?"

"I've slept too much and now I'm tired!"

Cheeks puffed, feet kicking restlessly — there sat the First Princess, Kreschenta.

She grabbed her golden hair that shimmered red in both hands and glared petulantly at Bery.

Across from her, the red-haired servant Bery smiled down at her hands as they worked.

What she was knitting with yarn was a scarf.

Threading each stitch carefully, shaping it as she went.

"Nn……well then, would you like to try too, Kreschenta-sama?"

"No thank you. Too much trouble. You can just buy a scarf at a shop."

"That's what makes it worthwhile, though. Self-satisfaction, perhaps, but……knitting each stitch carefully, thinking about the person who will wear it……fufu, it's getting cold, and I think a white scarf would suit Krische-sama perfectly."

"……How many do you intend to make?"

It was getting cold, Bery claimed, and she had already knitted several scarves.

Gray, brown, green — saying *any of these would suit her*, and left unchecked, she seemed likely to cover nearly every color in existence.

"You just want to score points with onee-sama."

"Is it so strange to score points in the hopes of pleasing the one you love?"

Kreschenta glared at Bery with increasing displeasure, and Bery laughed with evident enjoyment.

She pressed a finger into those puffed cheeks and said with a smile, *That won't do*.

"You're ruining that lovely face. Smiles, now, smiles."

"……How nice for you, being so cheerful. You were all gloomy before, but the moment you found out onee-sama won, it's been nothing but grinning."

"My, what a mean thing to say. ……Nn, there we are."

She lifted the scarf and spread it out, checking for any loose stitches.

The ends were finished with lace, but otherwise it bore no pattern.

Simple, with a focus on durability.

Krische tended to prefer simple things — practical things — so Bery had decided that fussing over decoration would be less effective than prioritizing function.

Satisfied with the result, she nodded, then glanced at the window, which had been kept closed.

"……What is it?"

"Oh, nothing……"

She had looked that way on a whim.

But after a moment, there came the sound of horses, the area grew noisy, and voices rose from the soldiers.

The voices contained a girl's name, and——

"There, Bururun, be good and stay still for a while. ……It's been a long time. Kirik, could you please look after Bururun?"

"Sir. Bururun, is that……the?"

"This horse's name. Krische named it since it was inconvenient to have nothing to call it."

Bery clutched the scarf tight at the sound of that voice.

Then she opened the carriage door.

"——!"

Krische had just handed the reins to the escort squad leader.

When she saw Bery, her face bloomed into a radiant smile.

Without even a running start, she sprang up and dove into Bery's arms.

The impact sent Bery tumbling back onto the seat — onto Kreschenta's thighs — and a shriek went up.

"Bery! Have you been well?"

"……Yes. And you, Krische-sama, you're not hurt?"

"Ehehe, yes."

Bery squeezed tight, pouring strength into her arms as she embraced that slender body.

It was almost painful for Krische, yet she smiled, happy.

Those watching from around them, too, felt the reality of the civil war's victory settle in at the sight, and they shared smiles — all except Kreschenta, who was being crushed beneath Bery's rear and back, and whose agonized cries went unheard.

"……From now on, we'll be together again. Always. Krische wants to cook lots of things."

"Yes, of course. Fufu, while Krische-sama was away, I learned all sorts of imperial dishes, and together with Kreschenta-sama——ah."

When Bery finally noticed and lifted herself up, what she found beneath her was Kreschenta, face scarlet, features contorted in fury.

"Th-th-this……how dare a mere servant sit her rear on, on, my——"

"Oh, that reminds Krische, it's been a while, Kreschenta."

"'That reminds'……?"

Kreschenta froze, her eyes glistening.

Krische paid it no mind.

"O-onee-sama! Do you know how hard I've worked to——"

"……What are you getting angry about? Honestly. That won't do."

Krische closed the door, then kissed Kreschenta.

Sitting across Bery's lap, she stroked Kreschenta's head.

"Good girl, good girl."

"……S-something like that won't fix my mood……"

Even as she said it, the anger was already half gone.

She looked up through her lashes, cheeks flushed, watching Krische's hand pat her head.

"I-I'm so sorry, I sat on you without realizing……"

Realizing what she had done, Bery flushed and apologized.

"……Do you really think you can be forgiven with words like that?"

Kreschenta glared at Bery, and,

"Kreschenta."

Krische said it with a single sideways glance.

Kreschenta fell silent with an *uuu*.

Then, as if remembering, Krische took a candy from the small pouch hung around her neck.

"……Krische has come back to Bery, just as she promised."

"Yes. I have been waiting, all this time."

And she pressed it to Bery's lips.

With a quiet laugh, Bery took the candy into her mouth, then wound the freshly finished scarf around Krische's neck.

Krische looked at it curiously, feeling the texture with her hand, pressing it against her cheek, and smiling with delight.

*It's warm*, she said, burying her face in Bery's chest, and——

"……Krische is home."

After a brief pause, still leaning against her, she said it.

At those words, Bery's eyes glistened, and she lowered them, wiping away the tears threatening to spill.

She nodded, lifted Krische upright, placed a hand on her cheek, and smiled.

"Yes. ……Welcome home, Krische-sama."

Her long eyelashes trembled as she closed her eyes and slowly leaned in.

As if to prove those words were true, the kiss was long.

The candy's honey had begun to dissolve, its sweetness fragrant.

A kiss sweeter than any fruit — filled with love.

――For Krische, it was the feel of a long war's end.

## Alignment
 
**Order** — Values rules and discipline, universal principles, and places importance on an ordered society and law.
**Chaos** — Values the individual and their emotions and thoughts, placing will above social discipline and morality.
**Neutral** — In between.
**Good** — Altruistic disposition. Places greater importance on the morality one believes in than on personal gain, and is willing to die for it.
**Evil** — Selfish disposition. Places great importance on personal gain and does not shy from stepping on others to obtain it.
**Neutral** — A middle-ground disposition that does not lean strongly toward either.

## How to Read Kingdom Noble Names
 
Example: 1, Gildanstein = 2, Kalnaros = 3, Vel = 4, Sarcarinea = 5, Alberan
 
1. **Given name:** Gildanstein
2. **Largest territory administered:** Kalnaros. In principle, all administered territories would be listed here, making the name extremely long; it is simplified in practice.
3. **Male royals with succession rights use Vel; female royals use Vera.**
4. **Honorary title:** Sarcarinea
5. **Family name:** Alberan
 
Formally, words indicating military rank — Marshal, General, and so on — or palace offices such as Chancellor are inserted between 4 and 5, and words indicating peerage such as Grand Duke may follow the family name. However, as the transliterated names are already long enough to make one's eyes glaze over, the text prioritises clarity by appending the relevant rank or office in kanji at the end as needed.
 
Generally speaking, elements other than the family name are not necessarily passed down to children, though it is customary for the legitimate heir to inherit the land and title without issue. The honorary title in position 4, which denotes martial achievement, is the sole exception — it is never inherited.

### ○ Krische Rinea Christand 『Order / Evil』
 
A young woman of the unhinged variety who kills people without hesitation. Adopted daughter of House Christand. Protagonist.
 
She is a royal cursed child — a "baby that did not cry," as they are called — the erased First Princess of Alberan. Condemned to death, she was spirited away by Nora, the servant assigned to care for her, and grew up in Karka Village.
 
She is an anomaly lacking in empathy, feeling no pangs of conscience over murder or violence. Yet having had strong affection lavished upon her by good people, she has gradually come to understand ordinary ethics and emotions.
 
She generally takes people's words only at face value and is not skilled at reading feelings. Set that aside, and she is a literal genius of superhuman ability in every respect — she can observe any technique once and absorb and develop it with ease.
 
Her basic operating principle for everything is *of course I can do this*, and she has a strong attachment to her own excellence. She is diligent.
 
The same applies to her relationships: she acts on a profit-and-loss basis as a rule. But because she is poor at reading others' feelings, she tends to overestimate what others give to her and underestimate what she gives to them. As a result, she often demands an outsized "return" on whatever pure goodwill someone directs at her, and spares no effort in providing it. This stems from her discomfort with human relationships, yet that particular aspect of her could be called genuinely good-natured — whether one sees her as simply an anomaly or as a girl who is too pure depends greatly on the observer.
 
As an individual she is an overwhelming presence with no equal, yet she is prone to dependence and is mentally young and fragile. She is the type who grows more mentally vulnerable as she deepens her bonds and finds human happiness, and compared to the days when she was self-sufficient and placed no value on others, she has gradually drifted further from the "perfect being" she once aspired to be.
 
**★ Combat Command**
 
Extremely aggressive in both individual combat and tactics. A commander with the nature of a hunter who prioritises above all else the mobility to deliver lethal damage to the enemy — always using manoeuvre to disrupt them, draw them into traps, and never fighting in a situation unfavourable to herself. She prefers the "headhunting tactic" of making maximum use of herself, her strongest piece, to deal with enemy commanders as a priority without hesitation. Against enemies who come at her head-on, her strength is nothing short of overwhelming.
 
She asks no more of her subordinates than the minimum, expects nothing beyond that, and constructs her tactics around winning with what she has — yet despite this, she has the unfortunate habit of never being satisfied unless she pursues the maximum possible result.
 
Though ordinarily supplemented by the Black Flag, she frequently overtaxes herself and falls ill, yet invariably pretends nothing is wrong, which makes the problem worse. She is the troublesome sort of commander who will break down unless others help cover for her, and her sole flaw as arguably the strongest figure in the field is something extremely elementary — she is too stubborn to manage even her own health properly, which is really quite childish.

# Chapter 115: The Queen and Alberinea

After enduring a lengthy proclamation laden with the civil officials' flowery rhetoric — the queen's coronation.

Following a brief interval, the victory ceremony began.

The reason for combining them on the same day was a matter of finances.

Musicians, cooks, travel expenses for the nobles — a single ceremony meant a massive outlay.

Doing it all at once might not cut costs in half, but it still saved a considerable sum — and given the kingdom's precarious situation, they wanted to minimize the occasions that brought powerful nobles responsible for national defense together in one place.

The coronation — a sacred rite welcoming a new queen.

Many had objected to mixing other events into such a day, but Kreschenta had insisted strongly that her coronation was achieved not by her own power alone but through the struggles of all her subjects, and so this arrangement was made.

In truth, she would rather have avoided holding such a ceremony at all — but it was a necessary formality for both domestic and foreign audiences.

If it had to be done, she would make it as meaningful as possible.

The palace's intelligence network had deep ties to Gildanstein and could not be trusted, so for managing the populace, she used Elrouga's personal intelligence network and Daglis of Kirzalan, who had sworn personal loyalty to Krische.

During the parade, Krische was publicly presented as a pitiful princess who had been driven from the palace due to internal squabbles and branded a cursed child.

First impressions were more important than anything.

Krische, dressed up, possessed beauty more than sufficient for a princess. At the very least, she did not look like some wicked cursed child, so shaping that impression was easy.

And once the people thought *that girl is no cursed child*, even if rumors branding her as such were spread again, they would not accept them.

This was already the second time, after Gildanstein's earlier smear campaign. Naturally, a second attempt would be less effective.

Kreschenta, for her part, wished to go further — to formally restore Krische as legitimate royalty and make her a figurehead queen.

It would elevate her own reputation as a princess who stepped aside out of sisterly deference rather than desire for power, and it would make things politically and diplomatically easier for Kreschenta. More time together, too.

There were merits on many fronts — but making Krische queen would limit her freedom, and the inability to freely deploy Krische as a military asset was a significant drawback.

There was also the matter of Krische's personality.

*'Things like that are Kreschenta's job. That was the arrangement from the start, and Krische has her own work to do.'*

Kreschenta had anticipated the response, but the proposal was rejected immediately.

Cooking with Bery, housework. And military duties on the side.

Not a shred of desire for power existed in Krische's head; Bery came first.

Kreschenta, who had puffed up angrily, was placated with three cookies and two head-pats, and gave up with a *fine*.

Even so, the belief that Krische should hold a certain degree of power remained in Kreschenta's mind.

Youth was a factor, but beyond that — her extraordinary and incomparable battle record.

From what she'd heard, there were issues with her popularity as a commander — but regardless, she was an individual capable of overturning even strategic disadvantage single-handedly.

She should be placed in a special position, set apart from other generals, with preemptive authority to clear obstacles whenever she set about creating something new.

The title of military supreme commander would present too many problems given Krische's personality.

A position of freedom, unbound, yet carrying authority — the queen's right hand and advisor.

That was the new role Kreschenta decided to create.

In this war, the number of corps commanders she had personally slain reached seven, and battalion commanders approached twenty.

As for centurions, trying to count them became absurd — and the martial record of Krische, who obsessively hunted down commanders, was anything but ordinary.

Two of the enemy general kills technically belonged to Battalion Commander Begil and Nozan, but Krische's influence had been significant in both cases, and one of them was the enemy supreme commander.

To say she had guided the princess to victory was no exaggeration — it was indisputable fact.

A record of achievement that astonished even Kreschenta, who had believed in her sister's prowess.

There was no one here who could question it.

"Considering her martial achievements and ability alone, the position of supreme military commander — Marshal — would be fitting. Yet she has stated that her age, and her loyalty to the current Christand Margrave — Selene-sama — prevent her from accepting such a post. Even so, weighing all circumstances, it is impossible to confine my sister to the rank of a single army's general. ……Therefore, in conjunction with my coronation, I have decided to establish an appropriate title."

Kreschenta smiled. Krische's lips pursed slightly.

*'Then onee-sama should be supreme commander! That's exactly right!'*

*'That kind of thing doesn't suit Krische, so Selene should do it. Krische has to go here and there, so just let Krische keep the Christand army and——'*

*'Don't be silly — if even Krische's age is questionable, people would do nothing but complain if I became Marshal. At least if General Velraigh——'*

It had been a decision reached after lengthy debate.

"The old word for warrior — Rinea. The Senior Knight title, earned solely through martial achievement, lasting only a single lifetime. Adding new characters to the Rinea title based on one's deeds is customary — but my sister's accomplishments in this war are without precedent even in the annals of history."

Kreschenta stood and took the treasure sword resting beside her.

Bezelia — the great sword that only the monarch was permitted to wield.

She presented it solemnly to Krische at her side.

Krische did not kneel — she received it standing.

Equal to Kreschenta, the queen — at least, that was the message Kreschenta intended to convey.

"As my sword — as the queen's sword. As the symbol of martial might. As my other half, possessing the will and the power to shatter foreign enemies and defend this land. My sister shall receive the title of Alberinea — 'Heavenly Sword' — the highest military rank alongside Marshal."

The Lightning Knight — Vezrinea.

Just as Bogan had held it, adding a name denoting martial valor to a Senior Knight's title when their achievements exceeded its bounds was a common honor.

Aulgorn's Nilkrinea, meaning "guardian"; Gildanstein's Sarcarinea, meaning "Black Lion" — it was typical for the epithet born of one's exploits to be appended.

But this time, it went far beyond that convention.

"My sister will serve as advisor on military affairs for the kingdom, standing alongside the supreme military commander — the Marshal. In practical terms, her authority will correspond roughly to that of the existing Marshal, but the supreme holder of authority will remain the Marshal as before. One might describe it as a position similar to assistant supreme commander."

*That is far too* — a civil official started to object, but Kreschenta's eyes — that violet — immediately turned toward him.

"I have also nominated the Marshal and the advisor. Given that Margrave Velraigh has declined, the Marshal shall be Margrave Christand, who likewise made great contributions to this civil war despite her youth. Her advisor shall be Count Faren, whose long military career and many achievements speak for themselves."

Those kneeling on the crimson carpet received these words as a matter of course.

For the Marshal's position, the leading candidate would have been Nozan. But given the precarious relations with neighboring powers, he could not leave the east.

He had only just been assigned as eastern general when the civil war erupted, and his rebuilding of the east had been left half-finished. Handing it off to someone else at this stage was not feasible.

That left the other general, Selene. The issue of youth existed, but she had ample talent for delegation — and with Elrouga Faren, the experienced strategist, serving as her advisor, the youth issue could be offset sufficiently.

There were, in fact, no other suitable candidates.

The old general Felwors Keesliton had once held the Marshal's title and was the strongest candidate — but having sided with Gildanstein, he was under house arrest.

He could not, at this point, be placed in a position commanding the entirety of the military.

For those who had fought together, the appointments were proper — but for the civil and military officials standing on either side, meaning those who had sat out the civil war, or who had cooperated with Gildanstein even passively, it was a different matter.

Selene and Nozan had only just become generals; Elrouga and Krische had been corps commanders.

Even granting that Krische could be treated as royalty, there were grievances over the other appointments. Those who had previously held the position of central general naturally viewed this as nepotism.

Kreschenta observed their demeanor and spoke.

"I understand that some of you are dissatisfied. It is with that understanding that I have chosen this path."

With those violet eyes, she could see it instantly.

Hostility, defiance, doubt. Kreschenta's eyes had, since birth, captured nothing else.

Expressions, gestures. She caught every flicker of emotion, identified each individual, and filed the information away in her mind.

To deal with them later.

――Kreschenta did not forgive those who bore her ill will.

"……In this rebellion, many sought my life, and in the process I lost Nora — the attendant who had helped and supported me since birth……I did not know whom to trust, whom to turn to for help. I spent that time without even that much."

She lowered her eyes. To look sad.

If appearing weak made them drop their guard, she would play the weak. She would bow her head.

Kreschenta felt not a shred of hesitation or guilt about deceiving people in this way.

"All of it is due to my own failings — I have no intention of using my age as an excuse. But I knew far too little about those who serve me. I was frightened, afraid, and in the end I fled the palace. ……It was then that I was saved by the late Christand Margrave……helped by my sister and so many others, and was able to return. But even now……"

She wiped away the tears she had "allowed to show," and raised her face.

A frail, young girl — yet one with a strong will.

That was how she wished to appear.

"――I ask for just a little time. Time to determine who each of you is, where your strengths lie, and how you should be employed. ……I know full well that you may see this as a calculated appointment favoring those who sided with me in this war — blatant favoritism. But please, forgive my inexperience."

Kreschenta continued.

"……I ask you to bear with it for now. In time, I will answer your devotion. I will become a queen worthy of that answer. So, until then——"

With the face of a fragile queen, as if to invite their pity.
