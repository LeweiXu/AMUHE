# Chapter 61: Something Precious

Stroking the cheek of Krische as she slept in the bed, Bery let out a sigh.

She had been sitting in the chair placed right beside the bed for nearly the entire day.

Two days since she had returned. Krische had not woken even once.

"……So thin, so worn……"

Her white cheeks had lost their fullness, and dark circles ran deep beneath her eyes.

She had heard that Krische had pushed herself recklessly.

Bery had cleaned her body, tidied her hair, and laid her in bed.

She spent most of her time at the side of the girl who occasionally showed signs of distress.

The fever still had not broken.

The physician had diagnosed it as nothing more than extreme overwork rather than a serious illness, but that did nothing whatsoever to ease her worry.

Even a body that wields mana is mortal. Far more resilient than an ordinary person, yes, but when the time comes to die, it dies all the same.

Bery placed a damp cloth on Krische's forehead and smoothed the blanket that had shifted out of place again and again.

A knock sounded, and Selene entered.

"Bery, is Krische——"

"……She is still sleeping."

Selene cast her eyes down at that, and approached the bed.

After finishing her work, Selene too had been spending her time here.

Kreschenta was helping Selene with her duties more than before, and the other corps commanders had been taking the lead in handling tasks with consideration for Selene.

From sunset onward, Selene was here more often than not.

"……She's hot."

"Yes. ……But better than when you came yesterday."

Selene stroked Krische as if to check the fever, then wiped her eyes with her sleeve.

She was remembering when Krische had returned.

They had cremated Bogan's body and held a grand funeral alongside the war dead.

By that time, Selene's feelings had settled somewhat.

No matter what, she could not spend forever crying.

She had cried herself to sleep at Bogan's side, held the funeral, and little by little reclaimed her composure — managing to eat despite having no appetite.

It helped greatly that Bery had been at her side the whole time, watching over her with quiet care.

The retreat had gone well under Gallen's leadership, but there was one problem.

Krische and her century had still not returned.

No — that was not quite accurate. Some had come back, carrying the seriously wounded.

Two soldiers for each critically injured person.

Yet even after a week, Krische herself remained in the mountains.

"……Um, ojou-sama, is Krische-sama——"

Bery asked Selene with nothing but an anxious face.

For the first three days or so, Bery had seemed to be minding Selene's feelings.

There must have been a sense of reassurance somewhere in her — that Krische would be fine.

However, ever since hearing from returning soldiers that Krische had developed a high fever, her anxiety had been growing with each passing day.

Everyone here felt the same.

Bery could tell from Selene's face as she returned to the office from outside, yet even so, she asked.

Kreschenta, too, was restlessly sorting documents.

"……'No impediment to continuation of the mission,' she says."

"I……see."

Everyone was worried about the situation, yet powerless to do anything.

The Dragon's Maw was under Gildanstein's control.

Sending a mountain-trained messenger was the most they could manage — but in response to the recall order, that was what Krische had answered.

"……Um, Selene-sama, perhaps I could——"

"No. Understand your position. You are to stay here."

"But……"

Selene stroked Kreschenta's head. She hung her face.

She bore no grudge.

If only this war had never happened.

But regretting that was pointless.

The war had started because of her.

Yet that did not mean everything that occurred in it was her responsibility.

It was not as though there had been no choices.

If one were willing to choose, there had been countless options.

But considering Krische, weighing sentiment and principle, advantage and disadvantage — it was her father who had chosen to fight after weighing all of that, and he had simply given his life as a nobleman for what he believed.

"I won't blame you alone, Kreschenta. ……You're my precious family, just like Otou-sama."

"…………"

"Whatever the case, it was a conclusion the whole family chose together. Whatever the result, if there is responsibility to bear, it falls equally on all of us."

"……Yes."

She stroked her head.

Selene had already accepted the existence that was Kreschenta, and on top of that, decided to protect her.

To break a vow sworn upon one's name — that was something Selene's pride would never allow.

As a noblewoman — as the daughter of a noble hero who had given his life for his duty — it was unthinkable.

She closed her eyes for just a moment, and spoke.

"……I've settled things with the corps commanders. Tonight, I'm going to bring her back."

"That's……"

"It's fine, it's the mountains. There won't be a problem if we go at night. I'll take some capable escorts familiar with Bernaich from the Fourth Corps, so it'll be alright."

The soldiers who had returned said Krische was at her limit.

Even when Dagra urged retreat, she would not listen, simply stating that they must continue the delaying action.

To halt the enemy's pursuit and buy time for the army to regroup, they needed to continue this — and that was her current duty, she said.

She would not return until she had properly fulfilled her duty.

The soldiers told Selene this with worry in their voices, describing how Krische would say these things while barely able to stand.

Selene had even sent a messenger under her own name, but Krische had ignored it.

The reply was those words: no impediment to continuation of the mission.

The round trip took two days. The messenger had been a wasted effort.

In a way, she understood.

Because the last thing Krische had seen was the person she loved, Selene, striking her cheek.

It had been the worst thing she could have done. She had regretted it over and over.

But she could not make excuses, could not claim she hadn't meant it.

Because as a matter of fact, Krische had been frightened, and that was exactly how she had taken it.

"I've asked Anne to prepare my armor now. Bery, could you make something light to eat?"

"……Certainly. I'll prepare it right aw——"

Then came a rough knock, and the door opened without waiting for a reply.

"Selene-sama! Krische-sama has returned!"

The instant she heard those words, Selene flew from the room.

Wrapped in a blanket and carried in someone's arms, she looked like an infant, and her suffering was plain at a glance.

Bery immediately arranged for a doctor, and Selene and Kreschenta guided Mia, who was holding Krische, to the room.

The blanket was soaked through with blood, and when they removed her clothes, her shoulder was badly swollen.

Seeing the battered, wound-covered state of the soldiers, it was a mercy she had gotten off with only that much.

They told Anne to bring hot water, cleaned her filthy body, and laid her in the bed.

Her beloved sword, which should have been sturdily made, was nicked with countless chips like a bamboo whisk.

The cloth wrapped around the grip was hardened stiff with blood, and the skin of both her hands was covered in burst blood blisters.

If only she had made time somehow and gone earlier — not entrusted it to a messenger, but gone herself.

Regretting that, she slept at Krische's side.

And since then, Krische had not woken.

Krische always went to such extremes.

She had always known this.

To meet expectations, Krische treated the unreasonable as a matter of course.

She pushed herself past her own limits.

Her standard was always her own, and she refused to accept anything less than what she considered natural.

Fighting in the center of the Dragon's Maw, reclaiming the sword from Gildanstein, taking over Selene's command.

While doing all of that without rest, what she prioritized above all else was worrying about Selene. She came to check on her.

She had tried to comfort her.

*——And despite all that.*

Selene twisted her face and embraced Krische.

"……Ojou-sama, please don't torment yourself so."

"But, I did something terrible to this child……"

Bery pressed her lips together.

Nothing she said would help.

The office — the whole fortress — was permeated with a dark air.

They had lost a hero and been defeated at the Dragon's Maw, so it was only natural.

The atmosphere here was suffocating, yet the greatest source of that was the anxiety over Krische.

If they were to lose Krische on top of everything——

No matter how much she tried not to think about it, that fear crept into her heart.

"Sere……ne……?"

The two of them lost their words, and looked at Krische.

Hazy and uncertain.

But through the gap between long silver lashes, beautiful violet peeked through.

"Krische!"

"Krische-sama!"

Selene leaned her face close, and for an instant, Krische showed a look of fright.

"S-sor……ry……"

Haltingly, sadly.

Understanding the meaning of the words Krische had spun, Selene wrapped her arms around that small body.

"You don't have to apologize, Krische……it's my fault, not yours."

"Sere…ne……?"

"I'm sorry. I never meant to hit you……I'm not angry, I don't hate you or anything like that. ……I truly, truly love you."

Krische lifted her face in confusion, glancing sideways at Selene as she clung to her.

Then she looked at Bery.

Bery, quietly wiping her tears, smiled with relief.

"……Just as I told you. There is no chance whatsoever that ojou-sama would hate you, Krische-sama."

"Th……at……"

Krische still looked troubled.

She wavered over whether to embrace Selene, moving her bandage-wrapped hands.

"……But, Selene, you're crying. ……You look so, so sad."

"These are called tears of joy. Even when you are very happy, tears can come. You were asleep for two days, and she was terribly worried about you."

"……Happy tears."

"Krische-sama, whom she loves dearly, has finally woken up. She is overjoyed."

Bery's hand caught the damp cloth that had slipped from her forehead, and stroked her hair aside.

Krische, still dazed, looked slightly ticklish as she nestled into Selene.

"……You haven't come to hate Krische, have you?"

"I haven't! I haven't. ……I'm sorry, your cheek — it must have hurt?"

"……Krische is fine."

Selene raised her face and kissed Krische's cheek.

Krische opened her eyes just a little, and showed a face of relief.

As though to wipe away the drops that had fallen from Selene, she placed her hand on Selene's cheek, tracing it with her thumb——

"Nn……"

Then she raised herself slightly and pressed her lips to hers, and pulled away.

A smile appeared on Krische's lips, and Selene too placed her hand on Krische's cheek and kissed her.

It was a gentle kiss.

"I properly love you. ……Always, from now on."

"……Selene."

Relief drained the tension from her body, and she sank into the bed.

"When Krische wakes up, you won't have started hating Krische, will you?"

"I said I haven't. ……I swear upon the name Selene — I will never, ever come to hate you. Rest easy."

Krische nodded.

Her breathing was somewhat ragged, and she coughed softly now and then.

Selene rubbed her body, and Krische watched her absently.

"Krische-sama, would you like some water?"

"……Yes, please."

Selene gently raised Krische's body.

Bery picked up the water pitcher, drizzled honey into it, and stirred.

Pressing a cloth to her chin so it would not spill, she brought the pitcher to Krische's lips and let her drink little by little.

"How are you feeling? Would you like something to eat?"

"……Um."

"Please be honest. Would you prefer to sleep?"

After a brief pause, Krische said, "I'd like to sleep."

She must have been feeling unwell.

She had eaten nothing and must have been hungry.

For Krische, with her tremendous appetite, to say such a thing — it must be truly bad, and Bery furrowed her brow.

"I will have something easy to eat prepared for when you wake. Please rest for now."

"……Yes."

"Starting today, you are on rest until your health returns, Krische-sama."

"But, Krische has……work……"

"Krische is on rest. Please, I'm begging you, just rest."

Gazed at by Selene, Krische nodded meekly.

Bery smiled.

"In return, I will spoil you so much that you'll want to crawl out of bed to escape it. Ojou-sama and Kreschenta-sama will spoil Krische-sama to their hearts' content as well, so by the time your body heals, you may have become such a pampered child that you won't be able to work at all."

"That would……Krische would be troubled by that……"

Her cheeks reddened with a blush separate from the fever, and Selene let out a wry laugh.

"For a while, that's exactly how it should be. ……Will you listen to my request?"

"Uu……yes."

"Good girl," said Selene, stroking her head, and Krische narrowed her eyes.

"……If it's Selene's request, Krische will do anything. ……Anything at all."

Krische reached out and stroked Selene's cheek again.

"So, Krische wants Selene to keep……liking Krische."

*——After all that, how deeply had this girl been wounded?*

Those eyes were like flawless, innocent gems, yet they swayed quietly, anxiously, fearfully.

Two days unconscious, body covered in wounds, having killed more people than she could count.

All of it, for Selene's sake.

And yet all she wished for was that single, small thing.

She would not have minded resentful words, or being cursed at.

She would have been fine being slapped until her face swelled, being screamed at like a child throwing a tantrum.

But Krische harbored not a shred of such feelings, and simply directed a terrifyingly pure devotion toward her.

Endlessly beautiful, unadorned, crystalline words and love.

Lost for words.

Tears nearly spilling again, she held them back and pressed her forehead to Krische's.

She felt the heat of her, and gazed into those eyes.

"You don't even have to listen to my requests. You don't have to push yourself, you can say no and be selfish — I still care about you so much, and love you so much."

"……Selene."

"If you say you're anxious, then I'll tell you the same thing every day until you're not anxious anymore. ……I'll never do anything like that again. I will never betray your trust. Absolutely never."

*——So just, please, believe me.*

Selene spoke in a quiet, whispering voice, her golden lashes wet.

Her long golden hair mingled with the translucent silver upon the sheets.

Pale pink softly overlapping, the girl sunk in white-gold smiled.

"……Yes."

She closed her eyelids, and as if she had reached her limit, immediately began to breathe the quiet breaths of sleep.

Selene tidied the bed and slowly pulled away.

"……Will this love rival matter cease to be a joke, I wonder?"

"Y-you……"

"Fufu, now you can't tease me anymore either, ojou-sama……ah."

In a joking tone.

Bery giggled like a young girl, laughing happily — and then immediately lowered her face.

She traced beneath her eyes with slender fingertips, and sniffled.

"I'm sorry. ……I've caused you trouble, too."

Sitting in her chair, she shook her red hair and shook her head.

She was pressing both hands to her face.

"That's not……it's just, once I felt relieved……that's all."

Minding Selene's feelings.

And not wanting to make Krische anxious, she had been holding it in.

She who always watched over them — even though she was not strong herself.

Selene knelt before Bery, whose shoulders trembled as she pressed her face, and embraced her.

"……Thank you, Bery."

Bery shook her red hair again, and Selene laughed.

"Fufu, you can't tease me about being stubborn either, can you?"

"……I'm not being stubborn."

"Shall I let you have that? But——"

She stroked that red hair, and said,

"Maybe we're alike in that way. ……Even if I hate that about myself, I really do love that about you, Bery."

"……Mine isn't like that."

"Your voice is breaking, you know."

With a wry smile, she rested her head on Bery's shoulder.

Putting up a front, being stubborn. Selene and Bery shared their worst qualities.

But perhaps it was precisely because of that.

"Maybe that's why the same things look beautiful to us."

She whispered it, quietly.

# Chapter 62: Liquefaction

A rakura fruit, low in acidity and high in sweetness.

Sealed in a small bottle with honey and left to steep for about four days — such was this particular offering.

The texture was smooth.

The rakura fruit melted away, mingling with honey, its faint tartness somehow refreshing.

The delicate fibers of the rakura were soft, dissolving on the tongue.

"How is it? Honey-steeped rakura."

"Ehehe……it's delicious."

Just as the rakura was steeped in honey, Krische too was steeped in sweetness.

Something gentle on the stomach and nutritious — that was how Bery had given Krische treat after treat.

What arrived at her lips was an endless parade of sweets.

Krische was practically a nestling.

Both hands were wrapped round and round with bandages, giving her the perfect excuse to be fed.

At first she ate only a little — but after three days, her appetite had returned.

Krische had settled onto Bery's lap, rubbing her cheek against her and indulging herself completely.

Kissing when the mood struck, clinging to her, having her cheeks and head stroked.

Krische, utterly corrupted, was no different from a small child.

It was impossible to believe that until just days ago she had been swinging a sword and slaughtering people en masse.

Taking whatever food was offered, nestling up to Bery in warm comfort.

When sleepy, she would drift off to the sound of Bery lying beside her, and when she woke, she would kiss. Cling. Nuzzle. That was the entirety of her current daily routine.

A greedy happy life, packed with everything Krische loved.

She was, at present, living purely by instinct.

Krische was not entirely without thoughts on her current state, but she silenced the various voices of reason by telling herself that resting and being spoiled was her duty right now.

"Bery."

She raised her face slightly and begged for a kiss, and Bery smiled and placed one on her lips.

The soft sensation of lips. What was conveyed was love.

Krische kissed her back many times to convey her love in return, and clung to Bery.

She shifted position and pressed her face into Bery's chest — and those drooping eyes of Krische were glazed, somewhere between waking and dreaming.

It had become too blissful for her to distinguish whether this was actually real.

Her cheeks were slackened beyond redemption, flushed pink.

Nothing but the feeling of love ran rampant, affirming instincts she usually kept in check.

It was precisely the sensation of her brain melting.

*——To rationalize away her reason, Krische had reviewed her former self.*

*Being spoiled is embarrassing. Being obsessed with food is also embarrassing.*

*From the perspective of rest, such emotions were nothing but obstacles, both physically and mentally.*

*When resting one's body, one must not feel shame about such things.*

*And so what was born was a new persona that could only be called 'Spoiled Krische' mode.*

*Things that Krische would normally be embarrassed by, 'Spoiled Krische' could indulge in without worry — wallowing in pleasure, sinking into depravity with full permission.*

*Being held, being kissed, being fed, devouring sweets.*

*These were embarrassing things.*

*But — right now it couldn't be helped, because she was in 'Spoiled Krische' mode.*

*Ignoring all worries and anxieties, not caring about being called a fool.*

*Krische was now the invincible 'Spoiled Krische.'*

*'Spoiled Krische' could easily carry out things that normal Krische would find embarrassing.*

*Krische welcomed the newly born 'Spoiled Krische.'*

*'Working Krische.'*

*'Lady Krische.'*

*'Glutton Krische.'*

*'Clingy Krische.'*

*'Spoiled Krische' had consolidated only the finest qualities from the countless Krisches — there were no blind spots left.*

*The rational Krische who valued her self-control had thus successfully removed her own limiters — which is to say, she had become an even bigger fool than usual.*

Her form was now nothing short of a slime, clinging to Bery and demanding affection.

"Bery, I love you……love you so much."

Chu-chu, she begged for kisses like a little mouse, and Bery kissed her despite her flustered look.

The face of Bery dealing with such a Krische was bright red — flushed all the way to the ears.

Krische's reason-shaking cuteness was already a lethal weapon.

While she was happy that Krische was being spoiled, the intensity of Krische's ardent displays of love was almost frightening.

She wanted to indulge her. She wanted to become a fool together.

If it had been just the two of them, perhaps she could have stopped thinking and done just that.

But in this room, there was a third pair of eyes.

Bery glanced sideways, and caught Anne averting her gaze, face bright red.

*——Argan-sama must remain at Krische-sama's side.*

*Then cleaning the room and attending to the daily necessities must be my duty* — Anne had reasoned thus and stepped into this den of sin, but what she had witnessed was an irrefutable, definitive scene.

No — not a single scene, but an ongoing affair, still in progress.

Krische paid no mind to Anne whatsoever, pressing against Bery as usual.

Because right now she was in 'Spoiled Krische' mode.

"Ah, oh my, there's some dust here……"

*——Not paying any attention at all. Completely absorbed in my work.*

With the air of a terrible actress, Anne said nothing about it.

*Calm down, calm down. Krische-sama is simply a person of somewhat childish disposition* — she steadied her own heart.

Anne had seen Krische kiss Selene and Kreschenta since her return. She understood that Krische was, in truth, a kiss fiend.

She could also understand that there was no indecent emotion behind it; it was nothing more than affection.

The painful bandages on both hands. The blistering on her feet from the heavy boots had been terrible too.

Her injured shoulder was likewise tightly wrapped, and Anne understood that this small, delicate girl had worked harder than anyone.

And yet the scene was utterly transgressive.

The eyes of Bery — Anne's revered model of servanthood — were moist with embarrassment, her cheeks dyed a charming crimson.

Against her, the white maiden wore a translucent pale-pink negligée.

The white thighs peeking from beneath the hem were endlessly provocative, and with heat-dampened violet eyes turned toward Bery, she clung, nuzzled, kissed.

Anne watching this felt as though her face might burst into flames.

She recalled her memories of the capital, measured them against what she was seeing now, and desperately tried to interpret the scene before her in a favorable light.

*——I should consider this a sign of trust.*

*I should see it as being recognized as a servant who can keep this transgressive secret locked in her heart.*

*Seen that way, this was a most welcome thing* — or so it should have been.

Anne forced herself to that interpretation and continued her distracted cleaning.

Then she noticed a small pouch placed on the shelf, and tilted her head.

Krische noticed it from the corner of her eye and called out.

"Anne, bring that here, please."

Krische beckoned Anne over and had her bring the small pouch.

"Y-yes!"

Inside were some small round objects.

As Anne wondered what they were, Krische told her "They're candies," and opened her mouth toward Anne.

Bery's shoulder seemed to twitch.

"Um, uh……"

She must mean for Anne to feed her.

Anne took a deep amber candy from the pouch, and nervously, slowly brought it toward Krische's lips.

Cherry-pink lips snapped up the candy, and Krische placed both hands on Bery's shoulders, raised herself up, and pressed her lips directly to Bery's.

"……!"

"Mm……"

Anne froze, and Bery's white cheeks flushed an even deeper red. Even her neck had turned crimson.

Krische smiled with satisfaction — "Krische managed to give it back properly" — and licked her lips.

Bery's face was a mixture of embarrassment, happiness, and shyness — an indescribable expression — and she murmured "Thank you" as she drew Krische close.

"It took Krische a little while, but. Is it good?"

"Y-yes, very……"

"Ehehe……"

Krische twisted her body and raised her face as if expecting something.

Bery froze again, then closed her eyes for a moment, and as if steeling herself, kissed Krische.

The candy exchange. Mouth to mouth.

Anne could only watch.

The candy passed from Bery back to Krische's mouth.

Rolling the given candy around in her mouth, cheeks relaxed, Krische pressed her face against Bery looking utterly happy.

*——The very picture of pure innocence.*

She was not thinking a single indecent thought.

This princess simply regarded these extravagant acts as skinship.

Krische beaming, and Bery with eyes moist from embarrassment.

Watching the contrast, Anne understood Bery's hardship for the first time.

Krische was far too delicate and far too bewitching.

She possessed every beauty a girl could have, yet was supremely unguarded.

She was like a chicken dancing beside a pot.

Even Anne — who had no such proclivities — felt as though she might have a momentary lapse in judgment from this kind of defenselessness. What must it feel like to have it directed at you day and night?

Spending every hour of every day with this princess would be impossible for her. Her reason would not hold.

Anne shook her head, and deepened her respect for the perfect servant Bery even further.

And then, at that very moment, came a knock at the door.

Anne hurried toward it. Depending on the visitor, the scene filling this room could be problematic.

But the door opened before she reached it, and two girls appeared.

Selene, swaying her graceful golden hair, and Princess Kreschenta.

Both of them spotted Krische sitting on Bery's lap being spoiled, and their eyebrows twitched.

With an exasperated, indescribable expression, but neither of them said a word.

"Selene, are you done with work?"

"Y-yes……more or less."

Wanting to say something as she eyed Krische and Bery, Selene rubbed the bridge of her nose as though holding herself back.

Kreschenta puffed her cheeks with apparent displeasure and trotted quickly over to Krische.

"Come now, onee-sama, you need to be in bed."

While shooting Bery intermittent glares — Anne was well aware that the princess was hopelessly fond of Krische.

Indeed, if anything, Kreschenta normally appeared the more mature of the two, but when faced with Krische she became jealous and childlike, true to her age.

Krische doted on Bery, and Kreschenta doted on Krische.

The sight of Kreschenta's dissatisfaction over this arrangement was already a familiar one.

"But Krische just finished eating."

"Uu……um, A-Argan-sama, I'll take over."

"Kreschenta's chest is all flat, so Krische prefers Bery."

Krische, for her part, tended to act grown-up around Kreschenta, never abandoning her stance as the older sister, which had created a peculiar power dynamic.

The royalty — the legitimate crown princess, no less — could not raise her head to Krische.

"Selene."

"……What?"

Krische beckoned Selene over with a wave and had her bring her face close, then grabbed Selene's cheeks and pressed her lips against them.

It was done with such speed that she had no chance to refuse. Selene's eyes went wide and she froze.

Just like that, a candy was pushed into Selene's mouth, and——

"Ehehe, it's Bery's candy. She says sweet things are nice after working hard."

Krische peered up at Selene with an upward gaze, as if gauging her reaction.

Selene floated a sour expression, as if torn between what to say and whether to scold, and sighed.

"I-I see……well, thank you. That makes me……happy."

She had pushed Krische too hard, said terrible things, and struck her cheek.

Selene could not bring herself to scold Krische; the only words she could give were those.

For a while, it would be better to let Krische rest without thinking about anything.

To that end, Selene intended to be as sweet as possible.

Desperately biting back her words, enduring the urge to pinch those cheeks, she stroked her head instead.

Seeing this, Bery giggled with amusement, and Selene glared.

"Y-you……"

"Fufu, no, it's just. I thought perhaps you can understand my feelings a little now."

"J-just because of that, don't think I'm not going to say something to you. This sort of thing needs to be corrected little by little."

"I've already given up, so I thought rather than suffering the embarrassment alone, it would be more strategic to create allies. If ojou-sama is also embarrassed, I feel much better."

"Haah……"

Bery said this while petting Krische, then asked, "Are you done with work for today?"

It was still only evening. Earlier than her usual return.

"Yes, well. I've been told to take some rest too."

"……Good work. Shall I prepare a meal?"

"Nn……yes, please."

Selene nodded while rolling the candy around in her mouth.

Out of the corner of her eye she caught Kreschenta — who had been trying to pull Krische away from Bery — being pulled in instead and kissed, but Selene somehow endured and ignored it.

She eyed the blushing, not-entirely-displeased Kreschenta with exasperation, then called out to Anne.

"Anne, could I get some soup and bread? For me and this one."

"Y-yes……!"

Anne, who had been frozen stiff at the sight as always, ladled soup from the small pot prepared in the room into a bowl.

It was Anne's cooking, in place of Bery who was spending her time at Krische's side.

Anne had a catastrophic number of flaws, but she was at least capable of making a proper, ordinary meal.

"Here you are — lamb and vegetable soup."

Today's vegetables in a soup with lamb stewed until it was falling apart.

She set it on the table with confidence, and puffed out her chest.

Neither Krische nor Bery were particularly harsh in their evaluation of food made by others.

She had been told "The potato wasn't really necessary," but beyond that, she had received a passing grade.

"Thank you. It looks delicious."

"Oh, it's nothing compared to what they make, but……"

When Selene smiled gently after smelling it, Anne bowed and mentally pumped her fist.

Kreschenta eyed the potato that had dissolved beyond spoonability, crumbling apart, and furrowed her brow.

"……Isn't this stewed too long? Anne-sama, did you leave it on the fire and forget again?"

Anne froze, and Kreschenta complained but tasted it nonetheless.

Krische watched, glancing back and forth as if deliberating.

"Business went well after that, and I opened a shop and was accepted into a guild. I've left the traveling peddling to my son."

Membership in a merchant guild was every merchant's aspiration.

Only trustworthy merchants with established shops in a town were granted entry.

The guild vouched for a merchant's identity, and with the guild's capital and credit, large transactions became possible.

The path to becoming a great merchant began with joining a guild.

Krische had heard at least that much — that going from peddler to guild-affiliated merchant was quite an accomplishment.

"Congratulations, merchant-sa…… Yaruz-san."

"Thank you. Today I'm here on guild business…… from the Vaazler Trading Company."

Vaazler was a trading company dealing primarily in foodstuffs and textiles.

Its history was far older than Christand's.

It had fairly deep ties with the Arna Empire, and its business was centered on the northern part of the kingdom.

It had been Vaazler that Christand had sought cooperation from during the war with the Holy Elsren Empire.

"……I've heard about Margrave Christand. You must have endured terrible grief."

Yaruz bowed.

Bery lowered her eyes slightly.

"The company intends to continue the same relationship as always. To have met again by chance and find ourselves facing the same direction — I must thank the gods for such fortune. Please don't hesitate to call on us."

"Yes. Thank you. The company has been a great help."

"……May the guidance of Arshe, goddess of fortune, be with you. More than anything, I'm glad to see you well. I'd been worried about what had happened since then."

Yaruz bowed once more and walked away.

He must have had work to do. Krische watched him go, then looked at Bery.

Bery smiled wryly.

"It's a small world. I've crossed paths with him several times myself, but I never imagined he was an acquaintance of Krische-sama's."

"He used to come peddling to Krische's village. After kaa-sama and the others died, he gave Krische lots of vegetables. Said it was a 'service.'"

"……Is that so."

Bery took Krische's hand and began walking.

Krische thought for a moment and said:

"Krische hasn't looked into it much, but how is Vaazler?"

"……They have been very generous to Christand. Considering how much money the company has invested here and how it's been spent, you may take it as a safe bet that they've staked their fortunes on Christand."

Bery answered what Krische wanted to know.

For Krische, trust was less about emotional feeling and more about alignment of interests.

"If the prince's faction wins, their interests will be seized by the prince's own trading companies. That would be unfavorable for them. As a company, Christand is a fellow traveler — a partner on the same ship. Short of something extraordinary, they will remain an ally."

"Then it seems safe."

Bery stroked her head and said:

"Merchants are people who value profit, loss, and trust. In that sense, Krische-sama might find the relationships easy to understand."

"……I see."

Krische nodded thoughtfully.

"It would have been nice if Christand were a merchant's house."

"……?"

"……Things like honor and righteousness — sometimes Krische doesn't understand what Selene and the others mean. Krische understands that it's a kind of aesthetic. But Krische doesn't understand a value system that chooses death for the sake of that aesthetic. ……Once you're dead, it's all over."

She continued as if sighing.

"What Krische wants is just a tiny bit. A tiny bit that Selene wouldn't have to do dangerous things to get. ……Like, say, somewhere in the mountains, together with Bery and everyone. If everything Krische cared about were right in Krische's hands, Krische could protect it all, and feel safe, and……"

"It's hard to put into words," Krische said, lowering her eyes.

Bery waited in silence.

"It might be a little inconvenient at first, but it would be fun. Nothing to worry about…… but Selene would probably say she can't do that. Selene cares about something Krische doesn't really understand, and if Krische dragged her along by force, Selene wouldn't be happy, and then Krische would feel…… something……"

*——So, Krische thought it would've been nice if things were different.*

As if trying to grasp something vague, she repeated herself.

"……Then Bogan-sama wouldn't have had to die, and Selene and Bery wouldn't have been in danger, and Krische wouldn't have had to do unpleasant things and could just be with Bery and Selene forever. ……And even if something like this happened, they could just run away."

"I see."

"……Ah. But then Kreschenta would be all alone, and that would be a bit sad. Hmm……"

Bery smiled gently, warmly.

"I like 'what-ifs' too. But rather than what-ifs about the past, I prefer what-ifs about the future."

"The future?"

"Yes. ……It's called a dream."

Walking, she stroked Krische's head and continued.

"……What happened to Bogan-sama can no longer be changed. No matter how much we think 'what if,' the past cannot be undone. But a 'what if' about the future — if you work hard enough, you might actually make it come true. ……So I prefer dreaming of future 'what-ifs' to past ones."

*——Perhaps it's like that thing I mentioned before — the way things fall into place.*

Bery giggled and looked up at the sky.

The evening sky was a blend of indigo and vermillion.

"……Deep in the mountains, living quietly. Fufu, I understand what you mean, Krische-sama. Your dream is the same as mine."

"……The same?"

"Yes. Quiet and modest, perhaps a little boring and inconvenient…… but together with the people we love, without worry. I've been thinking the same thing as you, Krische-sama."

The shadows of clouds were deep and dark.

The crimson of the sun at the moment of its fall was all the more dazzling, yet gradually being swallowed by the darkness of night.

But true darkness would not come.

The gentle moon and the glimmer of stars began to softly illuminate the cold dark of the sky.

Until the dazzling sun appeared once more from the eastern sky.

Bery gazed in the direction Yaruz had walked.

"That man, too, must have dreamed for a long time as he traveled — of having his own shop, joining a guild, becoming a fine merchant. He worked hard for that, and so he achieved his dream, found happiness…… and perhaps now he dreams a new dream."

"Dreams come in all sizes," she said with a quiet laugh.

"……A dream is like a moon that lights up the dark night. Even when the sun sets and night falls, if you spend the moonlit night dreaming, the sun will rise again and morning will come. If you can imagine a wonderful tomorrow awaiting you, then no matter how hard things are now, you can keep going."

Bery took Krische's hand.

"I…… dream of cooking with Krische-sama every day at the estate again, of having tea together with the young mistresses…… and of that continuing as though it were the most natural thing in the world. If we seize peace, that modest dream will come true."

"What about you, Krische-sama?" she asked with a smile.

"……Krische has never thought about things like that."

Krische seemed slightly embarrassed.

But she pictured it in her mind, and continued.

"But, Krische does love Bery's dream very much."

Just half a year ago, it had been the most ordinary of everyday lives.

All Krische wanted was that.

"Krische also wants to cook with Bery, eat delicious things with Selene and everyone, and have tea parties. Krische hopes that will keep going forever, and……"

*——When Krische imagines it, it makes her feel…… something.*

She placed a hand over her heart and gently closed her eyes.

"Is that so? Fufu, then you and I are fellow dreamers, Krische-sama."

"……Yes. The same."

"I'm sure ojou-sama and the others are thinking the same thing too. There must be many people here, too, who wish for peace — who wish for that kind of small, everyday life."

She adjusted her grip, lacing their fingers together.

Interlocked so they would not come apart.

"And because everyone is working toward that, it will surely come true. Please don't be anxious — if we just keep doing our best now, then surely——"

Krische nodded and looked up at Bery.

"……But in your case, Krische-sama, doing your best to the point of collapse is not allowed. Please refrain from anything like last time. If you had not woken up…… I kept thinking about what I would do."

"……Yes."

"Will you promise me that we'll make this dream come true together?"

"Yes. ……Krische promises."

Bery squeezed the interlocked fingers tight.

Krische squeezed back.

Just from that touch, there was something — something that seemed to connect them, something she could almost understand.

All the way to the kitchen, the two walked hand in hand.
