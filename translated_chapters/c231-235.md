# Chapter 231: Promise

"Hahaha, well, I suppose you'd call it a natural lifespan. ……I've lived quite a long time."

A voice like dried wood.

Elrouga said this while lying in his bed, in his own manor.

In the room were Krische and Bery, along with his adjutant Quinez.

"……Skeleton isn't allowed to become a skeleton yet."

Krische, dark circles under her eyes, spoke in a quiet voice as she held Elrouga's hand.

It had been a week since he had suddenly collapsed during his duties at the military academy.

He had woken up three days before, and had been bedridden since.

Extension of the body——the construction of virtual musculature.

He could no longer manage even that, and having relied on it for so long, he could barely move his own body.

The doctors had thrown up their hands, calling it incurable.

It was, however, apparently a common illness among elderly mana wielders.

The ability to manipulate mana would decline sharply, and the aging that mana had kept at bay would advance rapidly, destroying the organs and leading to death.

Krische had been practically living at his bedside the entire time.

Mana worked as an energy even on an unconscious level.

Preserving deteriorating organs, breaking down toxins, replacing them with new flesh and blood.

She had been converting mana crystals into mana, channeling it into Elrouga's body, manipulating his flesh as it normally functioned——she had hoped this would halt the aging, yet somewhere in her heart she understood that his body was drifting toward a gentle death.

"To have Krische-sama say such things and receive such devoted care……there cannot be another man as blessed as I in all the world."

Even so, today was a good day, and precisely because of that she convinced herself her efforts were working, and Krische pressed on, for all the good it would do.

Elrouga gazed at her with tender affection, feeling the mana flowing in, and let his expression soften.

"And that is exactly why……it is enough, Krische-sama. As I have said, old trees wither and are destined to become nourishment. That is nature's way."

"……Krische doesn't really understand metaphors."

Krische lowered her eyes as if in refusal.

"……What a troublesome person you are."

Elrouga smiled, his face creasing.

Then he narrowed his eyes.

"——If you try to pick up everything you can, eventually your hands will overflow and the weight will crush you. I told you this once before."

Krische nodded silently.

"Perhaps Krische-sama could extend my life. For an extraordinarily long time, even. But during that time, if Bery-kun over there, if Selene-sama, if Her Majesty the Queen……were to fall into the same perilous state, you have but two hands at most. It is not an old man like me that you should be saving."

"……But."

"……Even now, for the sake of this old man's life, you have such dark circles under your eyes. This is a great loss for the kingdom."

Humans are not omniscient or omnipotent, Elrouga continued.

"……There is a limit to what one can do. Krische-sama should value your own time more."

"But……"

"With Krische-sama's power, in the same span of time, I believe you could save hundreds, thousands of people, and guide them toward happiness. To spend such time on a single old man is far too great a waste. ……If emotion cannot convince you, then please consider it from the standpoint of profit and loss."

Elrouga lifted his trembling right hand and stroked Krische's cheek.

Then he wiped the tears spilling there with his thumb.

"……It is a strange thing. Before I knew it, your sentiment grew so prominent, and yet when we first met you were a far stronger person……you have become quite weak."

A wry smile, then a gentle one.

"Your heart has grown rich. ……If there is a world after death, I would dearly love to tell Bogan-dono, chest swelling with pride."

"……"

Krische could say nothing, merely letting her tears be wiped away.

Elrouga watched with satisfaction, wiping each drop until they ran dry.

"……Quinez. Having served as my aide all these years, you possess experience, ability, and talent beyond reproach. You are a man worthy of succeeding me. ……I wish to entrust my soldiers and the military academy to you."

"……Sir."

The stout adjutant, who had been listening in silence, set his jaw, clicked his heels, and saluted.

"I do not know how you truly felt about me……but as my subordinate and comrade-in-arms, I trusted you above all others. ……There are aspects I find slovenly, but that bad habit of yours——always wanting to consider matters with some margin——will become a strength in a rear-echelon posting."

Sir, Quinez answered again.

His lips trembled faintly.

"……I leave everything to you. With that, I am at peace. You are an excellent man who has never once betrayed my expectations."

"……Understood, Elrouga-sama."

That will do, Elrouga laughed, and Quinez wiped his eyes and laughed as well.

"If I may speak honestly, you were someone I found rather difficult, sir. Strict and always making impossible demands……and even when you laughed, your smile was frightening."

Then once more he clicked his heels and gave a formal salute.

"……But having served as your adjutant is the pride of my life."

"Heh, is that so."

"Yes, I swear it on this name."

Quinez answered, and then came a knock at the door.

Pardon me, said the slender noblewoman who appeared.

Wearing a beautiful red one-piece dress, golden hair swaying gracefully, she glanced at Krische and shot Elrouga a look.

"My, to think you would make Krische-sama cry when she's gone to the trouble of nursing you. How terrible."

"Ah, this isn't——"

"Whatever he may have said, it's nonsense. Please pay it no mind."

Vanatela smiled, gently pried Elrouga's hand from Krische's cheek, and dabbed at her eyes with a handkerchief.

As always, unchanging, Vanatela wore a bright smile.

"You really do have a sharp tongue. What exactly do you think I am?"

"A wife may say whatever she likes about her husband. Because you're bedridden looking like a bag of bones, Reifas is too scared to even come out of his room. Before complaining, I'd like you to appreciate the trouble I'm going through."

Vanatela spoke as though angry, then leaned down to bring her face close to Krische.

"……Thank you so much for this past week, Krische-sama. You have shown my husband far more kindness than he deserves."

She traced the dark circles under Krische's eyes with the handkerchief, as if gazing at something painful.

Vanatela knew well that she had barely slept beyond brief naps.

"……Any more than this and it will harm your health. Please rest."

"But……"

"It is all right. I was prepared for this from the day I married my husband, and he has accepted it as well——there is no greater happiness than being able to laugh like this with the end in sight."

Isn't that right, Vanatela asked Elrouga, and the old man nodded, the corners of his mouth pulling upward.

As always, a gentle smile that looked wicked.

"I have lived my life to the fullest. These last ten years especially surpassed all else……it is thanks to Krische-sama. Parting is a lonely thing, but if drawn out, it will only leave regrets. ……This is for the best."

"……Skeleton."

"As you can see. ……Please do not trouble yourself. Go and rest."

Vanatela smiled, then turned her gaze to Bery.

Bery nodded, stroked Krische's head, and said, shall we go.

After a moment's hesitation, Krische nodded and let herself be led by the hand.

"Skeleton, um……"

"……I am glad to have met you. Truly, from the bottom of my heart, Krische-sama."

She had been about to say *see you tomorrow*, but wavered, and lowered her eyes.

"Yes. ……Krische is glad to have met Skeleton too."

She said that, and stepped outside the door.

Quinez too gave a salute and followed the two of them.

Left in the room were only two.

Vanatela stroked Elrouga's cheek and smiled.

"Hehe, you've gone and become even more skeletal. At this rate, after the cremation I might suspect you were still underdone."

"Truly……your rude manner of speaking hasn't changed since the day we met."

"My. I was quite a bit more reserved back then, properly."

Shall I make some tea, Vanatela asked, and Elrouga nodded quietly.

Despite her manner of speaking, Vanatela brewed the tea with the utmost care and arranged the teaware.

"The funeral will be such a bother. I might regret not having chosen a younger husband."

"I suggested exactly that at the start, but you were the one who ignored me."

"……Was that so?"

Vanatela smiled wryly, propped Elrouga up, and brought the teacup to his lips.

Slowly, so as not to spill, she gave Elrouga his tea.

"If only you had been about twenty years younger. By any measure you were born far too early. If you had been, then we could have……"

She started to say it, then stopped.

Then she smiled softly.

"No fair bringing that up. ……I suppose I should have been born twenty years earlier."

Vanatela set the teacup down and narrowed her eyes.

Slowly, she pressed her lips to her husband's.

"……I'll love you forever. Even after you're gone, for eternity."

Elrouga accepted it, and smiled.

"And I you. ……Which is precisely why I would like you, still young, to find someone new."

"No. Hehe, I've grown so used to your face that I can't imagine anyone else. ……It's your fault."

She kissed him once more, settled into the chair by the bedside.

Then she leaned against Elrouga and closed her eyes.

"There's no one better than you, Elrouga. ……At least, not for me."

"I have done a sinful thing."

"Yes, you're terrible. ……Take responsibility until the very end."

After that, words fell silent.

Elrouga raised his trembling hand and stroked the back of his wife, who trembled more than he did.

Words were no longer needed.

The funeral came four days later.

Lady Vanatela of House Faren even managed smiles for the mourners, comforting her weeping son.

Some spoke admiringly of her composure in never shedding a tear; those who knew nothing of the truth said it had been a loveless marriage.

Given the age difference, such was to be expected.

Despite such eyes upon her, Vanatela did not waver, remaining resolute throughout.

She herself placed the kindling on the body, and even as she gathered the cremated bones into the urn, her composure never faltered.

"……Thank you very much, Your Majesty the Queen. Margravine Christand."

Royalty and those close to it——or those who had rendered great service to the kingdom.

In the royal cemetery where such people were interred, Vanatela bowed in thanks together with her son and the household servants.

"Please think nothing of it, Vanatela-sama. Faren-sama was a loyal servant who devoted himself to the kingdom; he deserves to be sent off with a state funeral and rest here. ……In mingling blood, may he find peaceful eternity."

Kreschenta, clad in a dress as red as blood, spoke these words, clasped her hands, and offered a brief prayer.

Selene, in military uniform, watched, then addressed Vanatela.

"……I'm sorry. I know you didn't want such a grand funeral."

"No. ……It is the result of what my husband accomplished, and that it was recognized. As his wife, nothing makes me prouder."

Even if the bereaved wished for a quiet funeral, there was the matter of station.

It was something even Selene could do nothing about.

A state funeral was warranted, but considering Vanatela's feelings, it left a complicated impression.

"……And Krische-sama and Bery-sama as well. Thank you both from the bottom of my heart, for giving my husband time together at the end, even if only a brief while. ……And for all the help you gave."

"……No."

Wearing an unfamiliar military uniform, Krische shook her head.

Vanatela smiled wryly, then leaned in close.

"Hehe, such a face doesn't suit you at all. My husband was always saying he loved Krische-sama's smiling face……if you look like that, he'll be sad."

"……Yes."

Krische nodded, and Bery said, shall we be going then.

"Yes. ……If you'll excuse us, Vanatela-san."

"Of course. May we next meet with smiles."

Vanatela gave an elegant bow, and Krische's party did the same.

Kreschenta and Selene walked ahead, watching Krische's state, and Bery wrapped an arm around Krische's waist to follow.

They walked in silence for a time, and just before boarding the carriage, Krische turned to look back.

She saw Vanatela, face bowed and covered with both hands before the grave, and Reifas clinging to her.

Krische stared, then lowered her eyes.

Bery said nothing, simply took her hand and led her into the carriage.

The carriage headed for the royal district.

Kreschenta tried to say something, but seeing her sister's state, gave up.

Selene gazed at Krische with worry, lost in thought.

Anne and Elvena, the two servants, were the same——the mood was too heavy for words.

Only the sound of wheels rolling over cobblestones broke the silence, and after a while, when they arrived before the manor, Kreschenta turned to Selene.

"Selene-sama, I'd like to discuss future matters."

She said, and,

"Of course. My room?"

Selene agreed.

Then the two turned their gaze to Bery, and Bery nodded quietly.

Then Selene glanced at Anne and Elvena, and they nodded.

I'll go on ahead, Selene said, and the three followed.

After a short pause, Bery lifted Krische into her arms and said softly.

"……Shall we rest a little?"

Krische nodded, and was carried to her room just like that.

Changed out of the military uniform, into a negligee.

"……Why do people die?"

Sitting on the bed, Krische let the words fall, drop by drop.

Bery thought for a moment, then smiled.

"A very difficult question. If asked why people die, the only answer I know is: because they were born that way."

She sat beside Krische, stroking the smooth silver hair, and narrowed her eyes.

"No matter what it may be, all things have a beginning and an end. From cooking and laundry and kitchen work, to thoughts and stories. Why do we die, as a question, leads directly to why were we born."

Then she looked up at the ceiling.

"Setting aside the biological reasoning, if I were to define it deliberately……I suppose death is a result."

"……A result?"

"Making a meal is enjoyable, but the final purpose is to set it on the table and have it eaten. And whether you yourself are satisfied with the finished product——that is the result, isn't it?"

Searching for words even as she wove them, Bery let herself fall straight backward.

She sank back onto the bed.

"Since death is the fate of all who are born, perhaps we were born in order to die. ……Regardless, if the person can accept death as that result, then no matter what anyone says, it can be called a happy ending."

Gazing at the canopy of the bed,

"Everything accumulated over a lifetime is contained there, and they were satisfied with that result."

Then she took a strand of red hair in hand, playing with it as she smiled.

"……Faren-sama and Gahren-sama……the head of the house and nee-sama too. They all smiled in the face of their own death. They said it was a good life."

For a while Krische sat in silence, head bowed.

Then she turned toward Bery and buried her face in her chest as if collapsing.

"Bery too?"

"Hehe, yes. Someday I too will……ah."

Then lips sealed the lips that had been about to speak.

Staring intently with those jewel-like violet eyes,

"……Krische absolutely refuses."

She declared, one-sidedly.

Bery laughed softly and said, please don't worry, as she cradled Krische's head.

"It's something far, far in the future. After I've become a wrinkled old grandmother……hehe, by then Krische-sama may have changed so much that she won't even recognize who I am. Perhaps you won't even kiss me."

"That would never happen."

Bery smiled wryly and stroked Krische's head.

Silver hair ran through her fingers like silk, tickling as it went.

"……You said you'd stay by Krische's side forever."

"……Well, this is a problem."

Bery said happily, and thought a moment.

Then she rolled Krische onto her side and pushed her down.

Krische, yielding entirely, looked up at Bery and said,

"Bery doesn't break promises."

Bery's eyes widened, then narrowed.

She kissed her tenderly, and smiled.

"Then I shall have to work very hard, sticking to Krische-sama so closely that she grows sick of me, for the next several decades."

"……Several decades isn't forever."

"Hehe, unlike ojou-sama, talking Krische-sama around seems quite the ordeal."

Once more, she gently pressed their lips together.

Then she rose and lifted Krische into the bed.

And while stroking her forehead, she said,

"Please don't worry. I am terribly bad at seeing Krische-sama's sad face, so I won't do anything to make Krische-sama sad."

"……Really?"

"Yes. Hehe, I'm not honest enough to be proud of before the world, but I would never lie to Krische-sama."

I swear it on this name, Bery continued, and Krische nodded quietly.

"For now, please rest a little. When things are hard, that's the best thing to do. As Vanatela-sama said, Faren-sama will be sad if Krische-sama makes such a face. Let us make sure you can stand before his grave with a smile next time you visit."

As her forehead was stroked, Krische asked absently.

"……Does Bery believe in souls too?"

"As Galla-sama once said, it seems far happier to believe they exist than to believe they don't. ……I'm sure Faren-sama is watching over Vanatela-sama and Reifas-sama and Krische-sama even now."

Bery answered with a smile, and said, the same goes for me.

"Even if someday I die, I'm sure I'll watch over Krische-sama——"

"……You mustn't say things like that."

"Hehe, I'm sorry."

Bery smiled wryly and covered Krische's eyes.

It's a promise, Krische said, and it's a promise, Bery answered.

Krische closed her eyes, and she must have been exhausted.

Before long, she was breathing softly in her sleep.

Bery simply gazed at her sleeping face, with tender affection.

# Chapter 232: Turning Point

Eight years since the Five-Nations War——Elderant.

In the forest, dappled sunlight illuminated several small graves.

Before them, offerings of deep blue kilme flowers, beautiful in the light.

Before those graves, a half-naked man swung a greatsword nearly his own height, as if dancing.

Against imaginary foes he dodged the blade, slashed, and continued his dance, scattering sweat from every part of his body.

His well-formed face bore an unkempt beard that had gone unshaved for who knew how long.

His body, sculpted as if carved, was covered in countless scars.

Proof of surviving deep wounds across innumerable desperate battles——despite being mere training, his movements held a ferocity that bordered on killing intent.

"……Toba, is it."

He stopped his sword-dance and fixed his gaze on a single point among the trees.

His concealment was good enough that even a wild beast would not have noticed, yet it was seen through. The man dropped from the treetop with an air of exasperation.

Red hair slicked back, a face that looked somehow frivolous.

He wore a jade tiger pelt and carried a spear on his shoulder.

On his left ear, the black feather ornament marking the great chieftain of the great tribe Akaz.

The young king of Elderant, Toba Akaz.

"With a celebration feast going on, here you are training gloomily at a graveyard, Red. Your dream was the unification of Elderant——"

"——That was Shelna's dream. All it amounts to is a change of kings. It's not what Shelna aimed for."

Red Lani cut in.

Then he looked toward the small grave adorned with kilme flowers.

"……Besides, my purpose lies beyond that."

"I figured you might have had a change of heart, but no luck."

Toba scratched his head.

"Feni's been looking for you. I know you were head over heels for Shelna, but how about making a living woman happy instead of a dead one? It's funny coming from me, but my sister's got good looks, and unlike me, she's an honest, good girl."

She's crazy about you too, he said, settling onto the root of a large tree.

"You've fought enough. Shelna would want you to be happy, not out chasing revenge."

Earnest, caring words.

Red drove his greatsword into the earth and answered.

"……I'm grateful to you for taking us in."

He clenched his fist as he spoke.

"But either way. ……The result of that war made it clear. Sooner or later, every nation that could stand against Alberan will be gone. In a few decades, Elderant will be bowing its head to Alberan too. Licking Alberan's boots and begging for mercy——that's not for me."

He turned his gaze east——toward the kingdom.

"If we're going to strike, now is the last chance. To seize western Alberan and sow the seeds of chaos in the capital region. Gather up every faction with a grudge against you, and crush them in one breath. If the chaos lasts, Elsren will join in too. Not bad odds for a gamble."

"Well, you're not wrong about that……I've heard old Keesliton's gotten quite weak too. If we're going to move, now's the only time."

This was information from spies.

A few years ago, he had ceded the general's post to his adjutant on grounds of poor health, staying on as an advisor.

His poor health seemed genuine; lately he was rarely making public appearances.

Given his age, this was only natural.

He had already passed not just a hundred, but a hundred and twenty.

It was a wonder that he still stood as an obstacle to Elderant.

Red spoke.

"The failure last time was getting bogged down in the advance on the center. All three nations were stopped cleanly at the perimeter, unable to create any chaos. The cause was their thorough preparations——and Alberinea's overwhelming strength."

"Ha, she went around the entire country and won overwhelmingly three times. A ridiculous monster, that one. ……Honestly, I never dreamed she'd be brought down, whatever the outcome."

Toba fixed Red with a sharp look.

"……You're talented. You're probably the strongest warrior in Elderant now. ……But I don't think you're better than Shelna. Practically speaking, what are you going to do about Alberinea?"

"Like you say. I'm not arrogant enough to think I can beat her in a sword fight."

Red said, and approached a rock beside the graves.

He gathered mana, wedged his hand into the gap between the rock and the ground, lifted it, and rolled it aside.

Beneath it was a jar.

"……What's that?"

Silently, Red opened the lid and drew out a single mana crystal.

"……Priestly staves I collected during the wars——their mana crystals. Thirteen of them."

Inscribed upon them was a formula for firing mana.

Toba's eyes widened. He glared at Red for an instant——but after a moment, his look turned to exasperation.

"I see. I'd been wondering why the blue staves kept disappearing. You were pilfering them. Lying through your teeth with a straight face."

"Sorry."

Every tribe in Elderant had a high priest who presided over ceremonies.

Their symbol was a staff, and many of these staves had mana crystals mounted upon them.

Mana crystals inscribed with advanced formulae that could discharge mana like a flash of light.

The 'blue staves' left behind by Elderant's sages were treasures that every tribe's people had to defend with their lives.

During this Elderant unification war, many had been lost along the way, causing an uproar——but the culprit, it seemed, had been right beside them all along.

"I can't win head-on. I'll take it in stages. ……I've done my research, and Alberinea's battles are simple and straightforward: headhunting. She moves to personally take the enemy commander's head and end the battle early. With the skill to cut down Shelna that easily, she'd have nothing to fear."

Red pointed to his own neck.

"I use my head as bait to draw her in, then end it with a simultaneous volley of blue staves at close range. No matter how fast she is, she can't dodge the flash of a blue staff. The first target isn't the Alberinea army——it's Alberinea alone."

Even if I die, as long as Alberinea dies, it's over, Red said.

"After that, split the army and send them running across the land. Let them rampage as they please. The goal of plunging Alberan into chaos will be achieved. ……We probably can't destroy Alberan, but at least if we throw the country into disorder, we can slow them down."

Toba narrowed his eyes at Red's words, thinking.

It's a gamble, Toba said.

It's a gamble, Red answered.

"Those dissatisfied with Akaz's unification of Elderant broke free, seeking liberty, and charged into Alberan——we can use something like that as a pretext. Whether I die or survive, things can't get worse than they are now. If things keep going as they are, Elderant will eventually have no choice but to submit to Alberan."

"……Awfully selfish of you. Afraid I can't cooperate."

"I know. ……If you're going to kill me, decide now."

Red turned his back on Toba and sat cross-legged on the spot.

"……You're right, Toba. Shelna wouldn't want this. This is my own selfish revenge. ……But even so, I can't stop until I've finished everything."

"……You won't reconsider?"

He pressed his spear against Red's back.

"……Injustice is everywhere, but so is happiness, just as much. Enough that you'd stumble into some without even looking. If you just changed your way of thinking."

Only countless scars were there.

Having fought alongside him, Toba knew how many desperate battles the man before him had endured.

"Stop driving yourself into a corner, Red. You've done more than enough. ……Take a wife, make a family, and use your head, not your sword, at my side to build the new Elderant you and Shelna dreamed of."

Put down the sword, Red, he repeated.

"Think it over. ……And you still haven't repaid the debt of me taking you in."

"Because I owe you, I left the choice to you. I won't complain if you kill me."

With his back turned, blade pressed against him, Red answered.

Upright, without a trace of fear.

"……You'd make my sister cry?"

"I can't think of happiness in a world where the ones who killed Shelna and the rest are living without a care."

"……Damn you."

Toba threw down his spear and scratched his head.

He thought a while, then spoke.

"I regret saving you. I'll let it slide, but there's a condition."

"……What?"

"It'll be a year or so. ……Until then, live with Feni."

Red frowned and turned around.

Toba's eyes were dead serious.

"……It'll only make her sad."

"Good that you're aware. You're the one who made her fall for you. Take responsibility. ……If you get a kid in her, at least it'll be some comfort even after you're gone."

Red lowered his eyes.

"I'm afraid I can't make her hap——"

"Die here, or accept the condition. That's all I'm asking."

Toba looked down at Red with something like pity, and sighed.

"Try living like a human being first, Red. ……If you still think it's impossible after that, do as you please. Well, either way——any bastard who makes my sister cry is dead to me."

Then he picked up the spear he'd thrown down and turned his back.

"I'll explain the situation to Feni myself. Come see me after."

Without waiting for an answer, he walked away.

Red watched him go, stared at the greatsword driven into the earth, then turned his gaze to the small graves.

The graves of his mentor, his comrades-in-arms, and his beloved.

The deep blue petals seemed to float, lifted by the sunlight.

*'I thought I'd try growing kilme. ……I've never thought about growing something before.'*

He had given them to her as a child, saying they resembled her eyes.

What a beautiful blue, he'd said.

*'……Will they grow properly, I wonder. I hope they turn out a beautiful blue.'*

It was not a particularly rare flower.

But from then on, every time she saw kilme in bloom, she was delighted.

The reason she started growing them was probably something that small.

"……They're a beautiful blue this year too, Shelna."

He bowed his head, and continued.

"The kilme you were growing. ……Tell me what you think."

No answer came to that trembling voice.

Only a faint breeze, brushing through the trees, sounded softly.

* * *

"So Elderant has been unified under Akaz. It's not Veze or Mekrea, but at least the west should settle down for now. Though one can never tell with barbarians."

Wearing a white one-piece dress, feet kicking idly on the bed with no regard for propriety.

Queen Kreschenta handed the bundle of parchment to Elvena and surveyed the dark-faced Selene.

She toyed with golden hair that gleamed red, and furrowed her brows in displeasure.

"Are you listening? National defense is your job."

"Oh, I'm sorry. Elderant, right."

In her usual military uniform, Selene had been staring into her tea. She thought a moment, then answered.

"The current western general is General Keesliton's right hand, an experienced officer. If anything happens, I think we can leave a fair amount to him."

Felwors Keesliton's right arm——Tolka Karnarius.

Having served as Felwors's adjutant for many years, he was more than capable as one of the four generals guarding the borders.

With Felwors remaining as an advisor, it was more than sufficient.

"……That said, we need to think about the next western general. They're both quite elderly. I have a few candidates, but……"

Nozan Velraigh.

Elzaldo Gokkalus.

Dagren Garkha.

The three generals of east, north, and south each had capable sons.

Dagren's and Elzaldo's sons were currently serving as corps commanders and adjutants in their respective armies, while Nozan's son was presently at the military academy.

On that front there were no issues, but Felwors's son had died in battle and he had only a daughter, and Tolka, while married, had not been blessed with children.

Felwors's son-in-law was no warrior, and Tolka's adopted son, while reasonably capable, had been left with lasting injuries to his leg from the Five-Nations War.

From there, the path forward was difficult.

The remaining candidates were Aleha and Teknrea——the married couple of House Remin.

And the various corps commanders.

There was nothing wrong with Aleha. Teknrea too was a sound choice considering future growth, but that would thin the central general ranks.

On top of that, both seemed quite content with how things were, and given the capable officers among the corps commanders, it seemed most reasonable to choose from there.

"I'll leave that to you. Please select someone suitable in consultation with onee-sama."

"……Right. I'll discuss it with Krische when she gets back."
