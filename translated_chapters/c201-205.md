# Chapter 201: Side Story — The Hero's Tale of the Beginning

In a forest.

Several dozen soldiers had gathered around a campfire, and at the center stood two men.

"What do you think you're doing, Christand? I told you not to pursue too far."

Roughly cut hair, grown out and swept back. The one glaring with sharp eyes was the centurion carrying a bow — Gallen.

Facing him, Corporal Bogan met that gaze with a bold composure befitting his large build and stern features.

"Sir. I was aware of the order — but it was not a reckless pursuit. It was within range for me to finish them off, and in fact, I did."

Swaying his slightly overgrown golden hair, Bogan stated this coolly.

He had taken down a man equivalent to the enemy's second-in-command — not a bad achievement.

"……I acknowledge you possess exceptional ability. Your achievement is well and good. But you are a corporal who left my command on his own judgment — and the question is whether such independent action is permissible. Three men were wounded. Had you not abandoned your command, that might have been prevented."

"Sir. But all three can resume combat after a brief rest. They barely count as injuries."

"……Centurion Gallen, the casualties are my responsibility for having been temporarily entrusted with the squad by Corporal Christand."

The one who spoke up from among the men surrounding them was a handsome young man with copper-red hair.

It was Nozan.

Despite his words, his eyes directed something like hostility at Gallen.

Gallen met that gaze and laughed.

"A snot-nosed brat who doesn't even know the principle of responsibility, talking big. You are a lance corporal, not a corporal, and merely a squad leader under Christand's command. I gave no order for you to take command of the squad, nor did I give Christand any such instruction."

Then he narrowed his eyes.

"……Hold your tongue. The responsibility lies with Christand, and then with me. Come back when you can wipe your own arse."

Nozan's face showed open displeasure, but he said, "My apologies, sir," and took a step back.

Gallen returned his gaze to Bogan.

"……Are you fighting alone, Christand? Do you believe your judgment is more correct than mine, and more correct than the battalion commander's, and more correct than the corps commander's, the general's?"

"……No, sir. It is not."

"That officer's head may be a small prize to you, but it is a fine achievement. You must be pleased. But wars are not won until you take the head of a higher-ranking enemy commander — the enemy general. You are like a mutt that lunges at the bait right in front of its nose."

Bogan furrowed his brow, and Gallen stepped closer, glaring down at him.

"The primary objective of the mission was to raid the enemy supply train. An unavoidable chance encounter is one thing. But your recklessness and its results will have put the enemy on higher alert. At the very least, it's a result that conflicts with my intent — and if this accumulates, it will affect even the battalion commander's and corps commander's tactical plans. Do you understand that?"

"……Sir."

"And understand this: that kind of behavior will one day cost not only your own life, but the lives of your comrades. ……That's all for today. Everyone, return to your positions. Take your rest."

Gallen said this and left the scene. The men who had been watching likewise settled in to rest.

Nozan approached Bogan, who stood with a disgruntled expression.

"……He's certainly full of lectures. Tiresome, isn't it, Corporal."

"His logic is sound. It's all correct."

Even as he said this, he looked unsettled as he walked over to a tree, leaned back against it, and sat down.

Nozan followed and settled against the tree next to him.

"I'm in the mood to curse the circumstances of my birth. Had I not been born the third son of a baron's house, I wouldn't be covered in mud, nor would I have to take orders from a boar-chasing huntsman."

"Don't say that. At least the captain is a master archer. His command isn't bad either."

Captain Gallen could not wield mana, but he was no incompetent — he had risen through the ranks from the very bottom.

He was not a bad superior officer.

"……A bit timid, though."

But the problem was that he erred too far on the side of caution.

Gallen feared the attrition of his men far too much.

"Indeed. It's because he lacks the skill that we have."

Nozan agreed with a laugh.

He was the youngest in the squad, yet brimming with talent — in swordsmanship, he was second only to Bogan within the unit.

This was not contempt but fact.

Noble and commoner — the presence or absence of mana was an absolute divide on the battlefield.

No matter the situation, a mana-wielder could often fight through it.

But Gallen was not a mana-wielder.

His timidity surely came from that.

Being assigned to the special light infantry squad rather than a regular line infantry unit had been well and good.

What had been disappointing was that their captain was a commoner who could not wield mana.

A captain's ability naturally influenced the tactical options available.

Gallen was faithful to his duties, strict in discipline, a man worthy of respect as a soldier — but he had the flaw of fearing the squad's attrition far too much.

Following him and achieving results in this unit was no simple matter.

"The road to generalship is long. Well, this too is an education. Compared to a man born into a battalion commander's seat, the experience we can gain is surely more valuable."

"That's an outlook I'd like to emulate. I look forward to you rising quickly and pulling me up after you, Corporal."

— Several days later.

His stern face enclosed in a helm, Bogan glared through the thicket ahead.

"……What will you do, wait for the captain's orders?"

The one who asked with a grin was Nozan.

Bogan grimaced and fell deep into thought.

"Getting caught a second time would be undesirable, but……"

Before them, an Elderant supply train marched along the road — eighty wagons, roughly one hundred fifty escorts.

Flanked by forest on east and west, it was a perfect opportunity for an ambush.

To the west of the forest, on this side, were Bogan's fifty men; to the east, Centurion Gallen's fifty.

A coordinated pincer attack. To sweep up the enemy in a single stroke.

That had been the plan, but the mounted man who appeared to be the enemy commander was not in the expected center or rear — he was at the head.

Gallen, who had been circling around, had not yet reached his position.

The assault was to begin when Gallen sent his signal — but at this rate, there was a risk of letting the target escape.

Elderant people made the forest their home. Once they fled, pursuit would be difficult.

A pincer attack was certainly advantageous, but letting the opportunity slip for its sake was a fool's errand.

There was no reason not to go now.

And Nozan was here. That alone made the situation manageable.

"It's now or never. Nozan."

"Sir."

He raised his left hand and signaled to the rear.

The enemy appeared completely unguarded. They walked along the road chatting with the men beside them.

The enemy commander, wearing no helm, with braided hair swaying.

The man occasionally glanced around, apparently watching for danger, but his subordinates were the problem.

"……Let's go."

He tossed a small stone across to the far side of the road, rustling the brush.

The enemy soldiers glanced left for an instant, and in that instant he drew his longsword and charged ahead of the others.

Leaping over the thicket, he burst onto the road and cut down one. Nozan followed and cut down another.

"Enemy atta——"

Then he struck the head from the enemy commander, who had been about to raise the alarm.

A terrified face — he had been young. Inexperienced, likely.

A few more fell to his sword, and then his own men came pouring in after him.

But the complete lack of resistance made Bogan feel a flicker of wrongness.

Elderant was a warrior society — strength was fundamentally valued over pedigree.

Naturally, enemy commanders were formidable to some degree, yet both the escorts and the commander had been far too weak.

Those terrified eyes just before death — what did this mean?

Bogan thought, and the answer came at once.

"——There he is, at the head!! Go!!"

The one who bellowed was a man from the middle of the column.

He tore off his helm and shouted, and soldiers emerged from the wagons beside him.

Tattooed faces, bearded — and there was the great discrepancy.

The warriors they had fought before, the ones who appeared to be elites — they had all looked like that.

Tattoos and beards — likely symbols marking warriors within the tribe.

"We've been had, Nozan."

"The sort of trick you'd expect from savages."

But Bogan did not lose his composure, and neither did Nozan.

There was some confusion among their men, but they were elite light infantry too. It was no major problem.

The enemy's ploy was merely a small trick — the men who emerged from the wagons numbered around thirty at most.

Those were the only disguised wagons. One hundred fifty had simply become one hundred eighty.

He considered that perhaps this was the result of heightened alertness caused by their earlier actions, just as Gallen had warned, but now was not the time for such thoughts.

"I'll take the vanguard."

"Go."

The words barely left his mouth before Nozan was charging at the enemy commander.

"Leave the enemy commander to me and Nozan! The rest of you, crush the escorts!!"

Bogan shouted to his men, and immediately gave chase.

Nozan Velraigh's sword was flowing — he cut through the enemy commander's guards with dazzling grace.

——But the enemy was no ordinary foe.

The great spear he wielded tore through the air, driving at Nozan.

He barely dodged, but the follow-up sweep could not be avoided entirely, and Nozan was forced to block it with his sword.

His body was sent flying. He took a breakfall and rose instantly, but the enemy was a fraction faster.

Bogan drew his short blade and threw it. The man bent back to dodge, then leaped far to the rear.

The distance widened.

"Not bad, for Alberan's gutless lot. My name is Geilbiig of Mekrea. Remember the name of the one who will kill you."

——A formidable mana-wielder.

An opponent who had overpowered Nozan head-on — whether even Bogan could win was uncertain.

Still, retreat was not an option.

"The name's Bogan Christand. Remember it, savage!"

He shouted and charged. The enemy chose a thrust.

He struck the man's great spear aside with his left gauntlet, knocking it off course, and closed inside the weapon's reach.

The man gave a slight retreat, twisting his body.

He turned his back toward Bogan — Bogan's confusion, and in that instant's opening, the butt-spike of the spear shot out from beneath the man's arm.

Bogan pulled back reflexively, but not enough.

The butt-spike struck his leather-armored stomach — not piercing, but the impact shuddered through him.

"Tch——Corporal!!"

From the right Nozan closed in and swung at the man.

But even that the man dodged, kicking Nozan in the torso to send him flying before turning back toward Bogan.

"It's over!!"

Cheeks twitching into a grin, he thrust the great spear forward.

Bogan could not recover his stance in time and chose to defend —

But then——

"——Wha?"

An arrow pierced the man's temple from the side.

His eyes rolled upward, and he crumbled.

"The enemy commander is dead! Finish off the rest!!"

The one who had loosed the shot was Gallen, emerging from the eastern forest.

Without so much as a glance at the commander's head, he gave Bogan — who was pulling himself upright — a single look.

"Kouz, your squad will support Christand until he's fit to command again!"

"Sir!"

"The rest of you, to the rear. Moraz, take half your men to the tail end. Priority is burning the supply train. Driving off the enemy will suffice."

Having issued his orders, Gallen nocked another arrow.

Short but rigid — his personal war bow, drawn with ease, and the arrows it loosed pierced enemy after enemy.

At a speed and accuracy that allowed no common foe to close.

He did not boast of his feat. He simply, steadily, put down the enemy.

"Corporal Christand, are you injured?"

"……No. Nozan?"

"Ngh……I'm fine as well."

Nozan rose, coughing.

The large squad leader Kouz looked at the two of them as if they were troublesome charges.

"Captain Gallen's orders. Reorganize immediately."

"……Understood."

At the very least, he had no intention of defying that order.

They finished the cleanup and pulled back.

Gallen said nothing.

He let it go — he would not even hear their apology. Only the bare minimum.

It was not until two nights later — after they had returned to friendly lines and safety was secured — that he finally spoke about the matter.

"——Well, no amount of lecturing will change you, I expect. Draw your sword, Christand."

"Sir……?"

"Velraigh too. I'll take you both at once."

Bogan and Nozan exchanged glances, then looked at the smiling Gallen.

"If you two win, I'll leave the army on those terms. Christand, I'll recommend you to the battalion commander as fit for centurion at that time. Do as you like with the squad."

"B-but Captain Gallen……"

"Hold your tongue, Kouz. ……What's that look, Christand? Dissatisfied?"

Gallen directed a faintly mocking gaze at Bogan.

Bogan frowned, met his eyes, and spoke.

"Your anger is justified. I acknowledge you saved us from a dire situation. ……But is that not taking us too lightly? I have no desire to humiliate you. Neither one of us alone — Captain Gallen would not stand a chance."

"I'm asking whether you'll do it or not, Christand."

He tapped the scabbard of the short sword at his hip and laughed.

"Heh, they say a lapdog raised in a rich city can do nothing but bark, and it seems exactly right."

The words were an unmistakable provocation.

Both Bogan and Nozan now directed open hostility at Gallen, glaring.

"……I am still a noble, however minor. Even from a superior officer, an insult to my pride is not something I can laugh off."

"Pride is all you have, Christand."

"……I gave you fair warning. Nozan."

Nozan drew his sword, visibly irritated.

Fighting two against one against a man who could not even use mana — that itself was a disgrace.

Nozan took his stance, and Gallen, looking exasperated, set his bow and quiver on the ground.

Then he drew the short sword, and —

"I believe I said I'd take you both at once."

"Tch——!!"

At those words Nozan lunged.

In a single breath he closed five ken, raised his sword high, and —

"Wha——!?"

What was hurled at his face was the scabbard of the short sword.

To dodge it, Nozan twisted aside reflexively, losing his balance as he passed Gallen's flank.

But Gallen hooked Nozan's foot as he tried to slip by, tripping him.

And before the tumbling Nozan could rise, he threw a short knife into the ground right beside his face.

"That makes you a battlefield casualty. You're from a noble house — they'll hold a grand funeral."

Nozan was speechless, frozen. Gallen smiled and turned his gaze to Bogan.

"What are you waiting for? ……Won't you come?"

"……That was merely a trick."

"Indeed, Velraigh was an idiot who died to a mere trick. But, well, you'll likely make a similar spectacle of yourself."

Bogan, too, drew his sword.

"……I cannot promise I won't injure you."

"Why not stop talking and show me that vaunted sword?"

He clicked his tongue and charged.

In a single stride he closed most of the distance, circling to Gallen's swordless left hand.

He swung the longsword from a high guard, and —

"——!?"

Gallen dove toward Bogan's feet as though falling.

He caught Bogan's legs and lifted, rising in the same motion to slam that large frame into the earth.

Then he pinned Bogan's right arm and torso underfoot, pressing the point of the short sword to his throat.

"That makes you a casualty too. ……That the corporal and squad leader of this unit are both such idiots — how disheartening."

Stunned, Bogan stared at the short sword leveled at his own throat, then looked up at Gallen.

There was no smile on Gallen's face. Raptor-sharp eyes pierced through Bogan.

"……Well, Christand? Any objections?"

Not a single watcher uttered a word. The air was frozen.

Gallen showed no sign of raising his voice.

And yet it was a voice that seemed to reverberate through the very organs.

"……None, sir. Captain."

At those words, Gallen withdrew the short sword and removed his foot from Bogan's arm.

"What you say is true. Both you and Velraigh surpass me in every ability. In terms of strength, there is no comparison. But you two are overconfident in your own power. Offered the chance to become centurion if you win — a rare opportunity — you clung to pride, refused to cooperate, and fought separately."

Lying on the ground, Bogan listened.

"The result was that you threw away your own advantage and were defeated one by one. What would you call that tactically?"

"……The lowest of the low."

"Indeed. And on top of that, you insisted on fighting with swords. Did I set any rules? I only said to win. Had you done as I did — thrown the scabbard at me — it would have been over. Every single one of your weaknesses stems from overconfidence in your own strength and arrogance."

Gallen went to pick up the scabbard he had thrown, and only then did Bogan pull himself up.

Sheathing the short sword and re-fastening it to his hip, Gallen continued.

"You two can only beat opponents you can already beat. Worse, you'll even lose your lives to enemies you should have beaten — just like that. Why do you form squads? Why create units? What is tactics for?"

"……To defeat an enemy whose strength exceeds your own."

"Yes. In your hearts, stripped of pride, you already understand this. ……You learned it from the enemy you fought this time, didn't you? There are warriors among the enemy that even two of you together cannot defeat."

He thought of the enemy commander and clenched his fist.

At the very least, the opponent had been an equal or better — no, against two-to-one odds, they had failed to finish him and been driven into a corner.

Even if they had continued the fight, whether they could have slain him was doubtful.

"But even against such an opponent, a soldier is never permitted to lose. And so you secure every advantage in order to win. That is why I nag so much."

"…………"

"That said, I think you understand well enough without more lecturing. You already know, in your hearts, which of your choices were mistakes and what you should have done."

He came close and looked down once more.

Not with anger, but with eyes that held something gentle.

"You are young, and talented. ……First, strip away that arrogance, and learn as much as you can while there is still time. And to learn, acquire the art of staying alive."

With that, Gallen extended his right hand.

"There is no need to chase glory. With your abilities, in time you will find yourselves in a fitting position before you know it."

Bogan stared at that hand for a long moment.

Then he extended his own right hand in answer.

His body was pulled firmly to its feet.

Gallen looked at his face, nodded, and glanced at those around them.

"It seems Christand and Velraigh would like to take on meal duty today. A rare opportunity to boss around a corporal and a squad leader — enjoy it to the fullest."

Laughter rose from all around, and Gallen, too, smiled as he looked at Bogan.

"No objections, I trust? Christand."

Bogan stared in disbelief, exchanged a look with Nozan, who had come over.

Then, slowly, he placed his right hand over his left breast and saluted.

*I offer you my life* — that was the meaning the salute carried.

"……From now on, there is no need for my family name. Simply my name will do. Captain."

Gallen looked into his eyes and nodded once more, then —

"……Very well, Bogan. Get to work."

He pressed his thumb to his left breast.

*I accept your loyalty as my own blood* — that was the meaning that answer carried.

* * *

Seated in a chair, Bogan held a still-small Selene on his lap.

He was telling her a story from the old days.

It was about Gallen, who had visited the estate the day before.

"——And the one who saved me from that crisis was the captain."

"I-is that so……um, Father, the battlefield stories are really sca——"

"The captain appeared, and with a single arrow, pierced the enemy commander's skull."

"Eep……"

Bogan pressed his finger against the temple of little Selene's head.

Unaware of his cowering daughter, his mind was in the past.

"The arrow came flying, *whoosh*, and pierced through his skull, and I could see the man's eyes roll upward right before me. Truly unerring precision — surely the man didn't even have time to feel pai——"

"I-I don't want to hear any more of that story!"

"? Selene, where are you——"

Selene scrambled off his lap and broke into a run at the same moment the door opened.

"Oh, Ojou-sama, what's the matter……?"

"Beryyyy, I asked about Gallen-sama, and Father, *hic*, kept telling scary stories, and……"

Selene threw herself at Bery, who carried a tray of cookies, and burst into wailing tears.

Bery looked troubled, set the tray on a shelf, and picked Selene up.

"……Bogan, honestly."

Behind Bery, Lazura stood with her temple twitching, hands on her hips, glaring at Bogan.

Bogan stared in bewilderment at the crying Selene, then looked at Lazura.

"Bery, could you calm Selene down for me?"

"Um, yes, Nee-sama……Ojou-sama, it's all right. You're safe. ……I've just baked some cookies — would you like some? I'm quite proud of this batch, so I'm sure they'll……"

Bery carried Selene to the sofa with one arm.

Lazura, beautiful face alight with anger, beckoned the head of House Christand over.

"Bogan, come here."

"Ah, right……"

For some time after, Lazura's berating could be heard echoing through the room.

And Selene, too, did not stop crying until it was over.

# Chapter 202: Those Who Worry and Those Worried About

The morning sun streamed in — the time when she would normally be up had long since passed.

She sat up, petite yet womanly in her bare form.

Covering her face with both hands, Bery Argan heaved a sigh.

"……Ahh."

*——What have I done.*

The lamenting words had no voice behind them.

She had been awake for some time, but there was a part of her that did not want to be awake.

She had tried to keep pretending she was asleep, but in the end, you cannot fool yourself.

"Ehehe, good morning……"

Perhaps noticing that the arm wrapped around her had pulled away, Krische spoke in a soft, drowsy voice.

A small yawn, and she sat up, leaning against Bery, stretching.

"……?"

Tilting her head at Bery, who covered her face with both hands, she straddled Bery's thighs.

And then she peered at Bery's face, gazing over the covering hands.

"Is something wrong?"

"Hahh……it's nothing."

Giving in, she removed her hands and gazed at the girl straddling her.

She placed both hands on those cheeks, kissed her lightly, and smiled.

"……Good morning, Krische-sama."

"Yes, good morning, Bery."

Krische replied in high spirits, then fell into thought, and with a grin as though she had made a great discovery, said —

"Krische learned something for the first time."

"……Um?"

"Krische had always thought it was something done between a man and a woman, but it seems even between two women you can have interco——*mmph!*"

But her mouth was swiftly covered by a palm.

Her face scarlet, Bery leaned close and stared at Krische.

"……I understand what Krische-sama was about to say. The meaning is indeed not incorrect, but that word is terribly inappropriate — it is like calling a dish that has been served to you 'feed.' ……You are never to say it out loud."

*Understood?* Bery asked, and Krische, startled but obedient, gave a small nod.

Bery narrowed her eyes as though glaring, observed Krische's expression, and sighed once more.

"……Goodness. Let's get dressed and prepare breakfast. You must be hungry, yes?"

She released her hand from Krische's mouth and spoke with a wry smile.

"Ehehe, yes. Krische might be very hungry……"

A little embarrassed, yet honest, Krische answered.

* * *

Dusk — the great road connecting the capital and Wulfenite.

Inside a carriage on the return journey.

"Honestly though, you really are good at speeches. I have to admire how readily you can say things you don't mean at all."

"How rude. I haven't said a single thing I don't mean."

Kreschenta had, as always, settled sideways in the carriage with poor posture, leaning against the wall, and was patting Selene's thigh with her foot.

The gently smiling Queen of just moments ago had vanished entirely.

She watched Selene with a faintly mocking smile.

"I genuinely want them to do their best, and I genuinely don't want soldiers to die, and I genuinely don't want the territory ravaged — every bit of it is true. When I simply let my heart speak, that's what comes out."

"Ah, yes, yes……my apologies."

Selene replied, looking exasperated.

The Elsren Holy Empire's army — which Krische was scheduled to ultimately defeat — was the final phase.

Naturally, compared to the south and west, the eastern front would have to endure a prolonged defensive war, and the soldiers tasked with holding the line while on the defensive bore an enormous burden.

A defensive war was, at its core, a battle against morale.

While the attacking side had momentum, the psychological toll of receiving and deflecting the enemy's attacks was immense — and the longer it continued, the more conspicuously that toll manifested as declining morale.

On top of being attacked from three nations simultaneously, with no end in sight and reinforcements uncertain.

The mental exhaustion of soldiers forced to absorb the enemy's offensives in such a situation was incomparably greater than that of the commanders who knew Alberan's overarching strategy.

No matter how exceptional a commander Nozan Velraigh might be, he could not eliminate all of that dissatisfaction and fatigue.

And so — a personal battlefield inspection and address by Her Majesty the Queen.

The moment word of Krische's defeat of the Galshan forces reached the capital, Kreschenta had moved at once, heading east.

The purpose had been to give the soldiers hope, to convey that this war would not continue much longer, and to restore the flagging morale — and Kreschenta was, as expected, nothing short of a prodigy.

Not only at Wulfenite, but at every town they stopped at along the way, she repeated her speeches.

None were rote phrases — the content differed entirely each time.

Towns that had sent many soldiers, towns crowded with refugees, towns that had offered substantial financial support, towns that had not even the economic means to do so — she likely held information on every town across the entire kingdom in her head.

No — in some cases, even down to small towns and villages.

The words she opened with, the things she spoke of, varied greatly from town to town, and in some towns, depending on the circumstances, she offered only gratitude to the people rather than asking for cooperation.

A different speech for every town — even the escorting soldiers were visibly astonished, their respect for her only deepening, some moved to tears.

"I'm different from an incompetent who simply reads off prepared remarks. The right words for the right place — that's what governance is. It's easy to make fools happy, whether they're commoners or soldiers. You just give them the words they want to hear."

With a bit of embellishment, Kreschenta added, pointing to her own eyes with a fingertip.

She had shed tears with ease. When speaking with wounded soldiers, it was as though she were barely holding back her own tears — no one could possibly see through her performance.

Even Selene, watching up close, found it convincing enough to nearly believe.

In any case, Kreschenta was indeed the same kind of being as Krische.

The direction may have differed, but she was beyond the bounds of common sense all the same.

"Both you and Argan-sama really should show me more respect. If you bowed your heads and begged, 'Please teach us,' I might even teach you how to give a proper speech."

"Mm, I suppose. You're a fool, but I'll give you honest credit for that sort of thing. There, I'll even praise you."

"Hmph……"

Selene patted Kreschenta's head with a smile, and Kreschenta glared back, displeased.

"Would you teach me sometime, Your Majesty?"

"That sort of thing is precisely what I find irritating."

Selene giggled, shaking her shoulders, while Elvena, seated across from them, looked on with mild exasperation.

The incomprehensible power dynamics within House Christand — after this long an acquaintance, even she had come to grasp them.

The Queen's unguarded self was rather endearing, and Elvena smiled quietly.

"But the cheering and the respect……it truly was incredible."

The one who spoke up was beside her — Anne.

Kreschenta tilted her head and looked over.

"You say giving people the words they want to hear is simple, but it's far more difficult than that……and by doing so, you gave so many people hope and saved their hearts. Seeing Your Majesty's greatness with my own eyes, I was truly moved once more."

Recalling the scenes, Anne's eyes welled with what could only be called tears of admiration.

"How many times are you going to revise your opinion of me……"

This was already the seventh time.

Kreschenta looked faintly put-upon and cast a *do something about this* look at Selene.

Selene smiled with some amusement and returned the look — *It's fine, isn't it? She says she's moved.*

Since the start of this journey, Anne had been praising Kreschenta daily.

It was rather like a court flatterer — but Anne had no such intent. They were simply her honest, straightforward feelings.

"Though you speak of your own deeds so harshly, the care and consideration for the people that lies behind them……from the bottom of my heart, I am proud to serve Your Majesty."

"Y-your feelings are understood. Please stop now. I've heard more than enough to be sick of it."

"Yes, my apologies. I simply couldn't help but convey it……"

Anne sniffled, and Elvena dabbed at her eyes with a handkerchief from the side.

Selene watched the visibly fed-up Kreschenta and smiled wryly.

At heart, if one set aside the strategic benefits, Kreschenta likely could not care less about the people — but Anne seemed to believe it was all an act born of Kreschenta's inability to be honest with herself.

Looking at Kreschenta's usual behavior, one could see how she would arrive at that conclusion. Kreschenta's words were often harsh, but when it came down to it, she was a girl who was soft and kind to those close to her.

Indeed, judging solely by actions and results, Anne's understanding was not entirely wrong.

Selene patted the annoyed-looking Kreschenta's head again.

"Hehe, good for you. When we get home, Bery will have a delicious feast ready for you."

Kreschenta glared at Selene, then rolled over.

She lay across the seat and rested her head on Selene's thigh.

"Argan-sama, you don't suppose she's been brooding and making herself worse again?"

"……Worried?"

"As if. It would just be a nuisance."

Krische pouted at the horse archers charging straight toward her, and hurled a spear their way.

From space-compressing acceleration to savage, abrupt deceleration.

Channeling the full momentum of her body through mana alone, accelerating — bending her whole frame like a bow, what her hand released was a monstrous war-spear.

The horse archers — the leading rider was pierced through, and the spear did not stop, punching a hole through the human body.

Fragments sprayed into the riders behind, maiming and killing, and a single throw shattered the cavalry formation.

Terrified horses bolted, throwing their riders.

The horses these nomadic-born archers rode were light, swift breeds — fundamentally different from the specially trained heavy mounts used by close-combat cavalry.

No horse, having witnessed a spear effortlessly kill a dozen, could remain unfazed.

A single throw was enough for one squadron.

In the gap, she threw another spear and destroyed a trebuchet. Three remained.

But from other sections of the enemy line, still more mounted archers were pouring out.

"Mm, that's a lot of them."

Throwing spears while being peppered with arrows was a nuisance.

Deciding to take a break for now, she vaulted over the moat, caught a slight protrusion on the wall with her foot, and scaled the thirty-shaku height with ease.

*Phew,* she exhaled, and the soldiers on the wall roared louder still. Krische covered her ears, annoyed.

A panicked cry came next, and what bounded along the top of the wall was a magnificent jade tiger — eight shaku tall and two jou long.

She leaped onto Gururun without a word, and the tiger carried her down inside the wall.

She sighed, looked up at the wall, and pouted in irritation.

"……What noisy people."

"Hahaha, it can't be helped. They're overjoyed that Krische-sama has arrived."

Nozan dropped down, having apparently followed by leaping across rooftops, and Granmeld landed after him.

She could hear the other corps commanders on the walls, raising their voices.

*Now that Alberinea is here, there is nothing left for us to fear* — they declared to the soldiers above, one after another.

"How are you feeling?"

"Krische didn't overdo it, so she's fine. Just taking a break before finishing the last three."

Krische said this as she circled her shoulders and stretched her arms.

Granmeld laughed in amazement.

"Well, well. Even seeing it again, it's astonishing. How a body like that can do something like that."

She probably weighed less than a third of what he did.

The javelin throw of a small, slender girl defied every expectation her appearance created.

She destroyed massive trebuchets with a single spear.

Her mere existence, by itself, transformed the very nature of siege warfare.

Were this an offensive siege, she might well infiltrate the enemy camp alone at night and slay the commanding general.

In this situation, a single individual was worth more than an entire army.

"I've also heard you challenged an ancient dragon and were granted its true name, just recently……"

"Um, Krische didn't challenge it — it was an audience……"

"Save it, Granmeld. Not the place for that kind of talk."

Nozan glanced around and sighed.

There were more than a few soldiers who worshipped the divine being.

"……It's all right, Krische-sama. We were briefed on the circumstances by Marshal's Aide Faren."

"Oh, is that so."

The fact that she had thrown a spear at the dragon was supposed to be a secret.

Nozan nodded with a smile toward Krische, who looked a bit flustered.

"Still, good timing. After Her Majesty's speech, and now Krische-sama arriving right on its heels — by now, not a single soldier still thinks this is a losing war."

"Krische heard about it from Selene. The Elsren forces gave you quite a time of it."

"In the open field, it was……frankly, I was relieved to get inside Wulfenite. We managed to spring a few traps and whittle them down, but……to the soldiers' eyes, it must have looked as though we were retreating helplessly."

A methodical, phased withdrawal prepared well in advance — direct combat avoided, casualties light, damage inflicted exceeding what was received.

But the picture visible to the uninformed soldiers was entirely different.

Many regarded it as nothing more than a rout.

That was expected — and precisely the reason the enemy had chased them without suspicion — but morale had dropped further than anticipated. The three-front war was partly to blame.

Some soldiers wore hollow bravado; others had the faces of the dead.

Nozan's forces had been overflowing with them, and the civilians remaining in the city were no different.

Any further deterioration might have sparked riots.

But the earlier "spectacle" had fully restored their fighting spirit.

The morale lost during the retreat could now be considered recovered.

Before the day was out, the tale of "Alberinea's trebuchet destruction" would spread through the city, completing the turnaround.

From the start, those in Nozan's army had almost all witnessed the power of her javelin during the civil war.

None would doubt the story.

"Mm, well, that's good then. We'll continue this later. Krische is going to go finish off the rest."

"Of course, we'll join you."

The jade tiger cleared the towering wall in a single leap, and Nozan and the others followed via rooftops.

Before long, the air was filled once more with the whistle of thrown spears and the crash of impact.

Tremors and cheering filled the heaven and earth of Wulfenite.

* * *

——That day, Alberinea single-handedly destroyed twelve massive trebuchets and three siege towers under construction.

The dead — caught in the collapses — exceeded two hundred, among them renowned engineers and master craftsmen, and the Elsren Holy Empire's army was forced into a retreat by one individual.

Alberinea's javelin was sufficient to repel an army of one hundred thousand.

Many who look at the Five-Nations War see Alberan's victory as resting entirely on Alberinea the individual.

If one reads the tales of her battlefield exploits at face value, one can scarcely conclude otherwise — and at the very least, no one disputes that she was the peerless warrior of her age.

That Alberinea was that great an aberration is stated as plain fact in numerous records.
