# Chapter 176: The Maiden Krische and the Envoy of Peace

Galshan divided its army into three large groups and marched them from the homeland toward Alberan.

When moving a massive army, the greatest problem lies in logistics.

Assembling an army of a hundred thousand in itself is not particularly difficult. For a great power like Galshan, one need only scatter money about and recruit soldiers.

But the real issue, above all else, was the provisions to feed them.

A village of fewer than a thousand inhabitants could not possibly produce enough food for a hundred thousand soldiers.

Even a great city of a hundred thousand would be the same — it might be possible to sustain them for a few weeks, but the limits would arrive swiftly.

Once assembled, a great army is not permitted to remain in one place.

It becomes a great serpent perpetually wandering in search of food, and the larger it grows, the faster its hunger accelerates.

Even hiring accompanying merchants and dragging supply trains behind to stave off starvation, a great army inevitably forms an endlessly long column.

The vanguard may depart at sunrise while the rear guard sets out at dusk.

In practical terms, marching a hundred thousand soldiers as a single body is impossible, and for this reason the nations surrounding Alberan had set the maximum force for a marching formation at thirty to forty thousand.

"General Elkaarl and General Kilrea both report their marches proceeding smoothly. It seems there were some minor skirmishes during occupation, but no significant resistance. They will rendezvous on schedule."

Inside the great tent, savoring a southern fruit wine, sat a single man.

Dark skin, coarse hair, and a well-groomed beard over a muscular frame.

Just under six shaku tall — not quite what one would call a giant, yet with muscles threatening to burst through his clothing and the savage countenance of a beast.

Galshan Vice Chairman Olgan possessed an indescribable aura of intimidation.

"Good. That is welcome news. Remind them once more to conduct themselves as gentlemen — no roughness. This will become Galshan territory hereafter."

"Sir."

Elkaarl to the east, Kilrea to the west.

Both men had fought alongside Olgan during the rebellion, and while capable as commanders, they had a somewhat rough streak.

He wished to avoid their worse tendencies manifesting and adversely affecting postwar occupation governance.

There are several ways to conduct a conquest.

The method of instilling fear through violence and ruling by terror is certainly one approach, but the opponent was not some barbarian tribe — it was Alberan. In technology, scholarship, and many other areas, they surpassed Galshan, and the populace there was comparatively well-educated.

It would be best to incorporate them into his nation as peacefully as possible, absorbing their knowledge and people, and fostering ill will toward Galshan would be counterproductive.

Galshan had once been a vassal state of Alberan, and owing to that historical relationship, the southern territories of Alberan already shared deep bloodlines with the Shaan people.

Postwar governance, if one avoided violent methods, would not be particularly difficult, and one could say that success or failure hinged entirely on the attitude taken during this invasion.

"What are Gahka's movements?"

The one who entered the tent, replacing the departing messenger, was a tall man.

Turning his gaze to Zalvarg — his own right hand, just returned from reconnaissance — Olgan's very first question came without preamble.

"Nothing unusual. It appears they are training around Nakria. However……"

"Hm?"

"A flag bearing the inscription of Alberinea — lightning and falcon — it seems the one dispatched from the center is Krische Christand, just as the Vice Chairman hoped."

"Truly?"

"I would love for you to tell me what purpose it would serve for me to lie."

Zalvarg answered Olgan's delighted expression with exasperation, then wearily took a seat beside him.

A young attendant hastened to pour him wine, and —

"No need. If I drink after being this tired, I'll fall asleep. Black soybean tea, please."

"Y-yes sir, my apologies. Right away."

At Zalvarg's words, the attendant ran off to fetch the tea.

Zalvarg scratched his cheek with an awkward expression and sighed.

Having risen from a mere commoner to Olgan's most trusted confidant, Zalvarg received, in a sense, even more reverence from the common-born soldiers than Olgan himself.

For Zalvarg, that deference was more uncomfortable than anything — it made him squirm.

"Get used to it already, Zalvarg. Just carry yourself with confidence."

"If you could show me a man who could act confident after going from cobbler to this position, I'd like to meet him……"

"You were better suited to making corpses than shoes. Like rain pouring upon the earth — wanted or not, that is the nature of a calling."

"……What a dreadful calling."

Zalvarg's shoulders drooped, and Olgan laughed with amusement, clapping him on the shoulder.

Then, draining his wine with relish, he turned a serious face to Zalvarg.

"So, what of the enemy forces in the end?"

"From what I could observe, approximately forty-five thousand. I've been counting by corps, so there may be an error of a few thousand."

One corps, five thousand.

In Alberan and Elsren, armies were fundamentally divided that way.

Galshan, which employed war beasts, had significant variation in formation personnel depending on the nature and purpose of each unit, but the Alberans clung to that fixed number with a peculiar stubbornness.

It was presumably a matter of operational convenience.

"One corps fewer than expected. That is somewhat concerning."

"Hmm. They withdrew their forces readily, invited us in…… I can't imagine Gahka resorting to petty tricks at this stage. But now that she has appeared, I cannot be sure. Not everything about this is a delight, it seems."

The possibility of a flanking maneuver through the western forest to strike their rear was very real.

If that happened, they would need to split their own forces — and this battle would shift from a simple decisive engagement to a contest of wits, a strategic struggle.

In truth, the battle was inherently supposed to be such a thing; a head-on clash was merely an unspoken understanding between the opposing commanders, Gahka and Olgan.

"A vexing situation. For now…… oi, send word to Kilrea in the west to watch for infiltration from the forest. Before the messenger runs."

"Sir."

Olgan issued his orders, then toyed with his beard in thought.

"Hmm, personally I'd prefer to dispense with cleverness and have a head-on clash…… No, Gahka should feel the same way……"

"It can't be helped. Alberinea came because the Vice Chairman invited her — this is a case of reaping what you've sown."

Zalvarg said it matter-of-factly.

By any measure, this war had begun to Alberan's disadvantage.

Moreover, if they lost, Alberan's capital would inevitably fall.

It was only natural that the Alberan side would be desperate to snatch victory, and it would be no surprise if they resorted to any means necessary.

Going head-on against a hundred thousand with genuine intent was a fool's errand.

The personal rivalry between Olgan and Dagreen was, after all, secondary to affairs of state.

"If this teaches you to be more modest in the future — "

"No, I've made my decision, Zalvarg."

"Hah?"

Olgan spoke suddenly and rose to his feet.

"I'm going to inquire about their true intentions. You're coming with me."

"……Hah?"

Zalvarg stared up blankly at the face of the lord he served.

"A battle of this magnitude. A once-in-history engagement that will settle the rivalry between me and Gahka. I cannot die in peace if I leave regrets here. If he intends, for the sake of his nation, to pursue nothing but victory, then I will resign myself and fight accordingly. But the possibility that it might be otherwise — "

"There is no such possibility, Vice Chairman. Are you sane?"

"Perfectly sane. Relax — no one would cut down an envoy who comes in good faith."

"……Please calm down for a moment. Besides, I've only just returned from reconnaissance……"

Half-resigned yet looking thoroughly miserable, Zalvarg protested.

"Catch the rain and turn it to harvest. The moment the thought strikes — that is the time."

Yet Olgan wore a manly, cheerful grin.

* * *

The walled city of Nakria was, quite literally, a key stronghold at the border between the kingdom's center and south, surrounded on all sides by walls.

A town had been built outside the walls, and around that, yet another wall.

Compared to the tall inner walls, the outer perimeter wall, rampart included, stood at barely twenty shaku.

Cracks ran here and there, and the construction could hardly be called robust, but in compensation, defensive towers had been erected at equal intervals, and a modest dry moat had been dug.

The defensive capability was passable for a city of its size — yet hardly formidable, and certainly not the kind of city suited for withstanding a siege by a great army of a hundred thousand.

Atop one such tower —

"Bunny, we should head back soon — it'll get dark and cold."

"Fwah…… you think so?"

Krische, who had been napping with Gururun as her pillow, was roused by a dismayed-looking Mia and Kalua. She yawned sleepily, and as if following its master's lead, the jade tiger yawned in turn.

Sunset was still a while off, but she stretched languidly upon seeing the sun beginning its descent.

There was little to do beyond a bit of routine training.

The general plan was already settled, and until the enemy arrived, there were only token meetings to attend.

The soldiers were learning about Galshan's war beasts and how to counter them from Dagreen's troops, but Krische had left such matters to the corps commanders' discretion.

There was nothing in particular for Krische to do, and she was seizing the opportunity to rest her travel-weary body.

The same went for the Black Flag Special Service — aside from training just enough to keep from getting stiff, they were free.

She had already taught them everything there was to teach, and there was nothing new to convey at this point.

Under the pretext of recovery, she had essentially granted them leave from the afternoon onward.

"……Haah, if Krische-sama has free time, Krische would appreciate it if she could at least give some words of instruction to the newer members, though……"

"That sort of thing is Vulture's and Mia's and Aleha's job. Besides, it's better for people who would desert at the crucial moment to desert now — saves Krische the trouble."

The purpose of granting leave was partly to let them rest and gather their strength before a hard battle, but it also served as a filter.

It was far better to have cowards flee beforehand than to have them lose their nerve at the moment of battle and throw the formation into disarray.

She had repeatedly explained that this would be an extremely dangerous assignment, and the leave had been granted on top of that.

Desertion was only natural — three new recruits had already deserted from the Black Flag — but Krische was not particularly concerned.

"Of course that's true, but…… if Krische-sama did that, the people who deserted might have found it in themselves to try a little harder."

"Unlike other units, Krische needs the Black Flag to actually perform, so Krische doesn't want soldiers who'll run at something as minor as being outnumbered."

*You shouldn't talk back to your commanding officer*, Krische said, poking Mia's lips with her finger.

Kalua laughed with amusement and vigorously ruffled Mia's head.

"Yeah, yeah. It's way better than getting other soldiers killed because you got dragged down by people like that."

"Mmm…… anyway, please stop messing up my hair."

Mia swatted Kalua's hand away and combed through her chestnut hair with her fingers.

"Well, General Gahka put on quite a public display of punishment today, so desertions should drop off around here."

Desertion could be punished by death without question, but the degree was left to the commanding officer's judgment.

This time, as an initial example, they had arranged for all members of the deserters' squads to formally plead for clemency, then let them off with light whippings.

From tomorrow onward, it had been declared that fellow squad members would be forced to beat the deserter to death as the method of execution.

Too harsh, and morale drops.

Too lenient, and desertions only increase.

When Krische asked why they couldn't simply kill the deserters outright, Dagreen had explained that balance was what mattered.

"Making an example is a rather deep subject."

"A-ahaha…… well, let's hope it has the desired effect…… hm?"

As they were talking, a sudden commotion erupted along the wall.

It was far too early for the Galshan forces to have arrived.

Krische tilted her head and stood to look south — approaching were seven cavalry.

They bore a blue flag indicating envoys, and a second flag showed a thunderstorm framed by wheat on both sides — the national flag of Galshan.

"……Envoys, perhaps?"

"It seems so. That's the Galshan Vice Chairman and his escort."

"Vice Chairman?"

"Yes. He's quite an important person."

The Galshan envoy — Olgan, wearing a white mantle that was thoroughly ill-suited to him, approached without any apparent caution, and upon reaching the south gate, raised his voice.

"My name is Olgan! I am an envoy from Galshan! I have come to meet Alberinea and your general, Gahka. Open the gate!"

Standing before the very gate of enemy territory with a tiny escort, his bearing was nothing short of bold.

The guards stirred with agitation, and a messenger could be seen running toward Dagreen's manor.

"Ugeh…… Isn't Olgan the enemy commander?"

"He's supposed to be…… I mean, you have to admit, that takes courage."

He flew the blue flag in accordance with the Sacred Convention.

That said, Alberan was cornered by three surrounding nations, and moreover, the very reason for the invasion was an alleged violation of the Sacred Convention.

At this point, there was no guarantee whatsoever that their side would respect the Convention and refrain from killing him.

Even if they spared him here, the option of quietly disposing of him on the road back was entirely viable — and the possibility that their side might simply deny all knowledge was more than plausible.

Fearless. Reckless.

Such words suited the act perfectly.

"Krische-sama, what shall we do?"

"Mmm…… they appear to be envoys, so let's go see them for now. Gururun."

Krische leaped onto the jade tiger, then bounded from the tower to the wall.

Vaulting over the cowering guards, she reached the top of the south gate, then dropped down the other side.

She landed right before Olgan's eyes, just as he was about to raise his voice again.

"?!"

Not even a cloud of dust — and suddenly, the massive body of a jade tiger materialized before them.

Their horses panicked, whinnying and rearing. The tall man positioned behind Olgan to the right — Zalvarg — had instinctively leaped from his horse and half-drawn his blades.

"Ah, Krische startled you, didn't she? Sorry about that."

But a restraining hand spread wide by Olgan, and the girl's voice, froze Zalvarg in place, a deep furrow forming between his brows.

Long silver hair — a strikingly beautiful face and violet eyes.

A black cloak bearing the faintly visible design of lightning and falcon across the chest.

At the sight of the girl seated atop the jade tiger, every eye went wide — all except Olgan, who began to laugh.

"……My, my. A personal reception from Alberinea herself — I am honored."

"Krische heard you say you came to see Krische and General Gahka. It's been a while, Vice Chairman Olgan, and…… Zalvarg-san, is it?"

"Yes. ……However, please pay me no mind."

Zalvarg kept his sour expression, staring into those violet eyes.

He realized that he had yet to release his stance, still gripping the twin swords at his waist. He took a deep breath, steadied himself, and straightened his posture.

His movements were slow, deliberate — as one might move before a predator, so as not to provoke its instincts.

The girl sitting atop the enormous magical beast before him was, more than the beast itself, what his body had registered as a threat.

The moment he released his stance, the suffocating pressure that had wound itself around him vanished — and that somewhat inorganic gaze cut away as well.

Sitting side-saddle on the jade tiger, she appeared utterly full of openings.

Yet what he felt was a primal terror — as though his life were held in her grasp.

He had already sensed at the capital that she was a far more formidable warrior than her appearance suggested, but feeling her hostility on his skin was another thing entirely.

At the very least, this was not an opponent he should allow to stand before Olgan on a battlefield.

The escort men behind him also wore tense, wary expressions directed at the girl.

"Your faces are too stiff, Zalvarg — all of you. We came to talk, not to fight. If you look like that, Alberinea will wonder what's going on."

"……Sir. My apologies."

"I beg your pardon for my subordinate's rudeness, Alberinea. At the very least, we are here today as envoys — we have not come to cross swords."

"Mm…… Krische was the one who startled everyone, so Krische doesn't mind."

Behind Krische, Mia and Kalua arrived belatedly and landed, placing their hands behind the small of their backs.

They straightened to attention as escorts.

Olgan paid them no mind, turning his gaze to the jade tiger and narrowing his eyes.

"And yet, I boasted of showing you beasts, only to be shown a beast first and surprised. What an embarrassment — perhaps I should have brought an elephant along. ……Though it would surely pale before the jade tiger."

"Now that you mention it, you did say something like that. But it would be rude to have you bring an elephant all this way just for Krische's sake……"

Krische tilted her head with an uncertain look.

This was a Krische who still did not grasp the true meaning of 'showing you beasts.'

She genuinely believed, even now, that he intended to delight her by showing her exotic animals.

Despite a battle looming, Olgan was remarkably well-disposed toward her.

To Krische, he was a thoroughly puzzling individual.

"It's unheard of, taming a jade tiger. You did so yourself?"

"That's right, Krische just fed it and it took to Krische…… Ehehe, Vice Chairman Olgan, do you like kitties too?"

"If asked whether I like them, I'd be hard-pressed to answer…… Well, as a warrior, I do have a fascination with powerful beasts. Knowing one has been tamed as a mount — that stirs the heart."

Olgan laughed and continued.

"……I am truly glad. This time, we have also brought a great beast that will surely delight you. I was uncertain whether to bring it, but……"

*Olgan-sama*, came a voice attempting to stop him, but Olgan waved his hand to quiet it.

"They will learn of it eventually. There is no hiding a beast of that size."

"……?"

"……It is a wondrous elephant of magical origin, Alberinea. A colossus that might well straddle these very walls — it is among our rear forces at present, but…… I look forward to showing it to you."

"A large magical elephant……"

Krische had only ever seen elephants in pictures.

And now, she was being told he intended to show her an especially rare, enormous one on top of that.

"Thank you, Vice Chairman Olgan. Um……"

"What is it?"

"Krische was a little puzzled as to why you'd come here before the battle, but……"

He was remarkably well-disposed indeed.

Why, at this point, would he go so far to please Krische?

She traced her cherry-blossom lips with a fingertip — and what followed was the product of deep deliberation.

"Could it be that Vice Chairman Olgan has come as an envoy of Galshan's surrender?"

Every soul present lost the ability to speak, and Krische smiled.

She did not fully understand the circumstances, but surely he had realized he could not defeat Krische.

Despite the depth of her deliberation, she did not think very deeply about the reason.

Her thinking was short-circuited — self-centered — and more arrogant than anyone.

"If so, then things are simple. You needn't worry about being considerate to Krische."

Oblivious to their dumbfounded expressions, Krische nodded to herself in satisfaction.

"Krische is quite busy this time, so if an unnecessary war can be avoided, that's most welcome. Krische, um…… yes, sincerely rejoices in the wisdom of Galshan and Vice Chairman Olgan!"

A hundred thousand Galshan troops against fewer than fifty thousand Alberan soldiers — were Galshan urging surrender upon Alberan, it would be perfectly reasonable.

But it was the reverse.

Fewer than fifty thousand Alberan soldiers, to the face of a hundred thousand Galshan, speaking as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

And calling it an 'unnecessary war,' no less.

It was unmistakable provocation.

"You…… !!"

"To dare provoke us even in this situation……"

Olgan's escort, enraged by the blatant provocation, raised their voices one after another, hurling abuse at Krische.

*Huh?* Krische tilted her head in puzzlement.

She turned to look at Mia, who was shaking her head desperately.

Even Kalua's cheeks twitched with exasperation.

Given how the two were reacting — *where did Krische go wrong?*

Unaware that she was fundamentally wrong from the start, Krische wore a troubled expression, then — struck by an idea — clapped her hands.

"Ah, sorry about that. 'Surrender' is probably not the right word. In this case, envoy of peace, perhaps?"

"Still with the provocations!"

"How dare you…… ! It's you lot who should be surrendering!"

A perfectly natural response.

*What did Krische get wrong?* she wondered, tilting her head further.

Into that scene rang a burst of laughter.

Olgan, who had looked stunned, suddenly began to laugh as though he found it unbearably amusing, slapping his own thigh over and over.

Krische stared at him curiously, and the men who had been shouting turned to Olgan in confusion.

Olgan went on laughing like that for some time, then finally looked up, wiped the tears that had formed, and regarded Krische.

"Hah-hah, my goodness — when was the last time I laughed this hard? You are endlessly entertaining, Alberinea."

"Haah…… Krische didn't intend to be entertaining."

Krische showed a hint of displeasure, and Olgan chuckled ruefully, then spoke.

"I wish to speak with Gahka before the battle. ……I would very much appreciate your permission."

"Yes. Well, Krische has no reason to refuse…… you'll swear upon the Sacred Convention?"

"……I swear by the rain that nourishes the harvest."

"Then Krische shall trust in that, and permit you entry with your swords and escort."

Krische turned behind her. Mia hurriedly signaled the guards atop the wall to open the gate.

And so, a meeting between the two opposing commanders would take place in the walled city of Nakria, on the eve of the decisive battle.

# Chapter 177: The Price of a Proposal

"Welcome, Olgan! You look well — I'm glad!"

"I was worried you'd have an upset stomach, Gahka. Hah-hah-hah, looks like worry was needless!"

One of the reception rooms in the Gahka family's great manor.

It was a separate room from the one used for war councils, naturally, but the sight of two enemy generals embracing the moment one walked in was something difficult to put into words.

A boorish tableau — as always, Mia took a step back, and Kalua wore a bewildered expression, wondering if this was really acceptable.

Dagreen's adjutant — Dalkeris, a warrior whose face bore countless scars — rubbed the bridge of his nose.

Dagreen clapped Olgan on the shoulder, directed him to the opposite sofa, told an attendant to provide drinks to the escort as well, and settled onto the sofa. Krische sat down a short distance from him.

Gahren was also in the room, watching the two of them and Krische with an indescribable expression.

"Last time, we parted saying 'next time on the battlefield,' but…… I never expected to meet like this before the battle. You surprised me, Olgan."

On his way to and from the meeting, Olgan had stopped by here.

They had drunk together, swapping tales of past battles, laughing together that the next would likely be the last — but Dagreen looked thoroughly delighted.

He poured wine into Olgan's cup.

"That was my intention too, but there's something that's been nagging me. ……Though it's already half-resolved at this point."

Olgan accepted the cup and glanced toward Krische.

Krische, who had been asking the attendant for tea instead — *preferably black tea, please* — tilted her head at his gaze, and Olgan gave a wry smile.

"I came to ask how you want to fight this battle. A contest of wits — or a contest of strength. Either way, the advantage is ours; I'll leave the choice to you."

"……Hmm."

Dagreen regarded Olgan with amusement.

Both had let their beards grow wild, with the vicious faces of bandits — the two of them sitting face to face made this look less like a war council and more like a gathering of brigands.

Seeing these two, no one would ever guess that both came from among the highest-born bloodlines in their respective nations.

They were like brothers.

Krische, clearly out of place, poured honey and milk into the tea she'd been served, watching the discussion unfold.

"You too now stand in a position where the fate of a nation hangs in the balance. If you exhaust every means and choose a contest of wits, then it is a shame, but so be it. That is the proper way for a soldier."

Olgan said this, yet continued in the tone of one posing a question.

"I would not deny that…… but the thought of our final battle ending with some petty exchange — it feels lacking. At least, that's how I feel. What about you?"

"I feel exactly the same, Olgan. What I desire is the same as you…… a contest of strength without tricks, a head-on battle."

Dagreen answered as if it were the most obvious thing, and Olgan nodded with satisfaction.

"That is what makes you my friend — however, what troubles me is that you don't appear to be doing so. Why hold forces in reserve? There's likely still a corps-sized force you haven't committed. Surely you don't intend to face me head-on with merely these fifty thousand?"

He drained his cup, set it on the table, and fixed Dagreen with a challenging stare.

"— I swear by the rain that nourishes the harvest. If Alberan desires a head-on decisive battle, then this Olgan shall, upon the pride of a warrior, meet it with his entire army in a fair and honorable fight. ……I will wait several days. Set aside political and strategic concerns, and bring everything you have to bear, Gahka."

His voice was filled with intensity.

Not the pitch of a man losing his temper — there was even a kind of composure.

Yet his voice resonated so deeply it seemed capable of cracking the metalwork in the room.

Everyone who listened felt their spines stiffen, and —

"Um…… so you really aren't an envoy of peace after all?"

One lone girl spoke with a troubled air.

An envoy before battle.

Krische, who had still harbored a faint hope that he had come for negotiation or a peaceful resolution, lowered the corners of her brows, for it appeared, yet again, that she was wrong.

"Hah, you jest. ……What commander, leading a hundred thousand, would surrender to a force of barely fifty thousand? If anything, it should be the reverse."

"If you look at the numbers, well, maybe, but…… even with a hundred thousand, it takes time to kill fifty thousand, and war is a contest of who takes whose head first. If Krische takes Vice Chairman Olgan's head before that, it's over, right?"

It was a provocation like a bucket of cold water to the face.

"Krische did fairly well in the civil war, and based on how Vice Chairman Olgan acted when we met before, it seemed like you understood that Krische is the stronger one……"

Krische had recently achieved 'fairly decent' complete victories in battle, and was 'fairly' famous.

Moreover, when they had previously met, she had been told things like 'you are everything the rumors say' and 'it is an honor as a warrior to meet you.'

That phrasing — acknowledging Krische's prowess, placing her above himself as a warrior — and judging from his reaction when they faced each other, it had not been mere flattery. He understood, at the very least, that Krische was far stronger.

"So Krische assumed that the reason you came at this odd timing was that you were startled by Krische's arrival and came to make peace."

If they wished to demand surrender from the Alberan side, that could wait until their army arrived.

Normally, one would first make the visible display of force — a hundred-thousand-strong army — intimidate the opponent, use the shield of soldiers' watching eyes, and then propose surrender.

Yet Olgan had arrived with only a handful of retainers, so as not to alarm the enemy.

In this situation, the Alberan side had the option of ignoring the Sacred Convention and quietly disposing of him — life and death were in their hands. Surely Olgan's side was not entirely blind to such possibilities.

If Olgan were acting purely as the army's commander, there was neither need nor reason to expose himself to the danger of assassination and place himself in such a perilous position.

The only explanation would be sincerity.

— Olgan had most likely come with some sort of offer or request.

Krische's reasoning, on the whole, was not wildly removed from the general way of thinking.

Naturally, an ordinary person would, given Olgan and Dagreen's personal rivalry, the fact that the enemy held twice their strength yet was advancing directly toward a decisive battle — deduce the reason to some degree from those circumstances. But Krische possessed no such flexible mode of thought.

The other side was being humble.

They knew Krische was extremely strong.

— *Of course: their goal must be to negotiate a peace, having been intimidated by Krische.*

She had simply assessed the situation before her as it was, rationally and directly.

Surely no enemy commander could be so dim as to waltz into the opposing camp for the irrational reason of enjoying the battle as thoroughly and honorably as possible — she had been convinced of that until just a moment ago, but something was clearly off.

His eagerness to show her beasts, his excessive 'consideration' — she had assumed it was because he did not want to make an enemy of her, but given this scene, it seemed otherwise.

Her grand plan of 'Demonstrating Krische's Strength to Scare Off the Enemy' had not succeeded after all.

*Where did Krische go wrong?* she wondered, and —

Olgan looked taken aback.

The escort men nearly raised their voices again — and were stopped by Zalvarg's hand.

After a time, Olgan began to laugh once more.

"Hah-hah…… I see, I see. So your earlier words truly were neither a joke nor a provocation."

Then he quickly narrowed his eyes and glared at Krische.

"……In that state, you genuinely claim that you can defeat my forces head-on with this disparity in numbers?"

"Yes. It's not as if there's been some sudden surprise attack, and normally, Krische thinks it's much stranger to fight without a prospect of victory…… is something wrong?"

"Not at all. But you do seem to have great confidence."

Olgan lifted the corner of his mouth.

Aulgorn Hilkintos — a resounding victory from a position of inferiority.

She had easily broken even the Black Lion Gildanstein.

In the past, she had even taken part in the war against the Holy Empire, earning a Rinea commission in a single battle.

The aura she exuded was palpable at a glance — a pressure unthinkable from a young girl.

There was something inorganic about those violet eyes, and within them dwelt a pride so absolute it bordered on arrogance.

It felt as though one were being looked down upon from a great height.

Those eyes held something unfathomable — as though they belonged to something that was human, yet not.

— *She faced a dragon, drew her blade, earned its recognition, and sealed a pact.*

The sort of absurd rumor that would normally be dismissed with a laugh — and yet, with this girl, it seemed entirely plausible.

"……Individual prowess is, in the end, just that — individual. I sense that your personal abilities are beyond doubt, given the legends — but personal martial skill is a trivial thing on a battlefield of tens of thousands. ……I will not ask again. Knowing all of this, you say you can defeat the army I lead in a head-on engagement?"

Krische thought for a moment, then smiled.

"Mmm, it depends on your definition of 'head-on,' but…… well, if we're talking about a straightforward single engagement, then yes. Krische is much stronger than Vice Chairman Olgan, and much smarter."

While delivering insults as naturally as breathing.

Trying to overwhelm him head-on and then reach Olgan was foolhardy — the enemy left wing's goal was, at best, to pin him down.

Given the circumstances, the enemy's aim was almost certainly a right-wing breakthrough.

"Still, there's little point in me deliberating over it. This is Elkaarl's playground. ……As a precaution, the main force will prepare for a breach on Elkaarl's left. Zalvarg, if they break through, they're your playmates. Happy?"

"……Hardly. Let us pray General Elkaarl holds the line."

He sighed as he spoke, but his gaze turned backward.

Zalvarg glared toward the Alberinea army's main camp.

* * *

As Olgan had anticipated, the Alberan army wasted no time exploiting its momentum.

Alberan's opening move was a full advance.

However, the Gahka forces advanced not in a single line but sequentially from the left wing — the entire army describing an oblique, angled formation — presenting at least a nominal defense against Kilrea's attempted envelopment on the right.

Naturally, even with their depressed morale, the Galshan forces did not simply stand and absorb this. They pushed forward their beasts — elephants, land dragons, and unicorns — and advanced in full.

Under normal circumstances, this would have begun with an exchange of archery — and indeed, volleys of arrows commenced between the Kilrea and Gahka forces. But between the Elkaarl and Alberinea forces, no such exchange took place.

Elkaarl's vanguard consisted of sixty elephants, thirty land dragons, and twenty unicorns.

To maximize their effectiveness, infantry had to be slammed into the enemy the instant the elephants disrupted the front lines.

Speed was everything, and that meant the infantry had to advance at the beasts' pace.

If archers were deployed ahead, the changeover would create disorder in the formation.

And if the changeover delayed the infantry, the beasts would end up exposed and isolated.

All of them had thick hides, but massed arrow fire was still a serious threat.

The handlers were not nearly as tough, and the beasts were, in the end, animals — they fought not for glory or great causes. In a perilous rain of arrows, even they would instinctively balk.

If arrows were concentrated on isolated beasts, even the initial charge would fail — and skirmishing archers, who fought in loose formation rather than massed ranks, were precisely the kind of enemy beasts struggled against most.

There was a real possibility of the vanguard beasts being whittled down without recourse.

Even deploying one's own archers to counter was of limited effect against scattered skirmishers.

The damage exchange would inevitably weigh heavier on the side losing its beasts.

For this reason, when beasts were placed in the front rank, offense was the best defense.

A simple, all-out charge produced the greatest effect while minimizing one's own losses.

If massed infantry stood visibly just behind the war elephants, the enemy would have no choice but to withdraw their archers early — because, as noted, any delay in the changeover would create gaps in their formation.

Against the combined mass of war elephants and the battle line behind them, a loss of cohesion was fatal — the enemy had to avoid that above all else, and a massed assault would inevitably force them onto the defensive.

Great beasts inherently created overwhelming superiority in a frontal clash.

Operating them required not intricate planning or trickery, but courage and boldness.

Even under a rain of enemy arrows, one pressed through with brute force, and used the beasts' raw combat power to pry open the enemy line — that was the ultimate and supreme approach.

Galshan's morale had taken a severe hit; Alberan's was at its peak.

But once the lines locked together, the morale gap would begin to close.

Galshan's right-wing general — Elkaarl the Giant — reasoned as much, and simply told his soldiers: fight as you've trained.

They had never lost a head-on clash.

At this morale disadvantage, even with war elephants, land dragons, and unicorns, a stalemate was conceivable — but a stalemate was, in fact, exactly what they wanted.

In a straightforward war of attrition, there was nothing to think about.

The numerical advantage would naturally hand them victory.

Just as in the laws of nature.

Once something in motion has stopped, it takes great force to set it moving again.

Once things stalled, it became a contest of shoving — without an external force, there was no way to break through and collapse the line. And the enemy lacked the reserves to create such a force.

Stop the opening move, and the battle was won.

It was that simple.

But —

"……Something is off. Why aren't they raining arrows?"

Seven shaku of superhuman frame.

Stark armor and helmet, adorned only with an elephant emblem carved into the breastplate and helm's center.

Surveying the battlefield from atop his elephant, Elkaarl added a scowl to his already stern face.

The Alberinea forces ahead were simply advancing their line, showing no sign of deploying archers against the incoming beasts.

"……Perhaps they intend to capitalize fully on their morale? Or to redirect us into a bypass, as Gahka once did?"

Even without comparing himself to Elkaarl, the man was small-framed for a soldier.

Deputy Commander Neikrul studied the Alberinea army ahead and answered.

In the past, Gahka had countered a leading elephant charge with a masterful formation shift, opening gaps in his line to channel the elephants through — brilliantly redirecting the charge.

War elephants, despite their appearance, were timid herbivores at heart. If gaps opened and guided them, instinct would choose flight.

They would avoid charging headlong into a wall of spears.

Only a commander who had fought elephants countless times could have managed such a maneuver — it was, enemy or not, magnificent generalship.

There was no question the Alberinea army shared knowledge of such elephant countermeasures, but even so.

"If it's one of those two, then the former. That bypass worked because the elephants were isolated out front. But this time, the beasts aren't just elephants, and infantry is right behind them. A move like that won't work here."

In this situation, attempting to channel the elephants aside would leave the gaps open for the trailing infantry and land dragons to shatter the line.

"Still, pressing forward on pure morale…… if those were Gahka's troops, that might not be a bad plan, but I wonder how many over there have ever faced elephants?"

"……I, for one, would never choose it. It's a reckless gamble."

If these were Gahka's troops, the choice of a straightforward frontal clash was viable.

Veterans who had long defended the southern border against Galshan incursions.

Accustomed to fighting beasts — and with morale that high, there was a real chance of devouring the elephants and land dragons, line and all.

But the opponent was the Alberinea forces — the kingdom's central army.

The single throw that had felled Alkazaris — Alberinea was without question an extraordinary warrior, and the troops she led were undoubtedly elite.

But that was as *elite against other humans.*

Few could perform at full capacity against beasts they had never seen before.

If this was a brute-force gamble relying on high morale — it was a reckless one.

"And yet, they show no sign of changing. They mean it."

Distance shrank even as they talked.

A gamble — but Elkaarl had no intention of being contemptuous.

The kingdom's soldiers were fundamentally different from Galshan's.

Equipped uniformly, subjected to rigorous training — strong troops.

Beasts aside, in a simple comparison of individual soldiers, the Alberans were superior.

If their gamble paid off handsomely, there was indeed a real chance of breaking through the line.

"Signal flag — order the right flank into an enveloping maneuver! Strike the enemy's flank!"

"Sir!"

"Advance the entire headquarters forward! All reserves, prepare to move!"

Elkaarl bellowed his orders. Seeing his own elephant begin to move, he turned his gaze forward once more.

Squinting at the distant Alberinea forces, he furrowed his brow.

"……War chariots? At that position?"

Only because he was atop the elephant could he see it.

He focused on a strange wagon mixed into the enemy left-wing battle line — set apart, splitting to either side.

"Two-horse team…… but there's no archer aboard, so it doesn't look like a chariot. It appears to be just a supply cart."

"Either way…… but why mix something like that into the line?"

A cloth-covered cart, drawn by two horses.

Its placement within the battle line was unnatural even for a supply wagon — and equally so for a chariot.

Chariots were relics from an era before stirrups, when mounted combat was impractical.

Even if one dusted them off, their use was no different from cavalry — flanking maneuvers.

Why would you embed one *inside* the battle line?

Incomprehensible — or rather, unsettling.

"Messenger — inform Dahlton at the center: unidentified wagons or possibly chariots have been spotted within the enemy formation. Exercise extreme caution!"

"Sir! I shall repeat the — "

"No time! Go now!! Relay this to His Excellency as well!!"

Elkaarl's shout was drowned out by a thunderous tremor — the battle-roar of the war elephants.

The elephants had entered their charge range.

Elkaarl raised his eyes again, and —

"— What is that?"

Beyond the stampeding elephants.

In the instant he had looked away, countless flags now fluttered from the enemy formation — crescent-moon skulls on black fields.

And seeping out like shadows, soldiers clad in black-painted armor.

* * *

At the boundary between Begil's Third Corps and Kokys's Second Corps.

Aleha marched alongside his soldiers, looking up at the sky, narrowing his eyes against the wind of the battlefield.

The enemy totaled over a hundred thousand.

Thirty thousand in front; their own side, half that.

By any ordinary reckoning, this was a suicidal charge.

A head-on clash was madness — that was what Aleha, in his days as a general, would have thought.

— But for Krische Christand, this was no gamble.

"I must say, this is enough to make a man's loins seize up, Vice-Commander Aleha."

"I'm the opposite, Squad Leader Zaaka. Being given the vanguard of a battle like this — my whole body's alive with it."

The handsome young man laughed.

The one-eyed squad leader returned the smile, answering with amusement.

"Reminds me of the battle at the Dragon's Maw. The enemy a vast army, and us nothing but a century — dragged along by Krische-sama, fighting for a week straight in the Bernaich mountains."

He traced the eyepatch over his left eye, closing it briefly.

"No praise, no support. Compared to that, this is downright luxurious. Best of all — it'll be over in a single day."

"Good to stay positive. ……It's all in how you look at it."

"……?"

Aleha studied the elephants ahead and spoke to Zaaka.

"On the battlefield, a man first feels the fear of death — but that's merely the result of failure. What you should be thinking of is the image of yourself succeeding. What you should always envision is your ideal self, and then look inward to find what you must become to reach that ideal."

His handsome face twisted into a rueful laugh as he continued.

"Thinking of yourself as a single soldier — that's what's positive and good. That's how I brought myself closer to my ideal, climbing from private to general. ……Hah, as you well know, that eventually turned to arrogance, and General Christand and Krische-sama broke my nose for me."

"……Can't exactly say much about that one."

Zaaka said dryly.

Hearing your commanding officer's tale of failure was always awkward.

"Well, the point is this. ……The moment you set foot on a battlefield, the stake is the same no matter what — whether the enemy is strong or weak, it's your life."

Aleha's eyes took on a glint of madness, his cheek twisting upward.

"The more powerful the enemy, the greater the glory when you bring them down. If you're paying the same stake regardless, isn't it better that the enemy be as mighty as possible?"

"……With respect, sir, you're insane."

Zaaka laughed — but Aleha neither rebuked nor denied it.

"Hah, consider this: a soldier is a man who stakes his one and only life on something as uncertain as glory and honor. You really think such a person is sane?"

"Can't say I do. I've come to understand it rather well lately. The quality of a soldier must be decided by the degree of his madness."

Zaaka jerked his thumb toward the rear.

"Whether to be glad or grieve at serving under the most insane commander of all — that's something I wonder about from time to time."

"The answer is obvious: rejoice from the bottom of your heart. ……In a mad environment, you'd be a fool not to be mad yourself, wouldn't you?"

"True enough. ……Is it time?"

"Yes. Just about."

The distance to the enemy vanguard — the war elephants — was under a hundred ken.

Within full archery range, and the elephants' charge distance.

Aleha had fought across the entire western Holy Empire as one of its soldiers.

Naturally, he had experience not only against the kingdom but against Galshan as well, and of everyone in the Alberinea army, he knew best how war elephants moved.

"Shall we be the guides leading every soldier into this mad battlefield, Squad Leader Zaaka?"

"Sir! Bogus — forward! Raise the flag!!"

Answering the call, the lowered banner was thrust aloft.

From behind came the toll of a great bell, and at that same instant, the elephants ahead shifted from a walk to a run.

— That was the signal.

Aleha burst forward, and Zaaka's five-man squad followed.

Every one of them — save the flag-bearer — gripped two spears.

Aleha dashed ahead of the battle line, then pushed forward alone, one step farther.

He tossed his left-hand spear into the air, switched the right to a reverse grip.

His body — accelerated to its absolute limit like a predator — planted a heel into the earth and whipped his torso.

"Ngh……!"

Seventy ken.

From the spring of his entire body erupted an infantry spear faster than an arrow.

A roar of wind — its target, the war elephant's brow, the base of the trunk.

A gap in the skull — the spear pierced straight through the nasal cavity, eviscerating the interior of the head with unerring precision. A single throw, an instant kill.

The great body toppled, gouging the earth as it crumpled.

"Alberinea's hunters — step forward! Elephants are nothing but targets — show these dimwit beast-tamers what Alberinea's warfare looks like!!"

Aleha roared, and the flag-bearer whipped the banner in circles.

Answering with a battle cry, two hundred warriors emerged in succession from the battle line.

Clad in the same black armor, each carrying spears, every one of them running faster than a horse.

They hurled their spears into the elephants before them — and once again, a thunderous cheer erupted from the Alberan army.

These were no mere javelin skirmishers.

Every last one was a mana user — a special javelin unit.

Alberinea's hunters — and what they loosed were weapons that could only be called magic spears.

The answering sound was the opposite of cheers.

From the Galshan side came the anguished screams of countless elephants.

Even at fifty ken, the spears pierced deep.

Galshan soldiers were jolted instantly from the frenzy of the all-out charge, flinching before the black-clad monsters before them.

They had not forgotten Alberinea, who had felled Alkazaris from a ri away with a single throw.

Nor the crescent-moon skull flags that had been flying there.

And now, those same flags fluttered in numbers before their very eyes.

The soldiers had been forcing their once-broken spirit back into a frenzy.

Seeing war elephants crash one after another to the sound of screaming wind — once the frenzy broke, all that remained was the terror of the unknown.

The transcendent Alberinea's magic spears did not end with a single throw.

An army of aberrations that could easily kill a war elephant from fifty ken with a single strike.

To them, everything before their eyes was a monster beyond all comprehension.

— *There is no way a mere twofold advantage in numbers can stand against monsters like these.*

That thought seeped into their minds and surfaced.

"Don't slow down! The elites are few — our numbers can push them back!! Advance without fear!!"

The ablest officers, those who retained their composure and analytical faculties even now, raised their voices.

But in this situation, the soldiers had no reason to believe them.

It was not only Alberinea.

The soldiers had told themselves it was merely one individual's prowess, that the overall advantage was still theirs — and yet.

"Hii——?!"

The reality before their eyes made that a lie.

Not just Alberinea.

Against Alberinea's army, even the war elephants and mighty beasts were outmatched.

The beasts that had always spearheaded the charge, always broken through the enemy lines.

The certainty that these beasts made frontal defeat impossible — that foundation was being demolished.

Some broke ranks in fear, pushed back and trampling each other.

The carcasses of dead elephants became obstacles — and the disorder rippled through the entire army.

Once a force's spirit was broken, it was no longer an army.

They had utterly lost the ability to advance, lost all cohesion, and —

"All forces — begin the charge!!"

From the opposing side, the royal crest — a sword pointed heavenward — unfurled on a great assault banner, and the battle cry marking the charge resounded louder than anything before.

In place of the lost thunder of the elephants' footsteps, *they* now made the earth tremble as they came.

Every Galshan soldier finally understood.

Which side would prevail in this head-on clash.

Which side would be trampled.

— All that swayed before their eyes was the black flag of Alberinea.
