# Chapter 56: Warrior

The sound of steel clashing leaked through the gaps between the trees.

A great blaze that scorched the heavens and the glimmer of stars and moon reflected through the forest, scattering countless points of light.

At its center stood a single man.

Clad in battered armor, a longsword in his right hand.

A short sword in his left.

A valiant helm fashioned after a hawk.

Like a raptor eyeing its prey, he leaned forward and pressed in against the enemies that came slashing at him.

*――Skilled.*

Bogan Christand clicked his tongue as he deflected a sword swung his way with the longsword in his right hand.

Then, pressing forward as he was, he slipped past the man's flank and drove the short sword held in a reverse grip through a gap in the man's armor.

There was no time to pull it free.

He released the short sword and, whipping his body outward, pierced the throat of the man behind with the tip of his longsword.

His breathing was ragged.

The men he had brought as his escort had all been excellent, but only two remained.

Against them, the enemy still numbered twenty, among them formidable warriors such as Nakilus Ferizar, a brute who wielded twin great-axes with effortless ease, and Walter Zargan, successor to the Zain school of swordsmanship.

"General, we shall carve a path――"

"……My thanks."

"Think nothing of it, General. We have no lives so dear we would begrudge spending them for you."

The two men smiled.

They had already accepted death, and smiled upon that acceptance.

"……That I was blessed with subordinates such as you――I count it my greatest pride."

"Ha ha, from our end, serving under you has been the greatest honor of all, General."

The other man nodded at those words, and both charged.

They cut into the enemies on either side, leaving but one before Bogan.

The moment the enemy raised his sword, Bogan pressed forward.

He slammed a palm strike with his outstretched left hand into the man's breastplate and leapt.

The black-armored warrior, focused solely on an attack by sword, toppled, and Bogan escaped into the treetops.

――But the great tree he leapt to next was shattered before his eyes.

"Guh!?"

He took a tumbling breakfall and rose to his feet immediately.

Hoisting the great war-axe that had so easily snapped the massive tree, Gildanstein laughed.

"I will not let you escape. Do not think you can flee from me, Bogan."

"……Indeed, a faint dream."

Gildanstein stood before him, as if showing off his unwounded black lion armor.

The two remaining escorts had been surrounded and run through with blades――once again, Bogan was encircled.

"……Let us have a bout."

Gildanstein tossed aside the great war-axe and drew the sword from his left hip.

It was a thick, broad-bladed longsword.

This man favored sturdy, heavy weapons.

"Sooner or later, someone will come to rescue you. If you can hold out until then, you win. A simple contest."

"And if I decline?"

"Regrettably, my mercy toward you ends here. This is the second time I have shown you grace. There will not be a third."

If they attacked together, it would be over in an instant.

Yet instead, Gildanstein pointed the tip of his sword at Bogan.

"――Take up your sword, as a warrior."

His bearing was nothing short of noble――truly befitting royalty in its majesty.

Bogan let out a bitter smile and answered.

"……I ought to be grateful, I suppose."

"Indeed. Be grateful. This is the result of paying you the highest respect I can."

Gildanstein was strong.

Even Kokys, whom Bogan trusted purely as a warrior, might not be able to defeat this man.

How far he himself could go against such an opponent――he had grave doubts.

Even so, it was surely less reckless than facing everyone here at once.

"This is my payment for the loyalty you once showed. Rest easy. Whatever happens, I will see to it that the Christand name endures. You were a man worth that much to me."

"Ha ha, I would prefer not to take those words as a parting gift for the afterlife."

Bogan smiled.

The path to survival had grown impossibly thin, no way forward in sight.

Even so, if this was to be the end, it was not a bad way to go.

At the very least, as a warrior, he could ask for no finer end.

"……Your Royal Highness, I have always felt that you, as a warrior, are worthy of respect. I bear no grudge. ……I shall accept your duel as you wish."

"That is well. Our contest of tactics never reached a conclusion. It is time we settled things."

Bogan put his left side forward.

He thrust his left hand out toward Gildanstein, drawing his right arm back.

The Rollka style――a traditional military swordsmanship.

Ordinarily, a small shield is used to invite, deflect, or even counter the opponent's sword.

Even empty-handed, the stance disrupted the opponent's sense of distance, the open left serving as a fist, a grapple――using the left as a lure, this swordsmanship was a battlefield art specialized entirely in breaking the stance of an armored opponent and delivering a lethal blow.

"……That stance is most impressive when it is yours."

"Velraigh has surpassed me by now. Age makes itself felt. ……Even in sparring, my daughter has begun to beat me."

This style required a powerful build.

Great height and long limbs.

Selene, who lacked these, had instead learned her swordsmanship from Krische.

Her frame, average for a woman and small for a warrior, she lowered further still, striking upward with slashing thrusts.

Sharp and swift, keeping her posture low to extend an otherwise inferior reach, Selene's swordsmanship――cutting in and breaking the opponent's stance with the blade――was splendid enough to make even Bogan marvel.

Such skill at only fifteen years of age.

The future of his gifted daughter seemed endlessly bright, and he let his mind rest there.

He would likely never see what lay beyond.

If there was anything to regret, it was only that.

"The pride of a parent――a joyful thing, but."

"……That is something I cannot understand."

So saying, Gildanstein also took his stance.

Gripping the sword with both hands, he assumed the middle guard――the sword of kings.

The sword, forged with lavish use of metal, had once been the weapon of heroes.

It had begun as a small blade wielded in one hand, but over time, longer and heavier greatswords came to be prized as demonstrations of martial authority and power.

And technique had evolved alongside them.

No shield raised.

Both hands gripping the sword, facing the enemy head-on, and from that stance, overwhelming them.

Squarely, from the front, eyes fixed on the opponent alone, cutting them down.

It was a stance that embodied the very way of the nobility.

The right hand, endlessly versatile, represented the heavens; the left, the axis that stabilized everything at its core, represented the earth.

The middle guard――founded on the principle of encompassing all of heaven and earth within the arc of the blade, enabling response to any attack――remained to this day the foundation of the sword that many a proud noble studied.

And Gildanstein's stance was breathtakingly beautiful, worthy of being called the sword of kings.

His feet, firmly planted on the earth, evoked the solidity of a mountain, and the tip of his readied sword conjured the vision of heaven's raging fury.

The furrow in Bogan's brow deepened at the stance that showed no opening, and he eased his grip on the sword.

Salva and those around him held their breath.

The hero Christand and the Black Lion Gildanstein.

A duel between generals who commanded vast armies was a rare thing to witness.

That the two who would fight were warriors whose names would live in history――it was only natural that tension rippled through them.

For some time, the two men simply watched each other.

Blood and sweat dripped from the point of Bogan's chin, and Gildanstein narrowed his eyes.

"You will not come? ……No, my apologies. Yours is a defensive style to begin with."

So saying, Gildanstein pressed forward.

He crushed the earth underfoot, collapsing the distance.

No feints woven in, simply and squarely from the front.

The sword swung up to the right drove the wind before it, a thunderous roar beyond what any sword should produce as it bore down on Bogan――

"Kh……"

But it was Gildanstein's handsome face that twisted.

Before him, Bogan's body had sunk so low as to nearly be prostrate.

As though a snake crawling along the earth.

He pulled his left hand back and twisted his hips, and Bogan's blade ran true toward Gildanstein's throat.

A strike extending from beneath the man's very feet.

Gildanstein reflexively swung his powerful arms to shake his sword loose and deflect Bogan's blade.

But even deflected, Bogan did not stop.

He raised himself and slammed a left palm strike into Gildanstein's breastplate with crushing force.

"Gu……kh."

But Gildanstein too was masterful.

Even as he was sent flying, Gildanstein's blade shallowly opened Bogan's left arm.

Bogan grimaced but took distance, shoulders heaving as he breathed raggedly.

Gildanstein, his breath disrupted by the impact that had penetrated his armor, nonetheless laughed.

"You say you have grown old――heh, that is far too modest, Bogan."

"It is precisely in old age that one must continue to hone oneself, else one cannot call oneself a warrior."

What Bogan had shown was Krische's sword.

A sword that shimmered with ever-changing brilliance, like a dance.

Not bound by stance, seeking only the optimal answer for each moment――the beauty of that swordsmanship had captivated even Bogan.

A sword that always aimed for nothing but the life, from outside the opponent's awareness.

He could not claim to understand all of it, but having crossed blades many times and watched her sword again and again, its philosophy had left a deep impression on him.

"Your Royal Highness wields the king's sword. Truly beautiful. ……But a sword honed solely to take life is also beautiful. I found myself captivated despite my age――by the swordsmanship of my new daughter."

"That girl……is it."

"Indeed. A crude imitation, child's play compared to what Krische wields. ……But it will serve to buy time."

"……Interesting."

From there, however, the fight could not be called evenly matched.

Though he had learned from Krische's sword, it was nothing more than a surprise strike.

A technique that used attacks from unexpected angles to confuse the opponent, break their stance, and thrust into the resulting opening.

Bogan knew that it was precisely because of Krische's small frame that the technique showed its full power.

His foundation remained the familiar Rollka style.

That had been nothing more than a desperate addition to compensate for what his own skill lacked.

Against an ordinary opponent, that initial strike would have sufficed.

But before Gildanstein's masterful swordsmanship, the same trick would not work twice.

The king's sword flashed sharply, shallowly cutting the chainmail, and it was Bogan who accumulated wounds.

A natural sense of balance that made nothing of the unstable footing, his body following the sword as he pressed in.

The thrust, concentrated to a single point, was unleashed with whip-like fluidity, piercing through plate armor.

With masterful control of tempo――the Royal Prince Gildanstein fought without the slightest danger, commanding the true dignity of a king.

"――Hah!!"

"Kh……!?"

――But there, Bogan showed his own tenacity.

Though covered in wounds, what Bogan possessed through a life spent on the battlefield was the raw sword of a warrior.

Even falling to a knee he swung his sword to force distance, and at times braced himself for injury to throw his whole body into Gildanstein, sending him flying.

At every opening, he seized the man's body, tried to topple him, to drag the fight from swordsmanship into sheer violence.

What Bogan, always so calm, brought forth in his death-ground was the beast within.

Before he was a hero――when he was merely a soldier.

The blood of those days, when he had risen from captain through the hell of the front lines, surged within him now.

All trace of reason had been scraped from Bogan's face, replaced by the savage visage of a wild animal.

Death near at hand, madness gleaming in his eyes. The corners of his mouth twisted upward.

It was a smile of pure aggression.

The mad grin of a wolf before its prey.

In answer, Gildanstein too pulled up the corners of his mouth and laughed.

His once-pristine black armor was now smeared with earth and scored with countless marks.

"Yes, Bogan! You are a magnificent warrior after all!"

"Ha ha, my blood has not boiled like this in ages! I could never let my daughter see this……!"

Bogan swung his sword.

It was no longer swordsmanship so much as the act of hammering steel.

Massive, sweeping swings――full of openings to the eye, but what lay within them was heat like a raging inferno.

A sword that made even Gildanstein flinch for an instant, forcing him back a step.

From Bogan's flesh swirled blue vortices of mana.

Thunderous roars beyond any sword rang out, gouging the earth.

"What is the matter, Your Highness! You cannot take my head by running alone!!"

"Tch!"

Gildanstein clicked his tongue and took distance.

*Target the openings in his attacks*――Gildanstein coolly sought to find them, but Bogan was a storm.

Countless flashes of the sword like a torrent of rain and roaring thunder.

Each individual stroke could be called crude, yet the power hidden within them called forth primal fear in any man who faced them.

――Lightning Quick Christand.

It was not merely his excellence as a general that had earned him that name.

It was the soldiers who had witnessed Bogan's ferocity as he stood at the very forefront of battle and unleashed that violence who had given him the epithet.

Even on the ground of defeat, he built mountains of corpses, ate the flesh, drank the blood.

The soldiers in his wake felt their hearts tremble, and surrendered themselves to their own madness.

Lightning Quick――it was the name of a warrior like the thunder that summons the storm.

That was the reason every one of them, from corps commanders down to centurions, revered Bogan as a hero. Every one of them had once had their hearts set trembling by the sight of Bogan, and it was for that reason that valiant men gathered beneath him and took up swords.

He conjured the wind, rallied all around him, and hammered a rain of blood and thunder upon the enemy before him.

The Bogan who stood here was truly a storm in human form, no less than his epithet.

The men watching from around them shivered, torn between blood that surged hot and a cold chill of fear.

The only one who could stand before it was Gildanstein alone.

Driven onto the defensive by that sheer violence, nothing but pure respect rose in him at the sight.

The image of the man he had once fought alongside on the battlefield.

Remembering it, Gildanstein pulled up the corners of his mouth.

"Heh heh, my spine is trembling. Magnificent, Bogan――this is what a warrior's battle should be!"

Each stroke was the kind that would break a sword on contact.

Heavy enough to deal a fatal wound even through solid plate armor.

Bogan had lost all finesse to thread the gaps in armor, yet the threat he posed now was incomparably greater than before.

So then, what to do――

"I knew it could be done with you! ……Bogan!!"

――There was nothing for it but to become a beast himself.

Target the openings. Strike at blind spots.

Such petty tricks he cast from his mind entirely.

This was a sacred warrior's battle.

With the crushing strength to nearly break the sword's hilt, he simply swung.

The fastest, finest stroke he could deliver.

The thunderclap that compressed the air――Bogan dodged it by twisting his hips, swung his left arm, and drove his fist through Gildanstein's helm.

The blow, enough to crush the gauntlet, warped the heavy helm and shook Gildanstein's skull.

Yet Gildanstein swung again, driving his blade through Bogan's right leg as he fell.

It was deep, but Bogan gritted his teeth and endured.

Bleeding profusely from every part of his body, the heat raising something like steam, Bogan stood.

Gildanstein hauled himself upright as if rolling, tore off his helm, and hurled it aside.

The crushed helm had cut his handsome face, but it was completely beyond his notice.

Bogan likewise cast off his own helm.

He spat blood-tinged saliva and leaned forward.

He scooped up dirt and flung it at Gildanstein, then charged.

Gildanstein paid it no mind, thought nothing of it as dishonorable, and pressed forward even lower.

The shoulder of the black armor buckled under Bogan's sword, and Gildanstein's face twisted in pain.

But the wound in the thigh had dulled Bogan's reach and the edge of his blade.

Gildanstein braced for injury and pressed further forward, driving his shoulder upward to send Bogan's massive frame flying.

Noting his left shoulder was damaged, Gildanstein gripped the sword in his right hand and pursued――a great overhead strike.

Bogan rolled to avoid it, kicked Gildanstein, and as the man staggered, rose to his feet.

And Bogan, too, raised his right arm.

"Kh……"

――But the countless wounds had drained Bogan's blood.

With too much blood lost, Bogan's footing gave way, and Gildanstein did not miss it.

A sharp stroke cut through Bogan's right arm, sending it spinning through the air.

"Gu, ngh……kh!"

Even as his arm was severed, Bogan hurled Gildanstein to the ground, then staggered to lean against a tree.

Blood poured ceaselessly from the arm, and Bogan's eyes swam, unfocused.

Gildanstein, slammed to the ground, coughed as he rose.

Then, breathing harshly, he approached and spoke.

"……A fine match."

He raised his sword as though resting it on his shoulder, and――

"This is the end, I would like to say, but however――"

Gildanstein turned his face toward the foot of the mountain.

Countless war cries echoed below.

Looking down, what appeared to be a battalion-strength force of infantry could be seen charging up the mountain, and the sound of hooves echoed from somewhere.

"……I cannot call this a victory. As expected, it is a draw."

He murmured.

"Your Royal Highness! Enemy forces are――quickly, his head――"

"……It matters not. With this much blood, death is inevitable."

"But――"

"I said it matters not."

He glared at the black-armored man, then looked at Bogan.

"I have no intention of displaying your head. ……I will take your sword and arm."

Bogan could not answer, only glared, and lost consciousness.

Gildanstein said a single word――"We go"――and broke into a run.

"……I thank you, Your Royal Highness."

Salva, following behind, spoke.

"I do not want your thanks. This is not for your sake. ……It is courtesy to a warrior I acknowledge."

"……Sir."

The words Gildanstein had called from atop the cliff.

And the sword and arm he held.

Whether they were genuine or not.

Few could say with certainty.

But Gildanstein had declared his own name and spoken.

For anyone who understood what a name meant to a noble, his words could not be doubted.

And the man standing atop the cliff was Alberan――a true noble of the royal bloodline.

The battlefield was shrouded in silence.

Selene, hearing the voice, fixed her gaze on the sword and arm held aloft in the distance.

Even so, she wanted to believe it was a lie.

Her vision, enhanced by mana, relayed the truth to Selene with merciless clarity.

It was the gauntlet and sword she had seen countless times before.

The gauntlet and sword of the father she respected above all others.

"……No."

Repeated strain and physical exhaustion.

Selene, who had been barely holding herself together, swayed on horseback, and the Fourth Battalion commander beside her――Barga――caught her.

"Selene-sama……please, hold steady. It is not his head. That he has brought the arm and sword may mean he still lives."

It was nothing but wishful thinking.

Even so, it had to be said. That was Barga's duty.

Selene wanted to cling to those words with her emotions, while some cool-headed part of her tried to deny them.

"……The soldiers are watching."

Barga said bitterly.

It was a cruel thing to say to a girl who had been forcing herself through grueling duties all this time.

But she was a corps commander.

She was not permitted to crouch and wail like a young girl.

Selene's gaze wavered with something like fear, then she closed her eyes and steadied her breathing.

Simply to remain calm.

The surrounding soldiers were watching Selene.

Bewildered and afraid, their earlier fervor gone, they now stood in a cold silence.

――The winds had shifted too.

And what she heard was cheering.

Not from her own army, but resounding from the enemy.

The soldiers who had been on the defensive now surged onto the attack.

The situation was half-finished.

Had the Second Corps' central breakthrough been fully completed, perhaps things might have been different.

But as it stood, they were in the middle of it.

Which meant the most dangerous state: their formations in disorder.

Hooves approaching. A messenger from the Fourth Corps.

"From the Fourth Corps Commander: ――The tide has turned. A reversal from here would not be easy and heavy casualties are projected. It pains me, but I judge the prudent course is to withdraw while our losses remain light. I request the First Corps begin withdrawing its troops. That is all."

The messenger watched Selene with concern.

――Was a reversal possible?

The chances were certainly slim. But could they afford to give up the Dragon's Maw?

She forced herself to think only of what was necessary, and nodded.

"……Messenger. Tell the advanced heavy infantry to withdraw. Krische will handle the light infantry's disengagement from the Second Corps. Tell them not to worry about that. And also, tell them: thank you for the concern."

The Fourth Corps Commander's commanding tone was likely born of concern for Selene's state of mind.

At the very least, while she stood here, she must be a corps commander.

The messenger gave a powerful salute, as if showing his respect, and ran off once more.

"……Krische?"

Then, at the edge of her vision, she caught a shadow racing up the cliff.

# Chapter 57: The Weak

Cutting down distracted enemy soldiers who had their eyes on the clifftop without hesitation, Krische frowned at the unpleasant atmosphere.

Even the hundred-man squad, which had been brimming with fighting spirit, had suffered a drop in morale, and the same was true for Selene's light infantry.

The entire Christand army was losing its strength from the sight of Bogan's sword and arm, and Gildanstein's words.

"Kh――follow the Corps Commander's Adjutant! Our role has not changed!"

Dagra raised his voice.

What had been moving smoothly had become stiff, and then stopped for an instant.

The flow toward victory that had been building was already gone from this place, vanishing into the distant void.

Krische let out a quiet breath.

Around the seventy-eighth head she had severed, she judged that any more would be pouring water on a hot stone and gave up.

The number of people one person could kill had no effect on the larger situation.

Krische's purpose in cutting down enemies was to amplify her soldiers' morale and diminish the enemy's.

Now that they had been swallowed by the enemy's momentum, killing more was pointless.

Krische did not particularly enjoy killing as a hobby; she did it only as a means to victory. If that work would come to nothing, she had no motivation.

Rather, this sort of dirty work was what Krische detested most, and if it would not produce results, she would have liked nothing more than to drop it immediately.

"……This area is fine."

Fortunately, as far as the immediate surroundings were concerned, the enemy's morale was also low.

The hundred-man squad's abilities were high, and from Krische's perspective, satisfactorily so; thanks to that, the morale of the enemy soldiers nearby had collapsed entirely.

Rather than showing any appetite for attack in response to the earlier declaration, they seemed to be hoping that this side would simply withdraw.

At the very least, there was no sign of an aggressive attack.

Krische twisted her hips and crushed a soldier's skull along with his helm underfoot, then leapt to land before Dagra.

"Vulture. Any further will be difficult. The Fourth Corps Commander is cautious, so he will be thinking of withdrawing. Selene will probably……follow suit."

What worried her a little was Selene.

Bogan's sword and arm.

That had been a shock great enough to stop even Krische for a moment.

Krische's benefactor, her guardian, a kind person who had shown her goodwill.

He might have been lost.

It was not yet confirmed, so she had pushed it out of her mind, but if she imagined that it had been Bery's sword and arm instead, she could vaguely picture how Selene must feel.

Selene deeply respected and loved Bogan.

"……I was about to suggest the same. Even here where we stand, the atmosphere is foul."

"Lead Selene's light infantry and link up fully with the Second Corps as planned. We can think about the rest later. Prioritize minimizing soldier losses above all."

"Sir."

Krische wiped her curved sword on a corpse and returned it to its sheath, then spoke.

"Well then, I leave the rest to you. Krische will, if possible, go take the Royal Prince's head. He is close."

"Wha? W-wait……"

――The chance was now, or never.

So saying, Krische leapt to the cliff.

She set her toes on the slightest protrusion and pushed upward.

Her body, defying gravity, seemed to float, swaying lightly as she nimbly scaled the cliff.

The great blaze on Mitskronia rose as if to scorch the very heavens.

"……Nn?"

He had likely been in the process of returning to his own lines.

Facing the blazing forest, Gildanstein had his back to her.

Around him stood roughly twenty black-armored soldiers. They all appeared to be seasoned fighters.

Their deployment, their numbers――Krische took it in, not so much observing as simply registering the facts.

The confusion on the men's faces. Before that confusion could turn to hostility, she moved.

Without hesitation, she targeted the gaps in their armor, throwing knives at two men who stood in her way while closing the distance.

A knife buried in one's throat――a corpse. The other still breathed.

The knife had gone slightly off its mark and failed to kill. She kicked the embedded knife deeper, finishing him, and drew the sword from his sheathed corpse.

"What are you doing!? Protect His Highness!"

Two men cut in before Krische.

One near, one behind――positioned to shield Gildanstein.

Paying them no mind, Krische pressed forward and brought the longsword down in a great overhead swing.

The man in front――a veteran――instantly raised his sword high. He had committed to a block.

Despite the ambush, the man's reactions were quick, his movements sound.

Even in this situation, his sword held a calm composure, and in that single instant, the vast experience and training of an entire lifetime seemed to bleed through.

Had the attacker been an ordinary swordsman, the man would have easily cut them down with his counter-stroke.

He certainly had the ability to do so.

But such technique and experience were worthless before Krische.

Krische turned the longsword in her hands, tracing an arc with its tip.

She effortlessly evaded the block, denying even the counter-stroke.

With that same momentum, she drove the blade sharply through the man's torso, armor and all, and released the sword.

Every attack was a killing blow, yet could effortlessly shift into a feint.

Deceiving even those called masters, turning all who stood before her into the weak.

Krische's swordsmanship already lay beyond the bounds of common reason.

"Zargan-dono!?"

A corpse was now merely a tool, a simple obstacle.

She leapt, kicked off the shoulder of the collapsing body, and reached one more――this time drawing the curved sword at her hip, and in the same motion as she landed, she swept the man's head from his neck.

Still in his stance, the man crumpled without so much as a reaction――and beyond him stood Gildanstein.

"Tch!"

Gildanstein instinctively leapt backward, and Krische narrowed her eyes slightly.

The distance opened. Too far.

She abandoned the aim for his throat and set her sights on his arm.

Krische's curved sword targeted the arm――the seam in the armor――shallowly slicing through the chainmail.

The one who wielded the curved sword was Krische.

Preternatural speed and precision turned even a moving target into a stationary one, slicing through chainmail as though parting linen.

Even legendary enchanted swords would fall short of the blade she wielded.

That single motion of leaping backward.

From that alone, Krische read the full measure of Gildanstein's ability, and closed the distance further.

Her step was a gale――Gildanstein instinctively slashed in response.

Extraordinary speed of blade.

Even from a poor stance, enough power lay within it to cleave Krische, bone and all.

But Krische twisted her body and dodged it with ease, slipping naturally inside his reach.

Concentration heightened to its limit slowed everything before her eyes.

Breathing, preparatory movement, muscle tension, the flow of mana.

She captured all of it, and ruthlessly drove into the faintest disturbance too small to even be called an opening.

Her posture was low, a step that seemed to scrape her head against the earth.

What Bogan had shown before was literally child's play by comparison――it was truly a snake crawling along the ground.

No seam between stillness and motion, her movement invisible even when seen.

By the time the opponent could react, she was already in the killing position.

What Gildanstein's eyes saw in that moment was his own inescapable death.

Even accounting for the advantage of ambush, the attacker was alone and they were elite hand-picked soldiers.

Overwhelming superiority should have been on their side, yet the reality before his eyes showed the exact opposite.

――The existence of a monster who imposed inferiority upon superior numbers.

Goosebumps rose along Gildanstein's spine, and still unsatisfied, Krische let displeasure show on her face.

Her stance was poor for a finishing blow, her center of gravity unstable.

One move was not enough; she needed two more.

She was supposed to have killed him in the first move, and requiring a total of three was inexcusable enough to make her frown.

She should have targeted the one she had stabbed with a knife instead.

That would have saved one step.

Reflecting on things only she could understand, her body was already in seamless motion.

As though she had intended it from the start, she seized Gildanstein's arm and twisted her body.

She slammed his frame into the earth.

She stomped down on his right arm that held the greatsword, and Gildanstein's face contorted in agony.

And Krische was about to bring her curved sword crashing down on his neck――when she reflexively dodged.

Faster than she could finish Gildanstein, a blade had swung at her body.

"K-Krische-sama……!"

The one who had swung the sword wore a familiar face.

Salva cried out, and Krische narrowed her eyes.

Betrayal. He had used some means to lure Bogan out.

That was how it was, then.

More blades closed in from all sides, and Krische carved through one man's neck as she leapt again.

She landed lightly outside the circle.

She could see Gildanstein, coughing, rising to his feet within the ring.

"Kh……Krische. I see――you came for my head."

Every one of them wore metal armor.

Her curved sword would be ruined before she could kill them all.

*My luck has run out,* Bargra thought, spitting to clear his dry throat.

――Hunting remnants.

Yes, they were here to hunt down remnants.

They were emphatically not the ones being hunted.

The Christand army had withdrawn all the way to Fort Belgash.

He had heard that the pursuit had actually resulted in losses on their side.

Therefore, the priority was first to firmly establish control of the Dragon's Maw, and then to dispatch a force larger than the Christands' to destroy them.

That was what had supposedly been decided above, and by the plan, the Dragon's Maw should have already been secured.

Gildanstein had returned to the capital ahead of the rest, to move against the palace nobles.

The commander left in charge was Geltz Willing――a general lacking in glorious achievements, but seasoned.

Nearly all of the Christand forces had completed their withdrawal to Fort Belgash, and there should have been nothing to worry about at the Dragon's Maw.

But a monster lurked on Bernaich.

Everyone knew who it was.

The cursed child――Krische the Head-Hunter.

A monster wearing the form of a silver-haired girl.

Leading a death-squad of black-armored soldiers, she was hiding in these mountains.

The ridgeline fortress had been burned twice, a visiting corps commander and his adjutant killed.

And then the engineers sent to rebuild it had been massacred.

Taking the matter seriously, the general ordered a large-scale sweep――but as a result, seven hundred-man squads had been annihilated.

This was the third major sweep.

The first had prioritized locating the enemy, using independent hundred-man squads to search.

But isolated squads were picked off one by one, and a battalion commander was killed in the process.

The second grouped squads in pairs for stability.

The result was the same: the death god devoured two hundred-man squads at a time with ease.

By the third time, it was half-desperate.

Four hundred-man squads formed each group, operating in mutual support.

The two battalions created five four-hundred-man groups, advancing in lockstep up the mountain and confirming safety all the way to the far side.

As an additional precaution, two more battalions were stationed with the engineers rebuilding the fortress.

This time, the measures were excessive to the point of absurdity.

They had to be, because otherwise no one would go up the mountain.

The survivors who came back from Bernaich were all mad with fear.

Most were too terrified to function as soldiers, and that terror inevitably spread to others.

Fear of the unknown was like that.

A mere hundred-man squad. And yet, by all accounts, it was a pack of monsters who leapt from tree to tree.

Deserters had appeared among the units assigned to Bernaich.

Soldiers who had fought at the center the other day swore they had seen the squad led by the cursed child up close, and begged not to be sent to Bernaich.

Even valiant centurions hesitated at the situation, repeatedly sending word up the chain that every precaution should be taken, and the general, Geltz, having concluded the matter was serious, had resorted to this somewhat brute-force approach.

A straightforward application of overwhelming numbers.

And yesterday, all troops had been pulled off Bernaich, with a full day of leave granted.

As expected, only the monster's hundred-man squad had been confirmed, and since they must be nearing their limit by now, the troops had been deliberately given a window to escape.

Geltz had never encountered a unit capable of operating in the mountains this long.

And given the small scale of just a hundred and their demonstrated ability to descend sheer cliffs, they could surely slip away easily even under a full-scale sweep.

Trying to destroy them was a near-impossibility, and making that the goal would only delay the strategic objective of "stabilizing the Dragon's Maw."

Nine times out of ten, the monster had gone home during the night.

The primary reason for committing this many personnel was to ease the anxiety of the soldiers climbing Bernaich.

Yet the anxiety would not leave, clinging to every man's chest.

Bargra felt the same――he considered being in these mountains a bad draw.

The corps commander, fearing for his life, had not even entered the mountains, leaving command to a battalion commander.

They were canaries thrown into a mine shaft.

Confirming its safety with their own lives.

It was midday now, but that brought no comfort.

There had been several daytime attacks.

The raids were not limited to nightfall.

"Bargra, don't be so tense. Just be steady and carry yourself with confidence."

"C-captain……"

"When you look like that, it affects the morale of the men under you."

Centurion Arond smiled and clapped him on the shoulder.

A deeply wrinkled old man. If you passed him on the street, you would never take him for a centurion.

But appearances aside, Arond was a battle-hardened soldier with over thirty years of service.

His rough voice carried depth, and Bargra nodded.

"I know, I know, but……I just can't settle down."

"Pah, they surely fled last night. And even if they remained, this time we are four squads together……no matter how skilled, they are few. Even if they spot us, they will not attack."

"Well……true."

Bargra laughed. Indeed, no one would be that foolish.

He dipped his head in apology, and Arond chuckled, *still green.*

"You are a captain. Stand tall and that is enough. The men will follow your example. So――eh, ge……kh."

――A gust of wind.

Something silver seemed to flicker before his eyes, and Arond's head tilted at an unnatural angle.

The neck was half-sheared, and a horrendous flood of dark red liquid erupted.

Something warm splattered over him before he could process what had happened, and he froze.

Screams.

Looking around, he found himself surrounded by corpses with severed necks that had appeared without his noticing.

And they multiplied between one blink and the next.

What he saw was a silver shadow swaying like two tails.

A pitch-black cloak.

Violet eyes found him, and Bargra too screamed and fell back.

Arond's body toppled onto Bargra, and in his panic, Bargra flailed his limbs wildly.

The cleanly severed cross-section revealed raw flesh, blood gushing forth.

That what drenched his entire body was Arond's blood――he realized only belatedly.

The violet eyes lost interest and moved to the next target.

"Ca-captain! Enemy atta――gh, eh."

Silver passed through.

The corporal who had been calling out to Bargra lost his head, which rolled away.

Still in the posture of running toward him, arm outstretched, the body fell.

Another scream from a different direction.

Black-armored soldiers had appeared from nowhere, driving swords into his comrades.

He could not count how many.

He had already lost all composure.

"Hi――"

Crawling, turning his back, he scrambled down the mountain.

He had soiled himself. He was not aware that his trousers were fouled with blood and urine.

He stumbled, crashing into trees and rocks, and kept running.

Behind Bargra, the screams of his comrades echoed.

He did not care. The fear of being killed was all that moved his body.

"Hey! What happened!? Where's Arond!?"

Fleeing blindly, he ran into another hundred-man squad.

He clung to its centurion――a man he vaguely recognized.

"E-en-enem-enemy attack――!"

"Tch, I knew it, but――gah, g……?"

The captain's strong eyes――at some point, a knife had sprouted from his eyeball.

And in the next instant, his neck was torn open.

The pink cross-section seeping blood――Bargra saw that clearly.

Silver hair danced, and before his eyes, new blossoms of blood bloomed.

Dancing, whirling.

The men collapsed like puppets with cut strings.

Every one of them, necks gaping wide, like great mouths opened.

His fear had pushed past all limit.

It erased all reason from Bargra, took his consciousness, and dropped him into darkness.

When he next awoke, the man was no longer a soldier.

The hallucination of necks splitting before his eyes would never, for the rest of his life, fade from Bargra's mind.

* * *

She staggered, bracing herself against a tree, and coughed.

Her elegant brows twisted, and a furrow formed between them.

"Kh……Corps Commander's Adjutant."

"I'm fine……"

*Cough, cough*――Krische leaned against the tree, coughing, and slid down to sit on the ground.

Dagra watched her and frowned.

The fever was plain to see, and her eyes were unfocused, faintly glazed.

For this entire week, day and night, she had continued the raids, always at the head of every assault.

She alone had barely rested at all.

Her curved sword lay where she had dropped it, not even returned to its sheath, and she took a drink from her canteen before coughing again.

The thick, cleaver-like curved sword――once beautiful in its way, its blade had grown dull, its very gleam lost.

She had let the hundred-man squad rest in shifts.

Most of the raids involved only half their number, like this most recent one, rather than the full complement.

In exchange, she ran constantly through the mountains, scouting the enemy, and standing watch so their position was not discovered.

The results were staggering.

The combat achievements defied belief for a force this size.

But the burden on Krische was far too great, and her limits were showing.

"……Casualties?"

"Begil and Zarka are wounded. No deaths. Sixty-eight combat-capable."

Light injuries that still allowed movement were not included in this count.

There was no one here who had not been lightly wounded.

Even Krische had slammed into a tree while retreating, injuring her shoulder.

Dagra knew that inside those gauntlet-wrapped hands, countless blood blisters had burst and bandages were wound.

Soldiers rendered unable to fight were sent toward the fortress with two escorts per person from among those still mobile.

Those judged unrecoverable or dead numbered about six.

Because the raids were kept short and confined to ambushes, losses were remarkably low.

"――That is enough. More than enough time has already been bought. The enemy will be immobilized for a while."

"We still have sixty-eight. Three more days of operations are possible. ……We still have provisions."

The provisions she had had the Second Battalion carry out had been dispersed and used to sustain them.

Hardtack and honey, mostly――they could not light fires, so that was all that was available, supplemented largely by what they took from the enemy.

Krische removed her gauntlets and took out a small pouch she had hung from her neck, tucked carefully inside her cloak.

It held a single candy. She confirmed it was clean, and narrowed her eyes.

"……Going by yesterday's withdrawal, Krische feels our harassment has produced considerable results. At the very least, Krische would like to, *kh,* hit the fortress engineers one more time……"

Suppressing the trembling of her body, she coughed softly.

Dagra spoke, concerned only for Krische's wellbeing.

"I understand the reasoning. However……more than anything, Krische-sama, your body is at its limit."

He did not use her title. Dagra addressed her that way.

He no longer saw her as a fellow soldier so much as the lord he was bound to serve.

"And a recall order was issued from Selene-sama the other day."

Krische flinched.

"……Krische is fine."

And she closed her eyes.

What she saw was Selene, clinging to Bogan's body and weeping.

She had known Selene would grieve.

Selene had been grieving far more than she had imagined.

Krische had been powerless to help.

She had been struck on the cheek.

*She may hate me,* she thought.

Regret swirled endlessly through her chest, making her feel ill.

"Krische should have killed him."

Why had she not killed Gildanstein right then?

If she had, she might have been able to comfort Selene.

She could have told her: *I properly avenged him.*

But Krische had gotten scared and run, and failed to take the revenge that should have been within reach.

An absurdly foolish course of action.

"……Krische has not met Selene's expectations at all. ……It is the worst."

"That is not true. You have more than……no, far more than met Selene-sama's expectations."

"Krische does not want Selene to think Krische is……useless, any more than she already does."

At first, Krische had told Dagra and the others that this was necessary to stabilize the army, to halt the pursuit.

This was an essential military task, and therefore the hardship was unavoidable.

But she could no longer maintain even that pretense.

Lately, she murmured Selene's name as if talking to herself.

As if reminding herself.

She was no monster at all. What stood before them was a hopelessly young girl.

Her violet eyes wavered with anxiety and sadness, sunk in self-reproach.

Dried blood clung to her hair; her breathing was shallow and uneven.

"……For now, please rest a little."

The soldiers around them, too, watched her with pained eyes.

This had been a grueling fight for them as well.

There had been casualties.

Yet there was no open complaint, because the girl before them always did more than any of them.

She was faithful to her role to the last.

When they were deep in exhaustion, she had charged alone.

She reaped countless heads with the detachment of a task, and yet kept watch over her squad's movements.

Many of them had been saved by her.

*If you are afraid of losing comrades, then kill the enemy who is targeting them first.*

She practiced what she preached more than anyone.

She did not want to reduce combat-capable personnel.

That was all that was in Krische's mind, and it was not from any sense of camaraderie.

But the fact of the action remained, and none directed ill will toward her.

Anyone could see at a glance that she was at her limit, and it worsened by the day.

And still the girl turned her will toward fighting.

Whatever her reasons, her faithfulness to the mission was, beyond question, worthy of respect.

"……After dark, Krische will raid the fortress. Krische will go scout."

She rose as she said it, staggered, and nearly fell.

The one who caught her was Kalua.

"Ah……"

"Oh, come on! Throwing a tantrum like a child, and look at the state of you――you're in no shape for a raid."

"K-Kalua……!"

Kalua scratched her head furiously.

None of them had been able to bathe properly for some time, and her scalp itched.

Mia raised her voice in alarm, but Kalua silenced her with a wave of her hand.

"Ah……um……?"

Krische looked at Kalua with puzzled eyes, and Kalua met them with a near-glare.

"If you really have to do it, I'll take a few people and handle the scouting. You get some sleep, bunny."

"But that's――"

Kalua pressed her hand to Krische's forehead and frowned.

The fever was astonishing; it was a wonder she could move at all.

She understood in theory that one could keep moving through mana manipulation no matter how spent the body was, but the state of things was absurd. Krische's body was trembling with fine shivers.

"This, that, whatever――I'm in better shape than you with this fever, aren't I? Ah, what's it called, captain――yeah, temporary assumption of command."

She looked at Dagra as she spoke, irritation plain, and the taken-aback Dagra nodded.

"……I judge the commanding officer to be in a state unfit for sound judgment. Captains――both of you, do you concur?"

"Sir. We concur."

Korintz and Tagel saluted immediately and agreed.

It was a right held by a second-in-command.

Used in cases where a commanding officer had lost their senses or the like, it allowed the next in line to assume command without authorization from above, by forcibly overriding the chain of command.

It was exercised with the unanimous consent of all direct subordinate unit leaders, temporarily stripping the commanding officer of authority.

Krische was the adjutant of the First Corps, and Dagra was the centurion directly beneath her.

Strictly speaking, his position was not that of an adjutant, but the regulation allowed for some flexibility: it could be performed by a person holding an adjutant-equivalent role.

Currently, the commanding officer was Krische, and Dagra was precisely her adjutant-equivalent centurion.

There was therefore no issue under military law.

It was seldom used because misapplication carried severe penalties, but it was also the soldiers' right to protect themselves from an abusive or incompetent commanding officer.

"Acting command is mine, Dagra. ……Krische-sama will rest."

"Eh, ah……"

Krische looked at Dagra in confusion.

But it was a lawful right defined by military regulation.

Krische had, for the time being, lost her authority to command them.

"Vulture, this is wrong……Krische is perfectly conscious and lucid……!"

"Nevertheless, I must judge that you are not in a state for sound decision-making. If you wish to dispute it, we will hear your case at the fortress."

"Kh……"

"……I judge that sufficient delaying action has been accomplished. All personnel, full rest, then prepare for withdrawal. We leave the mountain after nightfall. Ready yourselves accordingly."

Everyone saluted as one, and Krische glared at Kalua.

Weakly――eyes as frail as the girl herself.

"Look, I was asked by that Bery woman, right, along with Mia. Isn't that right, Mia?"

"Huh? Oh, uh, yes――um, I mean, Corps Commander's Adjutant. We were asked by Argan-sama to look after the Corps Commander's Adjutant."

Mia offered a confused but somehow earnest salute, and Kalua laughed, pointing to the small pouch hanging from Krische's neck.

"See? And you made a promise, didn't you? ……That candy――don't you need to let her eat it soon?"

"Ah, well, that is……but……"

"No buts about it. Honestly……how serious can one person be."

*Listen,* Kalua said, leaning in close and fixing Krische with a stare.

"……Corps Commander's Adjutant, this is a legitimate decision in accordance with military law. You have been ordered to rest by the acting commander. Understood?"

"Ugh……"

"Is it not the Corps Commander's Adjutant herself who always says, with this adorable little mouth of hers, that *rules are important*?"

Kalua indicated Krische's lips with one fingertip.

Krische's eyes darted away, and Kalua heaved a sigh.

*Someone get a blanket,* she called, stripping the blood-soaked cloak from Krische's shoulders and beckoning Mia.

"Mia, warm her up. It should help a little. I'll stand watch."

"Ah, o-okay……"

Mia took the blanket someone had brought, wrapped her arms around Krische, and sat down.

The girl's body, so impossibly small and slender, radiated alarming heat, and Mia frowned.

Krische, as though she had given up, offered no resistance, trembling as if freezing.

"……Please rest a little. It will be all right."

"That's right. It is an order from the acting commander."

Krische gave Kalua and Mia a faint glare, then quietly pressed her body close and closed her eyes.

It was more like losing consciousness.

Her weight suddenly increased, and within moments, the sound of soft breathing.

She had barely slept at all, come to think of it.

Since arriving here, she had only ever managed the lightest of naps.

Mia gently held the feverish body, and stroked her soothingly, as one would comfort a child――

"I understand the sentiment, but. ……That is no way to speak to a superior officer."

"Ow!?"

Kalua received a punch to the head from Dagra.

"……Good grief."

But even Dagra's face wore a smile of relief, and those watching all gazed at Krische's sleeping face with something soft in their expressions.
