# Chapter 196: Moon Mirror

"Damn it, are those people out of their minds? Don't tell me they've sent their whole army at us."

Red cursed as he cut down an enemy soldier with his greatsword.

The enemy's ferocious assault showed no sign of relenting. This was no mere harassment — the enemy was seriously targeting the center.

What they had seen upon emerging from the forest was the magnificent battle line of the Alberan army. An overwhelming rain of arrows.

The nighttime raid had been so utterly unexpected that their response was delayed, and in an instant, the Elderant army was pushed back.

Against a force so blatantly attempting encirclement, any sensible commander would never attempt a frontal breakthrough. One moment of difficulty and the encircler would be encircled — it was an act of madness.

Yet precisely because that was how they had thought, they were caught off guard, and the Elderant central army now found itself in a dire position.

What had kept them from collapsing was the forest lying between the two armies — terrain that favored Elderant troops — and the fierce resistance of the Hunting Party of Veze led by Red.

He ran left and right to stabilize the buckled front line, preventing the army's collapse — but even so, it was like trying to hold back the tide.

At this rate, the disadvantage would not change.

Red made his decision and led a counterattack forward.

But fighting through the forest and breaking through the enemy line, only to emerge on the other side — another enemy battle line awaited them. At least equal in number to the entire Elderant central army; including those who had pushed into the forest, the enemy force certainly exceeded forty thousand.

They must have left barely any reserves guarding against Mekrea to the south or Akaz to the north.

It was a full-force frontal assault.

Unease spread even among the elite Red led — the Hunting Party of Veze.

"Red, this is impossible! We've got no choice but to hold the line until Akaz or Mekrea get here! I'll take the rear guard—"

"You'd get yourself killed! You think they'll just let us retreat easily after we've cut this deep!?"

He rejected Bez's words immediately.

They had forced their way this far. A simple retreat would invite pursuit and heavy casualties.

"We take the commander's head and pull out! The banner's right there — let's go!"

The enemy had committed a large force into the forest between the two armies. What remained on this open plain was mainly archers. The infantry line was thin.

His soldiers were fatigued.

But they were the Hunting Party of Veze. The elite of the great tribe, the finest fighting force in all of Elderant.

Warriors forged on the battlefield — ordinary foes were no match for them.

"Out of my way!!"

At the head charged Red with his greatsword.

The blade was five shaku long — its thickness made it more slab than sword, yet he swung it freely, and before it, armor was meaningless.

He shattered braced spears, crushed shields, and took lives.

Rather than striking at the enemy's weak points, he had deliberately punched through the strongest part of their front — an instinct akin to a beast's.

Their opponent was the army commanded by the veteran Felwors Keesliton.

What appeared to be weak points were deliberately created weaknesses, mere invitations — he understood this instinctively. He knew that breaking through there would expose them to a storm of arrows from both flanks.

He burst through the battle line in a single breath, and the banner came into view — not a corps commander's flag but a general's standard.

His eyes found the female general issuing orders from horseback.

She, too, recognized them. She lowered the visor engraved with a face of wrath.

Dismounting, she took a longsword in her right hand and received a steel shield in her left from an attendant.

"Bez! I'm going to lock onto her — clear the flanks!"

"On it—"

His instincts were right.

What the enemy had positioned at the weak point was archers. Had they broken through there, the volley would have annihilated Red's force.

But because they had punched straight through the front, the archers were shaken too — they were a step behind in responding to the breakthrough.

If he could close with the enemy general before their archery commander regrouped, the enemy could never unleash their arrow rain.

Red surged ahead — low stance, closing the distance in an instant.

Like a beast running on all fours, the charge brought him before the enemy general.

His whole body twisted, and from there it was like a butterfly in flight.

He swung his greatsword, using its inertia to propel a feinting lunge, circling to the enemy's left — in a single instant, bypassing the side of the raised shield.

And riding that momentum, a centrifugal slash.

He would permit no deflection. All his strength would crush and cleave through every barrier before him.

"—Do not underestimate me!!"

But the enemy, too, was a warrior worthy of commanding an army.

Against the diagonal slash from upper right, she chose not to flee but to step in.

She twisted her body low — and the whirlwind she conjured was her own.

Unfazed by the pressure of Red's greatsword, she evaded the blade and spun her whole body like a spiral.

She slammed her steel shield into Red's body.

In Rollka-style swordsmanship, the shield was not a thing to protect oneself — it was a bludgeon to crush and flatten any enemy in one's path.

"Tch!"

Red abandoned his attack on reflex, kicked the steel bludgeon and backflipped — putting distance between them, he settled into his stance.

"My name is Teknrea Remin! A kingdom noble who protects the people of Alberan — their shield. ……Don't think you can look down on me just because I'm a woman, barbarian invader."

From within the visor of wrath came a hard, steel-resonant voice.

"So that title's no bluff, huh. ……I've no intention of underestimating you. I've seen more than my share of strong women."

Red thought of Shelna's face and smiled.

"I'm Red of Lani, under the great tribe Veze! I've got no grudge against you or Alberan, but die here and—!?"

An explosion thundered from behind, freezing him mid-sentence.

The female general, too, showed a momentary lapse, instinctively glancing toward Red's rear.

The sound had not come from within the forest. Red, born and raised in the forest, could not mishear. The rustling of leaves like ripples — without question, it had come from beyond the forest, from the main camp.

"Red, the main camp—"

"—I know!!"

Red shouted and pressed forward, greatsword in hand.

But General Teknrea leapt backward, opening distance, and raised her voice.

"Alberinea has arrived. All units, buy time! The enemy is few — there is no need to cower before beasts that have left the forest. If we hold them here, this battle is over."

"……You little—"

"Red of Lani, I acknowledge your strength as a warrior……but I'm afraid I cannot oblige. I am not a beast but a soldier, a shield for the weak. I have no intention of offering a fair fight to blood-starved beasts who lord their reckless courage over others. —Javelin troops!"

At her signal, the sound of wind cut through the air.

Snapping incoming javelins as they came, Red pressed forward.

Teknrea fell back further, and Red took another step—

"Red, it's no use! Don't chase! The enemy's offensive, this whole situation — something's wrong. Remember why you left Zren behind!"

But Bez's words stopped him in his tracks.

"The enemy's target is the main camp — this isn't just a night raid! Whatever magic they're using, they're trying to end this here! You need to fall back now and go protect Shelna-sama!!"

"But Bez—"

"Everyone willing to die, follow me! I'll take the general's head right here!"

"You idiot, don't just decide—"

"Shut up! You go to Shelna-sama! Mekrea will come up from the south soon — if I can hold out until then, I'll find a way to make it!"

Bez drove his sword into an enemy soldier's chest and declared:

"We can't afford to lose Shelna-sama no matter what. You go first!!"

"……Damn it, Bez — I'm punching you later."

"I'll beat you right back, kid!"

Bez laughed and charged forward.

Splitting the party in this situation — even if he took the enemy general's head, there was no surviving this.

And yet a staggering number of soldiers chose to stay at his words. Bez led them forward, taking Red's place as they charged toward the enemy general.

He would not return alive.

Red gripped his greatsword until blood seeped from his palms, glared at the female general, and turned.

Cutting down enemy soldiers behind him, he plunged into the forest — toward the main camp.

"Zren!"

"……I'm fine. Just a scratch."

"……"

Blood dripped from the arm of the great man — Zren.

Blue flashes and explosions.

The main camp itself hadn't suffered heavy damage.

It was spared because the strikes had concentrated on the rear battle line rather than the main camp itself.

But the cost — the chaos and damage were so great that even Shelna was shaken.

The blown-apart battle line and the few reserves left at the main camp were all but annihilated.

The remaining reserves had been committed to holding the front line in the forest ahead.

The main camp was nearly defenseless.

"Griffins……!"

The High Priest Jiegret, who had emerged unscathed, raised his staff and pointed it skyward.

Blue light set the mana crystal at its tip ablaze, and what it unleashed was a flash — the mana crystals carved into the priestly staff converted magical power and released it as offensive particles.

The flash pierced the wing of a griffin soaring high above in an instant, shooting one down.

Two men tumbled from it, and—

"……!?"

But the griffin riders surrounding them moved instantly, catching their bodies in midair.

These were no ordinary griffin riders.

Their handling was masterful — seasoned warriors held those reins.

All of them, most likely.

Such skill was not acquired overnight.

"……Kreisharana warriors."

Shelna stared wide-eyed, murmuring in a daze.

A report had arrived alongside the ancient dragon's visit to the capital — that Kreisharana envoys had visited Alberan and diplomatic relations had been restored.

It must have been true.

With them appearing on the battlefield, there was no room for doubt.

The Kreisharana griffin riders, who once dominated the skies, had sided with Alberan.

"Everyone still able, flee into the northern forest immediately!! Regroup with Kils's corps and re-establish the main camp!! —Jiegret-sama, Zren!"

"Do as she says! Leave behind those who cannot move!"

The griffin riders were few in number.

But they possessed unknown weapons, and their own main camp was just as thin.

What would follow was certainly the enemy's killing blow — to think they could handle it with the main camp alone would be far too naive.

They needed to take cover in the forest.

Regrouping from this state was impossible — she was decisive to the last.

Those who could not move would have to be left behind.

And that choice was, at the very least, the best one available in this moment.

Alongside the Bonjara bombardment, what burst from the south was black-armored soldiers and jade tiger cavalry.

The griffin riders, flying in from the west — directly behind the enemy main camp — had drawn attention that way, while the real main-camp assault came from the right, from the south. The plan had been to swiftly take the enemy general's head and throw the main camp into chaos, but—

"Mm……they're fast on their feet. Gururun, no playing around."

Krische pouted, patting the jade tiger's neck.

The jade tiger, as if to say *fine*, crushed the skull of the enemy soldier it had been rolling around with its foreleg.

The remaining body twitched and convulsed, but no one paid it any mind.

This was not combat but cleanup.

The black-armored soldiers advanced through the encampment, finishing off wounded soldiers left behind.

"This speed of decision……they anticipated us."

Krische let her inorganic violet gleam in the firelight, surveying and capturing everything around her.

The enemy had fled quickly.

Normally no one would abandon their main camp and run, but this general must have excellent judgment.

Not south but into the northern forest — the speed of that decision.

And the enemy's troop disposition — clearly, the enemy had been wary of a strike from the rear.

They had known exactly where Krische's force would emerge and what they were targeting.

*A 'reasonably' clever opponent*, she thought, narrowing her eyes.

Still, judging by the state of this main camp — even if they had noticed, it could only have been at the last moment.

Had they known from the start, they would have reinforced the camp more heavily.

The enemy had merely stacked inferences from the night raid and arrived at the correct answer.

Confusion still reigned, and the girl found a clear solution in an instant.

The bombardment had completely disrupted the enemy's cohesion.

Ordering an immediate retreat had been a wise decision, but there was no way they could be organized — there were no traps.

In short, the surprise attack had simply turned into a pursuit.

"Krische-sama, processing of the main camp banner is complete."

She nodded at Dagra's words and sighed that one more task had been added.

A troublesome opponent — she should kill them quickly.

"Krische wanted to display the head here, but……it can't be helped."

While having Bonjaras thrown at the enemy soldiers emerging from the eastern forest, cleanup of the main camp perimeter was essentially complete.

Bonjaras were not specifically for bombardment.

Rather, their primary use was for encounter battles.

They were a ranged attack option carried by the Black Flag Special Service. Handling was restricted to squad leaders and above — those skilled in mana use and possessing a certain composure — due to the risk of accidents, but the psychological impact of a single Bonjara in encounter-heavy situations like this was immense.

During the Galshan war, the tight formations had meant she'd confiscated most of them to account for the possibility of failed throws, but as the debut for hand-thrown Bonjaras, the results were quite satisfactory.

Fear of the unknown weapon kept the enemy from leaving the forest.

"Including mop-up, operations here are concluded. We'll end fighting here and pursue the escaped enemy general. Krische and Vulture will go ahead, the rest follow as you can."

"Yes, ma'am!"

"Gururun, that way."

More work. Letting them escape was most displeasing.

That said, it was merely a matter of taking a bit more time.

"Hiii—!?"

The fleeing enemy was the main camp's elite — but unlike the Black Flag Special Service, not every one of them was a mana user.

It took only an instant for Krische to catch the rear of the retreating force.

Even mana users could not outrun the jade tiger's legs.

Against ordinary humans, they might as well have been standing still.

Advancing through the forest at a measured pace so as not to get too far ahead, she shot arrows into the backs of fleeing soldiers.

For Krische, killing with a bow was no different from target practice.

When the enemy presented their backs, there was no longer any chance of missing.

One, two, ten, twenty—

"Oh, thirty. Ehehe, shooting them in the back is easy."

She leapt down and replenished her arrows from the body of a killed archer.

Elderant's use of short bows was rather convenient in situations like this.

Seeing Dagra and the others catch up, she pushed forward again. Krische repeated her target practice.

The Black Flag soldiers felt their spines stiffen watching their commander ride her jade tiger, mass-producing corpses.

Not a single one was missed. They merely had to run.

A single drawn and released arrow meant one dead. Thicket and darkness made no difference.

Even those who held their breath and hid became corpses.

Even the veterans, Dagra included, could not entirely suppress the chill that crept into their hearts.

"To be frank, I feel something close to dread. What an astounding person she is."

"……Krische-sama is that sort of person."

To Waltza, who ran beside him studying the corpses, Dagra replied.

"This is war — as a soldier on the battlefield, Krische-sama is merely doing what is natural. ……It is only that Krische-sama is far too accomplished at it."

The enemy was the enemy, so she killed them. Simply that.

It was only natural for a soldier, and she was doing nothing strange.

But Krische was an extraordinarily gifted aberration.

Treating people as nothing, effortlessly.

Battle never became battle; what she performed was never combat but always killing.

One-sided, merciless, cold.

"Watching that figure from behind, one suddenly realizes that all the glittering deeds, the honor, the radiance of the battlefield — all of it was nothing but deception. ……Krische-sama is the ideal we soldiers wished for, and precisely because of that, she is the furthest thing from what we desired."

Violence to take from others.

Violence to impose the unreasonable.

The essence of war was, ultimately, nothing more than that.

"Staking our lives in mutual combat — a warrior's pride is merely a justification applied after the fact. Krische-sama sees only the true nature of war. And she makes us see it. ……The truth we refused to look at, the truth we covered over."

That was why they felt dread, Dagra continued.

The war against the Holy Empire — the mountain battles.

Krische had been exactly that: a hunter.

The one who kills, never the one who is killed.

Now as then, nothing had changed.

She did not look away from the unreason that occurs on the battlefield, nor did she flee behind pleasant-sounding words.

More earnestly than anyone, she gazed upon what the battlefield truly was.

War was a world ruled by the logic of mathematics, without warmth or feeling — nothing but filth born of human vice. That was how she perceived it.

The longer one spent with her, the more one came to know her.

She was pure through and through.

The dread one felt came precisely from the sensation of having what one had hidden behind 'pleasant-sounding words' laid bare.

"More than anyone, I curse myself for feeling dread of Krische-sama."

Waltza regarded Dagra.

He turned his gaze toward Krische, who ran ahead continuing her 'target practice,' and behind his eyelids recalled a certain scene.

*'……That's right. Krische wants to hurry home to the estate and do cooking and tea parties with Bery and Selene, so if asked what for, that would be the reason.'*

What do you fight for? The answer to such a question.

Drenched in blood, having killed dozens, having stolen their lives — without so much as a pang of conscience, smiling at a happy future.

She was endlessly, absurdly off-kilter — a blood-soaked innocent slaughterer.

Beyond comprehension, refreshingly so.

And yet Waltza could not find the words to express all the emotions that had risen in him at that moment.

The young girl who clung to her servant without a care for who was watching — she was, right now before his eyes, simply carrying on her slaughter.

As naturally as breathing, as though it were the most obvious thing in the world, she ended countless lives.

Someone's parent, someone's husband or lover, renowned heroes or craven cowards.

All entirely irrelevant, without distinction.

On the battlefield, wielding the only thing that could be called righteous.

—And so she scattered death.

"……What is truly terrifying is, at the very least, not that young girl."

In a kinder world, she could have been endlessly kind.

"I think I finally understand why a man as upright as yourself would swear such loyalty to Krische-sama. ……You do not regard her as a soldier."

She was merely a mirror.

What was reflected there was not her essence.

"What I felt the moment my arm was severed……was likely something close to that."

Waltza said this, and touched the prosthetic on his left arm.

# Chapter 197: Indigo and Violet

Starlight and faint lamplight — Keesliton army left wing, the main camp of Teknrea Remin.

The man would likely never stand again.

The enemy commander who had collapsed still drew breath, but javelins riddled his entire body. A spear pierced his belly, and his left arm was nearly torn off.

Most of the man's subordinates — the enemy's assault force that had pushed ahead — had been dealt with.

"A magnificent fight. I take back calling you barbarians. ……At the very least, you are men deserving to be honored as warriors."

She set down her steel shield, raised the visor engraved with wrath, and rested the longsword on her right shoulder.

Teknrea Remin praised the man without reservation.

Whether it was for the young man who had retreated, or for pride.

Regardless, the man had never intended to return alive.

They had been outnumbered, but every one of them had fought as though death held no meaning — a ferocity that made Teknrea fear for her life despite holding the advantage.

"Bez, was it. I salute your valor. ……Have you any last words?"

"……Nothing. ……Not a bad……way to die."

The man spoke.

On a face that was — for a woman — quite sharp-featured, Teknrea allowed the slightest softness, and nodded.

"Graceful to the end. I do not claim deep knowledge of your faith, but……"

She picked up a fallen sword and placed it in the man's hand.

Elderant — it was one of the faiths that had once been widespread even in Alberan.

When a warrior dies, let them hold their weapon.

Then they may depart to the afterworld as a heroic spirit.

"Die as a warrior. You are, at the very least, a man who deserves to meet death thus."

"……My thanks."

Hearing those words, she drove her longsword into the fallen man's neck.

Finishing him, she flicked the blood away.

In this situation, there was no need to display his head.

"We've weathered it, for the moment. Splendid command, my lady."

"Stop with 'my lady.' How old do you think I am, Milkaruz."

Teknrea was already approaching forty. She was well past the age for being called 'my lady.'

The old adjutant — Milkaruz — laughed with evident amusement.

"I swore to call you that until you settle down, my lady."

"That frivolous oath is null and void. Karlea has given birth to three boys. There's no need for me to bear children at this point — someone will inherit the Remin house. Give it up already."

Exasperated and displeased, she spoke, and Milkaruz only looked more amused.

"Had I known it would come to this, I should never have taught you the sword. My lady, who wouldn't have hurt a fly, now reduced to this……how lamentable."

"People have their aptitudes. You're probably the only person in this world who wants to see me in a dress and dancing."

She answered Milkaruz's quip, then turned her gaze toward the enemy main camp — beyond the forest.

The army of the tribe called Mekrea, which had been deployed to the south, had begun a complete rout.

According to messenger reports, Alberinea had assaulted the main camp and killed the enemy general in a single stroke. And almost immediately after that — the explosion at the enemy main camp.

She did not know what had been done. But that, too, was surely Alberinea's work.

Just as Elrouga had predicted — the sheer speed left her stunned.

She remembered the face of the girl who sat in the council.

The marshal showed a composure incredible for her age — but the girl beside her had no commanding presence, appearing exactly like the child she looked to be.

To be honest, up until just a few days ago, all Teknrea had felt was anxiety.

The fate of the Kingdom of Alberan rested on that single girl.

A strategic plan built entirely, as a matter of course, around her.

It all assumed her victory — an absurdly convenient plan that seemed anything but sane.

Persuaded by fellow central general Gokkalus, she had agreed out of desperation — with invasions from three nations, it was a situation all but lost, and there was nothing to clutch at but straws. Yet she had never imagined such a result.

Since the war's start, in just half a month, Alberinea had crushed not only Galshan but was already on the verge of defeating Elderant.

*'Krische will just circle around the kingdom and take out the enemy armies one by one, so just hold out until then. Krische has chores and things to help with at the estate too, so about a month……hmm, at most two months and Krische would like to have everything wrapped up.'*

Everyone had doubted the sanity of the girl who said this with a straight face.

And yet not one person had raised their voice against it — surely because they had all felt the same thing.

An emotion beyond words.

The jewel-like violet peeking from behind beautiful silver hair.

Reflected there was an absolute, unquestionable confidence.

Speaking of it as though describing a minor errand for the next day — her manner had something that brooked no objection. And now, at last, Teknrea understood.

"……Reason and common sense got in the way. But the one who forged a pact with the divine being — perhaps I knew from the very beginning."

"My lady……?"

"The moment I saw the divine being appear in the capital, I knew one army alone could never prevail."

Teknrea, too, had been there at the training grounds that day.

The titanic body to rival the royal castle, its boulder-like scales partly shed — the sheer magical power that distorted the very air simply by its presence.

And from its back, accompanied by the jade tiger, the girl who stepped down.

A scene from a painting — something that defied reality.

"I thought that even with the nation's full might, could such a thing truly be felled — yet Alberinea challenged a being worthy of being called a god, alone."

Publicly, it was said to have been an audience.

But Alberinea had been wrapped in bandages head to toe, covered in wounds like the dragon. And the light that had pierced the sky the day before. The sudden restoration of relations with Kreisharana. The unmistakable respect from a people who revered the divine being and honored martial strength.

In the capital, various rumors and speculation had circulated about the dragon's visit.

But those who had been present at that moment — they alone understood which was the truth.

The assassination attempt on the queen — the servant caught up in it.

The countless nobles killed in retaliation were surely her doing as well.

Because Alberinea was a being who would challenge a divine being for the sake of one beloved servant — and succeed.

"A prodigy who bends a nation to her will — she is truly a child of the gods, an Alberan. The title of Heaven's Sword must be the appellation for one who, in mortal flesh, reaches even the divine."

Rationally, Teknrea had felt this war to be reckless in the extreme.

Seeing the listless girl in the council room had only deepened the feeling.

And yet — the scene Teknrea had witnessed that day had been seared far too deeply into her heart.

To a greater or lesser degree, the reason the central generals had approached this war with optimism must have been precisely that.

"It is as reassuring as having a divine being as an ally. But it is also……frightening."

She called to mind the girl who had overflowed with tears as she embraced her servant.

And she closed her eyes, imagining everything that must have transpired in the process.

Born of a ducal house of royal blood.

She had known of the custom of royal cursed children, but she had not believed in it.

Yet a power far too great for any individual to hold — she understood well why someone like her would be called a cursed child.

If there existed a self that feared not even the gods, it would be the ultimate unreason for many.

No matter how sincere the wish, the hope — she would overpower it.

An individual who obtains everything she desires — the very existence of such a being is tantamount to a natural disaster.

"There is nothing to be gained by dwelling on it. ……One can only pray that her sword is wielded solely for the defense of the nation."

That said — if that unreason were visited only upon the enemy.

Humans are such conveniently self-serving creatures, Teknrea reflected.

* * *

Here was the main camp of the Veze army's left wing, in a forest blazing with bonfires.

The Elderant central army — the Veze forces — had no reserves to spare.

The fierce assault from Keesliton's forces pressing from the east, from the front.

To repel it, right wing, center, and left wing alike had committed everything.

Shelna, too, could only flee to one of these positions — and with the enemy approaching from south and west, it was half-inevitable that she would end up with the rough warriors of the Kils corps guarding the far-left wing, a tribe under Veze's banner.

"Aron! Form a battle line facing south toward the main camp immediately. We merge with Akaz to the north and withdraw from here at once."

Gasping for breath, only Shelna, Zren, and a handful of elite had arrived ahead.

Leaving the rear to High Priest Jiegret, she had brought only the swiftest mana users, and what she had reached was the main camp of Aron Kils's seven-thousand-strong corps.

The tall warrior chief, Aron Kils, looked down at Shelna with open confusion on his face.

His tattooed, muscular upper body was bare, and his only armor was a set of gauntlets.

Given the night raid, he wore even less than Shelna, who had managed only a breastplate and gauntlets.

"……I heard some strange sounds, and now you've abandoned the main camp to come here?"

"It was an emergency. A full corps of enemy forces that defeated Galshan is closing from the south. The enemy has elite forces with Kreisharana griffins — mana crystals that explode wiped out the camp reserves."

She stated this in rapid succession and sighed.

"……If we don't move immediately, we'll face pursuit not just from the raiding force but from their main army too."

"……Have you lost your mind? Night raid or not, you're panicking far too much. Abandoning the main camp and ordering a retreat out of nowhere—"

Shelna drew her sword and pointed it at him.

"Aron, there's no time for debate. Follow me in silence."

"……Or what, a duel?"

Irritably scratching his head, Aron glanced at the men behind him.

Kils's soldiers had their hackles up, weapons in hand.

"That's terrifying, I'm sure. I can't beat Veze's Sword Princess in a fight, true enough, but if you kill me here, do you really think these men will fall in line? Ordering people around is your right — I don't deny it — but maybe you should think about the situation."

Aron glared at Shelna, but did not draw his sword.

Cutting down an unarmed opponent without so much as a word would have no legitimacy.

It would not be a duel governed by custom, but simple murder.

"The only people who'll meekly obey someone who turned tail and ran from their own camp are trained dogs. That won't satisfy anyone's sense of honor. I'm not Red. Are you willing to talk like a rational person?"

"……I'll discuss it with you as much as you like afterward. The situation is urgent beyond measure. Please — just follow me."

"Ha, first threats, now 'please'?"

"……Aron."

Bleeding from his wounded left arm, the great man Zren stepped forward.

And dropped to both knees, bowing his head.

Shelna and the others called "Zren," but he remained silent, pressing his forehead to the ground.

"What're you playing at, Zren? I've got no taste for getting my kicks from a man's groveling."

"This is urgent. ……Just for now. For now, please follow Shelna-sama. If you still have grievances after, you can tear me limb from limb — I won't complain."

Aron looked at the kneeling Zren, and scratched his head roughly.

Then he clicked his tongue and glared at Shelna.

"……This is a debt Kils is owed by Veze. Surely you won't say no to that?"

"……Very well. That's fine."

"I've lost the mood. I'll do as you say, but……Zren, don't forget what you said."

"I swear it. ……You have my gratitude."

Aron turned to his men—

"Tell the vanguard! Prepare to turn tail — we're linking up with Akaz. All reserves to the rear—!?"

The instant he began giving orders, only a handful of those present leapt.

A blast of wind tore through the spot where Aron had stood a heartbeat ago, and the great tree beyond splintered apart.

—It was not human. A colossal beast.

The warriors recognized it instantly—

Surrounded since birth by extraordinary people — her heroic father, his subordinates, and then Bery and Krische — she tends to undervalue herself and impose excessive effort. An earnest, overly responsible personality.

When she finds free time, she pours it into study and administrative work — a born workaholic. Since becoming marshal, "I'm busy" has become her catchphrase, but establishing a military academy to fulfill her father's wishes has her full of motivation.

She adores Krische as a little sister but keenly feels the danger of her abnormality. She views having to send Krische to war as proof of her own inadequacy, and her goal is to give Krische a life of peace far from the battlefield. She hates herself for relying on and using Krische, yet the weight of her responsibilities and circumstances won't allow otherwise.

She holds deep respect for Bery but also a sense of rivalry and jealousy toward her abilities, and dreams of one day making her say uncle.

Prefers fundamental tactics built on troop quality and numbers over risky gambits, and emphasizes securing strategic advantages beforehand. Her self-deprecation is, in her current position, a virtue — she evaluates others' abilities honestly and assigns roles without political coloring, earning genuine regard as the kingdom's military chief.

**○ Bery (Lipus) Argan — "Chaos / Good"**

A Krische-sama-over-the-world-type girl. Christand household servant.

Daughter of the fallen House Argan. Selene's aunt.

Originally frail, leading to extremely low self-worth and an introverted temperament. Pained by troubling others with her health, she adopted a deliberately cold demeanor as a child so others wouldn't have to worry — but being disliked by the servants only deepened her anguish, and she eventually came to wish for her own death.

Even after the family's ruin and her health improving at Christand, her self-loathing only grew. Entrusted by her beloved sister to look after the earnest Selene, she tried to be bright and cheerful in her sister's place — but that too went poorly, and she cursed herself for being unable to do anything right.

Meeting Krische and receiving that pure affection and goodwill was what saved her.

Outwardly gentle and approachable, but harbors some distrust of men from the Argan family's fall. Combined with her introverted nature, she keeps a line drawn in her relationships.

A natural talent with excellent efficiency — too gifted to waste as a mere servant, but content in her role and unwilling to step onto any public stage.

Currently directs nearly all her talent and passion toward cooking. Both Bogan and Selene always considered her abilities wasted.

Surprisingly stubborn despite her appearance; logical on the surface but intuitive at heart. When she feels something is right, emotion takes precedence over reason.

She holds deep affection for Krische and places her happiness first, though possessiveness has been growing. Her love — rooted in high self-loathing and an introverted personality — carries strong dependency, but since Krische shares the same tendency, it is rather beyond help.

Lately she has come to terms with many things: what Krische desires is what she desires. She feels this is right, and believes it is not a great mistake.

Perhaps in the self that exists beside Krische, she has found the something she had always been searching for.

Appearance: Red hair cut at the shoulders. Brown eyes. Small-framed. Larger chest. Youthful, pretty face.

→ On Krische: The most beautiful existence in the world. The purpose of her life. Wants her to stay by her side forever.
Strengths: Housework including cooking. Etiquette. Sophistry. Self-defense.
Likes: Housework including cooking. Teaching. Doting on Krische.
Dislikes: Self-serving people. Herself.
Worries: She crossed the line.
