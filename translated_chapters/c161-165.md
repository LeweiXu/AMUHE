# Chapter 161: Cookies

"Th-the rooms are done…… Bery-sama, I've finished Selene-sama's room."

"Thank you, Elvena-sama. Fufu, you've been getting ahead of things ever since you arrived, and now I'm running out of things to do myself."

Having gone looking for Bery, who was supposed to be doing laundry, Elvena found her already in the kitchen preparing a meal.

From the look of things, the soup was already finished.

Elvena stiffened, mentally retracing the day's work.

After the two of them had gathered underwear, nightclothes, and the rest of the laundry, Bery was to handle the washing. Elvena was assigned room cleaning.

She cleaned the room assigned to her, then cleaned Selene's room. When she went to clean what was nominally Krische's room — the room shared by Bery, Kreschenta, and the three of them — it was already spotless.

The bed, neatly made compared to the morning — that was where the confusion began.

Bery was supposed to be doing laundry. Yesterday's rain had prevented hanging anything to dry, so there should have been a fair amount. Even accounting for how quickly Elvena had worked, she couldn't imagine Bery finishing in the time it took her to clean two rooms.

Yet when she hurried to the courtyard, the laundry was already strung up on the line, drying.

The moisture clinging to the fabric had clearly been there for some time. It must have been hung out around the time Elvena started on the second room.

What on earth was going on — Elvena grew more and more bewildered.

For the time being, she picked up a mop, intending to clean the front entrance and back door she had passed earlier. Yesterday's rain had left the entryways a little dirty.

— But there was no trace of dirt anywhere.

Before she had gone out to the courtyard, it should still have been there — yet all she found was a polished floor.

Where had Bery disappeared to?

She hurried outside to the jade tiger — Gururun's area. Gururun, looking sleepy and content, was lounging in the shelter that had been built for her.

She had clearly already eaten.

The horse, too, was munching happily on hay.

By this point, it wasn't confusion anymore — it was a chill running down her spine.

It wasn't even noon yet. Glancing at the kitchen from outside, she noticed steam rising from the window.

She rushed into the kitchen, and the soup was already done.

"Is something the matter, Elvena-sama?"

"N-no……"

Depending on how one looked, Bery might appear even younger than Elvena.

Silky red hair. The top of her head sat just slightly lower than Elvena's, and her large eyes were those of a young girl.

She looked youthful yet possessed full breasts and a slender waist — combined with her overall presence, she seemed like a composed, graceful lady, a truly enigmatic woman.

Wrapped in her apron dress, the way she tilted her head carried a mature allure, and at the same time something charming.

— But one must not be deceived by appearances.

For all that she looked this way, she was a superhuman on par with Krische.

Since coming here, Elvena had mostly been assisting Krische. Housework was something done on the side; it was only after Bery had been stabbed that Elvena had begun working on it in earnest.

Tending to the queen and maintaining the estate — she and Anne had divided the work between them to fill in for Bery, but the sense that something was off probably began there.

This estate was simply too large.

When she thought about it, the estate had been in near-perfect condition every time Elvena came back. No different from when she had been at Roland's estate, which employed a large staff.

What's more, Bery also served dutifully as the queen's attendant, so she couldn't possibly have spent all her days on estate management alone.

She, too, was doing it on the side.

On top of that, she had been entrusted with Christand's account books, and casually helped Krische and Selene with their paperwork as well.

Given that she could produce any figure without a moment's pause, that head beneath the red hair must have recorded nearly everything about Christand.

Krische's claim that Bery had managed the Gurgain estate entirely on her own — the story Elvena had found hard to believe.

Once it turned out to be no exaggeration, she should have understood then.

She was the servant among servants — Bery Argan.

For the current Elvena, she was an opponent whose shadow she couldn't even step on.

"……U-um, is there something after all?"

Bery's cheeks colored faintly, and she looked around at herself with some bewilderment at the intensity of Elvena's gaze.

Elvena came to her senses and shook her head.

"Ah, Bery-sama. You have hay in your hair……"

"Hm? Oh, so I do. How embarrassing……"

She plucked the straw out and pressed a hand to her cheek, looking sheepish.

It was charming, but Elvena couldn't afford to be charmed.

So it was Bery who had fed the horse and Gururun after all. The slim possibility that Krische or Selene had fed them before leaving was now gone.

"Seeing your work up close like this…… it really drives home how far I still have to go."

"Um…… that's not true at all. Elvena-sama, you're more than——"

"No, no. ……Compared to the volume of your work, mine doesn't amount to even a third…… no, a fourth, a fifth. It's made me realize how much I've been taking for granted."

Elvena sighed with an expression that said she was truly ashamed of herself.

Bery gave a troubled smile and approached, gently stroking her head.

"I know this place inside and out, and I've simply learned where to cut corners. The cleaning is only the bare minimum, and the effort I put into each task is quite different from yours. You push yourself a little too hard, Elvena-sama — perhaps you should ease up a bit?"

Her gaze was directed slightly downward. Yet the smile on her lips was soft and refined, overflowing with a feminine appeal.

Being patted on the head didn't feel unpleasant; it only brought a sense of comfort.

She was simply a beautiful person, through and through.

"Do what's needed where it's needed. Handle things that take time in parallel…… the principle is simple. If you relax, get a bit more accustomed to the work, and learn to see the whole picture in terms of flow, I'm sure you'll pick it up quickly, Elvena-sama."

"……Yes. Thank you. Um…… would it be all right if, starting tomorrow, I shadow you for a while?"

"Mm…… yes. Let's try going through things together for a little while, shall we?"

Bery said this and stepped away, ladling soup into a small dish.

"Would you be willing to taste-test this for me?"

"Of course."

Elvena smiled and nodded, accepting the dish.

What tickled her nose was the rich aroma of vegetables melted into the broth. The ingredients were soft and tender, and the moment it touched her lips, a deep savoriness delighted her tongue.

How did she manage this while doing everything else?

It was an artful dish, so painstakingly crafted it made one wonder.

"……The ingredients have melted right in. Today's is wonderful too."

"I see."

*If it's delicious, then I'm glad* — she smiled with what looked like relief.

Elvena gave a wry smile. Could she really still feel uncertain, making food this good?

It was more delicious than anything Elvena had ever tasted at Roland's estate.

Every vegetable, every cut of meat — whether it was the care taken in preparation, the exquisite balance of seasoning, or the combination of both. Even a simple grilled dish became a work of art.

For a soup of such depth, if one asked what exactly made it so delicious, Elvena's palate could hardly explain.

Yet for all that skill, she wasn't satisfied.

Perhaps she was perceiving things on a level Elvena couldn't fathom.

"……You started this in the morning, didn't you?"

"Yes, though I only did the prep and left it on the heat. I soaked the laundry in soapy water first, and then from there."

Elvena finally understood where the gap between herself and Bery lay.

Not one task at a time, in sequence — but many, in parallel.

Bery was looking down from above at the whole picture, processing work as a single surface.

Their fundamental way of seeing things was different.

Just as she would finish prep work before the water boiled, she applied the same thinking not just to cooking but to everything.

"See, when you hear it explained, it's really quite trivial, isn't it? Once you're used to it, it's not terribly difficult. You organize your plan for how far to get today, build your schedule accordingly, and it's just a repetition of small things — like cleaning while the laundry is drying."

"……I see."

The words were not as simple as they sounded from her lips, but when she said them, they somehow rang as if they truly were the simplest thing in the world.

"So please don't overdo it. Think of it like a little game of building blocks — nice and easy."

"……Right now, you look like a fairy-tale sorceress to me, Bery-sama."

"Oh, you say such things. Fufu, but I'm sure you'll soon realize the sorceress was just a conjurer after all. I still have a long, long way to go."

Looking a touch embarrassed, she laughed teasingly, as though gently scolding.

In this way, she was somehow quite like Krische.

According to them both: *Of course anyone should be able to do this much.*

They both set the bar far too high for themselves. Blind to how others perceived them, their eyes forever fixed on the summit — hopelessly oblivious to the praise of others.

Whenever they received a compliment, the two of them always smiled that same troubled, uncertain smile.

Perhaps it was precisely that quality in each other that drew them together.

They had every right to take pride in their abilities, yet remained endlessly modest.

Krische praised Bery, and Bery praised Krische — and surely, for as long as they lived, neither would ever be satisfied with herself.

Their way of being was that of seekers, and come to think of it, Selene and Kreschenta were much the same.

Christand, it seemed, was a gathering of such seekers.

Understanding at last what the wall she had vaguely sensed actually was, Elvena sighed.

When the wall itself kept retreating, what could one do?

Half resigned, yet she smiled with determination.

"Then first, I'll start by becoming a conjurer. I look forward to your continued guidance, Bery-sama."

"U-um…… please, relax……"

"Yes. I must do my best to reach that stage as quickly as possible."

She might never surpass her, but she could follow in her wake.

Surely that much was possible, and if so, effort was the only way.

Before the flustered Bery, Elvena was nothing if not earnest.

* * *

Everyone returned for the midday meal.

Soup and bread, a light lunch — then the cleanup.

For now, another ordinary day. The cooking seemed fine, too.

Krische seemed a little less energetic than usual, as though feeling unwell, which was a little worrying, but Elvena couldn't stop her if she said she was going to work. She had mentioned she would wrap up early — perhaps it would be best to use less meat tonight and make something easy to eat and nutritious.

From the afternoon, Elvena and she tackled the household work. Bery sat in her room doing the account books, thinking about such things.

"Would you like a refill?"

"Thank you. Um…… you're welcome to take a break, you know?"

"No, I'd like to observe your work today, Bery-sama."

Elvena sat beside her with perfect posture. Her well-defined features were sharp and attentive, her hair neatly trimmed at the shoulders and gleaming.

Not a single opening — the very picture of a servant.

Bery gave a wry smile and scratched her cheek.

Anne had been a diligent worker too, and Elvena was the same. Lately she had been even more earnest, to the point where Bery felt almost apologetic.

It was simply her nature, but she was pushing herself too hard, and it was hard to know what to do. Enthusiasm in itself was a wonderful thing. But Bery worried she might wear herself out, and being gazed at with such sparkling, admiring eyes was rather ticklish.

She sipped the tea Elvena had poured and let out a breath.

Even if she couldn't taste it, the warmth and aroma offered some small comfort.

"Elvena-sama, please, have a seat."

"Um…… yes."

She stood, fetched a teacup for Elvena, and placed it on the desk.

Then she poured tea, had her sit, and closed the ledger.

She had thought a little idle chat might help distract her — but that was precisely Bery's greatest weakness.

"H-have you gotten used to life here?"

"Yes. Everything was so new at first…… I was a little overwhelmed, but I've grown quite accustomed to life itself."

"I see. That's wonderful……"

The conversation trailed off. Her gaze wavered.

Work talk or cooking talk, or perhaps the sort of pleasantries that amounted to etiquette — those were fine. But casual conversation, when she really thought about it, was difficult.

A conversation with no business or purpose — what was she supposed to talk about?

Perhaps the weather would be safe.

*— It's nice that it cleared up today.*

*— Yes, lovely weather.*

No, that wouldn't do. The conversation would end right there.

Then again, Elvena's past was not a cheerful one. She could hardly ask about old times — that would be off-limits.

Today's work was finished. Then perhaps tomorrow's work — no, that would only make Elvena tense up more.

Elvena was staring at Bery's face, looking puzzled.

Seeing her expression, something occurred to Bery, and she nodded.

"W-would you, um…… please pass on my thanks to Kalua-sama, when you get the chance?"

"To nee-san……?"

"Yes, for the get-well gift she sent the other day……"

About two weeks ago, on her way to escort Krische to the estate, Kalua had picked out some fruit and given it to Bery as a get-well gift.

If she started from there, perhaps the conversation might go somewhere — so Bery reasoned.

But Elvena stared steadily at Bery, thought for a moment, and smiled.

"Understood. I'll pass it along."

"Y-yes…… thank you……"

Contrary to Bery's plan, silence fell again.

Elvena sipped her tea, watching Bery all the while. Bery's eyes darted left and right, searching for something.

For Bery, it was an uncomfortable atmosphere.

But it lasted only a moment.

What broke the air was a soft laugh spilling from Elvena.

"Fufu, I think this might be rude of me to say, but……"

"U-um……?"

"Bery-sama, you're a very endearing person. ……Forgive me. That was a little mean of me."

Elvena laughed merrily, saw Bery's blank expression, and wiped the tears from the corners of her eyes.

"You don't need to force yourself to be considerate. I appreciate the thought very much, though."

At last realizing she had been teased, Bery's cheeks flushed red.

Which only delighted Elvena more.

Elvena gazed at Bery with a smile. Bery looked down, embarrassed, and spoke.

"I-I'm sorry…… this sort of conversation is a bit of a weak point for me."

"No. If anything, it puts me at ease…… so Bery-sama has things she's not good at, too."

"That's just you giving me too much credit, Elvena-sama. I'm only good at keeping up appearances — really, I have more shortcomings than strengths."

"Fufu, if that's merely keeping up appearances, then what am I to do, unable to achieve even that?"

"W-well……"

When Bery faltered, Elvena's smile deepened and she let out a laugh.

Once more, she said, "Forgive me," with a wry smile.

"But truly, in that way Bery-sama is very much like Krische-sama. You're both so excessively modest, I'd say……"

"Krische-sama aside, I'm just……"

"I think if I asked Krische-sama the same thing, she'd say exactly what you just did."

"Uu……"

The look of embarrassment on Bery's face was, for all the world, perfectly age-appropriate — the look of a maiden.

In contrast to her usual composure, Elvena observed that expression with cool detachment, refining her eye.

Mistress and servant alike — the two of them were irresistibly tempting to tease.

"Forgive me. Watching the two of you…… I feel a certain envy."

"Envy?"

"Yes. You seem so very happy."

At those words, Bery's cheeks reddened further.

There was no hiding how she and Krische spent their days together. Elvena had witnessed it on a daily basis, and a certain numbness had set in — but having it stated aloud brought with it an indescribable embarrassment.

"This isn't teasing, I mean it sincerely. It's not about romantic feelings, per se — it's more that such a relationship itself seems like a beautiful thing, if that makes sense……"

Elvena herself looked embarrassed as she said it, smiling wryly.

"I get a little embarrassed myself saying it, but a relationship where two people fulfill each other the way you do…… it's something to envy, I suppose, how to put it…… It's just that I find it so wonderfully fated — that in this wide, wide world, you could meet someone who fits so perfectly."

*I wouldn't make much of a bard, would I* — Elvena said, looking bashful.

Given the flow of conversation, it was a ticklish thing to say aloud. But Bery thought it over, then looked up with a trace of anxiety.

"……Does it really look that way?"

"Wh-what…… are you not confident?"

The unexpected words caught Elvena off guard.

Bery's gaze wavered, and she spoke.

"No, I…… of course, I love Krische-sama. More than anyone."

It was a declaration.

Given all the daily life Elvena had observed, there was no room to doubt it — but hearing it stated so plainly carried an impact all its own.

Despite having brought up the topic herself, Elvena's face went red, her eyes swam, and she recalled the time she had once asked Krische about Bery.

"Krische-sama too, loves someone like me…… I don't doubt that feeling in the slightest. I have sworn to devote my life to Krische-sama."

"Y-yes……"

What an utterly alike pair of mistress and servant.

"But the real me…… is a terribly pathetic person, and I wonder whether someone like me really deserves to be the most important…… whether someday, she might become disillusioned with me. Just a little…… I'm afraid."

Elvena had heard similar words from someone else before.

Frighteningly pure.

That was perhaps why they resembled each other so deeply.

"W-well, you see, Bery-sama——"

"……?"

"I was just going to say——"

Elvena was cut off by the sound of a knock.

As Elvena started to rise, Bery stopped her with a gesture, stood, and opened the door.

"Welcome home, Krische-sama."

"I'm home, Bery. Elvena too…… a break?"

"Yes. Just having a little chat."

Krische pressed her face against Bery, clinging to her, and Bery held her close.

Elvena thought, with a touch of exasperation, that there was nothing for Bery to be anxious about — and watched the two of them as she fetched a new cup and poured tea.

Then she noticed the bag Krische was carrying and tilted her head.

"Krische-sama, what's in the bag?"

"Um…… well, they're cookies Krische made as a snack over there. Krische made them for Neigal and the others, and there were some left over…… so, for you two."

"Fufu, thank you. Shall we have some together, Elvena-sama?"

Bery smiled and moved to let her go, about to take her hand.

But before she could, Krische drew a bite-sized cookie from the bag and, without looking up, still pressed close, held it up to Bery's lips.

"……Bery."

Bery looked bewildered at this, but——

"U-um, yes……"

Glancing sideways at Elvena with some embarrassment, she gave in and opened her mouth.

She took the cookie, chewed, and swallowed.

"Today's are very crunchy cookies."

"……Are they good?"

"……? Yes, very——"

The instant Bery said that, Krische's body went rigid and the bag slipped from her hand.

The sound of cookies crumbling inside the bag rang out.

"……Krische-sama?"

Bery, too, froze at the sight of her.

Krische stared at Bery, backing away, as if frightened.

Her beautiful face contorted as though in anguish; her violet eyes glistened and wavered like the surface of water.

She shook her head again and again, staggering.

She simply stared at Bery.

"Krische-sama!"

— And then, just like that, she turned her back and disappeared down the corridor.

Bery moved to chase after her on reflex,

"……ah."

but stopped almost immediately, sinking to her knees and covering her face.

Her slender shoulders trembled and shook.

"B-Bery-sama……?"

Unable to understand what had just happened, Elvena approached her hesitantly. Bery did not respond to her voice.

Then Elvena's gaze fell on the bag of cookies on the floor. She picked it up.

She drew out one of the broken cookies, looked at it, and put it in her mouth.

"……!"

She nearly choked, and pressed a hand over her mouth.

The honey sweetness she had expected was barely there at all.

It was simply a cookie that was nothing but salty.

In that instant, she understood everything.

And all she could do was stare, stunned, at Bery's bowed figure.

# Chapter 162: The Celestial Maiden in Her Palm

"……You fool. Where exactly was any of that 'nothing serious at all'?"

"I'm sorry……"

Some time later, as the sun went down.

Selene returned to the estate with Kreschenta and Anne in tow.

She promptly heard the story from Elvena, then listened directly from Bery — sitting in a chair with her head bowed — and let out a deep sigh.

Anne, unable to bear what she had heard, left the room to search for Krische.

Kreschenta creased her brow, had Elvena pour her tea loaded with honey, and watched the two of them with an exasperated sigh, chin propped on one hand.

"Haa, Argan-sama, in a way you're an even bigger nuisance than ojii-sama. Right now, the one putting the kingdom at greatest risk isn't the three nations — it's you. Do you understand that?"

"……I'm sorry."

That was all Bery said in reply.

She did not raise her face. Her shoulders trembled quietly.

Seeing that, Kreschenta's eyes shifted uneasily.

"You shouldn't be apologizing, that's not what I'm trying to say——"

"Kreschenta, be quiet for a while or go to another room."

"……A-are you ordering me?"

"If you don't, I'll be genuinely angry."

Glared at sideways by Selene, Kreschenta looked displeased but fell silent.

She took a big gulp of tea, found it too hot, and set the cup down somewhat roughly.

*Honestly——* Selene looked away from Kreschenta and turned her gaze to Bery.

Sitting in the chair with her face lowered.

Her small frame looked even smaller, her thin shoulders even more delicate.

None of her usual composure was anywhere to be found.

But this was who Bery really was.

A small woman who was always forcing herself, merely wearing the face of a guardian.

Selene circled behind her and wrapped her arms around her from behind.

The body that flinched at her touch — she extended her arms to enfold it, pressing her cheek to Bery's.

Golden hair slipped and fell, sliding down Bery's neck.

Watching it, Selene spoke quietly.

"Why are you always like this? I told you to lean on me once in a while, to let me go to some trouble. Am I still that unreliable?"

It had been the same when her mother died.

Selene had cried endlessly, and Bery was at her side the whole time, comforting her, carrying on as usual, arranging the funeral.

The consummate servant, always.

Even though Bery — who had spent the most time with her mother — must have been the saddest of all.

When Bery was with her mother, she would be just a little indulgent. Look just a little younger.

Long ago, she must have left all of that behind.

*'If you keep being stubborn and putting on airs like that, you'll end up like Bery — a girl who can't be honest and let herself be spoiled. Children should act like children and lean on their mother, honestly.'*

*'Nee-sama, I…… have no intention of interfering with the young lady's upbringing, but would you please stop using me as a bad example? It makes things very difficult……'*

*'Well, it's the truth, isn't it? Fufu, you're welcome to prove me wrong, you know?'*

*'……Absolutely not.'*

Remembering the two of them, Selene narrowed her eyes.

"I can't know how painful it is for someone who loves cooking as much as you do to lose her sense of taste. ……But I know the reason you hid it wasn't anything like that."

If it were simply painful, she probably would have spoken about it without fuss.

As if to say it wasn't painful at all — *I'm fine,* like that.

Smiling that troubled smile, same as always.

That was the kind of person she was.

The reason she hadn't done that must have been something else.

"You were afraid Krische would be disappointed in you, weren't you?"

At those words, Bery's shoulders jumped.

Selene sighed, as though exasperated.

"I can see right through you, Bery. Did you know? I've been watching you since the day I was born."

If the person she respected most was her mother, the person she admired most was surely Bery.

Talented enough to do anything, hardworking, and yet never arrogant, never presumptuous. She always handled everything effortlessly, as a matter of course.

She was the same as Krische.

Never once had Selene beaten her at anything, and problems that made Selene rack her brain, Bery dispatched without a thought.

Unlike her mother, who had her occasional lapses, Bery was the consummate superhuman.

Ask and she would answer anything; wish and she would make it come true.

She was a different breed of being. She must never worry about anything. And even if she did, it would be on a plane Selene could never understand — there was a time she had believed all of that, and it was only after Krische arrived that she learned otherwise.

After Krische came, it was as if things kept peeling away from Bery, one after another.

*'If you would…… continue to stay with me like this, always, from now on.'*

The desperately earnest tone of those words, heard on the road to the capital, was something she remembered well.

She appeared to have everything, yet at the same time appeared to have nothing.

*'— At Krische-sama's side. I realized my meaning lies there. And if so, then my name belongs to Krische-sama, I thought. ……That it exists for that purpose — that is what I wished.'*

In certain respects, she and Krische were very much alike.

Tormented by something for which there was no answer, as though drowning in it.

That was surely because they both possessed the kind of power that could obtain anything, if only they reached out their hand.

"If you ask me, you're two of a kind, Bery. You both go around thinking you can solve everything on your own, that there's nothing you can't do — and that's exactly why some trivial flaw becomes unforgivable, you fools."

"That's not——"

"Yes it is. A matched pair of hopeless egotists, the both of you."

Without even a small stumble, without ever knowing their own limits.

And precisely because of that, every tiny crack in themselves loomed large, unforgivable, frightening — cowards, both of them.

They should have been the most dexterous of anyone, yet they were the most clumsy.

"Does losing your sense of taste erase your worth? Does it make Krische disappointed in you, make her stop loving you — is that what you think?"

"That's——"

"You really are a fool. If that's your argument, then what does that make me — someone who can't cook, can't do housework, and even when I try my hardest at my own specialties, Krische calls me 'a so-so klutz'? What am I, exactly?"

Selene unleashed a torrent of words, then glared at Kreschenta.

"Don't you dare say anything unnecessary."

"I-I wasn't going to…… unlike you, onee-sama, I can read the room."

Kreschenta, who had been about to interject, hastily looked away.

Selene sighed in irritation and squeezed Bery tight.

"You're always so quick to lecture everyone else, and yet when it comes to yourself, how can you be so faint-hearted? You're the one who says Krische's flaws don't bother you, Kreschenta's flaws don't bother you, even my flaws don't bother you — yet your own flaws are the only ones you can't accept? No one else will ever acknowledge them, ever forgive them — is that it?"

"……!"

Kreschenta looked displeased, as though to say *I certainly don't have any flaws* — but Selene's glare silenced her.

Bery kept her face down and listened to Selene's words in silence.

"Are all those things you always say just lies?"

"They're not lies——"

"Then trust us a little more. I think your cooking is wonderful, but your worth to me isn't just that. No matter how many or how few flaws you have, you're my precious family, the person I look up to like an older sister…… that's what I believe. Can't you believe it?"

Bery shook her head faintly and gripped Selene's arm.

"Krische feels the same way, you fool. Do you really think she was disappointed in you and went off somewhere? When you were stabbed, she risked her life and tore herself apart to save you — she even challenged a dragon, that's how much you mean to her — and you think something like this would make her lose faith? ……If you honestly believe that, then — even with you — I really will slap your face, Bery."

Bery's hand clung to Selene's arm like that of a small child.

*Honestly——* Selene smiled, closed her eyes, and pressed her cheek close.

"Once you've calmed down a bit, let's go look for her together. If you say you won't go, I'll go instead and gently comfort Krische myself — I'll say all sorts of things about you, true and otherwise, and steal her away. Is that what you want?"

"……That's not fair."

"That's what you get for not being honest. Do you realize I'm a little angry here?"

Selene said this, released her arms, and moved in front of Bery.

She crouched down, and gently wiped the tears that had traced down Bery's lowered cheek.

"Is it so humiliating to be comforted by your unreliable ojou-sama?"

"……It might be."

"Oh, come on……"

"But, just a little…… ojou-sama, you remind me of nee-sama."

Selene pressed her forehead to Bery's with a satisfied look, peering into her tear-dampened light-brown eyes.

"Hmpf. And whose daughter do you think I am?"

"Nee-sama's and the Master's…… fufu, you really are just like nee-sama in so many ways. Like being terribly clumsy……"

Bery smiled and pulled back, wiping her eyes.

"And the way you're somehow always so bossy despite that — that's exactly like her too."

"The one who's always bossy toward her mistress is you, Bery. ……What a sharp-tongued servant."

Her rosy lips formed a soft, girlish smile.

"Well, it's not as though I'm being rude. ……All the ways you're alike — they're the things I loved most about nee-sama."

Her red hair swayed softly.

And she kissed Selene's forehead.
