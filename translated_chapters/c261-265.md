## Alignment Key

**Order** — Values rules and discipline, universal principles, and organized society and law.
**Chaos** — Values the individual and their emotions and thoughts, prioritizing will over social discipline and morality.
**Neutral** — The middle ground between the two.
**Good** — Altruistic disposition. Places the moral code one believes in above personal gain, willing to sacrifice for it.
**Evil** — Selfish disposition. Places personal gain first and does not hesitate to step on others to obtain it.
**Neutral** — A balanced temperament that does not swing toward either extreme.

## How to Read Kingdom Noble Names

Example: 1. Gildanstein = 2. Kalnaros = 3. Vel = 4. Sarcarinea = 5. Alberan

1. Given name: Gildanstein
2. The largest domain among administered territories: Kalnaros. Properly this encompasses all administered territories, making the name extremely long; it is typically abbreviated.
3. Male royals with succession rights use Vel; female royals use Vera.
4. Honorary title: Sarcarinea
5. Surname: Alberan

Formally, ranks such as Marshal or General, and court positions such as Prime Minister, are inserted between 4 and 5, and titles like Grand Duke may follow the surname. However, as these names are already an eyeful in transliteration, the text prioritizes readability by appending whatever rank or position is relevant at the end in the original.

As a general rule, only the surname is necessarily inherited by children, but by custom it is common for the legitimate heir to inherit the domains and titles as well, provided no complications arise. The sole exception is the Honorary Title (4), which represents martial distinction and cannot be inherited.

When a person without a surname receives the Nea = Quasi-Knight abbreviated investiture, they typically take the name of their place of origin.
(Example) Gallen Nea Karka.

## ■ — The Mansion in the Phantasm World

### ○ Krische Alberinea Christand [Order / Evil]

*God-flavored child-type girl. Christand household ward. The protagonist. Alberinea. The mansion's bewitching presence.*

Called a "cursed child who does not cry," she was an accursed daughter of the royal house, her existence expunged. Fated to be killed, she was spirited away by her attendant Nora, and grew up in Karka Village.

A being incapable of remorse — a person lacking empathy, to whom killing and violence provoke no pangs of conscience — and yet, having received deep love from good people, she has gradually come to understand ordinary human ethics and emotions.

As a rule she can only take words at their literal meaning and is poor at reading feelings. Setting that aside, she is a genuine genius with superhuman talent in every domain, capable of absorbing and developing any skill at a glance. In everything she does she proceeds from the baseline assumption that *of course she can do it*, and her attachment to her own excellence is strong; she is a hard worker.

The same holds in her relationships. She generally acts on profit-and-loss calculations — but because she struggles to read others' emotions, she tends to overvalue what others give her and undervalue what she gives in return. For that reason, when faced with pure goodwill directed at her, she more often than not demands of herself an excessive "repayment," and by nature she spares no effort in providing it. This stems from her discomfort with relationships, yet that quality is itself genuinely admirable — and whether one sees her as a simple abnormal person or as a girl who is merely too pure greatly affects how one evaluates her.

As an individual she is an overwhelmingly invincible presence, yet she is dependent by nature and mentally young and fragile. She is the kind of person who grows mentally weaker the deeper her bonds with others become and the more human happiness she obtains; compared to when she was mentally self-sufficient and placed no value on others, she has been gradually moving away from the "perfect being" she desired to be.

Yet her attachment to being perfect is fading. She desires to be a person who can feel many things as an ordinary human being — rather than a perfection bought by cutting away everything — and she holds a small sense that she has grown up somewhat emotionally. She is mostly still a child, however.

She has inscribed on her own tongue the same magical cipher she inscribed on Bery's tongue.

Prompted by the dragon's-blood illness that came over Bery, she came to understand almost the entirety of the world's structure, found the soul and its origin, and thereby plumbed the deepest depths; together with Kreschenta she created the Zenith — a mana well that fills the planet itself with magical power. Using the world-transforming power of that immense mana she gave rise to the Phantasm World, overlapping with the real world — *Fuyofuyofuwan* (note: Krische's name for it) — and decided to take up residence there.

At the First Christand Council held in the new world, this name was formally decided to be **Krisneit** (from the ancient tongue, meaning "dwelling of the moon").

**☆ After Moving In**

She has obtained power literally equal to a god's, yet she still does housework in her apron dress, unchanged.

Selene has built cottages and vacation homes for them across the world; the work grows year by year, but there are no particular problems. Krische draws up a schedule and enjoys the housework.

Because she produces divine-quality items so effortlessly that she disrupts markets, she is forbidden from making things for money. She mostly helps with Selene's crafts, Bery's garden and vegetable patch, and the winemaking.

Hunting down rampaging magical beasts is also fundamentally her job.

Because the stockpile of meat keeps growing faster than they can use it (on account of number and size), she and Bery are researching and establishing various cooking methods for magical beast meat. It is generally tough, apparently, but rich in umami.

She periodically goes out of control, and when she enters her "'Spoiled Krische' mode" she becomes impossible to manage.

**△ In History**

In historical record she is the greatest hero in history, the one who led Alberan to continental unification; in the history of magic she is called, alongside Kreschenta, a Primal Spellcaster — an ancestor of all mages.

She took the field for the first time at age fourteen, riding the battlefield as the blade of Alberan's queen. The count of commanders' heads she personally took — large and small combined, without exaggeration — is said to number over a thousand.

She is also known as a genius tactician and inventor, but in many accounts written by friend and foe alike, what comes first is the ferocity of her personal combat ability; that she was an absolute presence in her era — destroying walls and catapults with thrown javelins, and the like — admits no doubt.

Her swordsmanship was especially peerless: wielding a small curved sword and slitting the throats of her enemies with ease, she was feared as the "Headhunter," and many commanders fell before her blade.

From the many cold-blooded battlefield anecdotes surrounding her, most books record that she was a pleasure killer with a mental deficiency — yet surviving journals of those close to her consistently describe her as a gentle person, and assessments remain divided.

In the history of magic, much writing and surviving mana crystals speak of her as the being who first created 魔法 (*maho*) — the arcane formulae she inscribed in the open air — and to this day her title, Alberinea, is used as the highest-grade designation for mages. Her artistically-inscribed formulae have yet to be perfectly reproduced; the cipher formulae inscribed on advanced mana crystals such as Jaleia Gashea's main core remain undeciphered. Given that advanced mana crystals of this kind were mass-produced, the prevailing theory is that there must have existed a formula for inscribing mana crystals automatically.

As for her end: it is said variously that, through the "Great Rite of Heavenly Change" wielded by the Zenith — the World Tree — she and Alberan's queen Kreschenta were sublimated into godhood, or alternatively that they failed and died. Traditions of people meeting them or being saved by them remain scattered across the world.

**★ Battle Command**

Both in personal combat and in tactics, extremely aggressive.

She is a commander of the hunter type: she prizes above all else the mobility needed to deliver fatal damage to the enemy, constantly uses movement to confuse and trap opponents, and never fights on ground that is unfavorable to her. She favors "decapitation tactics" — using herself, the strongest piece, to maximum effect, eliminating enemy commanders with priority and without quarter — and against enemies who come at her head-on she commands overwhelming strength.

Her standing practice is to demand only the minimum from subordinates and to expect nothing more, then to structure her tactics so she can win even on that basis — and yet she cannot be satisfied unless she always pursues the maximum battle result, which is her bad habit.

Ordinarily the Black Flag Special Service compensates for this, but the excessive burden on herself often causes her to fall ill, and the trouble is that she always pretends to be fine.

She is the kind of commander who makes trouble for everyone around her if they don't help prop her up; and the single flaw of this woman who can be called the strongest is a very elementary one — a childish inability to even properly manage her own health, because she is too stubborn.

That said, this is all true only when she was a mere human. When magic is employed, no being that can oppose her exists save Kreschenta.

**Appearance:** Long silver hair. Violet eyes. Very short. Slender. Modest chest. Fairy-like. Magical cipher on her tongue.
**Armor:** Everyday clothes plus gauntlets and reinforced boots. Garter belt.

→ *Self-assessment:* Very clever and accomplished, though still immature in many ways. A dunce. Bery's. A little grown up.
**Strong at:** Magic. Calculation.
**Likes:** Housework in general, cooking included. Sweet things. Being spoiled. Kisses, hugs, physical affection in general. Taking care of others. Watching Bery. Matching outfits. A nightcap. Winemaking.
**Dislikes:** Anything that disturbs the peace around her. War. Death of loved ones.
**Worry:** Being too happy.

### ○ Selene Argalitte Rinea Christand [Neutral / Good]

*Won't-tolerate-public-disorder-type girl. Head of the Christand household. The mansion's conscience.*

The only child of the Christand martial family.

She had a complex about being female, and was a hard-working girl striving to follow in the footsteps of her heroic father whom she respected. Until a few years ago she cursed the fact of her own womanhood rather than her gender, and before meeting Krische — a person of overwhelming talent — she imposed on herself an excess of effort that worried those around her.

While looking after Krische, who was talented but childlike and domestic in her ways, Selene's perspective broadened: she had previously been unable to see herself as anything other than a general's daughter. She came to cherish the small and everyday.

She is talented and diligent, and thus very capable, but she is fundamentally a worrier and prone to overextending herself.

Her father the hero Bogan, his subordinates, Bery, and Krische — raised among capable people, she tends to underestimate herself and is the serious, over-responsible type who often drives herself too hard.

She is the workaholic sort who, if left with nothing to do, would immediately throw herself into studying or administrative work.

After becoming Marshal she was perpetually "busy," but she overflowed with energy, as she was also able to fulfill her father's hopes by establishing things like the military academy.

She is deeply fond of Krische as a younger sister, but she also strongly senses a precariousness in her abnormality. She considers the very fact that she makes Krische fight to be a sign of her own inadequacy, and her goal is for Krische to leave the battlefield and live in peace.

She dislikes herself for relying on and making use of Krische, but the heavy responsibilities and circumstances she faces have not allowed that, and it has been a source of anguish.

She holds deep respect for Bery and simultaneously a sense of rivalry and jealousy toward her abilities and conduct — her dream being to make her cry uncle one day.

Prompted by the dragon's-blood illness that came over Bery, she was forced to confront her complex about the woman head-on, and for a time considered separating herself from them out of disappointment in herself. But realizing that Krische loves not Selene's abilities but Selene herself — her very awkwardness — she took time to accept this and resolved to walk on together.

**☆ After Moving In**

The young god's eternal paradise — and the Enforcement Officer of its Public Morals Regulation Committee (in practice, the only member).

She is the mansion's reason, working daily to police morals that would, if left unchecked, unravel without limit. She has established numerous mansion rules under the Committee, including mutual monitoring, and promotes measured interaction — but since she is the only person doing the policing, and keeps getting drawn in herself, formalism and enforcement alternate in endless cycles.

She is an extreme workaholic who would die if left idle, so she often spends her abundant time making things — forging swords, throwing pottery, and the like. She is one source of the mansion's income, and occasionally a masterwork sword or artwork of unknown origin and provenance finds its way onto the market.

Recently she has taken to digging for good iron ore or searching for clay at the starting point, and their travels generally consist either of accompanying Selene on such expeditions or of someone Bery brings up.

She has also become absorbed in house-building, and has set up various cottages and vacation homes in different places. The splendid cottage she built near Rigaleiwe's sleeping quarters has been destroyed eight times by visiting dragons' wingbeats.

**△ In History**

Widely known, even among the general public, as Alberinea's elder sister; any military professional would know her as "the mother of the military" — there is no one who does not.

She is a soldier who fully actualized and spread the staff system concept left behind by her father, the hero Bogan Christand, and was deeply involved in establishing the military academy whose purpose was to raise and standardize the capabilities of commanders — at a time when piecemeal individual education and self-study were the norm. For this she is often called the "genius military administrator," set as the counterpart to the genius military strategist Alberinea.

To this day the crest of her Christand family — "lightning and hawk" — is often inscribed at the front of most military treatises, and the command structure incorporating the staff system is known as the "Christand system."

She rose to Marshal young, and set down in writing the strategies, tactics, and commentary on Alberinea's strategies and tactics of her day; copies and reproductions of these remain as many officers' reference texts.

Some originals have sections that appear to have been added by Alberinea — colloquial corrections and margin notes made in response to the commentary — hinting at the great intimacy of their relationship.

Despite rising to the position of Marshal, de facto the highest in the Alberan Kingdom, she never took a husband nor had children, and the prevailing view from many accounts is that she and Alberinea were more than sisters.

**★ Battle Command**

Originally an aggressive temperament, but she constantly questions her own ability and does not overextend herself.

She places great importance on drawing out the full ability of her capable subordinates, and constructs tactics from the opposite perspective to Krische's.

She favors fundamental tactics — pressing with the quality and numbers of soldiers — and coordination over stratagems, and places emphasis on securing that advantage at the strategic stage.

Self-underestimation is, from the perspective of her current position, in some ways a virtue: her straightforward assessment of others' abilities and assignment of roles carries no political color, and she is valued purely as the head of the kingdom's military.

Because she came to stand at the position of Marshal without building up experience, she did not in the end spread her wings as a tactician — but she committed no strategic errors, and as a capable Marshal she guided the kingdom to long stability.

She continued to swing her sword even after moving, quietly pursuing mastery through accumulated effort.

**Appearance:** Long golden hair. Blue eyes. Slender. Average chest. Beautiful.
**Armor:** Silver-wind silver-wing plate mail.

→ *Regarding Krische:* An impossible little fool. But I love her dearly.
**Strong at:** Sword forging. Woodworking. Pottery.
**Likes:** Swordsmanship. Reading. Making things. Adoring Krische and the others.
**Dislikes:** War. Rule violations. Dragon wingbeats.
**Worry:** The mansion's public morals.

### ○ Bery (Lips) Argan [Chaos / Good]

*Can't-win-the-fight-against-herself type girl. Christand household servant. The mansion's witch.*

Daughter of the Argan family, a minor noble house that went bankrupt. Selene's aunt.

Originally sickly, partly because of this her self-assessment was extremely low and her disposition was introverted. Distressed at being a burden to others because of her body, she used to deliberately act standoffish in childhood so people would not worry about her — but this caused the servants to dislike her, which further wounded her spirit, and at some point she came to wish for her own death while still living.

When the family fell and she came to the Christands, even after her bodily problem was resolved, her self-loathing only grew.

Her beloved elder sister, having entrusted her with looking after the too-serious Selene, called on her to be cheerful in her sister's place — but that did not go smoothly either, and she cursed herself for being unable to do anything right.

She is one of those people saved by meeting Krische and receiving her pure love and goodwill.

Though she appears calm and personable on the surface, the collapse of the Argan family left her with a degree of mistrust toward men, and combined with her introverted nature she keeps people at a certain distance in relationships.

She is gifted — a natural talent, quick to find the right way of doing things, too good for a servant's position — but given her disposition she is satisfied with her role as a servant and does not wish to step into the public eye.

At present she channels most of her talent and passion into cooking, and Bogan and Selene constantly felt it was a waste.

Stubborn beneath the gentle surface, she appears logical but is actually intuitive. When she feels something is right she tends to prioritize emotion over reason.

She holds deep love for Krische and puts her happiness first — but her possessiveness has been growing stronger.

Her self-loathing is pronounced; her love, born of an originally introverted nature, has a strong dependent character. Since her object, Krische, has the same disposition, it is beyond managing.

Lately she has shed various constraints; what Krische wishes for is what she herself wishes for. She feels this is all right, and she believes it is not a great mistake. Or perhaps in the self that lives alongside Krische she may have found what she was always looking for.

Having inscribed on her tongue the same magical cipher as herself, Krische stirs in her a certain... improper sentiment. *Matching outfits.*

She had considered that her dying of the dragon's-blood illness might not be bad for all of them — but when Krische sought her even after death, she was freed completely. *— It's Krische-sama's fault.* The excuse for binding her to eternity forever was, in truth, what she had desired from the bottom of her heart.

**☆ After Moving In**

The young god's eternal paradise — and the one whose morals are being policed.

With the Christand household's affairs now gone, Krische and Kreschenta forever swooping in to be spoiled has been inserted into her duties as a thoroughly unhealthy occupation; to evade Selene's lectures and preserve her own reason she spends her time improving the varieties of vegetables in her garden and vegetable patch, making preserves, and planning trips.

Her hobby garden has expanded year by year; the area around the mansion is planted with fruits, vegetables, and herbs from all over the world, and these and their preserves are another source of the mansion's income.

She travels in disguise as a peddler selling harvests and preserves in various places; since wine is similarly suited for sale, she pours energy into winemaking too.

But this has produced the unintended consequence of Krische and Kreschenta using "taste-testing" as an excuse to drink sake, pretending to be drunk, and indulging in being spoiled — giving rise to the terrible 'Drunk Twins': 'Drunk Krische' and 'Drunk Kreschenta.' The 87th Alcohol Prohibition Bill is currently under deliberation at the Christand Council.

**△ In History**

The world's most famous servant. Known colloquially as "Alberinea's servant."

The tale of Alberinea confronting a mighty ancient dragon single-handedly to save her beloved servant, and concluding a pact — this anecdote, with its simple and beautiful storyline, was extremely popular subject matter even in the Kleinmeil era; paintings, plays, and even songs based on it were created.

Several surviving journals touch on the kiss involved, and debate had arisen — but it moved toward resolution following one particular painting, *The Holy Being and the Maidens* (by Filipéne).

**Appearance:** Long red hair. Brown eyes. Short. Larger chest. Baby-faced, pretty. Magical cipher on her tongue.
**Armor:** Maid uniform (garter belt optional).

→ *Regarding Krische:* The most beautiful being there is. My Krische-sama.
**Strong at:** Housework in general, cooking included. Etiquette. Sophistry. Self-defense. Board games.
**Likes:** Housework in general, cooking included. Teaching. Adoring Krische and the others. Travel.
**Dislikes:** Herself, for being so weak to temptation.
**Worry:** Reason.

### ○ Kreschenta Farna Vera Alberan [Order / Evil]

*God-flavored dog-type girl. First Princess of the Alberan Kingdom. The mansion's dog.*

She was a "cursed child who does not cry" — one of the royal family's accursed — but through the quick thinking of a servant named Nora she was saved, and raised as a princess.

Like Krische, the high intellect comes at the price of missing empathy; she is a girl who feels no remorse for selfish killing. She has killed by various means those inconvenient to her grasp of power — her own biological father, younger brothers, servants — and does not hesitate to dirty her hands for self-preservation.

Having been nearly killed right after birth for being cursed, she is dominated by the obsession that she is always in danger of being targeted. She is in some ways a greater abnormal than Krische, but having been raised in the palace she has a pathologically high acting ability, and her abnormality has not come to light.

She acknowledges Krische, her true elder sister, as the only being on the same level as herself; feeling they could cooperate, she framed her uncle Gildanstein for the king's murder and took refuge with the Christands.

Through her interactions with Krische and the surrounding Bery and Selene, building relationships of trust, changes have also come over her inner world.

Having discovered the happiness attainable only by emptying her head entirely, she has become considerably gentler in some ways of late — but her essential nature and her obsession have not been lost.

Her attachment to safety and peace is even stronger than Krische's.

Her murderous impulse originates in her strong obsession with safety and her paranoia; she is not a pleasure killer.

She has been mentally settling of late.

Her growth stopped at exactly 1:1 Krische (T/N: she and Krische ended up essentially the same height and stopped growing there).

She has continued to regret having killed Bery with her own hands, and was unable to forget it. She had been terrified of Bery's dislike and dreaded their reunion — but pouring it out to Krische and achieving the reunion finally freed her from that suffering. She ceased to be human, and at the same time she at last became one.

**☆ After Moving In**

"*It was thanks to me that this world was created — I'm the most important. The master of this mansion. The god of this world*" — still self-important and boastful, unchanged.

Her usual pattern is to follow Bery around complaining about her work, then follow Selene around complaining about her crafts, trotting at their heels. She helps out on a whim.

The customary sight: Bery teases her, she gets huffy and goes to Selene; Selene makes fun of her, she gets huffy and comes back to Bery, back and forth between the two in endless rotation.

Because of her difficult nature — unable to be spoiled without an excuse — she deliberately engineers situations that allow various pretexts: "My head won't work on waking," "Holding my sweets hostage," "I'm drunk," "I'm cold," and so on, using her outstanding mind to manufacture excuses.

If those avenues are deliberately blocked, she reaches the limit of her patience (note: approximately the capacity of a small flask) and begins to sulk. Pacifying 'Attention-Seeking Kreschenta' requires a minimum of several days' worth of indulgence.

**△ In History**

She was, in practical terms, the ruler who achieved world unification (she overwhelmed the rest of the world's civilizations), and was feared as the Demon King of Alberan. In the history of magic she is called, alongside Krische, a Primal Spellcaster — an ancestor of all mages.

Said to make even the legendary Alberan queen Grabareine pale by comparison, she was a legendary ruler of uncanny foresight; she absorbed various nations without ever provoking great uprisings, and stabilizing vast territories led to records describing her as possessing god's eyes.

She commanded all surviving languages like her native tongue and was said to have memorized the financial situation of every city and village across the world; many accounts from the period praise or fear her abnormal intelligence.

Save for the Great Purge, known to few, her reign was without blemish; she prioritized above all else national prosperity and relief for the poor, and combined with her transcendent beauty this earned her overwhelming popular adoration.

Several reasons exist for why the kingdom functioned normally, without major problems, for nearly fifty years in her absence — but above all it is thought that this was down to the absolute charisma that was her hallmark.

The existence of the Great Purge buried in the dark; the behind-the-scenes activities of Alberinea after unification.

A posture of no mercy whatsoever toward political opponents; an abnormal level of ability.

The view that Alberinea and she were what are called "accursed children of Alberan" is strong, and the opinion that, like the founding king Bazarishe and Grabareine, she harbored a warped mentality is widespread.

However, regardless of all this, her achievements remain untarnished; it is fair to say that the peace spanning several hundred years in the early to mid Kleinmeil era was something she produced, and there is no room to doubt that she was an unprecedented wise ruler.

The reason she relinquished a perfected kingdom is unknown, but given the policy of popularizing schools and reforms based on recruiting commoners to government posts, along with the establishment of a parliament — policies dating back decades — it is taken as certain that this was a planned course of action, and active debate continues to this day.

**Appearance:** Strawberry-blonde long hair. Violet eyes. Very short. Slender. Modest chest. Fairy-like.

→ *Regarding Krische:* A foolish and kind elder sister. Quite the fool. No sense of shame.
**Strong at:** Lying. Acting. Calculation. Making excuses. Imitating a dog.
**Likes:** Herself. Being indulged. Physical affection in general. Cookies. Rewards. Guiding others. "Taste-testing" wine.
**Dislikes:** Being slighted.
**Worry:** Disciplining those who are haughty and incompetent.

### ○ Anne Giterns [Neutral / Good]

*Still-a-klutz-no-matter-how-many-years-pass girl. Christand household servant. The mansion's spectator.*

A young lady born into a marquis family. Various tutors were arranged for her and a rigorous education attempted, but to little effect; worried about her future, her parents sent her to serve as an apprentice in the Royal Domain.

There she encountered Bery — servant of the Christand household. Entranced by her, Anne threw away the Royal Domain position her parents had gone to great lengths to secure for her, nearly ending up disowned, and became a Christand servant.

Not unintelligent, not exactly clumsy either — but she is a stage-fright sufferer, a daydreamer, an act-first type, and she tends to fail at crucial moments, often frustrating those around her.

Her character is serious and good-natured; her pedigree, appearance, and bearing are not wanting, and if she could only sit still she would have no shortage of prospects — but she is constitutionally incapable of sitting still.

Recently, confronted with the morally questionable reality of the Christand household, she cannot stop daydreaming.

She has grown fond of Gallen, who is invariably kind and praises her, and secretly wants to have a conversation with Mia, who she senses has a certain similar air.

She has begun to feel a sense of crisis at the talented Elvena coming into her own.

From the day Gallen departed, she spent any spare time she found recording the Christand household's history thus far, and presented *In the House of the Noble Hawk* to her parents as a gift.

The second volume depicts the raw daily life of the Christand household in roundabout language, while also describing Gallen — a venerable old man worthy of respect. She wrote of his loving character; and in his place, having departed of natural causes, Anne set down the reason she decided to give her whole life to the Christand household.

It is something like a letter in the form of a book, and in truth the entirety of the first volume and most of the second is preamble.

She had realized that Kreschenta had euthanized Bery, and knew that Kreschenta was suffering because of it. She had been debating whether to say it — but watching Krische's unchanging kindness year after year, she ultimately decided to tell her.

**☆ After Moving In**

She periodically breaks plates; the total broken long since exceeded a thousand.

She spends her days as a servant alongside Elvena, helping Bery and the others. For some reason she is perpetually rushing about while writing fairy tales and stories in her spare time; she occasionally donates them to orphanages and the like when she goes shopping, but many depict the master-servant love of the Christand household, leading distressed recipients to worry about their educational value.

She has no idea at all that *In the House of the Noble Hawk* has become a famous historical document.

**△ In History**

Known as the Christand servant who wrote *In the House of the Noble Hawk*.

Its grandiose narrative voice and the unexpected faces it gives to famous figures initially prompted some to suspect it was a later-period fiction; but cross-referenced with journals left by people close to the subjects, it is now recognized as a work that came very close to their truth.

For that very reason the lost second volume — believed to contain content about the dragon pact and the Five-Nations War — is deeply mourned, and counterfeit second volumes have appeared more than once alongside rumors about it actually surviving and having leaked.

The content's habit of returning to self-reflection at every opportunity has made Alberan researchers unnecessarily knowledgeable about Anne's habits. In *In the House of the Noble Hawk* she holds Bery in almost bizarre esteem — calling her a genius beyond the reach of ordinary people, the servant among servants, someone beloved by the goddess of beauty and service — and for a time the theory that Bery had coerced her into writing this was popular.

**Appearance:** Black hair gathered at the back, reaching the back. Dark brown eyes. Average build. Cute.

→ *Regarding Krische:* The master of the servant she respects, and a forever-young girl of gentle heart. Bewitching innocence.
**Strong at:** Passable cooking. Passable housework in general. Passable etiquette. Charitable interpretation. Poor timing. Storytelling.
**Likes:** Daydreaming. The Christand household. Gallen. Storytelling. Observing the mansion's daily life.
**Dislikes:** Her own lack of concentration.
**Worry:** Unwholesome thoughts.

### ○ Elvena [Chaos / Good]

*Pretend-to-watch-over-while-actually-guiding type girl. Christand household servant. The mansion's little devil.*

From a village in the kingdom's south; the village head's daughter. The youngest of four sisters; Kalua's younger sister.

She had boarded a carriage to follow her elder sister into the city, but lost sight of her while searching for her, and was unluckily caught by slavers. Because of her refined appearance, her body was sold. Roland was one of those clients; she had been bought by him and made to work as a servant in his estate, but was rescued by Krische and assigned as a Christand servant.

Her disposition is good-natured, but her ethical sense was somewhat warped by the environment she lived through, and she has a little-devil quality. A kingdom hero who elevated the once-fallen Christand baronetcy to the rank of Margrave in a single generation, rising from common soldier to general.
