# Chapter 141: Nyan-Nyan

The unveiling ceremony itself held no particular substance.

Words of congratulation from each nation were read aloud in honor of the Alberan queen's coronation, appropriate gifts were received, and there was nothing but flowery language — meaningless exchanges of formalities and surface-level pleasantries.

What mattered was the conference afterward.

In the royal castle's conference room, Queen Kreschenta was joined around a rectangular table by Grand Priest Zarnalibea, Galshan Vice Chairman Olgan, Elderant Kingdom Chancellor Krolus — and Archbishop Elbebart of the Empire's western diocese.

From the other nations came their number twos, or number threes — individuals with the authority to make decisions on their nation's behalf by their own will alone.

Elbebart, who held no such authority and was several ranks below the others, wiped cold sweat from his face as his complexion turned pale. He had essentially drawn the short straw to save the face of his nation's upper echelons.

This was a forum for discussions between nations. Properly, someone of Elbebart's caliber — a mere archbishop overseeing a western regional diocese — should not have been here; it should have been a primate, or a cardinal.

Clearly, he was not of sufficient standing to be present in such a place.

Looking uncomfortable, Elbebart fidgeted and glanced to his side, wishing for it to end quickly.

Seated there was Prince Klauze of the Kingdom of Kuslan — a small country to the southwest of the kingdom, far inferior to these four powers.

Yet, befitting royalty, his bearing was dignified even in this situation.

Elbebart had no allies here.

"However, Your Majesty the Queen. You speak of peace and peace, yet if that is your position, should you not first settle accounts for the kingdom's past barbarism?"

The one who spoke was Galshan Vice Chairman Olgan.

Kreschenta turned her eyes toward him.

Much like the southern general Dagren Garhka, his face was bearded and rough-hewn. He looked like he would be more at home as a bandit of some sort.

Yet appearances were deceiving — he was originally royalty, of all things.

The Galshan Republic had only become a republic some twenty years ago. Before that, it had been a monarchy just like Alberan, yet for some unfathomable reason, this man — despite being royalty himself — had raised a rebellion to purge the corruption of the royal house. After toppling the monarchy and establishing the republic, he reportedly took the position of vice chairman, declaring that 'if I sit in the chairman's seat, it would be no different from usurping the throne' — to Kreschenta, he was a mentally unhinged individual whose motives defied comprehension.

At any rate, his voice was loud, and Kreschenta disliked him for it.

"During the civil war twenty years ago — the northern territories the kingdom seized. The laments of those driven from their land have not faded even now. What are your thoughts on that?"

Kreschenta tilted her head as though troubled.

"That war was conducted at the request of the Galshan royal house at the time. It was by no means an invasion...... and besides, the matter should have been settled — with your nation's agreement — fully fifteen years ago."

"Agreement. I see, so you call that a proper negotiation. A conference held while brandishing military force against our weakened and diminished nation?"

Olgan pressed the question as if challenging her.

This was before Kreschenta had even been born. There was no way she would know the finer details.

"Naturally, this concerns my predecessor's reign. I harbor no particular feelings toward you personally, Your Majesty. However, as the one who inherits the Alberan Kingdom, I believe you should first settle the kingdom's past accounts before speaking of peace. ......If Your Majesty truly desires peace from the heart."

*Otherwise, those driven from their homeland could not be satisfied.*

Olgan's words were a clear threat.

Kreschenta deflected it with a smile.

"I see. Then would it suffice if we paid money?"

Kreschenta spoke, and continued before Olgan, who had furrowed his brows, could weave his reply.

"That is not what I mean, of course. The hatred and resentment born of war do not simply vanish clean the next day."

Show weakness once and it was over. In diplomacy, you would only be devoured. You had to maintain superiority through the atmosphere and the words at all times.

"Galshan and Alberan have fought war after war over the long years...... and surely the resentment born from all that killing cannot be resolved with a single payment of money?"

Kreschenta continued.

"If we go back several more decades, that land belonged to the Alberan Kingdom. The people whose homeland Vice Chairman Olgan speaks of — their grandparents had their homeland ravaged and ruled by Galshan. We have been repeating this very cycle. Do you not find it fruitless?"

"Do you intend to go back a thousand years next?"

"Fufu, that is not my intent."

Kreschenta laughed pleasantly.

"Vice Chairman Olgan's feelings for his people are admirable. But to address those feelings, I believe a long stretch of time is still required."

Wondering what she was getting at, Olgan crossed his arms and gazed into Kreschenta's eyes.

"......If there are people who wish to return to their homeland, and they are truly of that land, the kingdom will accept them to the fullest extent. The south is fertile land — there is much work to be done, and we can arrange both housing and employment. What has been lost cannot be reclaimed, but a new place of belonging can be created."

She met his gaze squarely and broadened her smile all the more.

What she wore was a somewhat impudent grin.

"All that remains is for our nations to join hands, and time enough for the hatred to fade and for new lives to take root. ......How does that sound? If the people's welfare comes first, this should not be a bad proposal for either nation. Of course, I must add the caveat — if Vice Chairman Olgan truly desires peace from the heart."

The young queen turned Olgan's own words back at him with evident amusement.

Then she brought to her lips the overly sweet tea brewed by the red-haired servant behind her — Bery.

The court nobles present were startled by her half-provocative words, but what rang through the room was a booming laugh.

"Ha ha ha, well well, forgive my rude question. Despite your youth, you are unshaken, with an air of authority about you. And quite astute, it seems. My apologies."

"Think nothing of it, Vice Chairman Olgan. It makes me happy to hear you say so."

Shimmering gold woven through red swayed.

The fairy-like queen graced her beauty with a gentle smile.

Inwardly, she was suppressing the urge to cover her ears against his noisy voice.

The question had been to gauge Kreschenta. If she were a mere idealist — a girl who, fearing war, would obediently hand over money as told — he had intended to squeeze her dry before any fighting began.

It was far more profitable than a costly war. Drain the nation's strength, then invade and carve off territory.

"I was astonished to hear that you had defeated the Black Lion — the Archduke of Alberan — and ascended to the throne, but I see, now I understand. The kingdom's future appears bright."

As proof, Olgan piled on more flattery to sidestep Kreschenta's proposal entirely.

He had no intention of agreeing to any arrangement that did not place Galshan in a superior position — and invading Alberan was likely already a foregone conclusion.

Kreschenta was unbothered and went along with it.

"Thank you. If peace can be brought to the kingdom hereafter, that is my wish as well."

"Those are words I cannot let pass."

The one who cut in was Elderant Kingdom Chancellor Krolus.

The old man toyed with his long chin-beard and glared at Kreschenta.

"It sounds as though you are saying the threat to peace lies outside. Which nation has sown disaster upon its neighbors in every age — history makes that plain enough."

"You say that is Alberan?"

"Indeed. When Alberan speaks the word 'peace,' it is nothing more than a preparation period for the next invasion. Deceived by those words, how many times has our nation been put to the torch?"

*Quite right, quite so,* Kreschenta thought, yet her smile did not waver.

The Elderant Kingdom bore the title of kingdom, but unlike Alberan's absolute monarchy, it was closer to a confederation of barbarian tribes. Rather than a feudal state, it was indeed a union — the chief of the strongest tribe became the overlord and claimed the title of King of Elderant.

The tribal chiefs were loyal to the king once one was chosen, but relations between tribes were naturally poor, and civil wars were frequent. Furthermore, every time a king died, the powerful tribes warred among themselves over the succession, depleting their strength — and Alberan would invariably find some pretext to pick off territory. This was the standard pattern.

"I do not mean to contradict you, but in the kingdom's western regions, not a day passes without reports of provocative border crossings and plundering by Elderant forces. Hearing such reports, we too must respond accordingly — first, I would ask for your nation's cooperation in not creating the sparks of war."

"You are merely attributing ordinary bandit raids to our nation and shifting blame."

Naturally, he would never acknowledge such a thing.

Elderant's territory extended westward to the mountain range, rivaling the kingdom's, but the reason it fell a step below its neighbors was surely due to such internal squabbling.

Technologically and culturally, it was reasonably civilized, but in mentality, they were primitives. Barbarians. A nation-shaped gang of raiders, and an exceedingly troublesome presence.

That said, the wealthy plains bordering the west had been largely carved away by the kingdom, leaving only forest. Since Elderant's territory was mostly forest, conquering it entirely would be difficult, and there was little to gain in return.

That it still existed as a nation was due to such circumstances, and Kreschenta slightly resented the previous kings for not having destroyed it before her reign.

The current king hailed from Elderant's eastern regions and was naturally not well-disposed toward Alberan. Judging by this chancellor's aggressive posture, an attack was certain.

Both south and west were the same in the end — there was no reason not to attack the kingdom.

To the east, Elsren had sent an utterly meaningless envoy, and there seemed to be no shortage of incompetents elsewhere either.

Deciding early on that averting war from this position would be difficult, Kreschenta gave up on war avoidance and prayed for the conference to end quickly.

* * *

"That queen is quite the figure."

"Did she win your approval?"

"Yes, she did. When we return, it's time to prepare the army."

"You approve of her so you're going to attack......? And you're starting preparations now?"

In the white corridor built of Forged Stone, the words of Vice Chairman Olgan drew an exasperated look from Zalvarg, the tall and lean Master-at-Arms.

An unremarkable face with nothing particular to note — despite holding the position of Master-at-Arms of Galshan, the highest-ranking military officer, Zalvarg's face lacked any glamour. He was originally neither noble nor even a proper soldier — a commoner, a shoemaker. Twenty years ago, during the civil war, he had joined the rebel side, distinguished himself on the battlefield, and risen to become Olgan's chief aide. He was the warrior whom Olgan trusted most.

He had accompanied Olgan on this visit as head of the guard.

Olgan himself had no interest in power, but Galshan's government was sustained by his personal prestige. Regardless of his title of vice chairman, he was in substance Galshan's leader, a man who must never be lost.

The strictness of having Master-at-Arms Zalvarg as his escort was only natural.

"If we attack, Garhka is the opponent. We could spend several years preparing and it still wouldn't be too much."

Dagren Garhka. The fierce general who had defeated Galshan time and again.

Only that man could fight so capably against elephants and other ferocious beasts. An iron-walled formation that never lost its flexibility — when Galshan envisioned the kingdom's army, his was the first face that came to mind.

When Olgan had heard of the civil war and swiftly wrapped up the campaign against northwestern Elderant to watch for an opportunity, the reason he could not move was that Garhka was watchfully guarding the south.

Challenging him while forces were committed to Elderant was too dangerous.

Accepting Kreschenta's invitation this time was partly out of a desire to see her for himself, but above all, it was to give Galshan time to consolidate.

In a few months or a year, the kingdom could not fully recover from a civil war. Unlike an ordinary war, the wear on both sides in a civil war all fell upon one's own national treasury.

In the same span of time, Galshan — which already had reserves — would naturally gain the upper hand.

Before leaving Galshan, there had been an overture from the Empire. Most likely, unlike last time, this would be a joint operation.

The same request had come during the Empire's previous invasion, but Olgan had declined then because it felt wrong to strike at someone's back while they were away. Olgan knew nothing more stirring than a battle where life and pride were both on the line. He had not particularly wanted to resort to the cowardice of striking his chosen rival Garhka from behind.

"......Hm?"

Olgan turned his gaze to the courtyard.

There stood a single large man and a beautiful silver-haired girl wearing a pale blue dress.

"Mm, this is......"

"Hahaha, fine craftsmanship, is it not? This was made long ago by the same artisan who made Bogan-sama's — after Corps Commander Melkikos shouted at me to at least take some care with my armor......"

During the ceremony, Kokys had attended in his armor, and while waiting for Bery — who was attending Kreschenta — he happened to encounter Krische.

To pass the time while waiting, they had been chatting.

Krische showed interest in the valiant tiger-shaped helm.

Kokys removed the helm to show her and began explaining its origins.

"As you can see, the tiger is——"

"Nyan-nyan."

Krische looked up at him with sparkling eyes.

"Ehehe, it's a cat, isn't it. Bery likes them. They go 'nyaa nyaa.' Kokys, do you like them too?"

"......Huh? Ah, no, it's not a cat, you see......"

"......I do think calling you Kokys rather than Corps Commander Agrand is fine too, but...... the thing is, among the army's soldiers, affectionate nicknames have become fashionable."

"Oh, nicknames?"

"Yes, you know, like Skeleton. Ehehe, Krische named that one. Because he has a skeleton-like face!"

"I-I've heard as much......"

The only person who actually called Elrouga by that terrifying nickname that no one else dared utter was Krische, and the fashion for nicknames existed not in the army but only within Krische's own mind.

Kokys responded with supreme composure.

"Since Kokys is going to be working under Krische from now on, Krische thought it would be nice to have a nickname rather than just calling you by name. Krische couldn't come up with anything for a while, but just now it clicked. From now on, Krische will call Kokys 'Nyan-nyan'."

"......What?"

"Corps Commander Varkus is Woof-woof, and, well, the two of you have a similar feel. The two of you together as Woof-woof and Nyan-nyan is just wonderful! And it's easy to say too!"

Bouncing lightly on her toes, Krische was in the highest of spirits.

A beaming smile overflowing from Krische — one he had never once been shown since their first meeting — concealed enough force to make even the renowned warrior Kokys flinch.

"Here, Nyan-nyan, Krische will return your helm."

She rose on tiptoe and hopped up to place the 'valiant tiger-shaped helm' on Kokys's head, then nodded with satisfaction several times.

Kokys glanced toward the nearby pond and studied the reflection of the armored giant within it.

A warrior's body, wrapped head to toe in muscle and steel armor.

That was Nyan-nyan.

It was sheer madness.

*——I see. So this is how Marshal's Advisor Faren and Granmeld were done in.*

*I will not make the same mistake.*

Kokys hurriedly raised his voice.

"N-no, wait, Krische-sama, th-that nickname is really......"

"......Oh, someone's coming? Is that an acquaintance of yours, Nyan-nyan?"

But Krische's attention was already elsewhere.

The sound of 'Nyan-nyan' spoken in that sweet voice — its terrible naturalness struck Kokys as a sign that it was already too late.

"Hmm...... that's someone from the ceremony earlier. Vice Chairman Olgan of Galshan?"

He was admittedly smaller than Kokys, but still large enough to qualify as a big man. Walking toward them in ceremonial dress that seemed ready to split at the seams over his muscles was a bearded middle-aged man.

"Well, a familiar face. Christand's......"

"Corps Commander Agrand, Vice Chairman."

Kokys immediately straightened his posture and clicked his heels.

When addressing a foreign dignitary, one placed both hands behind the back to show the absence of hostile intent.

"That's right, Christand's corps commander. When so much time has passed, even my memory gets hazy. Though a man like you is not so easily forgotten once seen."

Krische observed Kokys's conduct, then lightly lifted the hem of her skirt and curtsied.

She looked every inch a princess, radiating elegance.

It was hard to believe this was the same fiend who moments earlier had been forcing a nickname like 'Nyan-nyan' on her subordinate.

"......And this person, who was at Her Majesty's side——"

"This is Krische Alberinea Krishtand-sama."

Kokys answered without a moment's delay.

Krische did not mind such things, but royalty did not introduce themselves by custom. It was natural for a nearby attendant to make the introduction, and Kokys treated Krische as royalty for now.

He was also somewhat uneasy about letting Krische speak freely with a foreign dignitary.

"......Alberinea."

Who the person standing beside Queen Kreschenta had been — everyone had been wondering. They had heard the rumors: the queen had an older sister, and it was that sister who had defeated the generals on Gildanstein's side during the civil war and struck down Gildanstein himself — the hero.

Everyone suspected this was her, yet no one could openly ask. To inquire after a member of the royal family by name was the height of rudeness in any nation.

One had to either research beforehand or wait, as now, for the other side to make introductions. It was customary for the other party to take care not to cause embarrassment.

Rinea was an honorary peerage denoting a Senior Knight of the kingdom, and attached to it was Alberinea — an unfamiliar title seemingly derived from the kingdom's name.

Surely, this girl was the one.

A doll-like expressionless face, with only those observing violet eyes gleaming inorganically.

No sociability, no cheerful smile.

Resembling Kreschenta and yet not — a beauty that somehow sent a chill down the spine. The mana she wore about her was what one could only call serene.

Physical enhancement — phantom muscles formed from mana.

Noticing that it was already deployed, Olgan furrowed his brows. He considered whether she was on guard against him, then instinctively sensed that was not it.

There was not the slightest tension, stiffness, or distortion in her standing posture.

From the very start, as a matter of daily habit — she always existed in that state.

The concentration and stability required were fearsome.

Maintaining mana usage in daily life was extraordinarily difficult. Compared to the body's own strength, the force was far too great — while manageable on a battlefield, the fine adjustments required in ordinary life made it nearly impossible.

When Olgan had first heard the rumors of Alberan's other princess, he had been half-skeptical. The Sword Princess, whose name had reached even Galshan — who, despite losing the kingdom's hero Bogan Christand, had slain the ever-victorious Aulgorn Hilkintos and the Black Lion Gildanstein Alberan.

And she was said to be a girl not yet fifteen.

The battlefield was not so forgiving that such a girl could dance across it. Having known battlefields better than anyone, Olgan had doubted.

He had assumed it was nothing more than stolen credit.

But looking at the girl before him, he could not dismiss it as impossible.

To that degree, this girl's presence stirred something indescribably unsettling within him.

Unlike any warrior he had ever laid eyes on.

Not the same kind of oppressive presence as Kokys.

The sensation of his spine crawling — it was the same as what he had felt in his youth, when he was brought close to a captured magical beast.

A fear born from instinct.

"......I see, Alberinea — seeing you up close, it is clear. You appear to be everything the rumors say."

Olgan extended his right hand.

Krische looked at it curiously, then held out her own hand with a slightly reluctant expression.

His hand, like his face, was covered in hair. It looked unclean.

"As a fellow warrior, I am honored to meet you. Perhaps, in the not-so-distant future, we may meet again."

"Umm...... thank you."

Her right hand clasped in his grip, Krische contemplated how she would kill him if he attacked, while giving a slight bow.

She fundamentally disliked handshakes, which surrendered the freedom of one hand, and on top of that his hand was hairy and slightly sweaty. All the more unsanitary.

Already in Krische's estimation, Olgan's rating had tipped toward 'dislike.'

As soon as her hand was released, Krische promptly produced a handkerchief and thoroughly wiped her hand.

Olgan paid it no mind, but the faces of both Kokys and Master-at-Arms Zalvarg went rigid with shock.

It was staggeringly rude — a breathtaking breach of etiquette.

In some circumstances, it could escalate into an international incident.

That said, Krische was a self-proclaimed model of good conduct — in her mind, cleaning one's hands after touching something dirty was only logical, an eminently hygienic and sensible practice.

Any discourtesy not registered in her knowledge simply did not count.

Without the slightest doubt, she meticulously cleaned every part of the hand that had been shaken.

Kokys and Zalvarg could only stare at her, dumbfounded.

"Galshan has many beasts not seen in the kingdom. When the time comes, I would very much like to show them to the Alberinea."

"Err...... thank you......?"

It was a sort of declaration of war, but Krische simply tilted her head, wondering if this was some kind of standard greeting. He was saying he would show her all sorts of beasts, so she ought to give her thanks for now.

That was the extent of her understanding. The roundabout phrasing of 'let us meet on the battlefield' failed to register at all.

"Then, until we meet again. Let us go, Zalvarg."

But unaware that his meaning had completely failed to reach Krische, Olgan swept his mantle aside with the air of a man whose declaration was complete, and strode away.

Zalvarg immediately followed.

"Did you see that, Zalvarg? That is a monster not to be underestimated by appearances."

"......She is certainly remarkable. Especially the part where she immediately wiped her hand with a handkerchief after a handshake."

"Hm?"

"No, it's nothing. ......Did she win your approval as well?"

"She did. Heh, the kingdom is an interesting place...... Now I'm truly fired up."

Grinning like a beast, Olgan wore a beast-like grin.

* * *

"What's the matter? Nyan-nyan."

"I-it's not just the name, but...... that was not acceptable. Wiping your hand immediately after a handshake."

"......? Krische likes to keep her hands clean."

"Th-that's not the point, you see......"

# Chapter 142: The Royal Palace Incident

Heavy infantry alone cannot kill skirmishers, for example.

Unable to catch them in their light gear, the heavy infantry would be slaughtered one-sidedly.

Yet skirmishers alone cannot resist cavalry. Run down at speed and dragged into close-quarters range, they have no armor to protect them.

But cavalry cannot kill spearmen who hold their ground. Even if they attempt a charge using their speed and weight, the spearheads will skewer horse and rider alike.

Each exists in a triangular relationship, and that is precisely why an army fields all three.

Against the enemy's skirmishers, send cavalry. Against the enemy's cavalry, present spears. Against the spears, pour arrows.

But the Black Flag Special Service was both cavalry and spears. Already possessing both at an extraordinarily high level, if the ability to deliver one-sided ranged fire was added, nothing could stand against them.

Outside the walls of Albernaria.

In the large-scale training grounds constructed there, the Black Flag Special Service was conducting exercises.

The Black Flag's training area was typically tucked away in a corner of the training grounds, near the city walls. A section surrounded by sparse trees, not quite enough to call a forest, was allocated for their training.

Within the grove, javelins hurled by the Black Flag soldiers pierced through trees with thunderous impacts — and the snap of powerful bowstrings rang out from every direction.

"Vulture, how is the progress of the drilling?"

"No issues. Extremely smooth, Krische-sama, Gallen-sama."

Gallen, accompanying Krische, surveyed the scene with interest. The two of them had been inspecting the other corps as well, and this was the last stop.

As the Black Flag fell directly under Krische's command, this was Gallen's first time observing their training — everything was new and fascinating.

Despite it being new-recruit training, there was no sign of any of the basics being drilled — no marching in formation, no defensive formations, nothing of the sort.

"The unit gained fame from its actions during the civil war, which has helped. The recruits sent by the selection squad are all brimming with enthusiasm at having been chosen for the kingdom's finest. Even when ordered to undergo brutal training, they grit their teeth and endure. Ideal recruits, I'd say."

"......Hmm, that's good to hear. That appears to be javelin training, but the things they're holding look a bit different."

The thunderous sounds that had been ringing out were of trees being gouged. What the Black Flag soldiers gripped and hurled at the trees were dully gleaming iron rods — though hemp cloth was wrapped around the grip, the entire rod appeared to be solid iron.

"They're simple iron rods. Ordinary javelins break too often and the expense adds up, so when Krische-sama told me to think of something...... Mia."

"Oh, yes."

Mia grabbed one of the iron rods from where they were propped and brought it over, holding it out to Gallen.

Taking it, Gallen nearly stumbled from the weight but caught himself. For Gallen, who did not use mana, it was absurdly heavy.

"......They throw things like this? Mana-users really are built differently. I could never hope to throw this."

Gallen returned the iron javelin to Mia with a look of astonishment. The girl, far smaller and slighter than him, received it with ease and bowed her head.

It was likely due to innate mana capacity. Setting aside swordsmanship, Mia had some of the highest raw strength in the unit.

"Haha, even among us, only a handful can throw these properly. What's being trained here isn't so much javelin throwing as physical control."

Though slender, it was nearly six feet of solid iron. No human's muscle power could throw it, and even swinging it was far from easy.

Only by using mana to control the body and stabilizing the core could this iron javelin be sent to its intended target. Most of those training here with the iron javelins were new recruits, and the primary objective was to teach them how to handle phantom muscles and their bodies.

Once they learned to use their bodies without relying on physical muscle strength, it would carry over to actual javelin throwing and sword training.

"Since we expanded from a century, Krische wanted to train them in things beyond close combat — so javelin and archery training are the main focus right now."

"Archers?"

"Yes. Kurofuyo operates alone a lot, so Krische wants the unit to be able to coordinate ranged and close support internally. Krische heard from Nyan-nyan that ojii-sama used to do something similar back in the day."

"......Nyan-nyan?"

"Kokys."

Gallen widened his eyes at the word, then after a beat, laughed with amusement.

"Ha, so Kokys is Nyan-nyan. I see — paired with his appearance, that's quite an entertaining name."

"Ehehe, right, ojii-sama! Krische thinks Nyan-nyan is a really great nickname!"

What a spectacularly ridiculous nickname — hearing the name 'Nyan-nyan' bestowed on his former subordinate Kokys, Gallen laughed with genuine delight and patted Krische's head.

Gallen saw absolutely nothing wrong with the punishment-game-level naming inflicted on Kokys. It was his darling grandchild's doing — strict with himself and others though Gallen was, Krische was the one exception.

Krische, believing she had been praised, nuzzled up to Gallen and happily softened her cheeks.

*Ojii-sama really understands,* she thought, in the highest of spirits.

What emerged was a negative spiral. Seeing her grandfather's reaction, Krische grew ever more confident that her naming sense was, in fact, not bad at all.

The hopeless naming sense, now armed with newfound confidence and the authority of the Alberinea, made Krische akin to a natural disaster descending without warning — the threat grew by the day.

Vulture — Dagra — felt pity for the newest victim, Kokys, and for the future casualties to come, and glanced sideways at Mia. Mia shook her head vigorously as if to say *it wasn't me.* The one to blame was Kalua, not her. Being held responsible for this was far too unreasonable.

"......Indeed, there was such a time. During a retreat, I gathered the remnants into an improvised ranged support unit."

This was from the days of leading Bogan and the others. At the time, they had lost a battle due to an incompetent commander and were under fierce enemy pursuit, reduced to leaving everything to luck.

If it had come to that, then better to gamble — Gallen decided to turn the tables on the pursuing enemy's commanders. He could see that the enemy was growing complacent in their victory.

Gallen had Bogan and the others run ahead and gather up the scattered remnants — from which he assembled a small javelin unit and an archery unit.

If spears and shields created equilibrium on the battlefield, it was ranged weapons that created advantage — the superiority generated by one-sided fire became ever more decisive the smaller the engagement.

Gaining an advantage of two thousand across an army of ten thousand changed nothing. But shaving twenty men from the hundred directly in front of you had an immense effect on the morale of both friend and foe. In a battlefield where tens of thousands of lives clashed, it was a trifle — but every individual could only feel the advantage and disadvantage created by the few dozen around them.

He gave spears to the men with low morale, designating them as javelin troops and forbidding them from close combat. Even poor javelin throws would find a mark against pursuing cavalry — the targets were large. Above all, numbers were what mattered.

He used himself as bait, standing before the enemy and luring a charge, then had them fire in unison. The advantage thus created, he charged in and struck the disordered enemy——

"A strong shield, yet also possessing decisive breakthrough power...... I see, if archers are added to cover the remaining gap, there will be no weakness."

What Krische was trying to build with the Black Flag was a small army.

The Black Flag was already composed entirely of mana-users — the finest infantry, capable of serving as powerful cavalry as well. If ranged fire capability was added on top of that, no enemy could hope to stand against them.

"Kurofuyo's problem is that their swordsmanship is overall rather poor, so Krische wanted a means to one-sidedly defeat opponents who would beat them in pure combat ability. Lots of people died in the last battle, after all."

"......I'm sorry. If I had managed things a little better, perhaps the losses could have been reduced."

"Ojii-sama is not at fault. Krische heard from Selene that ojii-sama fought incredibly hard."

Some researchers theorize that this power seeps in from an overlapping parallel world, while others hypothesize that the world itself is formed from mana — but all such views remain mere conjecture based on observed properties.

**○ Mana-Users**

No living thing is entirely devoid of internal mana, but those whose bodies have been physically altered by an exceptional concentration of mana are defined as such. The term primarily refers to humans, but in a broader sense also includes magical beasts — mana-bearing animals.

Mana-users are, as described, physically altered. It is said that their bodies contain internal organs made of mana-based spiritual substance. These organs decompose ingested food and waste products into mana — as a result, mana-users no longer require excretion, among other physical changes from ordinary organisms.

The effects of mana on the body are significant. Aging is generally slowed; their bodies tend to stop maturing in young adulthood, with gradual aging beginning only past thirty. However, individual variation is great, and some are said to have remained unchanged in appearance past fifty.

They are also resistant to disease and tolerant of poisons and alcohol. Mana-users are generally viewed as biologically superior to ordinary individuals.

On the other hand — perhaps as a tradeoff — mana-users tend to suffer higher rates of infertility compared to non-users, and the birthrate among nobles, who are almost universally mana-users, skews low. This tendency weakens when one partner lacks mana, but mana-using nobles often find physiological revulsion toward non-users — who age faster and require excretion — and such marriages are generally disfavored.

Most mana-users are hereditary, but spontaneous cases occur. Given that magical beasts are more common in deep forests where mana concentration is high, some researchers believe ambient mana density plays a role.

**○ Magic Crystals**

Crystalline bodies said to form from mana solidifying underground, physically resembling quartz.

Their physical properties are similar to quartz, but within these crystals, mana can be manipulated as within a living body. By inscribing patterns called 'magical formulae' into them, various forms of physical manipulation become possible through mana.

Light, heat, cooling, water attraction — in some cases, energy can be radiated to burn or destroy objects. Applications are varied, but due to poor mana efficiency, they are rarely used as weapons.

Extensive research was conducted in the past, but military applications proved impractical in terms of efficiency. Currently, researchers known as 'mages' conduct personal research to invent new formulae, while also serving as 'magical engineers' specializing in the production of everyday magic crystal devices. In modern times, the two roles are often conflated.

Research into 'magic' — the technique of manipulating and applying mana outside the body or a magic crystal — has been pursued by many mages from past to present, but no breakthrough has yet been achieved.

**○ Phantom Muscles**

A type of magic available to mana-users.

Also called physical enhancement, this refers to the use of mana for bodily augmentation. By constructing a spiritual-substance body overlaid on one's own physical frame, one can control the body and amplify muscular strength, gaining high physical capabilities. While even unconscious activation occurs among mana-users — making the technique itself not particularly advanced — the deeper one attempts conscious mastery, the greater the concentration and mental stability required, and the difficulty rises sharply.

In some cases, the enhanced physical abilities prove uncontrollable, and the resulting recoil can injure the user — a double-edged sword. Particularly in the extreme conditions of combat, even experienced mana-users find it difficult to wield freely. Skill in this technique is said to define a mana-user's worth.

Battlefield mana-users often build heavily muscled bodies, but this is primarily to absorb recoil and control excessive force. Many also wear armor of weights no ordinary person could bear, for stability — yet some warriors believe these very efforts take them further from the ideal.

**○ Magical Beasts**

Mana-bearing animals.

Most are existing species born with mana; generally larger than their base animals, with a tendency toward blue-tinged coloration.

Without exception, they possess excellent mana control. Combined with their massive frames, the resulting physical capabilities far exceed those of human mana-users. Magical beasts are feared worldwide — or, in some regions, worshipped as local deities.

The reason human mana-users do not grow to similar proportions is unknown, though the prevailing theory suggests that human willpower unconsciously suppresses mana's enlarging effect.

Magical beasts are presumed to be long-lived, but their danger makes verification impossible.

Tigers, wolves, hawks, boars — most commonly, beasts native to mountains and forests produce magical variants, though exceptions exist, and it is believed any species may potentially produce a magical beast.

Many maritime accidents are also attributed to magical beasts, and numerous seafaring nations devote considerable effort to countermeasures.

**○ Dragons**

Also referred to as ancient dragons or holy spirits.

Intelligent beasts said to have once ruled the world. Most notable are their immense mana reserves and colossal bodies. Their fortress-like frames and mineral-like scales, their mana beyond any magical beast's measure — ancient records state that a dragon's roar reshapes the landscape.

In the distant past, a territorial dispute among dragons — the Dragon War — saw countless dragons rampaging, and many civilizations were destroyed in the devastation. The name of the Lusheran Empire, which once flourished only to be annihilated after provoking a dragon's wrath, is well known.

However, as no sighting of a dragon in flight has been recorded for centuries, dragon lore is often regarded as mere fable. While they remain objects of worship in some regions, skepticism about their existence and abilities persists.

Their blood and flesh are said to contain potent power. Many legends tell of humans who consumed it and gained great strength, and the dragon's body is treated as a kind of elixir.

**■ —— Simulation-Game-Style National Overview (as of Chapter 1)**

**○ Alberan Kingdom — Difficulty: ★★★**

A former superpower located in the continent's relative northwest.

In the past, this nation controlled half the continent. No nation on the continent is ignorant of Alberan, which forged the Sacred Convention with the dragon and established it as international law. Though it lost much territory after splitting with Elsren, it has not lost all its former power and remains a strong nation possessing a formidable military, advanced technology, and robust economy.

However, surrounded by strong powers — Elsren to the east, Elderant to the west, Galshan to the south — all on hostile terms, caution is required. Furthermore, in the main scenario *'The Two Cursed Children,'* the current king has fallen ill from an unknown disease, and a covert succession struggle is underway between the princess and the prince. The royal court seethes with unrest, and a misstep can plunge the player into crisis early on.

A strong nation, yet one requiring care on many fronts.

Also note that this nation occasionally produces individuals of extraordinarily high ability — be wary. They are difficult to identify, and in most cases their personalities are deeply flawed, making them a double-edged sword if mishandled.

The nation's talent pool is already excellent, so early elimination of these individuals is one viable strategy. In post-Elsren-split scenarios, the policy *'Elimination of Cursed Children'* allows for their removal at birth, but it is imperfect and carries significant drawbacks — their exceptional abilities may turn against you.

Whether to use them or eliminate them — that choice alone dramatically changes the play experience. A fascinating nation.

★ Recommended unit: **Light Infantry**

As befitting a former superpower, the army's average capability is uniformly high across the board. The vast accumulated knowledge and expertise from conquering half the continent has produced a military structure still worthy of a king's army, with high redundancy.

Alberan's appeal is being above average in everything. If forced to single out a strength, it would be their light infantry. Trained in Alberan's terrain of forests and mountains, the light infantry are exceptionally capable, and combined with the aforementioned redundancy, they can reliably execute highly sophisticated tactical coordination.

However, their defensive capability is no different from other nations' light infantry — handle them with care.

**○ Holy Elsren Empire — Difficulty: ★★**

A nation that split from Alberan; its territory is more than double Alberan's.

This great power was the catalyst for the superpower Alberan's collapse, and it holds much of Alberan's former territory.

With its staggering economy, population, and diverse military — this is arguably the continent's foremost nation.

It has expanded by conquering and subjugating territories, spreading its state religion for control. The problem, however, lies in the friction between the powerful Pontifical Court and the emperor-led parliament. Beneath the surface, conflicts abound, and politics are unstable. The wealth gap is severe, religious corruption and graft are rampant.

Regional lords hold strong authority and the risk of rebellion is ever-present, requiring periodic wars to redirect their frustrations.

Starting power makes it nearly unbeatable, with ample talent. But neglect domestic affairs and you may find yourself in crisis — a precarious nation.

★ Recommended unit: **Nomad Cavalry**

This vast nation fields diverse units, able to combine the strengths of its many subjects and provinces into a single military. That is its overwhelming advantage — and if forced to choose, the nomad cavalry drawn from the empire's numerous pastoral peoples would be the pick.

Said to ride before they walk, their horsemanship is unmatched. On flat terrain, mounted skirmishing alone can defeat most opponents with ease. The cavalry itself is excellent and numerous, and used well, it's an easy game.

Whether to crush the old rival Alberan to the west or expand eastward — either way, proper use of these units makes it straightforward.

**○ Elderant Kingdom — Difficulty: ★★★★**

A tribal confederation whose territory is mostly mountains and forests.

A collection of tribes once ruled by Alberan. Though it calls itself a kingdom, in reality it is closer to a tribal alliance. Its territory rivals Alberan's, with numerous mines, but the lack of plains means limited farmland, frequent food shortages, no major cities beyond the capital, and a modest economy.

The many tribes spread across the land are mutually hostile, with constant inter-tribal warfare. Technologically, it lags behind its strong neighbors.

An outdated nation where old customs persist — but its advantage lies in mobilization numbers. The warrior-society ethos remains strong: every man, even a farmer, takes up the sword and becomes a fighter instantly, requiring no training. Their primitive lifestyle maintains their biological strength, and the madness of charging through arrow fire in light gear is sure to terrify any opponent.

A cultural backwater harboring immense potential — the direction you steer it changes everything. A rewarding nation.

★ Recommended unit: **Light Archers**

Forest-dwelling, these people produce far more hunters than other nations. Soldiers who normally fight as light infantry can switch to archery as needed.

Short bows mean limited range, but their marksmanship is unparalleled — even in dense forest with poor visibility, their arrows fly true.

They can also hold their own in melee against equal numbers, making them overwhelmingly advantaged in forest combat where formations are difficult to maintain.

**○ Galshan Republic — Difficulty: ★**

A multi-ethnic nation holding vast territory south of Alberan. Once an Alberan vassal state, it broke away during the Elsren split when Alberan was weakened.

The southern territory is largely arid wasteland, but periodic highland torrential rains deposit rich soil along rivers, giving it unrivaled wheat production.

The nation's name derives from these powerful rains — *Galshan* means 'strong rain.' It possesses a unique rain-worship religion.

Long leveraging its abundant wheat through land and sea trade agreements across the continent, it is a wealthy trading power. Since transitioning to a republic, internal affairs have stabilized without upheaval.

Whether to first consume Alberan and Elsren or ignore them and expand elsewhere — the options are many, and none are difficult.

★ Recommended unit: **Beast Soldiers**

They have embraced diverse cultures and peoples, and the pinnacle of this is the military use of animals.

Acquiring beasts used in various lands and learning the techniques to handle them, they have domesticated a wide variety of creatures as they would horses.

Beast soldiers are the product of encountering and crushing every culture and absorbing every lesson — animals are inherently stronger than humans, and mastered properly, no one can break through a frontal assault.

Formations, tactics, combat arts — all were devised against human opponents. Beast soldiers instill terror, and a single deployment can throw the enemy into utter chaos.

**○ Arna Empire — Difficulty: ★★★★★**

A maritime nation north of Alberan.

Long allied with Alberan through a deep historical partnership.

Flanked east and west by mountain ranges with the sea to the north, it has developed metalworking and shipbuilding. Despite its small territory, maritime trade gives it formidable economic power, and the high-quality magic crystals from its abundant mines make it the continent's leader in crystal processing. Exports are dominated by these.

However, its small size means a large army is impossible, and with allied Alberan to the south, overland invasion is impractical. Its powerful navy suggests a naval approach, but that too has its challenges.

Deeply devoted to holy spirit worship, the resulting theocratic governance makes aggressive expansion politically difficult; reforming the internal structure would be a prerequisite.

★ Recommended unit: **Crossbowmen / Heavy Crossbowmen**

Having obtained crossbows from the distant east through maritime trade, they disassembled and improved upon them. Their ranged capability relies heavily on crossbowmen.

Compared to bows, crossbows have slower rates of fire, require direct fire, have shorter effective range, and cost more due to their complex mechanism — but requiring no training to use, their mid-range armor-piercing power and accuracy more than compensate.

For pure defense, no better weapon exists. Arna provides crossbow training to all citizens regardless of age or gender, making every citizen a potential crossbowman — perfectly complementing a nation of small population but high wealth and technology.

In field battles, skilled engineers provide larger repeating ballistae and catapults, giving their warfare a distinctive character.

Naturally, these siege weapons are also applicable to offensive operations, and their technological prowess alone holds the potential to dominate the surrounding world.

**○ Creisharana — Difficulty: ☆☆**

Descendants of once-mighty warriors.

A tribe that formerly ruled much of the kingdom's north, they were defeated in a past war with Alberan and now live quietly in the mountains of Albyagel to the kingdom's northwest.

An extremely strict warrior society preserving ancient customs. Boys undergo severe combat training from a young age. Even a man who normally tills fields and crafts daily goods becomes a warrior capable of overwhelming trained regular soldiers of other nations the moment he takes up a spear.

The proximity of magical beasts and their ascetic, harsh environment has forged their fighting strength — in terms of average individual soldier capability, they would rank first or second on the continent.

The problem, however, is population.

Their military mobilization rate relative to population is abnormally high, yet total fighting strength barely reaches 1,500.

Their griffin cavalry — rulers of the sky — number fewer than 100, making offensive operations against other nations hopeless. If invaded, even their elite would be overwhelmed by sheer numbers.

An old non-aggression pact stemming from the Sacred Convention with Alberan prevents attack, but Creisharana is currently on a path of slow extinction.

Yet perhaps serving the holy spirit that once saved them, and fading along the way, is the path they chose.

★ Recommended unit: **Griffin Cavalry**

Individual soldier quality is remarkable, but the truly extraordinary asset is the griffin — the fierce beast they train and ride.

Five feet at the shoulder with great wings, an eagle's beak, and a lion's body — this creature soars freely through the sky, and the griffin cavalry riding them add a new axis to the two-dimensional battlefield.

Cavalry flanking maneuvers are always powerful, and counter-measures exist. But against griffin cavalry wheeling through the sky overhead, no army on the ground can do anything — and the warriors astride them are the tribe's finest.

A well-timed strike can deal a fatal blow to even a vast army. The key to victory for this outnumbered nation lies with the griffin cavalry — of that there can be no mistake.
