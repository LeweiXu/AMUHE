# Chapter 206: Temperature Gap

Faced with the need for a fundamental reassessment of their strategy for the capture of Wulfenite, the Elsren army withdrew ten ri from the city.

They abandoned the deployment of massive trebuchets and instead increased the number of smaller catapults.

Destruction was now unavoidable — but as long as only one person could do it, they simply needed to overwhelm him with numbers.

Intelligence regarding who this individual was had been relayed by former members of Aleha Sarshenka's army, as well as a spy who had escaped Wulfenite despite sustaining serious injuries — but none of that information offered any solution.

It did not provide a means of dealing with an absurd individual who possessed firepower and range exceeding even the large mounted crossbows, and who could destroy massive trebuchets from beyond their reach.

Refreshingly straightforward — there was no option but to resort to brute force through sheer numbers.

"Your face is still pale, Duke Sarshenka. Forget about that — no one considers it your blunder."

"……Yes, sir."

The war council in the great pavilion — the atmosphere was unbearably heavy.

Even Bazlar Lukazan spoke with something approaching pity.

He understood well enough how much effort Biinal Sarshenka had poured into this.

Aleha Sarshenka, whose popularity among the common people was rising — in order to crush this man's younger brother, who had been growing into an eyesore, Bazlar had pulled various strings after the previous war. Even so, he had never intended to destroy House Sarshenka itself.

After the war, he had planned to grant them suitable honors and ultimately bring them into his fold.

At the very least, Biinal's preparation of the siege equipment should have been sufficient atonement.

That all of it had been crushed by a mere individual — by Alberinea — stirred something like sympathy in him.

And in the two days since, Biinal had been working at a frantic pace to construct smaller catapults.

Whatever the outcome, Bazlar intended to at least ensure the house was spared dissolution.

At least, that was how far he'd thought it through.

But the damage that incident had dealt to Biinal's psyche was immense.

Since then, Biinal had been unable to eat and had barely slept.

Siege equipment demanded meticulous preparation.

Materials prepared in the workshop, endless fine adjustments — the siege towers and massive trebuchets had been procured at enormous expense.

For all of it to vanish without producing a single result was naturally enough to drive him to despair.

He had lost countless engineers whom he had desperately gathered, and the costs sunk into them could never be recovered.

"Within a few days, we should have a sufficient number of catapults on our end as well……"

"Very well. I'm counting on that."

Fortunately, the engineers and craftsmen they had brought were skilled.

Since large-scale siege warfare had always been a strong possibility, they had already made some preparations for smaller catapults — so it was not as though they were completely out of options.

His mind was consumed with a single question: how much merit could he wring from the materials at hand, and how much could he limit his losses?

Other nobles had catapults of their own ready as well.

All he could do was pray that his catapults would not be the ones targeted.

Bazlar turned his gaze from him and asked.

"Any issues to the north and south?"

"No movements to report."

The one who answered was the army's vice-commander, Linkara Wokal.

He was nearing a hundred years of age, an old man by any measure, but among those present, he was the general who had spent the longest on the battlefield.

He had been Bazlar's instructor since childhood, and Bazlar placed deep trust in this man above all others.

"I had expected an attack coordinated with that incident, but……"

"It seems there was none. What is your assessment?"

"Their aim is, as expected, a war of attrition. They will not sally forth."

Linkara pointed to Wulfenite on the map as he spoke.

"A three-sided encirclement — similar to last time, but the fact that we haven't taken Wulfenite is significant. With Christand dead and the northern threat eliminated, one could argue conditions are better than before, but……"

He ran a hand along his cleanly shaven chin and continued.

"Given that a swift decisive engagement is difficult, the best course would be to withdraw as we are — secure territorial gains and be done with it."

"You always say nothing but dull things. I'm asking you to consider how we might press the attack on top of that."

"In that case, it would indeed come down to brute force."

Bazlar directed a weary look at him, and Linkara laughed.

"First, the north — attacking the Arna forces from our side is out of the question. Breaking through that great forest would be extraordinarily difficult. We can disregard this entirely."

The northeast of the Kingdom of Alberan — what separated east from north was the great forest.

A natural fortress; breaking through it would require accepting massive casualties.

And after the previous war, the Arna Imperial forces had established a newly constructed fort with the great forest at their backs, making it equally difficult to assault.

"Well, the same applies to them. The current force of ten thousand horse archers should be sufficient. They won't attack."

Twenty thousand Arna Imperial troops against their own ten thousand.

But theirs was a single-branch force made up entirely of horse archers. That was more than adequate against such an opponent.

When employing nomadic cavalry, it was often more effective to form them as an independent unit rather than integrating them into the main army.

Nomads were accustomed to a life spent herding sheep and horses.

Provide them with provisions, grant them operational discretion, and their mobility would allow them to dominate the vast plains.

The enemy would be lured in, their numbers whittled away by raids during the march, and if they sallied out in force, they would be crushed without even managing a proper engagement.

And naturally, the Arna forces — who surely understood this — were unlikely to venture out.

"To the south is Garkha. A dangerous opponent in a straight fight, but so long as we don't take the bait and avoid a decisive engagement, there's little cause for concern."

Their forces there were thirty thousand troops plus five thousand horse archers.

If the enemy left the safety of their city to attack, they could be lured in and cut off from behind.

Having learned from the previous failure, so long as a decisive battle was avoided, this was an opponent they could manage.

"At the very least, there are no immediate problems to the north or south. But attacking in either direction from our end is equally difficult. The question becomes how to capture Wulfenite with our current forces, but……"

Wulfenite lay in the north of Alberan — between the Kureiru Mountains, which stretched from the Dragon's Maw, and the Kalbyas Mountains to the south, at roughly the midpoint between them.

Behind Wulfenite, two central armies plugged the gaps between the mountain ranges north and south, making a forced breakthrough difficult.

Target one of those armies, and the defenders would inevitably sally from Wulfenite.

Not only would they face a flank attack — even if they managed a breakthrough, their supply lines would be severed.

They could not advance without first dealing with Wulfenite.

"That is what everyone wants to hear, Linkara. With Galshan defeated, we cannot afford a prolonged strategy. We need a means to achieve a decisive result."

Bazlar spoke.

The loss of thirty-five thousand in the south to Garkha — forces that should never have been needed — was particularly painful.

The enemy had Nozan Velraigh's thirty thousand in Wulfenite, with two central army commanders and forty thousand somewhat to the rear — seventy thousand in total.

Against that, their own force stood at one hundred thousand.

An advantage, but no longer an overwhelming one.

"First — one option: postpone the capture of Wulfenite."

"……Postpone?"

"Pin fifty thousand against Wulfenite's thirty thousand, and using them as a shield, break through one wing. Swiftly destroy the twenty thousand directly ahead with fifty thousand of our own. How the enemy's remaining wing — twenty thousand — responds is a significant question, but even accounting for twenty thousand enemy reinforcements arriving from the flank, the worst case is fifty thousand against forty thousand. The advantage remains ours."

Fifty thousand could not capture the fortified city of Wulfenite.

But they could hold the garrison in place through a siege.

Meanwhile, the plan was to break through the central army, ravage the royal capital region, and isolate Wulfenite.

In any case, Alberan surely had no reserves to spare either.

It was aggressive, but if they could break through — a strategy that would have been far easier to execute had they not committed thirty-five thousand to Garkha in the south.

But Nozan Velraigh had withdrawn into Wulfenite as if coordinated with Garkha.

"However, the enemy commander is Nozan Velraigh. Whatever one's views on praising the enemy, he is an exceptional general. That delaying action and phased withdrawal — given that he holed up in Wulfenite only after we committed forces to the south, he has almost certainly anticipated this option. We should assume he has made appropriate preparations."

The generals, who had been focused solely on the capture of Wulfenite, had shown impressed expressions — but those words made them frown.

Bazlar placed a hand on his chin and regarded Linkara.

"Even taking that into account, it doesn't seem like a bad plan?"

"Indeed. However, given that Galshan has been defeated, the uncertainties are considerable. From what we hear, Galshan was defeated despite having twice the enemy's strength against both Alberinea and Garkha. It would be easy to dismiss Galshan as incompetent, but I do not take this lightly."

Linkara answered.

"Elkaarl, Kilrea, Zalvarg, and Olgan. Those are names of genuinely capable commanders. With the exception of Kilrea, they have each crossed blades with the Holy Empire on multiple occasions. You should know that they were by no means incompetent."

"……Hm."

"We should not regard Alberinea — who overwhelmed the best Galshan had to offer — as a mere distinguished commander. In fact, this army of one hundred thousand had its siege equipment destroyed by a single individual and was forced to retreat with our backs turned to the enemy."

A faint murmur rippled through the tent.

This was the army of the Grand Duke of the Holy Empire, Bazlar Lukazan.

For a vice-commander, the statement was exceedingly harsh.

Bazlar's expression flickered with displeasure, but then he smiled.

"Settle down. It is unpleasant, but it is fact."

His low voice resonated through the tent.

"So your concern is whether fifty thousand can truly contain the thirty thousand in Wulfenite — is that it?"

"That is part of it. But also this…… whether the west — Elderant — has already been defeated, or perhaps crushed entirely."

"……Oh?"

Bazlar smiled at those words.

"Heh, that's a bold claim."

"I am a worrier, unlike Your Grace."

*This is a pessimistic projection, but,* Linkara continued.

His deep wrinkles carved even deeper as he spoke — smiling, yet with utterly serious eyes.

"The Alberinea army that defeated Galshan marched west. From what we can gather of the southern intelligence, that much is almost certainly true. But Alberinea herself, who should have been leading them, is here."

Linkara moistened his tongue with black soybean tea and continued.

"The normal assumption would be that she passed through the capital after the Galshan campaign and came here. But given the speed of Galshan's collapse, there is a very real possibility that Alberinea struck a blow against Elderant and then pushed ahead alone."

"……I see. If your concern proves correct, there is a very real chance that a full army-sized reinforcement could appear."

"Yes. If that is the case, the enemy's rear forty thousand will avoid a decisive engagement and wait for the main Alberinea army heading here from the west before launching a counterattack."

*A forced breakthrough carries too many uncertainties,* Linkara said.

"If my prediction is correct, those reinforcements will appear from the west within one to two weeks. In the absolute worst case, four or five days."

As he spoke, Linkara mentally pictured the territory of Alberan.

Alberinea had arrived two days ago, when the siege equipment was destroyed.

Assuming the absolute worst — an army cutting straight across the center of the capital region on the most direct route — it would be a ten-day march.

An individual riding relay horses and pushing ahead alone could do it in roughly four days.

"However Alberinea managed it, she has tamed a jade tiger and uses it as a mount. We don't know its exact speed, but let us assume she used relay horses. If Alberinea set off ahead simultaneously with the defeat of the Elderant army, that would be about the right timing."

"I see. I understand your concern. ……You said first — one option."

"Yes. Being excessively pessimistic and becoming timid would be foolish, but should the worst case occur under this strategy, even defeat becomes possible. At ninety thousand against one hundred thousand, our numerical advantage thins considerably."

Linkara drained his black soybean tea and declared.

"We should leverage our advantage."

"……Advantage?"

"The horse archers, Your Grace. Fourteen thousand horse archers in a single independent formation, unnecessary for siege warfare — send them through the gaps in the enemy line to the royal capital region. Have them threaten the enemy's supply lines and strike at the enemy forces positioned behind Wulfenite from the rear, while buying time against any reinforcements if the situation demands it. ……Meanwhile, the full remaining force captures Wulfenite in one stroke."

A single horse archer force could never be caught by an infantry-based enemy army.

The outer ring of Alberan had been evacuated to prevent looting, but the royal capital region still had villages that could be raided, allowing them to resupply locally.

"The burden on them will be considerable. We cannot take too long. The capture of Wulfenite will inevitably require brute force, and we must be prepared for heavy casualties — but even so, with eighty-five thousand troops, capturing Wulfenite against its garrison of thirty thousand is entirely feasible."

Bazlar considered for a moment, and a bestial grin spread across his masculine features.

"It's certainly a more aggressive plan than your usual style, but…… not bad. A full-on blood-and-flesh siege over the walls."

"……Personally, the height of folly. The worst of the worst strategies."

Linkara spoke with evident reluctance.

A siege pressed in such a short timeframe should never be attempted without exceptional equipment.

The cost of the massive trebuchets and siege towers was the lives of tens of thousands of soldiers.

"As I said, my preference is to consolidate territorial gains here and lock them in as a result."

"You're the only one who thinks that, Linkara. If we had taken the rich southeast, perhaps — but the east alone isn't enough spoils to divide. Do you mean to start a civil war?"

Bazlar directed his gaze to the assembled generals — the nobles.

Linkara's words were perfectly reasonable from a military standpoint.

But the man gave far too little weight to the realities of noble society.

It was precisely because there was profit to be gained that these men had come, investing their own fortunes to raise troops and equip them.

The eastern territories of Alberan had once belonged to Elsren — not bad land by any means — and in terms of reclaiming lost territory, a passing mark.

But it was not enough to cover the costs of both the previous war and this one.

Had they secured Alberan's wealthy southeast, the conversation might have been different — but ending things here was simply not an option.

Nobles going bankrupt, like the Sarshenkas, would emerge in droves.

Unlike Alberan, in the Holy Empire — where nobles held their own territories and power — that would be a fatal problem.

In the worst case, it could provoke a mass revolt by desperate, ruined nobles.

Preventing that would require House Lukazan to shoulder the burden, and the enormous losses that entailed would be significant even for the Lukazan house itself.

Furthermore, it had been Bazlar who induced the Papal Court to declare a holy war.

If mud were smeared across his reputation a second time, the imperial throne would slip beyond reach for generations to come, no matter how skillfully he maneuvered.

On every front, the choice to end things here was simply not possible.

"As discussed, the primary objective is the capture of Wulfenite. Karud, your role is critical."

"Sir. Leave it to me."

The supreme commander of the nomads they had brought along this time — Great Chieftain Lanu Karud smiled with his thin face and saluted.

"Understand that your contribution here will be valued accordingly. In light of your prior service as well, I shall petition His Majesty the Emperor to grant you limited autonomy."

"Most gracious of you. ……We shall perform to your every expectation."

And with those words, his narrow eyes grew even narrower.

* * *

The war council chamber held the Velraigh army's corps commanders in rows, and seated beside Nozan Velraigh was a girl with silver hair.

Beautiful, utterly out of place in this room.

"Woof-woof."

"……Yes?"

She furrowed her brow and called out.

The one who responded, a deep scar across his cheek lending him a fierce countenance, was Granmeld Varkus — he turned his gaze to the girl, Krische Alberinea Christand, with a questioning look.

"And then, Baldy Four-Eyes."

"……Yes."

Behind spectacles atop a bald head — a face that already looked neurotic now showing fatigue — the next to respond was Saldan Galkalon.

Without ever being given a chance to object, his nickname had somehow become firmly established.

"And Skeleton, and then giving an affectionate nickname to the Agrand Corps Commander too, Nyan-nyan…… Krische has been thinking, and…… perhaps Krische has been doing something very rude?"

"……Rude, you say?"

Saldan asked, glancing over at Granmeld.

Granmeld nodded.

*Could it be — had she finally realized that the nicknames she bestowed were not merely rude but catastrophically so* — a faint hope glimmered in his eyes.

Those nearby also wore strangely tense expressions as they watched the girl's young, beautiful face.

"……It feels like General Velraigh is the only one being left out, among the three subordinates of the Lord Protector."

But naturally, Krische would never notice her own error.

In her mind, the conviction that her nicknames were wonderful things had already hardened into certainty.

"When the Lord Protector's three subordinates are together and only General Velraigh is called 'General Velraigh,' it feels too formal. And on top of that, only his subordinates get affectionate nicknames……"

Everyone was rendered speechless by Krische's concern over something no one had given a moment's thought.

Her expression was unusually serious.

Her face radiated an unmistakable air of deep remorse.

"……Your consideration alone is more than enough, Krische-sama. I always feel your kindness clearly."

*——It's finally come.*

Wearing a smile on his handsome face and delivering his answer with easy grace, the emotions raging within were indescribable.

It had started with a centurion branded Vulture, and Skeleton.

Then Granmeld's Woof-woof, and even Kokys — whose name in her mind had permanently become Nyan-nyan — she had told him as much herself.

And now, the newest addition: Baldy Four-Eyes.

Every last one sounded more like an insult than a term of endearment — and yet, at this point, what he felt was less revulsion and more an inexplicable sense of exclusion.

She was a genius beyond the reach of common sense, an absolute being without equal on the battlefield — yet her temperament was endlessly gentle and pure.

There was not a shred of malice in any of these names; rather, they carried a strong sense of affection. Everyone present understood this.

——And yet, he alone remained "General Velraigh" to her.

What came to his heart was, again, an emotion he could not quite name.

He was by no means envious — and yet, it was somehow painful all the same. Such was the bind weighing upon Nozan Velraigh, a man who had inspired dread in countless enemies and allies alike.

What Nozan and the others were about to conduct was a tactical meeting.

A daily affair; the basic policy had already been set, but it was still an important meeting.

It would be the simplest thing for Nozan to say, "Then let us begin the meeting, Krische-sama," and cut her off here.

The timing was right for it.

But was that truly the right thing to do — was it right to reject her innocent goodwill, her desire to show closeness through a nickname?

"…………"

He noticed that every pair of eyes in the room had settled on him.

How would Nozan decline — or what nickname would he be given?

Everyone was keenly interested.

If he refused here, it might leave an irremovable haze in everyone's hearts.

——On the eve of a momentous battle.

What sort of nickname was she planning to give him?

Even Nozan himself could not honestly claim he had no interest whatsoever.

He could not recall ever having been given a nickname.

It was a temptation beyond description.

He did not want a nickname. But he was curious.

And this was not his feeling alone — it was the consensus of the room. Nozan realized he could not stop her.

Brow furrowed, her face unusually serious, her hand on her chin, Krische was staring intently at Nozan's face from the side.

Those jewel-like violet eyes held not a trace of humor — she looked for all the world like some kind of artist.

No one would ever guess that from those lips came names as infantile as Woof-woof, Nyan-nyan, Skeleton, and Baldy Four-Eyes.

"Krische gave the affectionate nickname Flappy to Rigaleiwe-san…… oh, the divine being's Rigaleiwe-san. The name is a bit long, so."

She was utterly cavalier with the divine being's true name.

"I-I see……"

"Yes. Rigaleiwe-san is a big lizard-like creature with wings, and just by flapping them all *whoooosh*, it can create a small storm."

A terrifyingly irreverent statement.

Were she not the kind of being whom the divine being had deigned to share its true name with, it would have been the height of blasphemy — had she been an ordinary person in another era, it would have merited execution.

No one could even muster a retort.

"Krische thought it was a very good nickname, but Lila, and Chieftain Alkirens-san, and the Kreisharana people all begged Krische to please stop……"

Had anyone been present, they would have stopped her without question.

She was visibly displeased, but it was only natural.

"General Velraigh has a featureless…… hmm, uninteresting…… a boring face? Um, well, something like that, so Krische had a bit of trouble thinking of one, but since there are lots of feathers painted on the armor, Krische thought something along those lines might work."

"……A-ah."

In his youth, he had sometimes been called girlish — but never once in his life had his face been disparaged.

Having it so casually dismissed as boring stirred something even in Nozan.

Whether having an interesting face would have been preferable — that was debatable, but the alternative was its own form of suffering.

"That is…… you mean, Flappy?"

"That won't work. Rigaleiwe-san's wings have this grand, powerful feeling — a real *FLAP-FLAP*. For General Velraigh, even generously, it would be more like a flutter-flutter or a patter-patter…… hmm, Krische feels like something is coming, right about here."

Krische pointed to just above her head. What on earth was trying to descend from there?

He had a vague sense of being subtly insulted, but anyone would come up short compared to a dragon.

*There is no malice,* Nozan told himself, accepting it in silence.

"Yes, that kind of direction after all. Krische might have something!"

"……!"

Every face was riveted on Krische with deadly seriousness.

——But what rang out next was the sound of a bell.

"Messenger! Entering!"

Footsteps came clattering down the corridor, and a single messenger burst into the room.

"The enemy army is moving! A single force of approximately ten thousand cavalry is heading south!"

"……Any other movements?"

"From what was observed just now, there was no significant movement in the enemy main body!"

"Understood. Is that all?"

"Sir!"

When Nozan raised his hand, the messenger saluted and withdrew from the room.

"Krische was so close to getting something…… *muu.*"

Nozan glanced at Krische without thinking, but she was already on her feet, her lips pouted in dissatisfaction.

"This is likely the movement we anticipated. Cavalry alone — their purpose is rear-area disruption."

"Y-yes……"

And then Krische looked at Nozan.

The rare intensity from moments ago had vanished; her face now clearly showed irritation.

"In that case, while we can't say for certain, the high probability is that the enemy intends a general assault on Wulfenite once their preparations are complete. It seems they've concluded that Krische's army is cutting through the capital region to reach here. *'General Velraigh,'* Krische will go take a look outside first."

No sooner had she spoken than Krische pattered off.

She left the war council chamber at a trot.

In her wake, an indescribable atmosphere lingered, and everyone directed indescribable looks at Nozan.

Nozan cleared his throat and spoke in a serious voice. "Saldan."

"Although I don't think they'll attack just yet, go confirm the eastern wall as a precaution. If there are no issues, leave your adjutant in charge as things stand. ……We will continue the meeting."

"……Sir."

Saldan exited through the door as if pursuing Krische.

In the war council chamber, nothing remained but a vaguely unsettled atmosphere.

# Chapter 207: Sent to Paradise

——It was the morning of the second day since the opening of the battle for Wulfenite.

The walls stood twenty-five shaku tall — and the eight-shaku-deep moat before them, its water level drained by the damming of the Arzlen River, had taken on the appearance of a mass grave.

The water remaining below the knees had been dyed crimson, and the countless dead were piled within.

A nauseating stench of death hung in the air.

"Keep going! Get more bridges across!"

The moat was at most four ken wide.

The Elsren Holy Empire had deployed engineers into what had become a collective tomb, hauling waterlogged logs and lashing them together to build bridge after bridge.

Once bridges spanned the moat, the space beneath them became a roofed work site.

This served the dual purpose of making an assault on the wall above easier while also allowing sappers to dig beneath the wall — creating tunnels to undermine it, attacking the defenses from both above and below.

Wulfenite's walls, constructed of high-quality stone, could not be easily destroyed by mere catapults.

The thick earthen rampart faced with fitted stonework was nothing short of artistic.

Massively funded and improved during Alberan's golden age, this city remained to this day the most formidable fortified settlement on the continent.

Despite being a city of one hundred thousand, its entire perimeter was enclosed by walls such as these.

There was not a single weakness to be found.

To attempt to storm this fortress city after losing the massive trebuchets was sheer madness.

Given months, perhaps it could have been starved into surrender through isolation.

But Elsren did not have that kind of time.

The advantage of the three-nation invasion had crumbled, and pressure from the south was already mounting.

They had no choice but to burn through ninety thousand lives to scale these walls.

"Damn it all, we've got ladders on the wall — what the hell is the vanguard doing!?"

"It seems the fighting atop the wall is fiercer than expected. ……The presence of that Alberinea is the real problem."

So answered Tig Magnus, the old adjutant, to the words of the bearded, fierce-faced Elsren Holy Empire corps commander, Orkana Rekarishu.

Their position was directly opposite Alberinea.

And from here, the girl's figure was clearly visible.

On the inner side of the wall — perched on the red roof of a tall clock tower, bread in her mouth as she drew her bow, a black cloak over silver hair.

Behind her, she leaned against a yawning jade tiger, the very picture of indolence.

And yet, a battalion commander — a mana wielder — who had leapt from a ladder was effortlessly shot down by two arrows from the girl, tumbling off the wall to the cheers of the defenders above.

Her presence was clearly bolstering morale across this entire section and impeding the assault.

The value of mana wielders in siege warfare was incomparably greater than in open battle.

The wall top was a confined space.

No matter how many tens of thousands of troops one possessed, the fighting atop the walls was always a clash of small numbers against small numbers.

In such engagements, individual strength mattered more than coordination — and mana wielders could brute-force a victory in these small-scale fights.

But Alberinea was relentlessly picking off those very mana wielders.

The previous day, catapult fire had destroyed two of the defensive towers on the wall, crumbling a section and winning an advantage on the north wall.

By the previous night, several bridges had been laid, and staging points had been established within the moat.

Sapping operations were proceeding smoothly.

But since Alberinea had appeared this morning, there had been no change in the situation.

If anything, it had worsened — with catapults being destroyed by Alberinea. Despite having more than sufficient forces to take the wall, they still could not achieve the fall of the north wall.

Wulfenite had inner walls as well — smaller than the outer wall, but present nonetheless.

Roughly dividing the city into four quarters along the cardinal directions, with a central district broadly encircling the inner fortress.

Unless the outer wall was first secured, the assault on those inner defenses could not even begin.

"Damn it all, I'll go myself——"

"Hahaha, Corps Commander Rekarishu — I thought you'd say that."

The one who rode up on horseback and called out wore a patch over his left eye.

He showed some signs of age, but his face was clean-shaven even on the battlefield, and his features were keen.

"……What do you want, Corps Commander Milkorse?"

It was Mead Milkorse, assigned alongside him to assault the north wall.

A former general, once Orkana's superior officer — a career soldier who had risen from centurion. Their personalities clashed, and Orkana disliked him.

"You're a corps commander now, same as me. You've no right to give me orders."

"Of course. This is not an order — it's a proposal."

"……A proposal?"

Mead nodded, a faint smile on his lips.

"As long as Alberinea is there, we'll never break through this wall with a straightforward push. While we still have the numerical advantage, we should force a breakthrough. You and I are of the same mind — am I wrong?"

"……Even so, what's the advantage of teaming up with you?"

"I've already obtained the general's permission. You and I will attempt the breakthrough together — that's an order."

Orkana's face soured.

The general commanding both Orkana and Mead was the son of a ducal house.

A young man who had bought a general's chair with money and pedigree — and Mead had him well in hand.

"……It's that side of you I can't stand."

Using authority to manipulate others.

Mead was a fine warrior in his own right, but a schemer.

The antithesis of Orkana, who valued being a pure warrior above all else.

"I know. But this time, I swear on my name — the credit will be split cleanly in two, no arguments. I may deceive people, but I don't lie. You know that."

"……That just makes it worse. Fine — I can't refuse anyway. What's the plan?"

"You'll like this one. ……You and I force a bloody path through."

Orkana narrowed his eyes and glared at Mead.

"……That's not like you."

"You understand why, don't you? The previous defeat hit me hard. I'm hanging on by a thread."

In the last war, Mead had been entrusted by Bazlar Lukazan with the defense of Wulfenite.

But Aleha and Bazlar's defeat and subsequent withdrawal left Wulfenite isolated.

Besieged and forced to abandon the city, his forces suffered heavy losses during the retreat — and the consequence was that he had returned this time not as a general, but as a corps commander.

Given the situation, Mead had made no mistakes — one could even feel some sympathy.

In the Holy Empire's military, one's standing was heavily influenced by how much money and how many troops one could provide.

Having lost his retainer nobles and his funds and soldiers, Mead would only continue to fall if things went on like this.

That this man was desperate for distinction was certain.

Whether he could be trusted — hearing the plan out first was not a bad idea.

It would be easy enough for Nozan to step in and deliver a conclusion on Krische's behalf, but that would only deepen the soldiers' doubts about Alberinea.

"This is troubling. Krische isn't very good at this talking business……"

Rolling a candy in her mouth, she idly wished Kreschenta could take her place right about now, and rested a hand on her chin.

"Krische only moved the corpses from inside the city to outside. You were attacking, so Krische couldn't open the gates and had to use the catapults. If your side wants to handle the burials, that's fine with Krische, but there are still mountains of dead inside. If we're calling this a battlefield cleanup, then Krische expects you to handle disposal on our side too — is that agreeable?"

Bazlar showed a flicker of surprise at the unexpectedly straightforward reply, but kept it from his face.

"Oh? That's a rather candid response for someone who has done all this. Of course — if it can be done, we shall attend to your dead as well."

"In addition, you currently hold approximately five thousand of our prisoners. In exchange for their return, the ceasefire for cleanup would last until tomorrow — how does that sound?"

"Two days?"

"Yes. Blood and flesh have splattered absolutely everywhere, and the cleanup inside the city is terrible. Krische is using the prisoners for that, so their return would be tomorrow."

Bazlar looked up at the girl's face and considered.

A straightforward offer to return prisoners — what was the intent? Were their provisions reaching a limit?

No — impossible. They should have three or four months' worth of stockpiles at minimum.

Was there significance in buying time?

That possibility was very real.

"Two days is unnecessary. One day is sufficient."

"Then our talk is over. Let's resume the fighting."

Krische said it without batting an eye.

"Right now, the ground there is buried in corpses — but that's only a problem for you, the attackers. For us, holding the walls, there's no need to clean up; it's an advantageous situation. That's the real reason you proposed a battlefield cleanup, isn't it?"

She stroked the jade tiger's neck and smiled with elegant composure.

"If you want that resolved, then our cleanup inside the city should be handled on equal terms. And Krische believes our soldiers deserve time to rest as well. If you're the one talking about order and justice, then surely fairness in negotiation is indispensable."

Her violet eyes simply gleamed in the sunlight.

She gazed down upon Bazlar from above.

"What tiresome posturing. War is fought to win. And the side that kills more and breaks the other's morale is the side that wins. ……If you want to talk about respect for the dead and order, then why not settle it right here — a one-on-one duel between you and Krische?"

She patted the jade tiger, and it leapt from the wall to the blood-soaked earth.

The great beast strode forward as if treading on the flesh of the dead, and halted before Bazlar.

"That way, not a single Alberan soldier or Elsren soldier needs to die beyond this point. Only one more body to add to the pile. ……Krische is tired of all this, honestly."

And Krische, now standing on the ground, drew her razor-sharp curved sword.

Her eyes shone with an inorganic violet light.

"If you're scared, Krische doesn't mind making it one against eight, including the guards over there……"

The blade she leveled did not waver even a fraction.

She wore an inorganic smile, like a doll.

"——Everything you said is just about wanting to win. Let's stop the tiresome posturing, shall we? Krische doesn't want to be here either."

Bazlar's face twisted with fury and his hand seized the scabbard of his sword, but Linkara checked him with a hand.

Krische looked up at Bazlar — and looked down on him at the same time.

"Whether you accept Krische's terms or not. ……The choice is yours, not Krische's."

"……Wretched girl."

Bazlar spat the words and yanked his horse's reins, turning on his heel.

Linkara, wearing a sour expression, spoke.

"……Very well, Alberinea. The ceasefire will hold until dawn the day after tomorrow. Prisoner return by the end of tomorrow — is that satisfactory?"

"Yes. Krische will send an envoy from our side shortly. The details can be settled then."

"……I swear upon my name as Duke and Vice-Commander of the Elsren Holy Empire, Linkara Wokal."

"Krische swears upon her name as well. You may go."

She said it with transparent disinterest, leapt onto the jade tiger, and returned to the top of the wall.

There, she addressed Nozan.

"The ceasefire is until dawn the day after tomorrow. General Velraigh, please arrange things as you see fit."

"Sir. ……Splendidly done."

"Either outcome would have been fine…… well, this wraps things up."

She gave a small yawn and stretched lightly.

"Krische missed breakfast, so Krische is heading back. Around lunch——"

"No, please take the rest of the day off as you are. You've been working without rest — you must be tired."

"Mm…… ehehe, then Krische will take you up on that."

Krische answered happily, then sent the jade tiger trotting toward the city center.

——*This wraps things up.*

Krische had truly meant it — either outcome would have been fine.

Twenty days from the end of the western campaign, once the ceasefire ended — the northern bypass was already complete.

That very midnight, true to her words.

Black shadows descended from the dark sky above Wulfenite.
