# Chapter 171: Trivial Days

The discussion known as the Christand Trial was held that very evening.

The one brought before judgment was Bery Argan (the accused) — servant, and also founder and high priestess of the 'Krische-sama Is Adorable Church (militant fundamentalist sect; heretical cult),' who had been operating in secret as the shadow ruler of the Christand household.

The red-haired servant, well-versed in intrigue and cunning, who had won every political battle with silver-tongued skill, sat in a chair in the center of the room with her face downcast — the very picture of a prisoner awaiting sentencing.

Perhaps even her brilliant mind could not conceive of a reversal from this position — there was none of her usual commanding presence about her.

Before her:

Sitting on a desk with legs crossed — an utter breach of propriety — was Selene Christand (the inquisitor), chairwoman of the 'Christand Public Morals Improvement Council (membership: one; the chairwoman alone)' and Marshal of the Kingdom, holding a single piece of underwear spread before her eyes.

She studied the strange undergarment known as a garter belt at length — then glanced at Krische (the victim; post-dinner and content), who sat nearby sipping tea and observing how things unfolded in her capacity as chairwoman of the 'Post-Dinner Discussion Promotion Committee (powerless).'

Selene stared at her little sister's face, then closed her eyes.

Brandishing the garter belt before Bery, she spoke.

"Just what were you thinking, having Krische wear something like this?"

"For Krische-sama's sake, of course, Ojou-sama. What other reason could there possibly be?"

The incident had started after dinner.

The culprit was Krische, who had excitedly shown off the garter belt to Selene, gushing about how wonderfully comfortable the new undergarment felt.

The quality of the craftsmanship surely played a role.

The tailor was a third daughter of a great noble house — Arika Sazubern.

Being a female tailor, her designs were naturally excellent, but when it came to underwear and undergarments she prioritized comfort above all.

And that was precisely what Krische valued in clothing.

Furthermore, within Krische's mind there was an additional and overwhelmingly important evaluation factor — the 'Bery Bonus,' which was the fact that 'Bery had gone out of her way to order this specially for Krische.' Given that, her delight was only natural.

However, for Selene, the reality that her beloved little sister had suddenly begun happily showing off what appeared to be rather risqué underwear was impossible to accept.

Taking advantage of Krische's obedience, Bery was trying to lead her down an unwholesome path — confronting Bery along those lines was an equally inevitable development.

Arika Sazubern's name was well-known.

Her tailoring had originally not been a trade at all, but a hobby that began with her own outfits and accessories.

It had become the talk of high society among noble ladies, and her popularity was founded on designs with a slightly mature allure.

Indeed, the embroidery on this so-called garter belt was elegant.

There was no denying that the design and everything about it was quite beautiful, but presumably this undergarment's purpose was much the same — what one might call feminine allure.

There was a clear suggestion of intent to please the beholder.

To have Krische wear such a thing.

For Selene, who was usually on the losing end, this was the perfect ammunition to use against Bery.

"Really? I find that very hard to believe. How can you prove there are no personal, indecent desires behind this?"

"……? I'm quite at a loss as to why I'm being accused in this manner."

"You……are you really going to play dumb?"

"I'm not playing dumb at all. Isn't it Ojou-sama who is saying strange things?"

Bery raised her head and let a composed smile play across her lips.

"That is merely a waist wrap with stocking garters attached — a purely functional undergarment. Could it be that Ojou-sama sees something risqué in it?"

"……What?"

"I see. Perhaps in Ojou-sama's mind, this appears to be something quite risqué and indecent. ……To me, it looks no different from a corset — simply a support garment."

"……!"

*——You think it's risqué because you're the one looking at it that way, aren't you?*

Judge and judged — this was a single move that reversed their positions.

With just one move of brazen defiance, Bery Argan had transformed her own weakness into her opponent's.

"D-don't twist logic at me! You——"

"Logic-twisting, you say. I am merely stating what I think. And simply voicing my concern…… Ojou-sama seems to look at Krische-sama not as a sister, but as something more——"

"I am not looking at her that way, you fool! That's your thing, you coward! Always twisting logic and……!"

"Character attacks when cornered. ……The way you get so worked up only makes you more suspicious."

"You……!"

Bery let out a sigh as if exasperated.

And she was certain of her victory.

Selene, having seen this, would undoubtedly lash out at her.

That said, given how thoroughly satisfied Krische was, Selene could no longer ban her from wearing the underwear on the grounds that it looked too mature.

She was a Krische fundamentalist.

She did not want to make Krische sad, and the original purpose was simply a waist pad for the belt — just underwear.

It was not as though anyone would see it on her, and it felt wrong to refuse to use something that had been specially made just because it looked inappropriate.

Bery did feel bad for Selene, but she could no longer back down either.

"In this case, the one who ought to be judged is not me, but — with all due respect — Ojou-sama. Please, look within yourself. Which of us is truly being indecent——"

"……I see. So you're saying I'm the one being strange for thinking this is risqué."

"……?"

But Bery furrowed her brow at Selene's words.

All that remained was to talk the enraged Selene in circles — or so it should have been, but Selene, who had been on the verge of losing her temper, suddenly turned calm and fixed Bery with a level gaze.

"It doesn't look that way to you, and this is a perfectly normal undergarment?"

"Yes. ……As I have been saying all along."

Even as she answered, Bery's gaze wavered — something felt off.

The initiative she had been so sure she held had somehow returned to Selene's hands.

"……I see. Then Bery, perhaps I was wrong."

Selene smiled.

"But if it's such a fine thing, why not wear one yourself, rather than just Krische? You seem awfully fond of this underwear."

"……What?"

Bery froze.

It was a perfectly natural counterattack — to put it bluntly, Bery Argan had simply won too many times.

One-sidedly, utterly, she had won every political battle.

What existed within her was a kind of arrogance.

The assumption that Selene could never beat her in an argument.

Absolute confidence in her own abilities — like Krische, she was a person incapable of understanding how losers felt.

She had been complacent, assuming Selene had learned nothing from her many defeats.

But Selene had examined the reasons for those defeats, alone, over and over.

Seize the initiative through sophistry, strip the opponent of composure, then steer their thinking.

Bery's underhanded tactics always followed the same pattern — making the other party lose their cool so she could manipulate things to her liking.

What must one do to win? There was only one answer.

Do not be swayed by sophistry, do not lose your composure, and deliver a move the opponent never anticipated.

In this case, one simply had to ride the opponent's own logic.

"That's right. Someone like you would look wonderful in such mature underwear. It's a good opportunity — I think you should try your hand at modern fashion for once."

"I-I'm, well…… a servant, so……"

"'Because I'm a servant' — you don't need to hold back like that. Yes, as the head of the Christand household, perhaps I'll give one to you as a gift, in thanks for all your hard work."

Selene stood and approached Bery, holding the garter belt spread wide.

Bery groaned, face flushing red, eyes darting.

She was an endlessly attractive woman, but Selene also knew she was the sort who found displaying that kind of femininity more difficult than anyone.

"……Or what? Is there some reason you don't want to wear one? Surely you haven't been making Krische wear something you'd be too embarrassed to wear yourself?"

"Th-that's……"

"Of course not. Then there's no problem at all…… Krische, wouldn't you like it if you and Bery matched?"

The conversation was suddenly directed at Krische. She thought for a moment, then smiled.

"Ehehe, yes! Krische would like to match with Bery too."

"Uuu……"

In any case, Bery must have been unable to object in the face of Krische's delight and given up trying to persuade her.

Selene had already resigned herself to this matter being settled.

Regardless of anything, Krische liked it. It was too late.

But she could not allow the same thing to happen again, and more than anything, she could not stomach being talked into submission at Bery's whim.

Punish her, and strike back even once.

That alone was what Selene was after now.

*——With Krische wishing for it, Bery could never say no.*

This was her checkmate.

More a draw than a victory — but either way, Bery would also suffer for it.

Unlike Bery, who had aimed only for victory, Selene had given up on winning from the start — she had been thinking solely of making her opponent pay.

The result spoke for itself — perhaps not a win on points, but she had won the match.

Proof lay in Bery's expression, flushed and frustrated, eyes darting about.

Satisfaction filled Selene — however.

"Onee-sama, look at me! I'm matching too."

Wearing undergarments fitted with a garter belt.

The one who gleefully threw her arms around her elder sister was the leader of a third faction, the 'Matching with Onee-sama for Now Alliance (hedonist),' Kreschenta Alberan (the judge; post-dinner and content).

Paying no mind to Bery and Selene's trivial exchange, Kreschenta had Anne help her out of her dress and briskly donned the garter belt, looking thoroughly pleased.

Straddling Krische, she cheerfully showed off how she looked in it to her sister.

"You look great, Kreschenta. How do you like it?"

"It's not bad at all. Garter rings always squeeze the thigh in an awfully bothersome way."

The ring-like garters that held long stockings in place on the upper thigh were a somewhat inconvenient affair.

Loosen them and they served no purpose, yet tighten them and the thighs felt bound and constricted.

In that sense, the idea of suspending them from the waist was genuinely clever, and Kreschenta was honestly impressed.

The dangling garters were a slight nuisance, but that was unavoidable.

Above all, what mattered to Kreschenta was that she matched her sister.

"……Fufu, I'm so glad Kreschenta-sama is pleased with it as well."

"……You."

Bery looked at the two of them, closed her eyes for a moment, then spoke.

She had invited Kreschenta to bathe before Selene returned.

Naturally, this was to secure her as an ally.

Even Bery did not believe she could maintain the upper hand against Selene and Kreschenta simultaneously.

Take them on one at a time — she had begun her campaign in proper adherence to the fundamental principle of dividing the enemy's forces.

During the undressing, Kreschenta had naturally snapped at Bery upon seeing the garter belt Krische was happily showing off.

But with the winning card of a delighted Krische in hand, there was no way Bery could lose.

*'Actually, I received a sample for Her Majesty the Queen as well. What do you think? You would match Krische-sama. I'm sure Krische-sama would be so pleased. Why not try it on later?'*

Matching Krische. Krische will be happy.

These were the magic words to use against Kreschenta.

*'That's right, Kreschenta wears long stockings a lot too. Once you try them on, you'll probably like them. Ehehe, matching with Krische.'*

*'M-matching……'*

*'Yes. ……You don't want to match?'*

*'I-I don't not want to! If that's what Onee-sama says, then……'*

From there, she had flattered Kreschenta carefully so as not to sour her mood.

Maneuvering her into putting them on at this particular post-dinner timing was also entirely Bery's doing.

The battle is decided before it is fought.

Indeed, this seemingly spontaneous conflict had been, from start to finish, in the palm of this servant's hand.

Selene understood she had been played, and bit her lip.

But even so, she had no intention of conceding victory.

"……That doesn't mean you can escape this, Bery. I will make you wear one too, no matter what — that I swear."

"I suppose there's no helping it. The situation has taken a turn for the worse. I shall resist no further."

Had Kreschenta's entrance come a little sooner, she might have been able to smooth-talk her way through again.

But everything had come too late, Bery sighed.

There must have been some contempt for Selene within her, she realized.

"But, if both Krische-sama and Kreschenta-sama are wearing them, and now myself as well……"

"……!"

Understanding this, what appeared in Bery's eyes was wicked delight.

"Surely the head of the Christand household wouldn't be the only one not wearing one…… would she?"

"Wh-why do I have to as well……"

"In this situation, is there perhaps some objection? Could it be that Ojou-sama has some complaint about this undergarment? Some reason she doesn't want to wear it?"

*Returning your own words,* Bery let a smile full of malice spread across her face.

When she couldn't win, scorched earth.

She might not win on points, but she would die before losing the match.

The graceful, intelligent lady was a façade — Bery Argan was, at her core, a hopeless woman.

"Krische-sama, what do you think? At this point, why not have everyone matching?"

The answer to that question would undoubtedly be spoken in the affirmative.

All Selene could do was listen in stunned silence and glare at Bery in frustration.

Anne Giterns (the spectator), clutching Kreschenta's dress, watched this trivial battle with bated breath.

With a war looming and the specter of national ruin before them —

The Christand household was at peace.

"And another thing, you always——"

"O-Ojou-sama, that's enough already…… I understand."

Night had fallen, and Krische and Kreschenta were already asleep in bed, embracing each other.

Anne had been sent back to her room, and only Bery and Selene remained awake.

The golden-haired young lady raised her brows, drinking Bogan's liquor while glaring at Bery and laying into her.

"You don't understand. Always trying to deflect like that……"

Bery had already changed into her nightgown, and was now undressing Selene and changing her as well.

Even through it all, Selene continued her nagging.

*Can't be helped,* Bery thought with a rueful smile.

Unable to contain her frustration, Selene had gone to bathe and returned with two bottles of liquor, and had been drinking since — the collateral victims Krische and Kreschenta had been permitted an early retreat to sleep, but Bery, naturally, was being kept up like this.

Selene grabbed the bottle and poured into Bery's empty glass, setting it before her with a thud.

"Don't you dare try to run, Bery. You can't refuse your master's drink, can you?"

"I really don't do well with strong liquor like this……"

"Drink."

Bery sighed and accepted the glass.

Even one glass had been quite something; her face felt like it was on fire.

One of the bottles Selene had brought was a northern distilled spirit that Bogan had favored.

Apparently it was produced in an island nation to the north of Arna, where it was drunk daily.

The amber liquid had been flavored with herbs. Pierim, perhaps — it had a sweet fragrance.

In that regard, being able to enjoy the aroma made liquor not a bad thing, but even the smallest sip sent the alcohol searing down her throat.

She pressed a hand to her mouth and looked at Selene helplessly.

"It really is strong. If possible, I'd rather have that one……"

She pointed to the wine on the shelf.

It was a nightcap kept on hand for sleepless nights.

Selene flatly refused.

"I want to see you actually drunk for once. You're always the same as ever. 'Because I'm a servant,' pulling back a step, putting on that act."

"That really isn't the case……"

"That's how it looks."

Selene stood and settled into the sofa.

Then she patted the spot beside her.

Bery sighed, but complied and sat down next to her.

Selene laughed happily and leaned against her shoulder.

"I absolutely hate that grown-up act of yours."

"My. Fufu, but I am in fact older than Ojou-sama, so……"

"That's exactly what bothers me. What kind of face do you make when you say things like that?"

Selene took Bery's cheeks between both hands and stared into her face.

Her gaze drifted awkwardly — and that face, no matter when one looked, seemed like a girl's, lovely and yet somehow childlike despite its beauty.

Selene pouted.

"If you and I just stood there silently, people would absolutely think I was the older one."

"It's only appearances. I'm sure in another ten years or so I'll look a little more……"

"I wonder. Something tells me you'll stay exactly as you are. There are famous cases of people whose looks don't change even at fifty."

Selene said it with displeasure and let go, sinking deeper into the sofa.

"And it'll be me who ages while you stay the same. What a raw deal."

"Being blamed for something imaginary……"

"No, I'm sure that's exactly what will happen. Shouldn't you apologize in advance for the trouble you'll cause me?"

"Haa…… I'm sorry."

"Fufu, I forgive you."

She cheerfully linked her arm with Bery's and rested her head on her shoulder.

"……I'll have to make sure you don't waste that apology."

She murmured that softly.

Bery reached for Selene's head and stroked her hair as though combing through it.

"Did something happen?"

"……Nothing. The strategy meeting went smoothly, everything's running fine…… peaceful enough that I can sit here and chat about nothing with you. Days are full, smooth sailing — a happy life, I suppose."

She squeezed the arm she held and pressed her cheek against the shoulder.

"But maybe that's exactly why. ……I keep thinking about whether I've overlooked something, whether I've really done everything I should've."

"……I see."

A fifteen-year-old girl, Marshal of the Kingdom.

It had been decided in the flow of events, an unavoidable inevitability.

But still, the burden was far too great.

Bery turned to face her and drew the slender body into an embrace.

"Realistically speaking, we have no recourse. It's a hopeless situation — our only lifeline is Krische. No sane strategy exists for this. We're asking for three unwinnable battles against more than double our numbers. Even an amateur would know it's impossible."

Selene pressed her face against Bery's chest and exhaled.

"And my job is to dress it up like some perfectly crafted strategy, hand it off, and sit in the back looking important. I'm sick of my own incompetence."

"Faren-sama and everyone else all racked their brains to reach this conclusion, didn't they? Then it must be the best answer…… it isn't Ojou-sama's fault."

"……Even so, I can't help thinking — was there something more? Something else I could have done……"

Rubbing her back, closing her eyes.

Bery smiled softly.

"……What I think is this."

"……What?"

"Trying too hard to act grown-up never ends well — that would be my conclusion."

And with that she lifted Selene in her arms and stood.

Selene gaped up at her, eyes glistening, and Bery laughed, carrying her to the bed where the other two slept.

"From tonight, Ojou-sama will be joining us."

"What's that supposed to mean……"

Bery giggled and settled in beside Selene.

She drew her into an embrace.

"See? At the very least, this should bring a little peace of mind."

"……Are you making fun of me?"

"Not at all. I recently realized myself that thinking alone is no good, so I simply felt I ought to pass the lesson on."

Selene lifted her face from Bery's bosom — a girl's sulking expression.

Bery stroked her cheek and smiled.

"And it seems to me that Ojou-sama needs some time where she doesn't have to act grown-up. ……You're welcome to be as spoiled as you like, without thinking about anything at all."

Selene glared at Bery, and creased her brow in a show of pique. "Honestly."

"How long do you intend to keep treating me like a child?"

"Fufu, to me, you'll always be Ojou-sama."

"I'm not some fool like Krische. If that's how you're going to be, maybe I ought to do something to make sure you can never treat me like a child again."

Selene grinned mischievously and reached for Bery's chest.

Bery shyly hid her chest with her arms, then pressed her forehead against Selene's.

"Honestly, I'm going to have to stop letting Ojou-sama drink from now on. What dreadful drinking habits — I really must be the one to keep an eye on you."

"……It's because you belittle me, Bery. If you keep up that attitude, I'll become a nasty drunk every single time. ……And I saw through your little trick of changing the subject to get away from my drink."

"You noticed?"

"Obviously, don't be ridiculous. Honestly, who do you think you are?"

She giggled and clung to Bery, closing her eyes.

"……But, fufu. That side of you — I love it."

Selene whispered.

"I know. ……Good night, Ojou-sama."

"……Mm. Good night."

Bery, too, closed her eyes with a contented smile.

And so the two of them fell asleep in each other's arms.

# Chapter 172: The One Who Bares Fangs at Reason

Clear skies as far as the eye could see, and a warm breeze hinting at spring.

The south of the kingdom — the Milhirea Plain.

The invasion army assembled in the Galshan Republic against Alberan numbered over 120,000 in total.

And for this decisive battle — the engagement that would determine the outcome of the entire grand invasion — some 100,000 soldiers had been gathered.

Even surveying the history of Galshan from its days as a kingdom, this was likely the greatest number of participants ever committed to a single engagement.

The opposing lines — the Alberan army deployed one ri ahead — clearly numbered no more than 50,000.

A great army, certainly, but the disparity was plain.

Rather than anchoring their rear, Alberan had drawn up its battle line with its left flank resting on the walled city of Nakria, preventing an envelopment from the Galshan right, which enjoyed overwhelming numerical superiority.

But on their far left, 20,000 Galshan soldiers faced no enemy directly, free to act as they pleased.

Flanking maneuvers, attacks from the side — the moment battle began, Alberan's right wing would be threatened not just from the front but from the flank and rear as well.

It was nothing more than a half-measure.

Even if they had forced the flanking to one wing, with Alberan's inferior numbers — they could not hold for long.

Moreover, Nakria likely held only the bare minimum garrison needed for defense.

This deployment was wholly defensive — a stationary city could hardly mount a counterattack. The enemy had effectively abandoned one wing entirely.

A right wing being pressed, and a left wing unable to move.

Alberan would inevitably be forced to attempt a frontal breakthrough, and that was impossible.

"The kingdom seems to have steeled its resolve, Captain Bilkees."

Addressed by his young adjutant, Gal Bilkees — captain of the Galshan elite sand-lizard cavalry — nodded.

From within a helm shaped like a dragon's open maw — the fangs lining top and bottom — he fixed his sharp gaze on the Alberan soldiers directly ahead.

"Yes. They've come this far without fleeing — the kingdom's soldiers are warriors worthy of respect, Elke."

The beast he rode was a creature best described as a giant lizard.

The sand lizard — also called a land dragon — was encased in a natural armor of hard, tawny-brown hide, with great fangs, claws, and a massive tail — a fearsome predator.

Its legs could not match a horse or camel, but its combat prowess more than compensated; at the very least, this cavalry had never lost a head-on engagement.

No — the Galshan military was that sort of force to begin with.

By taming and wielding beasts that surpassed men, they held direct, head-on destruction of the enemy as the supreme art of war.

Against cavalry, they loosed camels — which horses feared.

Against skirmishers, they unleashed swift war hounds; against heavy infantry, boulder-like unicorns.

Countless beasts, their power wielded to the fullest to overwhelm any army.

"Considering the number of beasts we're fielding…… yes. Had the numbers been reversed, it might have been a fine, even fight as usual."

Since Galshan had begun employing beasts, it had never been bested on open ground against equal or lesser numbers.

Dagreen Nakul Dougarinea Gahka.

The fierce commander of Alberan who had repelled Galshan's spear time and again understood this, and had always assembled forces exceeding theirs before facing Galshan in battle.

That was how he had continued turning back their invasions — but this time was different.

"Call it a doomed nation's fate. No matter how brilliant the general, one cannot undo a diplomatic defeat."

Elsren, Elderant.

This time, a temporary alliance had been forged between the two great powers bordering Alberan and Galshan.

The greatest difficulty in any invasion is not destroying the enemy, but defending the homeland.

If one simply wanted to win a battle, sending enough force to break through the enemy's defenses would suffice.

What mattered was securing one's own body while thrusting the spear — how to guard against invasion from other nations. And an alliance allowed the forces normally spread across national defense to be freely committed to battle, just as they were now.

The advantage of deploying defensive forces directly as invasion forces was something even a child with no knowledge of strategy could understand.

If two attack one, who wins?

That was not strategy but the laws of nature — one simple, incontrovertible truth of the battlefield.

War, of course, was not so simple.

It was not unheard of for a single genius to defeat an incompetent opponent with superior numbers, but the Galshan army was led by Olgan — the war-god who had been born to royalty yet stood with the people and overthrown the old order.

Defeat was inconceivable. What was about to begin was a foregone conclusion.

Gal looked to the left.

At the forefront of the Galshan right wing's battle line where Gal was positioned stood sixty magnificent war elephants, each with a shoulder height of twenty shaku.

Enormous beasts with long trunks and tusks — but one among them, positioned toward the left, was exceptional.

Its skin, naturally gray, was stained blue, and its body was perhaps twice the size of a normal elephant.

It was nothing less than a moving fortress, and its enormous legs would not kick a horse aside but crush it underfoot.

A single sweep of its long trunk would scatter armored warriors like chaff — before this creature, battle lines were meaningless.

Alkazaris — a magical beast born by chance during Galshan's breeding program.

The monster named 'The One Who Tramples All' carried an enormous turret and soldiers upon its back, its eyes regarding everything before it as though they were pebbles.

"……Regardless, this will be the last battle with Alberan."

"Indeed. ……To participate in this engagement at your side — I consider it the highest honor."

"Hah, you sound like someone heading to their death."

Gal chuckled at that, and just then —

A murmur rose from the soldiers. Gal followed their gazes.

Riding atop an elephant, heading from their position toward the center, were Olgan and his right hand, Zalvarg of the Twin Blades.

And coming from the other direction, mounted on what looked like a puppy compared to the elephant, was Dagreen Gahka.

And beside him——

"……Is that a jade tiger?"

"It appears so to me, but……"

The jade-green coat — a body enormous even for a tiger.

A long tail swaying lazily side to side as it walked at a leisurely pace — it was a jade tiger, and seated upon it sidesaddle was a girl.

Silver hair, a black cloak.

Even at a distance, the figure was clearly a girl — and she sat atop a magical beast said to never be tamed by humans.

"……The kingdom's Alberinea. I had heard that the mount of Krische Christand was a jade tiger, but it seems it was no jest."

Gal growled the words, narrowing his eyes.

The reinforcements from the central army, commanding Alberan's forces alongside Dagreen Gahka.

The cursed child of the royal house, draped in countless rumors.

An extraordinary genius and an aberration — word of her had reached even distant Galshan.

She had easily defeated the undefeated General Aulgorn Hilkintos, personally slain the Black Lion Gildanstein to end the civil war — a beautiful girl yet masterful with the blade, her tally of severed heads beyond counting.

The very reason the invasion of Alberan had been accelerated was said to be her — the one who had forged a pact with the ancient dragon Yagelnaus and invited it to the capital.

Every last tale sounded dubious, but presented with such an imposing sight, he could not dismiss those rumors as entirely false.

Gal was not so young as to underestimate someone based on appearance.

He took pride in his honed body and technique — yet he also knew full well that there were plenty of youths whose talent exceeded his own.

It was an instinctive thing.

What he felt upon seeing the distant figure of the girl was fear — fear of the unknowable.

At the very least, his instincts told him this was no ordinary opponent.

The Alberan army had placed her in the center, ahead of the renowned Dagreen who had repelled Galshan's invasions time and again.

Whether for political reasons or some tactical design, the prevailing view within the Galshan army was that she could hardly pose as great a threat as Dagreen.

For that reason, voices had been loudest insisting that the center facing Krische Christand should merely apply pressure while the overwhelmingly superior left wing broke through the Alberan right to cut down Dagreen early.

Dagreen Gahka was a bitter rival to Galshan and to their supreme commander Olgan alike.

Even if tactically imperfect, there were things a warrior must prioritize above tactics.

Olgan had told his soldiers that this battle would also serve as the final chapter in their long conflict with Alberan.

If tactical logic aligned with that sentiment, there was no reason not to choose that path — and yet, Supreme Commander Olgan had placed the greater emphasis not on Dagreen, with whom he had crossed blades countless times, but on Alberinea.

That alien quality, the unsettling air she exuded, the inscrutable decision of his lord — all of it told Gal that Krische Christand was not to be underestimated.

"……Elke, change of plans. Prioritize breaking through the enemy."

"Breaking through, sir?"

"Break through. Call it nothing more than instinct, but something tells me it's unwise to lock horns with that sort of opponent. Pass the word to the banner-bearer."

"Yes, sir."

The sand-lizard cavalry that would charge at the very forefront alongside the elephants had been granted a measure of discretion; their role was fundamentally to support and substitute for the elephant corps.

They were to slip into the gaps in the enemy line torn open by the charging elephants — disrupting, or expanding the breach.

With their overwhelming numerical advantage, he had assumed that simply disrupting the enemy line would suffice.

But against this opponent, that felt far too passive.

Something that dwelt only in those who had spent long years on the battlefield settled over Gal, compelling him to issue orders.

Why had the enemy chosen an open-field decisive battle instead of a siege against twice their numbers?

He had assumed they had some trick up their sleeve, but dismissed it as the death throes of a cornered foe — and yet, could he truly be so sure?

An alarm was sounding in Gal's heart, warning that a misstep could bring devastation beyond anything they anticipated.

In the center, both commanders had finished their parley and were returning to their respective lines.

Rather than Dagreen, who was rallying his men at the front, Gal kept his eyes on the girl.

She gave no sign of issuing orders — simply sat atop the jade tiger, passing before her soldiers — and then, from the ranks, a banner bearing a crescent moon and skull was suddenly raised.

Several soldiers in black-lacquered armor appeared and moved to her side, handing her a single spear.

"……Brave. She means to slay Alkazaris herself."

"It seems like recklessness to me."

No one could look upon Alkazaris's bulk and feel no fear.

Its resilient hide repelled not only arrows but even throwing spears with ease.

Its footsteps shook the earth; at full gallop it outran any horse.

Before the enormity of Alkazaris, human beings were nothing more than insects.

Even so, she meant to take the vanguard — to embolden the soldiers trembling behind her.

"Recklessness though it may be, it is bravery deserving of praise. I hear she is remarkably young, yet she understands war well."

War is morale.

The will to fight transforms frightened individuals into soldiers — into functioning parts of a military organism.

Every tactic and technique rests on that foundation; morale is the bedrock of everything in battle.

Against more than twice their number and enormous beasts — no words, however fine, could do more than pour water on a hot stone.

Soldiers who have been forced into an 'unwilling readiness for death' are brittle things.

The moment they are put on the back foot, they will shatter with ease.

In such a situation, what is demanded of a commander is not deep intellect or tactical insight.

It is the kind of courage that makes soldiers think: *For this leader, I would——*

Whether or not the attempt would succeed, her actions were without question the most praiseworthy thing a general could do.

And her appearance was effective.

Without a helm, her long silver hair streaming — displaying her girlish appearance for all to see was surely meant to impress upon every soldier that she was someone to be protected.

He had wondered what manner of judgment led her to stand on a battlefield in nothing but a cloak, but for the purpose of rallying troops, it was not a bad choice.

She wielded herself as a symbol to command her army.

"She must have some confidence in her swordsmanship and spearwork. The attempt itself isn't bad. ……Though that's only if she survives the front line and manages to retreat to the rear."

"Indeed. If she seriously intends to charge Alkazaris, then from the flank——"

The girl moved the instant Gal was about to finish speaking.

One ri separated the armies — and from that distance, the girl stepped forward and accelerated.

She carried the spear as though shouldering it.

What she was about to do was comprehensible — yet its purpose was not.

What possible use was a thrown spear from that distance? Gal and everyone watching furrowed their brows——

"——!?"

A beat later, what resounded across the battlefield was the sound of shattering.

How could one describe that noise?

Like a siege engine pulverizing a castle wall — or like an overripe fruit bursting.

After a moment, a dull, rain-like patter filled the air.

Red rain, laced with solid matter, staining the earth in mottled patches.

For an instant, Gal's eyes caught something — something leaving the girl's hand from one ri away.

Half-understanding yet unable to accept it, he raised his gaze — hesitantly, fearfully — to the left, toward Alkazaris.

Voluptuous, vivid, drenched in blood — a flower of flesh.

The white gleam was surely skull. Blood and meat spilled from within it.

From the center, like a pistil, the thick shaft of a spear jutted upward.

The long, massive trunk and tusks, and that enormous body — these alone still hinted at what it had once been, but it was plain to every eye that it would never move again.

The grotesque anomaly that had trampled all things fell without ever entering battle, tilting and collapsing like a puppet with its strings cut.

The earth shook, and with the rumble came screams — surely from the soldiers crushed beneath it.

Countless cries echoed across the field.

Gal turned his gaze, slowly, like a rusted mechanism, back to the girl.

The girl had leapt back onto the jade tiger — showing neither joy nor pride.

As if she had merely finished a simple chore, she had already turned her back on them.

The accompanying standard-bearer waved the banner wider, and the soldiers beside her raised their voices.

After a moment of silence — what erupted from the enemy lines was a roar that split the sky.

In stark contrast to the speechless Galshan soldiers, the Alberan troops howled with enough fervor to cow the beasts themselves, raising their spears high and thrusting them toward the girl as though in offering.

And through the path made by those soldiers, the girl rode on as if nothing had happened, and vanished.

Every Galshan soldier who had not known her understood in that instant.

One and only one beneath heaven, she who bent and trampled reason — and was permitted to do so.

"……That is Krische Christand."

——She was the Alberinea the kingdom held aloft with pride.

A monster in human form, who bared her fangs at the very laws of nature.

# Chapter 175: The Gambling Warrior and the Card Sharp

——The Kingdom of Alberan is exploiting the divine being for purposes of aggression.

That was the justification given for the three-nation joint invasion, originally expected around summer due to consultations over the new queen's accession, now launched in spring as soon as the snow melted.

The Sacred Convention included the principle of not raising arms against dragons, but the regulations pertaining to dragons were manifold — among them, clauses forbidding the monopolization of their power and its use for national interest.

Tribute offered through faith was permitted as a special exception, but soliciting a dragon's direct aid was a clear violation.

The visit of the divine being Yagelnaus to the capital Albernaria was loudly proclaimed by the three nations as evidence of Alberan's violation, and they broadcast their own righteousness far and wide.

Envoys from each nation arrived even in the Arna Empire — Alberan's ally — urging them to punish Alberan for violating the Sacred Convention. High Priest Zarnalibea traveled to the capital to confirm the facts, but——

*'It was the result of a personal audience with Onee-sama as an individual, not as a representative of the nation. There has been no violation of the Sacred Convention. And regardless — Onee-sama has become the divine being's covenant partner. Surely Arna does not intend to raise a blade against such a person?'*

*'However……'*

*'If Alberan dies, Arna is next. It will inevitably come to that. Fufu, we are in the same boat, after all. Please interpret everything favorably at this point. ……Of course, I do feel apologetic toward the Arna Empire.'*

——In any case, it was all a fait accompli.

The claim that what an individual had done was unrelated to the kingdom hardly held, given Krische's position as a member of the royal house — yet officially, Krische was treated not as royalty but as the adopted daughter of the late Margrave Christand.

The fact that she had never been formally reinstated as royalty proved useful here.

Naturally, Arna could have demanded punishment for Krische as an individual — but she was now the dragon's covenant partner.

For a nation built on faith in divine beings, demanding punishment of such a person was unthinkable; from a religious standpoint, Krische stood above even the Priestess Princess.

They could hardly pass judgment on the silver-haired girl who sipped her tea and watched everything unfold with an utterly carefree expression — Krische, who looked as though she hadn't a thought in her head.

Moreover, Alberan was Arna's shield.

Kreschenta's argument was sound, and——

*'……Haa. Well, what's done is done. Let us simply pray that Her Majesty does not forget her words of apology.'*

——they had no choice but to accept the kingdom's position in full.

Zarnalibea, soothed by Bery's sweet cookies, nevertheless spent the following days running around Arna with a gnawing stomach, bowing to the priests.

The enemy's goal was to sow discord.

They had surely not expected such minor maneuvering to actually detach Arna.

In the end, their machinations produced nothing more than a trivial discontent within Arna — no significant impact.

If this war dragged on, such seeds might eventually sprout, but the kingdom had no intention of letting that happen.

——A short, decisive war.

That was all the kingdom had its eyes on, and inevitably, the three invading nations would be forced into the same approach.

The massive armies being assembled by each nation could not be hidden.

Reading their movements through spies, Kreschenta issued an early proclamation throughout the realm, calling for civilians to evacuate to the kingdom's central region.

The primary focus was the people of settlements and villages that fell outside the Sacred Convention's protection — those, in other words, who would be subject to plunder — and the state even dispatched wagons for their transport.

Kreschenta, of course, did not possess a shred of goodwill — but it was a rare humanitarian measure.

All the more reason it earned tremendous goodwill from civilians who had lacked the means to flee, elevating the reputation of the great young queen.

Though the internal war had once again required the kingdom to hemorrhage a tremendous sum, what the kingdom needed now was not gold but soldiers — and converting those evacuees into soldiers was easy. The righteous cause of fighting for their great young queen, to reclaim their homelands from the three vile nations, was what stirred their hearts above all.

And stripping these people from their villages served a purpose beyond merely creating soldiers — above all, it was meant to demoralize the enemy.

Brutal marches, grueling training, a life spent cheek-by-jowl with death.

For soldiers enduring such daily torment, violently taking women and plundering wealth was the ultimate stress relief.

Strip them of that in advance, and the soldiers' frustration would explode — and they would have no means to vent it.

It was a matter of army size.

That all three nations would assemble massive forces was self-evident, but sating the appetites of such masses required a certain density of villages and settlements.

Cities of any significant population were, under the Sacred Convention, nominally protected from plunder.

One could hire prostitutes through commerce, but the numbers available from cities were pitifully limited — and even if prostitutes were brought from the home country for the soldiers' comfort, the sheer scale of the invasion force made that impractical.

A great army inevitably burdens its logistics with countless problems.

Here, the enemy's use of the Sacred Convention as their casus belli would backfire.

They were attacking Alberan for violating the Sacred Convention.

They could hardly violate it themselves.

They would need to behave with even more 'propriety' than usual to maintain their pretext, and inevitably, that restraint would massively inflate their soldiers' frustration — evacuating the villages and settlements was done precisely for this reason.

The enemy soldiers would suffer in the occupied land, unable to sate their desires, drowning in the pretense demanded by their masters.

And that frustration would inevitably drive the soldiers to demand the advance on the kingdom's heartland.

*Once we reach the center, women and gold are ours for the taking* — the officers would try to pacify them with such promises.

That would naturally push their thinking toward the short-sighted, tilting their calculations toward a decisive engagement.

A massive, frustrated army — a war of attrition was not an option.

That was the opening the kingdom would exploit.

"This is my first time riding alongside General Gahka."

"Oh? You too, Nyan-nyan?"

"Indeed. North and south — we've fought in the same wars in the east and west, but never stood on the same battlefield."

Listening to *Nyan-nyan* with an air of resignation, Kokys — mounted on his horse — spoke to Krische, who sat sidesaddle on the jade tiger.

The marching column headed south.

Surveying the wide, unthreatening plains, Kokys stroked his chin in thought.

"From what I gather, rather than someone who employs intricate tactics like Faren-dono or Bogan-sama, he seems the type who prefers head-on battle…… that is, probably similar in temperament to me. Our views tended to align when we talked."

Krische sifted through her mental records and nodded.

"That's what Krische thought too. The records weren't very detailed, so Krische couldn't quite pin down what sort of person he was…… Krische only spoke with him briefly."

Her mind held the records of nearly every past battle, but records were just records.

While the royal archives contained a great many, the quality varied enormously by author.

Some were lavished with excessive embellishment; others were so terse as to note only that *such-and-such numbers fought, and the right wing prevailed* — making it difficult to reconstruct the actual situation.

And the records pertaining to Dagreen Gahka were overwhelmingly of the latter sort.

She had formed a rough picture from the circumstances described and from their brief conversation at the victory ceremony, but it appeared her assessment was not wrong, Krische mused.

Strong in frontal battle, but less adept at indirect approaches.

Somewhat heavy-handed on offense, yet never making critical errors — suggesting a good eye for opportunity.

His record was not undefeated, but even in his losses he had minimized damage, never suffering a catastrophic blow.

On the whole, a general of dependable stability.

"In that case, can we count on the quality of his soldiers?"

"He's a man who has fought on the front line for many years…… on that point, we can trust him. Above all, I hear he commands the deep respect of his men. They will be brave, without question."

"About as much as the Second Corps?"

"Most likely."

The quality of soldiers mattered enormously.

In wargaming or staff exercises, for example, Kokys was probably on the weaker side.

But in actual battle, the Second Corps was truly formidable — Corps Commander Kokys's leadership included, they wielded a power that defied simple arithmetic.

Dagreen, too, was likely a general who excelled at precisely that.

Field the strongest soldiers and draw out their maximum potential.

It was a difference of approach.

Tactics, by definition, are the art of striking the enemy's weak points — in essence, a way of fighting that diminishes the enemy's strength.

But someone like Kokys always maneuvered his troops so that *his own* forces could perform at their absolute peak.

The difference between aiming at 30 with a force of 60, and aiming at 100 with a force of 120, perhaps.

That was why Kokys sometimes launched what appeared to be reckless head-on assaults against the enemy's main body.

Naturally, such charges sometimes caused unnecessary casualties — but they could also catch the enemy off guard and produce unexpected, sweeping successes. Therein lay the difficulty of tactics.

Whether to detour to a distant bridge to cross a raging river, or to swim straight across.

Depending on the circumstances, that way of thinking was not without merit.

"Well, it's reassuring that they seem reasonably strong. The enemy force is predicted to exceed 100,000, I hear. Nyan-nyan, where would you like to be positioned?"

"Why, for a grand battle of this scale — if I might choose, dead center."

"Perfect. Krische was hoping to put Nyan-nyan in the center too."

The dead center against an army of 100,000 — and he was happy to volunteer. The man was insane.

The walled city of Nakria, the intended battlefield, sat on the Milhirea Plain, flanked to the west by a great forest and to the east by mountains.

The central army had committed 35,000 troops, but in practice only the 15,000 under Alberinea's direct command were usable.

The reason was the western forest.

There was a significant risk that Galshan would infiltrate forces through it to circle behind them.

A substantial reserve had to be stationed in the rear to prevent this, requiring 20,000 troops placed under the central general for that defense.

And Dagreen's forces reportedly numbered just over 30,000 — combined, their side had roughly 50,000 at best.

A great army, certainly — but the enemy exceeded 100,000. Literally an order of magnitude apart.

The notion of volunteering for the center against that, and being pleased about it, was incomprehensible.

The soldiers gathered each night, fired up about fighting for their country and their families back home, but that was nothing more than a veil over their fear.

Even among the Christand troops, reputed as the kingdom's finest, everyone was on edge.

That Kokys could laugh in this situation was simply abnormal — to Mia, it only reinforced the sense that they were a different species.

"A deputy commander who wears her feelings on her face. Shameful."

"Ugh."

*Am I really going to survive this?* As she thought that, Dagra — beside her — rapped her on the helm.

"You're oddly brazen yet cowardly — I can't make heads or tails of you."

"Uuu, I-I'm not brazen……"

"Don't talk back to your superior. That right there is what I call brazen, you fool."

"Gah."

Another rap from his gauntlet. She looked up at Dagra, displeasure plain on her face.

Dagra gave a rueful chuckle and sighed. "Honestly."

"We do what needs doing. That's all. Do you really think Krische-sama would fight a battle she couldn't win?"

"……I don't think so, but."

*He didn't have to hit me twice.*

She answered with a hint of petulance, and Kalua, who had been watching, laughed and grabbed Mia's shoulder.

"Captain, it seems Vice-Captain Mia still has complaints. That face is saying 'He didn't have to hit me twice' in her head."

"Kalua! I-I was not thinking that!"

"……Haa, you two, really."

Dagra rubbed his brow in deeper exasperation. Noticing, Kokys let out a hearty laugh.

"Better than looking down. Don't be too hard on her, Dagra."

"Sir. My apologies for the disgraceful display……"

"Think nothing of it. Your name is Mia, yes — the adjutant?"

"Y-yes, sir! Corps Commander Agrand!"

Mia snapped to attention, and Kokys chuckled and raised a hand.

"If each soldier kills two, it's over. You and yours have proven you can cut down ten apiece in a single battle — what is there to fear?"

"Huh? W-well……"

"No matter how vast the army, you only face the enemies directly in front of you. It's not as though 100,000 will rush you all at once. Surely you understand that much."

The logic was absurd.

And yet, the reasoning was technically sound — Krische had drilled this into her until she was sick of hearing it.

"At company scale, engaging two or three hundred is perfectly normal in mountain warfare, even for an ordinary unit. Disadvantageous, certainly, but remember what you did at Bernaich with Krische-sama. In raw numbers, you were outnumbered far worse — dozens of times over — and yet you fought through."

"Y-yes…… that's true."

"The situation is no different. The objective then was delaying enemy movement; the objective now is destroying the enemy command. Only the objective has changed — what the individual soldier must do remains the same: win locally and maintain local advantage. That single point."

Kokys tapped his own head.

"Think simply. You don't need to worry about what comes next. Your commanders will figure out how to turn your immediate successes into victory. That's what an army is. ……And your commander is the kingdom's Alberinea — where is there room for doubt?"

Then Kokys glanced to the side — where Krische, having grown bored of sitting, was stifling a yawn and pretending to groom herself while trying to lie down atop the jade tiger.

The sight was the polar opposite of the speech's content — not an ounce of tension or seriousness.

Kokys felt an indescribable emotion, and cleared his throat.

"Well, the, ah…… point being. There's no need to fret so much. It's not as though we need to slaughter all 100,000. The number we'll actually fight is modest."

"Yes. Thank you very much……"

Glancing at the Alberinea who remained utterly ordinary despite the coming decisive battle, Mia managed a nod.

She certainly could not imagine this girl picking a fight she would lose.

*Sacrifice myself for glory* — she surely harbored no such noble, aristocratic conviction.

She meant to win, and even in these circumstances, she did not doubt it.

"Mia really is a worrywart. You should learn from Nyan-nyan."

"……Y-yes."

Sprawled on the jade tiger, only her face turned their way, Krische spoke.

A languid, unmotivated posture, as far from valiant as one could imagine.

Even knowing her strength, it was a sight that stirred vague unease about whether things were truly going to be all right.

"Same as always — the plan is simple. The objective is what Nyan-nyan said: destroy the enemy command. If things go well, the battle will be over in half a day."

"……Half a day?"

"Yes. If everything meshes properly, that's the end. Krische wins."

And lying there as she was, she declared it.

Kokys alone wore a deeply entertained smile, and nodded.

The walled city of Nakria, situated in the kingdom's south, lay between the great Bilfe Forest to the west and the rugged Kalbyas Mountain Range to the east — the southern gate to the capital.

Aside from dispatching garrison forces to various cities, all three Galshan army groups were marching straight here.

Jewel-like violet eyes, looking straight ahead.

She smiled with a troubled expression.
