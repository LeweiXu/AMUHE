# Chapter 251: Wish

Minimal furnishings, then books and a desk――just a few pieces of furniture, and a canopied bed.

An old woman sat in a chair by the window, narrowing her eyes against the sunlight streaming in from outside as she looked over a bundle of documents.

Her features were beautiful even in old age, and possessed a certain grace.

Her thin, gaunt body was hard to imagine as that of the kingdom's highest-ranking military officer――the former Marshal who had once commanded the entire continental army, yet something of that gallantry remained.

Golden hair streaked with white, faded of pigment, now closer to silver.

Her face was perfectly serene, having fully accepted even the death that loomed nearby.

A knock sounded at the door of the room where she sat, and she said with a smile, "Come in."

Guided by that voice――clear despite its rasp――a girl peeked in.

Silver hair tied in twin tails, wearing an apron dress.

A teapot and cookies loaded on a tray, the girl gazed at the old woman, and――

"Krische is back, Selene."

Her voice rang out like a tinkling bell as she called the old woman's name.

"Welcome back, Krische. That was quick."

Selene Christand looked at the cookies she'd brought and smiled wryly; Krische, for her part, toddled over to her side.

Setting the cookies and the pot on the table, she leaned in close and kissed her.

"……It happens every day, but still. I never imagined I'd still be getting kissed looking this wrinkly."

Selene Christand said it in an exasperated tone, reacting to the greeting that had never changed, even now.

"Ehehe, Selene is Selene, after all."

Happily, with a girl's smile, Krische answered as though it were the most natural thing in the world.

"……Honestly, you never change, you little fool."

She pinched those cheeks, then told Krische she'd like it straight today.

"Certainly, madam," Krische answered, playing the part of a servant.

The quiet sound of tea being poured filled the room, and Selene listened to it while gazing out the window.

For a while, an unnatural silence filled the room.

Birdsong, the rustle of curtains.

Cool wind drifted in through the window――and then, softly, Selene spoke.

"The eternity you wish for is something beyond all measure."

Her voice was slightly hoarse, yet clear.

"It sounds wonderful, and very beautiful. Perhaps a peaceful, tranquil utopia could indeed be called a paradise. ……But stability means refusing change."

*――That is not a way for people to live,* Selene continued.

"Both you, and Kreschenta, and everyone else, too. Don't you think we've all changed a great deal? Compared to when we first met."

"……Yes. Krische thinks maybe we have changed."

"Because so many things happened. Joyful things, and fun things, and sad things, and painful things…… I think that's what it means to live."

The old woman turned her gaze to the girl.

Judging by the difference in their appearances, they could have been grandmother and granddaughter.

"Experiencing all sorts of things, learning, overcoming――encountering something new and changing because of it. It's normal for the heart to change, and in a sense, eternal stability may be the same thing as death."

She spoke as though to advise, as though to instruct.

"The same food every day, the same scenery that never changes. If you asked whether a bird kept in a cage is happy, no one could answer 'yes' with certainty. Stability is, in other words, stagnation, and the price it demands is freedom of the heart. ……One's wings to soar through the sky, perhaps."

Listening to the birdsong drifting in from outside.

"Your birdcage is a self-righteous, closed cage. ……My opinion hasn't changed. And from the look of things, Kalua and the others turned you down too?"

"……Yes."

"That's only natural. Any bird that walks into a cage of its own accord would have to be quite the eccentric. Surely you've started to realize that by now?"

She laughed softly and tilted her teacup, wetting her lips.

At those words, Krische lowered her eyes.

She understood, vaguely, in some dim way.

The eternity she wished for was self-serving, and at the very least, no decent human being would even desire it.

Everyone honored the end of life as something to eventually be accepted.

It was an emotion Krische could not understand.

"……Is Krische being childish?"

"That's right. ……Honestly, I never thought you'd be *this* childish."

When Selene said it with exasperation, Krische's fingers tightened around her skirt.

"I'd hoped a few decades might make you grow up a little more, but…… I expected too much. You'll probably stay this way, won't you. For the rest of forever."

A hand reached to the girl's cheek.

The old woman's slightly rough hand lovingly stroked that soft cheek.

"……?"

"My opinion hasn't changed, and you're still a fool and a child, and what hasn't changed remains unchanged."

When Krische looked up, the old woman was gazing at the girl with a helpless sort of smile.

More white hair, more wrinkles, and yet even aged as she was, still the same expression of an older sister.

"But do as you please. ……No matter what choice you make, no matter what selfish whims you indulge, I'm your sister. I can at least look after your foolishness properly."

"That, means……"

She pinched the cheek of Krische, who had started to speak.

Toyed with that soft cheek, and let her mind wander to the past, as if reminiscing.

"I thought it over quite thoroughly. No matter how much I twisted logic and feeling, what I love is probably the same thing as what Bery loved――that foolish core of yours."

*What makes the flowers bloom is the seed,* she murmured, narrowing her eyes.

"Changed, and yet unchanged. I'm still stubborn, and you're still a fool. Kreschenta is still insufferably haughty, and Bery was a cunning, obstinate thing to the very end. ……Fufu, if what changes is a person's essence, then perhaps what doesn't change is also a person's essence."

If she had been a little more reasonable.

If Kreschenta had been a little more honest.

If Bery had been a little more willing to lean on others.

Just as Selene hated her own obstinacy, she didn't love every single part of them.

She could find faults without end――but all of it together was what made Krische, and Kreschenta, and Bery who they were.

"……If I love them despite all that, then there's nothing for it. Wordplay is Bery's domain, but in the end, perhaps love is just that simple and trivial a thing."

She pinched the cheek, pulled it, then ran her hand through the hair instead.

The silver hair was smooth as water, without a single flaw, as if to proclaim a perfection that was itself a distortion.

Flawless perfection, as though she had stepped from a storybook.

And yet all that surrounded her was irrationality, and so it warped.

Yet her soul, unclouded, gazed upon eternity.

"……What you do is your freedom. I leave it in your hands. I'd have no regrets if my life ended tomorrow, and I'd have no regrets if I had to put up with your foolishness for all eternity."

She was clearly warped as a person.

But that was precisely why.

That was precisely why Selene Christand had been captivated by her.

"Spoiling you this much, I must be a fool myself. ……Do as you please."

――Simply, by that warped beauty.

"Selene……"

Krische gazed at Selene's face as she said this, and smiled.

She stroked her cheek and gently pinched it between her fingers.

"……Yes."

Krische was happy, was blissful.

As always, it was her ever-modest smile.

Selene gazed back, smiled wryly, and spoke.

"If I had one wish, I'd like to give Bery a piece of my mind."

She wore exaggerated displeasure on her face.

"Most of what I've had to worry about has been because of you, and the reason you've stayed this foolish is mostly Bery's fault. Thanks to that, from the moment you arrived, I haven't had a single moment's rest for decades."

"……Selene makes work even when there's no work to be done, so Krische doesn't think it's entirely Krische's fault……"

"Don't talk back."

"Uuu……"

She pinched Krische's cheek and pulled, and Selene laughed.

In Krische's line of sight lay the bundle of documents――Alberinea's strategies and tactical theory, broken down and formalized into writing, one of the reference materials for the military staff department.

Since handing the Marshal's seat to Nozan's eldest son, she had spent her days writing various military documents to be left for future generations.

Selene knew that she was not a person of extraordinary talent.

But precisely because of that, there were things she could do――at some point, she had come to think that way.

Softening the sharp edges of a warped genius, smoothing them to fit with others――no one understood her better, and no one else could do it.

"The axle grease."

"……?"

"You grease the axle of the Gateal Goton to reduce friction, don't you? Those little things are quite important, I think."

"The clatter-clunk, uh――"

"Either name is fine."

――Where to find meaning in one's own life.

Whether one could take pride in that.

In the end, it all came down to that.

Some devoted their lives to valor and honor, others to baking delicious bread.

Comparing which was superior was a meaningless exercise.

If one could pour one's heart and soul into something one valued, then regardless of what it was, it could be called a fruitful life.

The answer had been right before her eyes all along.

She never wished for even a shred of the former, which she excelled at, but only for the latter.

Being a hero whose name was etched in history meant far less to her than spending her days as a servant in the mansion, and that was everything.

Stronger than anyone, and yet she chose a kitchen knife over a sword.

She put more effort into cleaning the mansion than annihilating enemy armies.

Depending on how you looked at it, it was foolish and absurd, but that was fine.

She was satisfied with that, and that was her happiness.

The meaning one found in life was like that, she thought.

Not a matter of talent or its lack, but something one found in that way.

The most foolish girl in the world pursued happiness above all else.

Comical and bumbling and clumsy.

And yet, in a moment of clarity, it was her own fixations that seemed foolish.

――In the end, it hadn't mattered what it was.

Yet Selene had always coveted something that someone ahead of her possessed.

Blind to all the other things that existed, staring only at that, her vision narrowed.

"……Names and words are trivial things. I'll call things however I like, and Jaragasha is Jaleia Gashea, and clatter-clunk is Gateal Goton. Do you understand?"

"Uuu……"

She pulled and toyed with the displeased girl's cheeks with both hands.

Everything was trivial in the end――even something like love was, ultimately, just a word.

The very attempt to rank even that had been the mistake from the start.

Even after all these years, Bery was such a vast presence that Selene envied her, was jealous of her.

But even so, Selene loved Krische, and loved Bery.

That feeling could not be compared to anything, and it was something only Selene could hold.

The muddy blend of purity and impurity was something she simply had to accept, and that was her own being, as she was.

"Fufu."

Love was, at its core, a self-serving thing.

What mattered was simply whether one could be at peace with it.

"……I love you, Krische."

Beauty and ugliness were merely matters of preference.

And if that love was accepted, there could be no greater peace than that.

"Ah, ehehe, Krische too!"

The press of lips was the same as it had been for decades.

Its meaning, the emotion it carried, unchanged for decades.

Realizing that had probably required time.

"……You really are a fool."

How many times had she said it――she repeated those words once more.

If she was a fool, then the one who loved her was an even bigger fool.

――But how profoundly happy it was to feel that that was perfectly fine.

"Mm…… delicious."

"Fufun, Krische mixed in dried nilkana in today's cookies. They might be a little too sweet, but――"

A cloying sweetness, with just a faint trace of tartness mixed in.

Those cookies tasted like everything that Selene's life had been.

* * *

A great mass rose from the earth, radiating vast quantities of mana.

It was, for all the world, a tower of blades thrust into the sky.

Its inner walls had begun to be invaded by the brown and green of trees pushing upward from the earth.

On the lower levels it was more like a forest, the trees absorbing the mana flowing through its hollow interior as nourishment, beginning to fuse with the tower walls.

By now it might be called a single great tree rather than a tower.

The outer walls still displayed the majestic beauty of a white tower, but that was, in a sense, merely an eggshell.

Nourished by the world itself, a mystical apex reaching toward the sky.

――If one were to name it properly, "World Tree" would be the most fitting.

Upon its summit, a girl gazed at a blue light.

Her pale pink dress fluttered as if stirred by wind that did not exist, and her gold hair, gleaming red, took on a violet hue in the blue light.

Countless patterns filled every part of the surrounding space, blue curves dancing around her――flowing outward from the blue radiance and into the sky.

As though to envelop the very planet.

Her breath was already the atmosphere itself.

Her fingertips commanded all creation, and her eyes saw through everything in this world.

If a ruler is one who governs a nation and lays down its laws, then a being who commands all creation and gives rise to the laws of nature could only be called a god.

She was no longer threatened by petty squabbles between nations.

Even ancient dragons of immense power were like fossils of a bygone glory.

――The girl held within her palm everything she had once sought.

"……What is it, Anne-sama?"

The girl asked in a voice like a ringing bell.

Without raising it, the voice was nonetheless clear enough to fill the vast space.

"Oh, nothing. Only that you are beautiful, as always."

The old woman who served her answered with a gentle smile.

Her hair, now mostly white where it had once been black, swayed gently.

The girl――Kreschenta turned with an exasperated look and walked toward her.

The old woman――Anne gazed at Kreschenta, then narrowed her eyes at the pillar of blue light.

"……No matter how many times I see it, it's an astonishing sight. When I was working in the royal district, I never imagined I would find myself in a place like this."

"It's like a fairy tale," Anne said with a wry smile.

"It's reality," Kreschenta answered with displeasure.

"This is now the seventy-third time we've had an exchange like this. Even looking like that, you say the same things over and over. You haven't changed at all."

"Wonder doesn't change much no matter how old you get."

"……You're the only one."

Hands on hips, Kreschenta looked up at Anne with a glare.

Then she let out a quiet sigh.

"Fufu, but Her Majesty the Queen hasn't changed either. ……Perhaps that is simply the nature of human beings."

"I am no longer a human being so much as a god. Don't lump me in with you."

"……Indeed, from the people's perspective, you may well be such a revered being, but……"

Anne nodded with a solemn expression and fell into thought.

Then she showed her soft smile again.

"To me, you remain very much the same as you were long ago. ……And if I think of even this as still a process, all the more so."

"……?"

"A story of someone reaching into the domain of the gods for the sake of the one they love――I think that is more beautiful and full of love than any story."

Kreschenta furrowed her brow.

"……Who are you talking about?"

"Perhaps the person Her Majesty the Queen just thought of?"

Anne said it merrily, and closed her eyes.

"There are things in this world that cannot be undone, I believe. ……But the opposite is also true."

"I couldn't care less about Argan-sama."

"……I think that trying not to care about something is the same as being conscious of it. If I may be so bold, that is certainly the case for me."

*――It's been quite some time since I last heard Her Majesty speak Argan-sama's name.*

Anne said so, and Kreschenta averted her gaze.

"In the end, I spent most of my life at the Christand estate. Not a shred of regret. I feel now that it was for the best, and even at this age, I'm happy every day."

*But,* she continued, smiling softly.

"If you asked me when I was happiest, it would be decades ago――when Argan-sama was still well. I was always making blunders, but the estate was always lively, and…… above all, Her Majesty looked so much happier than she does now."

"……That was nothing but tiresome. What are you trying to say?"

Anne folded her skirt, crouched before Kreschenta, and peered into that elegant face.

"If I may be so selfish…… I would like to see Her Majesty like that again."

"……You dare make demands of me, the Queen, knowing your station as a mere servant?"

"Her Majesty always says to speak up rather than stay silent."

"Please forgive your incompetent servant," Anne said, reaching out to stroke her white cheek.

Kreschenta watched it happen without resistance, then looked away.

"……How absurd. Everything I wanted has already come true."

"……Yes."

"And that remark makes it sound as if you don't make any blunders anymore. Before making selfish requests, perhaps you should focus on being a better servant to your mistress."

"……I have no words to defend myself."

Anne smiled sheepishly, then rose and bowed her head.

"Please forgive my impudence, Your Majesty. ……I'm old now, and I simply felt I should say this once more."

"……What a hopeless servant you are. We're leaving."

"Yes," Anne nodded, and then said,

"……I'm sure that Argan-sama――"

"That subject is closed. ……Can't you walk in silence?"

She cut the conversation short.

At those words, Anne bowed in silence and walked ahead of Kreschenta.

The two stepped into the magic-powered lift――and behind them, the pillar of light embraced everything in the world.

# Chapter 252: Reminiscence

The days flowed on. People departed.

Kalua passed away, and as if chasing after her, Mia passed the following year.

Elvena, too, not long after that.

Still, the days continued.

But it was a daily life approaching its end.

Less than three years apart, the next was Anne, whose health began to fail――

"Like an avalanche."

An afternoon past midday.

Seated on a sofa, quill in hand, writing with a smooth rhythm, Selene spoke.

Sprawled lazily across the sofa with her head pillowed on Selene's thigh, Kreschenta shifted to look up at her.

"……Once one collapses, everything follows. But I suppose this is what a happy ending looks like. People born around the same time dying around the same time."

"I couldn't care less. And that's nothing but the excuse of the incompetent. Just because death can't be avoided, you dress it up in pretty words and claim to be satisfied."

Kreschenta cut her off sharply, returning words with words.

"Fufu, you really are just like Krische, Kreschenta. You're saying the same thing in different words."

"Onee-sama may be a fool, but she isn't wrong. She's far more intelligent than the likes of you, Selene-sama."

Selene looked down at the beautiful face that said this, smiled wryly, and stroked her cheek.

Despite looking displeased, the exquisite queen offered no resistance.

"If everyone were ageless, there would be no custom of romanticizing death. Everyone would reject it and loathe it. ……Calling death beautiful is nothing but a convenient fiction."

*It's an excuse,* Kreschenta added scathingly.

Selene considered for a moment, then smiled. "Perhaps so."

"Well, from a societal perspective, it's more convenient to make it seem beautiful. Soldiers will eagerly risk their lives for honor's sake without being told, and there's nothing wrong with that, but…… even so, considered on an individual level, I can't see it as anything but foolish."

"That's an extreme position. Perhaps you should learn to see things a little more…… ambiguously."

"……Ambiguously?"

"Yes. All sorts of things. ……The human heart is, after all, an ambiguous thing."

*――Including yours.*

The old woman gently brushed the hair from the forehead of the girl lying in her lap, smiling tenderly.

"You are a very kind child at heart, Kreschenta. The only difference from Krische is how you think. Pretending you can't understand, mocking others, pushing them away――that's simply because you're afraid to acknowledge it. ……Isn't that so?"

"I am nothing like the rest of you. Don't lump me in with you."

"The differences are small. The things you share are far more numerous. ……Everyone is afraid of being hurt."

*Quite the lot of perfectionists,* Selene murmured with a soft shake of her shoulders.

"You, and Kreschenta, and Bery, and Krische, all of us. Our ideals are so terribly grand, and so we fixate on every little flaw. I suppose everyone has that to some degree, but this household seems to have gathered nothing but such people."

Bery, who could find no value in herself.

Krische, who could not forgive her own warping.

Selene, who could not accept her own inadequacy.

And Kreschenta, who tried to convince herself she was a flawless being.

"What a suffocating way to live. You should all relax more. ……You don't have to struggle so hard. The body naturally floats and drifts on water."

"……What are you trying to say?"

"You know perfectly well, you stubborn thing."

She let out a soft, refined laugh, then gestured toward the door. "Why don't you go?"

"Anne is your favorite, when all is said and done. You could at least go sit by her side and look after her."

"I'm already doing more than enough. Besides, what do you think I am? I'm Her Majesty the great and magnificent Queen."

Kreschenta said it with exasperation.

"……As I've said from the very beginning, onee-sama alone is enough for me. Whether the rest of you live or die is a mere afterthought. I only treat you decently because onee-sama values you. ……That's all it has ever been."

"Stubborn as always."

Selene smiled wryly, leaned back into the sofa, and began stroking Kreschenta's head.

Then she narrowed her eyes.

"I probably don't have much longer either."

Lately, she felt the weight of age more and more often.

For mana wielders, as long as they could still handle their mana, the effects of aging were not so severe.

At most, one would find oneself short of breath after vigorous activity, with aching joints.

But recently, manipulating mana itself had become increasingly unreliable.

At the very least, the fine mana control that had once come easily was growing more and more difficult.

She was nearly a hundred years old――and had lived a reasonably long life.

Not excessively long for a mana wielder, but not a short one either.

"……I see."

Saying that, Kreschenta twisted her body and turned on her side, still pillowed on Selene's thigh――naturally hiding her expression.

After so many years together, Kreschenta was surprisingly easy to read.

"I've decided to leave it to Krische. ……So the two of you need to have a proper talk and decide together."

"……I don't know what you mean."

"Fufu, …… Just a feeling."

Continuing to stroke her head, Selene kept her eyes closed.

"What's needed for any matter is a decision, and what's needed for a decision is acceptance, and for that, I think some kind of rite of passage is necessary."

It had been so for Selene, at least, and it must have been for Bery as well.

*'……Until you feel better, Selene, until it doesn't hurt anymore. ……Krische will stay by your side the whole time.'*

Whatever else, the fact that Krische had come to her room that day.

――That Krische had accepted Selene for who she was still remained carved deep into her heart.

And that was why she had been won over before she knew it, why it all came to seem so absurd――and why she was able to accept so many things.

People are weak and ambiguous creatures, and that is precisely why they need a catalyst.

For the courage to move one's feet, just a little, forward.

"It just seemed to me that you haven't been able to accept Krische's wish. ……If I'm mistaken, that's fine."

Kreschenta did not answer.

Selene said nothing more, only stroked her head.

In the sunlight streaming through the window, the golden hair gleamed red.

It slid through her fingers like silk, as smooth as it had been for decades.

She stroked it without tiring, and then, Kreschenta spoke.

"……I'll sleep like this."

"All right," Selene said with a wry smile, reaching for the blanket beside her to cover her.

Without asking anything more, she wrapped the blanket around the small body, leaving nothing exposed.

"……Good night, Kreschenta."

She said it in a voice full of love.

Without answering, Kreschenta closed her eyes.

* * *

――In the Christand kitchen, Anne sighed, alone.

A heavy atmosphere hung over the estate.

Day by day it grew stronger, and even Anne could feel it against her skin.

That was how large a presence she was in the Christand household.

What would happen once she was gone――

*'Ah!'*

As if to drive the foolish thought away, she clapped both hands against her own cheeks with a sharp *pah*.

*'……What are you thinking, Anne? At times like these, you're the one who has to hold it together.'*

She told herself so――no, she truly did tell herself, and wiped her eyes.

Perhaps she'd slapped too hard. It hurt enough to bring tears.

Rubbing the cheeks she'd struck, she heard the front door open――and from outside, the jade tiger's growl.

Krische must have departed for the front.

She closed her eyes, nodded once, and left the kitchen to go see Bery.

Hurrying down the corridor, up the stairs to the second floor and into the room,

*'Argan-sama!'*

Anne called out in a rush.

Bery was crouched before the wardrobe.

Head bowed, perfectly still.

When she noticed Anne, she looked up with a pale face and a wry smile.

*'……Anne-sama.'*

*'Are you…… your body, is it……'*

*'I'm fine. It was just a little dizzy spell. ……Perhaps because I tried to put on airs.'*

She smiled awkwardly, fingering the fabric of an apron dress.

It seemed like an appearance she hadn't worn in quite some time.

Lately, Bery spent most of her time in her nightgown.

She quietly closed her eyes and steadied her breathing.

*'Anne-sama, would you mind helping me change?'*

*'Y-Yes!'*

Anne immediately supported her body and sat her on the bed.

Then she took out a negligee and climbed onto the bed herself.

She received the apron Bery had been undoing, then unfastened the buttons at the bodice one by one from the top.

Carefully slipping the black dress from her shoulders, pulling it down from the hem.

A small laugh escaped, and Anne looked up.

*'Fufu, it's rather odd to say this after asking for help, but…… it's a bit embarrassing, isn't it?'*

A faint blush colored her cheeks as she said it.

Her large eyes were soft and narrowed, and long lashes flickered.

Perhaps it was because she had grown her hair long, unlike before.

She didn't look like a servant at all; she could have been a noblewoman of high birth, and without the apron dress, she was simply, breathtakingly beautiful.

Small-framed but with a well-defined figure that should have looked healthy, and yet she somehow appeared delicate.

Perhaps it was the gentle slope of her narrow shoulders and the slenderness of her waist, which looked as though it might snap.

Anne had thought from the first time they met that she was somehow ethereal.

Now, even more so.

It might have been because her limbs had grown so much thinner.

She shook her head to drive the thought away.

*'N-No! There is absolutely no need to feel embarrassed! Such physical beauty that would be the envy of any ordinary woman such as myself…… it is an honor for me to be allowed to attend to Argan-sama!'*

*'Ph-physical beauty……'*

Bery's eyes wandered in apparent confusion.

――Physical beauty had been the wrong word, hadn't it?

Aware of her blunder but setting it aside for now, Anne pressed on.

*'Compared to the debt of gratitude I owe you, there is no need whatsoever for reserve. Um, yes, that's right, please treat me like a dog or something of the sort.'*

As the words tumbled out from her heart, Bery stared at her with a blank expression.

Lips parted slightly, she gazed at Anne.

Then she lowered her face and covered her mouth with her hand.

Her shoulders shook with quiet, restrained laughter.

*She must have said something wrong again* ――as Anne pondered this, Bery told her,

*'Honestly, Anne-sama, you're such a delightful person. Fufu, truly…… as I've told you before, ever since Anne-sama came, I've always thought so.'*

*'I-Is that so……'*

*'I'm sorry, that was rude of me.'*

She wiped the corner of her eye, looked up, and smiled as she always did.

*'The truth is…… I was a little anxious about working with another servant. I'm not very good with people, so I wondered whether we could get along and work well together.'*

She took the pale pink negligee from Anne's hand and pulled it on over her head.

Looking a little shy, she continued.

*'But…… then Anne-sama came, and Elvena-sama came, and you were both so earnest and dedicated and kind…… I'm truly grateful for that now.'*

*'I-If anyone should say that, it's me…… I'm the one who has been so well looked after by Argan-sama. I'm sure Elvena-sama would feel exactly the same……'*

*And besides,* Anne said, her eyes wandering.

*'I've been nothing but mistakes, and if anything, I've caused Argan-sama nothing but trouble……'*

*'That's not true at all. ……The estate has become so much livelier, and it's been nothing but joy.'*

Lovely and beautiful――younger-looking than Anne, with a child's face, yet wearing the kind smile of a mother or elder sister.

Anne thought this must have been what won her over.

Charming, and somehow poised.

That was the sort of woman Bery Argan was.

She bowed her head in thanks, then moved to the upper part of the bed.

Anne bowed deeply in turn, and watched as she picked up a journal from the bedside table.

It was a journal of recipes she had made.

There seemed to be over a dozen volumes, something she had been keeping in her spare time for quite a while.

Partway through, the title changed from a simple *"Recipe Journal"* to *"Recipe Journal with Krische-sama,"* and today it was the latter.

Inside, she wrote――and sometimes the two of them would write together on the same page.

Recalling those two together brought a warm feeling, and Anne said,

*'……I do hope Selene-sama and Krische-sama will return home safely and soon.'*

*'Yes. ……I'd like to do something to celebrate when they return, but……'*

# Chapter 255: A Bird's-Eye View of the Self

Blocking an opponent's strike with one's own blade, or deflecting it.

Because there was no reason in the world why one should be able to receive the full-force strike of an opponent of equal build and technique.
