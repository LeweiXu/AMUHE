# Chapter 226: Things Unchanged

Clatter-clunk, clatter-clunk — connected wooden carriages rolling along iron rails.

The magic-powered rail-wheel vehicle — Gateal Goton.

Its name derived from Goton, the messenger of western mythology once worshipped in Alberan, and his beloved steed Gateal.

Facilitating the movement of people and goods, enabling administration and control.

That these nations were able to govern the vast territory of the continent was in no small part due to these iron roads.

This was the very foundation upon which the great development of the Kingdom of Alberan was built, and it would remain in use for ages to come — until, upon the fall of Kleinmeil, it was destroyed as a symbol of the ruling class.

"Muu, the clatter-clunk jostles at the joints, so it's no good."

"Stop complaining already. Compared to a carriage, the ride is so much smoother."

"Krische should have thought a little more about how to make the rails at the beginning……"

Clinging to Selene's arm, she puffed her cheeks in displeasure.

When they arrived at their destination, western Elsren — Klauzera — Krische was the first to hop off the 'clatter-clunk.'

On either side of the railway sat platforms built of Forged Stone, and though no trumpet fanfare rang out, lined up before the two of them were several dozen men crisply fitted in formal military dress.

And before them, on one knee, were two men.

"Piyo-piyo, it's been a while. Woof-woof too."

"Yes. I had the chance to see Selene-sama during the Elsren campaign, but…… it is good to see you both well."

Eastern Regional Army Supreme Commander of the Kingdom of Alberan, Nozan Velraigh.

And Eastern Regional Army General, Granmeld Varkus.

Nozan's refined features were unchanged, yet silver threads had crept into his copper hair, and wrinkles had deepened.

Granmeld was much the same — white hairs mingling with the black.

And above all——

"Somehow you two have gotten all scraggly, too."

——it was the beards they had both grown.

Nozan's appeared to have received some grooming, but a proper beard ran from around his mouth along his jawline; as for Granmeld, his had been left to grow completely wild.

With the men around her having been growing beards of late, Krische tilted her head, wondering if it was some sort of trend.

"……You two as well?"

"There are lots of them, but Aleha too. Apparently he's started growing a beard recently, so Krische gave him the nickname Scraggly, but…… now there are so many Scragglies that it's getting confusing……"

"Hahaha, I see. Well, when a man tries to add gravitas to his appearance, that tends to be the result. Aleha's is likely for similar reasons as mine. Granmeld is simply being lazy, though."

"In my case, it just keeps growing back no matter how much I shave, so I got tired of bothering."

Granmeld scratched his bearded cheek with a laugh.

He had never been particularly refined-looking to begin with, but now he was nothing short of a bandit chief.

Ignoring the difference in height, he could have passed for Dagreen's brother.

"Still, Krische-sama is as unchanged as ever. She doesn't age at all."

"Truly. It's absurd — this girl is only a year younger than me."

Selene looked at the girl who had her arm linked through hers and pinched her cheek.

It stretched softly with a squish.

Confused expressions were visible among the lined-up soldiers, but Selene no longer cared.

That stage had passed long, long ago.

"Muu…… Krische is a proper adult——"

"Even dogs and cats can get older. I'd like to see you look a little more age-appropriate, but……"

She squished and pulled at her cheek, then said, with exasperation, "well, that's never going to happen."

Nozan smiled wryly and glanced around at the surrounding men.

"Well, let us save the reminiscing for later. There is still a long way to go from here, so for now, let us head to the command tent."

"Yes. Um…… ah, Nekki, cars seven and eight."

"Sir. Oi, car seven first, move it!"

The man who emerged from the lead car — the driver's cab — was a man with a prosthetic leg who issued orders briskly.

Former Black Flag Special Service engineering squad — first-generation clatter-clunk operator, Nekki.

Retired soldiers from the Black Flag Special Service were given priority in being sent to work in railway operations.

Nekki, who had been involved from the early stages of the continental clatter-clunk network plan, had volunteered to be the operator upon completion of the inaugural vehicle, and since then had always been assigned to the most critical routes.

Within Alberan, the railway was considered particularly important, and most personnel assigned to it were military retirees — especially for the eastern-bound railway into former Elsren territory, where many former Black Flag soldiers were engaged in operations.

Krische released Selene's arm and toddled toward the rear of the train.

The workers hurried to remove the countless fasteners on cars seven and eight — and when car seven was opened, what rang out was a scream.

"Gururun."

Grrrr — what leapt out of the cramped cargo compartment, looking thoroughly fed up, was the eight-shaku, two-jou jade tiger.

Once outside, it gave a great stretch and yawn, then lowered its head to let Krische climb aboard.

Most of those present shrank back.

"The jade tiger is lively as ever, I see."

Nozan said, sounding impressed, and Krische smiled.

"Ehehe, full of energy. That's a good thing."

And she happily stroked Gururun's neck.

From there, they set out across the plains dotted with clusters of tents.

Soldiers saluted one after another at the sight of Krische.

Most were troops of the Central Alberinea Army, and not a soul among them wondered who the girl sitting atop the jade tiger might be.

Some among the Eastern Regional Army reinforcements and new recruits eyed her appearance suspiciously given that she was supposedly over forty, but behind her——

Marshal Selene, astride a large horse — Bururun the Second — looked quite young herself, and they accepted it as simply the way things were.

The presence of Nozan and the others, who had appeared alongside them, helped as well.

Selene parted from Nozan's group partway through, saying she would get a brief report from them, and the first place she headed upon arrival was the central tent of the Alberinea Army.

Around it stood, as always, soldiers in familiar black-painted armor.

"Bunny, it's been about two months~ Fufu, onee-san missed you."

"……Did you miss Krische?"

"Kalua, you're not at the 'onee-san' age anymore."

The chestnut-haired adjutant, arms akimbo, glared at the black-haired woman with exasperation.

"As long as a beauty stays a beauty, onee-san is fine at any age. Don't sweat the small stuff."

"……I don't think that's a small thing at all."

Mia, whose features had once been somewhat childish, had grown her hair a little longer, gaining an adult's poise and composure.

Kalua, too, had the look of a woman in her prime — the marks of age still faint upon her.

Krische smiled at the sight of them and said, "it's been a while."

"Elvena probably missed you two a little, not being able to see you."

"Fufu, I bet. I wanted to come by clatter-clunk too……"

"Kalua, stop complaining. There's loads of work to do."

"Haah……"

And then, appearing on the scene and saluting, was an old man clad in armor.

A shaved head and a prominent hawk nose. And a white beard.

Krische pursed her lips, put both hands on her hips, and glared at the man.

"……Krische told Vulture that since he's an old man, he would retire as squad leader once the handover was done."

"My apologies. There was much to hand over, and it has taken somewhat longer than expected."

Krische stared hard at Dagra, and in return Dagra wore a faint smile.

Kalua laughed cheerfully and patted Krische on the head.

"Come on, Mia can be a little slow on the uptake sometimes. During the handover, she kept going back and forth on this and that——"

"K-Kalua!"

"……Is that so, Mia?"

"Ugh, guh……"

Mia glared at Kalua, then looked at Dagra.

Then, with a sigh, she answered, "that's right," sounding distinctly unconvinced.

"Honestly. Mia is Mia as always. Vulture, it's dangerous, so you're not to go to the front, understand? Whatever the paperwork says, in Krische's mind Vulture is no longer the Kuro-Fuyo squad leader but the Kuro-Fuyo training instructor."

"Sir. However, please do not be concerned — aged as I may look, I am still more than a match for the younger men."

He struck his fist firmly against his armor, then rested his hand on his sword.

Krische pursed her lips as she said, "that's not the point."

"That's not the issue. Once this is over, you're retiring for real. The battlefield life is bad for your health. Especially after such a long journey……"

Krische told him, puffing her cheeks, and Dagra smiled and answered, "of course."

"The squad leader is Mia. Vulture is the training instructor and support role. Understood?"

"Sir. Understood, Krische-sama."

Dagra clicked his heels and offered a powerful salute.

Watching this with the sense that something didn't quite sit right, Krische leapt up onto the jade tiger.

Dagra shifted his gaze from Krische to Mia, and smiled wryly.

"……Sorry, Mia."

"Haah, it can't be helped. Don't worry about it, Taichou."

Mia's expression matched her words as she sighed.

The thankless role would surely follow her for the rest of her life — Mia had already resigned herself to that.

"If I think of this as the last battle, then I want Taichou here too."

"Yeah yeah, it just wouldn't be right without Taichou's face around here."

Kalua said, draping an arm over Mia's shoulder, and Dagra smiled wryly.

"……I am proud to have been the captain of this squad."

Then, adding, "I have been blessed with fine subordinates," Dagra nodded quietly.

* * *

——Two months later.

The stage for the decisive battle lay far to the east of the kingdom.

It was a place known as the Fangs of Wetharius, at the eastern edge of the former Elsren territories.

Its nature was not unlike the Dragon's Maw.

Here, a gap in the great mountain range that towered to the north, and to the south the vast sea.

This plain was the only road connecting east and west of the continent — the gateway to the continent's east.

On the eastern side, enormous fortresses and towers stood in a row, and from the cliffs a great wall stretched outward — Elsren had attacked this place countless times but never succeeded in breaking through. After dozens of campaigns, it remained undefeated.

The Wall of Galgain loomed there in intimidating grandeur.

Known even in the farthest reaches of the west, this great wall dividing the continent into east and west — standing before it, Krische——

"Selene! The sea! It's the sea!"

——was bouncing up and down on the sandy beach.

"Don't get so excited. It is incredible, though — the horizon……"

Around the Wall of Galgain, the shoreline was all sheer cliffs.

But moving westward, the cliffs gave way to sandy beach.

Above hung an azure sky with scattered clouds, and a beautiful blue sea.

The horizon seemed to shimmer, and the occasional whitecap undulated like a living thing.

"Beautiful. I'd only ever known about it from books, but seeing it in person is completely different."

Selene said, then looked down at her feet.

She leapt back with a yelp.

"There's a weird spider……!"

"……?"

Krische trotted over and grabbed the leg of the small creature that had startled Selene.

"You mean this?"

"How can you just touch it like that……"

"This one is probably the edible kind. There were smaller ones near Krische's village too. River spiders…… the name from the books is 'crab.'"

"River spiders…… ah, now that you mention it, I think I've seen similar ones."

"Krische has never eaten one either, but according to books they're supposed to be delicious. Apparently they eat the bigger ones by the sea quite regularly……"

The crab, held in Krische's grip, waved its legs and claws frantically.

Selene took a slight step back and told Krische, "throw it away."

"It's practically a spider. ……I am not eating it, understand?"

"Muu…… spider……"

She studied the crab intently, then decided it did look rather spider-like and set it down on the sand.

Watching it scurry away, she conceded that river spiders might indeed be relatives of spiders.

Krische's basic understanding was that insects were not meant to be eaten.

She knew there were people who ate insects.

It was a common topic on the battlefield.

In the village too, some people took bee nests and ate the larvae inside — but given that Grace had been very reluctant when offered, it probably wasn't the norm.

A lady does not eat insects — that was Krische's understanding.

And naturally, Krische, being a lady (self-proclaimed), did not eat insects either. Obviously.

"Ha ha ha, you two are missing out!"

A gravelly voice addressed the pair.

They turned to see three men approaching.

"It has been a while, Marshal, Alberinea."

"It has been a while, Olgan-san. You're early."

"Yes. Though between the rocking of the ship, the ground still seems to be swaying."

Chairman of the Galshan Republic, Olgan — and Southern Regional Army Supreme Commander, Dagreen Gahka.

His hair was lightly swept back, but the silver-streaked beard was left completely wild.

Even in old age, his powerfully muscled body showed no sign of weakening, and his restless energy was undiminished.

Behind him, Zalvarg hung back, looking rather ill.

"Crab is quite the delicacy, Krische, Selene. We stopped at a port town and had our fill of seafood cuisine, and the bounty of the sea is not to be underestimated. Strange-looking creatures though many of them are, the flavor is superb."

"Is that so?"

"Indeed. Best not to turn your nose up without trying. I had some brought from the port as drinking accompaniments. I was thinking of treating everyone to it at tonight's gathering after the council, but……"

Krische's eyes lit up with an "ohh," and Selene let out a small groan.

"We have a soldier among us who was born in a port town and has a skilled hand. Whether it will suit your tastes I cannot say, but seafood cuisine is something worth experiencing at least once. I too once dismissed crab as a bizarre curiosity, but those who call it unappetizing are few indeed."

At Olgan's words, Krische's purple eyes sparkled and glittered.

She sidled up to Selene, tugged at her sleeve, and peered up at her with wide, upturned eyes.

"Selene, why don't we try it……?"

"I, I……"

Selene leaned her body backward, clearly unenthusiastic.

Krische gazed up at Selene, pressing closer.

"Haah…… fine, fine."

"Ehehe. Maybe Krische will have a souvenir story for Ber——ah."

She spoke, then clapped her hand over her mouth as if catching herself.

Selene looked at Krische, her cheeks coloring faintly.

Then she called her "silly" and pinched her cheek.

"How far back are you…… you don't need to worry about that anymore. Having you worry like that makes me far more embarrassed."

"……Yes."

Selene sighed at Krische's searching gaze, gave her cheek another pinch, and then turned to Dagreen.

"Then we'll gladly accept your kindness. ……How are things with the ships?"

"Five are delayed, as I reported. Other than that, no incidents — no problems."

"The five that are delayed should take about ten days. Though I don't think it will have much impact."

Olgan looked at Krische.

Krische nodded and smiled.

"That's right. If they're that far behind, we can take care of it after seizing the port on the other side."

As always, as though it were obvious.

"We'll have broken through the day after tomorrow, after all."

She said it flatly, without a trace of concern.

# Chapter 227: Hero Hunt

Two armies deployed two ri apart.

That battle was truly one deserving to be called the war to decide everything.

Against Alberan and its allied Galshan forces, thirteen nations from the eastern coalition had joined the fight.

That the perpetually warring, fractured east — a patchwork of countless nations locked in endless conflict — had never been subjugated by Elsren was not solely because of the mountains.

There was the Wetharius Pact — that against any external threat to this land, the nations would set aside their enmities and unite in resistance.

Wetharius, the wise king of the small nation of Karent — who had once forged the anti-Alberan alliance, uniting the warring nations when Alberan first appeared in these lands.

Ruling the strategically vital crossroads between east and west, yet seeking no expansion.

The accord left behind by this man who proclaimed eternal neutrality in eastern conflicts endured to this day, and whenever this land was threatened by invasion from Elsren, the surrounding nations would cease their quarrels and cooperate, supporting Karent as allies of the pact.

The east, teeming with countless small states.

Once a great power like Elsren established itself within, the eastern balance of power would shatter catastrophically — like a tidal wave.

Therefore, any invasion had to be stopped at the gateway, without exception.

This was the shared understanding of every nation — and this time, the threat was the greatest in history.

Their opponent was the Kingdom of Alberan, which had swallowed Elsren, pacified half the continent, and reclaimed the glory of its former zenith.

Truly a battle to decide the fate of the world.

From every corner of the eastern continent, selected champions from nations great and small had gathered here — warriors of such caliber that even after witnessing Alberan's technological might, their fighting spirit remained unbroken.

Alfmarz, the peerless Martial King who ruled Gelganik, one of the three great eastern powers.

Nitrias, the undefeated war-god.

Rafar, destroyer of a hundred castles.

Simpha, the devil-child of the battlefield.

Though no great powers existed as in the west, the days of turmoil never abated.

The quality of their soldiers, the quality of their generals — neither was inferior to the west, and in terms of experience alone, some among them surpassed it.

Here stood the heroes of the east, their names known throughout the known world.

Against Alberan and Galshan's hundred thousand, the Eastern Coalition army — elite forces gathered from thirteen nations — numbered one hundred and seventy thousand.

Their positioning at well-supplied ports on both sides certainly played a role.

This was the largest single engagement in continental history — a battle of sheer madness.

"Once again…… war-god Nitrias, I am grateful for your cooperation. Without it, a coalition army of this scale would never have come together."

"It is too early for thanks, King Gelganik. I simply shared your view."

Two men on horseback.

Clad in armor modeled after the great serpent he was said to have slain with his own hands, the old general laughed.

"In nearly a hundred years on the battlefield, I have fought many a worthy rival. ……Yet I have never faced you. Were some outsider to interfere before that day, I could not rest even in death."

"Haha, I am honored you think so. ……You were my inspiration."

*I had resolved to kill you with my own hands someday,* Alfmarz said, the corner of his mouth curling upward.

Silver armor, engraved at its center with the royal crest of Gelganik — a sword and shield.

Countless names were carved into every surface of the armor, and he pointed to a single bare spot on the left breast.

"I have dreamed of inscribing the name Nitrias here, as one of King Alfmarz of Gelganik's great foes."

"Hahaha, quite the ambition. However, all of that must wait until after this great battle."

"Just so. ……If we do not win this fight, everything remains a dream."

Before them, a hundred thousand troops — the battle lines of Alberan and Galshan.

At the very front, innumerable beasts and black iron giants.

War-golems called Jaleia Gashea.

Slaughter puppets, each said to be worth a hundred men.

They were said to be one of the reasons mighty Elsren had fallen.

This was the first time either man had seen them in person.

"Seventeen attacking a fortress, head-on with ten. By any normal reckoning, it is insanity. Beyond just those iron puppets, we should assume they possess siege weaponry capable of breaching the Wall of Galgain."

"I too consider a siege defense a poor move. Every fortified city Elsren possessed fell within three days of being engaged. We should not rely on walls."

"……If we defend, we lose."

"Yes — to seize victory, we have no choice but to advance, accepting the cost."

Nitrias narrowed his eyes.

A small number of trebuchets were visible on the Galshan side, but there were none on the Alberan side.

What he had spotted earlier from atop the wall looked like some sort of siege bow.

But it was not a bow — it was the gleam of mana crystals.

"Most likely a specialized siege weapon utilizing mana crystals — we can assume it possesses at least enough power to destroy this wall."

"……So it is that, then."

"Yes. Futaria's Blue Eagle cavalry should prioritize that target."

As the two advanced, from the opposing battle line, two figures emerged through a parted gap.

One rode a horse — a woman in elegant armor with golden hair.

The other sat upon an enormous jade tiger, draped in a cloak.

More girl than woman, perhaps.

——The figure with silver hair trailing behind her seemed familiar.

"……That must be the rumored King's Sister, Alberinea."

"So it would seem."

The silver-haired girl smiled and said something to the golden-haired woman, who glared at the girl with exasperation and replied.

Even from this distance, though the words were inaudible, the exchange held no tension — not even a trace of nervousness.

They were certainly being taken lightly.

At the very least, it did not look like two people about to go into battle.

After a while, the two groups halted at a distance of roughly three ken, and it was Alfmarz who spoke first.

"I am the leader of the Wetharius Alliance and King of Gelganik — Alfmarz Gelganik!"

His voice boomed like a threat, shaking the air, and Alfmarz glared at the two.

"Marshal of the Kingdom of Alberan, Selene Christand."

But without a flicker of agitation.

The golden-haired woman — Selene Christand — gave her name in a cool, unruffled voice.

Alfmarz curled his lips in a provocative smirk.

"Two women representing an army — the men of the west must be terribly soft. I wondered what manner of mighty warriors would lead Alberan, conquerors of Elsren, and this is what I find. How anticlimactic."

"What a charming greeting. Is that how you choose your representative over there — arm wrestling, perhaps?"

Selene smiled pleasantly, elegantly.

She tapped a fingertip against her own temple.

"Your words suggest a head stuffed with nothing but muscle, King Gelganik. I would be grateful if you could refrain from such tedious provocations…… listening to them might lower my own intelligence."

Then, spreading her hands with exasperation, she smiled as if mocking him.

Alfmarz narrowed his eyes and laughed.

"……Oh? To speak to me like that — you have some nerve."

"We are both representatives of our respective armies — we stand as equals. What reason would I have to cower before you? ……Least of all before the king of a fallen nation."

It was cheap provocation.

Yet within Alfmarz's heart was honest admiration.

They were both commanders of armies this vast.

And yet this woman showed not a trace of tension, not a hint of shrinking or fear.

She must have endured her share of crucibles.

She was clearly no mere ornament.

"More importantly, going to all the trouble of exchanging pleasantries — is this an offer of surrender? If so, we might consider it."

"Ha, don't be absurd. I came merely to correct your arrogance. Surely you don't intend to challenge this great wall head-on with such a paltry force?"

He gestured to the battle lines behind him, then to the Wall of Galgain, and then to the sea.

"I thought a naval assault might be your true objective, but judging by appearances, that too seems absent. ……If that is the case, I can only marvel at the sheer recklessness. It seems Alberan has no one versed in military strategy."

Galshan and Arna, Alberan's allies, possessed formidable navies.

Seizing the port by sea, establishing a beachhead, and advancing the eastern invasion from there — this had been an anticipated scenario and, given the immense distance from the western continent, the orthodox approach.

By any rational measure, a frontal assault on this great wall was absurd.

And yet the enemy had chosen exactly that — a land-based frontal breakthrough.

The Galshan navy was present, but showed no signs of moving.

"The great wall…… oh, is that what they call that piece of set dressing over there in the east? Fufu, I nearly mistook it for a mere toll gate."

She gazed at the wall stretching as if to the horizon, and those were the words she spoke.

"If that's a 'great wall,' then what do you call an actual wall — a wooden room divider? I'm so stunned I'm at a loss for words."

Giggling as if in amusement.

The beautiful woman marshal did not waver from her composure, provocation after provocation.

"My apologies. You seem to think you're being challenged to battle, but that's not what this is. This isn't a war — it's just a march. Well, since we'll be passing through a 'toll gate,' perhaps we ought to pay a toll?"

"……I see. Between that Kreschenta and you, it seems you intend to mock us to the very end."

"Oh, excuse me — would you refrain from addressing Alberan's queen so casually? ……I'm afraid the people permitted to do so are rather limited."

Selene turned her horse and showed her back.

"Talk is pointless. Let's go, Krische. ……I wish you the best of fortune. I do hope you can at least manage to fall magnificently, as befits warriors."

And with that, she moved ahead.

The girl sitting on the jade tiger beside her regarded Alfmarz and Nitrias, her silver lashes shifting, eyes narrowing.

"……Krische has memorized your faces. Krische will come visit you two later."

*See you then* — accompanied by a beautiful, adorable smile.

Her purple eyes carried an unsettling, cold light.

"……We'll make sure to return the favor."

Alfmarz responded, feeling something prickle down his spine, and clenched his fist.

Alberan's Heavenly Sword — the cursed child, Krische.

Battlefield decapitator, death itself, an aberrant slayer beyond all reason.

There were countless words to describe her.

Like two tails.

She swayed the long twin braids tied with red flower ornaments and tapped the jade tiger, turning her back.

Wearing no armor, her long hair exposed, wrapped only in a cloak.

Dressed as though out for a stroll through town, nonchalantly taking to the battlefield and achieving results — a prodigy of madness.

One look and you could tell — a distorted presence.

At the very least, she was unmistakably a martial talent of extraordinary caliber.

Alfmarz too pulled his reins and turned his back, speaking.

"When Alberan swallowed Elsren with such ease…… there was a shapeless dread — like fearing the sky might fall. That they should never be underestimated."

"……That concern appears to have been well-founded."

Nitrias replied, and then the two rode in silence.

Alberinea, who was said to have challenged even an ancient dragon.

There was something dwelling within that girl — who appeared to be nothing more than a girl — that lent credence even to the most far-fetched rumors.

Before long, the two returned to the battle line before the wall.

Alfmarz drew the longsword at his hip without a word and raised it high before his soldiers.

*"————!!"*

Nothing more.

A frenzied war cry shattered the air and shook the earth.

The screaming soldiers showed no fear.

Elite troops handpicked from every nation, led by heroes and champions whose names resounded across the known world.

The most fearsome warriors of rival nations were now, in this moment, comrades fighting side by side.

A motley force assembled from thirteen nations, perhaps — but no rabble.

They were led by the great Martial King Alfmarz, and the undefeated war-god Nitrias.

Before generals so great they might become heroic spirits in death, none showed cowardice — and at least in this moment, they were one.

"——!?"

——What shattered that unity was the countless streaks of blue light racing overhead.

The thunderous roar of the Wall of Galgain behind them erupting in flame and crumbling.

* * *

The Alberan battle line — center, rear.

"No matter how many times I see it, the power is beyond belief. Even a wall like that is no match."

"……Truly. The age of fortifications is over."

Wearing a tiger-faced visor helmet.

The man fingering his white-streaked stubble with an air of exasperation was Kokys Agrand.

Astride a large horse much like Selene's Bururun the Second — Burun — he looked behind them.

There stood the weapons called Pishuune — mana projection cannons.

A base resembling a mounted crossbow minus the bow, and a tube-like main body.

Attached to the tip was a large, high-purity mana crystal.

Behind them, mages directed operations as soldiers loaded the next round — a mana crystal — into the base of the tube.

The principle was fundamentally the same as the Baumje Ira.

A mana crystal was collapsed, its released mana was accumulated in the crystal at the tip, given directionality, and discharged forward as a directed mana beam.

It could be described as an enlarged, improved version of what was known as a 'mage's staff.'

By harnessing the enormous mana released during crystal collapse, its power was dramatically enhanced, with a maximum range of ten ri.

Its long range and precision were in a class entirely apart from any previous siege weapon.

In rate of fire and transport efficiency as well, there was no comparison — a monster that truly rewrote what 'battlefield' meant.

"Muu, number twenty-seven Pishuun, lower the elevation. It's not hitting properly."

Beside the two.

Touching the mana crystal earring — the Ear Pururun — Krische pursed her lips and spoke with puffed cheeks.

"Mana crystals aren't cheap, you know. Aim carefully and treasure each one. Numbers eight and fifteen, Pishuun, continue targeting the tower. Numbers five and twenty-two, Pishuun, target the gate. Once ready, roll call starting from number one."

The designated mages raised their voices to relay orders, and soldiers bustled about making fine adjustments.

"Five, four, three, two, one — pishuun."

With a comically anticlimactic command, blue light streaked overhead, and the air trembled with a *pishuun*.

Explosions blossomed on the wall, followed a beat later by thunder and screams.

Alberan's soldiers raised their fists and erupted in cheers.

"You there, centurion. Have your unit's soldiers keep quiet. They're noisy."

"Y-yes ma'am! First unit, silence! Alberinea's orders cannot be heard!"

Krische called out to the centurion nearest to her, looking annoyed, and puffed her cheeks as she surveyed the wall.

Rising from the wall into the sky were aerial cavalry.

Likely intended for rear disruption.

"Anti-air Pishuun squad, prepare to fire. After the initial volley, overall command passes to Anti-Air Pishuun Squad Leader Koza. Engage in free fire until further orders."

A brief pause, then the count again.

# Chapter 230: The Weaver

Two years after the birth of the new life in the house of Duke Remin.

"——You have all been building up a reasonable amount of knowledge, but as you can see, the results were a mess. In wargame exercises the pieces move as you wish, but actually standing on the field and executing those tactics in a real operational context involves a fair amount of effort and hardship — and I trust today has made that clear."

A simple podium stood before them, flanked by kingdom flags.

Gathered on the plains were over thirty young nobles.

The nobles assembled at this military academy received, as a rule, two years of military education.

Commoners who had risen to the rank of squad leader or above and were deemed to have aptitude were also granted admission, though the training period for those with military service experience was one year.

They were currently training separately alongside the nobles who had enrolled the previous year.

Given that strong performance here could lead to a career as a staff officer with a rank equivalent to at least a battalion adjutant — and given the disastrous results of today's field command exercise — every face was deadly serious as they listened to the girl's address.

A cloak and one-piece dress, silver hair hanging in two tails.

There were those who found the sight of the beautiful, lovely girl disconcerting, but not very many.

By now, there was no one in the kingdom who did not know the name Alberinea, and the deference shown by the school's headmaster Elrouga and the instructors was plain for all to see.

All the more so when they saw the jade tiger yawning nonchalantly at her side.

Eight shaku at the shoulder, two jou from nose to tail — intimidating even from a distance.

Those seeing it for the first time felt their spines freeze under the pressure of the mana it radiated.

A magical beast could never be tamed.

Even warriors called heroes risked their lives to face one — and for this girl to stand calmly beside such an aberrant creature, no one believed she was the mere girl she appeared to be.

"What is important is knowing the position and status of every single soldier. Where the formation is breaking down — is it overextended, or falling behind, or not receiving orders — knowing this. For a commander, the most important thing is grasping the situation."

The girl raised a finger.

"From what Krische saw today, quite a few of you panicked and rushed into action. When in doubt, do the opposite — maintain the current state. Create a moment of stasis and devote it to assessing the situation. Please remember: all tactics rest upon accurate situational judgment."

*Understood?* she said with a stern expression.

Then she turned to the man beside her — Elrouga.

Elrouga twisted his skull-like features into a kindly smile and nodded, and Krische smiled in return.

"That is all from Krische. What you do here is only training, after all — mistakes won't get anyone killed, and nothing is irreversible. This is your chance to overcome your weaknesses, so make the most of it and learn all you can."

When Krische hopped onto the jade tiger, the group snapped to attention and saluted in unison.

Here again, new buds were peeking out in Alberan.

* * *

For young nobles without military service experience, the standard was a two-year training period.

The academy's curriculum began with classroom study, moving through repeated wargame exercises to build a tactical mindset.

The emphasis was on cramming knowledge — dropouts were rare at this stage — but what awaited those who had gained a measure of tactical perspective was a steep fall: live training with actual soldiers.

For nobles without campaign experience in particular, many struggled to even march a hundred men in formation, and the purpose was precisely to inflict that kind of humbling.

Even when the optimal solution was understood in the head, whether it could be executed was an entirely separate matter.

Having deliberately produced a cohort of over-educated thinkers, the program then subjected them to a grueling month of field exercises, teaching them firsthand the difficulty of directing a body of troops — and only after that did they revisit the fundamentals of marching and formation changes.

Nobody wanted to do tedious basic marching drills by choice, but once they had tasted failure, they understood the importance — that was the thinking behind it.

The first year had attempted a careful, step-by-step approach, but — human nature being what it was.

Especially among young nobles of privilege who had never known hardship.

Boredom with repetitive drills was inevitable; frustration grew as they clamored to move on to field exercises, and enthusiasm for basics dwindled — this revised approach reflected those circumstances.

Given that Elrouga in particular considered fundamental force management the very essence of tactics, the training here placed fundamentals above all else.

Evaluations, too, weighted those basics most heavily.

Such was the first-year introductory curriculum.

The second-year advanced curriculum was also classroom-based.

Those with military experience started here, and the initial coursework was essentially a review of the introductory material.

Command training was of course included, but the major difference from the introductory year was the emphasis on strategic education.

Strategic debates were incorporated, broadening their perspective to view the battlefield on a larger scale — and only those who distinguished themselves here were offered the path to a staff officer's career upon graduation.

If the introductory year was military education proper, then the second year was, in a sense, a staff officer selection exam.

Those appointed as staff officers would undergo additional training before receiving a rank equivalent to at least a battalion adjutant.

The prospect of attaining such a position regardless of battlefield merit, and regardless of whether one was noble or common-born, made the staff officer's post enormously attractive — and those entering the second-year advanced program were driven in a way the first-years were not.

The small stratagems needed for the first year were unnecessary here; all poured their full effort in.

"It looks like things are going well."

In the headmaster's office on the second floor of the academy's second building, located outside the capital walls.
