# Chapter 241: A Maiden's Unwanted Heroic Epic

Red had brought fifty thousand soldiers with him.

From the left wing, the northern side, ten thousand aimed at the main camp in a flanking attack.

From the right wing, the southern side, fifteen thousand swung wide below the river to strike deep behind the lines.

If it was Alberinea, she would without question push into the forest.

His movements were built on that premise.

No matter how advanced Alberan might be, there were limits to the speed of information relay. With an invasion that prioritized speed, the enemy could not possibly mount a fully prepared defense. At best, they could field something slightly more than a single corps.

He had not expected that the enemy, inferior in numbers, would engage him head-on in the forest. By necessity, he assumed it would be a hit-and-run strike aimed at halting his initial advance.

There was also the possibility that such a strike-and-retreat might target Red's head directly, but if so, that suited Red's purposes just fine.

Red wanted to create a situation where he and Alberinea stood face to face.

Strike the main camp from the side, deliver a blow to the rear to disrupt their formation, and drag the enemy headquarters out directly in front of Red.

From everything he had researched, Alberinea was an extremely aggressive commander who favored taking heads.

If she was hemmed in on both sides and the commanding general Red appeared before her, there was a high probability she would launch a counter-attack.

The enemy's main thrust — the judgment of Kokys Agrand.

The high discipline and command capability of the enemy corps on the left flank.

There were some deviations from his predictions, but if Alberinea was here, things would proceed as planned.

For that reason, Red did not consider this situation to be a particularly bad one.

The miscalculation was that the one leading the Alberinea Army was not Alberinea herself, but the Kingdom's Marshal, Selene Christand — though he would never become aware of that error.

"……What is she thinking?"

The land where Alberinea had once fought Aulgorn Hilkintos.

Flanked by forest to the north and river to the south, a broad meadow still spread through the great woods.

There, in bold formation, stood the Alberinea Army — roughly ten thousand troops arranged in two corps' battle lines.

And at their head, sitting atop a jade tiger, was a figure with long silver hair.

Red was seeing her for the first time, yet he did not doubt for a moment that it was her.

Even from a distance, her small frame was evident, wearing a fluttering cloak that in a melee would be easily seized.

A figure utterly out of place on the battlefield — conspicuously alien.

No — the reason she seemed so out of place was not just that.

The instant he had stepped from the forest path onto this field, an incomprehensible pressure had settled over his entire body.

Those ten thousand — the battle lines where Kokys Agrand and his warriors stood — were not what made him tremble. It was the sight of a single girl standing naturally at the very front.

"Good. ……Form battle lines."

"Yes, sir!"

Battle-fervor — no. It was not that.

It was different from facing Felwors Keesliton or standing opposite Kokys Agrand.

There was none of that boiling sensation, the blood-surging fury of meeting his nemesis.

Rather, it was as though ice water had been poured into blood that should have been seething.

Without thinking, he stared down at his own trembling palms.

*——Am I……frightened?*

He asked himself, and glanced around.

The soldiers around him were raising their voices, beginning to form their lines. But something was off. Their voices were pitched slightly too high, as though something was slipping out of alignment. Or perhaps too much force was being put into them. As if crushing their fear under sheer willpower.

Red was not the only one trembling.

What Alberinea had done was utterly incomprehensible.

The northern corps was holding their offensive well. It would still take some time before the southern flanking force made contact with the enemy rear. Even so, that window was brief. As the Alberinea Army's main camp, the logical move was to withdraw forces during that time.

Even if she intended a counter-attack or reversal, it would only have meaning if she at least *appeared* to be retreating. It was precisely because they leveraged ambush in the forest — an environment where numerical advantage mattered less — that such action against this large army could mean anything at all.

To stand in open ground like this and conduct a frontal battle was absurd.

If the enemy had numerical superiority it might be another matter, but whether viewed locally or as a whole, the disadvantage was overwhelmingly theirs.

Even if she were to take such action, she should have positioned herself at the exit from the forest where Red's forces now stood. There was not a shred of tactical sense in deliberately forming those ridiculous battle lines with the bridge at their backs.

With things as they were, a full withdrawal of the Alberinea main camp had become impossible.

Tactically, strategically — it was beyond comprehension.

No — and that was precisely *why* it was unnerving.

Now, for the Alberinea Army to safely exit this forest, the destruction of the twenty-five thousand under Red's command was essential.

Which meant that Alberinea intended to crush Red here with less than half his strength.

It was madness. It was reckless.

He had flanking forces moving on both wings. There were no reports of ambushes.

He could not see the iron war-golems she had supposedly used against Galshan and Elsren.

No matter how he looked at it, this was not the act of a sane mind.

And yet, looking straight ahead — at Alberinea, who simply watched them form their lines without issuing a single order — what he felt was still that same incomprehensible dread.

*——It was as though the entirety of this space lay within the belly of something vast.*

The advantage was his. The initiative was his.

And yet, regardless of such logic, a heavy air clung to his body.

"What is Alberinea……?"

"I don't know. But stay on full alert. At the very least, she has more than enough confidence in her plan."

Red could only answer the question that way.

Had he been in the opposite position, he could see no means of turning the situation around. Even setting aside the outcome, their advantage only grew further — there was no benefit to her stance that he could see.

And his soldiers felt the same.

The confusion was not limited to Red's side; it seemed to exist among Alberan's soldiers as well. In a situation like this, silently watching the superior enemy form battle lines — it was a clear deviation from the logic of military science. It was not in their original plans either.

In this brief window, the only information conveyed to them was that Alberinea had a secret strategy.

Under the heavy atmosphere, Alberan's soldiers exchanged uneasy glances — and yet, the fact that they did not defy their orders and continued forming their lines was a testament to their training and experience.

They knew full well what manner of being Alberinea was.

This was not the first time they had witnessed what appeared to be reckless action.

Opinions on her personality were divided, but there was not a single person in this army who doubted her overwhelming strength and ability.

Galshan, Elderant, Elsren — the invasions that had made everyone despair, Alberinea had shattered them all with ease.

Her power was beyond question, and the woman who had challenged a godlike ancient dragon, exchanged true names, and won its friendship was nothing less than a living myth.

And so they watched Alberinea with faint fear and strong expectation in their eyes.

And, paying no heed to such gazes, Alberinea moved.

Once she confirmed the Vezrea army's battle lines were mostly formed, Alberinea walked the jade tiger forward.

It was a leisurely movement — soldiers on both sides exchanged glances, wondering if she meant to offer an opening salute, but the jade tiger stopped partway, in the center between the two armies.

Then Alberinea used her curved sword to cut loose two large pouches hanging from the jade tiger's waist, opened their mouths, and flung their contents roughly around her.

What scattered across the meadow was a multitude of mana crystals.

As if her work was done, the jade tiger yawned, turned away, and headed back toward the Alberan main camp.

Then, as the jade tiger leaped over the battle lines, the girl picked up one of the crystals — and crushed it.

The hard mana crystal shattered with a high-pitched sound, transforming into blue, dense mana visible even to those without magical power. The mana from that first crystal gathered wholly into the girl's palm.

Within compressed time, simple curves of light intersected and diverged.

Three-dimensional blue geometric patterns burned like flame atop the girl's hand.

It could be called a kind of computational circuit.

A virtual brain that elevated the girl's thought — already transcending time itself — to still greater heights.

What it signified was one single law and logic.

To most, it would have looked like a dance, or something of the sort.

As though a priestess were channeling a god into her own body.

Or as though she herself were stepping into that domain.

She spun, dancing — and the girl's cloak and long hair swayed gently, and every mana crystal scattered across the meadow shattered at once.

——Accompanied by a crystalline chain of sounds that shook the heavens, blue flame and blue curves enveloped the girl.

"What……is that……"

Murmurs arose from both armies.

There was no one present who could have identified what she was doing.

It was exquisitely beautiful, like a work of art — and grotesquely, overwhelmingly vast, a congregation of magic circles.

Her long silver hair, like two tails, swayed left and right without any wind, her cloak trailing behind.

Blue light darted about her, and within that sphere, Alberinea raised a hand above her head.

The blue light gathered atop the girl's small palm, changed shape, and radiated blinding brilliance.

The light far exceeded the girl's own height, and sent sounds like the atmosphere cracking in every direction.

If one had to describe it, it was something like a spear.

——Or perhaps, it was lightning itself.

It was the lance with which a heavenly god proclaimed its wrath, the staff of storms to scorch all the world below.

The thing that drew an absolute line between god and mortal, heaven and earth.

The girl, grasping that tremendous power, ran lightly and simply hurled the violence forward.

No one could react to the approaching girl and her spear.

Not the half-fledged who had never killed.

Not the masters who had devoted their lives to martial discipline like ascetics.

Not even the heroes whose names were known to all in their land.

Indiscriminate, unrestrained, unreasonable.

"——"

The flash arrived before the thunder, and the blinding light that filled heaven and earth turned everything effortlessly to nothing.

The bolt of light that pierced the left wing of the Vezrea army's battle lines raised a pillar of fire as though scorching the sky, and a beat later the thunderclap shook air and earth.

Everyone present was struck dumb.

When the flash faded and the smoke cleared, what remained was earth gouged out and melted.

Naturally, the several thousand soldiers who had formed the left wing had ceased to exist entirely.

No — in the rain-like something falling from the sky, traces of them did remain.

If they were lucky, parts of their bodies might have retained their original shape, permitted to tumble upon the earth.

What was certain was that from common foot soldiers to renowned heroes, everything at that epicenter had ended a lifetime of accumulation in that single instant.

The girl's hands moved naturally, without pause, shaping violence.

A second lightning spear struck the right wing of the battle lines.

The same scene replayed — evaporating bodily fluids formed mist, charred and melted flesh fell to the surface a moment later.

Alberinea's warriors remained frozen, staring dumbly at the sight, and——

"R-Retreat! Fall back!!"

In the world where time had stopped, the first to move were the Vezrea warriors.

The difference in reaction was only natural.

Clear death and violence were directed at only one side.

This place was a world ruled by one single law and logic.

Everyone understood that standing against it was futile.

Just as no one raises a sword against a storm, no one braces a shield against one — it was simple natural law.

No matter how great an army they brought, no matter how perfect their formation, no matter how grueling their training or how desperately they struggled——

In the end, they were frail creatures without any means to defy the will of heaven.

Some stood stock-still, unable to process the reality before them.

Some had been blown off their feet and crouched down, barely clinging to breath.

And naturally, most of the survivors began to retreat, and even valiant warriors — those whose role was to command — screamed for withdrawal on their own initiative, without waiting for their general's orders.

——In this place, Alberinea was the one and only god.

No one doubted it. Two spears had decided the course of battle, yet Alberinea, unconcerned, fashioned a third great light-spear and hurled it straight ahead.

It was like kicking a pebble on the road — there was no particular meaning to it.

They would have cleared a path even if she merely walked; they would have fled before her.

She knew that, and yet it did not matter.

She hurled it. A pillar of fire rose. Another two or three thousand lives vanished.

She had found them vaguely *in the way*, and for nothing more than that *vagueness*, thousands of lives were taken without a thought.

The pinnacle of logic that a genius might spend a hundred years to weave.

Computed and woven in a single breath, the spear was art at the theoretical limit — incomparable to a dragon's breath — death itself, streamlined to perfection.

The lightning spears that carelessly pierced and collapsed the battle lines were violence far too immense for a single person to wield, and yet she felt not a shred of hesitation or fear in using them.

Everything in her sight was unpleasant to her, and was in fact obstructing her.

That alone was enough for her to deem them all worthy of death.

To her, this world was overflowing with things that did not matter.

She had no interest in their lives, no interest in what efforts had brought them here, what good deeds they had done, what wrongs they had committed.

Everything they had built was equally worthless to her.

No matter how many thousands, tens of thousands — or even hundreds of millions — of their lives were piled upon her scales, they would not waver in the slightest.

She had been that kind of being since the day she was born, and that had not changed.

——The girl born as a princess of the kingdom was, from birth, an aberration.

The girl moved forward without a thought for all those she had killed.

Surrounded by countless blue magic circles, her cloak trailing behind her — two long silver braids, tied with pale pink flower ornaments, swaying left and right.

She did not even run.

Blue curves appeared at the girl's back.

Mana formed wings and pushed her body into the air.

She floated lightly over the gap in the lines she had so meaninglessly created, and landed.

Before her was the headquarters — she observed that the commander-in-chief had not fled, and walked forward.

"……Are you the supreme commander?"

She addressed the young man standing dumbstruck, gripping a greatsword — and did not stop walking.

She was not particularly seeking an answer. Whether or not he answered, she could simply kill everyone.

If he answered, it would save a bit of trouble. That was all the question amounted to.

"……Yes."

The young man — Red Lani — dragged his greatsword into a stance, and looked at the soldiers flanking him.

Despite the situation, the soldiers suppressed their fear and nodded.

Everyone understood beyond any doubt that they would die alongside this commander.

They spread out, taking positions on both sides of the approaching girl.

Every one of them was moving now solely to give their deaths some meaning.

"You're Shelna's enemy. ……I'll have your life, Alberinea!!"

Red shouted and lunged——and Krische simply stopped.

She glanced at the soldiers to her left and right — and that alone was enough.

The floating blue curves raced toward them like lightning, too fast to follow, striking the crystals at their chests or clenched in their hands.

The last thing they saw was a pair of utterly expressionless violet eyes.

——In an instant, blue light engulfed them, and with a roar, flesh and blood burst apart and scattered.

Without accomplishing a single thing, their existence simply vanished.

If a reason had to be given — they had defied the one and only law and logic.

There was nothing more to say of their deaths.

Red no longer felt even shock at the effortless deaths of comrades who had fought alongside him for years, nor at the loss of every means he had to win.

As though wringing out everything he had built, he unleashed a slash from the side, cutting upward.

The girl dodged it by merely leaning back.

The next stroke, and the one after that — the girl read each blade at a distance of no more than a sheet of paper.

Four, five, six, seven, eight — every ounce of martial skill Red had accumulated in his life, the girl simply evaded.

To everyone watching this thing that could not be called a fight, it was plain as day.

What separated them was not the gap between child and adult — it was a simple principle.

A blade that would never reach.

As a human, as a warrior, even with the pinnacle of martial prowess — an unbridgeable chasm between heaven and earth.

To even compare strength, to speak of whether one could match the other, was presumptuous. An absolute rift in power.

It was, indeed, nothing more than natural law.

Frozen violet eyes watched Red as he swung his sword.

To Red, it felt as though he were fighting a shadow that could never be cut.

He realized that the strength honed through the hell of battlefields was worthless before this girl.

"Um."

The girl dodged his sword with ease, cocking her head expressionlessly.

Like a doll or something of the sort — an eerie gesture.

"Was that all, by any chance?"

He squeezed every last drop of mana from his body, seized total control of the greatsword's power.

He tried to strike with violence enough to tear his own body apart.

His sword was already producing speed beyond even his own perception.

But the girl still dodged by a hair's breadth, regarded Red, and then drew her curved sword.

"——!?"

She slid its blade along Red's greatsword.

Angling it diagonally against his edge, she created a tiny notch, and slashed through.

It was an eerily elegant and precise sword-stroke.

The greatsword inherited from his father was bisected by the girl's small curved blade, and Red, suddenly robbed of its momentum, stumbled forward and fell to his knees.

*'——I shall praise you as a warrior. And for that very reason, I pity you. ……You will gain nothing.'*

Those words surfaced in his mind.

Before him stood an opponent that no amount of effort could ever allow a human to reach.

"Saying I'm your enemy means this is revenge for the previous war, yes?"

The girl's iron-reinforced boot kicked the fallen Red in the abdomen.

The force was unimaginable from her slender, small frame — the blow pierced through his abdominal muscles, destroying internal organs, and his muscular body was lifted into the air with a dull sound.

"……But you're so weak. Why did you come to attack?"

Red coughed, spat blood, pressed his left side, and stood.

And still tried to drive a bare fist into the girl.

"Gu——"

"Krische did this, so that this wouldn't——"

Her curved sword severed his arm at the shoulder as easily as breathing.

Then she kicked his body at the same time, sending it rolling.

"……So weak, and Bery is in such a difficult state, and you're getting in Krische's way."

Not a single drop of blood had so much as touched the girl.

"And besides, you were the ones who attacked in the first place……"

The girl's beautiful face twisted, and she pressed her hand to her forehead.

Then she stepped on Red's remaining left arm where he lay.

"……Aah, Krische is really, very irritated."

Red stared up at the girl who was about to kill him.

Shelna's body hadn't taken a single wound from any real exchange of blows — it had been pristine.

Their fight must have been just like this. One-sided.

*——This was the sight she had once seen, all alone.*

"Sh……shel——"

"……Enough."

Before he could even finish speaking, Krische severed his neck without hesitation.

She grabbed the head, impaled it on a conveniently fallen standard, thrust it into the earth, and turned back toward the Alberan army.

Selene, Kokys, Begil —

Even the commanders could not move upon witnessing that scene, could not even raise their voices, and the soldiers were no different.

Amid them, one commander in black armor was the first to cry out in praise of the great Alberinea's victory.

He too had been among the frozen — his shout was instinctive, raised to prevent the Alberinea he revered from carrying out further slaughter, to make the victory definitive.

The warriors in black-lacquered armor responded in kind, and the soldiers followed suit.

What most of them felt toward Alberinea's power was a reverence that should properly be called fear.

To defeat an army single-handedly, in the most literal sense.

Many in history had been called war-gods or divine generals, but none had ever been the genuine article — except the one standing before them.

If one were to put it into words, it was a kind of myth. A heroic epic.

The fairy tales that adults knew were embellished fantasy — yet here they were, made real before their eyes.

The child abandoned by the royal house was found by a hero, became a warrior, became a hero, and reached even the domain of the gods.

They needed some explanation to make sense of the unreality they had just witnessed.

And so, with trembling voices raised high, they praised the hero Alberinea unto the heavens.

The divine child, guided by fate.

The great hero, born as such.

What was needed to accept that absolute individual was faith — and they raised their voices ceaselessly, as though to smother their fear of the aberration that was her.

The girl listened to those cheers with eyes cold to the last.

She had never sought praise, nor honor, nor faith.

She was thinking only of the one woman who waited for her return.

The battle, needless to say, ended shortly thereafter.

Having left everything to her sister and her subordinates, the girl raced toward the capital atop the jade tiger.

From time to time she clutched the small pouch hanging from her neck.

To her, it was the most precious promise of all.

She had done everything in her power, the best she could — and yet there was still unease.

But once she returned this candy to her as always, she would finally be able to rest easy.

*Just a little more, and my wish will come true.*

Resenting the interruption beyond measure, she tried to think only of her, to forget the battlefield, so that she could meet her with a smile.

A kiss, an apology.

Then, once more, reassurance that it really was just a little longer.

When everything was finished, things would be as before.

From now on, unchanged — just the usual, everyday life.

That was the girl's only wish, and she wanted to do so many things to make her happy, enough to forget all the suffering.

The girl believed she existed for that purpose, and did not doubt it.

Her place was not on the battlefield, but in the manor where she waited.

When the capital came into view, she pushed her pet harder than it should go, apologizing as she urged it faster.

Racing through the capital like the wind, she reached the familiar manor.

She took the pouch from her neck, and too impatient to enter through the front door, she stepped into her room from the balcony.

——But no one was on the bed. Only a single servant with black hair cut at her shoulders.

Seeing the girl appear, the servant stood dumbstruck, then her face crumpled and she covered it with both hands and fell to her knees.

"——"

Feeling the shadow of a thought she did not want to imagine, the girl asked timidly.

Contrary to that faint hope, the servant shook her head.

The small pouch slipped from her hands and fell to the floor, making a hard sound.

She could not even pick it up, and simply stood frozen in place.

As though time had stopped, as though all warmth had drained away.

Color vanished from the world, and her thoughts refused to understand.

She swayed, her eyes darting.

She pressed her forehead and shook her head.

Unable to accept that the most precious, irretrievable time in this world had been taken from her.

Inside her head, like tinnitus, the battlefield's high praise echoed.

As though they feared the girl, as though they were afraid.

——That voice, praising high the heroic epic the maiden had never wanted.

# Chapter 242: The Frog in the Great Sea

"Onee-sama, today Kreschenta made a pumpkin pie, your very favorite."

The girl who entered the room spoke in a bright voice.

Red-glinting golden hair — the beautiful girl in a white one-piece dress approached the bed, plate in hand.

Anne bowed to the entering Kreschenta and quietly stepped outside.

Kreschenta, well aware there would be no response, sat on the edge of the bed, set the plate of pumpkin pie on the bedside table, and stroked the cheek of her sister lying there.

She was not sleeping.

Yet it could not be said she was awake.

Her gaze was vacant, sparing Kreschenta only a glance.

Staring into some empty distance, Krische lay motionless, as though she had become nothing more than a doll.

When Kreschenta lifted her sister's body to a sitting position, it felt slightly thinner than before.

She deftly took up a fork with her right hand, broke off a bite of pumpkin pie, and brought it to those lips.

"Onee-sama, please eat. It's my pride and joy."

When pressed against her lips, Krische slowly opened her mouth, bit into the pumpkin pie, and swallowed it immediately.

For several days she had not so much as touched food or even water.

Having grown anxious as Krische's condition began to deteriorate, Kreschenta had pleaded desperately, and finally she had begun to eat again — but she never moved of her own accord.

She did not cry. She felt neither sorrow nor anger.

She had simply been like this ever since she came home.

"……Kreschenta, Krische isn't hungry."

"That simply cannot be true. Today as well, you will eat every last bite."

*You must get well soon, after all*, Kreschenta said, and Krische listened without a flicker of emotion.

Kreschenta pressed the next piece upon her regardless.

The meal was a mere task.

When she finished, as if fulfilling a duty, Krische lay back down.

Kreschenta started to say something, stopped, lowered her eyes.

Then, in a bright voice, she said she would go clean up, and left the room.

She leaned her back against the door and let out a quiet sigh.

She closed her eyes and asked.

"……What is it?"

Black hair swept and tied to the side.

Anne was watching Kreschenta with a worried expression, and lowered her eyes.

"……No, it's nothing."

Kreschenta glared at Anne with irritation, then looked away.

"Selene-sama should be coming home today. ……Perhaps that will change things for the better. While Kreschenta is away, please stay by Onee-sama's side and make sure she doesn't do anything rash."

Anne nodded — *yes* — and then moved her lips again, and stopped.

*What is it*, Kreschenta asked once more, and Anne bowed her head.

"……I am sorry. I have been harboring unworthy thoughts regarding Her Majesty the Queen."

She said it, and looked up.

Anne met Kreschenta's gaze squarely.

"But regardless of the truth of those thoughts, I revere Her Majesty the Queen as a lord worthy of my service and love. ……I simply wished to convey that."

Kreschenta looked back at Anne, and spoke as though exasperated.

"Unfortunately, Kreschenta does not place trust in words."

"Yet there are things that cannot be conveyed without words. That is why I wished to say it, just once. ……Please forgive my impertinence."

Kreschenta stared at the servant for a long moment, then looked away.

"……Nothing but incompetent servants. Do as you please."

Kreschenta said it and left.

Anne kept her head bowed until that figure disappeared around the corner of the hallway.

* * *

A bolt from the blue — that was the only way to describe it.

Krische had, quite literally, raced through the forest and hurled lightning spears.

To many soldiers, it must have seemed like a genuine natural disaster.

No — more than that. Krische's lightning spears effortlessly annihilated not dozens but over a thousand soldiers at a time.

Selene understood anew that even the Jaleia Gashea and the Baumje Ira had been considerably restrained by her standards.

Krische's absolute power, capable of bending the laws of the world itself if she wished — even stopping time.

It was easy to see why she had been able to fight that dragon.

*'——The principles of magic are actually quite simple once you get used to them, so I asked Krische-sama long ago to please keep it a secret and refrain as much as possible. It's a power that could become anything, after all.'*

When Selene had told her about Krische stopping time to save Bery, she had said it with a troubled look.

She always knew everything about Krische.

She understood the reasons and the intent behind what was hidden, and yet there was something, just a small something in her heart, and she swept it away with happiness.

It was always like that.

There were always two conflicting feelings inside Selene.

A clean emotion, and one that was somewhat dark, sticky.

Trivial. Small.

When it came to Bery, Krische was reckless, and for Bery she would do anything.

That was the Krische Selene knew, and having accepted that, Selene loved her — and loved Bery too.

So that was fine.

*'Selene-sama must be thinking it too, at least a little, no? That if Argan-sama weren't around——'*

Kreschenta's words, lashing out in frustration.

They could not have been heartfelt — and yet, the reason she had bristled for even a moment was that she could not say with certainty there was no sticky feeling stirred by those words.

*'I……I need a reason for being at her side. So at the very least, if I can't manage even the minimum of what I ought to do, I can't forgive myself.'*

She had wanted to prove it.

That she could literally stake her life for the two of them.

That she was prepared to do anything within her power.

No matter what feelings might be mixed into her emotions, she wanted to prove to herself that she loved them both with that much sincerity.

She could not do the things that Krische, Kreschenta, or Bery could — but to be of help to them.

Even Selene, hopelessly small and possessed of so little power compared to them — if she gave her absolute all, she could be just a little bit of help.

Selene had simply wanted them to believe that.

——And the result.

*'As Krische-sama said, it's already just a mopping-up operation. No trouble to speak of. ……Selene-sama, you may as well leave it to us and head back. General Remin should arrive before long.'*

*'I should——'*

*'That's a terrible face you're wearing. ……With all due disrespect as a soldier, Selene-sama, you're in a dangerous state right now. Don't push yourself.'*

Kokys said it as though looking at something painful, as though worried.

*'I do want to say this much — neither I nor anyone else saw any problem with Selene-sama's tactics. I don't mean to suggest any lack of ability. Even without Krische-sama, we would have gotten through this just fine. Please, don't blame yourself. You have done nothing wrong.'*

*'……Is that a kind consolation to a benefactor's daughter?'*

*'You're free to take it however you like. ……But I would like you to believe it's not merely lip service.'*

She had wanted to throw a tantrum like a child.

But Selene believed his words, at least somewhat, and understood the kindness behind them.

She did not want him to be disappointed, so she obeyed.

She had gone to the battlefield and accomplished nothing.

Without even managing the bare minimum of her duties, she had been sent back to the manor.

*'……Marshal? What about the battle——'*

*'Krische killed the enemy commander and ended it. The rest is a mopping-up operation; General Karnarius is in command. I'll brief you. General Remin, continue assisting as you are.'*

Passing Teknrea on the march, she delivered only the situation and brief orders to the puzzled woman, and rode on.

She was so pathetic, she wanted to die.

And when she returned to the manor——

* * *

——Whether she was awake or asleep, she could not say for certain herself.

# Chapter 244: True Name

Without the will to maintain herself, this body would readily transform into that sphere.

What had originally existed in that form had simply shaped itself to fit the vessel called a body.

It was natural law.

The soul was like a liquid — it had no inherent shape.

Poured into a bottle, it held that shape, but once the vessel was lost, it would quickly return to its original form.

The soul-substance had only a gentle cohesive property, and its shape was merely the container's shape; forming a sphere in weightlessness, as water would, was the natural state.

"I'm sorry, Alkirens-san. Krische was just so happy, Krische got a little carried away……that was thoughtless."

"If you understand, then that is enough. It is presumptuous of me before one such as Krische-sama, but consider it the advice of an old man, nothing more. ……Desire is inexhaustible. Even we sometimes find the thought crossing our minds — of devoting an eternity to our practice. The older we grow, the nearer death draws, the more so."

Vinsreel looked at Alkirens with faint surprise, but the chieftain merely smiled like an ordinary old man.

Then he indicated Vinsreel, and narrowed his eyes.

"Blessed with a fine successor, having passed on all that I have to pass on — and still. ……Yet humans are not individuals alone. We weave as a collective, build many connections, and live. I wish, as our ancestors did, to entrust myself to that flow. ……I find beauty in the common flower that withers and leaves its seed, rather than in the great blossom that blooms forever — and I wish to live out my life so."

His words were carefully chosen, almost supplicating.

Yet his voice was gentle — like an old man explaining things to a child.

"……Please, do not lead someone like me into temptation. I ask that you fully understand your own power, and from this point forward, give thought to every action, every word."

She understood she was being admonished, and Krische bowed her head sincerely.

Strangely, she felt that if Gahren or Elrouga had been here, they would have said much the same — and that thought only deepened her embarrassment at her own thoughtlessness.

"……I will be careful."

Alkirens nodded in satisfaction, and looked once more at the men.

The men nodded, excused themselves, and filed out.

"Lila, prepare a meal. And bring something easy to eat for Krische-sama."

"Yes."

Lila nodded and looked at Krische.

"……Whatever the case, I am glad you are safe. More than anything, that is what makes me happy."

Krische smiled in return, and Lila said:

"Personally, today's story was very……"

*——I wonder if Mother is also walking a new life by now.*

She gazed at the light within the crystal Krische held, murmured those words, and smiled.

"……Indeed. For her short span in this life, she will surely live long in the next."

Alkirens nodded at his daughter's words.

He was remembering his wife, who had died young.

* * *

Taking an extra night in the Kreisharana village as a precaution, Krische discussed the future with Kreschenta.

Kreschenta, who also seemed to have settled, offered lucid proposals to Krische's plans.

To release the vast mana filling the source, saturate heaven and earth, and create an environment where Krische and the others could manipulate the very world at will.

And then, using that mana, to bring forth a new dimension — one that weighted the soul more heavily than matter.

Creating a world they could freely control, where management and oversight would be simple, would make stabilizing the soul's vessel — the body — far easier.

They would simply need to assist Bery and the others until they adjusted to their new bodies and stabilized.

Their small garden — the eternal birdcage, where no enemy could exist.

Even having reached into the domain of the Creator God, what the girl sought was only safety and peace.

They refined plans spanning decades as casually as discussing tomorrow's schedule while lying down, and at sunrise they thanked Alkirens and the others, left the village, and returned to Rigaleiwe.

Krische expressed her thanks again, told the dragon she would likely be back soon, and performed the transference.

They chose sunrise to cause as little commotion as possible.

The two landed near the capital, slipped past the guards, and made their way to the royal estate.

Elvena, who had been feeding the jade tiger early that morning, noticed them and hurried to fetch Selene.

Before Krische could say a word, Selene wrapped her thin body in a tight embrace.

Krische apologized again and again to Selene, whose dark circles betrayed sleepless nights, and waited for her tears to subside.

Then Krische told her what had happened, little by little, and explained the plan for 'the eternal birdcage.'

The relief on Selene's face gradually stiffened, but Krische, still being held, did not notice.

"Ehehe, and there, everyone can be together foreve——"

"……Krische."

At Selene's voice, cutting in, Krische tilted her head.

"I'm glad you came back safely. And about Bery too. ……But that's a separate matter. I'll pass."

"Huh……?"

Selene pulled back, creating distance, wiped her tears, and looked at Krische.

The two servants, who had been listening to this sudden conversation, exchanged glances. Kreschenta narrowed her eyes.

"……Of course, I don't mean to interfere, and I'll support you. ……But, I don't want it."

"Why——"

"……I wonder if Krische can understand the reason."

Kreschenta said it as though exasperated.

"Are you sulking?"

"I'd prefer you not make light of it. ……It might be trivial to you, but I'm being serious."

Her voice did not rise.

Seeing this, Krische moved to close the distance.

But Selene gently held her back by the shoulder.

"Um, Selene, if you're angry——"

"I'm not angry. I love you the same as ever, and I understand how you feel. ……I believe I understand you."

Krische was wearing the mana crystal on a cord around her neck.

Selene watched her squeeze it tightly with both hands — and gazed at the light shining within.

It was a brilliance that was endlessly beautiful, endlessly radiant.

Bery's soul was hopelessly, achingly beautiful.

*What about me?* she wondered.

*How would I look to her?*

A heavy, muddy feeling pooling inside her.

If she had a soul, it must be something unbearably ugly.

"……It's not your fault. It's my problem. ……Please. Let me be alone today."

"Selene——"

"Please. Let me be alone. ……I'm tired."

If something so blindingly beautiful was worth staking a life for, she could understand why.

"……Tired of everything."

There was a story here, cruelly beautiful.

Beautiful characters, a tale of beauty worth dying for.

But Selene had neither the heart nor the right to keep watching.

"I'm……not like you."

——Unlike her, who had been born with everything, Selene was not a special being.
