# Chapter 91: Festivities

Two days since the capture of the Dragon's Maw.

Having relocated their base near Mitskronetia to the south, the army was undergoing reorganization――and in the meantime, a brief respite had been granted.

The Christand army, still with strength to spare, had swiftly completed their intake of prisoners, and Selene's advance preparations had gone off without a hitch.

By the morning of the second day, most matters had been settled, and with the departure set for the following day, the soldiers were given a half day's leave.

Many went to Mitskronetia to enjoy themselves, and the market was livelier than ever, but a fair number also stayed to rest at the army encampment――

"All right, preparations complete, General!"

"Mm. Whenever you're ready."

A portion of them had gathered in a ring to watch an event taking place in one corner of the camp.

The young General, Selene Christand, her elegant golden hair tied up and swaying.

Facing her was a woman whose contrasting black hair swung like a horse's tail――Kalua Belyus.

The two held wooden swords.

Selene wore her usual cross armor, riding trousers puffed at the thighs, and gauntlets, while Kalua wore only blackened gauntlets and shin guards.

What was about to begin was a sparring match between the famed black century's lead assault fighter and the General of Christand herself.

Selene looked somewhat like she was enjoying herself.

Krische, seated in the guest-of-honor seat that had been prepared without her notice, watched this and let her cheeks relax into a smile.

Beside her, Anne was providing Krische with tea and sweets, making it quite a comfortable front-row seat.

Among those gathered around, bets were being placed on who would win; the younger soldiers and most of the black century wagered on Kalua, while the veterans bet on Selene.

Those who had known Selene since childhood were well aware of her ability.

Having sparred with Krische and Bogan nearly every day, her swordsmanship was refined, and the average boastful fighter couldn't even hope to match her.

That said, even among the veterans there were those who placed more weight on real combat experience, and some bet on Kalua, a black century soldier who had racked up countless feats on the front lines.

As things stood, neither could be called the clear favorite――the odds were fairly balanced, with Selene holding a slight edge thanks to her greater renown.

"Krische-sama, who do you think will win?"

Anne, intrigued by the betting, asked.

"Nn……Selene, Krische would think."

Krische, watching from her premium seat, had little interest in the outcome, and was simply in high spirits thinking, *Selene looks like she's having fun, and that's nice.*

Still, if asked to pick one or the other, that was her answer.

Sparring, practice.

These things existed for the purpose of improving technique.

Without inflicting injury, without being injured.

In a fight to the death it might be different, but in a match conducted along those lines, Krische judged Selene would have the advantage.

Kalua's swordwork was self-taught.

Her technique excelled at killing rather than form, but there was a roughness to it.

While the rules were anything-goes, this was ultimately framed as a sparring match――so long as they fought to avoid injuring each other, that roughness would prove fatal.

Selene had devoted herself to honing away all excess from her sword technique and body movement.

The Rollka style that Bogan had favored.

The polar opposite of Rollka's emphasis on seizing the initiative after――the Zain style, which specialized in seizing the initiative with the blade forward.

Then, having learned the key principles of Krische's acrobatic style and freely combining them all, Selene's swordsmanship had already surpassed the ability of ordinary people to follow.

Kalua would likely be able to read it, but she would not escape a hard fight.

Even accounting for Kalua's superior physical ability and instinct, the current edge still favored Selene.

"But Krische thinks it'll be a good match. Two evenly matched bumblers."

"C-Corps Commander……"

Mia, listening from beside her, scratched her cheek at the blunt remark.

Sister or not, was that any way to speak of the supreme commander, the General?

"……Krische, I can hear you."

Selene said with exasperation, glaring at Krische while holding her Rollka stance.

――It was at that instant that Kalua moved.

A split second of distraction, the opening declaration.

The match had already begun.

A momentary gap arising in the middle of a staredown――Kalua, now purely a swordswoman, did not let it pass.

A forward-leaning posture as though kicking off the earth on all fours.

The beast-like sprint, with all wasted motion eliminated, closed the distance between them in an instant.

A wooden sword scraped along the ground like a whip, sweeping up from the lower right, aimed at the underside of Selene's arm.

It was the lunging upward slash that Kalua favored.

Swordsmanship centers on upper and middle attacks.

Those carry more power and are always potentially fatal to the opponent.

Naturally, defenses against such strikes are well-developed――and precisely because of that, what she preferred were attacks from below.

Against a sword swung from nearly straight below, almost no one could respond.

Selene caught it at the edge of her vision, yet she did not panic.

Nor did she think Kalua cowardly for attempting a surprise attack.

Because it was Selene who had deliberately created the opening to lure Kalua in.

Left-side forward shifted to right-side forward――by pulling her left foot back, she dodged the incoming blade by a hair's breadth.

"Tch!"

But it didn't end there.

Kalua's wooden sword swept past before her eyes――and Selene used her own sword to knock it sharply upward.

Momentum compounded by new force sent Kalua's wooden sword high overhead, and Kalua's stance crumbled.

An opportunity――but rather than press it, Selene furrowed her brow and leaped far backward.

Kalua's heel sliced through the air before Selene's face like a blade of wind.

Her stance had been broken, yet she paid it no mind.

Using that very momentum, she had unleashed a spinning back kick.

"Whew, that's scary."

Kalua, who should have been off-balance, held her sword as though nothing had happened and smiled.

"Not bad at all."

It was a beast-like grin, the corner of her mouth pulled up.

An innate sense of balance and physical talent.

What underpinned Kalua's rough, self-taught swordwork was that genius――her physical artistry, which stepped one pace beyond what ordinary people could achieve, was reminiscent of Krische's own.

That single moment of crossing blades――the men watching in a ring around them caught their breath.

It had started in a relatively friendly atmosphere, but once the lid was lifted, it proved to be a dead-serious affair with no room even for words.

The sharpness of blade and subtlety of technique, impossible to imagine from their appearances, drew admiration from everyone save one.

"Anne, the honey……"

"O-oh, yes!"

She had poured the tea and milk but left out the honey.

Anne, who had been absorbed in watching the match with the honey pot still in hand, hurriedly drizzled it in.

Mia looked sidelong at Krische, who seemed utterly uninterested in this closely matched fight, and shook her head in wonder.

Still, the two out there were dead serious.

Each held the other in a focused gaze, coolly thinking through their next move.

What filled Kalua's mind was admiration.

Selene's response to the upward slash from below――not blocking it, but dodging with ease, and on top of that, knocking the blade upward.

It wasn't even the kind of evasion that simply created distance; it would be more accurate to say she evaded in order to finish her opponent off.

*A similar type to myself――she looks like she's on the defensive, but in truth, this young lady is aggressive.*

There was no doubt that Selene, this young General, was the type who preferred to forcibly create openings in her opponent's guard and push in.

Kalua was glad to have confirmed that read here.

A misjudgment in this fight would have been fatal.

In any case, just as Krische had said, Selene possessed skill on par with Kalua's own, and on top of that, this masterful technique――in a match like this, the disadvantage lay with Kalua.

Had Selene blocked, the follow-up would have come; had she leaped backward to evade, the next strike would surely have finished it.

If the blade hadn't been knocked upward, Kalua could have pressed with a flurry.

But from that broken stance, Kalua had no such recourse, and so the spinning back kick from the hip.

Even so, she'd thought it would land a telling blow――but Selene had stayed calm.

She'd pulled back beautifully and reset the bout.

――*That ambush, handled so effortlessly.*

Kalua had known from the start that she was being lured into attacking.

Even so, she had closed the distance at what she considered more than sufficient speed, and the upward slash she'd swung was at full power.

Why had it been handled so cleanly? That was the question――but then she remembered that Selene was Krische's sister.

Krische, who favored a low posture, also centered her swordsmanship on attacks from below.

Hers were blades that came from even lower than low, sweeping along the ground.

Selene had responded with such ease, no doubt, because she had extensive experience receiving Krische's own sword.

Selene must have watched and sparred with Krische's ever-changing swordsmanship far more than Kalua ever had.

Which meant there was nothing surprising for Selene in the sword techniques Kalua had learned from that same school.

How to press the attack――Selene's eyes narrowed as she watched Kalua think.

"My turn next."

With that, Selene stepped lightly forward.

Straight on, posture low.

But not at full power. She closed the distance with room to spare, and just before slipping into striking range――she sank deep.

An upward slash from below, the same as Kalua's.

*A taste of my own medicine?* Kalua started to parry it, but stopped her hand at the last instant and twisted her hips instead.

The sword that had appeared to be an upward slash changed trajectory midway, transforming into a thrust.

Kalua dodged the thrust aimed at her heart.

But it didn't end there.

Selene, without killing the momentum, rammed her shoulder into Kalua and sent her stumbling back.

"Wha……!?"

A slight opening between the two.

――Sufficient distance to swing a sword.

Having pulled her blade back at the same time as the shoulder check, what came next was a sweeping cut from the lower left.

Her stance broken, unable to parry with her sword, Kalua had no choice but to leap backward.

And Selene followed in precise lockstep.

The wooden sword swept horizontally at Kalua's neck.

Dodge that, and a strike aimed at the sword hand. Pull the blade back, and another flash at the neck.

Torso, diagonal slash, neck, arm, leg.

The sword speed allowed no counterattack, pushing Kalua ever backward.

Endlessly calm, weaving feints into her combinations, Selene unleashed a seemingly infinite number of blades.

Return strokes layered upon one another, leaving countless afterimages in Kalua's eyes, bewildering her.

What Selene caged her opponent in was a prison of swords.

By mixing in feints, she preserved slack in both her blade and her stance, converting each into groundwork for the next move.

Stacking minute advantages, bit by bit, until achieving decisive victory.

Krische, who kept dodging Selene's all-out attacks.

――This was the swordsmanship Selene had built to catch her.

Having watched Kalua's opening strike, Selene had realized this was an opponent she must never allow to move freely.

The stamina to keep dodging without losing her breath, and those reflexes. That economy of movement.

Evenly matched――a formidable foe.

But once she'd seized the initiative and begun pressing Kalua, the limit was in sight.

Her opponent was not Krische.

That alone gave Selene room to breathe.

Selene, even as she swung her blade, assembled the future in her mind.

Just as Krische did, she dragged her opponent into her own rhythm, using her blade to restrict and guide the other's movements.

――*Three more strikes.*

Cornering Kalua as though playing a board game――

"Huh?"

And there, Kalua threw her entire upper body backward to match Selene's horizontal slash.

An enormous opening. She couldn't possibly recover her stance from there.

That thought lasted only an instant――an impact shot through Selene's chest.

"Ngh!?"

What had struck her was Kalua's feet.

Having bent backward, Kalua planted both hands on the ground, used the recoil to launch herself, and kicked Selene in the torso with both legs, sending her flying.

Having kicked Selene away, Kalua rose to her feet with fluid grace.

She did not let the opening slip.

She lunged savagely at the off-balance Selene, raising her wooden sword high overhead.

"Got y――……whuh!?"

But what was flung into her face was Selene's wooden sword.

Her body was already in motion――naturally, Kalua had to bat it aside, and that became a fatal opening.

Selene seized Kalua's arm, pulled her in as though hoisting her over a shoulder, and slammed her to the ground.

Then pinned Kalua's sword arm under her knee.

Kalua, as if accepting defeat, cradled the back of her head with her free hand and went limp.

"Ow……"

"Phew……my win for now, wouldn't you say?"

From this position, Kalua certainly could have kept fighting, but this was a match, merely a sparring session.

It wasn't as though the purpose was to trade blows and fight to the death.

"Ugh……I lose, General."

"Mm, thanks."

Selene pulled her up and smiled.

In terms of positional advantage, it was Selene's win, but she had seen Kalua's physical ability firsthand.

Even from that position, the outcome wouldn't have been certain, but as long as both sides were satisfied with the result, that was enough.

Cheers erupted from the watching soldiers, and amidst it all, the two shook hands.

Selene had been bogged down in paperwork lately, so this had been a welcome change of pace.

Opponents of equal skill were few and far between, and opportunities to fight them even rarer, so she was purely enjoying the chance Krische had given her.

Besides, little events like this pleased the soldiers too.

For Selene, the outcome didn't particularly matter as long as she didn't suffer a humiliating defeat, but having won in a proper match still felt good, all things considered.

"You're good. If this had been real blades on a real battlefield, I might have been the one to die. I'm already out of breath."

"Those words are the greatest honor. Haha, well, I'd heard about you from usa-cha……from the Corps Commander, but this was truly a wonderful fight. To have the General herself spar with me directly, I'm truly grateful."

"Likewise, it was a good way to blow off steam. I haven't sparred in a while, so it feels refreshing. I'm counting on you to keep looking after Krische, Kalua."

"Ma'am!"

Kalua saluted, and Selene returned it.

Amid applause and whistling, the two walked toward where Krische was seated.

Krische, who had been watching the two while eating a cookie, looked at Selene and sighed.

"Selene, you're a bit out of shape. Getting winded from something like this. You need to exercise at least a little, or you'll get all pudgy, Krische hears."

"Oh, hush. I just can't find the time."

She pinched Krische's cheek with a little squeeze and smiled.

Krische, looking somehow pleased, brought a cookie to Selene's lips.

"Mm, delicious. Anne, could you pour me some tea too?"

"Oh, yes……um, straight?"

"Please."

Before anyone noticed, a chair had been produced by some soldier somewhere, and Selene sat down.

The soldiers around them had grown animated; one of them stepped into the center to call for challengers, and someone else came forward to answer.

Selene watched the soldiers enjoying themselves with a happy expression, and Krische, satisfied with the look on her face, gazed up at Kalua.

"Being Krische's direct subordinate and yet losing, Krische ought to punish you, Kalua. But Krische will make a special exception today."

"Ooh, usa-chan's so generous."

"Selene looks happy too, so Krische will reward you with a cookie."

"Yay!"

Kalua took the offered cookie in her mouth, and Mia's eyes reproached the breach of manners, but she said nothing.

Kalua vaulted over the ring of onlookers, casually brought back two chairs, settled herself next to Krische as though it were perfectly natural, and beckoned Mia over.

Technically speaking, this area had been set up as a premium seating area for the Corps Commander and the General.

Mia felt awkward about it, but in the end, as usual, she was half-forced into the seat beside Kalua.

What had been a simple sparring session between Selene and Kalua had somehow turned into a full-blown festival.

Participants were recruited for a tournament, and people even brought chairs and tables to sit around Krische and company.

Merchants who had been visiting the camp seized the opportunity to start hawking food and drink, and before anyone knew it, Krische's table was piled high with bottles of liquor and snacks, placed there with a string of *here you go, please, help yourselves.*

As evening approached, soldiers returning from the market joined in, and in the second tournament that began at night, Second Corps Commander Kokys turned up, having heard the commotion.

He defeated tournament favorite Kalua, and in doing so won the right――which had somehow become the prize――to challenge Krische.

Under instructions from Selene to read the mood, Krische refrained from charging in herself, and instead spent more than a quarter-hour simply deflecting the powerful blows from Kokys's mighty arms.

The result was a decision by Kokys's own admission of defeat, but a tipsy Krische proceeded to lecture him for another full quarter-hour about this being wrong and that being wrong, complete with demonstrations.

From there, it turned into a solo recital by Dancer Krische.

"Um, you see, it's like this――Corps Commander Agrand too, but everyone uses too much wasted force in their bodies. You have to make your body flow like a……mm, a whip, yes, a whip, sort of like this……"

A white shirt and black skirt.

Fluttering about as she danced, occasionally flashing glimpses of white thigh beneath the skirt.

Her shirt, faintly damp with sweat, turned translucent in the firelight and heat, hinting at the skin beneath.

Swinging her sword like a dancer in her usual attire, Krische brought the venue to a fever pitch.

Normally this would have been the point where Selene stepped in to stop her, but Mia and Anne's well-meaning solicitousness had kept her cup filled, and she was already passed out. Kalua, similarly drunk, was swept up in the crowd's enthusiasm, calling *yeah, go, go!* along with everyone else.

*One more, one more.*

The unending chants for the dancer.

Krische, who thought she was teaching swordsmanship, was likewise in high spirits at the soldiers' enthusiasm.

Chest puffed out, explaining to the soldiers around her in a slightly big-sisterly tone――*this is how it's done, you see*――smiling softly as she twirled and twirled.

She danced on and on until Third Corps Commander Terrius Melkikos appeared, having come to find Selene on a minor errand.

And so the night of their victory celebration deepened.

# Chapter 92: Good Girl Krische

The following morning, unusually, it was Krische who woke up a hungover Selene.

Krische's army and Velraigh's army formed a column and marched south.

Kirzalan would serve as their base of operations for the time being, and both armies would operate from there.

Selene would lead her own forces and remain at Mitskronetia to stabilize the rear.

She would move as reinforcements or a reserve depending on the enemy's actions, but for the moment, she and Krische's group would be separated.

Krische would head to the capital with Nozan.

And that battle would be the final, decisive conclusion to this civil war.

Such was the plan going forward, but a short while before that.

"……So, what do you want, Vulture? Krische ordered you to be sent to the rear."

"I have come to request permission to accompany you on the coming campaign."

In the tent were Krische and Selene, along with Anne.

Mia and Kalua's Squad One, plus the two captains Korintz and Tagel.

Bilza and his Seventeenth Squad, who had been tending to him, were also present.

And before Krische, who sat with arms crossed and an irritated expression, was the centurion of the black century――Dagra, down on one knee with his head bowed.

"……Bilza, Krische entrusted Vulture to you. Krische gave that order. If it has not been carried out, this constitutes insubordination."

"Please wait. The order was mine, Krische-sama. As his superior officer, I instructed Bilza to bring me here――"

"Krische has not rescinded that order. Under normal circumstances, unless a separate directive or an urgent situation arises, the order of a superior――namely Krische――takes priority. Krische had the squad leaders thoroughly educated on this point."

*Vulture, be quiet*, she said, glaring at Bilza.

Bilza felt like his heart might stop, but he swallowed and met Krische's gaze without looking away.

"……Yes, ma'am. I clearly understood the order and am aware of the basic principle. However, I judged that Centurion Dagra was in a condition fit for combat command, and on that basis, I deemed the rear evacuation unjustified under my own judgment, and I brought Centurion Dagra here to seek a new ruling. The judgment and responsibility lie with me, as Seventeenth Squad leader."

Bilza, a slender young man, saluted, taking care to keep his voice from trembling.

Dagra had been eager to go straight to Krische, but Bilza and the others persuaded him to first visit the black century and ask for their cooperation in accompanying them.

There, together, they held a brief "Krische Persuasion Strategy Meeting," and while Dagra insisted on speaking honestly from the heart, they left him behind and devised plans for multiple scenarios.

The conclusion they reached was that appealing to Krische emotionally and asking for her understanding would be a mistake.

Everyone knew that Krische was fundamentally kind-hearted and a young girl at heart, but they also knew well her tendency to be strict about military rules and regulations.

They had more or less predicted it would come to this, and in that case, the answer they should give her was a "response that could not quite be called a violation of orders."

Military regulations were numerous, but not all were clearly defined; much remained vague.

――*Due to a change in the given circumstances, we sought a new ruling and brought Dagra here to Krische.*

With this line of argument, Krische would find it difficult to be truly angry.

They hadn't broken the order.

Krische's order had been to evacuate Dagra to the rear, deeming him unfit for combat due to his wounds.

But as it turned out, his recovery had been faster than expected, and if he was doing this well, one could argue that the premise of being "unfit for combat" had been an incorrect assessment, making it appropriate to seek a new ruling――this ambiguous excuse of Bilza's was precisely the kind of judgment Krische was worst at handling.

When it came to clearly defined matters, Krische upheld them strictly and dealt with violations harshly, but when it came to judgments that could go either way, she tended to freeze up.

Kalua, among others, frequently exploited this to slip nimbly away from the point of Krische's anger.

Would this work――they were breaking into a nervous sweat, but they appeared to have drawn the right card.

Krische sat on the desk, chin in hand, legs swinging back and forth as though weighing her words.

It was an improper posture, but it seemed to be a habit of hers when concentrating on something.

When that happened, she stopped paying attention to others' gazes.

Pouting her lips in dissatisfaction while swinging her legs――it looked entirely like a child, and Mia watched her with concern, while Kalua and Selene looked on in exasperation.

"Vulture, your stomach."

"……Ma'am?"

"Show Krische your stomach."

"……Yes, ma'am."

Dagra stood and rolled up his shirt, revealing the area around his wound.

Thick layers of fresh bandages were wrapped around his trained abdominal muscles.

Krische frowned, and――

"Wha!?"

――her drawn curved sword sliced through the bandages alone with its tip, perfectly precise.

Everyone's reaction was delayed for an instant; only Kalua made an impressed *ooh* sound.

The bandages fell limp, revealing the stitched wound on his side.

It was still far from fully healed.

Krische pouted.

A stab wound that deep.

Krische had estimated at least a month until full recovery.

Not even a week had passed since then.

She had wondered if perhaps the treatment had gone remarkably well, but looking at him now, she couldn't call it healed.

All Krische had done was apply compression to stop the internal bleeding.

"And you call this fit for combat command? Bilza."

"Y-yes, well, that is……"

"――Krische-sama!"

Dagra raised his voice and dropped to his knee again.

"It is true that I have not fully recovered. The pain remains. But I am able to take up a sword and fight, and I assure you that my ability to command is in no way compromised."

"……That's not what this is about."

"The coming battle will decide the fate of the kingdom. All the more reason――a little pain is nothing compared to……being unable to stand at your side as your support in such a crucial battle, lying in bed doing nothing. That would be a regret I would carry for the rest of my life. If you do not trust me, I will accept even the position of a common foot soldier, a mere orderly. ……Please, allow me to come."

At his words, Krische let out an *uuu* and swung her legs as though searching for what to say.

Anne, watching, thought it seemed like something out of a heroic tale and was deeply moved, while Selene said nothing and simply observed.

She was, as a sister, deeply pleased that Dagra had sworn this much loyalty to Krische, and she could tell that Krische wasn't really all that angry.

When Krische sat in this improper posture and brooded, what lay behind it was less anger than dissatisfaction――she was usually troubled about something.

And given the situation, it wasn't hard to guess why.

Krische was endlessly kind to those who directed affection her way.

Given that this was Krische's decision to make, Selene saw no reason to intervene.

Mia and Kalua occupied much the same position as Selene.

They knew Dagra was one of Krische's favorites, and while his actions came close to insubordination, they didn't for a moment believe she would treat him harshly.

Mia watched Dagra with concern and then looked to Krische; Kalua studied Krische's pouting, troubled face and smiled wryly.

After thinking it over for a while, Krische looked at Dagra's wound and gave him her answer.

"No."

"But――"

"Please, we beg you as well. Please allow our captain to accompany you."

Korintz and Tagel――the two captains knelt beside Dagra and bowed their heads, and Bilza's group behind them followed suit.

Krische let out another *uuu* and sighed before speaking.

"Um, you see……first of all, this isn't about trust."

She pointed a finger at Dagra.

"Vulture is Krische's excellent centurion. And precisely because of that, Krische needs Vulture to keep working hard under Krische's command, just as you have, for a long, long time to come. Um, yes, a long time, like until you're so old you can't even hold a sword anymore."

Krische hopped down from the desk, crouched down, and placed her hand on Dagra's head.

Dagra looked up in surprise and met Krische's eyes.

"After the civil war, the kingdom will be unstable. It won't be just one battle――there may be many. Through all of them, Vulture has to keep fighting under Krische's command. Krische will give Vulture all sorts of orders while Vulture is healthy and well, so Krische wants Vulture to do your best then."

"……Krische-sama."

"But if you participate in the next battle and your wound worsens and you up and die. Then Krische won't just lose Vulture, Krische will be stuck raising the half-trained Mia that Vulture was supposed to bring up, for good. That's nothing short of dereliction of duty."

Mia made a face caught between emotions and groaned, and Kalua laughed merrily.

Anne, who had felt a mysterious kinship with Mia ever since yesterday's festivities, watched her and felt as though she'd found a fellow sufferer.

"A soldier may have to die on an officer's orders. ……But Vulture is Krische's direct subordinate, so the only time Vulture is allowed to push yourself to death is when Krische orders it."

She said it with a straight face, pointing her finger.

"Until then, taking care of your health and living a good, long life is your duty as Vulture. It's an order."

Those words from Krische.

Dagra, looking at the girl who spoke them, lost his words, and he lowered his face and pressed his hand over it, as though to hide whatever was welling up from those purple eyes.

"Understood?"

Dagra did not answer, could not answer.

After a brief silence, he slowly spoke.

"……Yes, ma'am. Until this body crumbles and fades upon the battlefield. ……Until that day, I swear by my name――I shall serve at your side."

Without raising his face, without standing.

He pressed his hand to his heart in salute, and spoke.

Krische puffed her cheeks, as if to say *were you even listening?*

"So Krische is saying, you can't go crumbling on your own. When Vulture becomes a doddering old grandpa too useless to be of help, Krische will kick you out of the army, so you can die after that. Skeleton is nearly a hundred and still on active duty, so Vulture should at least manage that much."

"Yes, ma'am. ……I shall engrave that upon my heart."

Selene smiled with quiet satisfaction at Krische's words and took a sip of her tea.

Those around them understood the meaning behind Krische's words and were glad; Anne, who was by all rights a complete outsider in this scene, was so moved by witnessing this beautiful bond between master and retainer being formed that she was dabbing at her eyes with a handkerchief, murmuring *oh, how lovely, how lovely.*

"Eheheh, usa-chan's such a good girl."

"Huh? Wh――"

Kalua threw her arms around Krische and patted her head.

Krische looked confused and mildly put-upon, yet also a little pleased as she glared at Kalua――and Selene, who had been quietly sipping her tea, froze for an instant and stared at the two of them.

She had noticed it since yesterday's festivities, but Kalua had been paying Krische quite a bit of attention, and Krische seemed to have taken quite a liking to Kalua.

Judging by the way Kalua could do things like feed Krische with an *open wide, ahhh* in front of everyone without batting an eye, she was the pushy type who didn't care what others thought.

According to what Krische had told her, during camp when Selene wasn't there, Kalua and her adjutant Mia had even been sleeping beside Krische.

After Bery had gone all out, here was another suspicious presence that had appeared beyond Selene's line of sight.

Selene felt unsettled.

If she had only been quicker to praise Krische herself――no, her position as General wouldn't allow that.

"Ehehe, this big sis is glad she's usa-chan's subordinate too."

"Ehehe, is that so?"

Cheeks relaxing happily, Krische nuzzled her head against Kalua.

Krische was supposed to be the superior officer, but Kalua didn't seem to care in the slightest.

Selene was churning with vague unease.

She debated whether she should say something.

If she offered some admonishment about *that attitude toward a superior officer*――it could end up negating the good relationships Krische had painstakingly built, despite her difficulty with interpersonal matters.

This was fine for Krische.

And yet, and yet.

Having been staring at the two of them while searching for the right words, Selene's gaze fell on Dagra, still kneeling, and she finally managed to speak up.

"It's fine. ……Now then, Krische is done thinking. Krische wants to wrap this up quickly, so please answer honestly when you're asked questions. Krische needs you alive until tomorrow's execution, so do your best. Daglis, let's begin the interrogation."

"Finally……though it doesn't look like there's much left for me to do."

Daglis stepped forward and removed the gag. Roland, nearly unconscious, had his cheek slapped until he came around.

She must have been choosing where to cut, Daglis noted. The bleeding wasn't as severe as it looked.

Left alone, he would bleed to death eventually, but he'd hold for a while yet.

Practiced work that would put a seasoned torturer to shame.

*Kill me, kill me*, Roland slurred deliriously, and Daglis laughed.

"Answer my questions and the lady's nicely, and I'll let you rest. Officer Elze, you're the witness, so keep it together."

"I-I know that……"

The old man's voice was shrill.

Krische, satisfied that the problem she'd been mulling over had found its answer, looked down upon Roland――whose hell was only just beginning.
