# Chapter 181: Begil Sandika, or the Guardian of the Sacred

(T/N: Author's note: 1 ken ≈ approximately 1.8 m. 1 ri ≈ approximately 400 m. Half a ri is 200 m. These use the tactical-arc scale established in earlier chapters.)

――What Third Corps Commander Begil recalled was the night before the battle began, inside the great war tent of the Alberan army.

*"――So that's the general idea of how Krische's forces will move to dispose of the enemy war elephants and beasts. The enemy has superior numbers and elephants at the front. They'll be convinced that there's no way they can be outpushed, that at worst they can force a stalemate — so we flip that assumption on its head from the very foundation."*

A special javelin unit, formed of mana-users.

The magical javelin troops — formerly the "whoosh-whoosh soldiers" of the Kuro-Fuyo Squad, now the Black Flag Special Service company — would use their javelins to eliminate every elephant serving as the enemy vanguard before the lines even met.

A coordinated volley from trained mana-users — it possessed considerable power even against a full army, yet being javelins, the number that could be thrown at once was limited, and the damage they inflicted was finite.

Their purpose was ultimately the same as ordinary javelins — to disrupt the enemy's footing — but what set them apart from ordinary javelins was, above all, their power.

Whether the target was heavily armored infantry or an elephant clad in thick hide, their spears pierced through with ease.

Five men per elephant, and a single throw would more than suffice.

Squads would be interspersed near the front of the Alberinea army vanguard — the forces of Kokys and Begil — and upon the signal, begin their volley.

Producing results that utterly betrayed the enemy's expectations.

There was also the significant purpose of breaking in these soldiers, most of whom were new recruits.

Though they excelled in ability as mana-users, ordering them to charge headlong into the enemy line from the start would understandably give them pause.

In fact, the black century at the Dragon's Maw had mostly been paralyzed with fear until Krische led them forward.

However, long-range throws with well-trained spears could give them the chance to experience the power they had gained without danger, and hurling a weapon was less of a psychological burden than gripping a blade and driving it into flesh.

To turn these soldiers, whose numbers had been greatly increased, into a proper fighting force, it was essential to build their experience through such graduated steps.

Krische stood and went to the iron board behind her.

The formations of both armies were drawn out with magnetized pieces.

She climbed onto the platform set before it and stretched her arms to move the pieces, advancing them until the two armies interlocked — Gahren assisting her from beside it.

*"The blow to enemy morale will be severe. A full-charge attack is something you choose because you're certain you can overpower the other side. They can't possibly anticipate a complete failure, and at that point the enemy will unquestionably be on the back foot. Initiative is ours. From there, we layer our moves so we never relinquish it."*

Krische twirled atop the platform, her skirt fluttering, and placed both hands on her hips, puffing out her modest chest.

Then she looked down at the generals and corps commanders, caught Begil's gaze, and tilted her head.

Then followed his line of sight — to her own skirt.

*"Begil, is something the matter?"*

*"N-No! I was merely concerned that one might see beneath your skirt——"*

*"……Muu, you needn't worry. Krische is being careful."*

Pouting, Krische seemed somehow bothered by Begil's remark and pinched at her skirt, eyeing the generals and corps commanders seated in their chairs.

Standing on the platform would admittedly make things easier to see from that angle, but the skirt reached below her knees.

Surely there was no chance of her undergarments being visible. She nodded to herself and turned her eyes back to Begil.

*"Krische appreciates the concern, but it's quite all right. Krische is a proper lady, after all. Krische does not show improper appearances to men."*

*"Ma'am!"*

He sprang to his feet and snapped off a crisp, spirited salute. Watching this, Gahren, Mia, and the others looked exasperated, while Dagra rubbed the bridge of his nose wearily.

An exceptionally capable warrior and commander, yes — but the man had a troublesome character.

Had he not held such a position, he'd no doubt be the type to haunt taverns year-round, pestering the serving girls.

*"Krische's own movements will, of course, be the breakthrough of the disrupted enemy force. The first to move on Krische's right wing is the Third Corps Commander, this Begil here. The enemy will have lost their elephants and their morale will be badly shaken — it shouldn't be difficult, should it?"*

*"Of course not. No commander here would struggle with that."*

Krische smiled at his words.

*"So it begins with a single-point breakthrough from there. Making the enemy believe that's all there is — that's where it starts――"*

The right wing of the Alberinea army — the Third Corps.

Even as he gave the order for a full charge, what flickered through Begil's mind was the flutter of Krische's skirt.

Those were not her usual boot-length socks.

Not knee-length — above the knee.

Krische's white thighs — slender overall yet uniquely well-fleshed in that one place — the black lace line of the stockings cinching them had been beautiful, but still.

"……What on earth was that?"

Begil's sharp eyes had not missed it.

The lace at the top of those stockings was connected to some mysterious band that hung down from above.

What he had seen when Krische twirled on that platform, her skirt fluttering — it was no mistake after all.

Just now, during the javelin throw, Krische had performed a forward roll — and in that instant, he had clearly seen the mysterious band extending upward from above her thighs.

What could that possibly be?

It was something unknown to Begil, a man who prided himself on his expertise in the subject of women's undergarments.

――No, wait. Surely, that must be.

Begil recalled a piece of gossip his wife had told him.

Apparently, something called a garter belt had recently begun gaining popularity among the ladies of high society.

A new creation by Arika Sazubern, a famous tailor among the noblewomen.

It was something that upended all conventional wisdom about stocking fasteners, and — strictly between them — certain persons of high standing were rumored to be wearing them, his wife had said, though it was secondhand from a friend. Vague information at best.

At the time, he hadn't given it much thought.

He had merely been impressed that such an undergarment existed.

But reconsidering it now, things came into focus.

"Certain persons of high standing" was a vague expression, but it meant those connected to the royal house — in other words, Her Majesty the Queen and Krische.

And there was Krische, wearing this mysterious band.

Thinking about it, couldn't that indeed be described as a stocking fastener?

Begil was now certain of it.

In his mind, an image of the innocent Krische appeared, and from the name "garter belt" he imagined its construction, superimposing it beneath her skirt as if seeing through the fabric.

It was probably something that wrapped around the waist, like a corset.

Simple enough — a belt with garters, stocking fasteners attached.

From the waist, bands would pull up and secure the stockings.

Begil's superior intellect derived the optimal solution from scant information — constructing in his mind's eye the entirety of its design.

A madman's obsession — no, merely a lecher's curiosity.

"……Is something the matter? Begil-sama."

The former Second Battalion commander of the Christand army — now a battalion commander under Begil's command, serving effectively as his adjutant — Fagran looked at his superior's deeply furrowed brow and serious expression, and asked.

He couldn't have imagined that his commanding officer was seriously contemplating Krische's undergarments on the verge of this charge.

Having once served together under Granmeld, they went back a long way.

Their long acquaintance meant he knew what kind of man Begil was, but even he hadn't thought him that much of a pervert.

How to attack the enemy line, how to break it.

Fagran assumed he was deep in thought about that.

"Given this situation, there is nothing to deliberate. We crush the cowards head-on in a slaughter. That's the end of it."

No sophisticated tactics were necessary.

Just overrun them with raw momentum.

It was simple-minded, but in this situation, nothing else would produce a better result.

Fagran stated this as obvious from experience, and Begil nodded.

"I know. ……Krische-sama showed me something wonderful. No enemy alive can stand before me today."

Begil replayed the glimpse of Krische's thighs in his mind.

Fagran, unaware of what was going through his superior's head, recalled Krische and her subordinates — the Black Flag's javelins — and showed a powerful smile.

He was certain Begil was picturing the same scene he was.

"Hahaha, any man not fired up after seeing something like that isn't a man at all. ……The troops' morale is at its peak. Give the order."

"Indeed. He wouldn't be a man. ……Let's go. I've got something extra to look forward to now. ――Reks, give me a lance!!"

Begil bellowed as he seized the close-combat lance from his escort and leapt out from the battle line.

He appeared before a squad of the Black Flag that had slowed after dealing with the war elephants and unicorns — before Centurion Tagel.

"Centurion Tagel, my corps will take it from here! You men, catch your breath!!"

"Y-Yes sir——!?"

Tagel was taken aback by Begil's sudden appearance from the rear, but answered.

Whether his reply reached the man, however, was uncertain.

Begil accelerated in a single breath and was already far ahead, before the enemy vanguard.

――And with a single lance strike that thundered through the air, he shattered the enemy before him.

"Hear me, you rabble of Galshan! And you craven commander cowering behind them! Face to face with the glorious form of Alberinea — I, Begil Sandika, shall make you regret it!!"

As waves part around a rock.

Though the enemy corps commander was a mere three ken ahead, the Galshan soldiers directly before him tripped over themselves in fear, or fled sideways as if dodging him.

"――Third Corps, follow me!!"

The former First Battalion commander, deeply connected to the black century.

Normally an approachable, easygoing man — but the killing intent emanating from him now, here on this field, was that of a warrior without peer on the battlefield.

Tagel had never considered him merely an ordinary fighter, but the true face of Begil was an entirely different person.

A man who had risen from a common soldier, once serving as the right arm of the Great Wolf — Granmeld Varkus — and for many years the adjutant of the Wolf Pack, the kingdom's mightiest battalion.

*So this is the man called Begil Sandika,* Tagel thought, and with open respect he raised his voice.

He rallied the black soldiers nearby, taking control of the troops so they could resume action at any moment.

With the beast-hunting complete, their command was now entrusted to the valiant Begil.

War cries that threatened to burst eardrums — the sound of shattering battle lines and screams.

Imagining the magnitude of this charge, Tagel too let his cheeks pull taut, and moved to the side of the slightly delayed Fagran.

"Heh heh, Begil-sama is fired up for once. Keep ready to move at any time, youngster — once he gets like this, there's no telling what kind of recklessness he'll drag us into."

"Understood!"

"――Fagran, bring yourself forward! Command the rear!"

"Yes sir! Raise the flags, sound the trumpets! Center First Battalion, advance straight! Second Battalion, break the enemy to the left! Third and Fourth Battalions, break them to the right!! Force the path open!!"

A single battalion of one thousand men was arranged twenty deep and fifty wide as standard.

A single corps commanding five battalions would typically keep one battalion in reserve, making the combat frontage approximately half a ri.

The twenty-deep formation was necessitated by the existence of mana-users.

Each one was a formidable threat in their own right, but a mana-user who overwhelmingly surpassed the rest could easily break through a depth of ten men on their own.

Dividing centurions five to the front line and five to the rear, and keeping one full battalion as a large reserve force behind, were both measures premised on such breakthroughs by mana-users.

Even if one chose to extend the line — say, ten deep and one hundred wide — to outflank the enemy with a wider formation and attempt an envelopment from the wing, there was the fundamental disadvantage of the longer distance they would need to travel.

If the wall was broken straight through the center before those forces reached the enemy rear, it was all for nothing.

This was a conclusion, an answer reached on battlefields not only in Alberan but across the world, and the Galshan army was no different.

A thousand men in a column twenty deep.

However, they structured their army around companies of approximately five hundred.

And commanding those company leaders were the war-lion commanders, who served directly under the generals and held authority equivalent to a combined battalion commander and corps commander in Alberan.

If entrusted with an army of five thousand, a war-lion commander directed a mere ten companies.

The tactical freedom gained from maneuvering nimble five-hundred-man companies was high, and an excellent war-lion commander could, in some cases, gain an advantage over a kingdom corps of five thousand.

But the greater the tactical freedom, the greater the demand on the commander for enormous information-processing capacity and the ability to derive the optimal solution from countless options.

And the more the entrusted forces exceeded that threshold, the more their burden increased exponentially.

It was a military structure in which everything hinged on the excellence of the war-lion commander.

It severely lacked stability compared to the Alberan army. Its chain of command enabled excellent war-lion commanders to exercise nuanced tactical control, but in exchange, those war-lion commanders were tossed about by an endless flood of information and decisions.

If their orders were delayed, if their thinking paused even for a moment, the problems that needed to be processed would come crashing down like a tsunami, exceeding their limits.

This military structure was a problem deeply rooted in Galshan's history.

Once, the war-lion commander had been a privileged class permitted only to the nobility of the Galshan Kingdom. Between them and the company captains beneath them lay an absolute wall; commoners, no matter what feats they accomplished, were capped at company captain.

The Galshan royal house, believing that granting too much authority to commoners would inevitably sow the seeds of rebellion, had spent years building this system to solidify a power structure of royalty at the apex, then nobility, then commoners.

On top of that, they lavished the nobility with favor to keep them loyal — and yet the power structure collapsed when Olgan, born into the royal family, led his rebellion. Even now, with the republic established, that military structure persisted.

Olgan himself, from his experience in the civil war, felt the flawed command structure needed reform. However, he had voluntarily relinquished the authority to make unilateral decisions and taken the seat of vice chairman.

He held the position of Galshan supreme military commander, but this too was power merely entrusted to him by the parliament.

Reforming the military structure required parliamentary approval among many other difficulties, and moreover, for soldiers of common birth, war-lion commander was the most coveted rank of all.

There was fierce resistance to having even a portion of that authority stripped away, and the rank-and-file soldiers also wanted to be led by war-lion commanders of common birth.

It had its problems but also its merits, and the problems were not fatal flaws.

It was also a fact that commoner war-lion commanders had defeated the royal house and led the rebels to victory — and they were holding their own against other nations well enough as things stood.

When they told him *Why not leave things as they are?*, Olgan had no material to flatly refute them.

Military reform, in any era, requires enormous effort.

Even if the current state was a seventy out of a hundred, whether reform would raise it to eighty or drop it to sixty was unknowable until the lid was lifted.

Naturally, most soldiers preferred the status quo over reform with an uncertain future.

The belief that *we've managed well enough until now* was difficult to dispel, and the recognition that the current state was only a seventy was something only Olgan and a handful of others could see.

Reform would take time, and so here today, this very day, they faced the decisive battle against Alberan with the same old structure unchanged.

"War-lion commander! The central Seventh Company captain requests reinforcements——"

"I know!!"

On the left wing of Elkaarl's forces opposing Alberinea, War-lion Commander Lakoul was on horseback, his mind whirling at full speed, focused on how to survive this situation.

He had positioned himself not in the rear of the left wing but at its center, because the initial movement had been a full charge.

To detect and respond to whatever the enemy did as swiftly as possible — with that reasoning, Lakoul had placed himself relatively near the front.

But the situation had been overturned from the foundations.

The premise that *we are the ones attacking* had vanished along with the beasts, and he found himself in the worst possible scenario: taking an enemy charge while his troops were completely thrown off balance.

The front line would certainly be broken through.

This forward position was, by any analysis, dangerous.

Given the situation, he had no choice but to absorb the enemy's assault through the depth of his formation.

The force directly opposite him was less than half his number — five thousand at most.

But Lakoul's own front line was also only five thousand.

The rest of his forces were behind him.

In this situation, the preferred choice would be to push the rear forces forward, retreat himself to the back, and use the depth of the formation to absorb the enemy's assault.

The casualties would be heavy, but under the current circumstances, that was the best option.

Knowing the responsibility and value of the war-lion commander on this left wing, it was sound reasoning to believe he should never allow himself to enter a situation where he could not exercise command — a situation where he would have to cross blades with the enemy directly.

But they had lost Alkazaris. They had lost the war elephants, the unicorns, in an instant.

The soldiers' morale was on the brink of collapse.

If the war-lion commander who led them fled to the rear now, the front line would feel abandoned by Lakoul.

Then they would become nothing more than a disorganized mob.

It was plain that the current situation of *being trampled* would turn into *being massacred.*

The entire left wing teetered on the edge of total morale collapse.

Even if it was the best option, a choice to flee — to reset — could not be taken.

His role as war-lion commander was to inspire the troops here.

"The center breakthrough is unavoidable! Deploy the forces here to the left and right — annihilate the enemy as they break through!"

"Y-Yes sir!"

The breakthrough this far could no longer be prevented.

Then he would use himself as bait to draw the enemy in, then crush them in a pincer — a mobile defense to lure and kill.

The enemy corps commander was likely at the head.

If he could bring that man down, they could still recover from this situation.

Even failing that, simply creating enough time for the forces behind him to reinforce the front line would buy them a temporary stalemate.

With this numerical advantage, there were countless options beyond simply running.

But for that, he needed a small window of time — time to reform the front line.

And so Lakoul drew his curved sword, choosing the brave course of baiting the enemy and striking them down.

"Prepare the archers for a volley——, wh!?"

――But it must be said that he was far too slow in making that decision.

The shock of losing the war elephants in the blink of an eye.

Lakoul, too, had frozen for an instant.

And that delay now echoed across this field.

"I see the craven commander! Who gets to kill this tasty prey!?"

Bloodshot eyes and a smile brimming with madness.

The man who appeared, having carved through the vanguard, was a single warrior.

His half-plate bearing the image of a goddess was drenched in blood — it was Third Corps Commander of the Royal Central Army under Alberinea's direct command, Begil Sandika.

His roar was joined by a war cry like a pack of wolves.

A corps commander at the head, morale driven to its absolute peak.

Nothing could stand against them now.

The cowering front line — in the rear ranks, there were even those who had thrown down their swords and were fleeing.

Run through from behind with spear and blade, the war cries swelled beyond all limit.

"——Rescind the previous order! Stop them!!"

Lakoul screamed reflexively, trying to recall the forces he'd split in two.

But the troops who had already begun moving into a mobile defense formation were thrown into confusion — and in that instant, only a handful could respond.

The order had come only just now.

The soldiers serving as his personal guard were still right beside him.

And there was some distance between them and the enemy.

Lakoul judged that the charge could still be received.

"――Centurion Tagel, charge with me!"

"Yes sir! You heard him — our target is the commander's head!!"

――But the enemy closed the distance at a speed beyond imagination.

If only he had fled in that brief instant, his life might still have been spared.

But it could not truly be called a misjudgment.

It was true that he had misjudged his opponent — but no one could have imagined facing a charge from a century composed entirely of mana-users.

With the blood-soaked goddess-bearing corps commander at the lead, the soldiers behind him were also faster than horses.

Around Lakoul were only a handful of guards, his flag-bearer, and his trumpeter.

And those few soldiers quick-witted enough to have returned in that instant.

Against Alberinea's hunters, an equal or lesser number was not even an obstacle.

At point-blank range, what they unleashed was a volley of thundering javelins.

And their spears were the magical lances that had felled war elephants, pierced through armor.

The men who had become Lakoul's wall — it would have been harder to find survivors among them.

"Wh——!?"

"Dodged that, did you. Not bad."

Lakoul barely sacrificed his horse to avoid a javelin, then dodged Begil's follow-up slash as well.

As a man who held the position of war-lion commander, he was a warrior of corresponding skill.

He leapt backward, trying to open the distance.

"Ghe, ga……"

"――But you're no match for me."

But the madly grinning man — Begil — easily pursued his leap, piercing his throat with the tip of his longsword.

The half-turned sword stance — the polar opposite of Rollka-style swordsmanship — his Zain-style was a discipline devoted entirely to offense.

Through masterful footwork he kept his opponent constantly within range, seizing the initiative.

The instant an opening appeared, what followed was a killing thrust that could pierce plate armor.

Before the swordcraft and footwork of Begil Sandika, honed on the front lines of countless battlefields, retreating meant death.

When he pulled the sword from the man's throat, Lakoul's body sank to both knees like a marionette whose strings had been cut.

Begil lopped off the head without mercy.

Before it could even fall, he skewered it on his blade, displaying his swordsmanship as if putting on a show.

"Hah hah hah, what's the matter, you Galshan rabble? Your craven commander's head is right here! Heh heh — you probably can't understand the kingdom's language."

And, laughing, he raised that head high above for all to see.

"Well, no matter. I'll teach you now through experience. I'll do the very same to every last one of you. If you want someone to blame, blame your own ignorance for challenging Alberinea!"

Begil's follow-on forces expanded the breach while roaring with elation at their corps commander's claiming of a prize head.

In the mad world of the battlefield, at least, there was no one more revered than a masterful killer.

The things that should be cherished in daily life — everyone here knew that the one who stole, shattered, and trampled them was the one who brought victory and glory.

A world where the abnormal shine — and Begil was a beast who loved just such a battlefield more than anything.

Their respected war-lion commander's head displayed for all to see, yet not a single Galshan soldier on the field was mad enough to try to avenge him.

Every one of them was transfixed by the bestial ferocity the enemy corps commander radiated and by the black banner at his side, cowering in terror, their will to fight extinguished.

The arriving Fagran gazed at his superior with amusement and asked.

"Begil-sama, shall we continue the breakthrough?"

Compared to Granmeld, Begil lacked that overwhelming presence, and normally seemed almost gentle.

Perhaps that was exactly why.

When he bared the madness that dwelt within, he became something utterly terrifying.

When Krische had asked him to become Fourth Corps Commander and he had declined — surely it was because he had wanted to see this.

To serve under the former Wolf Pack adjutant — the deviant called Begil Sandika — and savor this sensation that made his spine crawl.

That emotion of mingled fear and reverence.

"No, we won't break through. We wait for Corps Commander Agrand to make his move, as planned."

He looked left — toward the center — and let his cheeks pull taut at the war cries and screams echoing from that direction.

"……No need to rush. For a while, we rest — and kill as many of these cowering enemy soldiers as we can, to prepare for what comes next."

"Understood, sir. A trampling, then?"

"That's right. ……How about it, Fagran — enjoyable, isn't it?"

"Heh heh, enjoyable indeed."

"As for me, this is more of a side job, really……"

Black Flag Centurion Tagel watched the exchange between these two abnormal individuals with bewilderment.

*So this is what a corps commander is like,* he thought with surprise, even as he took control of the scattered troops and ordered each group to rest briefly.

"……Centurion Tagel, do convey my exploits to Krische-sama on my behalf."

"Yes sir!"

"As a reward, I should very much like to be granted the privilege of personal training for my troops."

Begil laughed merrily — hah hah hah.

His aspiration to receive training as his reward — Tagel was once again filled with respect for such dedication to improvement.

――Not knowing that skirts were dancing through his mind.

Tagel offered him a heartfelt salute.

# Chapter 182: The Shattered Giant

(T/N: Author's note: 1 shaku ≈ approximately 30 cm. Jaleia Gashea stands 8 shaku, approximately 2.4 m. The jade tiger is the same shoulder height. One day = 12 koku (loosely split into 6 day and 6 night). 1 koku ≈ approximately 2 hours. Han-koku ≈ approximately 1 hour. Shou-han-koku ≈ approximately 30 minutes.)

――The circumstances, and the opponent, had been wrong.

Over decades of battlefield service, he had claimed one war-lion commander's head and two corps commander heads — a magnificent record.

Gal Bilkees was a fierce warrior who outmatched any ordinary fighter, and it was precisely because of this that, despite being of noble birth, he had been entrusted with command of the elite sand-lizard cavalry.

The fall of the royal house, the rise of the commoners.

Even as those who once held the privileged status of Galshan's former ruling class were ground into the dirt and trampled underfoot, he had been called "a noble among nobles" and commanded the respect of the rank and file.

Character, ability, and renown — it could be said that Gal Bilkees, who possessed all of these, was a rare warrior indeed.

He had watched the war elephants vanish in an instant, and in that very moment had ordered his subordinates into a full-speed charge.

Sand lizards were fast, if slower than horses.

And their hide was tough — he was confident it could withstand the attacks of the enemy's black-armored hunters, those magical javelin troops, unless struck dead-on from the front.

Sand lizards were, after all, merely oversized lizards.

Compared to elephants, their size — the projected frontal and lateral area — was small, and few possessed the skill to throw javelins accurately at such targets.

Unbelievable as it was, they were likely all mana-users.

But only a handful displayed truly exceptional skill — the rest seemed merely somewhat superior to regular javelin troops in power and range. At the very least, they did not possess the force to overwhelm his thirty sand-lizard cavalry.

The assessment of relative combat strength — the most vital ability for a frontline commander — was one he possessed, and his decisiveness and speed of thought were equally excellent.

He recognized that a morale collapse in the vanguard was inevitable if nothing was done, and judging that someone needed to score a victory to prevent it, he immediately launched into action.

To encourage the soldiers behind him — to show them that it was not entirely one-sided, that on some fronts their side was still winning.

If he stumbled even briefly, his rear would fail to follow, and the sand-lizard cavalry he led would be isolated and die for nothing.

Understanding that and still choosing to charge — that was who he was.

He was the commander of the valiant sand-lizard cavalry, and a warrior who lived up to that role.

Every decision he had made was correct.

The finest choices possible — in ten such situations, he would have succeeded in nine.

And that was precisely what made it a tragedy.

――It was simply that the circumstances, and the opponent, had been wrong.

"There's the enemy corps commander!! Crush him!!"

Bellowing louder than the enemy's war cries, Gal Bilkees issued orders to the comrades who had fought beside him for years.

In this situation — charging at the head without fear — it was because he knew his courageous subordinates followed behind him.

And they had no fear because their respected, brave commander rode at the head.

Despite this disadvantage, their morale was at its zenith.

And what appeared before such men was a powerfully built corps commander.

His silver-tiger visor lowered, a fierce gaze visible through the slit.

His full plate was clearly not thin sheet metal — its weight was evident at a glance.

His running feet tore through the undergrowth, each step carving deep gouges into the earth.

Before them — the valor of the enemy corps commander charging at the head.

He could tell at a glance the man was formidable, yet knowing that failure to bring him down meant only a dog's death ahead.

Ignoring the javelins from the enemy front ranks, the sand-lizard cavalry charged on.

"Wh!?"

Just as the distance was nearly closed — the enemy corps commander planted a massive step and leapt sideways.

What he had leapt onto was the head of a fallen war elephant.

Using that tough skull as a foothold, his next leap was like a boulder launched from a catapult.

The steel mass flew toward them, overshooting their estimate.

――He twisted his entire body, unleashing a blow that seemed to crush the very air.

The sound of its passage was a thunderclap — impossible for a lance.

It snapped the sand lizard's neck — a neck thicker than a man's torso — with casual ease.

Gal could not believe his eyes as he threw himself from the lizard.

The sand lizard's body, which must have weighed ten times that of an ordinary man, had been lifted clean off the ground.

Not a lance — an iron bludgeon.

Eight shaku and three sun, all steel — a thick, small-sword-like tip at the end of a heavy shaft.

He swung this weapon of insane proportions with ease, not the slightest waver in its center of gravity.

Before them stood Kokys Agrand.

A true monster — the undisputed number one among those who served under Alberinea.

Had his reaction been even an instant slower, the lance would have broken not just the sand lizard but Gal's own torso as well.

――But that was the end of it.

"……Wh?"

A black shadow appearing from the side precisely severed Gal's head from his airborne body.

"Captain Bilkees!?"

A refined face twisted with battle-madness — beneath the helm, its black crest revealed nothing but elation.

This was a man who had once risen to the rank of general in the Holy Empire on talent alone.

Aleha defied inertia, leaping backward, coiling his entire body, and——

"――!?"

He pierced the young adjutant's throat, sending him swiftly to follow his superior.

The halted lead riders blocked the following sand lizards——

"Fire!!"

――At centurion Dagra's command, what descended was a storm of magical javelins that could kill elephants.

The screams of lizards and men filled the field.
