# ※ A Rather Considerable World-Setting Introduction

■ — Settings

○ Knowledge current to the 1300s of the Unified Calendar.

○ **Mana**

A force that has existed since ancient times — mysterious particles drifting through living bodies and the atmosphere.

It responds to the will of living things and takes on a semi-material form called soul-substance. Normally it drifts through the atmosphere, though it sometimes coalesces like a liquid, and in the earth it crystallises into what can be called mana crystals; much about its properties remains poorly understood even today. In open air it tends to disperse, while in stable water or soil it tends to attract and concentrate — yet it sometimes moves contrary to this pattern, continuing to confound researchers.

It appears, at minimum, to possess the power to interact with, break down, and generate matter — though the precise principles behind this remain unexplained.

Among researchers, some argue that this is a force seeping from overlapping other worlds, while others advance the hypothesis that the world itself is shaped by mana; none of these views have moved beyond inference from observed properties.

The atmospheric mana concentration has increased due to the Zenith, and during the Kleinmeil era much research was conducted; the hypothesis that collective unconscious influence affects mana, formulae inscriptions, and the world itself has been proposed, though understanding of mana as an energy remains limited.

○ **Formula Inscriptions**

A means by which mana is used to interact with space and matter.

Called "command sentences upon the world," they take effect when a specific pattern is inscribed, forming the foundation of a mage's practice of magic and sorcery.

By inscribing mana with formulae that generate kinetic energy and govern the ten thousand phenomena, a variety of effects can be produced.

Currently, the foundational system is the one discovered by the Fifth Alberinea (who earned the Alberinea title through this discovery) — a combination of the four great inscriptions of earth, water, fire, and wind with the two-element inscriptions of stillness and motion. The primordial inscriptions used by Alberinea and the mages who preceded her are employed only in the manufacture and study of magical machinery.

A significant factor was the discovery that the formula inscriptions — theoretically capable of infinite expression and interaction — possessed what is called an inscription limit: descriptions exceeding a certain scope produce uncontrollable disruption in the formula, and so primordial inscriptions requiring vast numbers of inscriptions have been abandoned for large-scale mana use.

Given that the formulae inscribed by Alberinea and the genius Elgeinst who learned from her are extremely precise and minute, it is thought that this problem existed from the beginning; outside the 'Great Rite of Heavenly Change' recorded in manuscripts across the continent, no other example of large-scale magic being deployed exists.

Following an extensive review, the discovery of the four-symbol, two-element inscriptions was reached.

The incorporation of fire as a physical phenomenon, combined with the remarkable efficiency gains compared to primordial inscriptions, has led some to think that the faith of those with no understanding of physical phenomena or mages — as a kind of collective unconscious — influenced the law; this has deepened the mystery of mana as an already baffling energy.

○ **The Source**

A hypothetical entity proposed by some scholars.

It is said that in the depths of the world lies an enormous mass of mana, from whose overflow our world is being permeated — but this has not been proven.

○ **The Soul**

A matter of faith.

Said to be the essential nature of living things leading to rebirth or paradise, many researchers have pursued its clarification; they have not been able to prove its existence, and it is described by some as nothing more than unscientific belief beyond the grasp of even mana. 

○ **Mana Wielders**

There is no living thing without mana within its body; among them, those creatures bearing mana intense enough to transform their flesh are defined thus. This refers primarily to humans, but in the broader sense includes mana-bearing beasts and magical beasts.

Living things called mana wielders have, as noted above, transformed bodies; it is said that they possess soul-substance organs inside them that run on mana. These break down and convert ingested food and waste products into mana — the result being that their bodies require no elimination, among other physical changes from ordinary living beings.

Mana's influence on the body is significant, and ageing is generally slow. Their bodies often pause growth during young adulthood, then age gradually after thirty; though individual variation is wide, and there are said to be people who remain unchanged past fifty.

They are also resistant to disease and possess tolerance for poisons and alcohol; mana wielders tend to have robust bodies compared to ordinary individuals, and the general view is that they are biologically superior.

On the other hand, as though in compensation, mana wielders are generally more prone to infertility than non-wielders, and the birth rates of the nobility, who are almost universally mana wielders, tend to be low.

When one partner is not a mana wielder, this tendency weakens; however, noble mana wielders in particular tend to age early and often feel physiological aversion to non-wielders who require elimination, and such marriages are generally not favoured.

The influence of ambient mana concentration on the birth of mana wielders is considered certain, and documentation makes it clear that the proportion of mana wielders increased dramatically following the installation of the Zenith and the Great Rite of Heavenly Change.

The proportion born near the World Tree, which arose from the Zenith, is especially high, and many scholars cite this as the cause of the mage supremacist thinking — the ideology that considered mana wielders to be an evolved humanity — that emerged in the middle to late Kleinmeil period.

As a matter of fact, mana wielders are superior in every capacity to non-wielders except for the ability to produce descendants; and while they do not proclaim it loudly, there are mana wielders even today who regard non-wielders as no different from beasts.

○ **Mana Crystals**

Crystal formations said to be born from mana crystallising in the earth; closely resembling quartz.

Physical properties are close to quartz, but within these crystals mana can be handled as though inside a body; by inscribing magical formulae into them, various physical interactions via mana become possible.

They can serve as light sources, generate or remove heat, attract water — or in some cases radiate energy to combust and destroy objects; their uses are many, though from a mana-efficiency standpoint they are not often exploited as weapons.

Extensive research was conducted in the past, but military conversion proved difficult in terms of efficiency, and today a limited number of researchers called mages confine themselves to individually developing and devising new formulae.

These researchers also strongly function as magic artificers specialising in creating household mana crystal items, and today the terms 'mage' and 'magic artificer' are often used interchangeably.

Since the invention of magic — the technique of manipulating and applying mana outside the body and mana crystals — their relative value has declined, but even now they are widely used as a recording medium for formulae, and large mana crystals of high purity trade at high prices.

High-purity artificial mana crystals also exist, but cannot be mass-produced, and price rises steeply with size, so large mana crystals are often naturally mined specimens.

○ **Alberinea**

The inscriber of magic formulae — founder of adepts — spoken of as the primordial sorceress.

Elgeinst Ramiru discerned the existence of magic from the various mana crystals she left behind, continued his research, and with that technology brought a thousand years of stability to the continent.

Within Kleinmeil, Alberinea is the symbol of the great mage, and one is permitted to bear the name of Alberinea by virtue of monumental achievements in the history of magic. During the Kleinmeil era, the name itself carried no formal authority, but by custom those who bore it were regarded with reverence as standing at the apex of all mages, and tended as a result to occupy high positions.

Since the collapse of Kleinmeil, Alberinea has become a title bestowed on the apex of magic academies — the greatest sorcerer of the age.

○ **Mages**

A collective term for those who manipulate mana.

Magic academies have adopted the Kleinmeil classification, and apply the term 'mage' to anyone certified by a magic academy as possessing a certain level of ability. Formerly there was a distinction between those who could only inscribe mana crystals and those who could inscribe magical formulae — 'mage' versus 'adept' — but as formula inscription became common, both are now simply called mages.

Those with the capacity to teach and guide others are 'mentors'; mentors possessing the capacity to influence relations between nations are 'great sorcerers'; and at the apex stands the title of 'sorcerer,' conferred together with the Alberinea title.

The rank of a magic academy is often determined by its count of mentors and great sorcerers; the Albernaria Magic Academy is currently the highest-ranked magic academy, recognised by the coalition, and is permitted to have an exceptionally large number of mentors and great sorcerers enrolled simultaneously.

○ **Virtual Musculature**

A form of magic available to mana wielders.

Also called body extension, it refers to the enhancement of physical capabilities by means of mana.

It is the technique of constructing a soul-substance body overlaid upon one's own to manipulate the body and amplify muscular strength for high physical performance; mana wielders may employ it unconsciously, and as such the technique itself is not especially advanced. However, the more one attempts to use it deliberately, the greater the concentration and mental stability required, and the difficulty rises steeply.

In some cases one may be unable to control the high physical output, and the recoil can wound oneself — a double-edged sword. Wielding this freely under the extreme conditions of combat is difficult even for seasoned mana wielders, and it is not an exaggeration to say that skill in its operation is what determines a mana wielder's worth.

Mana wielders on the battlefield often train their bodies and clad themselves in a 'musculature of armour,' the main reason being to absorb such recoil and control excessive force. Sometimes they wear armour of a weight impossible for ordinary people to carry in order to achieve that stability — though many martial practitioners view this effort as actually moving one further from the ideal.

Today it is not especially mainstream as a primitive form of magic; outside of studying it as a foundation for mana manipulation, only some mana wielders learn it as a means of self-defence.

○ **Magical Beasts**

Beasts that are mana wielders.

Most are ordinary creatures that were born bearing mana; generally they are larger than the original beast, and their colouration tends toward blue hues.

They are invariably skilled in the use of mana, and the physical capabilities arising from their bodies combined with this are incomparable to human mana wielders; magical beasts are widely feared throughout the world as objects of terror, or revered as local gods.

The reason human mana wielders do not grow large is unknown; some researchers think that will influences mana and they unconsciously suppress it, and this view is comparatively accepted.

Magical beasts are presumed to be long-lived as well, but their danger has prevented verification.

Tigers, wolves, hawks, boars, and others — magical beasts of creatures that inhabit mountains and forests are more numerous, but other examples also occur, and there is a possibility of magical beasts arising from any creature.

Maritime accidents attributed to magical beasts also occur, and many seafaring nations devote considerable effort to countermeasures.

○ **The Phantasm World**

One of the worlds spoken of in folk belief.

Reine's Heaven and the like are a variety of this, said to be a different world from ours. It is fundamentally fictitious, and no one believes in its actual existence.

○ **Dragons**

Also sometimes called ancient dragons or divine beings.

Wise beasts said to have once ruled the world, their most notable features are their enormous mana and their great bodies. With frames described as fortress-like, scales like ore, their mana said to be incomparable to mere magical beasts, and old records note that a dragon's roar can alter the terrain.

In territorial struggles among dragons said to have occurred in the distant past — the Dragon Wars — countless dragons rampaged, and many civilisations were destroyed as a result; the name of the Lusheran Empire, which flourished greatly but earned a dragon's wrath and was destroyed, is well known.

However, as there are no records of dragons seen flying through the skies in the past several centuries, tales of dragons are often viewed as mere fables; in some regions they remain objects of faith, but there are those who harbour doubts about their actual existence and capabilities.

Their flesh and blood are said to harbour great power, and many traditions survive of humans who ate it gaining tremendous strength; their bodies are treated as a kind of elixir.

Two were hunted by Kleinmeil's dragon hunters, but the ensuing retaliation caused devastating losses and again made the dragon's terrifying nature known.

Much of the flesh and blood of the slain dragons dispersed as mana, and a portion magically preserved was consumed in research into immortality; many died in the process, and the Sixth Alberinea — said to have succeeded in achieving immortality through this research — was assassinated by the dragon-cult 'Holy Spirit Brotherhood,' and the secret art was lost.

The Fourth Alberinea, who possessed overwhelming power, met an unceremonious death before Kushenalars only thirty years into her tenure; the Fifth Alberinea, who discovered the four-symbol, two-element inscriptions, ended up dying in fewer than fifty years; having lost two great Alberineas in succession, mages developed a wariness about involving themselves with dragons, and became somewhat reluctant in that research.

Thereafter, pursuing immortality through research into magical beasts — which similarly were said to lack a natural lifespan — they would eventually produce what became known as the demi-human.

● Bery's mental notes.

● **Mana**

Something born from an energy called the source.

Flickers of the source, which pulses irregularly through multiple dimensions. The material world appears to be something it created; in what should be called eternal time, it is thought that various accidents accumulated through flickers irregularly generated by the source, and a material space-time — a material world — came into being.

Like an accident piled upon an accident, yet if there is possibility, given infinite attempts, even the inevitable can become the inevitable. Not far from the mark, I think.

The world is made from flickers of the source, and mana is those flickers.

Because that is so, if one considers that mana has the power to move through every dimension and transform everything — much falls into place.

Mana itself, beyond gently attracting one another and repelling when too densely packed, has no particular rules; even if physical time were stopped, without blocking the influence from the source world, placing it in a state of perfect stillness is impossible.

The trajectories generally considered baffling are so because, rather than with other mana in the material world, attraction and repulsion operate between mana in the source world — which cannot be observed from here; if the source world cannot be observed, fully understanding its laws is impossible. Hence the trouble mages have.

● **Formula Inscriptions**

Rule-sentences for performing world-alteration via mana.

Formulae inserted into and rewriting the world shaped by mana; in theory, anything conceivable can be freely altered.

The invention and subsequent development of magic without reliance on mana crystals, combined with the dragon-hunting problem, made it certain that humanity would develop this at an accelerating pace hereafter; out of concern for planetary collapse, the introduction of simplified inscriptions based on the four-element theory that had been argued by some mages of the time was decided.

It rewrites the entire world and sets limits on formula inscriptions: causing disruption in the formula for large-scale descriptions exceeding a certain scope, restraining them.

By constructing an automatic simplification mechanism — 'Fuyopita' (a name that sounds very like Krische-sama — the sound of it is charming) — that supplements efficient spatial and material interference from the essential world through specific inscriptions, formula inscriptions are controlled; simultaneously, dimensional interference leading to the source is pre-blocked.

Currently, so that no malicious space-alien, should one exist, can do dreadful things, the 'Global Safety Fuyopita-Net Plan' is in progress, with vast numbers of virtual minds automatically propagating out into the far reaches of the source world, extending the Fuyopita net.

The space-aliens do exist, so it seems — and that is probably because I said something to that effect once. I have been reminded once more: one must not say careless things before Krische-sama.

● **The Source**

An enormous mass of mana existing in the realm called the source world.

It is unclear what gave rise to the original; yet even this source is thought to itself have been a flicker or fragment of an even greater source — the 'primal source.' What brought that primal source into being remains unknown even now, and will likely remain so; that is probably as it should be.

Through the 'Global Safety Fuyopita-Net Plan,' the existence of other sources — differing in scale and state — has been confirmed. In the material world they appear to be connected to celestial bodies like the moon and the sun, but I, knowing only the logic of the material world, find the structure and laws of the source world difficult and beyond my understanding.

One who lives on paper can only understand things on paper. It is very difficult; I think only Krische-sama and Kreschenta-sama could truly comprehend it.

When I grow curious and ask about it, being unable to respond to Kreschenta-sama's repeated 'idiot' is a little frustrating, and I have decided to ask as little as possible.

In any case, the Fuyopita net replenishes mana from each source and, for its vast and unfathomable purpose, is apparently energetically extending its net out toward the far reaches of the source world today as well.

● **The Soul**

The essential nature of living things.

It may be that at the end of the unfathomable series of attempts by which something like Jaleia Gashea could emerge by chance, what came to be from the flickers of the source is the existence called the soul.

A portion of it seeps into the material world, shapes living things, or enters living things; through predation and self-replication, it evolves. A 'cycle of souls' was established between the source and the material world, became complex, and — as a result — perhaps gave rise to humanity. Though I don't know the details.

At the very least, the reason living things can freely grasp and manipulate mana is presumably because the existence called the soul, arising in the source world, evolved with that as a precondition.

It fundamentally exists overlaid upon a living thing that serves as its material vessel; in the case of mana wielders with a high mana ratio in their bodies, there are apparently cases where consciousness and thought are governed primarily by the soul rather than the brain.

When one reaches physical death, one is drawn toward the source and becomes one with it. Fundamentally without a vessel it is unstable, but depending on the strength of the will, one may be ejected as a flicker of the source while preserving the soul, leading to what is called 'reincarnation.'

However, I think it is fine for the mechanism to remain a mystery; and I have decided with them not to clarify it particularly.

To know the unknown is a pleasure, but that does not necessarily surpass the ignorant and unknowing pleasures.

● **Mana Wielders**

Living things thought to arise when soul-substance, relative to the usual balance of ordinary matter and soul-substance in a living thing, gains the upper hand. This includes magical beasts as well; in the phantasm world with its high mana concentration, they are very numerous.

The density and size of the soul itself presumably also plays a part, but as noted above I have not clarified this.

Because the proportion of soul-substance is high, affinity with mana is also high; in theory, through complete mana mastery of the body, it is possible to remain ageless. Since the body that was to vanish was also presumably brought closer to soul-substance by the dragon's blood, I think there is a high possibility that had it successfully processed that and survived, I would have gone on without ageing — but when I said this, ojou-sama became angry. I find that slightly unreasonable.

My present body too is simply one where the proportion of soul-substance has become complete; it is not so very different. By Krische-sama's intention, as before, desires exist, I feel heat and cold, my five senses are clear. Daily life is unchanged.

If I wished, I could make a body that requires no sleep, dismiss drowsiness — yet looking at Kreschenta-sama, who still complains irritably in the mornings, I feel she loves from the bottom of her heart the daily life carried on within that inconvenient body, and I find it endearing.

Those two, who can do all things freely, surely found their happiness within that very inconvenience.

● **Mana Crystals**

A stable crystalline form where mana combines gently without repelling.

Because mana is subject to the influence of human will, it tends to form away from human settlements or underground. The residual mana of a dragon's roar and the like may also crystallise.

Around the manor where we usually live, owing to mana turbulence they do not grow — though in the phantasm world they crystallise very easily; or rather, they tend to grow from the ground. They have on a few occasions grown through and collapsed small houses and cottages that ojou-sama built, so the work of periodically checking under the floorboards has increased.

Gururun seems quite fond of mana crystals, which can be called crystallised mana; when given them as a treat he bites and swallows them looking very pleased. They have no taste, but I wonder if they could be used in some kind of cooking.

● **Alberinea**

Krische-sama. The Heavenly Sword — originally a warrior's title, but it now appears to be an honorary title for mages. Going by what Meedeliarieze-sama says, the regard for her among mages is that of a divine being, but considering it is Krische-sama, that is in a sense inevitable.

Currently in peace, the person at the very top of what is called a magic academy — a school for studying magic and sorcery — bears the name Alberinea. That is a very good thing.

● **Mages**

What should now be called the collective term for those who manipulate magic. In the broad sense, mana wielders themselves are apparently sometimes called mages.

In the narrow sense, the lowest rank — those who can inscribe formulae into mana crystals. Those who can manipulate magic are called adepts; and those particularly excellent adepts are called sorcerers.

There seems to be much to it, but the names vary by region, and I have not investigated closely.

Magic gave individuals tremendous power, as I once imagined it would.

For that reason, large magic academies like the Albernaria Magic Academy are owned not by one nation but by the continental coalition, which gathers talented mages from each country, educates them, and distributes them — serving to maintain the balance of power between nations — and the current equilibrium appears to be well sustained by this.

Even as I have decided to show as little interest as possible and to remain uninvolved, peace is far better than conflict.

People compete. Perhaps in the future, conflict will never vanish entirely.

But I always hope that such attempts at peace will continue for a long time.

● **Virtual Musculature**

At this point it has become part of my body, and there are many times I forget I am even using it.

● **Magical Beasts**

Mana-wielding beasts, and existences born from the collective unconscious of people.

Whether imagined by humans or not, dragons in the broad sense would likely be included here.

On land, dragons can be said to be the greatest; but within the sea there are magical beasts the size of islands, and deep-sea sightseeing voyages, where strange creatures can occasionally be seen, have become one of our pleasures.

In terms of intelligence, they are generally smarter than others of their kind; some exist who convey their intent readily through telepathy. Still, the majority live by instinct, and their growth to great size is also thought to derive fundamentally from self-defence instinct and predation.

That humans do not grow large is presumably as had been said from the beginning, but martial mana wielders tend to be large-bodied individuals, and there may be some influence from this.

Gururun, who pretends not to understand while clearly comprehending most of what is said, stopped growing the moment he was told that if he grew any larger his food would be reduced.

After all, the element of wishful thinking is quite large — and thinking about that is rather amusing.

Perhaps this was also partly the reason that I and Krische-sama stopped growing so young. I, while saying various things, wanted to remain a younger sister who could lean on her elder sibling; Krische-sama too, I think. Perhaps Kreschenta-sama as well.

Even while stretching to reach, there is something in people that they don't want to surpass.

Thinking about such things, I can imagine various circumstances in various matters, which is a little enjoyable.

● **The Phantasm World**

A world created in a different phase from the material world.

Should I call it the obverse and reverse of a coin, or the inside of a coin? Or something that sees a golden coin as blue, or the world of a thin veil cast over a painting? Its relationship to the material world is something like that — a place made in the gap of the offset.

Close to the source, yet still a material world; if I were to describe it simply, it is a world where mana density is far higher than the material world, and one more strongly subject to its influence.

Although everyone has since grown accustomed to these bodies, the half-soul-substance bodies that realise immortality are not physically stable; without mastery by the soul they tend to collapse. Created to supplement this, as a world of high mana density where the influence of will is strong — I believe that was its original conception: a place to have us learn the technique of complete mana mastery of the body, as Krische-sama and Kreschenta-sama do.

I still remember how for the first few years Krische-sama worried about all manner of things to do with our bodies. After we grew accustomed, there was no particular problem, and we became able to go out into the material world as well.

Perhaps because we are existences from a different phase, we tend not to be noticed by people over there unless we deliberately draw attention; they are difficult to recognise. For example, even if one is greeted, the person over there feels they have been greeted, but the impression of what kind of person it was leaves no trace and is difficult to recall.

Rather like the inhabitants of a dream, perhaps.

If Gururun walks through a town pulling a cart, everyone steps aside; yet though that great body should be visible, no one registers his existence — which is remarkable.

However, some animals of sharp instinct seem able to discern the difference, and we are occasionally threatened by cats and dogs. And people near death, or those in extreme mental states, sometimes perceive us too; when travelling to mountaintops, at sea, or in deserts, or when holding revels, we occasionally encounter such people. They are mostly stranded travellers or ascetics, and tend to treat us as gods or something of the sort, which is somewhat distressing — though it is convenient as an excuse. Indeed, it can be said that Krische-sama and Kreschenta-sama are existences close to that.

As for what enters the phantasm world itself, it is mostly magical beasts; but an acquaintance of Krische-sama visited once. Someone who created, after the Alberan Kingdom — what is now called the thousand-year empire, Kleinmeil — I was quite surprised to hear it.

He had apparently just wanted to come and be praised by Krische again, Krische-sama said; but this is not a place one can reach merely by thinking of it.

He surely carried many thoughts of his own. Yet from what I heard, he seemed very happy — and I think that brief exchange alone was enough for him.

Near the manor, his grave still stands today.

● **Dragons**

Perhaps, like spirits and some magical beasts, existences born without form; the overwhelming power worthy of the name of gods seems to me to be because dragons were made as gods.

As a living thing they seem far beyond that category; I thought it fit to understand them as great flickers of the source taking shape — the awe of nature that brings cataclysm — formed into something. Even if not human, the fear of death is common to beasts too.

When asked, I thought about it and tried to answer, but there was no reaction; the next time a visit was made, a different topic was raised — and then, about thirty years later, I was abruptly told it was not a bad answer, and the true name was permitted.

The dragon's sense of time is still not well understood, but at least one was satisfied with it.

In terms of instantaneous thinking speed, they are surprisingly not so different from humans; the greatly differing aspect lies in an exceptional memory capacity, like that of Krische-sama and Kreschenta-sama.

Forgetting nothing, no matter how small; remembering things from the distant past as vividly as a moment ago — when the continuation of a brief conversation from three hundred years prior was taken up as though it were the continuation of yesterday's talk (though sound is not produced from the throat) I genuinely could not keep up.

Unlike Krische-sama and the others, who follow their desires of the moment (and are sometimes a bit dog-like), perhaps because there are no animal desires (indeed, closing the eyes is apparently not the same as sleeping), past and present are blurred.

What appears to be capricious thinking is likely rooted in that high memory capacity; within dragons it is apparently not considered a problem, and is simply normal.

This tends to mean that even in board games one takes a long time, enjoying them at leisure; and when actually engaging, the thinking pauses are repeated at a pace that is unbelievable. Rinasera-sama is particularly slow, but when I gave up and came to see it as simply that kind of thing, I found I could in a sense be at ease.

However, every time she devises new, complex games herself and attempts to win with numerous invented stratagems — that is a little unfair.

Yet the more complex they become, the shorter Rinasera-sama's daily thinking time, and she is in fact strangling herself; she has apparently not noticed. The more complex I guide things to become against me, the better — if I can guide things well, I should be able to close the final match, whenever it comes, with a satisfying victory.

The process of things is important, but a contest is still more satisfying when won.

Brought by his father, he visited the Academy of Magical Studies.

Alberinea, who reportedly showed her face only occasionally, happened to be visiting the academy that day.

"Alberinea, this is my son Elgeinst."

"Ni, nice to meet you……!"

A large research hall.

Among the mages facing countless work tables, she happened to be wearing an apron-dress for some reason — and so at first he did not realise the person was who she was, but apparently she was always that sort of unusual person.

He had heard from parents, grandparents, and their friends that she was unusual; but he had not expected her to be dressed as a servant, and regretted staring at her so shamelessly.

She was informally a royal princess and, ranking just below Marshal, Alberinea.

Normally one should not even be able to address her — or even be permitted to meet her gaze — the very summit of the clouds.

As the saying went, like a fairy — supreme beauty prefixed with the word *absolute*.

With a figure that might be called a small girl, her long silver hair and violet eyes were striking.

Alberinea, who generates the ultimate of logic and beauty, was herself, irresistibly, a beautiful existence.

Though it was too presumptuous, what Elgeinst felt toward her could well be called love at first sight.

He could feel his ears and cheeks burning as he bowed his head.

"You need not bow."

"Y, yes……!"

By a quiet lake's side, like the ringing of a bell.

Her voice's resonance, too, was beautiful.

"It seems he is nervous at meeting the Alberinea he admires……please forgive him."

(T/N: *Pikyun* is one of Krische's characteristic onomatopoeic expressions for quick, sharp movement. Left as-is following convention.)

"Yes. I shall continue to apply myself."

Elgeinst was dumbfounded.

His father Kulan, though he had been unable to succeed the director position from his grandfather who had stepped back from the front lines, was vice-director.

One of the top five mages in the academy.

His formula inscription was polished; for simple ones he could inscribe in the blink of an eye.

"U, um……!"

"……?"

That she described this as slow — what kind of formula inscription did *she* produce?

Alberinea traced a fingertip along her lips, tilting her head.

"I, I was rude——"

"……, Y, yes……"

Alberinea drew her face close and peered into Elgeinst's eyes.

It could not be said Elgeinst was ugly, but his face was not particularly handsome.

Its distinctive feature was narrow eyes that left one uncertain whether they were open or closed.

He could only be captivated by the beautiful violet looking into his own eyes, the long silver lashes framing them, the neat line of her nose, and her cherry-blossom lips.

(T/N: *Pipin* — another of Krische's characteristic 'aha' sounds; rendered phonetically. *Nemunemu* means "sleepyhead" — derived from *nemu*, drowsy. Her nickname for Elgeinst.)

"U, well……in, indeed, for my son, it would, it, it is a supremely honourable thing to……"

His confused father nodded, and *isn't that right!*, Alberinea smiled in delight.

Her smile, childlike, made her look even more like a blooming flower.

A little slow to realise she had given him a nickname.

"Well, let us see — since he is Tiru's grandson, let us make it Tiru's."

So saying, it shone with blue light.

"Something like this, I suppose. Inscribing large numbers of formulae takes time, but increase the count and it is quick. It does not work because everyone tries to do it on a single line. You cannot do properly unless you can inscribe at least this much in an instant."

Placed in the palm of the speechless Elgeinst: a frying pan of mana crystal.

If he were to try to replicate it — with Elgeinst's current technique, it would take several months.

What she had shown him was like casting threads carelessly into dozens of needle-eyes.

Smoothly, naturally, with extraordinary ease, as though breathing.

All the mages Elgeinst had spoken with until now had said Alberinea was the ultimate of mages.

Without room for doubt, what stood before Elgeinst was the ultimate.

The sensation of a kind hand.

Not a gallant sword or helm.

Why had Alberinea made a frying pan?

Thinking on the reason for that, Elgeinst nodded.

Normally the formula inscribed in a mana crystal releases its effect when mana flows through it in a single path.

However, in the lines Alberinea had inscribed, he noticed there were multiple branch points along the way.

Testing it by flowing mana — depending on which line he flowed it through, it changed to a knife, a ladle, a pumpkin.

Combining lines, something different again.

The value of a mana crystal inscribed by Alberinea was incomparable to a run-of-the-mill one.

Whether what he had felt for Alberinea was a young heart's love, or something closer to religious faith, he did not know.

But that he had wanted her to recognise him — that he could say for certain.

A full year to compile the formulae of the inscribed mana crystal into a bundle of parchment, then replicate them — inscribing a formula to replicate them.

Grandfather, both parents, the mages of the academy — all praised the nine-year-old Elgeinst who clumsily replicated Alberinea's mana crystal as a genius, but Elgeinst was not proud.

*Genius* was a word pointing to Alberinea; no matter how far Elgeinst went before her, he was nothing but a mediocrity.

Elgeinst was always in the academy, but Alberinea's visits were once every two or three months.

That was the only opportunity to speak with her.

Someone who paid no particular attention to standing — using polite language with anyone.

"The formula itself is done once you've memorised the pattern. Afterwards it is simply inscribing them in appropriate spaces and combining them. If one were to use an analogy from arithmetic — it is nothing but repeated addition and subtraction; without thinking anything complicated, just *pikyuu-n*, inscribe it like that."

"*Pi, pikyuu-n*, is it……"

She was, if anything, somewhat childlike in her manner — though he was embarrassed to admit it.

"In the end, any inscription that works is fine, Nemunemu. Approximate is fine. That is how Krische was taught."

A little surprised, then understanding — yes, of course.

Alberinea, too, had had a time when she was learning something from someone.

"The important thing is to make it as short, as concise, as waste-free, and as consistent in lines as possible. After that, if the formula settles well within, that is enough. The mana crystal used is different, so do not try to force an exact imitation — here, and here, it would have been more elegant to expand to this side."

"……I see."

Inscription was like painting a picture inside a mana crystal.

Each mana crystal — its purity gradients, its size, its shape — was different.

Even if one tried to inscribe the same formula in the same shape, since no identical mana crystal exists, nothing fully identical could emerge.

Looking at the 'frying pan' hanging from his neck — indeed, Alberinea's inscription had been laid so as to avoid the areas of low purity.

The purity of a mana crystal affects ease of inscription, and low purity also causes dispersion when mana is flowed.

*— Alberinea's mana crystal had consideration even for those subtle gradations of purity.*

"Krische too, trying to imitate long ago, inscribed in exactly the same way as Nemunemu remembers doing now — but it is the same as cooking: if the ingredients are different, so is the cooking method. Inscribe anything appropriate onto anything appropriate, for each occasion — that way of thinking is what is important."

*Bery told me*, said Alberinea, somewhat proudly, raising a finger.

"Ehehe, yes."

Alberinea placed a hand on her chest over the apron with a fond air.

It was not a name he had heard among mages.

"A truly splendid person who can do anything — the world's finest servant, a teacher who teaches Krische all manner of things……Krische's ideal, she is."

Gripping her chest, *I want to see her soon*, she smiled.

A smile filled with many layered emotions, beautiful beyond measure.

Asking his grandfather later, he was told she was a servant of hers, now deceased.

That Alberinea wore an apron-dress was because, thinking of a servant who died long ago, she dressed that way — so his grandfather said.

She had not even been able to be at that servant's deathbed, he said.

What the words *I want to see her soon* had meant.

At the time, Elgeinst could only interpret them in a pessimistic sense.

# Moonlight's Legacy — Part Two

The twenty years that followed were the most fortunate time for Elgeinst.

Devoting his days to research, speaking with Alberinea when she visited from time to time.

The technique of encrypting formula inscriptions like a puzzle through combinations, or the mechanism of reconfiguration across mana crystals with different effects.

A mana key that responds to poured mana and selects.

By then, no mage could keep pace with Elgeinst — yet even questions no one else could understand, she answered as though teaching a child arithmetic.

She was the very truth that mages had once pursued.

An unfathomable intellect that made all that could be imagined stand as imagined.

Equal to a god in human form, and there could be nothing more fortunate than being permitted to seek guidance from such a being.

The unknown, the more it was known, the more the unknown emerged; at times he would be shut behind a wall.

But a mere moment's conversation with her could strip away the wall that should have been before him and open a new path toward mystery.

The walls were lined with bookshelves and mana crystals, with three work desks.

Elgeinst's private research room, parchment stacked carelessly high.

"……Alberinea, I hesitate as to whether I should ask this."

"What is it, Nemunemu?"

He had long since grown taller than Alberinea.

She, said to be the same age as Marshal Selene Christand, should have been past ninety; yet there was not the slightest shadow on her beauty.

Her unchanged silver hair and beautiful large eyes.

A girl's snow-white skin.

Complete mana mastery of the body — she was human, and in one sense not human.

Unlike common mortals like Elgeinst, she replaced every aspect of her body's operation without remainder with mana.

With her level of ability — life itself would be eternal.

Even when Elgeinst one day reached death, she as his goal would remain in the far heights; he could not know how glad that made him.

"What principle generates the enormous mana of that mana tower?"

"Hmm……"

Alberinea put the tip of her finger to her cherry-blossom lips with a troubled look.

It was her habit when thinking.

Slightly adult-looking, somehow slightly childlike — that kind of gesture.

"It is a secret. Nemunemu is moderately clever, so perhaps one day he might come to understand."

Then she smiled and looked up at Elgeinst.

"Then I will strive to understand."

"Is that so — please do your best."

Saying so plainly, Alberinea stroked Elgeinst's carelessly grown-out hair.

If going by appearances, Elgeinst was far older.

But to her, he was surely still Nemunemu — the boy he had been when they first met.

Elgeinst now commanded respect from many mages, but she was always unchanged.

Elgeinst's feelings toward her were unchanged as well.

And that she treated him as a helpless child was what made him most happy.

"Nemunemu is still at the level of having become fairly good at cutting pumpkins, if I put it in cooking terms. He still has a ways to go on cutting fish and meat — it is moderately deep."

"Yes. ……Yet to hear that I have come to cut pumpkins reasonably well is in itself great progress for me."

Elgeinst gave a wry smile.

She often compared things to cooking.

She seemed truly fond of cooking, and when he asked why, she told him: because it has no answer.

Depending on the other person, the day, the physical condition, the mood — what is *delicious* changes.

Finding the optimal answer each time is what is enjoyable, she said.

That was probably something close to the reason Elgeinst was fascinated by mana crystals this way.

That it could never be reached, that it was without end.

For her, who knew all answers, what remained of the unknown was surely the heart itself.

"……First, I shall work until I can solve that mana crystal. I have not yet found even a foothold."

He was referring to a single mana crystal placed in the academy.

Left by Alberinea with the label *for study*, it was a kind of puzzle.

By flowing mana into the central portion, it could supposedly be solved — but when mana was flowed in from outside, the formula within changed, reorganising the path.

There were several hundred mana insertion points and branch points, and by flowing the correct amount in the correct sequence and combination, a path to the central portion was created.

The mechanism was thought to be a large triple-layered structure; he had managed to solve the second layer, but the third remained entirely beyond Elgeinst.

He had spent some sleepless nights at it, but honestly conceding his insufficient skill, had given up for now and returned to replicating and studying her mana crystals.

"Once you understand, it is simple, I think……but well, with Nemunemu as he is now, it still seems far off."

Alberinea again traced fingertip to lip in thought.

"But well, perhaps if he is at that sort of level, it is about right — I thought it might take some hundreds of years to solve."

"Once you understand it is simple……"

"Yes. Simple."

Alberinea's sense had grown too far removed from human.

Her *simple* was usually, to ordinary people, of unimaginably difficulty.

"……I will do my best."

"Ehehe, that said, you mustn't strain yourself. ……Don't think too hard — approximate is fine. Nemunemu is moderately capable, so you will naturally improve if you go about it approximately, no need to hurry. ……Rather, it is more important not to worry those around you. Look — there are shadows under your eyes."

Alberinea poked a finger at the beneath of Elgeinst's half-closed eyes.

"You should rest properly at home from time to time. If you carry on like this, eventually your eyes will truly not open from the drowsiness. Even without that, no one can tell if they are open."

Not quite an insult, and not not an insult.

Alberinea truly did not mind the smallest things.

Elgeinst had been able to like his distinctive face because she always called him by his nickname with amusement.

He knew this too was mere consideration, and that consideration was what made him most happy.

"……, Yes. Thank you for your concern, Alberinea. That is a troubling thought."

"Indeed. You must sleep properly at night, Nemunemu."

*This conversation is over*, she said, touching the door handle, and Alberinea turned.

"Krische has dinner to prepare so I am going back. Well then, Nemunemu — live long."

"……? Yes, I will take care of my health."

That day there was that addition, and he thought it a little odd, but let it pass as merely the flow of the conversation.

It was some time later before he realised it had been a farewell greeting.

Without particular change, Elgeinst continued to spend his days in the research room, waiting for her next visit.

"Here you are again, staring down that mana crystal today. Occasionally moving is bad for the body, Elgeinst."

A handsome face. Slightly dull golden hair.

Entering the research room as always without knocking, Waltza Remin looked exasperated; Elgeinst sighed and raised his face from the desk.

"For a mana wielder, manipulating mana is not so different from exercise. And I take enough rest. Waltza — occasionally try touching a mana crystal yourself."

"Difficult things like that don't suit me. It is enjoyable to think about actual applications — but research like this I leave to the specialists."

One of his few friends.

The Remin family had made substantial contributions to the Academy of Magical Studies and the Ramiru family, and through that connection had a close relationship with the Ramiru family too.

He had met Waltza when he was ten.

Careless where Elgeinst was neurotic.

With poor conduct, fond of drinking at taverns mingling with commoners — their characters were opposites, yet strangely not disliked, and Waltza appeared to feel the same.

Finding free time, he would visit like this.

"Well, indeed — you, who only ever think practically and about what is right in front of you, are not suited for it."

"A natural thing for a soldier. On the contrary, I respect people like you who don't think about what's immediately at hand, Elgeinst. Are you half dreaming?"

Laughing at Elgeinst's narrow eyes, he filled a cup with black soybean tea from the pot without asking.

Then he sat on the edge of the desk Tiru used to occupy, and picked up a mana crystal placed on the desk.

It was the main core of Jaleia Gashea.

"Don't carelessly try to probe the interior. It will explode."

When warned, Waltza saw the half-finished research notes on the parchment and furrowed his brow.

"……Ugh, is this the Jaleia Gashea core."

"It is. Alberinea suspended production of magical weapons generally some time ago. I thought I should at least study the theory for later."

*Though this core itself is entirely beyond me*, he said, examining the mana crystal.

What was used was a high-purity mana crystal the size of an infant's head.

He could understand that complex formulae were inscribed in it, but analysis of its interior was impossible.

*— What churned within the Jaleia Gashea core was formula inscriptions that filled the interior.*

Without any interference — without even flowing mana — the formula inscriptions inside moved and danced as though they were living things.

It was impossible to make out the formulae inside, and trying to probe recklessly would cause an explosion like a Baumje Ira. He had heard that one of the mages who tried to probe its secrets had died.

Elgeinst had given up on analysing this too.

Formulae Alberinea made so that they could not be solved — no number of ordinary people gathered could solve them.

"The mechanism itself is fundamentally simple. It receives mana signals and returns specified mana signals to the respective joint sections. It is the same as flags or bugles used in the military — you understand the principle itself, I assume?"

"Yes, the way Jaleia Gashea is used I learned lightly about in the military."

The military's flags and bugles convey instructions to the whole army; the corps commander receives them and passes them down to the end-most soldiers, who then act.

"Using that, even I could make a degree of puppet — though as things stand, it would not withstand practical use. It would be good if I could look inside this, but someone of my calibre could not grasp even a fragment of it. I thought I would try a different approach."

"A different approach?"

"What the core does with the signal received from the transmitter crystal is unknown. But I can analyse the signals transmitted from the core to each joint. By accumulating that vast data, eventually I should be able to produce something like a poor imitation."

The cipher inscription — the writhing formula inscription — was inscribed only in the core.

From there, the formula inscriptions that moved the joints — the formula that constructs virtual musculature each time — were difficult but could be understood to some degree with time.

Based on that data, if he could create a core from scratch, Elgeinst could produce something like an inferior Jaleia Gashea.

"……Even an imitation would still be a Jaleia Gashea, wouldn't it?"

"Between Alberinea and me — dragon and ant, our minds are simply too different. To make a monster like the original would not be enough with hundreds of years. What I am thinking is at most something that can construct a battle line and use it as a shield for soldiers."

*You do not understand, Waltza*, Elgeinst said with a wry smile.

"It is abnormal that a monster exists in this age which can effortlessly knock aside even warriors who have devoted decades of their lives to the blade. You, born into the Remin family, close to Alberinea and raised seeing Jaleia Gashea, may find it difficult to understand — but Jaleia Gashea is simply too advanced a technology for this era."

"……I can at least understand this is remarkable technology."

"You cannot. ……Jaleia Gashea is not merely centuries ahead — in terms of its level of completion, it is thousands of years ahead."

He said so, and stroked the core.

*Intellect itself* — no, even this was merely a fragment of a fragment of her intellect.

That she had appeared at all was a bolt from the blue in history.

He would believe her without hesitation if she told him she had come from a different world.

Research was fundamentally something where vast years were spent consolidating the foundations of basic research, then assembling building blocks of vast years.

But she could place finished building blocks in an instant just by thinking of them.

Whether the foundation was crooked made no difference; she produced results while leaving the crooked foundation crooked.

Her inventions had entirely outpaced civilisation's basic research.

It was like giving advanced applied equations to a world of people who did not know the four basic operations.

She was an existence that could only be called genius.

"Well, I don't not understand your Alberinea-faith. With that appearance, even our grandfather cannot hold a candle to her. ……Last time I finally got to spar with her — whatever I tried was read and I lost. I can't see myself winning in a thousand years."

Sipping black soybean tea with an exasperated look.

Among the military nobility, there were virtually no adults who didn't know Alberinea's military capability.

"A valuable experience. ……Though from my perspective as a mage — your mana movement is quite rough compared to General Remin or Alsang-sama. That is surely the first reason you are read."

"……I am told the same thing every single day."

"Hahaha — you too, learn magic. Inscription is good for developing delicate mana movement; one day it will serve your true pursuit of the sword."

"……I'll think about it."

Laughing at Waltza's reluctant reply, he closed his eyes.

"……This kingdom will surely become something equal to eternal. Her Majesty the Queen and Alberinea, as far as I can see, are ageless. ……Our duty as their subjects is to see that such persons are not vexed by trivia, that they may live in health."

To attend to all trivial affairs so that, without connection to war, free from the distraction of foolish things, she and her companions could live happy days in that small manor in the royal domain.

That was all Elgeinst conceived of as his work.

"To continue peace and stability, the diligent and humble effort of ourselves without decay is necessary. I too as a mage, you as a soldier, we must each recognise what is good in the other, acknowledge it, and develop together. ……It is just as well you came, I was thinking the same; I would like your help searching for materials."

"Saying something high-minded just to use me conveniently, is what you really want."

"Do not read malice into it. ……In return, when my puppet is completed I will show you first."

"……Am I supposed to be glad about that?"

There were friendships, however few; there was a goal to strive toward.

Surely a future filled with happiness would continue this way.

Elgeinst thought to himself, vaguely, in the midst of happy days.

*— But* — sometime after the funeral was held for Selene Christand, Alberinea's elder sister who had defended the kingdom as Marshal for many long years —

*That everyday life came to an abrupt end.*

Though he had been taking ample rest since she told him to, he still spent many nights in the research room.

That day too he spent in the research room studying her mana crystal, and what he suddenly sensed was something like a vague unease.

But Elgeinst was not the type to misread what his senses told him; he immediately understood it to be a trembling of mana.

Understanding at once that an enormous mana was moving somewhere, he hurried outside.

A few mages remained; they looked toward Elgeinst with surprise, but he paid no mind and went straight to the outside.

If that great a mana trembling were to occur, the cause could be only an abnormality in the Zenith, or someone other than Alberinea.

"This is……"

Coming out to the courtyard, the Zenith showed no abnormality; yet an even greater sight was in the sky.

The sky bright as midday.

And an enormous mana swelling, a light that placed a sun among the stars.

The sound would not arrive for some time — yet when it did, it shook the whole body, the very ground.

The vastness of that sound, thundering beyond thunder.

Despite being far distant, even wind was felt; Elgeinst fell silent.

Extraordinary mana was being scattered across the sky.

"Wh, what is happening, Ramiru-sama?!"

The other mages, perhaps noting Elgeinst who had rushed outside, had also noticed in the explosion's wake.

Hurrying out, they came close to Elgeinst.

Elgeinst did not answer; as they did, he too stared up at the sky in shock.

Chain explosions continued.

The trailing flash of a trajectory was, in principle, like that of a Pishuune-style mana projection.

But it was not such a *toy*.

If the countless mana dancing wildly in the sky were to fall directly below — this capital city would without question be reduced to scorched wasteland in an instant.

No need to think.

He understood whose hand was behind this.

Among Alberinea and the Queen, there was no other existence capable of such things.

Before logic could work, he was certain the two were fighting.

Too distant to see their shapes — only explosions and flashes were visible.

Yet at this distance it was visible, and the pressure of mana was felt.

If witnessed at close range, what kind of sight would it be?

Some fell, legs giving out; some fled somewhere in a panic.

Those who more than anyone wielded mana expertly — the mages — were the very ones who understood what scale the mana released above them was.

A single one of those lights descending would without question cause the deaths of ten thousand — and that number grew.

From hundreds, to thousands, to tens of thousands — impossible to count.

Light was flying around as if to cage something inside.

That light was released and described an arc — remarkable enough on its own; yet not just that. As though with will of its own, the enormous light was targeting something.

A sight that exceeded even Elgeinst's understanding.

Alberinea and Queen Kreschenta.

This was something that should be called a battle of gods — of those who had transcended humanity.

Not only Elgeinst and the others — the populace who had been sleeping also appeared on balconies and the road in their nightclothes, looking up at the sky.

Elgeinst had no room to give thought to them.

A time less than a quarter-hour in duration. (T/N: 半刻 in standard usage = quarter-hour.)

Elgeinst was simply overwhelmed by it, staring up at the sky unable to do anything else —

The vortex of mana suddenly vanished; the mages exchanged glances and spoke all at once.

During that time, Elgeinst continued to gaze at the sky, kicking off a wall and up to the roof of the academy to reach a slightly higher vantage.

# Moonlight's Legacy — Part Three

A thousand against fifteen thousand.

Between both armies, in the centre, walking through the air — a single man.

The mage-emperor ruling all mages of the continent, Elgeinst Ramiru.

Around Elgeinst, inscribing themselves — like lightning — blue formulae.

Formulae that can be inscribed in open air.

Magic's power for peace.

And using the power of magic, the instrument given by Alberinea, the rapid re-unification of the continent.

*— The world was peace itself.*

Then — what should he do next?

The more he thought, the more darkness seemed to close in before him, and what he felt was suffocation.

To escape it, Elgeinst reopened his research alongside the affairs of state.

These should have been not-bad days.

Rather — from the perspective of a mage, Elgeinst was passing his days in what should be called a paradise.

Ample funds; the enormous mana bestowed by the Zenith — the World Tree.

With the revolutionary power of magic now in hand, much of the unknown could be elucidated; he could reach for all manner of things that had previously been beyond his understanding.

An unknown that could not be solved in a lifetime was before Elgeinst.

People praised Elgeinst.

Calling him Emperor, calling him Alberinea, revering him.

What Elgeinst had obtained was, so to speak, the whole of this world.

And yet still — what Elgeinst felt was a sense of stagnation.

In days that should overflow with happiness, he felt emptiness and was at a loss.

The joy of knowing consoled Elgeinst for a brief time, but quickly it dissolved into void.

"……To know — to solve — and then what."

Sitting on a branch of the World Tree, he looked down.

The white capital in moonlight came into view; he looked at it and touched the branch on which he sat.

To not know things, and come to know them, is enjoyable.

That joy he knew. He had not lost it.

Many mages simply spent their days smiling in the joy of the pursuit of knowledge.

When Elgeinst devised some new magic they would rejoice; if he casually taught them something they would look at him with admiration.

He understood the feeling. He found them dear.

Once, Elgeinst was the same. One of them.

Alberinea — the unknown itself — a great pioneer, an eternal goal.

Something that defied understanding no matter how one tried.

For those who pursue, no existence is more wonderful than such; and the endless unknown before Elgeinst now was surely equal to it.

The pursuit of knowledge was for a mage, in a sense, on a par with eating.

Before him was an exquisite meal laid out without end.

No matter how much was eaten, hunger returned, and the food was also infinite.

Fork and knife given, plates laid before him — and yet somehow, his hand stopped.

He understood it was delicious, that satisfaction could be tasted — and yet his hand would not reach.

He flicked his fingers and inscribed a formula.

Mana changed shape, formed a likeness.

The girl depicted was unchanged, gazing at Elgeinst with a gentle smile.

"……I have solved your mana crystal. As you wished — the world remains at peace."

The girl simply continued to smile.

"From long ago, I……have also become much better at magic."

No answer came back; the likeness dissolved — and a drop traced his cheek as he bowed his head.

From the heights of the World Tree, a small rain fell upon the city below.

In a sky without a cloud — to the rain, no one noticed.
