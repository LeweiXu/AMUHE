# Chapter 286: Dozing — Finale

"……Do you have any regrets? Things you never put into words."

Selene lay there, letting Elvena do as she pleased, to the sound of the river's murmur and a few old stories.

She had been guided to set down her fishing rod and rest her head in Elvena's lap, and from some point on — Elvena stroking her cheek with gentle, unhurried touches — Selene had begun to let the tension out of her shoulders.

It had been around ten or twenty years after Bery was gone, she supposed.

"Strangely enough, not much. ……My sister always seemed so happy."

To be honest, she had been a little frightening.

Deeply loving, attentive, kind and considerate. She had welcomed Elvena as a member of the family rather than a servant — yet the impression Elvena had of her was of someone almost too earnest for her own good.

Always with her thread pulled taut, never letting it slacken — a few years younger than Elvena, yet as the kingdom's Marshal and the head of the great noble house Christand, she had always held herself to a standard that no one could find fault with, always presenting the image that her heavy responsibilities demanded.

Elvena could not claim to understand all of her feelings, but she could at least sense them.

The two princesses went without saying, but even the servants — she was such a superhuman that she made it look as though there were no hardships at all. Looking only at ability, her surroundings were without flaw or lack.

She made the impossible look easy with a composed expression, so in the presence of people like her, effort was simply assumed.

An ordinary person would push themselves to the brink of death and only barely meet the minimum.

The light in her room never went out late into every night, and yet before the household Selene always showed composure, spending her days as mistress of the estate with a smile.

Krische with a troubled look.
Bery with a worried look.
Kreschenta with a look that expected nothing.

All three of them, always looking after her with the same feeling — *relax a little more* — and yet gentle kindness can sometimes be a cruelty.

For someone who was desperately struggling to stand beside them, those words must have been ones she could not accept.

Elvena had felt it so strongly she worried the taut string would one day snap — there was so clearly no room to spare — and in fact, once, it had.

Perhaps that was why.

"Is that so. ……Eheheh, but I think people tend to have something like that in them. You can agonize over all manner of things with great effort, and in the end one happy smile from someone you love makes all of it — not matter anymore……I wonder if that's what it means to be fulfilled."

"……It might be."

The woman whose shoulders had finally relaxed looked, to every eye, completely at peace.

Had Elvena said something like this to the Selene of old, she would surely have taken in what Elvena was feeling even more deeply than Elvena herself.

She had always been a person of sincere empathy, someone who tried to be there for others — warmhearted, and vulnerable to her own warmth.

Elvena and Bery were probably both poor handlers of their own emotions, somewhere at their core remaining a little dispassionate.

Emotion came after logic; only after logic could they give expression to what was within them.

People like Selene and Anne were the opposite — their feelings preceded logic — and that was probably why she found them so dear.

Bery had seemed to admire and envy them from the depths of her heart, and Elvena held much the same feeling.

To have one's emotions expressed without regard for logic — that was something Elvena found a little ticklish, and of course there were times she found it heavy, or burdensome. She had chosen to confide in Bery that day because Bery was someone she thought understood her.

Not sympathy — but someone who would give her time to settle her own heart.

"There's no meaning in deliberately wounding yourself. Even a convenient sophistry is powerful if you yourself are convinced by it……though I do think relying on nothing but sophistry the way Bery does is a bit much."

"……Eheheh."

And yet today's Selene, in the same way, gave Elvena exactly what she wanted, in exactly the measure she wanted.

Where Bery would read the subtle waxing and waning of her feelings and slip into the gaps, Selene simply accepted everything as it was and enfolded it all.

The softness of that feeling was pleasant, and Elvena closed her eyes.

The thing at the root of it was, she was sure, something close to the same feeling.

And yet it also felt like something entirely different, which was strange.

A soft bed, or a sunlit meadow?

Each had its own particular comfort, and they were not the same thing at all — love was like that too.

Changed by the way one lived and the things one experienced.

"I think love is probably something like a box."

"……?"

"……When you give it a name, the inside of the box called love is vivid and rich. Looking into other people's boxes is delightful, because everyone's is different. If Krische-sama's or Mia-san's are clear and unambiguous, then Selene-sama's and Bery-sama's are intricate. ……Being here and simply watching them is just endlessly enjoyable……and that is probably why I feel no regret."

The exterior, the contents, sometimes even the name — different boxes.

Every person's was different, and even the contents of a single person's box shifted slightly depending on who they were with.

"It is rather like gazing at gemstones. ……The love within me for my sister remains beautiful even now, and will remain beautiful in perpetuity."

*——I wish to go on admiring it*, Elvena said with a smile.

Selene looked at her with a pensive expression, and Elvena stroked her forehead.

"In one sense, one could say it is beautiful precisely because it was never obtained. ……Even now I remain in an ambiguous dozing state, and yet there is something beautiful in my chest……and everything I see looks beautiful."

Her sister had searched for Elvena with such desperate persistence, and so Elvena had come to possess this present moment.

A jewel box of a daily life — and yet with edges too blurred to make out clearly.

The ordinary days Elvena lived now were made entirely of her sister's love, and of love for her sister.

There was nothing more beautiful than this, and she did not think it would ever be dimmed.

"……That is surely because you chose not to make it clear."

This world was an eternal morning doze, without the evening ever coming.

Made of that — and permitted to be that, in this place.

"……You and Bery both say things that are a little difficult."

"I said it deliberately because I thought Selene-sama would say that."

"……Are you being difficult on purpose?"

"I might be."

Elvena laughed softly, and Selene made a slightly displeased face and pinched her cheek.

Even through a pinch, what was conveyed was not pain but affection.

Selene said with an exasperated air:

"But I understand at least a little of it, you know. How old do you think I am."

"……I thought you would say that too, Selene-sama."

"……Well."

Her cheek was stretched and pulled, and then released. Selene knitted her brows in displeasure. Elvena smoothed the furrow with a touch and rocked her shoulders.

"In the end, it may be nothing more than me being stubborn because I couldn't have what I wanted. ……But if that is what makes me happy, then being stubborn is not so bad……Eheheh, we agreed on the sophistry part, so perhaps we really are a little alike."

"……You're saying you're the same as me?"

"The difference is whether the real thing is at your side or not, I suppose."

"…………"

"Watching Selene-sama feels like there are two different versions of me — which I find a little entertaining."

The person she had loved most had the person she loves most.

That was not evidence of unhappiness. And seeing someone one loves smile happily in love with another — and be smiled at in return — was not only painful.

All the more so when that person turned toward oneself with an affection infinitely close to the same love.

"……I have not conceded defeat to Bery."

"If it comes to that, I would say the same. ……I have not conceded defeat to Mia-san."

She watched the expressive face of the petulant girl and laughed.

No matter how many years passed, what does not change does not change.

"……Somehow, I believe Onee-san accepted me. I am a little sorry to Mia-san, but I am sure she never said *no*, and there must have been a daily life that existed alongside the jealousy and the complaints to Onee-san — a life that was, in its way, enjoyable."

As with people like them — Selene had been the Elvena of a *what if*.

The more one believed love to be something pure, the more it looked as though there were no room to enter — and yet love, originally, was not such a confined thing.

At least, the love around the people in Elvena's life was not.

With room and space and softness — without limit, able to hold all manner of things inside and still shine, having nothing to do with confinement.

"But love takes different shapes for different people, and each shape has its own beauty……I think I was simply someone who preferred to watch it, and be allowed to touch it from time to time."

That manner of loving, too, was probably different for each person.

The desire to be satisfied was one form, and the form of wanting to taste and gaze was not wrong either.

Where some people wanted to make a single gemstone entirely their own, others wanted to look at many gemstones with delight, and Elvena was simply the latter.

And this was the museum of gemstones to which Elvena had been invited.

None of it was Elvena's — and so all of it was Elvena's.

"Whatever the form, there is no hierarchy among beautiful things, I think……it is only a difference of whether one likes it or not, and in the end, the one who enjoyed it most is the one who wins."

Some translucent, some of complex hue.

Hers, too, resembled what Elvena had once desired — and yet, even without ever being possessed, it shone with beauty right there, right in hand, with no end to it.

The color changed with the angle of looking, and she never tired of it.

And Elvena, gazing at them, seemed to dissolve into that radiant happiness as well.

"Onee-san loved me……and the happiness of this place that Onee-san gave me is also something Onee-san gave to me. Setting aside nuance, it is something like that — and so the days I spend by Selene-sama's and the others' sides are enjoyable, and I am happy. ……And feeling that way is, I think, the proof of my love for Onee-san."

Again Selene looked contemplative, and then sighed once more.

"You know, you and Bery are both strange people. I've finally understood that much, at least."

"Eheheh, if that is so, I think Selene-sama is quite strange enough yourself……"

She laughed watching Selene make that displeased face again.

Strange people, really, every last one.

Without purpose, without end.

Ordinary, unremarkable people who genuinely intended to enjoy, from the depths of their hearts and in perpetuity, a tranquil daily life with no particular highs or lows — of course such people were not normal.

And precisely because of that, Elvena found this place comfortable, and could think sincerely of enjoying it.

A moment later, the expression shifted from a smile to something tinged with sadness.

"……But even so, there are times I feel lonely."

"Lonely……?"

"Yes. ……Even so, watching Selene-sama and the others getting along so warmly, I……sometimes find myself longing for another person's warmth, it cannot be denied."

"I, is that so……"

Elvena watched Selene's eyes swim, stroked her cheek, and brushed her lips.

"There are times when I may say something selfish — will you forgive me……?"

"Selfish, meaning……"

"……Shall I explain?"

"N-no……it's fine, but……"

What was she imagining?

Selene's face was beginning to flush again. Elvena smiled inwardly.

"No, that manner of doing it is unfair. ……Please forget it."

"A, ah, it's not that it's unfair, I mean……erm……g-give me time to think——"

"It is all right. I have no intention of troubling you."

Pressing her thumb to Selene's lips, Elvena said, *let us change the subject*.

"Just as I said — that it feels like watching another version of myself……I cannot think of Selene-sama as a stranger."

Selene, her lips covered, nodded in silence for the moment.

"The Bery-sama Selene-sama chose as her rival is a formidable opponent. It is certainly true that matching her will not be easy by ordinary effort. ……I too felt the same way, thinking I had not lost to Mia-san."

Selene made a face as if to say what are you suddenly talking about, but Elvena continued.

"……With that in mind, at the risk of presumption — if you would allow me to lend Selene-sama a hand."

"L-lend a hand……?"

She echoed the words as Elvena removed her thumb from her lips.

On the matter of Elvena's *selfishness*, Selene had been made to redirect the conversation and be deprived of a refusal. Inevitably, the atmosphere that arose made it difficult to refuse further.

"Yes. Bery-sama is, to all appearances, someone without flaw or lack — at the very least, as long as Bery-sama holds the initiative, gaining the advantage will be difficult. However, she does have a weakness."

"……A weakness."

"Yes. Whatever else she may be, Bery-sama is quite the bashful creature — she cannot hold confidence when it comes to herself, and she is vulnerable when that is pressed. I believe the important thing for Selene-sama to win against Bery-sama is first to attack such weaknesses and steadily build a psychological advantage."

"I, I see……"

There was, perhaps, an element of wanting to escape the earlier topic.

Selene nodded honestly. Elvena watched her and narrowed her eyes ever so slightly.

"And in order to seize the initiative, rather than spending each ordinary day in a vague, drifting way, it will be necessary to reconsider many small things. If you will give me permission, I shall cooperate in ways befitting the situation, discreetly, in ways that suit your wishes."

"Um……ah, that's appreciated, but isn't it — unsporting——"

"No, Bery-sama keeps Krische-sama almost always by her side, and frequently uses Krische-sama to evade Selene-sama's pursuit — which is effectively two against one. This is the orthodox approach taken in order to conduct a fair fight, and I will not interfere beyond that."

It is all right, Elvena continued.

"I am not trying to take Selene-sama's side. I am simply cooperating to create a situation in which Selene-sama can stand as an equal to Bery-sama. ……Just a small *little* bit of help. For instance, after dinner tonight——"

To enjoy the present to the fullest, she smiled.

*……Really, that rabbit of yours says things that are very rabbit-ish. An eternal paradise, of all things.*

*Eheheh, but……I am just a little relieved. Whatever else one might say about it, I was so full of anxiety about what the house would be like after Bery-sama was gone.*

*Well, there's that, yes. ……So, has Elvena decided?*

She thought her sister must have vaguely seen through her.

Whether it was after she and her sister became lovers, or whether she had noticed even before that and her awareness had grown stronger as a result — that part was not entirely clear. But somehow.

*Do you want to hear it?*

*Hmm……I won't try to get it out of you. The fact that Elvena isn't answering honestly means she doesn't really want to say, I think.*

But, her sister continued.

*The only thing I want to tell you is that you don't need to worry about me or Mia. ……If you want to talk, please talk; if you want to consult me, please consult me. Even if you cause trouble or ask something selfish, I and Mia will love you properly and accept you.*

What her sister's *something selfish* had referred to.

The answer could not be confirmed — but at least, she thought, if it had been Elvena rather than Mia who had confessed, her sister would surely not have been so surprised.

She had felt that her sister would love her — including that — and accept her.

*Having thought about it and being able to say you have no regrets — that's when I won't say a word. ……It's your life, Elvena.*

Of course, that was merely a vague sense.

Not a shred of evidence to support it — and yet Elvena found that curiously enough to be satisfied with.

*……Yes, Onee-san.*

Her sister would probably not choose the same path as that princess.

Understanding that, Elvena thought she had decided in that moment.

It had not been a conscious choice to take a different path.

Had the two of them wished to come along, she would have been glad.

But it was not about that. It was simply that — she had decided because she believed the feeling she had felt in that moment was something she could make eternal.

And even after a thousand years, she felt no regret.

Ordinary days repeated, never growing tiresome.

Meals and baths and sleeping beside one another — in this way they spent their days in complete dedication to things of the most trivial kind, and not one of them found anything strange in that. Without the existence of the nation or the lives and deaths of people to weigh upon their minds, this world where their heads were troubled only by the most foolish of matters was a flower garden through and through.

A child's ideal world made manifest.

And a number of foolish adults who cherished that ideal world — playing house in full earnest, every single day.

Neither proper nor wholesome — and if Elvena were to say to some passerby that she and the others had been living this way for a thousand years, that person would surely doubt their sanity.

They might frown and call them mad.

But even shown such a reaction, at least Elvena could say with her chest held high that this was the happiest place in the world, and she was certain the others would answer the same.

It was not normal, and if they were called mad she could not deny it — but whatever the case, it was certain that their sense of things had drifted.

Elvena and the others rarely counted what year it was.

Not because counting the years of eternity would be wearisome — for the first hundred years or so they had faithfully counted each year — but because the sense of a year had come to feel meaningless.

A year in childhood had seemed an unfathomably long span of time, but now a year was gone in an instant.

They generally perceived time in units of about four or five years.

That was probably because the interval for holding banquets with the dragon was roughly that long.

For a five-year-old, a year comprises a fifth of their entire life — but after a thousand years, it is one thousandth.

Phrases like *that was, what, two or three hundred years ago——* had been appearing in conversation for some time now, crops were harvested with the same feeling as watering plants, and birthday celebrations were merely a convenient excuse for a small feast.

Just as before, what they thought about was yesterday and today and tomorrow — and before long someone's mouth would certainly produce the phrase *that was, what, two or three thousand years ago——*.

By Elvena's hazy reckoning, it had been about thirteen hundred years since she had come here.

Selene and Kreschenta, Elvena and Anne, and Krische and Bery.

As usual, everyone went out shopping together.

That day there was quite a lot to buy, and as customary they split into pairs by drawing lots — since there was one young lady who threw a tantrum unless she was paired with her sister — and Elvena, at Anne's request, stopped at a bookshop and also browsed for sewing tools, thinking they might try making clothes and underthings, and then strolled about.

Most things were available on the other side as well, but cleaning tools, tableware, clothing, paper, and such things were not made there, and there was a fair amount of them.

Of course if they wished to, Krische and the others could easily replicate such things (conjuring identical objects from magical power, in the literal sense), but in principle they did not take such *shortcuts*.

For that reason, shopping at various places throughout the world, selling produce or wine or things Selene had made in order to manage finances — that was ordinary life. Yet Krische and Bery had become absorbed in cooking with local specialties from around the world, and so it had been more than a decade since they had last visited Albernaria.

It was the city with the most shops and the largest markets, and Albernaria was still the place they visited most — and yet even that was about the measure of things.

For the current Elvena and the others, with their altered sense of time, it was something that felt like a miraculous coincidence.

Shopping finished, they returned to the meeting place — but when the time came, Krische and Bery did not appear.

And they were not where they had gone, either. Consoling Kreschenta, whose displeasure was mounting, as they searched, it was at the hour of dusk that they emerged from the museum — the two of them, accompanied by two women.

Krische was holding Bery's hand, came hurrying toward them with a happy smile, and told Elvena they were Kalua and Mia.

"……Onee-san and Mia-san?"

"Yes, their reincarnations. Would you like to speak with them?"

One was a member of a race called beastkin, born in the later period of Kleinmeil. A black-haired woman in an apron dress, cat-like ears, a tail swaying behind her.

The other was a girl with golden hair wearing a robe.

Without having heard their voices, without having caught their gestures — and yet without needing to ask, from the mere sight of how they stood, it was immediately clear which was which.

Had the vaguely nostalgic air made her feel that way?

Or had it been something within them — something that could be called a soul — that she was perceiving?

Bery beside her also looked the question, and Anne who had been listening was startled. Kreschenta set aside her irritation and looked at the two in the distance with an intrigued expression, and Selene seemed to worry about Elvena in some way.

Elvena looked at their reactions, then smiled wryly and shook her head.

"No. Neither of them knows me……please do not worry."

"Are you sure? This is probably an extraordinary stroke of fate……"

"Yes. ……Just watching from a distance is quite enough."

Out of all the many people living in this wide world, what were the odds of a chance meeting like this, coming across just those two?

"Eheheh, could it be the effect of a certain charm from long ago?"

Whether this was their first reincarnation, or whether they had been reborn several times before — whatever the case, it was a small thing.

It was probably a vanishingly small probability, beyond the need to think about.

"Most likely. Ehehe, they are very close and their atmosphere is just like the two of them."

"……What a relief."

*——And so*, Elvena thought, *the meeting feels to me just a little like fate*, and her expression softened.

The two of them were still living in this world, as ordinary people, just as they had in those days.

If they knew that even now Elvena and the others were spending their days in the same foolish, happy way — what expression would they make?

She could imagine their exasperation, and smiled wryly.

Even reborn, the two of them were unchanged, living with what looked like enjoyment.

And Elvena was unchanged as well, still living happily even now.

That was enough — in it each person had chosen her own form, nothing more and nothing less.

Of course she felt joy for them, and yet she also did not feel she wanted to exchange words with them.

Simply imagining what lay on the other side of that was more than sufficient. Only — *may you be happy* — and she bowed her head.

While feeling glad that she could say so from the bottom of her heart.

The two of them returned the bow with an uncertain air, and Elvena smiled wryly once more.

"Thank you very much. ……Shall we go, Krische-sama."

"Yes!"

Krische bowed as well, and Bery did the same.

They turned their backs and walked on.

"……Was that all right?"

"Yes. ……The Onee-san I chose is the Onee-san who chose Mia-san."

She replied to Selene's solicitous question, and smiled.

Reincarnated — even if there were no memories on that side, they were still her sister and Mia.

But the two that Elvena had loved were still within Elvena's heart.

"And that Mia-san has never known the Onee-san who only I remember……and will always love. In effect it is as though I have won……but if I spoke to her and felt even a moment's pang of envy, it would ruin the feeling — so this is how it should be."

A street corner — there, faintly, a mingling of city and trees.

Step through it and there was an endlessly fantastical forest estate.

The haze between one thing and another — a dreamlike state named Eternity.

"……This place is the home I have chosen."

This was the world Elvena lived in, the daily life she spent.

Filled with modest happiness, an everlasting doze that would never wake.

Indistinct and uncertain and ambiguous, with no clear outline —

"You are stubborn too."

And yet, for those ordinary days spent in that doze, she had given a name: proof of love.

"……Yes, it seems I am."

A thousand years passed, and would not change in the thousand to come.

That was where to draw the line. And it was perfectly fine.

# Chapter 287: The Boy and His Onee-san — Part One

At the Black Flag Swordsmanship Dojo in the castle district of the royal capital, the doors were opened to children free of charge.

Naturally they required some sweeping, care of equipment, and simple odd jobs, and instruction was fundamentally limited to teaching the basics of how to swing a sword — but by policy, no one was turned away.

The dojo had ample space even so.

When it was being built, members of the unit had donated prize money and the like, making it the largest in the capital.

However devoted the Black Flag Special Service might be to the admiration of the public, people who could continue rigorous training on admiration alone were few. Those who genuinely wished to learn swordsmanship generally preferred established dojos with long histories.

The Black Flag Swordsmanship Dojo, known in name only, had an unusual number of new enrollments for a dojo but also a certain frivolous image, and the proportion of students who actually stayed was not large.

It was partly those circumstances that gave them room to open the doors to children for free — and the masters also liked that *relaxed* atmosphere, so they did not consider it a problem.

They had enough students to keep this large dojo out of the red, and the place had been built from the start as somewhere for familiar faces to gather in a peaceful world — they cherished that kind of place more than anything.

* * *

That was in one corner of the dojo.

About twenty children, twelve and under, were assembled there. The one conducting the children's instruction that day was Zaaka — an old hand from the very founding of the company of a hundred.

He looked like an old man, with a formidable face and a blade scar that had taken his left eye. His body was still that of a warrior, and when a girl who had arrived with a cookie in her mouth came to stand beside him, the impression of menace was only made sharper by contrast.

His instruction was strict but his stories were entertaining, and during rest periods he was very kind — the most popular master.

After he had explained the day's training and asked if there were any questions, a boy — Kyuris — steeled himself and spoke.

"Um, is Krische-sama really strong……I mean, strong?"

An idle question he had been carrying around lately. When he put it into words, the silver-haired girl tilted her head.

The pretty onee-san in the apron dress.

Very beautiful, but cute and childlike at the same time.

Apparently she was sixty or seventy or some considerable age, but to Kyuris, who was turning eleven this year, she looked nothing of the sort.

He had never seen her swing a sword.

At the dojo she was eating cookies, talking with head master Kalua or the superintendent and the seasoned Black Flag Special Service members — that was all. She was more like a neighborhood onee-san than a warrior.

The sturdy former-soldier masters were clearly more likely to be strong, and in fact they were strong.

Their swords were fast enough that you couldn't follow them, and the abilities of the head master — Kalua, known by the alias Black Cat of Alberinea — in easily dispatching those very masters, was something Kyuris could understand.

But it was simply too hard to believe that this strange onee-san was a match that Kalua could not hold a candle to.

And to begin with, the ordinary dynamic between Kalua and Krische was something like an aunt and niece. Leaning on her, climbing into her lap to be held — there was no fighting spirit to speak of, not even a fragment of a warrior's pride.

"Kyuris! You are——"

"Hih."

"Zaaka, don't shout."

"Yes, however……"

When Kyuris spoke, the forbidding face turned a glare on him.

Krische told the Zaaka beside her to stop, and considered the matter without particular concern, tracing a finger along her lips.

"Hmm……Krische would say she is, well, on the stronger side, perhaps."

"On the stronger side……so then, Zaaka-shihan and Kalua-shihan really are stronger?"

"Idiot. I've said a dozen times that even I, let alone Kalua, am not fit to touch her feet."

"Um……but, Krische-sama is about as small as me, so I wondered if it was really true……"

Strong was not the word — if anything she looked rather on the weak side, but he held back in consideration of Zaaka's presence.

Alberinea was the continent's greatest hero, known to all.

Every manner of monster cut down in a single stroke; spears thrown to topple city walls; charging into an army of ten thousand alone to cut the general's head from his shoulders — her feats of valor were without end.

Before he came here, Kyuris had admired Alberinea and wanted to become such a warrior, but the Alberinea he had witnessed here was so utterly at odds with those stories.

The only thing that matched was the beautiful face.

Frankly, given that he had admired her, the feeling of disappointment was large.

"It is true that Krische is small, so in that sense one might say she is not strong. A larger body is undeniably an advantage……and people who cannot beat Krische simply lack technique and experience……hmm, now that I think about it again, whether she is really that strong……"

"K-Krische-sama……please be more confident. If you say that, it puts our position in a difficult one."

"But what Kyuris says is true. For instance, a version of Krische one shaku taller would be much stronger than Krische, and with that in mind a Krische who is not even five shaku might not be all that strong……"

(T/N: One shaku ≈ 30 cm, five shaku ≈ 150 cm; she is saying that a taller version of herself would be stronger.)

"……A-ah, but such an impossible hypothetical——"

*Hmm*, Krische tilted her head, and Zaaka kept telling her in exasperation that she was unquestionably strong, the strongest, without peer, working to convince her.

Watching this, Kyuris's doubts only deepened.

He could see, just by watching, that she was a very kind person.

The Queen's elder sister and the second-highest ranked military officer — she ought to have been unimaginably important, and yet she was polite to everyone, unpretentious and approachable; that was admirable and he could understand it.

The members' strong respect for her was visible, and they were the commoner-born soldiers of the Black Flag Special Service.

That their advancement was thanks to Krische was undeniable, and it was not strange that out of gratitude they would tell everyone around them with some exaggeration that she was wonderful.

The kind princess and the master of the instructors — of course they would want children like Kyuris to like her, to have respect for her, and that feeling was understandable.

Moreover, that she was truly an undefeated person who had commanded tens of thousands on the battlefield was a fact — whether the person commanding those tens of thousands was strong or weak with a sword was surely a minor matter.

It was better to be strong, but even if weak, Alberinea was Alberinea. The kingdom's hero.

Putting a little embellishment on that was surely permissible, and rather, was it not wrong to point that out openly like this, in front of people?

Watching Zaaka try over and over to convince Krische that she was very strong, the strongest, without equal, Kyuris bowed his head.

"U-um……I'm sorry. That was rude of me……"

From the way she stood and walked — stillness.

Because the killing stroke, once released, could not be reacted to.

Because it was a sword exceeding human reaction.

From the same Zain guard as Wesuriar, as if thrusting out a sword.

Zain's sword. A divine-speed killing stroke that surpassed human reaction.

From Wesuriar's standpoint, who aimed for the limits of human ability — it was beyond human capacity.

Not even a speck of deviation in the accuracy of the movement, and a reaction speed as if transcending time itself — without those, it would not have been possible. The work of a god.

Only someone with pride in their own swordsmanship could perceive the unfathomable distance.

Equal to a dragon, that violet eye — Alberinea was a divine instrument in human form.

Do not challenge it; do not resist. Never test it.

A person could only hide from a storm, only prostrate themselves before lightning.

Praise her, hold her in awe and reverence. That kind of song came to mind, and Wesuriar had sweat breaking over his entire body.

Knowing in a single moment the nature of the one he had challenged and resisted.

"A thrust does expose the body in a moment of full extension — so it must be released at a timing where there is no question the opponent can be killed, where there is no possibility of a counter. Hearing her words, Kyuris was also recalling words he had heard from Buck this morning.

The image of a child tripped by having a downswing arm pulled, and the image of the expert before him, overlapped.

The gulf between their ability was incomparable — but the principle was probably the same.

Using the opponent's movement to break their stance.

Simple and clear in words.

But Krische had done it as if it were the same thing.

The expert had been tripped before him as if he were a child.

Even head master Kalua before Krische was like an adult and a child.

Words he had heard many times, and doubted each time.

"Zain-san, what is the matter?"

Wesuriar, not yet recovered from the confusion, slowly stood up.

"Then, once more."

"……Ha."

At those words, one deep breath, and he took his guard again.

A moment to extinguish the inner turmoil.

Again the freezing pressure filled the space; a beat passed, and then the next instant.

"Ha……!?"

To Kyuris's eyes, Wesuriar again seemed to vanish.

In a flash he closed the interval, the point extending.

But the sword passed through empty air.

Her long hair like two tails swayed, and Alberinea stood at Wesuriar's back.

Wesuriar's rigidity lasted only an instant — the slashing stroke like a whirlwind released at the turn was dodged by her at the last instant; the second and third strokes already seemed to pass through her like a sword through air.

Whatever was smiling with the smile of a young girl — it was like a phantom.

Against the sword flashes that would have cut ten out of ten ordinary persons in two without any of them knowing what had happened — the movement swayed and drifted, unhurried to the point of looking almost slow.

"The thrust is not bad. The others too, each individual one is not so terrible——"

When the sword flashes exceeded ten, Wesuriar swung a freezing horizontal slash.

Alberinea, who should have had the upper half of her body swept away, slipped through it — crawling along the earth — and from beneath Wesuriar's feet, the curved sword extended upward.

"The transitions are a little loose, perhaps."

The blade bent at an angle — its edge came to rest against the man's throat.

Tracing it ever so smoothly, as if caressing that soft flesh.

The face unchanged — still the *neighborhood onee-san* smile.

Only the warped curved sword in hand and the image of the blade toying with the throat of a man who deserved to be called an expert — those alone were warped.

"The problem is that there is a wind-up in every movement. Killing a person doesn't require a fast sword, so excessive speed is unnecessary."

She said it, and stepped in.

Just that — and yet the movement was frighteningly smooth, and by the time one registered that it was a step, Krische's body was already in front of Wesuriar.

Wesuriar made an instinctive backward leap, but Krische easily closed on him, gripped his collar and pushed him down, and simultaneously pressed the blade to his throat.

"Besides, when you try to move fast or swing a fast sword, the inertia is strong and you become bound to it, and the choices available from that point are limited. Swords cut of themselves as long as the blade is square — there's no need to be attached to speed."

"Like just now — if you step in within a range you can control, Krische can take the second move against whatever sword the opponent swings. That said, if the opponent takes distance, it becomes a stalemate — but a person's body is built so that moving forward is faster than moving backward."

This time, stepping in while swinging the right curved sword in a large arc inward from the outside.

A horizontal slash, and yet strangely relaxed.

Not slow — but not fast either.

She could see Wesuriar instantly attempt to knock the curved sword away.

"Ha……"

At that moment, Krische's sword slipped through it — no, her very body seemed to slip through Wesuriar's body — and in an instant she was behind him.

A kick to the backs of the knees to bring him to kneeling, and from behind the curved sword was pressed to the throat — drawn across it in a gesture of cutting.

"Fast movement is required only in cases like this — when it will certainly kill the opponent without fail. What should be thought about in a fight is: how to keep as many options as possible for oneself, how to reduce the opponent's possible actions, and lead them to zero and kill them. Fast movement should be used only when the opponent's stance is insufficient — when they physically cannot respond."

Just a little more force, and Wesuriar would be dead.

There was a hopeless difference in ability between the two, beyond words like *strong*.

Again she stepped away, raised a finger, and explained.

"Everyone tends to get fixated on instantaneous speed, but what matters is not speed — it is time to reach. Fast swords are not bad in terms of reducing time to reach and killing with a small opening, but in the end what matters is forcing a fatal opening in the opponent — ——"

Is the idea behind Zain style basically this as well, Krische asked.

"Ha……you are right."

"……In military deployment situations, it is difficult because the opponent's situation is hard to see and there are many blind spots — but in a one-on-one confrontation like this, there are no blind spots. Everything is visible, so it is very simple. Calculate all the opponent's possible actions, eliminate them stage by stage, break them — and that is the end."

She said it as a matter of course, without dropping her smile.

"Driving the opponent to a point where they can do nothing, hands or feet——"

She watched Wesuriar take his guard again, and stepped in.

She kicked the high-speed sword Wesuriar swung, flicking it upward.

"——making them defenseless, then quickly slitting the throat — that is the ideal for moving on to the next."

She pressed the sword lightly to the throat.

A one-sided, mechanical lecture on killing technique directed at a master.

The throat was a vital spot, and guarding it was the most basic of fundamentals.

It was not as though Wesuriar did not know that — but without discussion.

She simply repeated.

Alberinea loved with an almost pathological quality the way her warped curved sword did not dull — to slice off the soft flesh of the neck — and the alias attached to her was: the Neck Reaper.

Kyuris was recalling the many stories he had heard about that.

From common soldiers to brave warriors, from masters to heroes — without distinction — Alberinea reaped necks.

Like harvesting wheat with a sickle, human necks, carelessly.

"If there is time, it is good to think about how to kill the next person to kill while still killing — cutting off limbs is one option Krische doesn't prefer because it damages the sword, but——"

Just as in the stories — swordsman Wesuriar Zain was being killed, and yet it seemed utterly natural.

His sword was unlike anything Kyuris had ever seen.

Not the kind that overwhelmed you with force. Not the kind that filled you with pressure.

If he were to compare — it was like something that when you came to realize it, had been winding around from your feet to your throat: something like a serpent.

As if in a gentle passing breeze, your throat was suddenly being slit — such a non-rational, outlandish image came to Kyuris himself.

*Among the greatest of the unreasonable things I spoke of earlier. ……On the battlefield, there is no greater unreasonable thing than standing before her. She breaks through a battle formation as easily as kicking through undergrowth, and takes a general's head like picking fruit.*

Merkes's words contained not a thread of lie or pretense.

Because the principles that govern people ceased to hold, she was called the *Heavenly Sword*.

And so she flatly denied even Wesuriar's killing blade.

"——well, that is roughly the basics of a sword, I think. Of course I am not saying Zain style is bad. Kyuris, your practice sword."

"Huh, ah……yes……"

Krische sheathed the curved sword and took the practice sword Kyuris held out.

She moved some distance away — about six ken — and faced Wesuriar, whose face had gone pale and drenched with sweat.

The guard the girl took was a half-body with sword forward — Zain style.

"A half-body guard with sword forward — naturally the preliminary movements become fewer, so in some situations it is effective. Assessing the opponent's reaction speed, and with this much of a gap in ability——"

Stillness to stillness, skipping motion.

A practice sword passing through the side of Wesuriar's throat — the figure of Krische, momentarily lost to sight.

Her swirled silver hair and cloak drifted and fell, one beat late, as if reclaiming lost time.

——That was the sword of Zain, as if leaping beyond time itself.

"Taking the shortest most fastest initiative against the opponent — this guard is by no means bad either."

Even Wesuriar, the legitimate Zain successor, seemed to have reached his limit.

He was startled backward at the sudden appearance of the practice sword and her, and sat down heavily.

A beat late, the barely cool breeze that reached Kyuris felt strangely cold.

"In Krische's view, a *kyukyu* (quick-quick) feeling is inferior to a *yuruyuru, fuwaa* (gentle-gentle, floating) way of swinging — that is her preference — but most people cannot even swing a sword in a *shupan, bishuu* (snap-and-flash) fashion like Zain-san's minimum — so learning Zain style to acquire fast footwork and sword movement is one approach."

(T/N: All onomatopoeia are Krische's characteristic invented sound-words for sword techniques and are rendered phonetically throughout.)

The register from first to last was unchanged, and if one had to say which was more serious — the cooking was more serious.

At least to her, swordsmanship was evidently only at that level.

"With cooking too, handling a knife and kitchen tools proficiently is the bare minimum. What matters is the thinking of how to employ those techniques for the sake of delicious food. There is no single answer to this, and even Krische's teacher says she is only halfway — an inquiry without end, requiring exploration that never terminates — but in this regard, the sword is similar."

She nodded several times, continuing without noticing that not a single person was speaking.

"A sword is after all, in the end, simply a matter of cutting into moving meat. There is no comparison to the depth of cooking. But in both, first learning the minimum technique and increasing the range of one's possible actions is a common thread — and being mindful of the sufficient minimum, not clinging to a single technique, and thinking — that is the important thing."

Do you understand, Krische said, turning with a spin, and looked at Wesuriar, who was still sitting on the ground.

No air of intimidation — a gentle, childlike smile.

Atmosphere and everything, including the violet eyes, all of it — she was always just the *onee-san*.

Without distinction between child and adult, amateur and master.

He understood: she was a being who looked down on people just like this, and was permitted to.

The soft violet that the boy had always seen — it now felt like something unutterably terrifying, and he shuddered.

"Zain's……sword……"

Wesuriar, who had been watching her in stupefaction, rose and took a knee, bowing his head.

"……I have been shown……the depths that lie beyond it."

A strained voice, and the fist pressed to the ground was visibly trembling.

What Alberinea was.

No one would call the one who had challenged and resisted her — not knowing — a fool.

Every one of them was watching him with something like pity, and even that gaze was probably cruel.

"……Hmm, I don't quite understand, but — if you understand, that is good. It seems you can swing a sword to some extent, so from now on instead of a *kyukyukyu* feel, think in terms of a *fuwafuwaa, kyuu, shubiit* feel for the overall composition."

(T/N: Krische's onomatopoeia: 'kyukyukyu' = aggressive/stiff swordwork; 'fuwafuwaa, kyuu, shubiit' = flowing/soft movement with precise moments of sharpness. Rendered phonetically.)

"……, As you wish."

"Do your best. Kalua, we've finished here so let's go see the other groups."

"……I'm starting to think that because of usachan, the entire exchange gathering is heading toward its end."

"……? Oh — Kyuris, here you go. This is a good opportunity for you, so please have Zain-san and the others teach you various things."

"Y-yes……"

How he was supposed to be taught anything in this situation, he didn't know.

He didn't think the demoralized Wesuriar could recover to a state where he could give instruction any time soon — but Kyuris could only nod.

In the freezing air, only she herself, not noticing, was cheerful and in a fine mood.

Without an inkling that she had crushed what one man had probably staked his entire life on and refined with extraordinary effort — satisfied as if she had given excellent instruction.

She and Kalua mounted the jade tiger, and she disappeared somewhere — the only warmth left the scene, and what remained was nothing but an oppressively heavy atmosphere.

The complete defeat of the Zain legitimate successor, without even being able to lift a finger — it was apparently accepted by all present as: understandable, against Alberinea.

The reason was also that she went around *breaking* the hearts of the masters of the gathered dojos in the same manner.

A forest hides the trees.

Alberinea was not someone to challenge — she was someone to receive guidance from.

That losing was never shameful became an atmosphere that formed naturally.

She also attended the exchange gatherings that became annual from the following year.

The apron-dress her — by some combination of that incident and the setting, she somehow ended up running an outdoor cooking class at what was supposed to be a swordsmanship exchange gathering — and seeing the sight of the grandmasters of the capital's dojos bowing and scraping before her, as if they were her disciples or something of the sort, gave Kyuris, who had witnessed that incident, an odd sense of relief.

That this was the same person as that — was something to be afraid of.

With the carefree movement on the battlefield, Kalua had said. And imagining her cutting necks with that same smile, the image came easily enough now — but he would probably never see it in his own life.

He did not particularly want to.

For Kyuris, the royal princess Alberinea was still a kind, odd onee-san. That was fine.

The image of her distributing cookies suited her far better, and his question of *is Krische not weak* had fully dissolved.

Her swordsmanship was not within a range that could be learned from, and at least for those of Kyuris's caliber, being taught the sword of Kalua — who had apparently learned from Krische — was more intelligible and more fruitful.

*It'll be fine, it'll be fine — whether it was yesterday or twenty years ago it's more or less the same thing in your case, Mia. ……It's just throwing by force.*

*This you……!*

True to her words, it was practically an underarm toss with almost no run-up.

But the heavy infantry lance she threw toward Kalua passed through the tree behind her and snapped it.

The children watching were all frozen by that terrific force.

If it had struck, a human torso would have been lightly blown away.

Even if the opponent was the superhuman Kalua — to release that violence without flinching at close range was still not the act of an ordinary person.

She was, she appeared gentle, but was also a monster who had passed through countless battlefields.

*Mia, one spear that you broke is equivalent to how many pumpkins worth, do you think. No, it is not a matter of money — just because you paid for it yourself does not mean it is all right to use it roughly. It is a spear that a craftsman put great effort into making.*

*……I'm sorry. But I had planned to stick it in the tree, I didn't intend to break it, with years of nothing I've just, I……*

*Making excuses is undignified, Mia-superintendent. Everyone else threw it from a distance, but throwing it toward me——*

*Quiet!*

*Mia, Krische is giving a lecture.*

*Uuu……why do I have to……*

But whatever the case, from that point neither Kyuris nor the other children ever again said either of those two looked weak.

Twenty years out of practice, a casual toss without even a proper run-up — and that force. How much force a full-effort throw from her would have was not even something to imagine.

From that point everyone paid her respect with great care not to anger her.

A quiet person when angry is the most frightening — Mia too, he now understood, was one of those.

"Kalua and Zaaka and the rest have a hangover. I don't think they'll be out until early afternoon. ……Yesterday everyone got together to drink for the first time in a while."

*I stopped partway through myself*, Mia said with a slightly disgruntled air — and her manner compared to the other fighters was so remarkably ordinary — but that was probably what misled so many people.

Don't underestimate by appearances — the masters' lesson was etched deep in his chest.

Making oneself look weak was itself a technique, Kyuris understood now.

"I see. Understood. ……Though it's not really an atmosphere for training right now."

"Well, that's because Krische-sama is here."

Mia smiled wryly watching the children gathering around for cookies.

Kyuris laughed, thinking he had been in that position too once.

Alberinea's handmade cookies were genuinely of the finest quality, but they were not extravagant confections — simple, somehow rustic, with a gentle honey flavor.

Everyone loved those cookies she brought.

*Take care then*, Mia said, heading off somewhere, and they bowed to her, looked at each other, and smiled wryly.

"Why don't you go get some too, Kyuris?"

"……How old do you think I am. I'm fifteen already."

Boys like the rowdy Kyuris of old had settled down after coming here — that sort of reputation had spread, so it seemed.

Days when the primary school was not in session — like today — brought especially many, and the dojo was half a children's minder, but the masters were as always unbothered.

"——The longer a battle is even, the longer it drags on, and fatigue accumulates. You cannot swing the sword you want to. You face situations like loss of weapons. So today I intend to teach simple techniques for dominating a grapple with an opponent."

Both Kyuris and Sherisia had long been entrusted with children's instruction, and he was used to it — but there was still some nerves when the one standing beside him was Krische.

She would generally not interfere in instruction unless asked, but Kyuris was still immature in the extreme.

Being observed with the eye of the ultimate swordfighter was quite a pressure, and even Sherisia — who normally was confident, handling children with practiced ease — her face was slightly stiff.

Sherisia demonstrated the technique for the day using Kyuris's body, spoke to the children, and asked Krische:

"What do you think? Krische-sama."

"Hmm……a very clear explanation, I think. The sufficient minimum……indeed, a wonderful phrase that applies to all things."

Krische, having her own words quoted back to her, said *Sherisia has grown*, and nodded with satisfaction over and over.

Seeing that, Sherisia also swung the two bunches of hair at her ears, turned to Kyuris, and smiled proudly.

**On How to Read Kingdom Noble Names**

Example: 1, Gildanstein = 2, Kalnaros = 3, Vel = 4, Sarcarinea = 5, Alberan

1. Given name: Gildanstein
2. The largest territory within the lands one administers: Kalnaros. In practice all administered territories would be included, making it extremely long, so it is abbreviated.
3. Male royalty with royal succession rights: Vel; female royalty: Vera
4. Honorary title: Sarcarinea
5. Surname: Alberan

Formally, between 4 and 5 there may also be military rank such as Marshal or General, or palace positions such as Chancellor, and after the surname there may be titles such as Grand Duke. However, as the horizontal letters were already eye-glazing in their length, the text prioritizes clarity by attaching necessary rank and title in Japanese characters at the appropriate points.

In principle, everything except the surname is not necessarily inherited by children — but conventionally the legitimate heir usually inherits lands and title without issue.

The honorary title in position 4, indicating military achievement, alone is not inherited.

When a person without a surname receives a *Nea* = abbreviated investiture as a Junior Knight, they generally take the name of their home area.
(Example: Gallen = Nea = Karka.)

○ = Surviving in the extra chapters
● = Deceased in the extra chapters

■ — Characters from "Moonlight's Legacy"

**● Elgeinst Kleinmel Alberinea Ramiru** 『Neutral · Good』
The Alberinea-fanatic type of young man. Director of the Academy of Magical Studies. First Emperor of the mage empire Kleinmeil. Nemunemu.

A genius who from childhood touched Krische's mana crystals and became entranced by the beauty of their inscriptions.

From a young age he frequented the Academy of Magical Studies, and fell head over heels for Krische herself when she came to visit.

He is prouder of being called *reasonably excellent* by her than of anything else, and poured all his time and talent into magic in order to be recognized by her.

To him, Krische was a god herself, and he spent his days in research in order to decipher her remaining mana crystals — to draw closer to her.

His eyelids hung somewhat low, and he had a sleepy look — and so he was named Nemunemu.

☆ After the *relocation*

When she disappeared he was crushed by despair, but believing that the *Great Rite of Heavenly Change* she had performed held all the keys, he stood up again — ascertained the existence of inscribed forms that did not rely on mana crystals, such as the lightning-lance she had used on the battlefield — and threw himself into the research and stabilization of magic.

Her mana crystals were encrypted, and attempts at analysis would trigger a self-destruction mechanism taking the surroundings with them — but a single mana crystal with no such protection, described as for study, had been left at the research institute for many years.

He spent several decades constructing a magical analysis device, and only by combining it with magic did he finally break that protection.

What appeared before him was an illusion of Krische, made of magical power.

*I made it so that it dissolves easily when you inscribe a form into the floofy thing, but you've done well. The big scary one — Jaragasha and such — I've made properly unbreakable, so please give up on those. Magic is not a bad thing — use it for peace.*

She raised a finger and what she said was that kind of message.

*……Oh, there is probably none, but — if Krische is still nearby, if you tell me, Krische will come and praise you directly.*

Only that remained, and the mana crystal dissipated.

Magic's power for peace.

He repeated the words she had left, and looking around, he saw that the kingdom had begun to shake in the absence of the Queen and Alberinea. It was certain that the tranquility she had left behind would eventually crumble, and understanding that it was probably his own duty to shape it into a new form — he quietly took hold of the magical weapons, and with the great power she had left, organized with magical scholars the magic society *Moonlight's Legacy* — Kleinmeil. He sought the cooperation of his army friend Waltza Remin, and resolved to move for the stabilization of the continent.

He had some resistance to calling himself Alberinea — the name that should be used to describe a god — and using it, but there was no other name to unite people's hearts. And there was no better name to make clear that they were not rebels but inheritors of her and the others' will.

After founding the nation, he called himself Emperor and brought peace to the continent.

Attending to affairs of state while spending each day in research.

The inscription of the *Great Rite of Heavenly Change* he had seen that day and burned into memory.

Only he did not believe that Alberinea had died in a failed experiment, and was certain she was still alive somewhere.

He continued his research in order to meet her again, to have her say a single word to him — and then, a hundred years later: facing unavoidable death, summoning his last strength, his final experiment.

The old man who had become nothing but bones and skin — what he beheld was a beautiful world beyond description.

After a little while, floating down, drifting from the sky — a single girl.

——Oh, who is this. It has been a while, Nemunemu.

They exchanged a little conversation; he told her the world was still at peace, and that he had unlocked the mana crystal she had left — and she smiled and stroked his head.

He closed his eyes at that touch, feeling he had no more regrets, and thus fell into a satisfied sleep.

△ Historically

The founding emperor of the thousand-year empire Kleinmeil, and a great mage said to have mastered the heights of magical arts — inscribed in history as a *Houjutsushi* (Ritual Magician) at its peak.

Possessing power capable of shifting military strategy single-handedly, the anecdote of wielding his might during the Far Eastern pacification — where ten people annihilated an army of ten thousand — is well known.

From a historical standpoint, many scholars view the appearance of Elgeinst and the *magic inscription users* — the *Daimadoushi* (Great Mages) — he led as having ended the age of heroes.

He is sometimes held up as a mage rivaling the Queen and Alberinea, but Elgeinst himself said he did not come close to the feet of either, and it is recorded in writings of the time that he said *claiming the name Alberinea for myself is beyond presumptuous*. The fact that to the present day no one has deciphered Alberinea's mana crystals is taken by many as evidence that Alberinea was indeed a being in a class apart.

He consistently maintained that he stood not out of private motive but for the continent's peace, and in his time no great reforms were made — governance was conducted with parliament at its center, as in the final period of Alberan, and he held himself to be a symbolic emperor.

He participated in parliament while holding veto power, but once parliament was stable he did not intervene in its decisions, and never exercised that veto.

Like Queen Kreschenta, he did not favor extravagance and did not indulge in luxury for himself, and through such conduct he gradually came to be accepted by the common people as well, seen as her true and proper successor.

Taking this as a model, emperors from the following generation onward considered it noble to hold great authority without wielding it, and that convention was inherited across several hundred years — and no one doubts that he was a wise ruler worthy of a place in history.

He was known to spend each day alongside affairs of state in personal research, and after abdication became absorbed in it. He is believed to have died due to a failed experiment.

His purpose was never spoken publicly, but it is recorded that he said on occasion he wished to see the great Alberinea once more — leading to speculation that it was aimed at resurrecting the dead, or at time travel.

He maintained his youth for an unusually long period, lived for two hundred years, and yet had no established life-extension technique — he was aging in his final years.

These two hundred years are often considered one limit of lifespan made possible purely by the body's mastery of magical power.

Appearance: White hair so long it trailed to the floor. Brown eyes. Pathologically thin. Eyes that seemed closed even when open. A long beard.

→ Krische: A goddess. A shining guiding star. The being who gave him his purpose.
Specialty: Magic. Spells. Magical analysis. Magical weapons design.
Likes: Alberinea. Analyzing Alberinea's mana crystals.
Dislikes: Those who misuse Alberinea's wisdom.
Worries: None.

**● Waltza Targuk Gasheārinea Remin** 『Chaos · Good』
The bad-influence friend of a serious young man type. Duke. Grand Marshal of the Kleinmeil Mage Empire.

Childhood friend of Kleinmeil Emperor Elgeinst Ramiru.

Their association began when his grandfather Aleha brought them together. Waltza — who loved swords and the outdoors — was the opposite of Elgeinst, who shut himself away day and night in an unhealthy way, never associating with people, absorbed in magical research. Worried about him, he forcibly dragged him outside, and that became the occasion for their friendship.

A pattern developed in which Waltza, escaping from his tutor, would hole up with Elgeinst — and ultimately Elgeinst was half-invited as a private tutor, and tasked with teaching him academics.

Elgeinst had near-scholarly knowledge in academics as well, by dint of his absorption; being taught by him meant no complaints were leveled, and between friends it would be informal.

That had been a calculated proposal on his part — but Elgeinst, realizing that teaching someone else deepened his own understanding, saw it as a good opportunity and put effort into it. The education ultimately became stricter than any private tutor, and he deeply came to regret this.

As a device for making Elgeinst — shattered by losing Krische — keep living, and as a goal to redirect his attention to, he set the objective of a swift coup d'état — the founding war — and made him bear a symbolic responsibility. But he was troubled to the end that he had pressed upon his friend a role incompatible with the life his friend desired.

He himself had reservations about Krische and Kreschenta abandoning the kingdom and walking away, and he did not specifically have a strong ideal about the kingdom's future or the people — his actions came largely from a personal feeling: the legacy of his admired grandfather and father.

He performed the role of an idealist in order to rouse Elgeinst, and said the words *this was also Krische's wished-for wish* while pushing him forward — but he was sorry for having pushed his friend into a position he could not escape from and loaded him with a great burden.

Watching his friend kill his own heart day by day in his unfamiliar role as emperor, and deeply troubled whether someone who had pushed him into that position had the right to say it — but as his own death drew near, worried about what would become of his friend thereafter, he resolved and in the end confessed it to him.

*You have worked enough, and fulfilled Krische's wish. So next, as a person — go and fulfill your true wish*, he said.

△ Historically

First Grand Marshal of the Kleinmeil Mage Empire, and the most trusted aide of the first Mage Emperor.

One of the heroes of the Kleinmeil founding war — the central figure in the coup d'état and subsequent war.

Possessing many magical weapons including Jaragasha Gasheā, the fact that in twenty-seven engagements he remained undefeated demonstrates the depth of his military genius and knowledge — and at the time when Alberinea had been lost, there is no room to doubt that he was the greatest or second-greatest commander of his era.

He is believed to have left numerous military treatises focused on magical weapons deployment, but most have been lost and no originals remain.

However, many military works from the Kleinmeil period were produced referencing them, and are recorded as citing them frequently.

It is said that founding emperor Elgeinst Ramiru and he were close friends from childhood, and several songs and stories featuring Elgeinst and him as protagonists remain.

**● Kulan Ramiru** 『Order · Good』
The hard-working man who falls just one step short type. Vice-Director of the Academy.

The eldest son of Tiru Ramiru, director of the Academy of Magical Studies, born between a couple of mages — a thoroughbred.

Gifted from childhood by virtue of his bloodline, but having been raised watching the Academy of Magical Studies, a place where those called geniuses gathered from across the kingdom, he grew up unable to have confidence in himself.

He deeply feared failure, and due to his nervous nature even his technique of inscription involved more perfectionism than necessary — while his ability was sufficient, it was that temperament that cost him the seat of director.

He was someone capable of very careful inscription, and for that reason received high evaluation from Krische, who directly conducted his instruction on many occasions — but the very detailed instruction conversely worsened his nervous tendencies in a vicious cycle (needless to say, Krische did not notice).

For those reasons he held some sense of aversion toward Krische in his younger years, but having come to his position of Vice-Director and accepted that *around here was about right for him*, that faded over time, and he became one of those who revered her, the childlike woman.

△ Historically

Elgeinst Ramiru's father. Vice-Director of the Alberan Kingdom Academy of Magical Studies.

■ — Characters from "Varkus"

**● Klaze** 『Chaos · Neutral』
The petty villain who wanted to make a family type. Granmeld's older-brother figure. Leader of the Varkus gang.

Born at an orphanage affiliated with a temple dedicated to Almera, the god of compassion.

His soldier father died in battle; his mother also died of illness, so he came to live at the orphanage. The orphanage, however, was secretly connected to slave traders and conducted human trafficking.

A girl who had been like an older sister to him being sold off was the trigger that led him to steal money with his younger brother and leave the orphanage — but it could not go well, and he ultimately drifted to the slums and came to live there.

His mother was the daughter of a merchant family, and having received a good education in childhood, he rose to the fore as a leader of street children in the slums — but life was harsh. The fact that his younger brother died of illness — with no money to buy medicine, no money to have a doctor examine him, and being unable to save him — became a trauma, and he came to strongly desire a stable life that was not hand-to-mouth.

His reason for maintaining a small group called *Varkus* and clearly distinguishing inside from outside was rooted in that, in a fear of the grief of losing those close to him.

——If this child is this full of energy, he probably won't die before me.

The reason he who refused to add to his inner circle picked up a certain child one day was something of that nature — and the reason he gave that child the name of his younger brother, though the child's personality was the polar opposite of his quiet, gentle brother, also came from that.

**○ Karris Dearudo Rinea Toonu** 『Chaos · Neutral』
The rough-and-tumble wild man type. General's aide. Former bandit.

Born in an area that had long been contested between Galshaarn and Alberan — a mixed-blood with Shaarn ancestry.

Both languages were spoken in his homeland, and he could speak two varieties of Western Common and Shaarn. His quick-tempered nature led to a fight with his parents, and he left the craftsman family home to become a caravan guard — valued for his ability to speak two languages and his strong fists, though quarrels continued there too.

From Galshaarn to Elsren and then to northern Alberan, repeating parting-on-bad-terms, he concluded he wasn't suited to being employed by others and became a bandit.

With extensive knowledge of caravans from experience, he collected passage tolls in the great forest, hunted other bandits, and expanded his territory — but hearing rumors of Granmeld the bandit-hunter, he went to challenge him to a duel, was overwhelmingly defeated, and finding it to his taste that Granmeld lived even more freely than himself, he joined under him.

A large man with a powerful physique, wielding a great axe, with an appearance described as a *rough-featured face*, everything about him large — and contrary to that appearance, extremely sharp-witted, and trusted as one of Granmeld's reliable subordinates.

After Begil transferred, he served as Granmeld's aide on the battlefield.

Self-conscious about his large nose.

△ Historically

Aide to Granmeld Varkus.

Known as one of the fierce warriors who wielded a great axe freely, resembling his patron in their large-bodied look.

Said to be a subordinate who had followed him since before his military service, and believed to have been a fellow bandit.

**● Tokusrenia Rinea Ardia** 『Order · Evil』
The well-born young man who fell to the dark side type. Battalion commander.

Second son of the Ardia family — a long-standing family in the north.

Growing up being compared to his brilliant older brother who was many years his senior, he was originally a kind-hearted young man, and considered his own role to be supporting his brother — he spent gentle days in that thought.

But his respected eldest son died in battle. Having entered the military as heir in his brother's place, he was crushed by that great responsibility.

The battalion commander was his brother's former aide. Given the position of provisional company commander in the battalion by virtue of that connection and his family background — but the battalion commander, who understood Tokusrenia's nature and ability and knew he was not suited to rough work, did not trust him and kept him away from danger as the brother of a former superior.

The members too whispered behind his back that he was a pampered noble-born with only his family name, nothing like his great elder brother — and before his eyes shone the company commander Gallen, earning brilliant distinction.

The soldiers, the company commanders, even the battalion commander — all praised the commoner-born, risen from the ranks — and even the lesser nobles among his subordinates directed genuine respect toward him.

His men said they wished they had that kind of superior, and tormented by strong inferiority and urgency, at just that time — a surprise attack on the battalion main body.

He clawed desperately at the opportunity that fell into his hands with the deaths of the battalion commander and others.

But even after forcibly seizing the merit and becoming battalion commander, the contempt only grew stronger each day.

*Why was it Tokusrenia and not Gallen*, their eyes all said — and no amount of effort would erase it.

Gradually the inferiority transformed into hostility toward Gallen, and he thought of using power to dispose of him through legal means — but Gallen returned from certain death as a matter of course, earning unbelievable distinction.

The company of companies, the soldier's hero — the more his reputation grew, the more Tokusrenia's ill repute grew, and by that point there was nothing but thinking of how to remove Gallen from the military.

And so he ordered him to burn villages — the act of the just he was known as — and succeeded in driving him from the military. But even those who had harbored slight sympathy for Tokusrenia turned away, and in the next battle his headquarters was struck in a surprise attack, and Tokusrenia died isolated.

*Better to have no incompetent superior*, it was whispered quietly.

**○ Rosine Varkus** 『Neutral · Good』
The pushover live-in wife type. Granmeld's wife.

Daughter of a capable administrator entrusted with Granmeld's administered territories.

Her father was originally a merchant of the Vaazler Trading Company, recruited by Norzan, and entrusted with asset management and trade under him.

Her mother was also a distant relative of the Velraigh family, and Norzan, sparing no effort in building the connections that Bogan disliked, was consolidating his foundation in this way — but the problem was Granmeld.

Norzan, troubled by Granmeld who had no interest or motivation in territory management and administrative foundations, sent him her father, who came to be entrusted with all matters of the Varkus household.

Rosine was born around that time, became attached to the blunt but gentle Granmeld, and began intensive study in order to serve the Varkus house like her father.

While still half-trained she helped her father and spent time at his side — within which what had been attachment became love, which she recognized, and she launched a fierce (by her own standard) campaign of approaches.

She learned his food and drink preferences and attended to them with the utmost care, always came out to greet him on his return, took the initiative to make snacks when he was drinking, visited him on various pretexts to consult and confer, stayed late working, and spent half the week's nights at the estate.

But from nervousness her expression was stiff and she never once showed a smile.

Granmeld, thinking she simply had a serious disposition, was impressed rather than aware — and they spent close to ten years that way.

The occasional small consideration and praise Granmeld expressed.

She was confident the feelings were being conveyed at least in part — and was shocked to be told *if you're thinking of the household, go to whatever man you like without worry* — and burst into tears, greatly disconcerting Granmeld.

She quite likes the life of looking after the sloppy Granmeld — in life, in women, in various ways — with small complaints, as his wife, and they spent peaceful days together after that.

Seeing such a pair of them, Norzan laughed too: *collared and turned into a dog*, indicating his once-mad-dog in jest.

■ — Characters from "Once Upon the Throne of God"

**○ Borok Riigia Andorzea** 『Chaos · Good』
The once-in-a-generation mountain man type. Mountaineer. Earl.

The great man who succeeded in the solo ascent of Greybyalbe (called Grabayaalbe in Krische and the others' time) — the highest point in the world.

He had already conquered numerous mountains and world trees before this, and there was no one in mountaineering circles who did not know him.

An extremely stoic person — not condescending to others, only tormenting and tempering himself — he boasted physical capabilities that were incomparable to an ordinary mage.

Having reached countless peaks and earned enormous profit from the books he published — his lifestyle was plain simplicity itself. Without sparing anything for the improvement and production of climbing equipment, travel expenses, or bribes for entering holy sites and private land — he spent almost all the income from his books on mountaineering.

After the Greybyalbe ascent he poured all his effort and energy into the development and prosperity of his hometown, using his personal connections — a type who, once he decides on something, charges straight ahead.

Having seen the *sisters selling nilkana* and the apron dress, he published a book called *Once Upon the Throne of God*.

In it he looked back on his past mountain travels in what was also a kind of autobiography, and described an encounter with the goddesses he had not recorded before — which drew the interest of certain Alberan researchers, who came from far away to hear his account in person.

△ Historically

The transcendent mountaineer known for the autumn solo ascent of Greybyalbe.

The ascent of Greybyalbe, thought impossible, and the Andorzea South Face Route gave dreams to many mountaineers — but he remained for three hundred years afterward as Greybyalbe's sole ascender.

His meticulous climbing records, standing at the boundary between death and life, were also strong in narrative, beloved even by general audiences who had no interest in mountaineering — and after the Greybyalbe ascent in particular he is said to have held overwhelming popularity.

*Once Upon the Throne of God*, written several decades later, recorded the truth of the Greybyalbe ascent and the encounter with the goddesses, and created controversy.

*Could those have been Alberinea and the others, who had become gods* — it is still discussed by some Alberan researchers.

■ — Characters from "The Boy and His Onee-san"

**○ Kyuris Ennis** 『Neutral · Good』
The once-upon-a-time wild kid type. Bookseller. Assistant master at the dojo.

A boy who attends the Black Flag Swordsmanship Dojo out of admiration for the Alberinea legend.

However, the Alberinea he met was exactly a neighborhood onee-san. She showed not a fragment of interest in swords, brought handmade cookies as treats, and spent her time at ease chatting with the members and going home — a mysterious person bearing no resemblance to the Alberinea of the many stories.

Day after day without once swinging a sword, pouring tea in an apron dress and eating cookies while conversing — one day, the boy put to her a long-held (two-year) question.

After seeing Krische's incomparably different level of strength, for a while he was tense whenever he saw her — but she was always the same cookie-onee-san.

He came to deal with her again as before — as a *slightly unusual onee-san*.

After that he inherited the family bookselling business, served as assistant master at the dojo teaching swordsmanship to boys and girls, and after Krische was gone he taught what he knew about her.

△ Historically

He left written records of his time at the Black Flag Swordsmanship Dojo, and they are one of the valuable records describing Alberinea's person.

The childlike, youthful character, the image of her smiling and distributing cookies to children, matches certain historical materials about Alberinea such as *At the House of the Noble Hawk*, and it is apparent he held a very favorable impression of her.

He also describes the Superintendent who was the Black Flag Special Service commander — Mia — writing once about the terrifying power of a spear throw he witnessed, and recording with some speculation that she may have been a warrior rivaling Kalua.

**○ Sherisia Kirusubeegu (Ennis)** 『Neutral · Neutral』
The rumored flower on a high ledge type. Head master of the dojo.

A girl who attends the dojo out of admiration for Krische and Kalua.

Having grown up in a single-parent household, she spent much time alone, and had a strong awareness that she must protect herself. From a young age her soldier father gave her basic sword instruction.

Her eyes were sharp and her head keen, and she was blessed with talent.

After that she developed strong admiration for Krische, and started bundling her hair in two tails like two tails, and putting effort into both sword and cooking.

Because she grew up in a single-parent household, when her father was away she often stayed at Kyuris's house, and the two grew closer.

*Whether your sword is strong or weak, I think it is fine to like someone you like.*

Hearing that straightforward response from Krische, she deliberately lost.

Later she succeeded Kalua as head master.

△ Historically

Second head master of the Black Flag Swordsmanship Dojo.

Wife of Kyuris Ennis; his written records describe her as a beautiful woman skilled with a sword.

In the records she is said to have inherited a sword resembling those of Alberinea and Kalua, and it is recorded that she wielded a sword similar to the two.

**○ Wesuriar Zain** 『Order · Neutral』
The type trampled underfoot by a giant's single step. Legitimate successor of Zain-style swordsmanship. Head of the Zain sword dojo.

A master born into the house of Zain — a house of swords — who from the time he was aware of himself has walked the path of the sword.

The Zain family is a distinguished noble family, but putting technique's pursuit and inheritance first, and with the policy of *serving the royal house with swordsmanship*, they relinquished their peerage quite some time ago. Their successors are also spread across various locations, invited by noble houses of the royal family and duke level and living by sword instruction and dojo management.

The rule that the strongest practitioner of the time takes the name of legitimate successor is still inherited — when the time comes for a changeover, the Zain successors scattered across each region are called and gathered, and the strongest practitioner of Zain's sword is selected. There are those like Begil who dislike being bound to anything and do not participate, but every several generations a legitimate successor who is not of the Zain family appears — a fair contest.

Wesuriar who survived that contest was a practitioner called a master among masters — but before Krische, without being able to lift even a finger, he was made to know the gulf in ability.

After that, in her sword he felt the reality of the ultimate sword the sword-saint Alkaras had sought, and became the first time in several generations to bite into the secret documents and add to them — grinding down and adding Alberinea's teachings.

Seeking stories from Begil who knew her well also led to more exchanges, but the conversations always went off track and were not much use in that direction.

△ Historically

One of the anecdotes describing Alberinea's strength — the master of Zain-style swordsmanship.

He is recorded thereafter as having received instruction in the sword from her as her student.

Currently there are styles that claim descent from this line, and the secret documents are said to have once contained writings about Alberinea's sword — and styles claiming to have inherited that secret art remain — but the original was burned when Kleinmeil collapsed.
