**Attributes:**

Law — Values rules, discipline, universal concepts, and places importance on an orderly society and its laws.

Chaos — Values the individual and their emotions and thoughts, prioritizing personal will over social discipline and morality.

Neutral — In between.

Good — Altruistic temperament. Prioritizes one's own moral convictions over personal gain, and is willing to die for them.

Evil — Selfish temperament. Prioritizes personal gain and does not hesitate to trample others to achieve it.

Neutral — A moderate temperament that leans neither way.

**How to read kingdom noble names:**

Example: 1. Gildanstein = 2. Kalnaros = 3. Vel = 4. Sarcarinea = 5. Alberan

1. Given name: Gildanstein

2. Largest territory under management: Kalnaros.
Originally all managed territories would be included, making the name extremely long, so it is abbreviated.

3. Male royals with succession rights use Vel; female royals use Viera.

4. Honorary title: Sarcarinea.

5. Family name: Alberan.

Formally, between 4 and 5, words indicating military rank such as Marshal or General, or court positions such as Chancellor, may be inserted, and after the family name, words indicating peerage such as Archduke may be added. However, since the horizontal text is already long enough to make one's eyes glaze over, the text prioritizes clarity by appending relevant ranks and titles in kanji at the end as needed.

Generally, everything aside from the family name is not necessarily inherited by children, but customarily the legitimate heir tends to inherit the territory and peerage unless there is a particular issue. However, the honorary title in position 4, which denotes martial distinction as a warrior, is never inherited.

When a person without a family name receives the abbreviated investiture of Nea (Junior Knight), they most commonly adopt their place of origin as a surname.
(Example) Gallen Nea Karka.

## ■ — Main Characters

**○ Krische Alberinea Christand — "Law / Evil"**

A mentally unhinged girl who can kill people without batting an eye. Adoptive daughter of House Christand. Protagonist. Alberinea.

A "cursed child" — an infant who did not cry — born of the royal house, and an erased First Princess of Alberan. She was supposed to be killed but was smuggled away by her caretaker servant Nora, and was raised in Karka Village.

An abnormal individual lacking empathy, who felt no pangs of conscience over murder or violence; however, having been showered with strong affection by good people, she has gradually come to understand ordinary ethics and emotions.

She fundamentally cannot interpret people's words beyond their literal meaning and is poor at reading emotions. Setting that aside, she possesses superhuman talent in every domain — a genius in the truest sense, capable of absorbing and advancing any technique with a single glance.

She operates on the basic assumption that "Krische should naturally be able to do anything," has a strong attachment to her own excellence, and is hardworking. The same applies to interpersonal relationships — she fundamentally acts on a cost-benefit basis, but being poor at reading others' emotions, she tends to overestimate what others give to her and underestimate what she gives in return.

As a result, when someone directs genuine goodwill toward her, she often demands an excessive "repayment" of herself, and she never begrudges the effort required. This behavior stems from her unease with interpersonal relationships, yet this aspect of her can be called genuinely virtuous — opinions of her diverge greatly depending on whether one sees her as a mere aberration or a girl who is simply too pure.

As an individual, she is an overwhelming, peerless existence, yet she is dependent by nature and mentally young and fragile. She is the type of person who becomes spiritually weaker the more she deepens relationships and gains human happiness — compared to the time when she was spiritually self-sufficient and placed no value on others, she is gradually drifting further from the "perfect existence" she once desired.

Yet her obsession with being perfect is fading, and she has come to wish she could become someone who feels a wide range of things like an ordinary person, rather than the perfection achieved by discarding everything else.

Since leaving Karka Village, her hobby is appreciating Bery's face. Beautiful.

**★ Combat Command:**

Extremely aggressive in both personal combat and tactics. A commander with the nature of a huntress who values mobility above all else for inflicting decisive damage, constantly disrupting the enemy through maneuver, trapping them, and never fighting from a disadvantageous position.

She favors "head-hunting tactics" that maximize the use of herself — the strongest piece — to eliminate enemy commanders without mercy, and boasts overwhelming strength against enemies who come at her head-on.

She fundamentally expects only the bare minimum from her subordinates and builds tactics on the assumption of no expectations, yet despite this, she has the bad habit of always needing to aim for maximum results.

While normally compensated for by the black century, this often leads to excessive burdens on herself and collapses from poor health — and yet she always pretends to be fine, which makes her a nuisance. A troublesome commander who will break down if others don't support her; the sole weakness of this strongest of fighters lies in something extremely basic — she is too stubborn to even manage her own health properly, like a child.

Appearance: Long silver hair. Purple eyes. Very small build. Slender. Modest chest. Fairy-like.

Armor: Casual clothing with gauntlets and reinforced boots.

→ Self-assessment: Very clever and excellent, but still immature in many areas. Might be a little foolish.

Strengths: Killing. Magic. Calculation.

Likes: Housework including cooking. Sweets. Being spoiled. Kisses, hugs, all forms of physical affection. Taking care of others. Appreciating Bery's face.

Dislikes: Anything that disturbs the peace around her. War.

Worries: Bery.

**○ Selene Argalitte Rinea Christand — "Neutral / Good"**

A serious, loving, unfortunate girl. Head of House Christand. Marshal.

The sole child of the martial house of Christand. She harbors a complex about being female, and is a hardworking girl striving to succeed her heroic and respected father. Until a few years ago, she cursed being born a woman rather than a man, and until she met the overwhelmingly talented Krische, she had been pushing herself to such excess that those around her grew worried.

As she cared for the talented yet childish and domestic Krische, her own perspective — which had been limited to seeing herself only as a general's daughter — broadened, and she came to value the small moments of daily life.

Talented and diligent, thus very capable, but fundamentally a worrier prone to overexertion. Raised surrounded by outstanding people — the hero Bogan and his subordinates, Bery, and Krische — she tends to underestimate herself and impose unreasonable effort upon herself, possessing an excessively serious and responsible personality. A workaholic by nature who, even when she finds free time, would likely devote it to study or administrative work.

Since becoming Marshal, "I'm busy" has become her catchphrase, but she is brimming with motivation as she fulfills her father's hopes, such as establishing the military academy.

She dotes on Krische enormously as a younger sister, but keenly senses the danger in her abnormality. She considers the very act of making Krische fight to be proof of her own inadequacy, and her goal is to free Krische from the battlefield so she can live a peaceful life. She hates herself for relying on and using Krische.

**★ Combat Command:**

Originally of an aggressive temperament, yet she constantly doubts her own abilities and avoids overreach. She prioritizes maximizing the abilities of her excellent subordinates, building tactics from the opposite perspective of Krische. She favors fundamental tactics and coordination that overwhelm with quality and numbers over clever schemes, and emphasizes securing that advantage at the strategic stage.

Appearance: Long golden hair. Blue eyes. Slender. Average chest. Refined beauty.

Armor: Silver wind-and-wing plate mail.

→ Krische: A foolish, absent-minded child. Cute. Krische should be allowed to live as an ordinary girl.

Strengths: Horsemanship. Command.

Likes: Swordsmanship. Reading. Doting on Krische.

Dislikes: War. Her own powerlessness.

Worries: Bery being stabbed and Krische setting off to kill a dragon.

**○ Bery (Lips) Argan — "Chaos / Good"**

A girl who would choose Krische-sama over the world. Servant of House Christand.

Daughter of House Argan, which fell due to business failure. Selene's aunt.

Originally sickly, which contributed to extremely low self-esteem and an introverted temperament. Troubled by the burden her body placed on others, she deliberately adopted a cold demeanor as a child so that others would not have to worry about her, but being disliked by the servants only worsened her condition, and at some point she began living while wishing for her own death.

Even after her family's fall and arrival at the Christand household resolved her health issues, her self-loathing only deepened. Entrusted by her beloved sister to look after the overly serious Selene, she tried to act cheerful as a substitute, but that too went poorly, and she cursed herself for being unable to do anything right.

One of the people saved by meeting Krische and being shown her pure love and goodwill. Outwardly gentle and personable, yet she developed a mild distrust of men during the fall of House Argan, and combined with her introverted nature, maintains a line in her personal relationships.

A natural talent who is too gifted to remain a mere servant, yet her temperament leads her to be satisfied with her role as a servant, and she does not wish to be in the public eye. Currently, she directs almost all of her talent and passion toward cooking, and Bogan and Selene always felt her talent was being wasted.

Despite appearances, she is stubborn, and though she seems logical, she is actually guided by intuition. When she feels something is right, she prioritizes emotion over reason.

She harbors deep love for Krische and thinks first of her happiness, though her possessiveness has also been growing. Her strong self-loathing and originally introverted nature give her love a dependent quality, but since Krische shares the same disposition, the situation is beyond help.

Recently she has let go of many things — what Krische desires is what she desires. She feels that is fine, and believes it is not a great error.

**○ Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan — "Law / Evil"**

A mentally unhinged girl who can poison people to death without batting an eye. First Princess of the Kingdom of Alberan.

A "cursed child" — an infant who did not cry — born of the royal house, but through the quick thinking of a servant named Nora, her life was spared and she was raised as a princess.

Like Krische, she possesses high intelligence at the cost of lacking empathy — a girl who feels no pangs of conscience over self-serving murder. She has killed her biological father, brothers, servants, and others by various means to seize power, and does not hesitate to dirty her hands for self-preservation.

Having nearly been killed as a cursed child immediately after birth, she is dominated by an obsessive conviction that she is always in danger of being targeted. In a sense she is an even greater aberration than Krische, but having been raised in the royal court, her acting ability is pathologically excellent, and her abnormality has never been exposed.

She recognizes her biological sister Krische as the only being equal to herself, and upon feeling they could cooperate, she framed her uncle Gildanstein for regicide and took refuge with the Christands.

Through building relationships of trust with Krische and those around her — Bery and Selene — changes have occurred within her as well. Having discovered the happiness that can only be obtained by emptying one's mind, she has mellowed considerably of late, though her essential nature and obsessive fears have not disappeared.

Her attachment to security and peace is even stronger than Krische's. Her murderous intent derives from that intense attachment to security and obsessive fear — she is not a pleasure killer.

Appearance: Long reddish-golden hair. Purple eyes. Very small build. Slender. Completely flat. Fairy-like.

→ Krische: The only being in the world who is her equal. Rather foolish and worrying. Spoiling the wrong person.

## ■ — People close to Krische or House Christand

**● Bogan Argalitte Vezrinea Christand — "Law / Neutral"**

A heroic man of noble resolve and decisive action. Selene's biological father. Bery's brother-in-law. Deceased. "Lightning Quick."

A hero of the kingdom who elevated the fallen baronial House Christand to margrave status in a single generation, rising from corporal to general. Having risen through the ranks himself, he understood the military's many problems, and assigned multiple tactically gifted aides to each corps commander, successfully distributing the processing burden and raising capability standards.

Known for fierce attacks, staged tactical withdrawals, and brilliant tactics, yet he believed that Christand's strength lay not in battlefield tactics but in the high information-processing capability enabled by advance preparation.

Though composed now, in his youth he often took reckless actions, preferring to swing his sword on the front lines. His rough swordsmanship mowed down countless enemies, and the sight of him charging forward earned him the name "Lightning Quick Christand" among the soldiers.

Unusually for a noble, he remained faithful to his dead wife and took no concubines.

He died after a duel with the Royal Prince Gildanstein.

**★ Combat Command:**

Originally an aggressive temperament and an outstanding frontline commander, but after taking a wife, he checked this impulse and prioritized being a commander who maximizes his subordinates' abilities. He believed that a general's duty was to discern each individual's qualities and use them in bold offensives and withdrawals to create dramatic chaos and upheaval on the battlefield in his army's favor. To achieve this, he considered raising the capability of subordinate commanders essential, and always trained those under him at one rank above their own — centurions to think as battalion commanders, battalion commanders as corps commanders.

Exceptionally skilled at reading when to withdraw; he never made a mistake in a fighting retreat, no matter how unfavorable the situation.

Appearance: Blond hair slicked back with white streaks. Blue eyes. Large, muscular build. Fierce, rugged face. Mustache.

Armor: Dull-steel falcon plate mail.

→ Krische: A prodigy of rare talent. Warped but kind.

Strengths: Rollka-style swordsmanship. Organization building. Command and operations.

Likes: Military history. Alcohol. His daughter.

Dislikes: Political strife. Extremists.

Worries: There being no young man worthy of his daughter. Finding someone for Bery.

**○ Gallen Rinea Karka — "Neutral / Good"**

A plain, sturdy, and unsociable man. Krische's adoptive grandfather. Bogan's former superior and adjutant to the Alberinea Direct Command.

Once a talented centurion who rose from common soldier, a remarkable figure who achieved numerous feats alongside Bogan despite possessing no mana. He earned the respect of his subordinates and had the ability to aim for general in the future, but retired from the military out of remorse for burning a village under superior's orders.

Afterward, he worked as a hunter in his home village of Karka, living peaceful days with his daughter and her husband, and their adopted daughter Krische. When a bandit attack killed his daughter and her husband, and Krische could no longer remain in the village, he left with her and returned to Bogan's service.

A long-suffering man who rejoices more than anything that Krische has found understanding and happiness in the Christand household.

He had his thoughts about being the one always left behind as those around him pass away, but he reminded himself that surviving comes with its own duties, and continues to push forward.

Recently, he has been seeing his beloved daughter's image in the clumsy Anne.

Never overextends, never overcommits. A commander with a hunter's nature who waits for the decisive moment of enemy vulnerability. Retreat, withdrawal, ambush — born a hunter, he favors tactics that are the polar opposite of "fair and square," preferring to exploit the enemy's carelessness and openings.

Kill the enemy without losing soldiers. He loathes needless losses above all else, yet when opportunity presents itself, he will pivot to an all-or-nothing offensive — an extreme on both ends.

Broad perspective; excels at command in chaotic melee.

Appearance: Carelessly grown white hair. Dark brown eyes. Muscular. Stern, unsociable face.

Armor: Dull-steel half plate.

→ Krische: A little different from others, but a good, kind girl — kinder than anyone.

Strengths: Archery. Command and operations. The forest. Adversity. Never losing sight of himself.

**○ Mia Nea Kirnan — "Neutral / Neutral"**

A capable girl who gets a raw deal. Vice Commander of the Black Flag Special Service Company.

From the village of Kirnan in the kingdom's north. Though distant, it neighbors Karka Village where Krische was found.

Initially joining the Christand military for work — thinking she might carry luggage or the like, using her natural strength and stamina — she was spotted for her mana aptitude and excellence by Krische, drafted, and found herself fighting on the front lines before she knew it.

Uneducated, with the knowledge of a mere country village girl, yet quick-witted and sharp by nature. A rare talent who had learned to construct imaginary muscles through mana without anyone teaching her, possessing excellent instincts in every domain; however, a stubborn and somewhat clumsy disposition means her swordsmanship ranks closer to the bottom of the black century.

She tends to make mistakes at critical moments and cannot help talking back. Her capable friend Kalua's existence invites easy comparison, and she is frequently singled out and scolded unfairly.

She was popular in her village but has low self-esteem and is dense; though she was confessed to by village men on several occasions, she let every chance slip by without realizing.

When she returned to her village, a childhood friend who had been making earnest advances confessed to her again, but she kindly advised him to take things more gradually and declined, prioritizing the Black Flag Special Service.

Since returning from her visit home, her sense of belonging to the Black Flag has only intensified, and she trains with redoubled vigor.

**★ Combat Command:**

She has studied fundamental tactics, is sharp-minded and broad in perspective, and is very capable as a centurion-level officer. She has learned melee command for units composed of mana users, able to fully utilize their abilities which differ from ordinary soldiers'.

She emphasizes stability through coordination and cooperation leveraging her soldiers' abilities.

**○ Kalua Nea Belyus — "Chaos / Neutral"**

A girl who seems easygoing but is surprisingly weak to pressure. Special Operations Squad Leader of the Black Flag Special Service Company.

From the village of Belyus in the kingdom's south. Daughter of the village chief.

Originally a demure young lady of the chief's household. Known for being caring, clever, and comely, she was beloved by the entire village.

She was supposed to learn trade from a merchant connected to her family for the village's further development, but when her youngest sister was kidnapped by slave traders, she abandoned everything and ran across the country searching.

Traveling alone as a woman was no easy thing, and she wore down body and spirit against various harsh realities while continuing her search for years, but her strength could not reach and she found no leads.

At some point she realized resignation had filled her heart, yet she could not stop. She chose to keep fighting out of inertia, and volunteered for the Christand military — which opposed the royals said to be connected to many slave traders, the infamous Gildanstein.

The only person who calls Krische "Bunny" to her face, she often looks after the helpless, child-like girl.

There was an element of compensating for her lost sister Elvena, but having had her sister saved, she now pledges heartfelt gratitude and loyalty to Krische.

Through various experiences during her solo travels, she adopted a rough way of speaking and boorish mannerisms, cultivating a tomboyish demeanor. That said, her upbringing instilled proper etiquette, and she can conduct herself as a refined lady when she wishes.

Jaded and uninterested in romance, but rather weak to pressure.

Extremely quick-witted, resourceful on the spot, and possesses aptitude as a commander. However, she prefers to swing a sword herself and tends toward tunnel vision, so to leverage her abilities, she needs to be positioned in the rear-center rather than the very front.

She is a cut above in terms of individual-level coordination, able to instinctively direct and work with other soldiers on the fly.

**○ Dagra Rinea Alkus — "Law / Good"**

A serious man who loves military life. Company Commander of the Black Flag Special Service. Vulture.

A commoner mana user. He has no single outstanding trait, yet has no weaknesses either — experienced, perceptive, and the kind of soldier who respects military discipline and orders that Krische favors.

Stern yet always sincere with his subordinates — an ideal centurion. During the war against the Holy Elsren Empire, he participated in Selene and Krische's special assault force as a centurion.

His solid performance earned him selection as commander of Krische's direct unit, the "black century," composed entirely of mana users.

Initially, he held excessive fear of Krische's abnormal abilities and coldness, but upon seeing the trust directed at him, the magnitude of the role entrusted to him, and the true, life-sized figure of the girl Krische Christand, he pledged heartfelt loyalty.

Serious, strict with both himself and others, and a born soldier who loves military organizational life above all else. He had assumed marriage had nothing to do with a soldier like him, but was pursued ardently by a friend's daughter and fell — he is a father of one.

His shaved head is simply maintained by daily shaving for hygiene in service; he is not actually bald. His hooked nose is prominent, but he is by no means ugly.

Deeply moved by Krische's consideration in refusing to let him accompany her due to concern for his health, he is prepared to lay down his life for Krische in any capacity, public or private.

Had he accompanied her, there was a high probability of death, though there is a possibility Gildanstein could have been slain before Nakilus arrived.

Having been entrusted with the company and receiving Aleha and Waltza as exceptionally capable subordinates, he is brimming with motivation from the moment of his return.

**★ Combat Command:**

Experienced and hailing from light infantry units often deployed in special situations such as mountains, he always returns to fundamentals and does not err in judgment regardless of the situation.

Accustomed to fighting in numerically inferior circumstances — raids, sabotage, diversions — he demonstrates full command capability even in chaotic close-quarters melee when cornered.

Unlike Mia, he was not purely cultivated in mana-user units, so he tends to make safer choices based on experience when handling his troops. However, this results in less attrition, and the black century under his command is extremely stable.

**○ Aleha (Klauzera Shuindel Sarshenka) — "Law / Good"**

A hero-chasing man. Former general of the Holy Elsren Empire. Formerly of the ducal House Sarshenka. Vice Commander of the Black Flag Special Service Company.

Third son of a great noble house, pushed into the military as if driven away by his brothers. After being entrusted to the military, he displayed his talents from a young age, amassing countless achievements from his time as a centurion, earning the title of "Holy Knight" and the rank of general while still young.

He participated in four battles against the kingdom's hero Bogan Christand — twice as a battalion commander and twice as a corps commander — and showed remarkable performance every time, but was defeated in all four due to Christand's staged withdrawals and fierce counterattacks.

While harboring strong hostility toward Bogan, he held proportionate admiration for him, collecting records of battles Bogan was involved in, sometimes visiting the actual sites, and studying his tactics.

During the recent invasion, he was outmaneuvered by Krische and suffered a devastating defeat against the Christand military. This forced the previously advantageous Empire into retreat, and those who resented him seized the opportunity — while he escaped execution, his territory was confiscated, and his general and Holy Knight titles were stripped. # Chapter 147: What Was Left Behind

From morning, the royal palace was astir.

Before Queen Kreschenta appeared in the audience chamber, the restless ministers exchanged glances and whispered among themselves about the abnormal events of the previous night.

When someone mentioned that so-and-so had been killed with their limbs torn apart, another voice rose — that must be a false report; the person I heard about was someone different.

Not one of them grasped the full picture.

No one imagined that seventeen great nobles had been killed in a single night.

Yet as the meeting time drew near, the absent faces became conspicuous.

It was then that they understood.

Seventeen prominent nobles, beginning with Duke Arcasakos — all those whose names had been mentioned in the earlier conversations — not one of them was present.

Of course, there were various legitimate reasons for missing a meeting — illness and the like — and absenting oneself from a meeting before the queen was rare but not unheard of.

Yet nearly half the usual faces had vanished, and everyone felt ice along their spines at the thought of *what if*.

If they collated everything that had been said and assumed it had all actually happened, then in a single night, seventeen had been butchered.

The greatest incident since the founding of the kingdom.

Just as their confusion reached its peak, the solemn fanfare of trumpets sounded.

Kreschenta entered late as always, walked the blood-red carpet as always, and took her seat on the throne as always.

Wearing her usual adorable, gentle smile.

"Good morning. It seems everyone has gathered."

What endured was the dust-covered pride of those who clung to it.

——That was Kreisharana.

* * *

Elevated wooden houses standing in rows — the large settlement called "the village" in Kreisharana — its central square.

"Jade tigers and indigo deer……"

A white-bearded elder furrowed his brow at the report.

Kreisharana Chieftain Alkirens.

Left behind by the times, a thousand warriors who lived by the faith of the martial power they once held — this was the man who stood at their summit.

Having come out to the square upon receiving a report moments ago, he personally directed the intelligence gathering — in his bearing, despite his age, lived the power befitting a leader.

Arms folded, he cast a stern gaze at the messenger.

"Yes. Each one likely felled with a single blow…… This is no ordinary skill. The Warrior Leader is currently tracking the footprints left at the scene."

What had begun as a search for his daughter Lila had now shifted entirely to investigating the anomaly that had befallen the mountain.

"Vinsreel."

"He suggests that women be gathered somewhere secure, in case of emergency."

Alkirens's expression grew even graver.

When magical beasts were sighted near the village, non-combatants were gathered in one place to prevent confusion during the fighting.

Against creatures like jade tigers, which ran freely across ground and trees, such confusion could be fatal.

The site where the traces were found was quite far away, but Vinsreel was saying that against such a foe, even that level of caution was warranted.

Vinsreel was the village's strongest warrior — the Warrior Leader.

He had become a warrior at the age of thirteen, personally slaying more than ten magical beasts.

Since becoming Warrior Leader, not a single beast had been allowed near the village — and if Vinsreel thought this situation demanded such measures, it was surely a genuine emergency.

"Drettar! Assemble all griffin cavalry, including those off duty. Sound the tiger-entry bell!"

"Yes, sir!"

The warrior called Drettar flew his griffin to the bell tower.

The great bell rang across the mountains.

In response, countless bells from distant settlements echoed in reply.

Once the tiger-entry bell sounded, all men aside from the griffin cavalry ceased their work and seized their spears.

The chosen griffin cavalry — Kreisharana's professional warriors — were not the only fighters. The men called levied warriors had devoted every moment of leisure to training — fanatics of the martial way.

They fortified the defenses of their own scattered settlements.

Women were gathered in pre-designated nearby storehouses — having lived under constant threat of magical beasts, their response was in some ways faster than a trained army's.

Their warrior society endured, and its strength remained undiminished, precisely because of such threats. Decadence was a word unknown to Kreisharana.

With the bells, the air grew taut. Young and old, men and women alike rushed about.

"Alkirens, what is the situation?"

"An intruder in the mountains, it seems, Elder. ……Apparently alone or in a very small group, but capable of killing jade tigers and indigo deer."

A bald old man with a beard reaching his chest.

The previous chieftain — Elder Bikirles — hobbled forward with his cane, a warrior supporting his shoulder. It had been a long time since he had appeared in public.

His thin limbs trembled, yet his eyes were sharp.

A single push might topple him, but the old man still radiated unmistakable authority.

"Not an army?"

"No. From the traces, it appears to be someone from the kingdom."

"……Then the target is the divine being."

"I can't say for certain, but…… the probability is high. There have been no such reports yet, however."

The location where the magical beasts were found was far from the forbidden domain.

And warriors were already flying about searching for Lila.

If the intruder were wandering in search of the forbidden domain, their location would eventually be pinpointed.

Only the Empire's high priests and the Kreisharana knew its location.

Unless the intruder made a beeline for it, there was still time.

"……As a precaution, I'll send some warriors ahead to reinforce the forbidden domain's watch. Azraban! Take six men and proceed at once to monitor the road to the forbidden domain alongside Velvas."

"Yes, sir. Right aw——"

At that moment, a shrill whistle pierced the sky from the direction of the forbidden domain.

Looking up, a lone warrior on a griffin.

The bird glided in and landed before Alkirens.

"Elder. Chieftain. Whistle signals from the entrance to the forbidden domain."

"……What?"

"Two of my companions have gone to investigate, but it seems someone——"

Alkirens's eyes flew wide, and he roared his orders.

"Go call Vinsreel back immediately! Azraban — I'm going myself. Drettar! Once all warriors are assembled, leave a skeleton guard and bring the rest to the forbidden domain! Now!"

* * *

*'Bery, Krische thinks this one turned out pretty well! Is it good?'*

*'……Is that pilkrus? How lovely — just a touch of tartness, with sweetness…… Fufu, it's very delicious.'*

So that the person she loved would think it was good.

*'Ehehe, does Krische look cute?'*

*'Yes, it suits you perfectly.'*

So that the person she loved would think she was pretty.

Small gestures, little extra efforts, hoping to bring a moment of happiness.

She existed for the sake of making Bery happy, and Bery's happiness was her own.

There was always more happiness than she could hold, right there — and it would go on forever.

She had always thought that way, and so when it suddenly seemed to vanish, for one instant, everything ceased to matter.

Her mind stopped working.

There was a sensation of coming undone — and so she decided: she would make it continue.

She thought about what must be done to continue it, and set her eyes on the task.

At least while she was focused on what needed doing, she could avoid looking at everything else.

Whether this was right or wrong — she didn't care.

If there was a possibility, it was what must be done, and as long as possibility existed, she could keep pursuing it.

That was all that mattered.

She knew there was no such thing as eternal life.

Someday, surely, it would come.

But that was the far future — at least not now.

At least, it was not supposed to end like this.

So she would continue.

Because as long as she did, she could think of that alone.

Because then, she could believe the ordinary days would return.

"……We are almost there, Krische-sama."

Past the cliffside road of the forbidden domain, the terrain gradually became rolling hills.

Beyond them lay the dragon, she was told.

After the sentinels, she had encountered two more attacks — she had neutralized roughly five Kreisharana warriors and gained four more spears.

Eight-shaku spears; they looked slender and light for their length, but had iron cores driven roughly a shaku deep into the tip.

Simple in appearance, but surprisingly sturdy and well-made.

If the legends were accurate, she could never have too many spears — gaining extras here was the most welcome development of all.

The jade tiger that had been following them seemed to grow frightened; its pace lagged.

Probably because of the dragon.

The closer they came, the higher the temperature climbed, and the air here was saturated with an extraordinary density of mana.

Even Krische felt slightly dizzy — such a thick concentration. Given the being that produced it, it was no wonder a mere beast was terrified.

Still, if the tiger chose not to follow, that was fine too.

"Gururun doesn't have to come if she doesn't want to."

She said as much and walked on; the tiger hesitated, but trailed after Krische.

Whether out of gratitude for the meat, or simply having grown attached — it was a good thing, but also a worry.

She was not counting on it in a fight.

Given the likelihood of it being caught up in what came next, perhaps she should have forced it to stay behind.

Letting an uninvolved creature die was, like the Kreisharana, regrettable.

At the very least, as a "good girl," one would think of it as pitiful — that was probably normal.

But she no longer had time to deliberate.

Krische crested the hills and looked down.

Below was an enormous basin, roughly two ri across in every direction.

On the far side rose a massive cliff face, encrusted with a staggering amount of raw magic crystal that shimmered blue.

——Before that blue wall, something like a rocky mountain.

The grey shape was the size of a small hill — and the blue light that its very body emitted.

"……That is the divine being — Yagelnaus-sama."

Lila spoke in a tense, quiet voice.

Krische's eyes went wide, and she stared for a long, expressionless time.

She did not speak. She did not move.

Lila watched her with bewilderment.

Perhaps she had reconsidered?

——No, that was not it.

"Bururun. Spears."

Krische took every spear from the horse's back and hurled them, one by one, down into the basin.

"……Um, Krische-sama."

The basin's slope was steep.

She could descend, but climbing back with the horse would be difficult.

A precaution, most likely — yet watching the spears thud one after another into the earth below, Lila seized the girl's sleeve in a pleading gaze.

"……If the conversation fails, Krische probably won't have much room to spare. A minimum preparation."

With that, Krische leapt into the basin.

The jade tiger followed. Lila hesitated, then followed too.

It was as if she had been swallowed whole by something enormous.

The mana density in the basin transcended "dense" — it was as concentrated as the mana inside a living body.

As the two drew closer, there upon the rock-mountain body of the dragon — a vast, red-purple eye opened,

"——"

For an instant, even Krische's body felt a chill of rigidity sweep through.

Like a serpent rearing its head, the dragon's neck — tall as a tower — rose.

And as if demonstrating the natural order of things, it looked down upon Krische from its immense height.

A wolf before a rabbit.

A serpent before a frog.

That something which arises when instinct comprehends the gulf between predator and prey.

At the very least, it was an emotion Krische had never felt in her life.

A crawling sensation along her spine — and the moment that eye opened, her entire body erupted in gooseflesh.

The jade tiger that had barely kept pace let out a whimper, turned tail, and curled up at a distance, trembling.

——The eye was utterly inorganic, like a jewel of red-purple.

Not threatening, not mocking.

The mere act of seeing warped the air itself.

Its gaze alone disrupted every thread of mana in the vicinity, disturbing the world's harmony.

The mana that spilled from its body was beyond measure.

Even if there were a thousand Krisches — even ten thousand — their combined mana would never approach what this being possessed.

This was truly the embodiment of heaven's will.

Like a thunderstorm. Like a tsunami swallowing the earth.

It was not a beast, and should not even be called a living creature.

Seated upon the indisputable summit, it was the only being permitted from birth to look down upon all life.

Existing simply because it was meant to — a thing equal to a god.

——That was the being called a dragon.

"Di——"

Lila dismounted the trembling Larnel and fell to both knees.

She pressed her forehead to the earth, and in a shaking voice, opened her mouth.

"Please…… forgive this…… sudden intrusion, Yagelnaus-sama……"

As a priestess, Lila had visited here several times before.

Yet the atmosphere was clearly different.

Something was distinctly, unmistakably different from all those other times.

What to say — the words she had prepared fled her mind. She could not think of what she should say; her body was frozen with terror.

The dragon made no reply and rose.

Its scales — like the surface of a rock face — shifted, and bat-like wings — resembling nothing as small as any bat's — spread wide.

When it stood, its head threatened to overtop the cliff behind it; its body rivaled a fortress — the ancient texts had recorded only truth.

Its gaze was directed at one person — the girl who stood facing it squarely.

Red-purple met the girl's inorganic purple, and held.

After that, neither the dragon nor Krische moved.

An unnaturally long silence fell.

It was as if two predators who had chanced upon each other were measuring the moment.

If one moved, the other would surely follow.

That conviction — sensed on instinct — left Lila unable to so much as lift a finger.

All she could do to show herself harmless was keep her forehead pressed against the earth.

——It was the dragon's voice that broke the equilibrium.

*'This is a rare visitor indeed. ……Small though you are, you stir in me a tremor I have not known in an age.'*

It was as though something was raking directly through the inside of her skull — a voice and yet not a voice.

The dragon did not open its mouth.

Its voice did not travel through the air, but permeated the skull directly through mana.

By the voice alone, the surrounding mana quivered and warped.

The sensation was like having a foreign presence invade the interior of one's own body — a discomfort beyond description.

*'Those eyes — I see. You do not underestimate. You do not scorn. ……Knowing full well, with that small body, you believe you can slay me.'*

Being here was like being inside the dragon's stomach.

The sky was high above and their bodies stood in the open world — yet what the two felt was an inescapable sense of confinement.

*'You…… believe this, do you not?'*

*——How very interesting.*

The dragon continued, then turned its gaze to the spears around them.

And then once more, fixed its eye on Krische.

*'——With these twigs, you intend to slay me?'*

There was a note of amusement.

Like a predator before its prey.

If one were to name it, it would be called delight.

Krische listened to the words resounding in her skull and stepped forward, composed.

"……Krische had hoped it might not come to this."

Strangely, her body was almost trembling with tension.

She had never once considered the possibility of being killed by someone.

Excepting infancy, no matter who the opponent, escape had never been difficult, and by now she was confident she could destroy anyone who turned hostility her way.

But the being before her was the exception.

*——Perhaps what Krische feels is fear.*

She observed that sensation from somewhere distant, as though watching from outside herself.

Her legs wanted only to run.

But the being had already acknowledged her presence.

Fleeing now was no longer possible.

To put distance between them would mean losing even the slim chance of victory.

And that was all it was — a chance, not a certainty.

She had not underestimated it, but confronting it up close, feeling its pressure in her own body — she understood this was a harder opponent than she had imagined.

"Krische came to ask if you would share some of your flesh and blood."

And yet, the reason she could stand here calmly was something she pondered.

Surely, three years ago, she could not have stood at all.

She would have turned and run before it even noticed her.

Because nothing had frightened her more than the thought of being killed.

*——So please, never leave my side.*

She pictured the red hair, and that smile.

She pictured losing it.

Ah — perhaps kaa-sama had felt this way.

There was something even more frightening than her own death.

*——Please…… please, just spare this child.*

That was why kaa-sama had stepped in front of her.

If it came to that — if it meant preventing that — she didn't care what happened to herself.

Perhaps that was how much kaa-sama had loved Krische.

"At first, Krische intended to kill you and take the heart. But if you would grant the request, that would be acceptable too…… Killing you seems quite difficult even for Krische."

The fear was there, and yet she could believe it did not matter — surely it was because she felt this way.

To believe so felt like something very happy.

As though, just a little, her warped self had become a bit more normal,

"……But from the look of things, you're not going to just say 'Sure.' Krische wouldn't say 'Sure' either, in your position…… Krische understands it's an unreasonable request."

She wished that feeling was true,

"So, with that in mind. What Krische should say to you is: obey, unless you wish to die."

And so the girl declared to a being equal to a god.

"Krische will not yield a single step. Krische cannot yield. ……Then further words are meaningless, aren't they?"

Having witnessed the vast mana, the overwhelming majesty.

And yet her words held not the faintest trace of fear or reverence.

Her quiet voice rang with unyielding strength,

"Die, or submit to Krische — the choice is yours."

A clear, unmistakable will lived within it.

She would spit in the face of the natural order itself, and drive her blade into the heavens without hesitation.

For her wish, for what she sought — she would trample heaven and earth, all of creation, and claim dominion.

——The arrogant one.

That was the essential nature of the creature called human,

*'……No scales, no fangs, no wings to take to the sky.'*

and she was its purest expression.

*'With that tiny body and that pittance of mana, you would threaten me?'*

What followed the voice was a discontinuous wave of mana that did not quite form words.

It bore a strong resemblance to human laughter.

*'……I see. How amusing.'*

Before the fearless beast, the dragon spread its wings wide.

Wings vast enough to swallow a small village whole — beyond the scale of any living thing.

Merely opening them set the air spiraling, warping the vast mana.

*'Then. ……Whether you can slay me or not — try with that body of yours.'*

And the dragon beat its wings once.

——What struck the windless world was a gale that would have flattened trees.

A single wingbeat turned calm into storm and carved the earth.

The undergrowth was peeled up like planking, and even small stones became projectiles — a single motion filled the entire two-ri space with death.

Even so, the girl was calm.

"……I'm sorry. Krische thought it would come to this from the start."

"——!?"

She spoke a single line, then seized Lila's arm and flung her backward.

Then she herself dove back — and wrenched free one of the spears driven deep into the earth.

*——She would not miss.*

A pebble grazed her cheek.

She did not even register the sting. She simply advanced into the storm.

As always, unchanged — her body trembling, yet her mind ice-cold within.

She took a deep forward lean.

The turbulence born along the ground — she slipped beneath it, into the gaps.

She read every shift of the wind, every speed, and calculated every resulting deviation.

To decipher the workings of all things, to seize them, to turn them to her advantage — that was always her role.

She did not doubt her own abilities.

What lived there was a madness-laced self-regard.

Nothing was impossible for her — she believed it from the bottom of her heart, and she acted.

Her heel struck the earth.

The sudden deceleration channeled every force upward through her body, into her arm, into the spear.

The task was the same.

Before the spear she released, a mere tornado was no obstacle.

It mattered not if what stood before her was an army, a fortress — or a dragon, or a god.

——What her hand had always released was a spear that pierced all in its way.

The small girl's body.

Her entire frame cracked like a whip, and the spear released with a thunderclap streaked toward the dragon's body.

It crossed nearly a full ri in an instant, and punched through the dragon's ore-like scales with ease.

The base of the left wing — it bored through the flesh, gouged, and tore.

The spear's force did not stop there; it shattered the wall of magic crystal behind the cliff.

Pulverized crystal and crimson blood-mist mingled — and the color that bloomed was purple.

Behind the girl.

——What the warriors saw first, as they crested the hill, was the girl's god-slaying spear.
