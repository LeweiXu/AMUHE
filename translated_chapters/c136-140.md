# Chapter 136: Loyalty

Aleha and Waltza were to be temporarily assigned as training instructors for the Black Flag Special Service, working alongside Dagra and the others.

Ideally, they would be employed as generals or corps commanders, but considering the trust of others and the kingdom's reputation, it would not do to use them openly for the time being. With the conference accompanying the queen's debut imminent, Selene and Kreschenta were in agreement on the desire to avoid unnecessary complications.

The concern was the Christand corps commanders, who had crossed blades with the two.

It was deemed necessary to inform them in advance, and so a meeting was arranged at the Christand estate for introductions, but――

"Hahaha, this is our first time meeting face to face, but……I remember our encounters well. You're skilled for your age――I'd love to have a light exchange of blades again if the opportunity arises."

"Yes, I'd welcome that wholeheartedly. If I can have another chance at a rematch, there's nothing I'd want more."

"The General spoke highly of you as well. If he could hear that Aleha Sarshenka is now sitting in this very room, even the General would be astonished."

The one laughing merrily was a massive man who looked as though even his face were made of muscle――Second Corps Commander Kokys Agrand.

And beside him, wearing a sinister smile, was former Fourth Corps Commander and Marshal's Advisor Elrouga Faren.

Casting a warm glance at Krische, who sat beside him endlessly eating cookies, Elrouga then directed his gaze across the table, past Aleha and Waltza, to Selene.

"General Velraigh won't object either, so long as he hears it was decided by Selene-sama and Krische-sama. He's a straightforward man in that regard."

"I see, I'm a little relieved. I was wondering whether you might have reservations about them."

Setting aside others, if those who were close to her family and had long served under her father showed reluctance, Selene had been thinking she would need to reconsider their handling.

No matter how talented and promising as future generals, it would be a greater problem if it sowed discord.

The issue with Krische was that she cared far too little about such things.

Even against an enemy she had been killing just days ago, once the war ended, it was all smiles and hello.

In a sense, it could be called a healthy attitude, but it was so excessively healthy that the people around her could not keep up.

Seeing the two men's demeanor and taking some measure of reassurance at last――Selene finally felt as though a weight had been lifted from her shoulders.

Leaving everything to take its course, hands off, the discussion entirely in Selene's hands.

The cookie-cracking puppet that Krische had become finally opened her mouth.

"For the time being, Krische will assign both of you as adjutants within the Black Flag Special Service. With the personnel increase, there probably aren't enough people who can command……Krische is thinking of eventually entrusting the Fourth Corps to you."

"That would be advisable. Your command and operational skills are――more than proven."

The First Corps would go to Keith, the former Third Battalion Commander, who had already been entrusted with various duties.

The Second Corps would remain under Kokys as before.

The Third Corps would be given to Begil, the former First Battalion Commander. Begil had always possessed the caliber for it, so there was no problem.

But the Fourth Corps was the issue; it needed to be reconstituted.

The Marshal's Advisor――Elrouga, who might need to move to various regions as Selene's hands and feet, also required forces at his disposal, and the soldiers and officers of the former Fourth Corps had been reassigned wholesale to his command.

Since the Christand army under Krische would operate as central reinforcements dispatched to various fronts, being down one corps was not a major problem, but eventually they wanted a four-corps structure.

Yet suitable candidates were hard to find.

Fourth Battalion Commander Barga had never gotten along well with Krische, so he was moved to serve as Keith's adjutant.

Second Battalion Commander Fagran seemed content with his position as battalion commander and was placed under Begil. On top of that, he apparently had reservations about becoming a fellow corps commander alongside Begil and Granmeld, who had been his superiors in the Wolf Pack.

Fifth Battalion Commander Gainz was also excellent, but had declined, saying that as a hunter-born man with shallow tactical knowledge, he was not suited for corps command. As a result, there was no one who neatly fit the Fourth Corps Commander position.

They could have selected someone from Terrius's former Third Corps, but the most promising officers had been transferred either to Nozan in the east, where restoring combat strength was a pressing concern, or to Felwors in the west.

There were many capable officers, but when it came to entrusting someone with corps command, those remaining lacked the necessary track record.

That was when Aleha appeared at just the right time.

He had originally held the rank of general――setting aside the various complications, his ability was beyond question, and as a corps commander, there was no more fitting candidate.

Eventually, of course, they intended to use him as a general, but that required years and proven results, and as a former enemy general, he needed even more time to build trust from those around him. From every angle, he was the ideal person to keep close at hand.

That said, the reorganization of the Fourth Corps had not yet been planned out, and things at the palace were still unsettled.

So for the time being, his post would be under Dagra, working as Krische's general assistant and training instructor for Mia and the others as part of the Black Flag Special Service.

"Appointing a former enemy general as a corps commander is quite bold, I must say……"

Elrouga regarded Aleha and Waltza.

The two straightened ever so slightly under his keenly intelligent gaze, which carried an almost cold edge.

Though until recently they had held the rank of corps commander, Elrouga's military career and battlefield achievements were illustrious.

Military men showed deep respect not only for rank but sometimes equally for such experience.

Having fought him repeatedly as enemies, the two knew well that Elrouga Faren was a man whose ability matched his current position.

Their deference was only natural.

"You stated that you learned your tactics from Christand and respected Bogan-dono as a rival to surpass――I believe those words are no lie. Traces of such influence were visible in the tactics you employed, and I had long sensed an extraordinary attachment."

Elrouga spoke as though recalling the past.

"Meeting you in person, I can see you harbor nothing hidden in your hearts. Your eyes are clear. I believe these two are warriors worthy of trust."

"Is that so? Ehehe, Krische thought they were probably fine too, but if Skeleton says so, then Krische feels relieved."

To the smiling Krische looking up at him, he returned his sinister grin and patted her head.

Though somewhat thrown by the name "Skeleton," Aleha appeared relieved to learn he had apparently passed the test, and bowed his head with a grateful, "Thank you."

"Direct your thanks to Krische-sama. ……However, just one thing."

"……What is it?"

"Krische-sama is the most gifted and most excellent warrior of all. And she is also the most kind-hearted and pure. ……No matter what may come to pass, will you swear here and now, upon your name, to pledge your unwavering loyalty and never betray that trust?"

Elrouga deepened his smile at the slightly surprised Aleha.

"I intend to live long for Krische-sama's sake, but I am getting on in years. I would like to be able to face my end with peace of mind."

Aleha nodded with a serious expression, as if those words had made everything clear. If that is the case, then of course.

He stood, walked to Krische's side, knelt, and placed a hand over his heart.

"……Without ever turning my back on this debt of honor, I swear to devote this sword for all my life――as a warrior and no more, I pledge upon this body, upon the name of Aleha."

"Err……?"

Krische looked at Elrouga in confusion.

Elrouga smiled and told her.

"'I shall engrave your oath upon my heart' is the standard reply in situations like this."

"Haa……I shall engrave your oath upon my heart."

When Krische spoke the words, Aleha raised his face and said, "I am honored."

"It is an old custom. Should such an occasion arise again, you need only recall what was done today."

"Nn……there are all sorts of things, aren't there. The books don't really cover them, and Krische doesn't quite……ah, you may sit?"

"Yes."

After she said this, Krische recalled something from the past and looked up at Bery, who stood with perfect posture beside her.

Bery tilted her head, and upon recalling the mention of "old customs," her cheeks colored faintly. She raised a single finger before her lips.

*It's a secret*, her mouth moved silently, and Krische smiled with a look of quiet amusement.

Selene stared at their secret exchange with a look that was somewhere between exasperated and sulky.

The underground laboratory of the royal palace.

"The frame is complete, Krische-sama."

Neigal, the wounded soldier leading the Black Flag Special Service fabrication squad, nodded approvingly at the finished frame of the figure and called out to Krische.

Before Krische, transparent glass containers and small bottles filled with powders and mysterious liquids were lined up. She had been examining and holding up magic crystals, not particularly involved in the figure's construction.

At his call, Krische nodded and stood, walking to the platform in the center of the room to study the iron figure lying upon it. Elvena followed close behind.

It was something that could only be described as an iron skeletal specimen with the torso and head replaced by birdcages.

Unlike the previous version connected by chains, this one used ball joints, with magic crystals inserted on their inner surfaces.

The limbs were broadly similar to a human body, but the legs in particular were built thicker to bear the weight, and two heavy springs were attached to the Achilles tendon area of each leg.

Considering the load that would be placed on it, the feet would bear the greatest burden, and this was an attempt to alleviate that strain.

The feet were roughly twice the size of a human's and somewhat misshapen, already fitted with large wooden shoes.

Ungainly as it was, the weight was distributed accordingly.

"We've shaved and adjusted the finer details, so there shouldn't be any problems."

"Is that so. Krische will activate it."

Krische held up her necklace and channeled mana through it.

The magic crystal at the pendant's tip flickered irregularly, and the magic crystal inside the birdcage head――the core――responded with a slightly different pattern of flickering.

In a chain reaction, the heart section glowed, and from there the magic crystals connecting to the four limbs emitted a blue luminescence.

She told Neigal and the others to stand back, then continued.

"First, warm-up exercises."

Krische's magic crystal flickered again, and the core responded.

With a creaking sound, the iron figure sat up and stretched its torso backward in a full-body stretch.

It was a movement Selene often performed.

Then it descended from the platform, twisted at the waist, bent forward, backward, left, and right, and began doing calisthenics――the movements were comically absurd, but its appearance was less funny than deeply unsettling.

Elvena edged backward and hid behind Krische's back.

The others too watched the scene with startled expressions.

"……How very strange. I cannot begin to fathom how this works."

"Krische sends mana waves back and forth with it. Long flashes and short flashes――she just combines those into patterns to make it move. The mechanism is quite simple."

Krische once again made the pendant――the transmitter――flicker.

In that instant,

"――!?"

"Kyah!?"

Without warning, the iron figure lashed down at Krische with a knife-hand strike.

But it stopped just short, and the iron figure froze in place.

"The human body itself, speaking purely in terms of movement, isn't complicated. Muscles either contract or relax, and moving isn't really that hard with just that, is it? All Krische needs to send to each section is whether to extend or contract the phantom muscle, and the timing and intensity."

"……I see, I can sort of follow that."

Understanding it, however, was another matter entirely.

Given the sheer volume of patterns required, the only person who could manage them was surely the girl standing before him.

Elvena, whose knees had very nearly given out, gripped Krische's shoulder and shook her head at Neigal's questioning look. She, too, understood almost nothing about the patterning.

When the transmitter flickered, the iron figure snapped to attention and performed a salute, making a clattering, clanking noise.

It was the sound produced by the chains that had been stretched out to lock the ball joints in place.

Krische thought for a moment, then clapped her hands.

"Let's call it Jaragasha."

"……Um, Krische-sama……that is……"

"See, the chains go jara-jara, and it walks with a gashan-gashan. It's a very descriptive and wonderful name."

Krische nodded with a proud huff and looked at Elvena.

"Elvena was having trouble figuring out what to call it too, so this is perfect, right?"

"Y-yes……Jaragasha……"

Elvena cast a pitying look at the figure that had been saddled with such a ridiculous name.

Apparently satisfied with that reaction, Krische turned to Neigal.

"This will serve as the prototype for now. Starting next time, Krische won't have them make hands――she'll have the weapons attached directly, so it should be a bit easier."

"……? You won't use hands?"

"It increases the number of parts, and they're too complex――they'd break right away on the battlefield. The weapons will be larger than what ordinary soldiers use, so……durability would also be an issue. Krische had another purpose in mind this time, which is why she had them built."

Neigal and the others tilted their heads, and Krische pointed to Neigal's left wrist, then to the legs of the fabrication squad members who remained seated as they worked.

These were limbs lost on the battlefield.

"It's inconvenient not having hands or feet, so Krische thought she might as well build replacements fitted to your bodies. Since all of you can use mana, if Krische resizes the arms or legs from Jaragasha and makes them out of wood or something, they could serve as substitute limbs, right?"

It might take some getting used to, Krische said as though it were nothing special.

Neigal and the others were struck speechless, exchanging glances with one another.

Krische tilted her head and looked at Elvena.

Elvena, who had been told about this in advance, gave a wry smile and said, "They're surprised, you know."

"……You would go that far for us?"

"What do you mean, that far? You're soldiers that Krische is maintaining out of her own pocket, so pursuing efficiency is only natural. It's a hassle if Krische has to help with everything from hauling supplies to whatever else, and if you have even substitute limbs, you'll be able to work a bit more, won't you? Krische wants to eventually leave all of this sort of tedious work to you."

Otherwise there's no point in employing you, Krische declared with her hands on her hips.

Neigal and the others exchanged glances once more and nodded among themselves.

Though the missing arms were on different sides, they clicked their heels and performed a perfectly synchronized salute.

Some had tears welling in their eyes, clearly overcome with emotion.

"Thank you. ……We shall devote the whole of our lives to Krische-sama's service."

"That's like what Vulture said……Krische will kick you out once you're useless old men, so she doesn't need your whole lives. Just work hard while you still can."

"Yes, of course. ……Even so, that is the spirit with which we shall serve."

The one-armed man supporting the one-legged man smacked the latter's head, muttering, What are you crying about?

But his own eyes were just as wet, and when someone pointed it out, laughter filled the room.

Watching this scene intently, Elvena spoke to Krische.

With a gentle smile, she whispered.

"I, too, am proud to serve under Krische-sama."

"……What a bunch of strange people."

"You are the strangest of all, Krische-sama."

She let out a soft, lovely laugh and gently stroked that small head.

Then came a knock at the door.

"Krische, it's me. May I come in?"

"Ah, Selene!"

Krische pattered over to the door and ushered her inside.

Four people appeared.

Marshal Selene Christand and Marshal's Advisor Elrouga Faren.

And the Queen of the kingdom, Kreschenta Alberan.

Everyone went rigid at the faces that appeared through the door, hurriedly clicking their heels and straightening their posture――

"Right this way, Your Majesty!"

And the shadow that darted out ahead of everyone else.

All present――save Krische and Elvena――rendered their salute to the backside of Anne Gietelens, servant of House Christand, now the kingdom's foremost noble family.

# Chapter 137: Jaleia Gashea

"……So this is the weapon Krische has been building."

The underground laboratory of the royal palace――Selene approached the iron figure with great trepidation.

Its height was close to eight shaku. (T/N: ~240 cm / ~8 feet)

Taller even than the large-framed Granmeld or Kokys, and perhaps because it was made of iron, it exuded an uncanny sense of menace even in its skeletal state.

When Krische made the transmitter flash, the iron figure abruptly dropped to one knee before Selene――

"――!?"

Startled, Selene reflexively leapt straight back.

It had merely been a consideration to give her a better overall view, but in her surprise, Selene's hand had already gone to the sword at her left hip.

Krische looked at her with exasperation at the overreaction.

"……What are you doing?"

"Sh-shut up, I was just a little startled."

Flushing with embarrassment, Selene approached the iron figure again.

Elrouga too examined each section of the iron figure with an expression even more serious than usual, brow deeply furrowed.

The somehow sinister aura it exuded.

The frightened Anne sidled behind Elvena and clutched at her sleeve, and Elvena, wearing a troubled expression, kept glancing at the unreliable senior servant.

Though Elvena knew Elrouga was not a bad person, even for her, Elrouga was intimidating.

The question was more of a pretext to get close to her sister.

Once the principle was understood, the rest was easily imaginable even for Kreschenta.

Countless patterns were stored in the magic crystals, and the command tower――the head crystal――relayed information to each section accordingly. After that, the components communicated with one another, and as long as mana remained, it would repeat a set of actions until ordered otherwise.

She found the concept of transmitting mana through the air impressive, but that was about all.

There was none of the awe that the others showed.

"Pika-Piika."

"……Pardon?"

"*Pika* and *pikaa*――those are how Krische sends the commands, so she gave it a nice descriptive name. Please call it Pika-Piika from now on."

More astonishing than anything was the naming sense.

Between Skeleton, Woof-woof, and Bururun, Kreschenta already knew that Krische's naming sense was catastrophic, but her sister's linguistic creativity was indeed on par with a toddler's.

Kreschenta's cheek twitched.

"I-is that so……"

"And Krische has named this iron figure Jaragasha. Heh heh, a fine name, don't you think?"

The sister announced proudly.

Kreschenta's cheek twitched again, and she looked to Selene, at a loss.

Selene returned a look that clearly said *don't look at me, do something*, and Kreschenta's excellent mind whirred into gear with unusual urgency――searching through her own database.

And then, she clapped her hands.

"Wh-what a splendid name. Jaleia Gashea――yes, a truly wonderful name."

"Iron of victory……blunt, perhaps, but so very valiant a name. As expected of Onee-sama."

Pretending not to hear her sister's correction, Kreschenta glanced at Selene.

She had matched the sounds to an appropriate phrase from the ancient language she knew.

Jaleia meant to vanquish, to be superior, to excel.

Gashea meant that which gleams dully, ore.

These were often translated as *victory* and *iron* respectively, and while not a perfect fit for the iron figure, the name was not bad.

"Jara――Jaleia Gashea, that's an unusually good name, Krische. I'll give you credit for that."

Selene nodded at Kreschenta for devising such a plausible name.

Then she directed a pointed look at the fabrication squad.

"……You all agree, don't you?"

"Y-yes, ma'am! We feel it is a truly magnificent name. Jaleia Gashea……what a valiant name!"

A sudden farce had begun.

Though the soldiers sensed that the wish of the master to whom they had just sworn loyalty was being distorted, they could not fight against this tide. The men all took turns praising Jaleia Gashea as a wonderful name, as if by prior agreement.

Krische tilted her head with a puzzled *hmm?* and opened her mouth.

"Um, it's not Jaleia, it's Jaraga――"

"Onee-sama, more importantly, it seems both of them would like to know about its capabilities."

"Eh……nn, somehow……muu……"

It was blatant. A complete diversion.

Even Krische was developing suspicions, but Selene pressed the advantage.

"That's right, rather than the name, the first thing we need is an explanation of what it's designed to do. We need to think about cost, deployment, production, and all sorts of things, so I'd like that explanation first. Would you?"

Selene approached Krische in a placating manner and patted her head.

"Ehehe……that's true."

And just like that, Krische was diverted with the greatest of ease.

"Jaragasha is a weapon designed for short, decisive engagements, with the purpose of breaking through enemy battle lines."

She draped a cloth over the large wooden board against the wall, then stood on a box and began drawing diagrams with charcoal.

She drew two opposing battle lines――the top as the enemy, the bottom as friendly forces. The battlefield with equal forces on each side was sketched out swiftly using symbols.

"The basic deployment is in front of the battle line. They charge ahead of the infantry assault and specialize in disrupting and breaking through the enemy line. Their appeal is that they won't be injured or killed by arrows, so they can be handled a bit roughly."

They're superior to armored cavalry in that respect, Krische continued.

"As you can see, they're heavy, so they struggle on soft ground, but in exchange they can wield heavy weapons. Like Woof-woof, if you just send them charging in recklessly, they can mow down heavy infantry. Long spears are meaningless against this weight, and even in battles with equal or inferior numbers, if you deploy enough of them, you can break through head-on."

The model for Jaragasha was Granmeld.

Swinging a great iron mace, crushing enemies through their shields as he advanced.

From Krische's perspective, it was a crude, brute-force way of fighting, but against rank-and-file soldiers, that was more than sufficient. If you were simply competing on kill count, Granmeld's approach was overwhelmingly more efficient than Krische's.

*If there were a bunch of Woof-woofs, Krische could take it easy*――that idea was the fundamental direction, the very root of this iron figure.

Of course, Jaragasha could not literally fight like Granmeld, and its actions were patterned to a certain degree.

No amount of detailed instructions could match the adaptability of the real Granmeld in the flesh.

Nevertheless, clad in armor, its total weight would equal five grown men――with strength and mass far exceeding any human and sheer armor thickness, rank-and-file soldiers simply holding their formation would stand no chance.

It was built on the simple premise that shortcomings in technique and adaptability could be compensated for by raw physical capability.

"By filling the heart section with mana, operational time during combat maneuvers is about one and a half koku. Of course, installing additional magic crystals can simply extend the operational time, but given that each one requires about three mana-users to charge, the cost efficiency doesn't work out under current conditions." (T/N: One koku ≈ 2 hours, so one and a half koku ≈ 3 hours.)

"……One and a half koku. That's short."

"It can be made longer, but combat performance would degrade. This is strictly for short, decisive engagements――"

Krische answered Selene while adding to the diagram.

"It's not something you use for skirmishes, but for shattering enemy lines in a single blow. If the initial strike succeeds, its job is done, and if the initial strike succeeds, you've won. So there's no need to worry too much about mopping up the stragglers."

"That kind of concept.……It's similar to the elephants used in the south, perhaps?"

A monstrous creature twice the height of a man, with a long trunk and tusks.

She had never seen one in person, but she had heard such creatures were used in the south.

"Yes. The operational concept is probably similar. Though Krische thinks ours is more versatile, and superior in maintenance, transport, and defensive capability."

Detailed records of elephants existed from the era of Grabareine.

Their imposing appearance terrified soldiers, and their monstrous strength could flatten battle lines.

They possessed hides so tough and thick that half-hearted spear thrusts could never penetrate, and facing them head-on was considered reckless.

However, they were apparently timid creatures by nature, slow to maneuver, and not very intelligent.

Once they started running, they could only go straight, and they would bolt easily.

The established countermeasure was to open a path and let them charge through, then strike at them from the sides with spears and arrows.

The fierce southern general, Dagren Garhka, had apparently fought them several times, and Krische recalled being regaled with his boastful stories at some banquet.

"Hmm……fascinating. But surely it would struggle against mana-users?"

Having finished his examination of the iron figure――Jaragasha――Elrouga posed this question to Krische.

"Against someone on Woof-woof's level, it would struggle, yes. But this is designed to break through battle lines, not to fight mana-users, so that's fine. Its targets are strictly the rank-and-file soldiers that make up those lines."

And besides, Krische continued.

"Right now it's just the frame, but after this, Krische will wrap it in cloth to add bulk, then put plate armor over that. It's extremely resistant to impacts, and the armor can be made thicker than what ordinary soldiers wear, so it's quite tough. It could easily dispatch low-caliber mana-users.……If it were just destroying it, even Selene could probably manage with enough effort, but Krische doesn't think Selene's skill would be enough to cut moving iron."

"H-hey now……"

Feeling as though her skill had been casually insulted, Selene fixed Krische with a half-exasperated glare.

In this world, people who could cleave through plate armor and the enemy inside in a single stroke could be counted on a fraction of one hand.

Excluding superhuman figures like Kokys, Selene was confident she was reasonably strong, and being compared to them and spoken of in such a way felt rather unreasonable.

She was simply stating facts.

"Heh heh, I see. Well, if it truly has that degree of armor, very few could challenge it……there should be no need to account for such opponents."

Krische answered while rubbing the cheek Selene had pulled.

It hurt a little, but she recognized that pinching and pulling cheeks was also a form of Selene's affection.

Even as she puffed up her cheeks in protest, Krische seemed vaguely pleased, and beside her, Kreschenta was puzzled by the inexplicable reaction of a sister who appeared happy to have her cheeks pinched, and worried for her future.

Only a pervert would enjoy being hurt.

"The weak point is the head, then……how predictable."

"Krische needs it somewhat exposed for friend-or-foe identification, so that can't be helped. She's made it tall to make it harder to target, and it's quite fast, so it should be reasonably fine.……Even if Selene could destroy it given enough effort, Krische doesn't think Selene could cut moving iron with her skill."

"Fairly."

Krische held up the transmitter――Pika-Piika.

Behind Krische, Elvena bent over backward, and Anne very nearly collapsed, clinging to Elvena――the two propped each other up, forming a human letter A.

Krische was, as a rule, rather lacking in consideration for others.

"The formations we consider standard today took decades for their usefulness to be recognized, and even something as straightforward as a catapult only became widespread because trial deployments made everyone aware of its capabilities. Military men are conservative, especially because they value experience."

In an era when individual valor was paramount.

The tribe that would become the origin of Alberan was small and weak, more exploited than exploiting――but they possessed wisdom and technology that rivaled no other.

They shot down the enemy's champions with disciplined volleys, lined up shields and thrust spears, built forts and held their ground.

That was hardly an uncommon occurrence.

There would likely be a fair number of people who would react with hostility to this "revolutionary and super-amazing weapon made by Krische," which threatened to steal the arena of martial glory from warriors.

The possibility of problems arising from that was more than foreseeable.

Since Krische utterly lacked this kind of imagination, it fell to Selene and the others to take care, laying careful groundwork to prevent ugly outcomes.

"That's what Father struggled with when establishing the staff officer system. No matter how inefficient things are, nobody wants their position or power diminished. This is certainly a remarkable creation, but there will inevitably be complaints from those who have staked their lives and built their names through valor.……'Is it really appropriate to let puppets like this lead the charge in a battlefield of heroes?'――people will say things like that."

"Nn……well……"

Given the distance between the kingdom and the empire, there was inevitably a lag in response time.

He had already been considering pre-positioning forces within the kingdom under the pretext of exercises as a deterrent against other nations.

The primary defense concern was the loss of the Christand army in the north.

The northern forces had been the linchpin of defense, capable of striking at the flanks of invasions from both the west (Elderant) and east (Elsren).

With troops lost in the civil war, the vacuum in the kingdom's north concerned the empire as well, and they had been considering countermeasures――but then, Zarnalibea furrowed his brow.

Why had the critical north been left vacant?

Reconsidering, Queen Kreschenta's intentions became clearer.

Even if the person was less than ideal, the kingdom needed to install *someone* as northern general, or the army would be unable to act in an emergency.

Yet this queen, Zarnalibea thought, probably did not even consider it shameful.

She simply pursued her own interests to the utmost――that was the kind of creature she was.

She had them by the throat: if the kingdom sinks, the empire sinks with it.

"Fufu, I'm joking. Of course the kingdom will not forget this debt. We fully intend to meet you halfway on the tariff reductions you've been requesting."

Not a bad trade, Zarnalibea thought, sipping his sweet tea and letting out a breath.

The Arna Empire produced large quantities of mineral ores including magic crystals, and invested heavily in their processing and craftsmanship. These processed mineral goods constituted the empire's largest export. However, because the kingdom's northern territories also had significant mines, there was competition, and exports to the kingdom were problematic.

The empire's goods were of higher quality, its craftsmen more skilled, and their prices actually lower――but high tariffs had been imposed as a matter of course, to protect the kingdom's domestic mining and processing industries.

Sea routes were used for some exports, but the risk of shipwrecks kept profits lower than expected, and with the domestic market saturated and prices falling, the kingdom's tariff reduction would be enormously significant.

The empire had been requesting it for some time, but the kingdom had always balked.

An expeditionary deployment was a temporary burden, but the long-term benefit from tariff reduction more than justified the trade.

"Just a bit of mischief."

Kreschenta watched Zarnalibea's relief with evident amusement.

With Jaleia Gashea and other research centered on Krische poised to advance, the value of magic crystals would skyrocket starting from the kingdom.

Under the guise of tariff reduction, she intended to buy up large quantities of the empire's cheap, high-quality magic crystals while she could――but she would not say this aloud.

This is a good opportunity; I'd like to reform various things in the kingdom, but as things stand, I simply can't manage it all. And there are only so many people I can trust in the current central government……"

Kreschenta spoke with a look of troubled supplication.

The tariff reduction served as an excellent justification for the military deployment, since it would seem too sudden otherwise.

In effect, the empire would deploy troops to the kingdom for free――with a bonus, even――and the savings on military spending alone made the kingdom's gains enormous.

Behind her troubled face, Kreschenta was beaming inwardly.

"Well, it cannot be helped. We understand the kingdom's circumstances well enough."

"Thank you for saying that. Entrusting the north to the empire feels much more reassuring than leaving it with someone I may not be able to trust. It is absolutely not a case of convenient exploitation, I assure you?"

Zarnalibea could only smile wryly.

In truth, it was absolutely convenient exploitation.

But Zarnalibea understood the kingdom's situation to some degree.

The most frightening consequence of civil war was the internal discord it bred. That sowed further seeds of conflict, and misjudging the person to install as northern general could spawn a conflagration that consumed the kingdom.

The desire to move cautiously under current conditions was reasonable and prudent.

Completely unaware that he was not merely being exploited but devoured, Zarnalibea viewed her situation through the lens of common sense, decided it was unavoidable, and accepted.

"Tariff discussions can wait for another day. For now, let us discuss national defense. Onee-sama."

Kreschenta turned to the cookie-cracking puppet――her sister Krische.

Krische looked up from her cookies with a puzzled tilt of the head.

Kreschenta puffed her cheeks in an exasperated *mou*.

"This is about the military buildup in the north. Zarnalibea-sama is well-versed in such matters, so please explain it to him, Onee-sama."

"……Krische still thinks Selene would have been better for this."

Krische said with mild displeasure and turned her eyes to Zarnalibea.

This sort of discussion was better suited to Selene. Krische's presence here was half due to Kreschenta's selfishness.

"Err, is it alright to call you Zarnalibea-sama?"

"Of course, Alberinea. Please, go ahead. First, I'd like to hear how many troops you're envisioning."

A quiet, sweet-toned voice.

Unlike the crisp quality of Kreschenta's voice, it lacked all force.

What spilled from Krische's lips was truly the voice of a girl matching her appearance.

A matter of temperament, perhaps. The two seemed alike as sisters and yet diametrically opposed.

She appeared to lack everything necessary for commanding an army, and Zarnalibea felt a tinge of unease, but――

"Krische would like around 1,000 or 2,000. However, she would like you to select personnel from centurion rank and above who are capable of commanding a full army, enough for a two-front deployment."

What followed was even more baffling, and even Zarnalibea could not keep the confusion from his face.

"……1,000 or 2,000?"

"Yes. Krische can arrange the soldiers, so that number of personnel from the empire will suffice. The north lacks commanders, not soldiers."

"Having kingdom soldiers serve under foreign officers seems……fraught with problems. And with so few, it would hardly serve as a deterrent, would it?"

As Zarnalibea's frown deepened, wondering what she was suggesting, Krische nodded.

Kreschenta, listening beside them, sipped her lukewarm tea and kept glancing at her sister.

"That's fine. If anything, it's more convenient to be attacked than to deter them. Also, deploying a large force would create maintenance cost issues, making long-term stationing impossible. From the enemy's perspective, they'd only need to wait. If Krische were the enemy, she would launch the attack after the empire either withdrew or the financial burden started crippling the kingdom."

"……True, that may be so."

In peacetime, the kingdom's army was reduced to a fraction――sometimes as little as a tenth or even a hundredth――of its wartime strength.

Permanent employment generally only covered officers of centurion rank and above; the funds allocated from the central government to each region for military maintenance were barely enough to support those at captain rank and above.

This varied greatly by the region's importance and threat level, but regardless, maintaining an army of 20,000 placed an enormous burden on finances, and the kingdom simply lacked the capacity to permanently garrison that many in a single region.

If the imperial forces were brought in en masse, the full cost of their provisions and upkeep――excepting wages――would fall on the kingdom, and the fact that they were imperial soldiers created a major problem.

Since they were on an expeditionary deployment from the empire, the army could not be scaled down locally no matter how great the financial burden.

A temporary reinforcement was one thing, but over the long term, the fiscal impact would become impossible to ignore, and if the enemy chose to wait, an untenable situation would arise.

Pull out the army, and you're attacked.

Leave them in, and your finances collapse.

That kind of trap had to be avoided.

"So, personnel should be kept to a minimum. Under the guise of military exchange, we use kingdom soldiers for training, and the imperial officers and NCOs use that time to iron out any coordination issues. There's no need to focus too much on actual combat readiness. It's a show army."

"……A show."

"Yes. Whether the enemy comes from east or west, invading the north won't happen. The mere fact that the empire has troops deployed in the kingdom limits the enemy's options."

"I see……if anyone targets the north, they'd have to fight the empire itself."

"Exactly."

If an enemy targeted the north, even if they defeated the small expeditionary force deployed there, behind it stood the empire proper.

No one would be foolish enough to pick that fight.

And so the imperial expeditionary force could be weak; it would not matter. That was the logic.

The unease and confusion that had been on Zarnalibea's face were gone.

This girl had already mapped out the future war in her head in more than sufficient detail.

"What matters is the fact that the empire is leading troops there. As long as that condition is met, it's enough. Besides, if the enemy is cowed by a large imperial force, the tensions would drag on, and that would be exhausting.……If they're going to come, it's easier if they come sooner."

"Would buying time not be more advantageous for the kingdom?"

"The maintenance costs for the imperial army would be awful, and they'll invade within a few years anyway, so the result doesn't change."

They won't let a golden opportunity to attack the kingdom slip by, Krische continued.

Whatever happened, the enemy would invade before the kingdom fully stabilized. That was certain.

Delaying it slightly was pointless――a decisive judgment.

"……You seem quite confident. The complex situation of a kingdom targeted by three nations……if I were in your position, the stress would destroy my stomach, and I wouldn't be able to eat."

And she could also be called quite the confident one.

Conservatively speaking, this was a precarious situation with three nations potentially attacking.

Even the empire was tense about the kingdom's future.

And yet the silver-haired girl tilted her head quizzically, plucked a cookie, swallowed it, and――

"Nn, if all three attack, Krische just has to crush them one by one. Then there will be peace……there's nothing complicated about it, is there?"

*If anything, it seems very simple*, she answered with the face of a lovely girl.

She did not even entertain the possibility of losing.

She harbored not a single shred of doubt that she would prevail.

――Such words could only come from someone who felt it was obvious, from the very bottom of her heart.

"……I see. Now I understand why Her Majesty places such trust in you."

Precisely because those words were so utterly natural, she seemed utterly mad.

# Chapter 140: Highbrow, Lowbrow

"Ehehe, does it look cute?"

"Yes, it suits you perfectly."

Fluttering the skirt of her dress.

Krische, the dress-up doll, twirled happily to show it off.

Bery imagined the complaints Selene would later call *underhanded*, and smiled softly at Krische's lovely figure.

The opening skirmishes of the dress war ahead of the conference had already begun.

*'Um, Krische, Krische would like to pick out a dress for the conference ahead of time……'*

No breakfast, no pre-breakfast――a situation where one could only watch the end of the battle between the sister and the servant.

To prevent such a tragedy from occurring again, Krische had said this to Bery that very morning.

This was, of course, a most welcome proposal for Bery as well.

If she could secure Krische's agreement on everything from the dress to the accessories in advance, she would hold an overwhelming advantage in any future conflict.

That Krische herself had brought it up was critically important.

Even if she were denounced as underhanded and despicable, the justification that *Krische-sama herself wished it* existed as an unassailable defense.

Surely even Selene could not mount an offensive from that angle.

Against the busy Selene, who could not conduct advance operations, this was――from any perspective――a ruthless, underhanded, and despicable act. But in the servant's mind, the theoretical armor was already complete.

Bery fundamentally never bent her wishes for anyone.

Gentle and soft-spoken, she presented herself as a wise and understanding woman, but at her core, Bery Argan was extraordinarily stubborn and self-centered――steeped in the sensibility of a noble.

Her clarity in discarding everything for the sake of her own desires was almost refreshing; she would have been better suited as a soldier or politician than a cook.

"How unusual for you to suggest this, Krische-sama. I'm very happy to have you take an interest in your own dresses and outfits……"

"Ehehe, Krische also feels like she's recently come to understand what 'pretty' means."

Hands on hips, modest chest puffed out.

In a pale blue dress, Krische was adorable beyond measure.

Bery let out a soft *my* in surprise and gently combed through the silver hair.

"It's a little hard to put into words, though……"

Krische drew close and nuzzled up, gazing up at Bery.

Petite even compared to the already-small Bery, every natural little gesture of Krische's was captivating and charming.

Sparkling violet eyes.

Various books said that looking up from below was attractive to men, and indeed, Bery could appreciate the truth of it.

Watching Krische in her daily life made it easy to understand.

If a lovely girl like Krische were to sidle up and gaze upward while making a request, surely anyone would be willing to risk a life or two――because there was nothing behind it, Krische's innocence was magical.

"That may be so. Trying to put the feeling of 'pretty' into words is very difficult. Just……*pretty*, is all……"

A poet might manage it, Bery said with a troubled expression, and Krische said, *Even Bery finds it hard*, and cupped Bery's cheeks in both hands.

She rose on tiptoe, kissed her happily, then swished the skirt of her dress.

Understating it greatly, Krische was hopelessly adorable.

At the very least, Bery could describe this creature only with words like *pretty* or *cute*.

It was indeed difficult to put into language, Bery could only say, cheeks faintly flushed.

"But Krische thinks it's probably close to a feeling like happiness, so if Bery thinks Krische is pretty, then Krische is happy too……so, Krische will keep Bery company as much as she likes."

Krische's sense of *pretty* was, for Bery, something heavily colored by emotion, but what mattered to Bery was not what exactly Krische's *pretty* was directed at, but rather the joy that this was a good thing.

Krische's growth was the most important thing to Bery.

Whatever the case, the fact that Krische had developed interest in her outfits beyond mere stubbornness (absolutely skirts) was a good thing, and Bery did not probe too deeply.

The difficulty of putting it into words was also a reason.

"Ehehe, Bery is very pretty too."

"Fufu, thank you. But Krische-sama is much prettier."

Self-centered by nature, blessed with many talents, yet fundamentally low in self-esteem――Bery could not connect the fact that Krische had recently been staring at her face as though trying to bore a hole through it with any of these feelings. Bery was essentially oblivious to others' affection directed at her.

Part of the reason was likely that Krische's affection and physical closeness had always been, by any standard, excessive.

And Krische, for her part, saw no need to have Bery explain these feelings in detail, and was more than satisfied with the current state of things.

Bery called her pretty, and Krische thought Bery was pretty.

In Krische's mind, their feelings were mutual――and the Bery Scripture's teaching that truth is a trivial thing was firmly embedded within her.

Measured against that, there was no need to reconfirm each other's words.

From the start, theirs was a relationship that outsiders might question, so excessively physical had it been, and all that had happened was a kind of equilibrium being reached.

Krische was more than fulfilled.

"Oh, that's right."

As if remembering something, Krische headed for the shelf.

A magic crystal――encased in metalwork. A somewhat thick cord was attached to the metal fitting; presumably a necklace.

It had a certain ruggedness, but the blue glow of the magic crystal was beautiful.

"Kreschenta was saying something about poison, so Krische made this for Bery."

"Poison……"

"Yes. Krische gathered the common poisons available around here and made a poison-piririn that reacts to them. Hold it close and channel mana and it'll tingle if there's poison. Very easy to use."

"I see……"

Bery took it and examined it.

The complex formula inscribed on the magic crystal shifted and changed over time.

What Krische called *squiggly*――borrowed from the ciphers used in grimoires, Elvena had named it "cipher inscription."

It absorbed ambient mana, and that mana caused the surface layer of the formula to rewrite itself over time, protecting the core formula within.

When a mana-user like Bery touched it, it activated further, and the geometric patterns within the crystal shifted and undulated.

It was a mechanism entirely beyond Bery's comprehension.

Apparently, disabling it required drawing a formula in midair using magic――and even knowing the mechanism, attempting to crack it blindly would take who knew how many years.

Naturally, she could not read how this crystal functioned.

"How did you identify the poisons?"

"It was a bit of work for Krische too. When you analyze things down far enough, they become tiny particles, and those combine in various ways to make different substances. Lots of them stick together and form all sorts of things……"

Krische pointed at the ceiling and walls.

"See, it's like how wood and stone and such come together to make a house."

"Particles……"

"Particles. Our bodies are made of lots of particles too."

Krische rested her chin on her hand, thinking about how to explain.

"The heart, the blood, everything that makes the body move――it's all made of these particles, and poison is the same. When poison enters the body, its particles interfere with various particles in the body, and that causes illness or death……but there are so many kinds of poison, and figuring out which affects what, and how, is so enormous that Krische doesn't really understand it all."

*It's quite the problem*, she said, raising a finger.

Mana flickered and swayed.

"If Krische could figure it out, she might be able to make a universal healing crystal, but it would require thousands or tens of thousands of experiments, so she gave up on that and tried approaching it from a different angle."

"……Mana?"

"Yes. Mana can interfere with particles, breaking them down and recombining them. So Krische had this crystal memorize specific particle configurations and identify them. Like how you can tell it's a pumpkin just by touching it with your eyes closed."

The analogy was both clear and not, but the gist was understandable.

Despite the casual tone, the content was profoundly advanced――touching on the very workings of the world.

Something invisible to the eye that constituted everything in the world.

A fascinating idea――no, since she was saying it, it was likely fact.

Bery recalled something she had read in a book once.

"……I believe I read a hypothesis in a book once that the world is composed of mana."

The phenomena that arose when using physical enhancement or magic crystals.

Things being heated or moved without physical contact――matter was born from mana, and therefore mana could interact with all things.

Such a hypothesis had been recorded in an old book.

"Nn, Krische thinks that might be accurate. Mana can become small particles, and small particles can become mana. It's a question of whether mana came first or matter came first."

"The egg or the chicken……"

Bery laughed with quiet amusement.

She felt as though, in her youth, she had once dreamed of pursuing that kind of research.

"This is rather fun."

"Is it fun?"

"Yes. Learning things you don't know is very enjoyable."

Bery smiled, and Krische smiled back.

"Ehehe, Krische quite likes learning new things too. We have something in common!"

"Fufu, we're the same."

She stroked Krische, who threw her arms around her, and gazed at the magic crystal in her hand.

The shifting geometric patterns were beautiful, vivid.

The blue glow of the magic crystal was lovely, drawing one in.

Bery tied the cord of the necklace behind her neck and let it hang.

Though Bery was not one for ornaments, this was something special.

"……I will wear it always, and treasure it. Thank you."

"Ehehe, it looks wonderful on you, Bery."

Even a simple compliment carried weight when it came from Krische.

Bery kissed her forehead gently.

Looking ticklish, Krische then pouted slightly.

"But it came out a bit big. It might get in the way during work. Shall Krische remake it smaller?"

"It's fine. It wouldn't do to show it off, so I'll tuck it inside normally."

So saying, Bery slipped the necklace beneath her collar. It settled into the valley of her ample chest.

Krische stared at this fixedly, then said with a serious face:

"……Having large breasts seems convenient――like having pockets."

Bery flushed and smiled wryly.

Krische happily nuzzled her cheek against Bery's chest, declaring that she loved how soft Bery's chest was.

Then, looking embarrassed, Krische raised her face.

"A-about what Krische was saying earlier……"

Using our heads and arguing over such nonsense is something I'm sure Ojou-sama enjoys, and so do I. So there is nothing for Krische-sama to worry about."

Assuming Krische was worried that the bickering between the sister and the servant looked like a real fight, Bery spoke to put her at ease.

What Krische was actually worried about was the breakfasts that were lost as a result.

But she could hardly say outright that she wanted to do something about starving through breakfast――Krische's sense of propriety would not permit such a shameless admission without an ounce of wrapping.

Neither Ojou-sama nor I harbor any ill will toward the other. It really is just playful bickering――so please don't worry. We're not truly fighting, you know?"

Even Krische did not actually think the two were fighting in earnest, but she understood that acting as if she did was the proper social response.

This time, surely, she would not spend the morning of the conference with an empty stomach.

Selene would quietly back down, and while Krische felt a bit sorry for her, her own breakfast took priority.

She can never look like a formidable general no matter what, so a dress that raises our soldiers' morale is much more fitting."

"Eh? Uu……"

But a future beyond all expectation shattered Krische's relief with ease.
