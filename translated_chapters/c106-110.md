# Chapter 106: Obsession with Victory

Fifteen years ago――the eastern front.

A fierce battle was being waged there.

The objective was the invasion of the western territories of the Holy Elsren Empire, and the commanding general was Gildanstein.

Against the kingdom's rapid, unstoppable advance, the Holy Empire executed a scorched-earth strategy.

Having completely abandoned the west to conserve their strength, they launched a unified counteroffensive.

When the kingdom's Northern General, who had been participating in this campaign, was killed in action, the kingdom's forces were pushed back at once, and the eastern invasion army found itself in dire straits.

The Holy Empire had broken through to the north, striking them in the flank and rear.

――It was there that Terrius Melkikos found himself on the verge of death.

*'――Why did you save me, Christand? If you had simply pressed on, you could have withdrawn your troops without issue.'*

Southeast from the Great Forest that stretched across the kingdom's northeast.

The corps commander who had been defending a fortress built along the mountain range there――Terrius had been left behind during the allied retreat.

The messenger ordering the withdrawal had arrived late, and then been intercepted by the enemy.

*'Besides, you were supposed to be under the command of the central force――His Highness the Prince. Why are you in a backwater like this?'*

*'To rescue you, of course, Corps Commander Melkikos.'*

Bogan Christand.

He was an upstart, born of impoverished nobility.

As though reluctant to even replace his armor with new sets, his was covered in scratches――only the leather straps and metal fittings holding it together were new.

Without any engravings or ornamentation, one could charitably call it sturdy and practical armor, but it was simply worn-out. The gauntlets showed slight mismatches in size; it clearly was not custom-made but rather secondhand equipment that had been modified.

Despite holding the rank of corps commander, he showed not the slightest shame in displaying such a threadbare appearance――it was thoroughly typical of an upstart.

Terrius had disliked him from the very first meeting.

The first time he laid eyes on him was when Terrius himself was still a battalion commander――he had seen Bogan's name called as a centurion being recognized for meritorious service.

By the time Terrius became a corps commander's adjutant, Bogan had apparently risen to battalion commander under Elrouga Faren's command, and Terrius saw him being lavishly praised by the general of the time for his battlefield results.

Every time Terrius caught sight of him, it was always Bogan being lauded.

The polar opposite of Terrius, who accumulated steady results――Bogan achieved spectacular feats.

But such men inevitably disrupted the order of things.

Each of those feats had been accomplished through independent judgment, bordering on disobedience of orders.

One could only say it was justified because the results followed; otherwise, such reckless initiative would have been grounds for execution――acting on one's own endangered everyone else.

He would surely vanish from sight before long.

So Terrius had thought, but when he saw the man again in this campaign, Bogan had risen to corps commander, equal in rank to Terrius himself.

*'I don't like it. Your duty is to guard His Highness the Prince.'*

*'That very prince seems to be aiming for a reversal from here, it would appear.'*

*'……Has the recent success gone to his head?'*

In the recent retreat, Gildanstein himself had taken the head of the enemy pursuit force commander.

Together with this man.

*'He is not that sort of person. At the very least, as a general he is clear-headed, decisive, and possesses the strength to lead from the front――a general worthy of respect. Whatever the rumors may say, as a soldier he is the kind who doggedly, even if it means getting dirty, pursues victory.'*

*'He is merely reckless. Drunk on blood and glory. ……Well, fine. How do you intend to escape this predicament? The enemy force facing us alone numbers fifteen thousand. And their cavalry outnumber ours. Even if we flee now, surviving would be a gamble.'*

Terrius crossed his arms and thought for a moment, then spoke.

Personally, Bogan was not a man he found agreeable, but the fact remained that he had come to rescue Terrius when he had already been abandoned――that debt had to be repaid.

*'I will repay the debt. I will take the rear guard. ……Salva, I'm sorry, but you may have to die with me. Is that acceptable?'*

*'Yes, sir. I shall accompany you.'*

Salva smiled and saluted.

It was the good face of a man who had made his peace.

*'Christand. If any of my soldiers survive, I leave them in your care.'*

*'Haha, what are you misunderstanding? Our objective is not behind us.'*

Bogan said with a laugh.

*'Using your corps and mine――both of them, we lure the enemy into the mountains, destroy them, break through here, and take the head of the enemy commander arrayed before His Highness. ……As I said, the aim is a reversal from this very position. And I have come to ask for your cooperation in achieving it.'*

*'……Are you sane?'*

*'Perfectly. My men are all strong. You, too, and your soldiers――even in this situation, morale hasn't broken. Every one of them is resolved to die――they are fine soldiers with fine commanders. If we all become soldiers of the dead, there is nothing that cannot be done.'*

Bogan grinned like a beast.

*'The enemy is already convinced of victory; their hearts have left the battlefield. They are thinking about postwar rewards, their minds elsewhere――cowardice spreads. That is precisely why we must strike now, at this very moment when we are at our most desperate.'*

*'……You really are mad.'*

*'It is a battlefield, after all. You cannot endure it without a touch of madness. ……What do you think? If one must go mad regardless, I believe it is more enjoyable to go mad with blood and victory than with fear.'*

He had disliked this man for a long time.

But facing him like this, the allure radiated a fierce heat that burned in Terrius's chest.

*'Very well. In this situation, I might as well take your gamble. ……But afterward, as a fellow corps commander, I will have a lecture for you. Don't you dare run.'*

*'Haha, I would prefer to hear that lecture while savoring the wine of victory――'*

――As though savoring the memory, Terrius recalled those days.

On the battlefield, blood and steel danced through the air, and the atmosphere was filled with nothing but screams and the hard ring of metal.

The five thousand that had originally been arrayed before them.

In addition, five thousand reinforcements under Nakilus Ferizar, and the enemy main body――five thousand under former adjutant Salva.

The enemy was clearly moving to break through Terrius and decide the battle.

Of the four thousand entrusted to him by Selene, three thousand were deployed to block flanking maneuvers, and one thousand held in reserve against a breakthrough.

He felt no fear of the enemy.

He was grateful that no anger dulled his thinking, that he could remain calm in this place.

*'――I would rather bet on that child winning than on winning here in a head-on fight myself. Until then, protect me. ……I'm sorry to keep relying on you.'*

It was Selene who had given him that composure.

She, who must have wanted to avenge Bogan more than anyone, had set aside personal feelings and looked only at victory.

I am still immature――words that could, in a sense, be taken as weakness.

Saying that in front of everyone must have required considerable courage.

Looking back, Kokys, Terrius, Elrouga――any of them could have succeeded Bogan and taken command of the Christand army.

Yet the reason they had unanimously acknowledged the still-young Selene as their commander and supported her was that everyone recognized her character.

Never arrogant, never complacent, a relentless worker.

Despite her inexperience, she filled the gaps through effort alone, threw herself into military duties with vigor, and showed everyone that she had recovered quickly from her father's death.

A girl of fifteen was showing all of this to them.

If so, then they could not allow themselves to be swept away by personal grudges and sentiment.

She had served as their symbol, their check――the brake that kept Kokys and Terrius and many others from running wild.

And yet, despite understanding this, Terrius had nearly lost his composure after coming face-to-face with Salva again.

Her words had also been a rebuke to that very Terrius.

Recalling it, Terrius could not help but smile wryly.

She was just a child.

And she had admonished him, a grown man.

"At my age, how pathetic."

"……Corps Commander?"

What Selene possessed was only the military knowledge a fifteen-year-old girl could memorize.

As a general, she was still far from sufficient――barely passable as a corps commander.

Even so, she was impressively accomplished for a fifteen-year-old, but she could not begin to compare with Krische, who could effortlessly slay renowned generals and force her way through everything with overwhelming power. Compared to the Christand corps commanders, her lack of experience and unfamiliarity with the battlefield held her back, leaving her significantly lacking in combat capability.

Yet she was more suited to be a general than anyone else here.

She could clearly acknowledge her own shortcomings, and on top of that, without being arrogant about her rank, and without being timid either, she could boldly rely on those around her and make use of them.

That was a kind of talent in itself.

It was something the young could not normally do.

And for that matter, even seasoned soldiers who could do so were rare.

Everyone carried pride in their own abilities, and the more talented one was, the stronger that tendency became.

The undeniable genius――Krische, who at times even appeared arrogant, was the very epitome of this.

Yet Selene, while allowing her own talents to bloom, took the exact opposite path of her sister as a commander.

Overflowing fame and glory.

Even after being called a hero――even possessing strength superior to anyone else, she never grew arrogant and continued to discipline herself, just like Bogan.

She was truly Bogan's successor.

"Ferizar appears to be targeting the seam on our right wing――between us and Corps Commander Faren."

"Yes, sir, it appears so."

"Tell Corps Commander Faren that I am leaving that to him."

"……Is that wise? The burden on the center will――"

"Corps Commander Faren is skilled at handling that type. Even in this situation, he is more than――"

As he started to speak, a red-flag messenger came running toward them.

Saluting from horseback, the messenger reported.

"Corps Commander Melkikos, a message from Corps Commander Faren. 'Leave the boar to me and focus on your front.'"

"Heh heh……There you have it. Any objections, Neltas?"

"No, sir……none. Flag-bearer, signal acknowledgment to the center."

Laughing at the exasperated expression of his new adjutant, Terrius turned his gaze forward.

With this, he faced only ten thousand to the front――he would have to thank Elrouga later, he thought, and moistened his throat with water before thrusting out his sword.

"Left wing, prepare to charge! We are breaking the formation!"

"That old man. Making things troublesome."

The Christand central force maintained its echelon formation while creating a bend in the battle line.

Archers were positioned at the convex point, and what rained upon Salva's advancing force――the Gildanstein army on the right wing――was a storm of arrows from the flank.

While arrows from the front could be defended against to some degree, when flanking arrow fire was added, casualties escalated at an exponential rate.

"……Your Highness, what shall we do?"

"We charge straight through. Break through Terrius head-on. There is no other path to victory, and you and I have given everything for that purpose. Am I wrong?"

"No."

"If we die, that's the end of it. No need to agonize. Go at ease."

――Madness.

Salva looked at Gildanstein laughing in this situation and felt exactly that.

Make this charge, quite literally, the first and the last.

That was what Gildanstein had declared.

Throwing away every safety measure――breakthrough or death. That was the nature of this charge.

The Third Corps' wall was thick. Salva knew that better than anyone.

To Salva, Gildanstein's course of action seemed less bold than reckless, and yet this man declared it possible.

Ah, Salva recalled.

If there was something Salva lacked, it was the madness to throw everything away for the sake of victory.

Sacrificing thousands, tens of thousands of soldiers for victory, and if that was not enough, offering even his own life as a chip in the gamble.

And having made that decision, they smiled as if it were nothing.

Bogan. Nozan. Kokys. Terrius. Elrouga.

Everyone in the Christand army had been like that.

He had dreamed of advancement while resigned to remaining an adjutant, because he was aware of what he lacked.

He was not incompetent――he was skilled, even――but there existed those he could never match.

He had always directed genuine respect toward them.

"Every hero and every scoundrel, down to their grudges and sentiments――when you die, that's the end of it. In a way, don't you think there's even a certain refreshing quality to death?"

"Most people wouldn't think so, but……lately, I find myself not entirely disagreeing."

Salva answered while deflecting the rain of arrows.

Even if they won, the stain on his name would follow him for life.

Was he right, or was he not――he would agonize over that until the day he died.

That was all that lay in any future from here.

In a sense, death was salvation, and he could not bring himself to deny it.

"This world is like a cesspit. The moment you are born, even a jewel sinks into filth. Everyone is smeared with ugly muck, comparing who is dirtier. Watching it is almost amusing――before long, you start to enjoy the mud-slinging. ……Though I admit, when it finally ends, that has a certain satisfaction of its own."

Before he went mad, Gildanstein had reportedly possessed, even as a young man, everything demanded of a royal.

His elder brother, Crown Prince Sherbaza, was by no means incompetent and held sufficient qualities for the succession, but he lacked the brilliance that Gildanstein possessed.

――Not the Crown Prince Sherbaza, but Gildanstein should be king.

It was secretly said that the uproar within the palace over this matter was what drove Gildanstein to begin his deranged amusements.

What had happened inside the palace――Salva, who was young and a soldier at the time, did not know much about.

For most nobles, the palace and the royal family were things far above the clouds.

He had never had a proper conversation with Gildanstein until the betrayal was proposed to him――yet even so, Gildanstein did not seem as mad as the rumors suggested.

There was no question that he was an incorrigible villain, and not a man one could respect.

But whether everything about him was evil――that, Salva thought, was not quite right.

"……I do understand what you are saying. That said, even in a cesspit, a jewel is still a jewel. At the very least, the reason I stand here as a soldier now is because there is a part of me that believes so."

Gildanstein gave Salva a quizzical look.

Having discarded the custom-made royal armor, Gildanstein stood wearing a plain set of armor befitting Salva's adjutant, carrying himself with the same ease.

One could call it cowardly.

One could call it base.

It was a figure devoid of royal pride, coated in deceit――and yet what this man pursued was victory.

That purity held something that appealed to the soldier in Salva.

"Surely the same is true for you, Your Highness?"

"……Who knows."

"Well, it matters not either way. ……At the very least, here and now, I have resolved to be your shield and sword, and to share your fate."

Gildanstein twisted the corner of his mouth in amusement.

"Heh heh, I see. You are a finer weapon than I expected. ……If you're not broken after the battle, I'll put you to long use."

"……There may come a day when I turn this blade on its master, but if that suits you."

"The enchanted swords of legend are said to be exactly that. I used to admire them as a boy, but……"

Well, no matter, he laughed, and looked ahead.

"The enemy just moved their far left wing. What do you make of it?"

"They intend to catch and surround us. In this case, it is safe to assume they have placed their veterans at the far left. Our far right will not be able to hold."

"In other words――"

"――We continue straight ahead at full force and break through the thick wall before us. That is the simplest and the best course of action. ……If Your Highness cannot break through, we simply die."

"Hah, bold words. ――Forward, Salva. In this rain of arrows, pray only that your men can follow you."

And so――at the moment of contact, Gildanstein surged ahead.

Before him was a wall of pike infantry.

The countless pikes were the symbol of inescapable death for the soldiers leading the charge.

More than half of those in the front rank would surely be killed.

The massed pike formation always demanded an immense toll of blood from the enemy before it.

The only thing that could face pikes head-on was more pikes.

Yet Gildanstein had placed shield-bearing swordsmen in his front rank.

Massed pike tactics depended on discipline.

Only because of seamless coordination were pikes such a powerful arm.

If a motley force of hasty recruits clashed head-on, they would be overwhelmed, and a pike-against-pike struggle would consume a great deal of time.

That had to be avoided at all costs.

Time――he had to settle everything before Kokys finished devouring the bait of his own son.

Second Corps Commander Kokys was a formidable warrior.

In a head-on engagement, the outcome would be uncertain even at full strength.

And the moment such a battle began, it would mean Gildanstein's defeat.

The Third Corps before him was an unquestionable hard target, and Gildanstein judged it impossible to break through without pouring in every resource he had.

As a result, this attack was an all-or-nothing charge that committed even his headquarters reserve.

The collapse of his center and left wing at the hands of Elrouga and Kokys was already factored in――this action abandoned all of them.

If he failed to seize Selene, he too would be unable to recover.

Therefore: time.

Gildanstein had only to give everything to achieve his objective.

――An objective named victory.

He overtook the soldiers advancing in the front rank and burst to the head of the charge.

As if echoing the one-man charge Krische had once shown, he stood before ten thousand enemies, and ran.

"Out of my way, rabble……!!"

As though making a display for his own troops behind him.

At the instant before colliding with the enemy, he raised his great war-axe with all his might.

A steel-cored heavy axe――he braced as though wringing every fiber of his being, swung through, and released.

The weapon left both hands and flew with a thunderous roar, dragging the wind in its wake.

"Hiii, wha――!?"

The gale raised by the steel shattered and snapped the pikes held ready, tearing a hole in the enemy line.

The great war-axe expended every ounce of the energy given to it in pure destruction.

And Gildanstein drew his sword and immediately charged into the gap.

He raised the great sword high toward the heavens――the stance of a sword thrust toward the sky.

It was the very image of the kingdom's coat of arms.

His body traced a spiral, and he swept the blade in a horizontal arc.

Tanned leather armor was no better than bare skin before Gildanstein's mighty sword.

He channeled the full force of his body into each transmission――the blade he wielded was the fastest. The King's Sword.

Using technique to target an enemy's openings, striking at weaknesses――such things were not the essence of the sword.

Overpowering――crushing.

That was Gildanstein.

――It was the blade wielded by a king.

He swung to widen the breach, to disrupt the pike wall, and Gildanstein's blade cut through it all.

War cries mingled with cheers thundered through the air, crushing everything in that place.

"Advance!! Your victory lies only ahead!!"

Shouting at the top of his lungs, Gildanstein laughed.

He sheathed his blood-soaked sword and snatched up a fallen war-axe.

The work was the same.

Once the pikes were broken through, swordsmen were deployed behind them.

Short swords and medium shields, soldiers specialized for melee.

But they were no match for Gildanstein――for the King.

He made those who stood before him understand. He crushed them.

He made them recognize that they could not defeat this man, and seized their fear. Subjugated it.

What mattered was becoming the ruler of the battlefield.

Picking up one element from among emotions and reason, and asserting dominance through law, through words, through violence.

A 'King' was not something shaped merely by authority.

It was something always forged by power, and 'King' was a word that designated one who held such power――one who dominated everything around them.

Kreschenta, who had killed his respected brother――King Sherbaza.

Yet in his heart of hearts, he did not resent her.

The brother who was killed had been too good-natured, too careless.

It simply meant he had been too weak to stand as king.

Being killed was the responsibility of the one killed, and nothing more.

And Kreschenta had acted, had moved to make herself queen.

She had found a piece called Krische, been certain of victory, and challenged Gildanstein.

The equation was simple, clear.

What existed here was precisely that kind of contest.

Who was more fit to be sovereign.

This was a wager between two royals, staking their very existence.

There was no deception, no cowardice anywhere in it.

When challenged, one pursued only victory and acted.

Power, sentiment, will, ability――the total strength of all these produced the result and created the 'King.'

Kreschenta had used Bogan and the Christand army.

His pawn Hilkintos had been easily defeated, leaving him at a disadvantage.

In ability, she surpassed Gildanstein――but it was not over yet.

The contest continued until one side could no longer move.

――And until that moment, the outcome remained unknown.

"Sir Adjutant, please fall back!! We will open the way……!"

A centurion whose name Gildanstein did not know shouted as he caught up.

Adjutant――he must have judged from the armor Gildanstein was wearing.

"Time is precious. If you want me to fall back, get yourself in front."

"Yes sir! Did you hear that, you lot!? Squads one through seven, advance! Cut in from the right! Squads ten and twelve, take the rear!"

No mana user, probably a common-born soldier.

Though approaching old age, his voice still carried strength and force.

The air that only someone who had dedicated their body to the battlefield for decades could acquire.

――He was no poor warrior.

"What is your name?"

"Centurion Clarrias, Second Battalion, directly under the general, sir!"

"I see. Don't die, Clarrias. If you survive, I will arrange a reward that lets you live in leisure."

"Haha, gracious words. ……But that is a tall order, sir!"

He drove his sword through the enemy before him like a bodily tackle.

Seeing that pulling it out was impossible, he drew the sword from the fallen man's hip instead.

His skill and judgment were both good――but the enemy was of equal or near-equal caliber.

"In this situation, I have no room to think about the future!"

"True enough. ――Still, your work here is done."

What he spotted on the left was the gap created by their cutting in from the right.

He glanced behind him.

Salva was there, and he too had seen the same thing as Gildanstein.

"Understood!!"

Answering Gildanstein's gaze, Salva cut into the gap.

His blade was broad at the base, tapering to a sharp point.

Retaining sturdiness while keeping the center of gravity near the hand――a thrusting longsword.

When driven forward, it pierced cleanly through the gaps in enemy armor, claiming lives with precision.

Following in formation behind him were the general's finest elite guards.

Glancing in that direction with a smile, Gildanstein told Clarrias.

"The general has gone straight ahead. We continue forward and hit the enemy corps commander from the flank."

"Shouldn't we support the general――"

"No need. The enemy's attention will be drawn to him. What you must do is advance through their disrupted formation and wreak havoc. I'll lead the way. Follow me."

Past the first wall, pike infantry appeared again at a distance.

Archers darted out from gaps in the formation, delivering volleys from the flank without a moment's pause.

It was like assaulting a fortress.

Break through the pikes under a rain of arrows, and inside, swordsmen waited.

And when those were finally cleared, pikes again.

On top of that, archer salvos from the flank, small in number but devastating.

Christand's Third Corps Commander, Terrius Melkikos.

This man was a master of field fortification, but even in open battle he used human flesh to build a fortress where none existed.

He funneled enemy soldiers into the gaps he created, turned them into kill zones, and broke the spirit of the brave few who made it through by annihilating them.

Even while tilting the formation heavily in echelon, the skill with which he built double and triple defensive walls was magnificent――most enemies would lose their fighting spirit after breaking through pikes only to face more pikes and flanking fire.

Among the Christand corps commanders, not a single one was incompetent.

That was why the Christand army was the kingdom's strongest――why the peerless army led by the hero had earned its fame.

In the more than ten years since Bogan had been appointed general.

The achievements of him and his army had reached Gildanstein's ears in the palace again and again.

Repeatedly, repeatedly.

Perhaps, if he had not been royalty but merely a common noble――he could have simply been a sword, thinking nothing.

――Like Bogan, for instance.

"Move!!"

He cleaved the pikes before him with the war-axe.

Smashing, snapping, disrupting the spear wall, pressing forward.

He tore into the cowering enemy with blood and fury.

At the very least, for now, he could advance without thinking.

"Sir Adjutant, you are being reckless……!"

"I'm in high spirits. It's quite an enjoyable feeling."

"Haha, to think General Caldera's adjutant has such a valiant man among them……I must confess my ignorance. What is your name?"

"Gildanstein, Clarrias."

"Wha――?"

The distance to breaking through was no longer great.

There was no longer any need to hide.

"I grant you the honor of fighting beside the King. I'm leaving my back to you."

The centurion froze for an instant, then immediately understood.

The royal prince Gildanstein――the Black Lion wielded a great war-axe.

That much, at least, everyone knew.

"Yes sir! Understood!!"

He shattered the pikes, sent the heavy infantry behind them flying.

Salva had already engaged Terrius.

――And so, Gildanstein brought the Third Corps Commander within striking distance.

"――I won't say I'm surprised. If it's you, breaking through is no surprise."

Wearing armor engraved with castle-fortress motifs.

Terrius, clad in polished, gleaming plate, dismounted and drew his twin swords, addressing Salva who had appeared before him.

Breathing hard, his plain plate armor stained with blood and lacking any polished engravings.

Yet he stood there firmly, facing Terrius.

Two rows of pikes and heavy infantry in a triple line.

With spaces between to funnel the enemy, coordinated with archers to envelop and destroy anyone who broke through.

Narrowing the path, making them run, killing them with arrows and traps like a fortress――the flesh-fortress Terrius built could rarely be breached.

But Salva had split his force in two, using one as a decoy to disrupt the archer coordination.

He had brilliantly broken through the triple wall and arrived here.

Terrius gave honest praise.

Salva, who had long served as his adjutant.

He had adjusted the details of his defensive arrangement with Salva specifically in mind.

Salva should not have been able to break through――they should never have come face-to-face.

But that judgment had been his own arrogance.

Salva was calm――more passive than anything, by nature.

He was not the type to lead from the front and tear through battle lines.

Strangely, there was even a part of him that felt glad.

Somewhere in his heart, Terrius had also wished for Salva to appear before him like this.

"Looking back, it was a long association――but this is the end, Salva. You are prepared, I trust?"

Having sensed the breakthrough, Terrius had immediately gathered his men.

To kill the overextended Salva.

At most five hundred had broken through. He had a thousand.

Salva's men were exhausted. This was the end.

"The years I fought as your adjutant――I will never forget them."

"You are the one who threw them away. What a shame. ……Archers!"

A volley greeted the emerging Salva――but.

"Corps Commander, the enemy is……tch!?"

At that very instant, from another point, a war-axe appeared.

What they had deemed a decoy――a small force, perhaps a hundred.

"Onward, all! The head of Corps Commander Terrius Melkikos lies before us!"

Salva shouted, and the archers returned fire.

But the enemy emerging from the left flank was completely outside their formation's arc.

Terrius immediately turned to face them, and grasped the situation.

"……Your Highness!"

"It has been a while, Melkikos. Sorry, but I need you to die."

Shouldering the war-axe, laughing behind his helm.

Gildanstein narrowed his eyes.

"――I will kill you with my own hands."

# Chapter 107: The Old Soldier

(T/N: Author's note at chapter start: 1 ken = 6 shaku ≈ 1.8 m)

It carved the earth, raised sandstorms――and his brute strength crushed everything.

His opponent was the Black Lion, Gildanstein.

There was no question that this was not a foe Terrius could defeat.

"……Not bad at dodging."

"Even if I cannot win, I can hardly afford to die for nothing!"

Even using his swords as shields, receiving a single blow was impossible.

Doing so would see Gildanstein's war-axe cleave through them and Terrius with them.

All Terrius could do was dodge.

There was no path to victory. But he could not flee either.

If he fled, the left wing would collapse, and Gildanstein's entire force would cascade into where Selene was.

Buying as much time as possible so that would not happen――that was all Terrius could do.

The time he needed to buy was until Kokys noticed and turned back.

――But even that was likely no longer possible.

"Guh!?"

His left arm, sword and all, was severed and sent tumbling through the air.

Agonizing pain that nearly made him lose consciousness――he gritted his teeth and endured, rising to his feet.

He could not stall for much longer.

The soldiers protecting Terrius had been whittled down before Gildanstein's blade, reduced, stripped away――there was no room left.

"It's over. Surrender and I will spare your life, Terrius. This battle is done……thinking of the postwar, you are a piece I would like to keep."

Selene exhaled in relief and stepped back.

"You're late, Kokys."

"Running isn't my forte――the armor digs into the ground……well, but from here, rest easy. The Christand hound will drive away the danger to its master."

"Fufu, how reassuring."

Selene laughed, then narrowed her eyes.

Gildanstein was still there, sword in hand, his will to fight unbroken.

Pierced with arrows, bleeding from everywhere――she remembered her father's body.

The countless wounds it had borne.

Surely her father, too.

The hero Bogan Christand, too, had fought like this, covered in wounds.

"……It is over, Your Highness. Please surrender. No matter how strong you are, with Corps Commander Agrand here and you wounded as you are, there is no chance of victory."

Selene offered a formal call for surrender.

She knew he would not accept.

Still bleeding, still holding a sword, refusing to yield.

Whether from pride, or something else――

"If I surrendered from wounds and circumstances this mild, I could not face your father."

At the very least, what stood before her was a warrior.

"That was a fine battle. Be proud. Your father was a true hero――a true warrior."

At the very least, her father had fought a warrior's battle with this man.

――That was why, at the end, his eyes had seemed to smile.

"Your Highness!!"

Running toward him, battered, was the ragged remnant of a century.

Kalua and the others instinctively raised their guard,

"……That will do, Clarrias."

Gildanstein raised a hand to halt them.

"You are witnesses. ……I promised rewards that would let you live in leisure if you survived, but I'm afraid I cannot keep that promise. Consider yourselves lucky to have your lives."

"……Your Highness."

Gildanstein chuckled, heh heh, and hefted the great sword onto his shoulder.

Kokys stepped forward with a grave expression.

"That the general's enemy met his end in a manner not unworthy of respect――I am relieved from the bottom of my heart. ……Allow me to be your opponent, Royal Prince."

Gildanstein looked at Kokys; Kokys leveled the tip of his lance.

Everyone held their breath, but.

"You are not enough for the role, Agrand."

"……What?"

"On the battlefield, there is only one thing that has thwarted my sole purpose of victory. The one who stands in my way is not you."

Gildanstein looked up at the sky and narrowed his eyes at the sun nearing its zenith.

In the cold air there was heat; a warm wind brushed his body.

"――That cheer just now. I wondered what it meant."

Gildanstein reached toward the sun as he continued.

"If Nakilus had broken through, this situation would still be uncertain. My path to victory remained. But that was your headquarters. The small force Nakilus brought would not have drawn such a cheer. And if it had, he should already be here by now."

He clenched his fist, as though trying to grasp the sun.

"In other words, Nakilus is dead."

Gildanstein declared.

"But Nakilus was a man I trusted. At the very least, I do not believe he would have fallen in that situation. He would have crawled here if he had to. ……Unless there was another factor."

And he twisted his lips.

"I did not expect her to have reached this side already, so soon after the first day……and she has already killed my double. I did not underestimate her, but still……"

A figure burst from the direction of the headquarters.

Without armor or helm, silver hair and cloak streaming.

It covered the distance to them in an instant and stopped.

Gildanstein turned.

――He had meant to win.

From the moment he had killed Bogan at the Dragon's Maw, the scales had tipped his way.

Once he had secured the palace, gathered soldiers and commanders, victory had been in sight.

But Klare Markelus had not even bought time.

The undefeated general Aulgorn Hilkintos had been destroyed in turn.

The Dragon's Maw he had won at the cost of his life had been lost in a blink.

And today, her century had delayed him, costing him fatal time.

And so, this girl――Krische Christand stood here, barring his way.

The girl, her skirt fluttering, tilted her head with a frown and looked at Selene.

"Selene, is this a surrender?"

"……No. He is requesting a duel with you."

"……A duel?"

Looking even more puzzled, even more mystified.

Krische narrowed her cold violet eyes and fixed them on Gildanstein.

"After all that running, this is somewhat surprising. Krische did not think you were the sort of person to cling to that kind of incomprehensible sense of aesthetics."

She spun the curved sword in her hand and smiled.

"But rest assured. Krische will properly keep the promise. Whatever your reasons, whatever kind of person you are――Krische will kill you without fail."

Her expressionless eyes shone.

She smiled, and yet her eyes alone did not.

Only cold, merciless intent to kill seeped through.

"That will do. ……If we fight, you win. Even so, in this place, you are the opponent I must stake my life to challenge."

Laughing, Gildanstein raised his sword.

Center guard, the King's Sword. Both hands on the hilt of the great sword.

The right hand, which commanded the sword's endlessly shifting arc.

――The right hand was heaven.

The left, which formed the axis and foundation.

――The left hand was earth.

The King's Sword, which allowed response to any attack, was a sword for all enemies.

The King's Sword gathered the whole of heaven and earth into the blade as its guiding principle.

Gildanstein's stance was breathtakingly beautiful――truly a king's stance.

"Is that so. If you will not run, then all the better. Krische was already tired of this war."

Against him, Krische held her sword in her right hand but did not assume a guard.

Against the heaven and earth――the world he created, she stood natural.

Against the King's Sword that ruled heaven and earth, she was formless and pristine.

Her sword was one bound by nothing, dwelling in the interstice.

It existed outside the heaven and earth, outside the human law that Gildanstein's King proclaimed.

She was the void.

Not the perfection of technique.

Not the possession of power.

She transcended all swordsmanship, giving rise to the optimal of each instant.

Formless――her very mode of being was her own law.

"Once Krische kills you, it is all over――and then, everything will be settled."

She sank toward the earth, and the closing step was smooth.

The distance: five ken.

――Already within Krische's range.

An ordinary swordsman would have had his head taken before he even realized.

Her advance carried not a shred of violence; it was simply serene.

The transition from stillness to motion seemed not to exist; she was moving, yet seemed not to be.

It slipped through the gaps in awareness, as though the distance between them――space itself――were contracting.

But Gildanstein, facing her, could just barely perceive it.

Time seemed to stretch. His body felt heavy, as though submerged in water.

His entire body was wounded and fatigued; his condition was arguably the worst it could be.

But precisely because of that, he had discarded whatever came after, and could concentrate every nerve fiber on this moment to the absolute limit.

Sound fell away; his vision seemed to lose color.

His body, every ability he had cultivated, was directed at one thing alone: Krische's movements.

He was late to react to her smooth approach, but caught it quickly.

Not fatally late.

Krische had covered three ken in a single step.

She could easily close five ken if she wished.

The reason she planted her foot there was to respond to whatever attack Gildanstein made.

That was how she always seized the initiative, Gildanstein understood.

The foot she set down directly before Gildanstein was her left.

Gildanstein swayed the tip of his sword to the right, and Krische reacted instantly.

She moved to Gildanstein's left flank.

――But that had been a feint.

The moment she adjusted her stance to step toward his left, he reversed.

Gildanstein tilted his sword sharply to the left.

Her weight was on her left foot.

From that position, her center of gravity leaned right.

She could no longer escape to the left.

What Gildanstein unleashed with his entire being was a horizontal slash.

The shortest, the fastest, packing everything he had built into this moment.

A blow she should not be able to dodge.

She had no choice but to block with her sword――but from there, she leaped.

Without her right foot ever touching the ground, she jumped from the left.

And what she used as a platform was Gildanstein's own great sword, swinging toward her.

The horizontal slash, the upward-facing flat of the blade.

She stamped her right foot down onto it.

"Tch――!?"

Gildanstein tried to hold with all his might.

Krische was light. He should have been able to bear it.

But Krische had always mastered the art of using her maximum.

The jump had spent only the minimum force needed to reach the height of the blade.

All of her weight concentrated into that single point on her right foot――Gildanstein's sword was driven into the earth.

――And then, she stepped onto the blade with her left foot as well.

She treated the King's Sword as the ground, a foothold.

That was so she could transmit sufficient force into the curved sword in her hand.

At some point, the sword had left her right hand.

Transferred to her left, the blade lashed out, tracing a sharp arc across Gildanstein.

The twist of her hips, the spiral the blade described.

Atop the sword, her form was undiminished; the flash of her blade drew a beautiful arc of death.

Their eyes met.

Those expressionless violet eyes were wide, capturing all of Gildanstein.

As though seeing through everything.

The blade touched his throat, and it parted.

The kingdom's banner, far away, caught his eye.

――The coat of arms: a sword thrust toward the heavens.

But Gildanstein's sword was not in the heavens. It was driven into the earth.

The girl before him stood upon it, trampling it underfoot.

The first and the last. It was a moment of defeat.

Before his severed throat could end his consciousness.

He looked upon the whole of this world and laughed.

What was meant to be, as it was meant to be.

Everything he had believed in, everything that had been betrayed, all of it drained from his mind.

In the midst of it all, he looked at the one who had killed him.

She was God's child――the cursed child who bore the name Alberan.

――The rightful heir of the twisted, mad royal bloodline.

The girl swept the head, and leaped.

Gildanstein sank to his knees, and simply laughed, voiceless.

The girl who tilted her head at his laughing form was beautiful beyond measure.

A bewitching, innocent enchantment.

Since their meeting at the Dragon's Maw, he had staked everything on this challenge.

Cast aside everything else to defeat this girl.

But even that had not been enough.

For the sake of one's own desire, crush everything that stands in the way.

A king was power.

This girl would surely thrust her sword even into the heavens.

He simply laughed at himself, the counterfeit――and let go of consciousness.

* * *

――Selene witnessed the duel that ended in an instant, and closed her eyes.

Silence filled the air.

An outcome everyone had expected.

All too easily, all too swiftly――the nemesis that had tormented Selene lay upon the earth.

"Ehehe, it's over, Selene!"

Krische paid no heed to the body and threw herself at Selene.

Honestly, Selene scolded, pinching her cheeks, and surveyed the scene.

Kalua was staring blankly at Gildanstein's corpse, fists clenched.

She pressed her lips tight and bowed her head. Someone placed a hand on her trembling shoulder.

Kokys picked up Gildanstein's sword.

Even in a case like this, Gildanstein was a Grand Archduke of the kingdom. A royal prince.

A fellow citizen of the kingdom displaying a royal's severed head was not to be permitted.

Elrouga was quietly urging the demoralized enemy soldiers to lay down their arms.

Selene thought of her father, closed her eyes.

And while stroking Krische's head, she called out to Kokys.

"……Kokys."

"Yes, ma'am!"

At her voice, Krische immediately released her and clapped her hands over her ears.

What thundered through the air was Kokys's voice, shaking the very atmosphere.

"The head of His Highness the Royal Prince has been taken in honorable single combat by the daughter of the hero Bogan Christand――First Corps Commander of the Christand Army, Krische Christand-sama, thereby avenging her father!! Any further combat shall be considered an insult to both combatants who decided the contest with honor!! All hostilities are hereby forbidden! Both sides, sheathe your swords and cease fighting immediately!!"

And after a brief pause, he cried out,

"This battle is a victory for Her Highness the First Princess and for us――the Christand Army!!"

――And a cheer shook the earth.
