# Chapter 46: Vulture

In the forest stood Krische, surrounded by five people.

The five each held wooden swords at the ready, while Krische, as always, stood without so much as assuming a stance.

Her eyes were not fixed on any particular point.

Those narrowed eyes simply gazed vaguely at the scenery before her.

Facing her was Mia's squad.

Five against one――yet no hesitation about that remained.

After seven squads had already been laid low by her with nothing to show for it, there was no room for carelessness.

Forest ambushes, surprise attacks, and practical response drills had continued for a week now, producing minor injuries along the way in grueling training.

The black century soldiers, with their high physical abilities, were demonstrating capabilities incomparable to regular soldiers, and the rigorous training had stripped away any complacency.

Their surprise attacks were no longer simple flanking assaults――they now employed three-dimensional attacks using the trees.

At this point, they could likely finish off a mere supply column in less than half an hour. (T/N: 小半刻 ≈ roughly 30 minutes, half of one 刻 which is approximately one hour.)

On the fourth day, a joint exercise with the red-designated light infantry demonstrated their full capabilities.

An ambush against red troops during a march exercise――the red had been warned of the coming attack in advance.

It was a high-difficulty scenario, pitting the hundred-strong black against two of red's best centuries, yet the result was the complete annihilation of red's marching column with only seven black casualties.

Red, who had been told they were elite, were stunned by the result.

It had spurred their already-high motivation for improvement, but conversely, it had made black grow arrogant.

A crushing victory over red――who were being forged as crack troops――even if it was an ambush.

It was only natural that the black soldiers, feeling their own superiority as real for the first time, would react that way.

That said, this was obviously not what Dagra wanted, and word had reached Krische yesterday asking for her cooperation in tightening them up.

The result was mock battles, five against one, by squad.

At first the soldiers had hesitated――attacking a general's daughter five-on-one――but after watching three squads go down with ease, even they shed any complacency and got serious.

Yet the four squads that followed were also brought down effortlessly, and their arrogance had been doused with cold water.

The squad led by Mia was the centurion's guard detail――the very best among the black.

On the battlefield, they would operate as Centurion Dagra's escort, and aside from Mia, the squad was composed exclusively of soldiers with outstanding swordsmanship.

Members of the black――mana users who also possessed the skill level to qualify for red selection――were placed here, and in inter-squad competitions they invariably claimed first place.

With defeat after defeat chilling the air, Mia had concluded that this squad was the only one that could turn things around.

Five against one.

The advantage was clear, and everyone here possessed swordsmanship close to first-rate.

And their physical capabilities were incomparable to ordinary soldiers.

Yet Krische showed composure, even conceding the first move by telling them to strike from wherever they wished.

Mia hid herself in the treetops to watch for an opening, while the other four surrounded Krische on all sides.

Facing Krische head-on was a woman named Kalua, whose swordsmanship rivaled Dagra's.

*At the very least, we can corner her.*

So thinking, Mia struck a branch with her wooden sword, sending the signal.

Kalua was the first to step in, tasked with stopping Krische's movement.

Her long black hair, tied at the back, flowed in the wind, and her charge closed the distance with ease――

――Slashing diagonally downward at windlike speed.

A strike without a shred of restraint.

Against Krische, who wore no armor at all, even stopping at the last moment might have broken bone.

*That's too much*――Mia panicked, but Krische simply spun away.

It was as if her small frame had phased through Kalua's body.

With nothing more than a light twist, she circled behind Kalua and drove the sole of her foot hard into her back.

"Uggh!?"

Kalua's body was sent flying and tumbling as if struck by a horse.

Naturally, Kalua's body rolled right into the path of the three behind her who had been poised to strike at Krische's opening. The sudden obstacle disrupted their footing――and in the next instant, the wooden sword of the one at the edge was knocked from her hands.

Kalua, still rolling, swung her sword from the ground, but it was dodged; her arm was pinned under Krische's foot and her helmet lightly tapped with the wooden sword.

In a real fight, it would have been a fatal blow――at that point, she was judged dead.

In the midst of the confusion, the remaining two exchanged glances, split to either side, and circled around.

Even among the black, these were the best of the best――even amid chaos, they understood what needed to be done.

Seizing the moment, Mia kicked off a branch and slashed down at Krische from above.

But in the next instant, Krische's figure had vanished――

"Mia, above!"

――The impact driven into her skull sent Mia crumpling to the ground.

"The idea of attacking from the treetops isn't bad. But that's for ambushes. What's the point of hiding when Krische already knows you're there?"

"Yes, Krische is sorry……"

Krische tapped the wooden sword against Mia's helmet as Mia bowed her head.

Krische was quite short, and with average-heighted Mia bowing like that, their heights just about matched.

Krische had been carefully observing the training instructors' teaching methods.

Following their example, she was imitating a "fine training instructor," but the sight of her small body puffing out her chest and acting important was completely at odds with her delicate appearance――the very image of a child playing grown-up.

More comical than frightening, with a peculiar charm to it, but naturally no one said so aloud.

"If you're going to do it, you need to keep moving through the treetops, devising ways to keep your position hidden from the enemy. An aerial surprise attack isn't bad as a surprise attack, but be mindful that until you reach the ground, you're defenseless and wasting time."

The moment Mia had leapt down, Krische had switched places with her by jumping upward, kicking off a branch to drive her sword down onto Mia's head from above.

The remaining two were then picked off one by one.

A complete defeat.

"Your use of Kalua was also poor. Sending Kalua――who is at least comparatively competent within this squad――charging in from the front is something a fool does. What is the basic principle of tactics?"

"Use the weak as bait, and devour with the strong."

*Bonk*――her head was tapped again.

"If you understand that, why didn't you do it?"

"Yes, Krische is sorry……"

"'Sorry' is not a reason."

"C-Corps Commander's Adjutant!"

Kalua's long black hair swayed like a tail as she raised her voice.

"It was I who proposed that I charge from the front and buy time! Adjutant Mia accepted that proposal――"

"That's even more foolish."

*Bonk bonk bonk*――three consecutive raps on Mia's head.

Mia could only endure the dull pain seeping through her helmet and silently curse *why does this always happen to me*.

"Assessing the relative strength of both sides is the most fundamental of fundamentals for a commander. This might be forgivable if it were the first engagement, but this is your eighth. Can you not even gauge the gap in ability between yourselves and Krische?"

"N-no, Krische believed she understood……"

"If you understood, why didn't you act on it?"

"Um, that's, well……"

"Corps Commander's Adjutant, I'll take it from here."

Dagra spoke up, tense, to put an end to what seemed like it would go on forever.

Krische glanced at Dagra, then said "Well then, Krische leaves it to you" and tossed it to him.

"Mia is talented, but it's no good that she's indecisive and swayed by others' opinions. Krische's wish is to make Mia like Dagra. Can Krische count on you?"

"Yes, ma'am. I will forge her thoroughly."

"Against a mere two hundred, with these numbers and with the element of surprise, seven casualties. For a surprise attack, Krische's standard is seven casualties against one thousand. The results fall entirely short of what is required."

Krische swayed her silver hair and placed both hands on her hips as she declared this.

"Individual skill aside, it would be better to focus more on coordination training. Krische feels that the reason every army organizes soldiers into five-man squads is not being properly understood. Kalua, what is the advantage of the squad?"

"T-to always coordinate as five, to engage even a single enemy with all five, and to ensure numerical superiority!"

"Correct. Even if you cannot achieve five-on-one, creating even a momentary two-on-one situation gives you the advantage. All of you should be fundamentally superior to red soldiers, with the ability to generally win in one-on-one engagements. Yet seven casualties means the opposite――that red soldiers managed to create local numerical superiority against you. That is truly embarrassing."

Looking displeased, Krische puffed her cheeks slightly and continued.

It was an adorable sight, girlish through and through.

"Krische promised Selene that she would make all of you into a unit that defeats the enemy at a ratio of one to ten. With results like these, Krische would be too embarrassed to even report to Selene. According to Dagra's report, you're apparently satisfied with seven casualties against two hundred. Krische is disappointed. If there were at least a hundred Dagras here, Krische's requirements would be met, and you should be able to do that much as a matter of course."

The black soldiers looked at Dagra.

Dagra bore their gazes, though the weight of such expectations made him uneasy.

Regardless, being held in such high regard by this genius was not a bad feeling in itself.

"A soldier's job is to kill the enemy. To kill without being killed――that is the job. If you are not killed, you can kill again next time. If you save a comrade, your comrade can kill more enemies. That is what coordination means――how to kill the enemy without your comrades being killed, without yourself being killed. Krische truly wants all of you to understand that."

Krische's words were utterly clear.

Dagra spoke up to supplement them.

"……Look to your side. At your comrade's face."

Dagra stepped forward.

He had been privately asked by Selene to smooth over any misunderstandings Krische's words might cause.

"You are satisfied with where you are now, and you grow arrogant. I understand that feeling. You are talented――I think so too. ……But difficult situations exist in endless supply on the battlefield. When that time comes, what is lost because your strength was not enough――it is the face beside you. One by one, someone among them will be missing."

The soldiers looked at each other, then at Dagra.

"I have lost many comrades. Among them were friends. Among them were men I had been carousing with the night before. ……Each time, I grieved with frustration. If only I had been a little stronger."

Dagra looked squarely at those soldiers and wove his words in a quiet tone, as if speaking to each one personally.

"Grueling training is imposed upon you, and high results are demanded. Why? Because it comes from my own regret――my wish that you never endure the same. Because I do not want you to see the death-faces of comrades who ate from the same pot. ……The Corps Commander's Adjutant's words are the same. Seven casualties in training means seven dead in real combat. You understand what that means. From this point on, those seven should have been gone."

He did not bellow or raise his voice, and yet――Dagra was indeed an outstanding centurion.

A father to soldiers, through and through.

He knew how to draw their attention and bind them together.

"Coordination is the art of not being killed. And the art of protecting your comrades. Protect not your own body but your comrade's, and your comrade protects yours. Never forget this."

Krische watched the soldiers' faces, now showing understanding, and raised her estimation of Dagra once more.

Indeed, it was best to leave this sort of thing to Dagra.

Krische's words had been entirely literal, but if Dagra would flesh them out and add color, it could not be easier.

That said, it really would be better to have more than one Dagra.

Krische looked at Mia.

"For now, Mia. First, you need to understand Dagra's thinking and become capable of considering things at the same level. And then you will create more Dagras. Understood?"

"Uu……yes……"

"Krische's goal is to turn everyone here into a Dagra. For that, you must first become the second Dagra."

"Now then, shall we move on to the next?" Krische called up another squad.

That day, it continued until each squad had gone through three rotations.

"Man……I didn't think that bunny was *that* much of a monster."

"Don't call her 'bunny.' If Centurion Dagra hears you, he'll be furious."

While conducting two-squad-against-one-squad, ten-on-five training, one squad at a time was pulled for the five-on-one with Krische.

By the time it ended, they were exhausted, but the camp still needed setting up.

Today rest had been ordered afterward, and the black century, normally split into two groups, was making camp together for once.

The one chatting with Mia while gathering branches was Kalua.

A woman who, despite her sex, was among the unit's finest swordswomen; being in the same squad as Mia, they got along well.

Easygoing and adroit, the type who handled everything smoothly, Kalua was like an older sister who obligingly listened to Mia's complaints.

"I mean, I'd heard rumors that bunny was secretly incredible, but with looks like that, you just don't expect her to be *that* strong."

"Bunny" was the nickname the unit had given Krische.

Silver hair, snow-white skin, and a small stature.

When Selene or Bery came by, she would nuzzle up to them affectionately, and so, likened to an adorable winter white rabbit, she was called "bunny" or "bunny-chan" within the unit.

It was partly a derisive epithet born from resentment toward her strictness and cold demeanor――the way she looked at soldiers like they were pebbles――and Dagra flew into a thunderous rage whenever he heard it.

For that reason, Mia never said it aloud even in private, but Kalua, perhaps by nature, showed no particular concern.

"I mean, I even won a sword tournament in a decently big town, you know? After coming here and learning how to use mana, I was feeling pretty much invincible, but……"

"Centurion Dagra said with a dead serious face to absolutely never make her angry. You know, the rumor? That she tortured someone by cutting off their fingers to both hands without even changing expression."

"Ugh……is that really true?"

"Who knows. But it does seem like something she'd do……"

"True. I can totally picture bunny doing that with a blank face――"

"Are you talking about Krische?"

――The two froze.

With no warning, the voice that came from behind was somehow young and sweet.

Kalua immediately turned, placed a hand to her chest in salute, and Mia followed suit.

Krische returned the salute, then asked.

"Mia, Krische is looking for Dagra. Do you know where he is?"

"C-Centurion Dagra went to check the sentry posts……"

"Is that so."

Krische pursed her lips slightly and said,

"Well, Mia will do. Training resumes as before starting tomorrow. Krische is heading back, so please relay that if anything comes up, a report should be sent."

"Y-yes."

"Also, something Krische was curious about――is 'bunny' referring to Krische?"

Krische tilted her head and asked.

Inorganic eyes――impossible to tell what lay behind them.

Her doll-like expressionlessness cast deep shadows in the twilight, and it felt eerie.

A breeze, as if tickling the silence, brushed through the branches and leaves.

Mia glared at Kalua, and Kalua shook her head.

A wordless exchange played out for a while, and then Krische tilted her head the other way and asked again.

"……Mia?"

"Y-yes……um, that……K-Krische is sorry!"

"……? Why are you apologizing?"

Mia considered the meaning of Krische's words――*why are you apologizing*.

Was she literally not understanding?

Or was she asking, understanding perfectly well, whether Mia wished to be killed?

Unable to gauge anything from Krische's expressionless demeanor, cold sweat trickled down Mia's back.

"Also, there's a bit of a misunderstanding. What Krische cut off during the interrogation was only eight fingers. Krische did not cut off both hands and feet. Honestly, rumors are troublesome things."

*Rumors grow bigger without you knowing it*――remembering something Gallen had once told her, Krische put on a knowing face as she said this. In truth, she did not understand at all.

Even if it was only fingers and not all four limbs, it was no less terrifying.

Krische failed to grasp the fundamental point.

*So it's true*――their fear only intensified now that they had confirmation.

"Well, never mind that. Is 'bunny' the kind of nickname that requires an apology?"

"N-no, that's……!"

This girl, younger than herself, was a monster. Mia became certain of it anew.

Beginning with Dagra, those who had witnessed her on the battlefield――even battle-hardened veterans――spoke fearfully of her as "not human," "a demon," "utterly merciless," "the reaper."

The curved sword at her hip had cut down over a hundred without a single chip in its blade, and on the battlefield she had such composure that she deliberately avoided bone and armor, targeting only soft flesh to rend.

They said, one after another, that she was a being who must not be spoken of in the same terms as ordinary people.

And the words she had just spoken showed that such rumors were as close to fact as could be.

In place of the frozen Mia, it was Kalua who answered.

"A……! It's a term of endearment! Corps Commander's Adjutant!"

"A term of endearment?"

"Y-yes! That is, out of affection for the Corps Commander's Adjutant, likening your silver hair, white skin, and beautiful, adorable form to a white rabbit, w-we have been fondly calling you 'bunny' among ourselves!"

"Affection……"

Krische thought for a moment and nodded in understanding.

"Krische has never been given a nickname before, so it struck Krische as a bit strange. So that's how it works."

"Y-yes! E-even across the difference in rank, as comrades in the same unit, we have been using that name with affection! If it displeases you――"

"No, it's fine. As long you know it refers to Krische, and if it's an expression of affection, it's not really Krische's concern."

Pleased with the phrase "with affection," Krische nodded.

She was fundamentally indifferent to others, but being liked was better than being disliked.

She had no need for deference toward herself. What she demanded of them was obedience on the battlefield.

Obedience born from affection was surely preferable to obedience born from fear, and if they said they were trying to feel affection toward her, she would accept it plainly.

Goodwill in return for goodwill.

Krische had at least arrived at such a precarious sort of decency.

Having convinced herself of this, Krische shifted her gaze toward a thicket.

There was no one there.

"Is something the matter……?"

"Dagra."

Mia and Kalua furrowed their brows, and after a moment came the sound of parting undergrowth.

Slowly approaching from the dark thicket was Dagra.

The two looked at Krische in surprise, and Dagra eyed them suspiciously before calling out.

"Corps Commander's Adjutant, what brings you here?"

Krische stared at Dagra.

Dagra was a stern-faced man, his bald head and hooked nose his most prominent features.

A forged body.

An old scar cut deep into his cheek――he was the very picture of a valiant warrior.

Affection. Nickname.

Krische studied his face intently and fell into thought.

"Um……?"

"Vulture."

――At those words, the three of them froze.

"From now on, Krische will call Dagra 'Vulture.' Apparently there is a culture of affectionate nicknames here."

"Huh? Um……v-vul……"

"Vulture. Your head is bald and your nose is hooked. Krische thinks it makes for a very good nickname."

It was no longer a nickname but a simple insult.

Yet, paying no mind to the three frozen figures, Krische smiled.

Krische considered this an excellent idea.

"Krische is apparently 'bunny' because of her skin and hair color――a white rabbit. Isn't that right, Mia?"

"Uh, ah……um……"

It was Kalua who had said it, but in this context Mia was the superior.

Krische, who faithfully followed rules, naturally directed the accountability to Mia.

Dagra's bald head flushed red, veins bulging, his face beginning to twist with fury and hatred.

His gaze was fixed squarely on Mia.

Mia looked to Kalua for help, but Kalua averted her eyes.

"Since Vulture will essentially be a direct subordinate under Krische, Krische would like to build a good relationship, and giving a nickname seems like a good way to do that. What do you think? If you don't like it, Krische won't insist."

"N-no……to be bestowed a nickname directly by the Corps Commander's Adjutant herself is……t-the highest of honors."

"Is that so. Then it's settled――Vulture it is."

There was no taking it back now.

Having been told it was an honor, Krische nodded with satisfaction, wearing an expression that said she had done a good deed.

"Krische is heading back now. Instructions have been relayed to Mia. If anything comes up, send a messenger from Vulture to Krische. Do not miss the daily report."

"……Yes, ma'am. From here, I shall have a nice, long, thorough talk with Mia about tomorrow's plans."

"Yes. Please do. Mia, listen well to what Vulture tells you."

"Y……yes……"

Having said all this, Krische toddled off at a light jog into the forest.

She apparently did not like horses very much――she had come to this forest on foot.

She would likely return on foot as well.

"Right then, Mia. Let's hear a nice thorough explanation of what happened."

"I-it wasn't me! K-Kalua was the one who……huh!?"

Kalua had vanished without a trace.

The moment Krische had started running, she had quietly slipped away.

"Kalua? ……Even if Kalua was at fault, you are the adjutant, the one responsible for maintaining discipline. What were you doing not stopping it, you fool!!"

Mia received the second impact to her skull that day.

# Chapter 47: Waxing, Waning

*――The large room was spare: a desk, a bed, a wardrobe.*

*Besides those, just a few bookshelves.*

*But there was not a single unread book among them.*

*And so she spent her days on the bed by the window, gazing outside.*

*The sun set and rose.*

*Birds flew, flowers bloomed, and eventually they died and withered.*

*She felt nothing at all.*

*Eventually she too would join them, she thought――merely watching.*

*A servant tending the garden noticed her and looked down, resuming work with obvious haste.*

*Eager to get out of sight, the servant worked in haste, and the job was quite sloppy.*

*I would have done it this way. I would have done it that way.*

*Thinking such things, she ignored the servant and simply gazed at the garden.*

*When the garden bored her, she listened to the birdsong and the wind.*

*But as she focused her ears, gradually the footsteps echoing through the manor crept in, joined by the sounds of things being dragged and moved, the noise of conversations.*

*Among them, the sound of a wagon rolling.*

*It drew near along the corridor, and she heard voices.*

*"You go. I'm no good with Bery-ojou-sama."*

*"I don't want to either――she gives me the creeps. You never know what she's thinking."*

*Whispered voices.*

*"Haah, and Lazura-ojou-sama is such a fine, wonderful person."*

*"Lazura-ojou-sama must have taken all the good parts, surely. I'll go today, but tomorrow it's your turn."*

*"Fine, fine. Well, good luck then."*

*The wagon rolled to a stop before the room, and the door was knocked, then immediately opened.*

*They knew no answer would come.*

*What the servant had brought was tea and sweets.*

*She glanced sideways at the thin, servile smile, then quickly turned her gaze back to the window.*

*"……Please leave the wagon there. I'll drink when I feel like it."*

*"Um, well……should I clean……"*

*"I've already done it. And from tomorrow, you needn't bring tea either."*

*"But Lazura-ojou-sama said――"*

*"I said I don't want it. ……Did you not hear me?"*

*She studied the woman's face.*

*Anger, humiliation, displeasure.*

*She read the various emotions in the depths of those eyes, and――*

*"I'll handle the cleaning myself from now on too. I'll leave the laundry out. You needn't come unless I call. I like being alone, and it's no inconvenience."*

*She told her with a smile.*

*"Besides, I give you the creeps, don't I? I have no need for you to force yourself on my account either."*

*The woman froze.*

*"I'd been thinking for some time that it was tiresome, but if there had been any sense of professional duty or goodwill on your part, I wouldn't have felt right saying it. ……Thanks to you, I feel much better now. Thank you. Please, do go."*

*"B-but, just now, that was――my mouth just――"*

*"Excuses are unnecessary. I have no interest in you."*

*She ignored the woman and turned her eyes to the window. After a while, the woman left.*

*It didn't matter what anyone said.*

*Let them say what they pleased――it was the truth, after all, and there was no reason to hold back.*

*That was far easier, and much better than playing along with pointless pretense.*

*Neither pleasant nor painful.*

*She would die eventually, and that would be the end of this life――so it didn't matter.*

*The sun set, and a perfect, full moon rose in the sky.*

*The darkness of night looked brighter than usual, glowing gently.*

*She loved the full moon, that never waned――it was so beautiful.*

*As she gazed at it, the sound of knocking.*

*She ignored it, and it came again; she ignored that too, and it came a third time.*

*Sighing, she said "Come in."*

*"Here she comes~! Your personal servant, Lazura Argan, bringing dinner!"*

*She looked over and frowned.*

*A white-and-black apron dress――the kind servants wore. She had apparently gone to the trouble of putting one on.*

*Her sister was always free-spirited.*

*"Your devoted servant was just so worried, so very worried about a certain Bery-ojou-sama who apparently threw a tantrum. ……Ah, what a wonderful servant Lazura is."*

*"……I have no appetite."*

*"Now, now, don't go sulking like that."*

*Two chairs were brought beside the wagon at random.*

*Too lazy to prepare a table, she apparently intended to use the wagon as one.*

*Her sister was always careless and couldn't be bothered.*

*"Come along, ojou-sama. Right here."*

*"Ah……"*

*She was lifted up, just like that, and turned her face away.*

*Her sister laughed happily.*

*"You really are light, Bery. And small. You won't get better unless you eat properly, you know."*

*"……Eating won't change anything."*

*"That's because you keep moping around."*

*"……You don't understand, nee-sama."*

*"Obviously not. I'm not you, after all."*

*A face drew close, and a light kiss was placed on her cheek.*

*"But even if I don't understand, I love you dearly, Bery."*

*"I hate myself. And I hate you too, nee-sama."*

*"Liar. I know perfectly well that you adore me."*

*"Just a moment ago you said you don't understand my feelings."*

*"Every rule has its exceptions. Don't sweat the details."*

*Forced into the chair, her sister smiled and stroked her head.*

*――Her sister was always bright and kind.*

*"See? You're being honest. That's proof you adore me."*

*Beautiful, accomplished, brilliant――like a full moon that never waned, her sister was perfect.*

*And she herself was nothing but an unwanted burden casting a shadow over it.*

*Her vision blurred, grew hazy and distorted, and her sister gently embraced her.*

*Staining the apron with tears, she said quietly,*

*"……Please leave me alone."*

*"As if I could. When you're my lovely, clever little sister."*

*"I'm nothing but a burden to you, nee-sama. If only I had never been born――"*

*"Say that and I'll be angry."*

*Even as she said it, the hand stroking her head and the voice were endlessly gentle.*

*"……And I might even cry. If you don't want to make your beloved onee-sama cry, don't say things like that, Bery."*

*Her sister's voice, resonating in the quiet room, was what she loved most in this world.*

*Beautiful and clear.*

*And so, when she made it waver with sadness, she always came to hate herself.*

*"You say you're fine. You say you're all right. You say you have no worth. ……Maybe it truly doesn't bother you. But it hurts me."*

*"……Nee-sama."*

*"No matter how much you speak ill of yourself, to me, my sister Bery is precious――so, so precious, my treasure. I hate having someone I treasure that much spoken of so cruelly."*

*Her cheeks were cupped in both hands, and the tears that came of their own accord were gently wiped away.*

*"And you have no right to decide your own worth, Bery. Worth is determined by those who see it. No matter how much you mope, no matter how much you hate yourself, no matter how many people hate you, you are my treasure. Now and always."*

*"I'm not……anything like……"*

*Listen to your older sister.*

*She said something like that, made a face as though she were a little angry, and smiled.*

*"You're a strong girl, Bery. ……You're not crying because you feel sorry for yourself. You're crying because it hurts you to trouble me. A little sister so kind that she cries out of care for me――how could she have no worth?"*

*――I could never find you a burden, so please――be as spoiled as you like.*

*Her sister's smile was beautiful beyond measure, and it remained in her memory forever.*

* * *

The office was a little lively.

Since Kreschenta had begun helping, Selene's burden had decreased considerably.

The sight of her scolding Selene and Anne, only to be brushed off by Selene, was entertaining, and Bery was glad to see her changing for the better.

Bery placed her documents into a bag and stood.

The sun had already set. For now, the day's work was done.

"I'll bring these over to the logistics department."

"Yes. Be careful out there. There are some rough types around."

"Fufu, of course."

She left the room and headed straight outside.

She did not take any odd detours, going directly.

"Yes. The personnel I've brought number eight thousand five hundred. Of those, two thousand are archers, primarily hunters."

"Oh, two thousand."

Third Corps Commander Terrius nodded, impressed.

"Sending the recruitment notice to the villages as well seems to have paid off. As I wrote, personnel capable of riding have been left behind due to insufficient training."

"Yes, that's fine."

Unlike infantry, who formed ranks and executed relatively straightforward maneuvers, cavalry required an entirely different set of skills.

Given the cost and expense of horses, the riders needed commensurate ability――standards that could not be met by barely a month's training.

Especially since the Dragon's Maw was not terrain where cavalry could shine, there was no issue.

"I would like to receive some of those archers for the Third Corps. We've taken heavy losses."

"Understood. We'll work out the allocation later."

Bogan said this and indicated the map on the table.

It depicted the area around the Dragon's Maw.

Given how frequently it appeared in historical accounts, maps of the area were numerous and of high quality.

"To the west, the Third Corps is deployed on Mitskronia; to the east, the Fourth Corps on Bernaich. Both have repaired and occupied fortress ruins on the ridgelines. The center is held by the Second Corps, currently in a standoff at the narrows. Overall, we may call the situation favorable. What I've gathered you to discuss is our tactical development from here."

Bogan folded his arms and rubbed the bridge of his nose.

Two months of facing the enemy head-on, pushing and being pushed.

Naturally, the fatigue was considerable for Bogan as well.

"His Highness the Prince is proceeding cautiously. He withdraws where he should withdraw, conserving his forces, intending to grind us down. A war of attrition……but time favors the enemy more than us."

If they took too long, the unknowns――how the surrounding players would move――became unmanageable.

That was true for both sides, but the simple fact of the capital region's larger population pool mattered.

There was no question that the enemy had more room for conscription.

"I want a decisive result. The first objective is the prince himself, but failing that, the second is complete control of the Dragon's Maw. If a decisive battle can be fought on the capital region's plains, I believe we hold the advantage. For this discussion, the second objective alone will suffice. Are there any thoughts?"

First and second objectives.

On the battlefield, objectives must be unified, and priorities were essential.

If two objectives existed simultaneously, confusion would inevitably arise.

And that confusion would fracture the army, creating the conditions for piecemeal destruction.

For this reason, within the Christand army, the priority of objectives was always kept explicit.

Defeating Gildanstein was naturally the paramount objective that would end the civil war, but under the current circumstances, the difficulty was too high.

If that was deemed infeasible for now, then the priority shifted to creating conditions that would make it feasible――temporarily stepping down to a secondary objective.

Narrowing the objective meant exactly that.

"Yes. Regarding the second objective alone, Krische has a suggestion. Goshujin-sama." (T/N: ご当主様, "Goshujin-sama," literally "head of the house" — Krische's standard address for Bogan in formal settings.)

And it was Krische who answered.

A puzzle everyone had been racking their brains over.

A murmur of surprise ran through the tent at Krische's easy declaration.

"……Let's hear it."

Bogan drew a breath, but his heart was a swirl of relief and anticipation.

The reconstitution of the First Corps was naturally something Bogan had hoped and waited for.

Selene had supplied Bogan with combat-ready reinforcements while also scraping together eight thousand five hundred soldiers, a more than adequate performance that left her father amazed.

But what Bogan had anticipated even more was the silver-haired girl before him.

The strategy that had run rings around Sarshenka.

The memory was still fresh, and he had believed that she might be the one to produce a decisive outcome from this situation.

"Let us launch a general offensive the day after tomorrow."

"A general offensive?"

"Yes. Tomorrow, the Third Corps withdraws down the mountain――we'll use Bernaich to the east."

"……Withdraw. You would surrender the mountain?"

"That's right," Krische nodded.

It wasn't something she had just now conceived.

She had done a rough sketch of it on the way to the capital the other day, while riding in the carriage past this very place.

How would she take the Dragon's Maw if it were up to her?

She took a sip of her sweet tea, wetting her throat――

"Exactly. ……We give it to the enemy, and burn the mountain down."

――And with a smile like a little girl's, she said it.
