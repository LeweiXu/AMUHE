# Chapter 66: A Choice

"……A ceasefire. What a joke."

——An envoy from Gildanstein had arrived roughly three weeks after the battle at the Dragon's Maw.

What the envoy proposed was a ceasefire.

The faces in the council room were not bright.

"Let us refuse, Selene-sama. There's nothing to think about."

"……Corps Commander Agrand, calm yourself. The matter is not so simple."

Third Corps Commander Terious rebuked Kokys.

The reason they all wore grim expressions was that the ceasefire proposal was, whatever its intent, grounded in sound reasoning.

"However……"

"What Corps Commander Melkikos says is sound. ……If we refuse, we too stand to lose much."

Gallen spoke and drained his black soy tea in a single gulp.

Without looking at the adjutant pouring a fresh cup, he continued.

He too wore deep furrows between his brows.

"The ceasefire period is from the coming harsh winter through to harvest. Whatever the intent, it is well considered."

War generates enormous expense.

That burden naturally falls upon the people.

Food, and the labor of soldiers taken from the workforce.

The argument that from spring through the end of summer — the peak of agriculture — they should sheathe their swords for the people's sake was perfectly reasonable and grounded in principle.

If they bluntly refused, the princess's faction risked losing the public's trust.

From spring to summer, no nation typically waged war.

Most campaigns were launched from autumn into winter — precisely to avoid draining the labor force and weakening the state.

The timing was earlier than customary, but on the merits, the proposal was sound.

This had been a sudden civil war with poor timing, and they had placed heavy burdens on the people — granting them rest was, in a sense, not a bad thing.

The problem was that it would freeze their movements during winter.

"……The flanking maneuver is off, then?"

No one answered Krische's words.

They had not simply been sitting idle after losing the Dragon's Maw.

At a recent council, the strategy for retaking the Dragon's Maw had been decided.

Krische would make a wide flanking sweep around Bernaich.

Crossing the mountains, she would coordinate with Nozan Velraigh in the east for a simultaneous attack, advance into the center, take the Dragon's Maw from behind, and crush the enemy in a pincer with Selene's forces remaining here.

A single stroke to break through the formidable Dragon's Maw.

The west — Hilkintos — the south — Garhka — and the north — the Arna Empire — had still not declared their allegiance. The reason was unclear.

Was it a matter of conscience — trying to discern which side was righteous as nobles loyal to the kingdom?

Or was it wariness, because Christand still retained strength?

Their letters cited defense concerns as the reason they could not move, but their true thinking was unknowable.

The Arna Empire, as an ally, hoped for a swift resolution but had sent back a boilerplate response: *'Opinions are divided regarding intervention in another nation's internal affairs. We pray for your fortunes in war.'*

Before they decided on a flag.

If they were to join the war, the loss of Bogan made it more likely they would side with Gildanstein.

They needed to strike back before Gildanstein fully secured the capital.

But Gildanstein had framed the ceasefire as being for the people's sake.

If the fence-sitters were merely looking for a pretext to join, refusing the proposal could be the trigger.

"A counterattack is desirable. But……it's a difficult problem."

Even within Krische, who had been set to depart in a few days, emotion and reason were at war.

If the operation were canceled, Krische would not have to leave Bery and the others.

Even so, Krische's reason had concluded that they should attack now.

Slowly, like a silk noose tightening, Christand was being cornered.

She could sense it.

Krische looked at Kreschenta.

Kreschenta, wearing her princess's face, noticed Krische's gaze and let her eyes waver.

Kreschenta, too, seemed uncertain — and seeing that, Krische understood this was a politically difficult problem, and fell into thought.

Selene spoke.

"Krische, you also estimated that the capital operation would take until late spring at least?"

"Yes. There's a chance it might drag on a little, but……Krische thinks it should be resolved by then."

Krische looked at the map and pointed to the Dragon's Maw.

"At the earliest, early spring……if everything goes perfectly and smoothly, the onset of winter. If we're just talking about the Dragon's Maw, it's faster, but……"

If Gildanstein committed to a thorough delaying action and avoided decisive battle, even Krische could not judge how long things might take.

"……I see. But if we're going to move General Velraigh, we must see it through to the end in one push. We can't predict when the Empire might move again."

Selene sipped her black soy tea and sighed deeply.

"One thing is certain. His Highness the Prince will use the ceasefire to consolidate the center. If we agree to the ceasefire, we'll next be facing the full army of the capital — simply accepting the ceasefire would be the path to defeat. If we accept, we must also prepare the conditions to confront and overcome that force."

The downsides of refusing were large.

But the downsides of accepting were equally large.

"Corps Commander Agrand favors continued hostilities. Is that your view as well, Krische?"

"……Victory would end it all."

If they won.

But certainties never exist.

Otherwise, they would already have advanced into the capital region.

That was why the council room was heavy with tension.

"……Let us reduce the points of concern."

Kreschenta raised her voice and stood.

"West and south, and the Arna Empire to the north — if even one of them moves, the balance tips dramatically. The concern is that they would likely side against us in the current situation, and that refusing the proposal might trigger their entry. Is that correct?"

"Yes, precisely, Your Highness."

Selene nodded.

Inwardly, she felt unsettled.

She did not know what Kreschenta was about to say.

"I will travel to the Arna Empire as an envoy."

"……An envoy? To request their assistance?"

"Yes, ostensibly."

The assembly furrowed their brows, looking at the young, exquisite profile of the princess.

"Whether requesting aid will succeed, I cannot say with certainty. But if I visit personally, as royalty, the Empire cannot simply ignore me. If we notify them of the visit before they declare a position, they will at the very least be unable to take military action — don't you think?"

Krische's eyes widened slightly, and seeing that, Kreschenta smiled.

Their gazes met for only an instant.

"……The martial reputation of Margrave Christand was great, and for that reason, the Empire could not side with either faction. But now that he is lost, the Empire is the most likely to move. Preventing that alone would be a great help."

Kreschenta said this, and shaped her face into sorrow.

She lowered her eyes quietly, drawing her well-formed brows downward — her lovely countenance all the more striking.

Every gaze in the council room was drawn to that beauty.

"……I am nothing more than a banner. I cannot offer much aid by staying here. For my sake, you are all risking your lives in battle. For that sake, the beloved Margrave himself gave his life."

She knew the precise effect her voice and expression had on those around her.

Because she understood power, and understood the force it carried.

She drew gazes and minds toward her like a tongue pulling thread — toying with them.

There was some hesitation about exploiting Bogan's death in front of Selene.

But her intelligence existed to seek results.

To fulfill the role her sister desired, to carry out what her sister expected.

And those aligned with the results she herself sought.

So she would use everything.

That was the creature Kreschenta was.

"I will go to the Empire and extend the negotiations as long as possible. At least until spring. We may even announce that the Empire is on our side. That alone would serve as a check on the western and southern generals. In the meantime, I ask that all of you here put a stop to His Highness the Prince's — my uncle's — tyranny."

A trembling voice, yet strong.

Her violet eyes wavered, glistening, and a small tear fell.

Kreschenta wiped it and raised her face.

"My father — the earnest and noble king — had his life taken by that ambition. And now the kingdom's hero, the loyal margrave, has also been slain by cowardly means. If that beast-like ambition consumes all of the kingdom, what will become of its future? A ceasefire for the people's sake — all of it is merely words born of wicked desire……they cannot be trusted."

No one spoke to stop her.

For just a moment.

In that brief span, Kreschenta seized control of the room's atmosphere.

Before the weeping princess, the option of accepting a ceasefire no longer existed in the council room.

The mood had been utterly shaped.

Selene looked at the being known as Kreschenta anew, and felt a chill run down her spine.

If this girl had been their enemy — what then?

"……Thank you, Your Highness."

At Selene's words, Kreschenta shook her head and took her seat.

She surveyed the faces of the corps commanders and their adjutants.

All showed agreement, and when Selene directed her gaze, they nodded quietly.

"At her own peril, Her Highness has spoken. ……I too had originally considered accepting the ceasefire too risky. I wish to borrow Her Highness's strength and choose continued hostilities. Are there objections?"

Voices rose to confirm there were none, and Kokys laughed.

"If Her Highness lends us her strength, then — haha, well well…… Her Highness, Selene-sama, and Krische-sama. With three goddesses at Christand's side, even the goddess of victory herself will be tempted to drop by."

"……That verges on irreverence, Corps Commander Agrand. That said, I concur with the sentiment…… if the threat from the three directions is diminished, we can act without worry."

Terious answered, and glanced at the man beside him — Fourth Corps Commander Elrouga.

With his skeletal face, he too nodded.

"……I was of the view that we should not accept the ceasefire. This flanking maneuver is essentially a surprise attack. Now that the troops have been briefed, any delay risks the enemy learning of it. Let us go."

"Haha, unanimous then."

Krische eyed the booming Kokys with an annoyed look, then nodded to Selene.

Selene nodded in return.

"We will send envoys and resume preparations that had been suspended. Krische, Corps Commander Faren — I'm counting on you. You are the key."

"……Yes, Krische will do her work properly."

"Leave it to me."

Selene took a breath and drained her black soy tea.

* * *

"Good girl, good girl. There you go."

"……Woof."

Back in their room, Kreschenta was being doted on by Krische.

Perched on her lap, clinging to her, petted — and today, fed by Krische herself.

Having been thoroughly washed in the bath as well, Kreschenta's brain had liquefied with pleasure.

The consummate performance of moments ago had vanished without a trace; Selene watched with exasperation.

Bery observed the scene with amusement, pouring Selene another cup of tea.

"……But you're going too, Bery?"

"Yes — help with dressing, meals, and so forth…… there are many ways I can be of use. The escort will be trustworthy members from Krische-sama's century, so it will be fine."

For an audience at the imperial court, a proper attendant would be indispensable.

The options were Bery or Anne.

Given Kreschenta's position, they could not use another household's servants.

And Anne was frankly too uncertain.

Considering how much was riding on this, Bery was the only real choice.

"We'll take about ten from Korintz's squad as escorts. They're more trustworthy than pulling from the other squads."

Krische tightened her hold on Kreschenta's body and lowered her eyes.

Bery had suggested it during their talk in the bath.

It was not as safe as Kreschenta had made it sound in words.

There was no guarantee the Empire would not sell Kreschenta to Gildanstein.

Truthfully, she did not want Bery going on such a journey.

"Mugyu……"

"……Krische is a little worried, after all."

Her arms squeezed tighter, and the embraced Kreschenta was being crushed.

A modest bosom was pressed against her face, and she seemed to be suffocating — her hands lightly tapping Krische's arms.

"I'll be fine. Besides, there is no such thing as a safe place. Everywhere has some kind of danger…… and so it amounts to the same thing."

Bery approached and stroked Krische's cheek.

Kreschenta was flailing.

"And I, too, want to be of help however I can. I want to do everything within my power, just as Krische-sama and the others do."

"Bery……"

"Together again — cooking, having tea…… it's for that."

"……Yes."

Bery kissed her and ran her fingers through Krische's silky hair.

Krische nodded quietly, and Selene, who had been watching, spoke up.

"Um……it's a lovely conversation, but is this alright? Kreschenta is being crushed……"

"Oh……"

When Krische finally noticed and loosened her grip, Kreschenta coughed and sat up.

She held her squashed, reddened nose in both hands, and glared at Krische through teary eyes.

"Uu……how cruel. My nose was almost flattened……"

"Kreschenta, no getting angry. Here, a kiss."

"As if a kiss makes everything better……ehehe."

The moment she was kissed, Kreschenta's mood snapped back and she clung to Krische.

Then she glared at Bery over Krische's shoulder.

"A-ahaha……I'm sorry……"

"……Your sincerity is lacking."

Kreschenta puffed her cheeks, and Krische pinched them and stretched them out.

"……Kreschenta, Krische is entrusting this to you."

"Uu……I know."

"Good girl."

She stroked her head and kissed her again and again.

That Kreschenta had volunteered to take a risk and act had surprised even Krische.

It also made her happy.

She twirled the gold hair with its reddish tinge around her fingers, peering into those moist violet eyes.

"Once everything is over, we'll all have a tea party together. And after that, we'll be together forever and ever. So Krische will do her best. Kreschenta too, please."

"……Yes, onee-sama."

Selene watched the two of them — who would apparently keep kissing forever if left alone — with a dubious look, then spoke.

"More importantly, Kreschenta. Have you prepared your speech?"

"Yes, of course. I just need to do the same thing as in there."

"……You say it like it's easy."

"It actually is easy. I have a position, and I simply use it to give them the words they want to hear. The soldiers' morale is low from the defeat, so what they need is a reason to fight and hope. If I can fool them even temporarily, onee-sama and Selene-sama will handle the rest — won't you?"

"Yes. Though the word 'fool' does bother me."

She sighed and sipped her tea.

To Kreschenta, the soldiers were tools.

She felt no sympathy for their reasons to fight.

But she understood those reasons, and knew how to move them.

And the deaths of those soldiers stirred not a hair's breadth of emotion in her.

She was cold to the last.

As if reading Selene's feelings, Kreschenta touched a fingertip to her lips with a troubled look.

"Well, that's just a matter of word preference. Would you prefer 'inspire,' Selene-sama?"

"……What we do is much the same. I won't deny it."

Conscious or unconscious.

*What's different?* Selene thought.

To rouse soldiers and send them to fight is nothing other than asking them to wager their one and only life.

No matter how you reframe it, you are converting lives into chips for a gamble.

What they did was the same.

But was it arrogance to want them to receive it not merely as logic but as emotion — not reason, but feeling?

If they could, the two of them might move a little closer to normal, and come to feel ordinary thoughts and ordinary happiness.

The two of them were too young to understand that.

Young enough that she feared they might remain that way forever.

In truth, they probably could not distinguish between objects and lives.

She wanted to teach them that — and at the same time, she could not help wondering whether that would truly be good for them.

Because their strength, too, was something gained in exchange for their humanity.

"……Well, perhaps there's no right answer."

"……?"

Even when this war ended, the worries would never cease.

She could see a future in which she'd grow old ruminating endlessly.

"Bery, I'm planning to serve the soldiers something a bit special tomorrow. The day after, everyone will be going their separate ways for a while, so could you prepare a feast?"

"……Yes. Fufu, actually, Krische-sama and I were secretly discussing just that."

"Never miss a trick, do you."

She smiled softly and gazed out the window.

Her mood was strangely settled.

Failure was not an option.

But precisely because of that, there was no longer any need to agonize.

She needed only to do her best at what lay before her.

"……In any case, let's all do our best together. There may be hard and painful times ahead, but……"

*——Beyond that, something good is surely waiting.*

At Selene's words, the three of them nodded quietly.

* * *

——Around the same time.

"Mm, thank you…… You're really quite attentive. Krische is clever, but a bit clumsy about certain things, you see. Having someone as capable as you beside Bery is reassuring. Selene pushes herself too hard as well — I'm sure someone like you is a comfort to her."

"N-no! I'm nothing compared to Argan-sama……!"

"Haha, no need for such modesty. A person who supports from behind the scenes is what matters most. To these old eyes, you look like a fine servant indeed."

"Y-you're too kind……ehehe."

As per usual, Anne — sent away as though being gotten rid of — was being flattered to oblivion by Gallen.


# Chapter 67: Departure

Atop the battlements — there stood three girls and three men.

A girl with golden hair tossing boldly like a horse's tail, a blazing red cloak about her shoulders.

A girl with silver hair bound in two tails, catching the wind in a black cloak.

Ranked beside them were three corps commanders, embodying the martial might of the Christand name.

And standing at the center was a girl notably smaller than all the rest.

As if to declare her purity, she wore a white dress with light gold embroidery.

A deep blue gem speaking of hope hung from her necklace; on her left wrist, a bracelet wrought in the shape of a golden flower.

Adorned with only the minimum of ornament, her appearance could perhaps be called austere for royalty.

And yet her graceful golden hair — the crimson shimmer it cast set her figure ablaze with beauty, tracing the outline of her form.

*— This girl was the child of God, Alberan.*

*The purest jewel in the kingdom, most noble and beautiful of all.*

Below stretched fifteen thousand soldiers.

When the girl stepped forward, they lowered themselves to one knee in unison, rendering the salute of loyal subjects.

A similar sight had taken place not long before.

Then it had been the hero of the Christands standing atop these walls.

It was a moment that forced everyone to recognize anew that the hero was gone.

"— I am Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan. First Princess of the Alberan Kingdom and rightful heir to the throne."

Kreschenta spoke those words with solemnity, then gave a faint smile.

"But such formalities are hardly necessary, are they. …… Everyone, please raise your faces."

A sweet voice that seemed to caress the ear.

Amplified by mana, that voice carried not a trace of pressure or intimidation despite its volume, and arrived as softly as whispered words of endearment at one's very ear.

Her voice had not yet fully matured — more a child's voice than a woman's — yet as it passed through the ear and reached the mind, it carried a resonance impossible to dismiss.

The soldiers showed a moment's hesitation before rising, bringing their right palms to their left chests in salute.

"…… Not long ago, the kingdom's hero — General Christand — stood here …… and he spoke words to you all. Many of you here will remember those words."

*— As do I,* she said, casting her eyes downward.

At those words, there were those who trembled in their bodies and ground their teeth.

"The General said this. …… That he had risen not as a general but as a single citizen — as one who, just as you all, loved his country and loved peace."

Unhurried, neither anxious nor halting.

"All of you gathered here are warriors who stand on the battlefield with nothing but your bare lives laid bare, without ornament. That is why the General stripped away his ornaments and stood before you as a single warrior……"

Kreschenta shook her head as if troubled.

Her voice trembled, yet remained clear.

"…… I was born of royal blood, and have learned much and come to know much. Yet all of it is only for the moving of governance. …… And a speech such as this is no different. Words adorned with jewels like mine could not possibly reach you all."

Then she raised her face slowly.

As if looking at each soldier below in turn.

"…… I do not believe I can do what Margrave Christand, who was a hero of the kingdom, could do. But at the very least I wish, in this place, to be honest — to be as I am, without ornament. As one who, like the General, loves and cherishes peace."

A silence in which no one spoke a word.

Yet every eye was fixed on her.

Feeling this, Kreschenta continued.

"I am going, from here, to the Arna Empire. …… And I intend to ask for their aid."

The key was to give them hope.

As things stood, morale could not be called high.

"I am no warrior, and what I can do lies not on the battlefield — yet there are things I can do. The other day I sent a letter to the Arna Empire stating my wish to visit, and several days ago I received a reply granting permission. This is not a guarantee of their assistance. …… But the granting of permission to visit means nothing less than that the Arna Empire holds that intention."

A quiet murmur of surprise rose from the lips of the soldiers.

Kreschenta smiled inwardly.

In truth, the letter had been one-way.

Sent by fast horse just yesterday, she had no expectation of any reply whatsoever.

It was quite certain that the Arna Empire currently had no intention of aiding them, so it was merely a letter declaring, unilaterally, the intent to visit.

Somewhat presumptuous, but under the circumstances that hardly mattered.

"In this civil war, the Prophetess-Princess of the Arna Empire surely knows which side holds the right. And with the visit now permitted — then the Arna Empire will surely lend us their strength. With that support behind us, there will be nothing left to fear."

The purpose was not aid, but to neutralize the movements of the Empire and others.

The purpose of making this announcement so grandly was as a deterrent to others.

By speaking so openly of the Arna Empire extending support to the princess's faction, the rumor would naturally spread.

Once that happened, those who were currently neutral would be far less likely to extend a hand to Gildanstein.

That was where the aims of this entire occasion converged.

"Our beloved King …… my father, had his life taken by those who scheme to seize the throne by murder, and through vile treachery and conspiracy at the Dragon's Maw the General's life was also taken. After all of this, Gildanstein comes to us demanding a ceasefire. Citing consideration for the people, using a pretense that does not even reach his heart."

And it was also necessary to justify the rejection of the ceasefire.

Righteous cause was always important.

Though it was unavoidable that Krische would be branded a cursed child, Gildanstein's character was by no means admirable, and his ill repute had spread beyond the palace walls.

In that sense, constructing a justification was not difficult.

"…… What manner of heart could allow someone to speak such words? There can be no one who does not know the misdeeds of Gildanstein — of my uncle — who has wielded power to oppress the weak. Inflicting pain and death on people, toying with their hearts…… I once implored him to stop, telling him this was too cruel. What good, I asked, could come of such things."

No such fact existed.

Kreschenta had considered it none of her concern, and indeed had welcomed the fact that her political rival Gildanstein had chased such diversions and damaged his own reputation.

And now it could be put to practical use — so much the better.

"But what came back was a single word: that it was *fun*. For the sake of fleeting, depraved pleasure — in tormenting people, in casting them into the pit of pain and despair …… he feels nothing at all."

On the balance of all past conduct, the advantage lay with her.

The words came easily.

"If that is what it means to be the blood of the royal line, the legitimate heir …… then I do not mind being called a cursed child."

She shook her head and rested her hands on the parapet.

As if to display the image of a pitiable, kind-hearted princess.

"…… I do not demand that you follow me. Only — as those with hearts — I ask that you please, all of you, put a stop to my uncle's …… to Gildanstein's misdeeds. That alone is my wish. …… The future in which that man rules over the kingdom holds nothing but something darker than the bottom of the earth. To leave hope for the new lives yet to blossom in this kingdom — for the future — I beg of you——"

Kreschenta bowed her head, just so.

Without hesitation, without resistance.

Kreschenta's form was perfect, that of a weak — a powerless princess.

Heroic tales spoke often of the hero who rescued the maiden, and it was listening to such stories that boys grew into men, and into soldiers.

For such men, this was a finely crafted story above all others.

Standing tall for the sake of noble honor though pressed to the brink.

The situation she had been given intertwined with the tales they had known since childhood, clinging to them, stirring them to action without their awareness.

"For the sake of Her Highness the Princess — for the sake of the Kingdom!!"

Outside the battlements, someone drew their sword and raised it toward the sky.

It was Dagra.

He had been assigned the role of doing so at an appropriate moment.

The black century immediately responded by raising their swords, repeating the same words.

And that created a wave, spreading outward as though contagious, until every soldier followed suit and repeated the cry.

Selene, watching, felt a mixture of exasperation and genuine admiration — that she could spill out lies so smoothly.

As someone who knew all the facts, Selene had her thoughts — but exasperation was the stronger feeling.

Casting a sidelong glance at Krische, who was apparently impressed, thinking *My, Kreschenta really is good at telling stories,* Selene sighed inwardly, hoping Krische would never become anything like that.

At any rate, it was more than enough as a speech.

She looked toward Kokys.

A man of direct impulses.

Kokys, who seemed to have been genuinely moved by Kreschenta's speech, pressed at the corner of his eye, nodded, then stepped forward and drew the sword at his hip.

"Did you hear!! Her Highness the Princess has shown that she too fights for us! We cannot be the ones to lag behind. Our hero and our lord, our beloved General — he told us always. — *Be a single sword*, he said!"

Kokys's thundering voice shook the air.

Both Kreschenta and Krische, standing right beside him, kept their expressions still with effort, but inwardly they were desperate to cover their ears.

Krische had gone so far as to grip her skirt tightly inside her cloak with both hands.

"We are the sword of the kingdom. — And the General swore to become the sword of Her Highness the Princess who stands here before us. I too renew that oath here and now! Kokys Nakutra Rinea Agrand swears to become the single sword that cuts down every enemy that stands in the path of Her Highness the Princess!!"

Kokys swung his sword and declared it.

"And what of all of you! Will you shrink in fear of defeat and return to being ordinary men, or will you become that same sword!?"

A roar like a battle cry echoed through the fortress.

* * *

"Nn……"

When the speech ended and some time had passed, Bery and Kreschenta would be setting out.

Krische, too, would be leaving the fortress today for the flanking maneuver.

That it should come to this before a departure was, in a sense, only to be expected.

Krische was kissing Bery again and again, without end.

Krische, perched in Bery's lap with her arms wrapped around her, continuing her kisses.

Selene watching it with an expression that defied description.

Kreschenta glaring at Krische with evident dissatisfaction.

Anne, who had taken over the packing in Bery's stead, was as usual too distracted by the spectacle to focus on her work.

This state of affairs had continued for nearly a quarter-hour.

"……Goodness, honestly."

"Ah……"

Selene lifted Krische bodily from behind and settled her onto her own lap.

"I know you'll miss her. But it's nearly time for you to leave as well. If you keep clinging like that, Bery and the others will be late departing."

"Uuu……"

Krische still gazed at Bery with longing in her eyes.

The storm of passionate kisses.

Bery, her face flushed red as though light-headed, scratched her cheek with a troubled smile, unsure what to do.

Then she stood and brought her face close.

"That …… Krische-sama. This is not a parting for the rest of your lives. And so, the rest can wait until I return …… and then, when I do, please give me plenty more."

A soft *chu*, a light kiss, and she stroked Krische's head.

"So the story goes, love deepens in the time spent apart. I am certain that the kiss at that moment will be exceptional — a truly wonderful kiss unlike any before."

"…… Exceptional?"

"Yes. Brimming over with so much love, a very happy kiss."

Bery said this while kissing Krische again, as if reluctant to part.

She repeated it twice, three times, placed her hands on Krische's cheeks, caressed them.

Looking into those eyes, tracing those lips, Bery narrowed her moistening gaze and drew her face closer and closer——

"……Stop that."

"Aww!"

Selene tapped her on the forehead.

"Uuu, even from ojou-sama, to be hit……!"

"Look, what are *you* doing, joining in like that, really……"

Selene told Bery in exasperation, and Krische, deprived of the kiss she had been so close to receiving, jutted out her lips in displeasure.

Selene pinched those lips between her fingers and announced, *that's enough now.*

"If you're going to act like perfect fools, save it for when you're back home. Honestly."

"That is exactly right! Argan-sama has all the……  even I am about to be separated from onee-sama and yet……"

Kreschenta clung to Krische.

But this time Selene tapped Kreschenta on the forehead.

"What are you doing! I — I am a princess, you know!"

"Then kindly show some princely composure …… honestly, if you keep carrying on like this, we'll never get you off."

"Uuu…… Argan-sama gets to have everything."

Why must she be the one doing all this.

Selene sighed and pulled Krische's cheeks apart.

Krische let out a squashed little cry.

"Now then. Both of you, finish getting ready. And then I'll let you have just one more."

"……Since when did onee-sama's kisses fall under Selene-sama's permission system? You are saying this intending to keep her all to yourself, aren't you."

"……I am not."

# Chapter 70: The Back of the Back

Klare Markelus.

Sixty years of age — a lean man, a noble in name, beginning to show a degree of age.

He wore an elegant suit of armor passed down through generations, and surveyed from horseback the tens of thousands of troops spreading across the plain.

The sky was clear; thin clouds tore apart.

The wind was at their back — not bad, the man smiled.

The imposing figure of a general. A certain dignity resided there.

His virtues lay in his decisiveness and his courage.

A decision that others feared and anguished over — he did not fear it.

A model of the warrior's spirit — his men knew it well, and he possessed the qualities of a celebrated general who commanded genuine respect from them.

But his fault, if one could name it, was a nature that could not think things through deeply.

A man who, as a noble, held honor dear, disdained petty stratagems, and favored frontal battles.

If he alone were that way, it could not be called a fatal flaw.

In a situation of numerical superiority, he had the strength to secure victory.

But he had the bad habit of expecting the same from his adversaries — and of believing it to be so.

On the whole, one could describe him as not quite a fool, but a middling general with blind spots.

Were he a battalion commander or centurion, he would no doubt have been a supremely capable man.

Such was the assessment he deserved.

But for him, born as the eldest son of the Markelus Ducal house connected to the royal bloodline, such a path was not permitted; he inherited the general's rank as his father had.

In peacetime, that would have been fine enough.

But in war, it was a tragedy.

Against Nozan Velraigh, who had climbed through battle alone on talent alone, Klare Markelus was simply not the right man.

Whether the failure to finish Nozan off over these three days, despite numerical advantage, was to be attributed to Markelus's incompetence or to Nozan's excessive capability — that was a difficult question — but looking at the result, one should probably conclude that what separated the two was very significant.

Eyes lit with a wild grin bereft of reason, that axe-like curved sword bisected the soldier before her.

——Bisected. Truly.

From shoulder to side, cut through armor in a single stroke, and she was laughing, bathed in blood.

And from behind her the black-armored soldiers appeared.

The line of handpicked reserves was being cut through like butter.

"……!?"

Far too quick.

These were clearly not ordinary soldiers.

Their weapons were not even unified.

Some bore curved swords, great swords, axes, and iron maces.

Drenched in torn flesh and blood, they appeared.

*—— What in the world are those.*

Arriving before the voices of those around him was a shout from the left flank.

The cavalry.

Driving their mounts at a pace that wore down the horses' lives, they were racing back to his aid.

Dozens of cavalry — not many in number.

Some cavalry even had their horses fall from the sheer exhaustion.

Yet in this situation a cavalry charge could turn the tide.

*I will personally reward every one of them who survives,* Klare swore to himself — but from the black soldiers shot a single shadow.

Dancing twin silver braids.

Something that raced faster than the cavalry's charge passed through the line.

A beat late, countless sprays of blood burst out, and the lead cavalry fell.

Whether it was deliberate or by chance.

In a chain, the horses that followed toppled, and the cavalry's charge impetus was entirely lost.

"Korintz! Deal with the cavalry aftermath!"

"Sir!! Squads eleven through sixteen — our corps commander has set the table! A tasty quarry — scatter it!!"

At the commander's voice, part of the black soldiers burst forward.

Klare's eyes went wide at this too.

Their speed of thrust and jump — none of it was that of ordinary people.

Twenty-some who broke out — all of them mana-users.

And looking again, the weapons they carried were mostly heavy objects.

Great swords and great axes that an ordinary person could not wield.

*—— Surely not, every one of these black-armored soldiers——*

The half-certain realization sent despair flickering through Klare's mind.

Screams rang out from all around him.

His remaining escorts numbered only several dozen — and those screams were from those very men.

Within the black wave, silver was dancing.

For an instant those violet eyes and his met.

This was the second time.

Krische Krishtand.

The ward of Bogan Krishtand.

At the victory ceremony the other day he had glimpsed her from a distance in a dress.

A girl with a somehow doll-like, serene beauty.

He had heard the talk of her extraordinary achievements against the Empire.

But he had taken it with a grain of salt.

With her eye-catching appearance, something of 1 would be heard as 10.

At best, she had probably made some serviceable movements at the front.

Being the general's daughter, she would be lionized regardless.

*—— But that is not it.*

You knew upon seeing her.

What stood before him was clearly an alien thing.

Surrounding her were the cream of the army.

Not merely dozens of soldiers, but dozens selected from thirty thousand.

Yet the girl's blade, dancing freely in every direction across the battlefield, claimed a life without fail each time it flashed.

The black-armored soldiers were certainly extraordinary fighters, but she alone was clearly aberrant.

No helm, the one-piece dress visible beneath her cloak.

Aside from gauntlets and thick boots, she wore nothing that qualified as armor.

That was enough — this was a battlefield, and she did not believe for a moment she could sustain even a graze.

Those long braids swaying like tails behind her, that cloak swirling — she believed it impossible even for those to be seized.

What was visible there, undisguised, was an absolute, arrogant confidence.

*—— That was a monster wearing the shape of a girl.*

Even as he trembled, Klare gripped his spear as though he might crush it, swinging it around.

"Whoever wishes to take this head, state your name!! This Klare Markelus will be your opponent personally!!"

He shouted to kill the fear that had risen in him.

He no longer desired to live, standing here as he was. If he was to be killed, let it be a warrior's death, at least.

Stubbornly holding to his honor as a noble, he moved forward.

Those violet eyes blazed — and their gazes crossed.

The monster narrowed her eyes slightly and stepped in.

"General!!"

"Dora……!"

His adjutant Dora slipped her body in to shield Klare——

"Out of bunny's way!"

"——Ah, uh……!?"

But from the side appeared that black-haired wild grin.

Dora's body was bisected through the armor by that axe-like curved sword.

The black-haired woman drove the momentum, slamming Dora's corpse into the earth — and then the space between everything disappeared.

The girl had already stepped in.

For Klare, the last spear of his life — and the last stroke he would ever make as a warrior.

No hesitation; the same motion he had repeated countless times, he wielded his spear.

Noble-born, trained since earliest childhood in blood-and-sweat drilling.

Honed in the killing ground, refined in blood and flesh, this spear had pierced countless lives.

Today the spear was in brilliant form —— but the girl slid cleanly beneath it.

A low driving step, almost grazing the earth.

She had completely transcended Klare's imagination.

Before thought could move his spear he worked it, turning the butt toward the girl — and drove with full force.

But the girl's movement, that suppleness, was like a serpent coiling around the spear.

As if it posed no obstacle at all, the girl closed the distance.

Winding around the shaft.

Twisting her body as if running up the shaft to vault upward — landing on the horse's neck, right before Klare's eyes, the girl smiled.

"……How do you do."

"……!?"

The impact punched through his torso.

He understood he had been kicked off only as he tumbled through the air, upon hearing the words that followed.

"My name is Krische. Farewell."

A smile like a flower blooming on that beautiful face.

Krische introduced herself as Klare had desired, and bid him farewell.

Klare would make no answer to those words.

He would never speak again.

His neck was already split down the middle by her curved sword.

Kicked off as he was, the blood that spurted from Klare's neck did not dirty that girl — not a single drop.

The girl showed no further interest in the corpse.

She only toyed with those pure silver braids with her fingers, and surveyed everything around her.

Black cloak, snow-white skin, beautiful silver hair.

Seated atop the horse, the girl drew every eye without exception.

The men who had rushed out to save the general froze solid, and the black-armored century cut them down.

Kalua brought her sword down on the general's fallen head and severed it completely.

Then she speared it on the blade's tip and hoisted it high above her head.

"Behold this head! Your general has been slain by Krische Krishtand, First Corps Commander of the Christand Army!! Resistance is now futile — lay down your swords and surrender!!"

Kalua's voice rang out.

Less than one *koku* (T/N: 一刻 ≈ roughly two hours) since the clash with Velraigh had begun — the heads and helm suddenly raised made the men, one by one, lower their swords.

"You will not be harmed if you surrender without resistance! Though we have crossed blades, we are all citizens of the kingdom — now that your general is gone, battle is meaningless! Sheathe your swords peaceably and surrender!!"

The next voice was Dagra's.

The Christand soldiers raised a cheer that rang across the battlefield and raised countless banners of the falcon and lightning.

A battle once begun — its heat did not cool all at once. But the screams and the sound of steel that had been ringing out were gradually being painted over by cheers.

Though some fought on with desperate resistance, the great majority had lost the reason to stake their lives on it, and before that voice, knelt down one after another.

"Ha ha, a rare and complete victory. As expected of Krische-sama."

"Gaikotsu's performance was excellent. You are very good at opening the path."

Disarming the enemy around them of their weapons, Krische smiled up at Elrouga on horseback.

Elrouga wore a pleased evil smile and surveyed the enemies around them.

A death-god-like old man and a monster-like girl.

The soldiers of the Markelus army who watched them were trembling.

"Hagawashi, Mia, that was not bad movement. If you can keep up today's level from here on, Krische will be at ease."

"Sir!"

"And Kalua, well done. Hagawashi, Kalua did excellent work this time, please handle it appropriately. Fourth squad and ninth squad were moving quite well too, in a low-key way, I thought——"

As Krische offhandedly praised the soldiers' performances, cutting through the line of soldiers appeared a single man.

A helm and armor in the shape of a wolf, stained with blood.

In his hand he carried a great war mace longer than he was tall.

The man removed his helm and bent his knee.

"……Magnificent work, Krische-sama."

"With the situation as well prepared as it was, anyone could have managed it easily. Corps Commander Vakus."

"No, no — to do it so effortlessly as that is owing entirely to Krische-sama's abilities. I had thought I would devour them first …… but I cannot compete."

The scar on his face pulled taut as the man — Granmeld Vakus — laughed.

Then he slung the great war mace over his shoulder and stood.

"With that build of yours and that sword, it truly is something, how easily you do this. I thought so in the last battle too — what you do is outside my understanding."

"Krische has never found killing people difficult …… if they come at her, one need only lightly pare away the flesh, so it is simple."

Krische wiped the blood from her curved sword in rough strokes on a dead man's trousers, then took a cloth from her pouch and wiped away the remaining blood carefully.

Not a single chip on the thin blade.

Granmeld looked at her sword with a mix of amazement and exasperation.

How many men could he himself cut down with this thin sword — it would not exceed ten.

"A terrifying woman. …… Krische-sama, Corps Commander Faren. For now, to the main command post. The General is waiting there. I would like to briefly discuss future plans."

Krische sheathed her sword and nodded, then looked at Elrouga.

"……Indeed. Gaikotsu, shall we go?"

"Yes. It is a bit rushed, but……"

"……G-Gaikotsu?"

Granmeld stared at Elrouga in astonishment.

Elrouga met that gaze and glared.

"……What is it, Granmeld. Or should I now call you Corps Commander Vakus?"

"Ha, ha ha …… either one is fine."

Granmeld set off leading the two at the front, and quietly shook his shoulders.

Elrouga rubbed at the corner of his eye, and Krische was tilting her head in puzzlement.
