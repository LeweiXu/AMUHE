# Chapter 66: A Choice

"……A ceasefire. What a joke."

——An envoy from Gildanstein had arrived roughly three weeks after the battle at the Dragon's Maw.

The envoy's proposal was a ceasefire.

The faces in the council room were grim.

"Let us refuse, Selene-sama. There's nothing to deliberate."

"……Corps Commander Agrand, calm yourself. It is not so simple."

Third Corps Commander Terrius rebuked Kokys.

The reason for their grim expressions was that the ceasefire proposal, whatever its intent, rested on sound reasoning.

"However……"

"What Corps Commander Melkikos says is correct. ……If we refuse, we also stand to lose a great deal."

Gallen spoke and drained his black soy tea in a single gulp.

Without looking at the adjutant pouring a fresh cup, he continued.

Deep furrows sat between his brows.

"The ceasefire period runs from the coming harsh winter through to harvest. Whatever the intent, it is well considered."

War generates enormous expense.

That burden falls naturally on the people.

Food, and the labor of soldiers taken from the workforce.

The argument that from spring through the end of summer — the peak of agriculture — they should sheathe their swords for the people's sake was perfectly reasonable on principle.

A blunt refusal risked the princess's faction losing the public's trust.

Most nations didn't wage war from spring to summer.

Most campaigns were launched from autumn into winter — precisely to avoid draining the labor force and weakening the state.

The timing was earlier than customary, but on its merits, the proposal was sound.

This civil war had broken out suddenly and at a poor time, placing heavy burdens on the people — granting them rest was, in a sense, not a bad thing.

The problem was that it would freeze their movements through winter.

"……That puts the flanking maneuver on hold, then?"

No one answered Krische's words.

They had not simply been sitting idle after losing the Dragon's Maw.

At a recent council, a strategy for retaking it had been settled.

Krische would make a wide flanking sweep around Bernaich.

Crossing the mountains, she would coordinate with Nozan Velraigh in the east for a simultaneous strike, advance into the center, take the Dragon's Maw from behind, and crush the enemy in a pincer with Selene's forces holding here.

A single decisive blow to break through the formidable Dragon's Maw.

The west — Hilkintos — the south — Garhka — and the north — the Arna Empire — had all yet to declare allegiance, and the reason was unclear.

Was it a matter of conscience — trying to discern which side was just, as nobles loyal to the kingdom?

Or wariness, because Christand still retained strength?

Their letters cited defense concerns as the reason they could not move, but their true intentions were unknowable.

The Arna Empire, as an ally, hoped for a swift resolution, but had sent back a boilerplate reply: *'Opinions are divided regarding intervention in another nation's internal affairs. We pray for your fortunes in war.'*

Before they chose a flag.

If they were to enter the war now, Bogan's loss made it more likely they would side with Gildanstein.

They needed to strike back before Gildanstein fully consolidated the capital.

But Gildanstein had framed the ceasefire as being for the people's sake.

If the fence-sitters were merely looking for a pretext to join, refusing could be the trigger.

"A counterattack is desirable. But……it's a difficult problem."

Even within Krische, who had been set to depart in a few days, emotion and reason were at war.

If the operation were canceled, Krische would not have to leave Bery and the others.

Even so, her reason had concluded that they should attack now.

Slowly, like a silk noose drawing tight, Christand was being cornered.

She could feel it.

Krische looked at Kreschenta.

Wearing her princess's face, Kreschenta noticed Krische's gaze and let her eyes waver.

Kreschenta too seemed uncertain — and seeing that, Krische understood this was a politically difficult problem, and fell into thought.

Selene spoke.

"Krische, you estimated that the capital operation would take until late spring at the earliest?"

"Yes. There's a chance it might drag on a little, but……Krische thinks it should be resolved by then."

Krische looked at the map and pointed to the Dragon's Maw.

"At the earliest, early spring……if everything goes perfectly and smoothly, before winter sets in. If we're talking about the Dragon's Maw alone, faster, but……"

If Gildanstein committed to a thorough delaying action and avoided decisive battle, even Krische could not judge how long things might drag on.

"……I see. But if we're going to move General Velraigh, we must see it through to the end in one push. We can't predict when the Empire might move again."

Selene sipped her black soy tea and sighed.

"One thing is certain. His Highness the Prince will use the ceasefire to consolidate the center. If we accept it, we'll face the full force of the capital next — simply agreeing would be the path to defeat. If we do accept, we must also prepare the conditions to confront and overcome that army."

The downsides of refusing were large.

But the downsides of accepting were equally large.

"Corps Commander Agrand favors continued hostilities. Is that your view as well, Krische?"

"……Victory would end it all."

If they won.

But certainties never exist.

Otherwise, they would already have advanced into the capital region.

That was why the council room was heavy with tension.

"……Let us narrow down the concerns."

Kreschenta raised her voice and stood.

"West and south, and the Arna Empire to the north — if even one of them moves, the balance tips dramatically. The concern is that in the current situation they would likely side against us, and that refusing the proposal might trigger their entry. Is that correct?"

"Yes, precisely, Your Highness."

Selene nodded.

Inwardly, she was uneasy.

She didn't know what Kreschenta was about to say.

"I will travel to the Arna Empire as an envoy."

"……An envoy? To request their assistance?"

"Yes. Ostensibly."

The assembled commanders furrowed their brows, gazing at the princess's young and striking profile.

"Whether requesting aid will succeed, I cannot say with certainty. But if I visit personally, as royalty, the Empire cannot simply ignore me. If we notify them of the visit before they declare a position, they will at the very least be unable to take military action — don't you think?"

Krische's eyes widened slightly, and seeing that, Kreschenta smiled.

Their gazes met for only an instant.

"……The martial reputation of Margrave Christand was great, and for that reason, the Empire could not side with either faction. But now that he is lost, the Empire is most likely to move. Preventing that alone would be a great help."

Kreschenta said this and shaped her face into sorrow.

She lowered her eyes quietly, drawing her well-formed brows together — her lovely face all the more striking for it.

Every gaze in the council room was drawn to that beauty.

"……I am nothing more than a banner. I cannot offer much by staying here. For my sake, you are all risking your lives in battle. For that sake, the beloved Margrave himself gave his life."

She understood precisely what effect her voice and expression had on those around her.

She drew their gazes and minds toward her like a tongue pulling thread — playing with them.

There was some hesitation about exploiting Bogan's death in front of Selene.

But her intelligence existed to seek results.

To fulfill the role her sister desired, to carry out what her sister expected.

And those aligned with the results she herself sought.

So she would use everything.

That was the creature Kreschenta was.

"I will go to the Empire and extend the negotiations as long as possible. At least until spring. We may even announce that the Empire is on our side. That alone would serve as a deterrent against the western and southern generals. In the meantime, I ask that all of you here put a stop to His Highness the Prince's — my uncle's — tyranny."

A trembling voice, yet strong.

Her violet eyes wavered, glistening, and a small tear fell.

Kreschenta wiped it and raised her face.

"My father — the earnest and noble king — had his life taken by that ambition. And now the kingdom's hero, the loyal margrave, has also been slain through cowardice. If that beast-like ambition consumes the whole kingdom, what will become of its future? A ceasefire for the people's sake — all of it is nothing more than words born of wicked desire……it cannot be trusted."

No one moved to stop her.

For just a moment.

In that brief span, Kreschenta had seized the room.

Before the weeping princess, the option of accepting a ceasefire had ceased to exist in the council room.

The mood had been completely shaped.

Selene looked at the being known as Kreschenta anew, and felt a chill run down her spine.

If this girl had been their enemy — what then?

"……Thank you, Your Highness."

At Selene's words, Kreschenta shook her head and took her seat.

She surveyed the faces of the corps commanders and their adjutants.

All showed agreement, and when Selene directed her gaze at them, they nodded quietly.

"At her own peril, Her Highness has spoken. ……I too had originally found the ceasefire too risky to accept. I wish to borrow Her Highness's strength and choose continued hostilities. Any objections?"

Voices rose to confirm there were none, and Kokys laughed.

"If Her Highness lends us her strength, then — haha, well well…… Her Highness, Selene-sama, and Krische-sama. With three goddesses at Christand's side, even the goddess of victory herself will be tempted to drop by."

"……That borders on irreverence, Corps Commander Agrand. That said, I concur with the sentiment…… if the threat from three directions is diminished, we can act freely."

Terrius answered, and glanced at the man beside him — Fourth Corps Commander Elrouga.

With his skeletal face, he too nodded.

"……I was of the view that we should not accept the ceasefire. This flanking maneuver is essentially a surprise attack. Now that the troops have been briefed, any delay risks the enemy learning of it. Let us move."

"Haha, unanimous then."

Krische eyed the booming Kokys with mild irritation, then nodded to Selene.

Selene nodded back.

"We will send envoys and resume the suspended preparations. Krische, Corps Commander Faren — I'm counting on you. You are the key."

"……Yes, Krische will do her work properly."

"Leave it to me."

Selene took a breath and drained her black soy tea.

* * *

"Good girl, good girl. There you go."

"……Woof."

Back in their room, Kreschenta was being doted on by Krische.

Perched on her lap, clinging to her, petted — and today, fed by Krische herself.

Having been thoroughly washed in the bath as well, Kreschenta's mind had melted with pleasure.

The consummate performance of moments ago had vanished without a trace; Selene watched with exasperation.

Bery observed the scene with amusement, pouring Selene another cup of tea.

"……But you're going too, Bery?"

"Yes — help with dressing, meals, and so forth…… there are many ways I can be of use. The escort will be trustworthy members from Krische-sama's century, so it will be fine."

For an audience at the imperial court, a proper attendant was indispensable.

The options were Bery or Anne.

Given Kreschenta's position, they could not borrow another household's servants.

And Anne was frankly too unreliable.

Considering how much was riding on this, Bery was the only real choice.

"We'll take about ten from Korintz's squad as escorts. They're more trustworthy than pulling from the other squads."

Krische tightened her hold on Kreschenta and lowered her eyes.

Bery had suggested it during their talk in the bath.

It was not as safe as Kreschenta had made it sound.

There was no guarantee the Empire would not hand Kreschenta to Gildanstein.

Truthfully, she did not want Bery making such a journey.

"Mugyu……"

"……Krische is a little worried, after all."

Her arms squeezed tighter, and the embraced Kreschenta was being crushed.

A modest bosom pressed against her face, and she seemed to be suffocating — her hands lightly tapping Krische's arms.

"I'll be fine. Besides, there is no such thing as a safe place. Everywhere has some kind of danger…… so it all amounts to the same."

Bery stepped closer and stroked Krische's cheek.

Kreschenta was flailing.

"And I, too, want to help however I can. I want to do everything within my power, just as Krische-sama and the others do."

"Bery……"

"Together again — cooking, having tea…… that's what it's all for."

"……Yes."

Bery kissed her and ran her fingers through Krische's silky hair.

Krische nodded quietly, and Selene, who had been watching, spoke up.

"Um……it's a lovely conversation, but is this alright? Kreschenta is being crushed……"

"Oh……"

When Krische finally noticed and loosened her grip, Kreschenta coughed and sat up.

She held her squashed, reddened nose in both hands and glared at Krische through teary eyes.

"Uu……how cruel. My nose was nearly flattened……"

"Kreschenta, no getting angry. Here, a kiss."

"As if a kiss fixes everything……ehehe."

The moment she was kissed, Kreschenta's mood snapped back and she clung to Krische.

Then she glared at Bery over Krische's shoulder.

"A-ahaha……I'm sorry……"

"……Your sincerity is lacking."

Kreschenta puffed her cheeks, and Krische pinched and stretched them out.

"……Kreschenta, Krische is entrusting this to you."

"Uu……I know."

"Good girl."

She stroked her head and kissed her again and again.

That Kreschenta had volunteered to take a risk surprised even Krische.

It also made her happy.

She twirled the gold hair with its reddish tinge around her fingers, looking into those moist violet eyes.

"Once everything is over, we'll all have a tea party together. And after that, we'll be together forever and ever. So Krische will do her best. Kreschenta too, please."

"……Yes, onee-sama."

Selene watched the two of them — who would apparently keep kissing indefinitely if left alone — with a dubious look, then spoke.

"More importantly, Kreschenta. Have you prepared your speech?"

"Yes, of course. I just need to do the same thing as in there."

"……You say it like it's simple."

"It actually is simple. I have a position, and I use it to give them the words they want to hear. The soldiers' morale is low from the defeat, so what they need is a reason to fight and hope. If I can fool them even temporarily, onee-sama and Selene-sama will handle the rest — won't you?"

"Yes. Though the word 'fool' does bother me."

She sighed and sipped her tea.

To Kreschenta, the soldiers were tools.

She felt no sympathy for their reasons to fight.

But she understood those reasons, and knew how to move them.

And the deaths of those soldiers did not stir so much as a hair's breadth of emotion in her.

Cold to the last.

As if reading Selene's feelings, Kreschenta touched a fingertip to her lips with a troubled look.

"Well, that's just a matter of word choice. Would you prefer 'inspire,' Selene-sama?"

"……What we do is much the same. I won't deny it."

Conscious or unconscious.

*What's different?* Selene thought.

To rouse soldiers and send them to fight is nothing other than asking them to wager their one and only life.

No matter how you reframe it, you are converting lives into chips for a gamble.

What they did was the same.

But was it arrogance to want them to receive it not merely as logic but as feeling — not reason, but emotion?

If they could, the two of them might draw a little closer to normal, and come to know ordinary thoughts and ordinary happiness.

The two of them were too young for that.

Young enough that she feared they might remain that way forever.

In truth, they probably could not distinguish between objects and lives.

She wanted to teach them that — and at the same time, could not help wondering whether it would truly be good for them.

Because their strength, too, was something gained in exchange for their humanity.

"……Well, perhaps there's no right answer."

"……?"

Even when this war ended, the worries would never cease.

She could already see a future in which she'd grow old ruminating over them endlessly.

"Bery, I'm planning to serve the soldiers something a bit special tomorrow. The day after, everyone will be going their separate ways for a while, so could you prepare a feast?"

"……Yes. Fufu, actually, Krische-sama and I were quietly discussing just that."

"Never miss a trick, do you."

She smiled softly and gazed out the window.

Her mood had settled, strangely.

Failure was not an option.

But precisely because of that, there was nothing left to agonize over.

She needed only to do her best at what lay before her.

"……In any case, let's all do our best together. There may be hard and painful times ahead, but……"

*——Beyond that, something good is surely waiting.*

At Selene's words, the three of them nodded quietly.

* * *

——Around the same time.

"Mm, thank you…… You're really quite attentive. Krische is clever, but a bit oblivious about certain things, you see. Having someone as capable as you beside Bery is reassuring. Selene pushes herself too hard as well — I'm sure someone like you is a comfort to her."

"N-no! I'm nothing compared to Argan-sama……!"

"Haha, no need for such modesty. The person who supports from behind the scenes is what matters most. To these old eyes, you look like a fine servant indeed."

"Y-you're too kind……ehehe."

As per usual, Anne — sent away as though being gotten rid of — was being flattered to oblivion by Gallen.

# Chapter 67: Departure

Atop the castle walls――there stood three girls and three men.

A girl whose golden hair swayed heroically like a horse's tail, draped in a blazing red mantle.

A girl with silver-shining hair tied in twin tails, fluttering in the wind, clad in a black cloak.

Beside her stood the three corps commanders who embodied Christand's martial might.

And standing in the center was a girl of particularly small stature.

As if to display her purity, she wore a white dress with light gold embroidery.

A necklace set with a deep blue jewel signifying hope, and on her left hand a bracelet fashioned in the shape of a golden flower.

Her appearance, adorned with only the barest minimum of decoration, might have even seemed austere for one of royal blood.

Yet that elegant golden hair — the crimson shimmer radiating from it — made her figure endlessly beautiful, her silhouette standing out against the world.

――The girl was Alberan's child of God.

In the kingdom, she was its purest jewel, nobler and more beautiful than any other.

Below her lay fifteen thousand soldiers.

When the girl stepped forward, they knelt as one and offered the salute of vassals.

A similar scene had taken place not long ago.

Atop this very fortress had stood the hero Christand in her place.

Everyone was forced once more to recognize that the hero was gone.

"――I am Kreschenta Farna Viera Alberan. First princess of the Alberan Kingdom, and rightful heir to the throne."

Kreschenta said this solemnly, then smiled faintly.

"Though such formalities are unnecessary, I think. ……Everyone, please raise your faces."

A sweet voice that seemed to brush the ear.

Amplified by mana, it carried neither pressure nor intimidation despite its volume; it resonated like an endearment murmured close.

Her voice was still a child's more than a woman's, undeveloped and pre-pubescent, yet the sound that reached the mind carried an undeniable resonance.

The soldiers hesitated a moment before rising and assuming the salute, right hands pressed to chest.

"……The other day, standing here was the hero of the kingdom――General Christand……and he spoke words to all of you. Many among you surely remember those words."

*So do I*, she said, lowering her eyes.

At those words, some trembled and ground their teeth.

"The General said this: ……that he had risen not as a general, but as a single citizen, one who loved his country and loved peace, no different from all of you."

Slowly, without rushing, without faltering.

"All of you here are warriors who stake your bare lives upon the battlefield. And so the General cast aside his own adornments and stood before you as a single warrior……"

Kreschenta shook her head as if troubled.

Her voice trembled, yet stayed clear.

"……Born as I was into royalty, I have studied much and learned much. But all of that was merely for the affairs of state. ……This kind of speech is the same. Words adorned with jewels would surely never reach you."

And then she slowly raised her face.

As if gazing upon each and every one of the soldiers below.

"……I do not think I can do as Margrave Christand, the kingdom's hero, did. But I want to be honest――at least here and now, without adornment, to speak from the heart. As one who loves and cherishes peace, the same as the General."

A silence in which not a single person spoke.

Yet every gaze was fixed on her.

Sensing that, Kreschenta continued to weave her words.

"I am going now to the Arna Empire. ……And I shall request their assistance."

The key was to give them hope.

As things stood, morale could not truly be called high.

"There is nothing I, who am no warrior, can do upon the battlefield, yet there are things I can do. The other day I sent a letter expressing my wish to visit the Arna Empire, and several days ago I received a reply granting it. It does not guarantee their assistance to our cause. ……However, the fact that they have granted a visit can only mean that the Arna Empire has the will to do so."

A quiet murmur of "Ohh" welled up from the soldiers.

Kreschenta smiled inwardly.

In truth, the letter had been one-way.

She had only sent it by express rider yesterday, and had no expectation of a reply whatsoever.

It was certain that the Arna Empire had no intention of helping them at present, so she had simply sent a letter unilaterally announcing that she would be visiting.

It was somewhat rude, but at this point that hardly mattered.

"The priestess-princess of the Arna Empire surely knows which side holds the right in this civil war. And now that a visit has been granted――the Arna Empire will surely lend us their strength. With their support, there would be nothing left to fear."

The goal was not assistance, but to seal off the Empire's movements and those of others.

The purpose of announcing it so grandly was deterrence.

If the rumor that the Arna Empire was supporting the princess's faction spread this widely, it would naturally travel far and wide.

If that happened, the likelihood of currently neutral parties lending aid to Gildanstein would decrease.

That was the sole purpose of this occasion.

"Our beloved King……my father's life was taken and the throne usurped, and at the Dragon's Maw the General's life was taken through a cowardly betrayal. And on top of that, Gildanstein has demanded a ceasefire. Hiding behind hollow words like 'for the sake of the people.'"

She also needed to give them justification for rejecting the ceasefire.

A just cause is always important.

It was unavoidable that Krische would be labeled a cursed child, but Gildanstein's character could hardly be called virtuous either, and his infamy had spread well beyond the royal court.

In that sense, the justification was easy to construct.

"……What kind of heart allows one to speak such words? Everyone knows of Gildanstein's――of my uncle's evil deeds, how he has used his power to oppress the weak. Tormenting people, killing them, toying with their hearts……I once pleaded with him to stop, asking him what could possibly be gained from such cruelty."

No such thing had ever happened, of course.

Kreschenta had not cared about it in the slightest, and in fact had welcomed her political rival Gildanstein indulging in such entertainments and ruining his own reputation.

After all, it could be put to use like this, so what could be better.

"But……what came back was a single word: 'It's fun.' For the sake of fleeting, deranged pleasure, he tortures people, casting them into the abyss of agony and despair……that man feels nothing at all."

Given his prior conduct, the advantage lay firmly with her.

Weaving words was easy.

"If that is what royal blood — what legitimacy — means……then I do not mind being called a cursed child."

She shook her head and placed her hand on the parapet.

As if to present the figure of a pitiful, kindhearted princess.

"……I do not ask you to obey me. I only ask that, as people with hearts, you stop my uncle's……Gildanstein's atrocities. That is my only wish. ……In a future where that man rules the kingdom, only something darker than the deepest pit will remain. For the sake of new lives that will bloom in the kingdom, for the future, for hope――please."

Kreschenta bowed her head then and there.

Without hesitation, without resistance.

Kreschenta's figure was the perfect image of a frail, powerless princess.

Heroic tales often tell of heroes who rescue princesses, and it was by hearing such stories that boys grew into men, and then into soldiers.

For them, this was a supremely well-crafted story.

Rising in the face of adversity for the sake of noble honor.

The scenario they had been given intertwined and entangled with the tales they had heard since childhood, unconsciously rousing them to action.

"For Her Highness the Princess, for the kingdom!!"

Outside the castle walls, a man drew his sword and thrust it toward the sky.

It was Dagra.

He had been assigned the role of doing so at the right moment.

The black century immediately followed, raising their swords and repeating the same words.

And it became a wave, spreading outward, until every soldier raised his sword and echoed the cry.

Selene was half exasperated, half impressed at how fluently the girl could lie.

Knowing all the facts as she did, Selene had her thoughts on the matter, but exasperation won out.

Glancing sideways at Krische, who seemed genuinely impressed and was saying things like *Kreschenta really is good at telling stories*, Selene sighed inwardly, hoping Krische would never turn out like that.

In any case, it was more than sufficient as a speech.

She glanced at Kokys.

A straightforward man.

Kokys, apparently moved in earnest by Kreschenta's speech, pressed at the corners of his eyes and nodded, then stepped forward and drew the sword at his hip.

"Did you hear that!! Her Highness the Princess has shown that she too fights for our sake! We cannot fail to follow her lead. Our hero, our lord, the General we so revered, always said this: ――Be but a single blade!"

Kokys's thunderous voice shook the air itself.

Both Kreschenta and Krische, standing right beside him, made every effort not to let it show on their faces, but they desperately wanted to cover their ears.

Krische, for her part, was clenching her skirt tightly beneath her cloak.

"We are the kingdom's blades. ――And the General swore to become Her Highness the Princess's blade. I too swear anew here! Kokys Nakutra Rinea Agrand shall become a single blade to strike down every enemy that stands in Her Highness's path!!"

Kokys flourished his sword and declared:

"What of you?! Will you cower in fear of defeat and return to being ordinary men, or will you become blades as well!?"

A roar of cheering, like a war cry, echoed through the fortress.

"Nn……"

Once the speeches were over, Bery and Kreschenta would soon depart.

Krische too was leaving the fortress today for the flanking maneuver.

In the room before departure, it was, in a sense, only natural.

Krische had been endlessly repeating kisses with Bery.

Krische, sitting on Bery's lap, clinging to her, kissing her again and again.

Selene watched this with an indescribable expression.

Kreschenta glared at Krische with obvious dissatisfaction.

Anne, who was supposed to be packing luggage, was as usual unable to focus on her work, her attention constantly drawn to the scene.

This had been going on for close to a quarter-hour.

"……Hahh, honestly."

"Ah……"

Selene lifted Krische's body from behind and set her down on her own lap.

"I understand you're lonely, but. It's almost time for you to depart too. If you keep clinging like that, Bery and the others will be late leaving."

"Uuu……"

Krische still gazed at Bery with lingering reluctance.

A passionate storm of kisses.

Bery, her face flushed bright red as if dazed, scratched her cheek with a troubled, wry smile.

Then she stood and leaned in close.

"Um……Krische-sama. This is not a farewell for eternity. So, the continuation after you come back……when that time comes, please do as much as you like."

She pressed a light kiss and stroked Krische's head.

"According to what I've read, time apart causes love to swell. Surely the kiss at that time will be exceptional, the most wonderful kiss you've ever had."

"……Exceptional."

"Yes. Overflowing with so much love, a very happy kiss."

Even as she spoke, Bery kissed her with her own lingering reluctance.

Twice, three times more, she placed a hand on Krische's cheek and stroked it.

She gazed into those eyes, traced her lips, and Bery narrowed her moistened eyes, leaning closer and closer――

"……Stop that."

"Oww."

A light tap to Bery's forehead from Selene.

"Uuu, even if you are ojou-sama, to hit me……"

"Honestly, what are you doing joining in? Enough already……"

Selene spoke with exasperation to Bery, who was pressing her forehead, and Krische, cheated of her kiss, pouted in displeasure.

Selene pinched those lips between her fingers and declared: that's enough.

"If you want to do silly things, save them for when you're back. Honestly."

"That's right! Only Argan-sama gets to……even though I'm about to be separated from onee-sama too……"

Kreschenta clung to Krische.

But this time it was Kreschenta's forehead that received Selene's tap.

"What are you doing! I-I am a princess, you know!"

"Then perhaps you could behave like one……we'll never be able to leave at this rate."

"Uuu……only Argan-sama, it's unfair."

Why she had to be the one doing this, she didn't know.

Selene sighed and pulled on Krische's cheeks.

Krische let out a whimper.

"Enough. You two, go finish your preparations. Then I'll let you have one more."

"……Since when did onee-sama's kisses require Selene-sama's permission? You intend to hoard them all for yourself, don't you?"

"……I don't."

"……Ojou-sama has been suspicious lately, so I'm a little worried myself."

"H-honestly……"

Bery, apparently holding a grudge over the forehead tap, glared at Selene, then immediately giggled and shook her shoulders with amusement.

"Fufu, then Kreschenta-sama, shall we go greet the escort briefly? The farewell kiss comes after."

"……Very well."

"A-and I shall accompany you……!"

Kreschenta puffed her cheeks in a sulk, nodded, and left the room with Bery and Anne.

Selene sighed once more, squishing and playing with Krische's soft cheeks.

"Honestly, thanks to you I can't hold any tension. Even though things are about to get difficult."

Even as she said it, Selene's voice was full of gentle warmth.

"The silliness has infected Bery and Kreschenta too, what am I supposed to do?"

Krische shifted position on Selene's lap.

And looked up at her from below.

Klare, trembling despite himself, gripped his spear as if to crush it and swung.

"If any wish to take this head, name yourselves!! I, Klare Markelus, shall face you personally!!"

He had screamed to kill the fear rising in him.

He no longer hoped to survive. If he was to be killed, then at least a warrior's death.

With foolish sincerity, he stepped forward to uphold his pride as a noble.

Violet eyes blazed――their gazes crossed.

The monster narrowed her eyes slightly and closed the distance.

"General!!"

"Dora……!"

Adjutant Dora slid her body in front of Klare to shield him, and

"Don't get in bunny's waaay!"

"Wha, ah……!?"

From the side appeared the black-haired woman's crazed grin.

Her axe-like curved blade bisected Dora, armor and all.

The black-haired woman slammed Dora's corpse into the ground with the same momentum――and in the gap between, everything vanished.

The girl had already closed in.

For Klare, the last spear he would ever wield in his life, his final act as a warrior.

Without hesitation, he moved the spear as he had done countless times before.

Born to the great nobility, he had trained since earliest childhood until he vomited blood.

Honed by mortal danger, tempered in flesh and blood, this spear had pierced countless lives.

Today the spear was in fine form――but the girl ducked beneath it with ease.

A low-stance lunge that seemed to lick the earth.

She was completely beyond the limits of Klare's imagination.

Instinct moved the spear faster than thought; he turned the butt-end toward the girl――and thrust with everything he had.

But the girl's movement, her suppleness, coiled around the spear like a serpent.

As if the weapon were no obstacle at all, she closed the distance.

Winding around the shaft of the spear.

She twisted her body along it and leapt up, landing on the horse's neck before Klare's eyes, and smiled.

"……How do you do."

"……!?"

An impact tore through his torso.

He realized he had been kicked off only while he was in midair, after he heard the words that followed.

"Krische is my name. Goodbye."

A beautiful face with a smile like a flower blooming.

Klare had asked for names, and Krische obliged, giving hers and bidding farewell.

Klare would never answer her words.

He would never speak again.

His neck was already half-severed by her curved sword.

The blood that erupted from the kicked-off Klare's neck did not stain so much as a single drop on the beautiful girl.

The girl showed no further interest in the corpse.

Toying with a strand of her pure silver braided hair, she simply surveyed her surroundings.

Black cloak, snow-white skin, and beautiful silver hair.

Atop the horse, the girl's figure drew every eye.

The men who had rushed forward to save the general froze, and it was the black century that cut them down.

Kalua brought her blade down on the general's head where it had fallen to the ground, severing it completely.

She then pierced the head on the tip of her blade and raised it high overhead.

"Behold this head! Your general has been slain by our Christand army First Corps Commander――Krische Christand!! Further resistance is futile, sheathe your swords and surrender!!"

Kalua raised her voice.

Barely one koku (T/N: 一刻 ≈ approximately two hours) had passed since the collision with Velraigh had begun――upon seeing the suddenly raised head and recognizing that helmet, the men lowered their swords one by one.

"Those who surrender without resistance shall not be treated harshly! Though we have crossed blades, we are all subjects of the same kingdom, and with your general lost, the battle is meaningless! Sheathe your swords peaceably and surrender!!"

The next voice was Dagra's.

The Christand soldiers raised their cheers to echo across the battlefield and raised countless banners bearing the lightning and falcon crest.

Once a battle had begun――its heat did not cool at once, but the screams and clashing steel that had filled the air were gradually drowned out by cheers.

Some few offered desperate resistance, but the great majority, having lost any reason to stake their lives, knelt before those voices.

"Haha, a rare and total victory. As expected of Krische-sama."

"Skeleton did very well. You're good at opening a path."

Disarming the surrounding enemies and having their weapons collected, Krische smiled up at Elrouga from horseback.

Elrouga grinned wickedly with evident pleasure, surveying the defeated enemy.

A death-god-like old man and a monster-like girl.

The soldiers of the Markelus army who witnessed this were terrified.

"Vulture, Mia, not bad movement. If you can keep performing like today, it makes things easy for Krische."

"Sir!"

"And Kalua, good work. Vulture, Kalua did exceptionally well this time, so handle the commendation as you see fit. Squads four and nine also showed quite good movement despite being less conspicuous――"

As Krische was casually reviewing the soldiers' performance, a man appeared, parting the ranks.

His wolf-themed helmet and armor were stained with blood.

In his hand, a great war mace longer than he was tall.

The man removed his helmet and knelt.

"……A splendid performance, Krische-sama."

"With conditions that favorable, anyone could do it easily. Corps Commander Varkus."

"No, no, to accomplish it with such ease is surely thanks to Krische-sama's strength. I had intended to devour them before you could, but……I am outmatched."

The man――Granmeld Varkus――laughed, pulling at the scar on his face.

Then he shouldered his great war mace and rose.

"With that body and such a sword, to manage this so effortlessly. I thought the same at the last battle, but what you do lies beyond my comprehension."

"Krische has never found killing people difficult……if they come at her, she need only lightly shave away the flesh, so it's easy."

Krische roughly wiped the blood from her curved sword on a dead man's trousers, then carefully wiped the remainder with a cloth from her pouch.

Not a single nick on the thin blade.

Granmeld stared at her sword in surprise, then shook his head in disbelief.

How many could he kill with a sword that thin? He doubted he would make it past ten.

"A fearsome woman. ……Krische-sama, Corps Commander Faren. For now, please come to the main camp. The General is waiting. He wishes to discuss our next steps briefly."

Krische sheathed her sword and nodded, looking at Elrouga.

"……Yes. Skeleton, shall we go?"

"Indeed. A bit hasty, but……"

"……S-Skeleton?"

Granmeld stared at Elrouga with wide, astonished eyes.

Elrouga stared back and glared.

"……What is it, Granmeld. Or should I say Corps Commander Varkus now?"

"Ha, haha……no, either is fine."

Granmeld began walking ahead to guide the two, his shoulders trembling silently.

Elrouga rubbed the bridge of his nose, and Krische tilted her head in puzzlement.
