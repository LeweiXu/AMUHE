# Chapter 116: The Ceremony, Again

"Here, Kreschenta."

"Aah……ehehe."

Between the victory ceremony and the evening banquet was a brief intermission.

From the banquet onward, wouldn't it be better if Krische changed into a different dress? So suggested Selene, chairwoman of the 'Krische's Dress Should Be Black Council' — and for a moment it seemed they would plunge back into civil war once more.

However, it was here that Krische Christand, a member of the 'Krische Doesn't Care So Please Just Let Her Eat Breakfast Already Alliance,' proposed a ceasefire on the grounds that it was pitiable how Kreschenta could hardly eat properly at the banquet — suggesting they insert a snack break.

Purely for the queen, Kreschenta's sake, of course.

Yet concealed within this was a stratagem of considerable sophistication: by spoiling the alliance leader Kreschenta here and filling her belly, Krische calculated she could later enjoy the banquet alongside Bery without complaint.

Rubbing up against Bery as she made this proposal — Bery, spiritual leader of the 'Cute Dresses Suit Krische-sama Best Church,' found it an offer that benefited herself as well, and agreed with a remark that Kreschenta-sama would certainly be quite busy. And so civil war was averted.

Kreschenta, in the middle of changing, sat on Krische's lap in nothing but her undergarments, being hand-fed from the dishes prepared for the banquet. She was thoroughly delighted.

Setting aside the somewhat dissatisfied Selene, the room was filled with peace.

"Kreschenta-sama, how about this one?"

"Mm……anything is fine."

Kreschenta glanced at the black dress Bery showed her and answered.

Her body showed no sign of womanly development whatsoever, concealed in mature-looking pale pink undergarments, and she nuzzled her cheek against Krische's chest as she spoke. Her indifference toward the dress appeared to be genuine — Kreschenta devoted all her energy solely to clinging to her sister.

Playing the big sister, Krische patted her head, saying Kreschenta is such a spoiled child — but occasionally what slipped out was a small yawn.

She had been woken early in the morning and subjected to one tedious ceremony after another. Krische was feeling quite drowsy, and seeing this, Bery giggled.

"Haah……Marshal. The very thought of it weighs on me."

"Well, there's simply no one more suited for it than Selene-sama. It can't be helped."

Climbing around on top of her drowsy sister, Kreschenta rested her chin on Krische's shoulder and addressed Selene, who sat in the chair behind them, leaning back.

"Holding real authority over the military is the most important thing of all. Having the means to resort to force if necessary, and making that known broadly — personally, Selene-sama is far more convenient for various matters than General Velraigh."

It was politics.

Kreschenta was far too young and lacked accomplishments.

Naturally there would be those who looked down on her. If they looked down on her, they would also direct hostility toward her. There would be those who tried to use her.

What silenced such people without argument was always violence, and violence meant the military.

Naturally, it was far more convenient for Selene — who had a closer relationship with Kreschenta than Nozan — to hold the Marshal position when it came time to wield military power.

If the sole consideration were proceeding smoothly, the Marshal appointment could have been left pending, with Krische temporarily serving as 'Alberinea' in that role instead.

A figurehead Marshal could then be selected from among those dissatisfied with Kreschenta — chosen from suitable candidates and offered as bait for establishing order in the royal court.

But Kreschenta wanted to seize all power within the royal court at an early stage.

Considering that she might need to take forceful measures, it was preferable to exploit the chaos of the civil war's conclusion and quietly secure complete control of the military while the opportunity lasted.

Kreschenta intended to eventually replace more than half of the civil officials.

She had no wish to waste time and money on them.

"Besides, whatever the circumstances, repaying the debt of gratitude owed for this war is a promise Kreschenta has sworn upon her name. The Margrave's house becomes the foremost of the nobility as Marshal, granted royal domain. Kreschenta wants onee-sama and the others to live somewhere Kreschenta feels most at ease. At the very least, the rewards should be commensurate with the responsibility and hardship."

"I know that. ……It's just that my mind hasn't caught up."

Haah, Selene sighed.

Her very first battle had been the war against the Holy Empire.

As Bogan's successor, her first step as a military officer — striving to be worthy of that legacy — and in barely half a year from there, she had ascended to the highest military rank: Marshal.

This went far beyond a mere promotion.

The only people who could take this in stride were the two silly girls present — Krische and Kreschenta.

"Krische thinks it's fine, though. Selene is slow at thinki——takes her time thinking, so being in the rear where she can consider things carefully is probably……uuu."

"Oh, be quiet. I know."

Selene pinched Krische's cheeks from behind and pouted.

When it came to snap judgments and decisiveness, accumulated experience counted for a great deal. In her current state, Selene was still weak in that regard, and she could not be called a first-rate frontline commander.

Though her tactical and strategic thinking was excellent, talent alone could not compensate for inexperience.

The memory of being lectured by Krische was still fresh — *If fifteen people couldn't bring him down, you should have committed even more, pulled in the entire century and made killing Gildanstein the absolute top priority.*

Combat should always aim for minimal engagement and the shortest path to resolution.

If killing Gildanstein would end it all, then devoting everything to that objective and settling it should have been the overwhelming priority — Krische's words had logic behind them.

The situation at the time had been roughly even without Krische — with just one more small step, one minor additional move, it might have been possible to claim victory without relying on Krische at all.

The casualties had been heavy, and what remained was nothing but regret.

Even believing she had given her all, it had not been the best — the result of Selene's inexperience.

Because she understood that inexperience, she wished to hone herself further for a while — and that was precisely why the Marshal position, which had all but tumbled off the shelf into her lap, felt crushingly heavy.

"Setting aside ojou-sama's feelings on the matter……as a mere servant, I am glad that ojou-sama will be away from the front lines."

Bery spoke quietly as she checked the dress for loose threads.

When Selene glared at her, Bery smiled wryly and shook her head.

"I know nothing of the battlefield, so I cannot speak loftily. I do not think ojou-sama is inexperienced — rather, I believe you have worked harder than anyone."

"That's not exactly comforting."

Selene pinched both of Krische's cheeks discontentedly, squishing them back and forth.

Uuu, uuu, Krische moaned.

"Ojou-sama, you have been overreaching. Because you are the master's successor, you wish to fight as splendidly as he did — but that will likely never come to pass."

"……?"

"The master was blessed with talent, skill, and fortune — he charged through countless battlefields over decades, accumulating distinguished service and surviving mortal peril. He was a champion — a true hero."

She said it as though recalling, and smiled.

"Do you truly believe you can overturn everything the master built with a mere few years of effort? Is the master whom ojou-sama's, whom the Christand soldiers revere — is he such a small target?"

"……That's——"

"Of course, the battlefield may hold many lessons. Perhaps you cannot surpass the master without learning them. But would that not be perfectly fine? The master and ojou-sama are different people, with different lives and different things to learn."

Bery set the dress on the table and gently stroked Selene's head.

Selene made a sour face but did not resist.

"Even if ojou-sama cannot learn the things the master came to know, ojou-sama may learn things the master never could. In time, if ojou-sama can achieve something in some form that is no less than what the master achieved — I think that would be enough."

Her touch was tender and full of love, just as Selene's mother had once done for her.

Bery always treated Selene like something of a child.

"The position of Marshal, in particular, is not one you can obtain just by wishing for it, so this should be seen as a stroke of good fortune. ……I believe ojou-sama is more than capable of fulfilling such a weighty responsibility — more capably than anyone — and I harbor no anxiety about that."

"……You're saying you're anxious about me being on the front lines, is that it?"

When Selene snapped back, Bery laughed as if amused.

"Fufu, you mustn't take things so contrarily. Well, I admit I cannot say such feelings are entirely absent, from the perspective of one who waits……but that is not something unique to ojou-sama."

The hand that had been stroking Selene's head moved to Krische instead, and Bery narrowed her eyes.

Krische's lips, which had been toyed with at the cheeks, relaxed into a smile, and Selene released both hands.

"……It is a selfish thing. Forgive me."

"You don't feel bad about it at all, and yet you say that so easily — honestly."

"Oh my, you saw through me?"

Bery grinned mischievously and picked up the underwear-clad Kreschenta.

Kreschenta, who had been savoring Krische's lap, looked up at Bery and glared.

"Now then, it is time to get dressed, Kreschenta-sama."

"Krische-sama, once Kreschenta-sama has finished changing, shall we have a little nap?"

"Yes! ……Kreschenta, time to get dressed."

"Th-that's unfair."

Paying no mind to Kreschenta's protests, Bery dressed her.

Krische helped cheerfully.

Watching this, Selene smiled wryly and sat back down in her chair.

Carrying on her father's legacy. Becoming someone worthy of being his successor.

What was truly important — Selene considered this and simply watched them.

The great hall was entirely glass-walled, and the crimson of sunset poured in, making the splendid array of dishes gleam in the light.

The same standing-style reception as before.

Selene had Elvena, Kreschenta had Anne, and Krische had Bery at their side as they made their rounds.

Naturally, it could not go as smoothly as the other day.

Selene had become the highest-ranking military officer, and Krische, who held an equivalent standing, also found herself approached by unfamiliar nobles from all directions.

For Krische this was thoroughly tiresome, but the one who came to her rescue was Elrouga.

"Haah……Krische is so hungry."

"Haha, when you've become the queen's own sword, even taking a meal proves difficult."

Feigning an air of having important business, Elrouga addressed the two of them and led them out to the balcony.

Given that Elrouga now held the title of Assistant to the Supreme Commander, the number of people who could interrupt them there was limited.

An hour and a half after the banquet had begun, Krische was finally able to eat a proper meal.

Beside Elrouga stood a slender noblewoman — his wife.

Though slight crow's feet had formed at the corners of her eyes, she had the look of a sharp-featured, tall beauty, and her golden hair was elegant.

They seemed to be somewhat apart in age, but the way she held Elrouga's arm spoke of a close bond, and she glanced at Elrouga several times, looking uncertain.

"Ah, forgive me……I hadn't made introductions. This is my wife, Vanatela. Please do make her acquaintance."

"Pleased to meet you, um……"

Vanatela looked to Elrouga again with even more uncertainty.

As a young lady of the Christand house, 'Krische-sama' would be appropriate. But the head of the house was now Selene, and Krische had been granted the peerage of Alberinea, making her a great noble in her own right.

That form of address hardly seemed fitting anymore.

If one considered that Krische was originally the First Princess, then perhaps 'Your Highness' was appropriate, or perhaps 'Your Royal Highness' — or perhaps her proper designation was Archduchess Christand.

Then again, Krische had never been formally granted the rank of archduchess as a sibling of the sovereign — to call her that would also be questionable. Vanatela was at a loss as to how to address this girl.

Krische tilted her head, and Elrouga too tilted his head at his wife's behavior.

Only Bery understood, and she nodded and spoke.

"I have heard that she and Margrave Faren shared hardships on the battlefield and have a relationship that transcends rank. Please do not stand on ceremony — Krische-sama also wishes to continue the relationship as it has been."

"I see……pleased to meet you, Krische-sama."

Vanatela offered an elegant curtsy, and Krische returned it.

Elrouga finally caught on and smiled wryly, realizing his oversight.

"My apologies. Krische-sama is not one to stand on formalities. Please be at ease."

"……I would appreciate it if you told me these things beforehand."

Vanatela shot a slightly cross look at Elrouga, then turned her gaze back to Krische.

She studied that hair and beauty, and smiled with pleasure.

"My husband told me about you, but you truly are a beautiful person. That dress is lovely — it suits you wonderfully."

"Um, yes, thank you very much. Vanatela-sama's dress is very pretty too."

Krische's eyes kept darting to the dishes she had brought to the table.

Vanatela noticed the glances and smiled wryly, telling her to please not mind them.

Krische looked uncertainly at Elrouga and Bery, and both of them smiled as well.

"I brought you out here precisely because you couldn't eat. Please, go right ahead."

"Ehehe, yes. Skeleton, would you like some too?"

"Hahaha, I am a light eater, but……well, I suppose I shall have a little."

Vanatela froze at the name 'Skeleton,' and Bery gave her an apologetic look.

What Vanatela felt was confusion, but she saw her husband's unbothered demeanor, shot Bery a wry grin in return, and shook her head.

"That bit about transcending rank does seem to be true……fufu, Skeleton……"

Finding it amusing, Vanatela shook with suppressed laughter, covering her mouth as she glanced at Elrouga.

Elrouga must have anticipated this when he brought his wife along. He showed no particular concern, and with the benevolent mien of a kindly old man — worn on the grim visage of a reaper — he nibbled at the food with Krische.

"He frets about it so much normally. His own son cries every time he sees his face……and yet he's been so cheerful since coming back — so this is why."

"Krische-sama says he was very kind to her on the battlefield as well."

"Given that face, I never worried about infidelity……now I understand. He is a softie for children, you see — ah, no, that's a rude way to put it."

"No, fufu, Krische-sama is exactly that sort of person."

When Bery smiled ruefully, Vanatela returned the smile and studied Bery's face intently.

"Could you be Lazura-sama's……?"

"Ah, yes. Forgive my late introduction — I am her younger sister, Bery Argan."

"I thought you resembled her. I met Lazura-sama several times and we became quite close……sisters who are both so beautiful."

"No……"

Bery shook her head, looking uneasy, and Vanatela leaned in with a look of concern.

"I know this may not be the place for it, at a celebration, but……my condolences."

"Thank you. It was painful, but……I have come to terms with it."

"That is a relief……even so, please do not push yourself. You may rely on me at any time. Lazura-sama spoke of you often, Bery-sama, so I cannot think of it as someone else's affair."

Vanatela took Bery by the shoulders and peered into her face.

"Lazura-sama told me about you, but……to think someone this beautiful is living as a mere servant — what a waste. How would you feel about a marriage introduction?"

"Um, well……"

Her frame was petite, and the large brown eyes framed by long lashes made her look younger than she was.

But her features were remarkably well-formed, and her manner and expressions carried a composed, somewhat mature quality.

That very mismatch was likely what made her so captivating.

Bery possessed an indefinable charm.

She was far too beautiful to spend her life as a servant.

To Vanatela, who had arranged many fine matches in her time, this was a talent she could not leave untouched — she pressed closer, leaning in.

"I have quite an extensive circle, if I do say so. I will find someone worthy of your discerning eye, without fail, Bery-sama. Someone as young and beautiful as you would have suitors lining up — leave it to me."

"Well, I'm not really that young anymore……I will be turning thirty soon……"

Bery answered uncertainly, but Vanatela pressed still closer.

"I was thirty-five when I became my husband's wife, you know. You're plenty young. Rest assured."

"Y-yes……"

Bery took a step back, leaning away slightly.

Vanatela pressed forward another step, and——

"Now, Vanatela, that's a bad habit. Stop that."

It was Elrouga who intervened.

Vanatela glared at Elrouga, muttering *I was just getting to the good part*, hands on her hips, and sighed.

"Honestly, can you not see you're troubling her? Do try to show a little restraint."

"A woman of Bery-sama's caliber remaining single is a tremendous loss, I should think. You may not understand, but——"

"It is simply your hobby. ……Forgive my wife."

"N-no……"

As Bery smiled awkwardly, Vanatela asked:

"But truly, you have no interest in a marriage introduction? What I said was no lie — with your qualities, Bery-sama, I should think you could have your pick."

"Well, that is……"

Bery's gaze wandered, and it settled on Krische, whose cheeks were puffed out with food as she watched the exchange unfold.

Noticing the look, Krische toddled over to Bery, linked their arms, and softened her cheeks into a smile.

"Bery is going to be with Krische forever, so she won't get married. Right, Bery?"

"……Fufu, yes."

Bery smiled happily and stroked Krische's cheek with affection.

Krische pressed herself against Bery's arm and narrowed her eyes in bliss.

"I am quite satisfied with my life at Christand……with my life as it is now. Such things are not meant for me. ……I'm sorry, after you went to the trouble of offering."

When Bery bowed her head, Vanatela looked between Krische and Bery, and——

"……I see. It seems my concern was unnecessary."

She nodded, as though something had clicked into place.

# Chapter 117: A Place to Return To

The underground laboratory of the royal palace had the look of a somewhat oversized storeroom — shelves lined with countless books, metal rods of unknown purpose, and piles of magic crystals scattered about.

Elvena had finished a round of selection and duplication work from the piled magic crystals, and turned her gaze toward her mistress.

"Krische-sama, I've finished the batch……"

"Is that so? How many did you make?"

"Twelve. Combined with the ones from the other day, that brings us to fifty-six."

She packed the finished magic crystals into their designated case and turned her eyes to the object before Krische, who stood with arms folded.

On the platform before her lay what could only be described as a figure made of metal rods and a birdcage.

This rod-framed thing had four limbs and a head, simplified to resemble a human body. At each joint of the four limbs were twenty-four magic crystals in total. Inside the birdcage-like torso was one large magic crystal, and another sat in the cage of the head.

The joints were linked by chains, giving it the appearance of a poorly made model.

Its frame of seven shaku was eerie, and the cold blue glow of the magic crystals was decidedly chilling. (T/N: Seven shaku ≈ 210 cm / ~6'11".)

"Um……how is your side going, Krische-sama?"

"It seems capable of at least basic movement."

"……Will it actually move?"

"It will."

Krische climbed onto the platform and pressed her finger to the magic crystal at its torso — its heart.

As mana flowed from her fingertip, the light propagated, illuminating each magic crystal in blue.

With a rattle of chains——

"……!"

The figure sat up and stood.

Its seven-shaku frame was nearly tall enough to hit the ceiling of this underground room, and the sight of something inhuman moving right before her eyes made Elvena tense.

Clinging to its metal body was the shimmering blue of mana forming phantom muscles.

Despite its thin frame, it carried an unsettling presence.

The figure extended one hand forward, then brought it to its chest.

It performed a salute.

"……As I thought, the arm and leg joints should be connected with something more rigid than chains. The chains cause too much mana loss."

Krische narrowed her eyes, studying the figure's limbs.

The figure then began to circle the platform, around and around.

Clank, clank — with every step, the sound of chains and metal and floor reverberated.

It could not be wounded and would not die.

An iron soldier that would fight as long as its mana did not run dry.

"There are still problems with using it in combat, but see? It moves properly."

"……It does, move."

Once completed, this strange figure would take up sword, axe, or spear and kill enemies on the battlefield.

A weapon for killing — at the sheer strangeness of what this girl was building in her spare time between household chores, Elvena felt an indescribable chill.

It had not even been a week since she started work on it.

And yet its concrete form was already taking shape.

The phantom muscles that mana-users created and wore upon their bodies — their deployment and processing were being substituted by magic crystals, serving as the blood and flesh that moved this silent metal figure.

The formulae carved into the magic crystals were intricate.

Complexities Elvena could not begin to comprehend were inscribed within — she could not even tell what was acting upon what.

This figure was made of something whose principles she could grasp but whose workings she could not understand.

"If we model it after the human body, distributing the load between two thinner bones per major segment would be better than using one. Especially the legs — given that it's heavier than a human, they need particular attention……the contact surface area with the ground should be wider too. On earth, it would probably sink."

Krische took up a sheet of parchment and began transcribing the information from her own head.

She used no ruler or straightedge, yet the diagram was flawless in its precision.

Each sheet contained only a portion of a component.

Orders would be distributed among different craftsmen. This served partly to prevent information leaks, but mostly it was simply more efficient to split the work.

After a while, the figure returned to its platform, lay down, and went still.

As though nothing had happened, silence returned.

"Any problems with the duplication crystals?"

"Ah, yes……depending on the quality of the magic crystal there's a slight variance, but for mana measurement purposes it should pose no issue. I did try fine-tuning the ones I could, but……I'm not entirely confident they're done properly."

"Which ones?"

"These three."

Elvena handed them over, and Krische glanced at them once before nodding and returning them.

"That's more than sufficient. Let us finish with what we have for now."

"Yes."

"Elvena, could you deliver these, please? That will be the end of your work for the day……mm, you may take three days off and go home."

"Is that really all right?"

"Yes. You've worked hard, so it's a reward. Do make sure you take an escort, it can be dangerous."

Elvena bowed her head, looking pleased.

"I'll most likely be at home, so if anything comes up, I can come at once."

"I doubt anything will, but if it does, Krische will send word properly. Take your time and rest."

* * *

One of the small estates in the royal domain — originally meant as a preparation space for visiting dignitaries — had been given to the Christand house as-is, serving as both the Christand residence and the princess's bedchamber.

It was somewhat smaller than the previous Christand estate, but including Elvena — who often went home to Kalua and Mia's place outside the royal domain — there were at most six of them.

The little estate was more than spacious enough for all six.

Since Krische, Kreschenta, and Bery sometimes shared a room, there were actually rooms to spare, even counting the guest quarters.

"Ehehe, today's pumpkin pie was a masterpiece. The pumpkins in the capital really are tastier."

"They are. The quality of all the ingredients is wonderful……the shopping is enjoyable in its own way, so it is a little bittersweet."

Krische sat in her usual place — on Bery's lap on the sofa.

The two of them, freshly bathed and in their nightgowns, were as they always were — any sense of distance long since vanished, in a state that could not be shown to anyone else.

The thin fabric that let skin show through was undeniably provocative, and Krische, wrapped in the blanket draped over Bery's shoulders, would shift position now and then — burying her face in Bery's chest, clinging to her, kissing her.

In this decadent space there were only the two of them, and the civil war that had ended less than a month ago felt like the distant past.

The only word for Krische when she was in their room was *indulgent*.

Upon waking, she would pick at the previous night's leftovers for 'pre-breakfast,' then prepare the actual morning meal.

After breakfast and clean-up she would pack lunches and head to the castle, where she would attend to her various 'tasks.' But she would return home for snack time, help a little with cleaning and such, prepare dinner, eat, bathe with Bery, and sleep soundly.

Krische's daily cycle was arranged with 'spending time with Bery' as the top priority.

Compared to Selene and Kreschenta, who poured themselves into paperwork and governance even after meals, her lifestyle was the very picture of sloth — but the reason lay in a conference with the neighboring nations scheduled for the month after next.

Under the pretext of introducing the new queen, it was essentially an exchange of greetings — a customary event held with every change of reign.

Invitations had been sent not only to the Arna Empire to the north, as expected, but also to the Elderant Kingdom to the west, the Galshan Republic to the south, and even the Holy Elsren Empire, despite the recent war. Letters of intent to attend had arrived from each.

Only the Holy Elsren Empire had yet to reply, but with the other three nations confirming their attendance, it would be diplomatically difficult to launch an attack on the kingdom now.

The kingdom had secured at least two months of peace.

Though the possibility of attack had never been high to begin with — this being the season when winter was about to set in, and the kingdom was exhausted — this had now been extinguished entirely, freeing them to devote time to domestic recovery.

The fact that Kreschenta, immediately upon becoming queen, had proposed this introduction to the surrounding nations was aimed precisely at this, and her gambit was proceeding smoothly.

"Ah, that reminds Krische."

"……?"

Krische slid off Bery's lap and retrieved several magic crystals from her bag.

She placed them on the table, took one, and showed it to Bery.

"What is this?"

"A trial duplication crystal. It seemed practical enough, so Krische thought she would give one to Bery."

"Duplication……"

Bery, not quite following, took it in her hand and studied the formulae inscribed within.

Inside the crystal, countless lines formed a geometrical three-dimensional pattern.

At a glance, all she could discern was that it was a formula designed to affect something else — the only thing she understood was that an extremely advanced formula had been constructed.

"Let's use an eternal magic lamp for the duplication."

Saying this, Krische picked up one of the magic crystals — an eternal magic lamp.

This one was familiar.

"Try pressing them together and channeling your mana."

"……Like this?"

Bery placed the duplication crystal against the eternal magic lamp in Krische's hand and channeled mana into it.

The duplication crystal in Bery's palm glowed brightly, and inside it a small, new formula was inscribed.

It was the formula of the eternal magic lamp — a simple formula that merely produced light.

"Now it's memorized the eternal magic lamp's formula, so do the same thing with a blank crystal."

Krische now held a pristine, uninscribed magic crystal.

She pressed it against Bery's crystal, telling her with a look. Bery understood the intent and channeled mana——

"……I see."

In an instant, the eternal magic lamp formula was inscribed into the blank crystal.

"It temporarily memorizes a formula and writes it into another crystal. Elvena named it a 'duplication crystal,' so that is what Krische has been calling it."

"Duplication crystal……"

"This time it was a simple eternal magic lamp formula, but it works for much more complex ones too — so it is quite useful, Krische thinks. That is why Krische wanted Bery to have one."

Inscribing a new formula into a magic crystal was not terribly difficult in itself.

Bery could make an eternal magic lamp from scratch for something as simple as that.

That said, it was akin to sculpture using mana. The more advanced the work, the harder it became — requiring the skills of specialized magic engineers and sorcerers.

*Useful* was a word that utterly failed to capture what this was.

"This is incredible……"

Knitting her brows, Bery recalled the magic from before.

Krische was always several steps ahead of the era.

Her idle notions leaped lightly over what a talented person might achieve in a lifetime.

"Don't worry. Bery is the only one Krische is giving this to."

"Hm?"

"It reacts to the first mana channeled into it and inscribes an exclusive formula inside. So this duplication crystal won't function properly for anyone but Bery — and apart from Elvena, who uses one for work, only Krische and Bery have these."

Krische smiled from Bery's lap.

"Krische could have made it with a simpler formula, but Krische thought about various things and made it this way. This way, Bery can feel safe, Krische thought."

"……I see."

Bery chuckled softly and stroked her head.

"The method for erasing a memorized formula also uses magic, so only Bery will know how."

Krische flicked her fingers, tracing a formula in the air that bonded with the duplication crystal's formula.

They interacted in a composite manner, and the eternal magic lamp inscribed within faded away.

It was not a terribly complicated formula. Having watched and memorized it, Bery asked:

"……Are you truly sure you don't need my help?"

She had heard that Krische and Elvena were making something using magic crystals.

It had been Bery who taught Elvena basic magic crystal theory in place of Krische's explanations, which were too complex and intuitive.

Surely she would not be unhelpful.

And yet the one Krische chose as her assistant was not Bery, but Elvena.

Bery had the estate to manage and was busy.

She understood that Krische was being considerate — and yet.

"Anne-sama is here now……and the estate is smaller than before. If an extra pair of hands is needed, I would also……am I not able to be of help?"

Something unsettled in her heart slipped out before she could stop it.

Krische looked up at Bery, then lowered her eyes and clung to her.

"……Um, of course, having Bery help would be the very best, but, well, how to put it……that."

Krische faltered, as if struggling for words.

Bery could not see her face — only silver hair filled her vision. But she sensed the hesitation there, and shook her head.

*'Really? A pretty girl……wait, you mean——'*

*'Seems like she's being taken to a noble's house……I don't know the circumstances, but maybe it's that kind of thing. Apparently the village was attacked by bandits and a lot of people were killed……'*

She did have a memory that matched.

A tragic beauty, orphaned and sold off to a noble — that was what Mia had imagined on her own. But the girl was almost certainly Krische.

Afterward, there had been vague rumors of a girl who had cut down multiple bandits and been cast out of the village for being a deviant, but the story was too outlandish for anyone to believe, and the wagon drivers themselves dismissed it as nonsense. The rumor quickly faded.

Then again, if it had been Krische — that was nothing less than the truth.

"You've always been you, haven't you, Krische-sama……"

"……?"

"No, it's nothing."

Mia said it with a look of exasperation.

Kalua, who had been watching the exchange, thought for a moment and spoke.

"Mia, why don't you come along? You're not going to get another chance to go up north anytime soon……"

"Mmm, I've got a lot to do here……"

"No, don't worry about it."

Dagra cut in.

He folded his arms, looked at Mia, and smiled ruefully.

"You can at least show your face to the family back home. The work that needs doing is no different from an ordinary century. I can manage fine without an adjutant."

"But……"

"I told you to stop with the 'but's, didn't I? Honestly. ……You fought admirably in a hard battle while I was away. And on top of that, to come back in one piece……this must be some kind of fate."

Dagra said this, then turned to face Krische.

"Krische-sama, if possible, I would like Mia and Kalua to accompany you."

"All right, so Kirik's squad plus Mia and Kalua makes seven……including hands for chores, that should be plenty. About a month, most likely?"

"Sir."

"Krische has things to attend to, so that will be all for today. Mia, please handle preparations as you see fit. Krische's side will prepare provisions — it's the camping equipment you'll need to sort out."

"Um……yes."

Krische said this and left the room. Mia wore a vaguely reluctant look as she considered it.

"Mia, go arrange the wagon. I'll head over to Kirik's."

"Ah, sure. Got it."

Mia gave in with a *well, all right* sort of air and headed out. Kalua watched her go from behind.

"Captain, could I have a word with you?"

"……Mm, sure."

And after a moment's thought, Kalua addressed Dagra.

# Chapter 120: The Million-Pumpkin Woman

It happened one evening when the four of them were relaxing.

Kreschenta had been snuggling up to Krische — who sat on Bery's lap — going *wan-wan, kuuun*, being doted on. She leaped into the chair next to Selene at the sound of a visitor's knock — and Anne, without waiting for their acknowledgment, flung the door open with a brisk *'Right this way! Please!'*

Following with a wry smile was Gallen.

The entrance had been so brusque as to defeat the purpose of knocking, but at this point no one bothered to correct Anne.

Bery smiled awkwardly and rose to show Gallen to a seat.

"——Oba-san is?"

"Aye. Apparently she's been unwell more often lately, and the other day she collapsed in the middle of farmwork……well, it seems to have happened about two weeks ago."

When Krische asked what was the matter, Gallen showed her a letter and explained.

From Karka Village to the capital was a considerable distance. For a letter to arrive took about two weeks — roughly what one would expect.

"I was thinking of going to see her myself, but……the one Galla would be happiest to see is you, Krische. Of course, it depends on your work commitments."

Both Gallen and Krische had duties in the capital.

That said, Selene was now Marshal, with Elrouga serving as her assistant, and Gallen had become the adjutant to Alberinea — to Krische — meaning Gallen handled half the military work.

Krische finished the military reorganization paperwork faster than anyone — which is to say, she had already set up the system for Keith and the others to handle everything — so there was no particular work on that front. The finer details could be left to Gallen if he stayed behind.

What Gallen was concerned about was Krische's other business.

But on that front, too, there was nothing especially urgent on the schedule.

The basic instructions had already been communicated to the fabrication squad, and manufacturing the components would take time. What Krische herself needed to do was done — continuing after she returned would pose no great problem.

"This Galla-san is……?"

"Someone who took care of Krische a lot. You know, the person who taught her how to bake pies……"

Inside Krische, the scales were swaying, and she hesitated.

The round trip would take about a month. Not exactly close.

Though leaving the capital posed no issues, there was always more she could be doing.

She had loved Galla dearly since childhood, of course — but was it worth setting aside all manner of work to go? Krische considered——

"I see. That is worrying……"

Bery's absent-minded remark.

Hearing it, Krische reached her conclusion at once.

"Understood. But Selene, is that all right?"

"It's fine. I'm busy, but there's nothing I need Krische's help with……the vice-marshal has been working hard."

What the kingdom had lost in the civil war was enormous, and the entire military was currently being reorganized.

The imperial army that Kreschenta had brought along was stationed in the particularly troubled western and eastern regions, so national defense was not an issue — but filling the gaps left by lost generals and corps commanders could not be done so easily.

Soldiers could be recruited with money, but commanders could not be replaced.

While managing all that, Selene had seized the opportunity to establish a military academy in the capital.

The framework of the general staff system that had been in development in the north was transplanted wholesale, and a call for enlistment had been sent out to the nobility.

Its purpose was to take second and third sons who had ability but could not inherit their houses, retrain them as assistants to generals, corps commanders, and battalion commanders, foster competition among them, and incorporate the top performers into the military as staff officers — the realization of a concept Bogan had nurtured for years.

For lesser nobles it went without saying, but even the third or fourth sons of great houses had to start their careers as captains or centurions — on the very front lines.

Casualty rates were naturally high, and the loss of personnel who had received advanced education from childhood within noble houses was, indeed, a significant drawback.

Brilliant minds who lacked the sword arm had long been wasted this way, a chronic problem.

This initiative aimed to create a venue that gave such dormant talent a chance — and conversely, there were many in the military who had risen through martial prowess yet relied on instinct and acted recklessly in command.

A commander of great valor but little acumen, paired with a staff officer who could not swing a sword but excelled in intellect.

Combining the two could merge their strengths and compensate for their weaknesses, potentially forging a first-rate commander — that was Bogan's idea: creating famous generals through collaboration, thereby strengthening and stabilizing the military.

In the existing system, the demands placed on individual commanders were already excessive, and no matter how brilliant a general, prolonged campaigning degraded their performance.

Beyond adjutants, support staff to lighten this burden were needed, and Bogan had believed a sweeping overhaul of the military's organizational structure was necessary — from which the concept of the staff officer was born.

Unlike adjutants, who might assume direct command, these would be people dedicated to drafting operational plans and performing administrative calculations — a new role created expressly for that purpose.

This would reduce the commander's burden, yielding straightforward improvements in command quality along with tactical stabilization through the staff officers' operational planning.

Elrouga had shown strong enthusiasm for this.

He had originally been the most invested in the staff system under Bogan, but the obstacles had always been funding and the number of people willing to participate.

This time, however, the queen was backing it.

Those problems had dissolved, and letters of interest were already arriving in considerable numbers.

The remaining issue was how to handle the institutional rank of staff officers so as not to threaten the interests of current commanders, who were satisfied with the status quo.

But they were still at the stage of converting a single building into a school and hauling in books.

That was a matter for later — it would be years before the system was fully operational.

For now, the immediate goal was to take the best of those who gathered, slot them directly into the military, and assign them as provisional staff officers — effectively assistant adjutants — to the insufficiently qualified commanders who had been promoted during the reorganization, minimizing the decline in military capability.

Recruiting was still underway, so the real work had not yet begun — there would be time to iron out the details later.

Fortunately, many nobles were eager to join, partly thanks to Kreschenta stepping out front.

*'The final dream entrusted to us by the hero, the late General Christand — I must repay the debt of gratitude and loyalty I owe him.'*

When Selene had first raised the idea, Kreschenta had not even known that Bogan had harbored such a concept. But when the time came for the actual council meeting, she had spun an elaborate, lengthy fiction about how *'Bogan had personally shared this dream with her'* and pushed it through.

A queen built entirely of lies and performance.

And now that same queen sat beside Selene, lips pouting, glaring alternately at Bery and Selene.

*'It's not fair that only Argan-sama gets to!'* — one could practically hear the words.

If Krische was going, Bery would naturally go too.

Being left behind for a whole month while the two of them traveled must have been galling.

When Krische stroked her head out of Gallen's sight, Kreschenta puffed her cheeks instead.

She was every bit a child — a completely different creature from the one who handled affairs of state.

With a wry smile, Krische addressed her:

"Well, stretch your wings a bit while you can, but don't come back even sillier. The conference is the month after next, and……it won't be easy to go back once things start up again. Go while you still can. Bery, there's something I'd like to ask you to do as well."

"The luggage from the estate, yes?"

Bery nodded.

Though they had brought essential clothing and the like to the capital, almost all the furnishings and furniture remained at the Gurgain estate.

Naturally, one could not have the entire contents of a household — including expensive items — transported by strangers, so the matter had been put off.

This was a good opportunity.

"While you're at it, could you handle the……the sale arrangements for the estate?"

"……Yes."

For Selene, it was the house she had lived in her entire life — full of memories with both her parents.

The same was surely true for Bery.

But leaving an unoccupied estate unattended would only cause it to deteriorate, and it cost money to maintain. It was far too large to keep out of sentiment — better to sell it, turn it to coin, and let someone else live there.

"Keep what you bring back to a minimum — you choose. Everything else can be sold. I doubt this applies to you, but don't go bringing back every little thing."

"Fufu, I know. This estate already has plenty of decorations……I'll keep it to a minimum. Any requests, ojou-sama?"

"All the books……and tou-sama's liquor. I'd like to give it to people who had a connection to him."

"Yes. Understood."

Gallen nodded and smiled.

The wrinkled face softened.

"I'm glad there's business to attend to over there. Truthfully, I was wondering if it would be proper to take a whole month just for a sick-visit……"

"Things have been rather hectic until recently. This gives us a nice stopping point, Gallen-sama."

Selene smiled back, then looked at Krische.

"Well, don't stretch your wings so far that you come back even sillier."

"……Krische is not silly."

"You're the only person in the entire world who would try to name an elite unit the *Kurofuyo* Squad. Honestly. It's a good thing I noticed in time."

Along with the paperwork for Krische's military reorganization, a certain squad name had been tucked in among the documents: a mysterious entity called the *Alberinea Direct Command Kurofuyo Squad*.

What it produced in Selene was a headache.

She was thankful from the bottom of her heart that she had not stamped her seal in an assembly-line fashion.

Krische, still sulking and pouting, was told *that's enough* by Bery, who smiled wryly and chided Selene.

"Bringing up things that are already over like that. Poor Krische-sama."

"It's my own fault for leaving her unsupervised too long. Vulture, Skeleton, Woof-woof — I'd been holding my tongue, telling myself nicknames were fine, at least, but……"

"Vulture, Skeleton, and Woof-woof all properly appreciate the nicknames Krische gave them."

Krische, cheeks puffed in a sulk, argued back. Bery stroked her head with a troubled look and repeated *that's enough* to Selene.

"Your spoiling her is part of the problem, honestly."

"I am always on Krische-sama's side."

"Heh, you two do get along. That's good."

Gallen said this with amusement and rose to his feet.

"Krische, since you'll be stopping by Gurgain, drop in on Koz and give him my regards, would you? Tell him I'm doing well. It won't be easy for me to get up there."

Koz was a former subordinate of Gallen's and a skilled blacksmith.

He was the man who had forged Krische's sword.

Recalling that aged face, Krische nodded.

"Yes. Understood."

"Thank you."

With a bow to the queen, Gallen took his leave.

Anne followed him out, and — Kreschenta instantly threw herself at Krische.

Clinging to her sister, she glared at Bery.

"It's not fair, just the two of you traveling together."

"My apologies. It pains me as well, but……"

"Kreschenta was with Bery the whole time during the civil war, so as far as Krische is concerned, they're even."

"That's fundamentally different! Kreschenta wants to be with *onee-sama*, not Argan-sama!"

"What a spoiled child. Here, a cookie."

"Uuu……nm."

She glared — yet could not refrain from eating.

This Kreschenta did not possess the option of *not eating* a cookie offered to her.

She knew perfectly well it was an attack designed to pacify her — and yet she received the cookie head-on. A truly dignified royal, a queen of Alberan.

One cookie swallowed, then a second.

Watching Kreschenta's cheeks soften blissfully as she chewed, Selene muttered *silly girl* and said:

"If you're going there, then yes — have one more sword forged."

"A sword?"

"Yes. It's not something that can be made quickly, and it would be terrible if the one you're using now broke, wouldn't it? It's not the sort of ordinary blade you can just find anywhere — you need a spare."

"……Krische doesn't break swo——"

"Have one forged. Understood?"

The tone brooked no argument.

Krische moaned *uuu* and nodded.

Ten thousand pumpkins — paying such a vast sum for a spare sword was, to Krische, a dreadful waste.

"Bery, sorry, but please handle that as well."

"Of course. Given the distance now, we can't maintain the same sort of relationship with him as before……"

Nozan, Kokys, and many of Bogan's other subordinates had once relied on that shop for their swords, but now they were scattered.

Business would have dropped sharply.

Given how much he had done for them — forging swords practically at cost for Bogan's perennially cash-strapped subordinates, and helping in ways that went beyond mere transactions — paying generously was only proper.

The three small gold coins Bery had paid were meant to settle those 'invisible debts,' not merely as payment for one sword — but Krische still had not come to terms with the amount.

"Selene, Krische won't break a sword, so really……"

"……You're still so penny-pinching about these things, honestly."

"……? Why are you being so stubborn about this, onee-sama?"

Kreschenta tilted her head, and Krische sighed.

"Because……a sword that costs as many pumpkins as ten thousand — it's just, you know, wasteful……the first one was one thing, but a second?"

"Ten thousand pumpkins……"

Kreschenta considered for a moment, then looked at Krische with exasperation.

"What are you fretting about over a mere two or three small gold coins? Considering what onee-sama does, we could spend dozens and it would be justified. What exactly do you think you are, onee-sama? You are the First Princess and the kingdom's second-highest-ranking general."

"Haah……Kreschenta, listen. In the village, a family can get by on two pumpkins a day. Ten thousand pumpkins is an enormous amount of money——"

"Onee-sama, look at this."

Saying this, Kreschenta pointed to her own head — a silver hairpin shaped like a crescent moon.

A small accent piece, almost like a hair clip.

Wondering what her point was, Krische tilted her head. Kreschenta told her:

"This alone costs at least ten gold coins."

"……Huh?"

Next, Kreschenta held out her left wrist.

There sat a bracelet woven from two metals — gold and silver — intertwined.

"This bracelet, then — gold work and silver work, a very fine piece, with gemstones scattered throughout……let's see, it would easily exceed twenty or thirty coins."

"Um……"

"This necklace, too — the scarlet stone has excellent color, and the size is quite respectable……this would go for at least fifty. Depending on the appraisal, seventy or eighty……even this casual dress I'm wearing would easily surpass three small gold coins — it was made by a renowned tailor who has served the royal house for generations."

Krische was dumbstruck. Kreschenta said flatly:

"When it comes to tiaras, you cannot even put a price on some of them at a hundred or two hundred coins. And for a formal banquet, my entire outfit — in small gold coins — could easily exceed several hundred, even a thousand. ……Do you understand now? Given how much onee-sama has accomplished, the fact that she's using a sword worth a mere three small gold coins is, frankly, embarrassing to Kreschenta."

Krische was stunned by the value of the Kreschenta she had been carelessly cradling.

At current prices, roughly three hundred thousand pumpkins.

She was a bit silly, a bit absent-minded, and liked to be spoiled — but Kreschenta was a million-pumpkin woman.

What Krische felt was shock.

She stared, dumbfounded, at the staggering reality.

"……Honestly, onee-sama. You should think a little more about your position. Given your status, your sword is too *cheap*, if anything."

Bery, listening from behind, smiled wryly.

Kreschenta's argument came from her perspective as royalty and was somewhat excessive — but Krische was, in her own way, far too miserly.

Valuing one's possessions was a fine thing, but refusing to accept even necessities out of excessive frugality was a problem.

Especially when it came to weapons — they protected Krische's life. Bery, at the very least, wanted her to have a spare sword to ensure she was never caught short.

"Well, that's true. Krische-sama, you've hardly touched your salary or any of the rewards you've received……considering that, one sword is a trifle."

"Uuu……"

The military unit under Krische's command received organizational subsidies from the kingdom, and the maintenance costs for the standing Black Flag Special Service and its officers were almost entirely covered by those funds, with salaries serving only as a minor supplement.

The Christand house now had two earners in Selene and Krische, few servants, and modest living expenses. Krische, who despised waste, saw almost all of her salary go to savings.

The rewards from the war against the Empire and this civil war were also untouched, and given Krische's nature of spending only on necessities, her savings grew ever larger.

She spent nothing on ornaments or clothing — her only expenditures were on cooking.

Lately there had been some spending for her work, but it was negligible — there was no reason whatsoever to hesitate over the cost of a few swords.

But it was simply her nature.

Krische was the type to save by default, and she was extraordinarily reluctant to spend money.

While it would be problematic if she developed a habit of extravagance, Bery did think it was time she learned something of how nobles managed their money.

Krische's sense of finances was, at best, that of a town girl who liked to cook — worlds apart from what her current station demanded.

"……If Bery says so, then, well."

"Yes. ……As I have told you before, the reason nobles receive large sums is to distribute what they have received to many people. Of course, saving for future large expenses is a good thing — but it must not become an end in itself."

"……Yes."

"Fufu, so long as you understand."

Bery patted Krische's head. Selene watched with an exasperated look and sighed.

"She's always so compliant when it comes from Bery."

"Good daily habits make all the difference."

"You're just spoiling her, honestly."

"That's right, all the best parts go to her……Argan-sama is unfai— *nm.*"

"No, Kreschenta. Left alone, you start saying bad things about Bery right away. You may only say 'wan' for a while."

Uuu — Kreschenta glared at Bery while gnawing on her cookie.

When Krische called her name once more, Kreschenta reluctantly let out a *wan*.

"That said, Kreschenta, you say it's cheap, but do you know how many of the cookies you're eating right now one pumpkin is worth? You should understand the value of a pumpkin a little better."

"Kr-Kreschenta didn't buy——"

"……Wan."

"Uuu……wan."

Being lectured by her older sister on the value of money — something stuck in Kreschenta's heart.

To the million-pumpkin woman — who could say nothing but 'wan' — Krische launched into a passionate explanation of the value of a pumpkin.
