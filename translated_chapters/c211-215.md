# Chapter 211: Ingredients

*——The estate in the royal capital.*

"……Please refrain from groping my body."

"Oh, please do not say such scandalous things. I would never do such a thing."

"Who knows. Considering what you did to onee-sama, even I have reason to be afraid."

"N-now listen here……"

Bery lifted Kreschenta from the bed in a princess carry, her cheeks flushing with an awkward expression, before letting out a weary sigh.

Ever since that incident, Kreschenta's verbal attacks had been of this nature.

For Bery, it was a troublesome weakness — difficult to address, and even harder to refute.

Kreschenta, held aloft like a princess, watched Bery's discomfort with evident amusement, wrapping her arms around that neck and letting out a smug little laugh.

She squeezed tightly against Bery's womanly figure, rubbing herself close in an exceedingly good mood — because she had just woken from her afternoon nap.

Having thoroughly dispelled her everyday fatigue using a body pillow named Bery Argan, she was now fully charged and brimming with energy.

With cookies popped into her mouth upon waking and no further engagements on her schedule, all that overflowing energy had nowhere to go — and so it was only natural that she would devote herself to the education of the Christand household's substandard head servant, Bery Argan.

"Ah, Bery-sama, the horse needs tending to……"

As the two emerged into the corridor, Elvena appeared at a quick trot and stopped at the sight of Bery carrying Kreschenta. Bery gave a wry smile and said,

"Thank you. I will be preparing dinner with Kreschenta-sama…… Please feel free to take a break."

"Quite so. Argan-sama keeps shirking her duties under the pretense of napping together, so do take some rest."

"……Yes."

Elvena gazed at the sight of the adorable queen clinging to Bery, gave a small laugh, excused herself, and left — entering Krische's room as they passed by.

She would be straightening the sheets.

Elvena was quite attentive to such small details.

"So, what shall we make today?"

"Hmm, let me think……"

Bery looked up at the ceiling with a girlish expression, lost in thought, then

"How about we knead together some ground meat and onion, and make hamburger steaks?"

She smiled softly at Kreschenta.

* * *

Neither the Alberan side nor the Elsren side had anything in particular to do, and there were no issues regarding battlefield cleanup.

Battlefield cleanup itself was nothing unusual.

Though the reason given was often for the mourning of the dead, in practice it was a custom rooted in strictly hygienic concerns.

Improperly buried decomposing corpses released harmful agents that cursed the living.

This teaching had been passed down in the regions around Alberan since ancient times, but several centuries ago, the queen of the era — Grabareine — had investigated records to establish the causal relationship between war and epidemic, widely spreading the knowledge that improperly treated battlefield corpses served as breeding grounds for disease.

If it were only soldiers dying on the battlefield, that would be one thing, but infectious diseases spread to unrelated towns and wreaked havoc.

While the scale and damage varied depending on the time and circumstances, epidemics could become catastrophes capable of shaking the foundations of a nation. This could not be left unaddressed, and even during wartime, it had become customary for both sides to cooperate in cleaning the battlefield and handling the aftermath.

For smaller battles, this was sometimes done at dusk, but in large-scale engagements such as this one, it was not unusual to spend an entire day on it.

Once an epidemic broke out, it was an unstoppable disaster.

Given that the victims included not just soldiers but commanders as well, few would refuse this except under the most extraordinary circumstances.

While autumn or winter would be less pressing, in spring with its rising temperatures — wanting to dispose of the corpses before they decomposed was only natural, and for the Elsren side, which happened to be downwind, addressing this was a matter of urgency.

Granting two days to the Alberan side was a problem, but they estimated it would take half a month to a full month to capture Wulfenite.

The notion of ignoring and stepping over decomposing corpses during that period to assault Wulfenite simply did not exist, and the decision was a reasonable one.

They dug large pits, hurled the bodies in, stacked them up, poured oil over them, and set them ablaze.

With numbers this large, careful work was impossible, and many half-burned corpses remained, but few cared to look. They did not want to see.

The smell of burning flesh — the sound of oil crackling.

The inside of the pits looked like hamburger steaks kneaded from ground meat.

Saliva involuntarily welled in mouths, and some, seized by the perverse sensation, vomited bile.

The psychological state of soldiers already handling 'dismembered human remains' was abysmal.

Ordinary battlefield corpses tended to have relatively minor damage, but the bodies here were parts of people — handling mutilated corpses was an entirely different matter in terms of morale.

The bodies carried out by prisoners from within Wulfenite's walls were more often damaged than not, and what the soldiers felt was not anger but fear.

The sight of corpses literally pulverized, and the feel of viscera.

Ground meat that had once been people.

Having seen what became of those who entered the northern section, they deteriorated psychologically as they carried out what was burial in name only.

Contrary to what Bazlar and Linkara had expected — very few among those who saw the bodies of their fallen comrades harbored hatred toward Alberinea.

What they felt toward the girl who had hacked corpses apart and flung them with trebuchets was nothing but terror.

And the men watching from atop the walls felt much the same.

Gazing down at the enemy soldiers performing 'burials' from a distance, what stirred in their chests was relief.

Had they been born in a different country, they might have been among them.

They watched the mountains of corpses loaded onto carts, watched the entrails that spilled and dragged along the ground, and were grateful above all else that they had been born in Alberan.

At the very least, they were not people who had to face Alberinea as an enemy.

Many of the soldiers on this eastern wall had heard Bazlar Lukazan's words.

They knew that his words were not entirely fabricated.

If one asked whether this was right or wrong, it was undeniably cruel.

And yet, just because Alberinea was such a monster, they had not the slightest intention of defying her.

While she was the most terrifying existence imaginable as an enemy, she was nonetheless the nation's savior — as an ally, there was no one more reassuring.

Their awe for the woman who had destroyed trebuchets through personal martial prowess alone had not vanished, and even now the enemy still held twice their numbers.

Everyone correctly recognized that this psychological and situational advantage had been created by her.

*'What worthless posturing. War is fought to win. And the side that kills more and breaks the enemy's morale wins. ……Respect for the dead, maintaining order — if you insist on that, then why not just have Krische fight one-on-one and settle it right now?'*

She was without question a merciless slaughterer, and yet her words held a certain truth.

The battlefield was a world that demanded beings like her.

What one desired on the battlefield was not a compassionate, gentle commander, but a strong one — even if cruel, even if mad.

Better to win through atrocity than lose while preaching morality — that reasoning was sound, and she was the very embodiment of it.

And Bazlar himself, who had avoided a fair fight with Alberinea and fled back in fear of defeat, had proven her mad correctness.

Far better than losing.

That was the excuse they made to themselves.

The soldiers on the wall glanced behind them, then turned their eyes to Alberinea, who had appeared atop the tower.

"Things are quite clean now. Progress looks good."

"Ugh, this is awful……"

Atop the tower stood the jade tiger and Krische.

The woman beside them — her long black hair tied back in a ponytail and streaming in the wind — furrowed her brow as she watched bodies being thrown into the pit and burned.

"……Well, it looks like there won't be many problems."

"That's right. Two days without fighting is the best outcome. Would you say the use of the corpses was effective?"

"A little too effective — this big sis is kinda pulling back a bit, but yeah, sure."

Kalua said with an exasperated tone, looking up at Krische.

"Ahaha, well, glad to see you're doing well. Baldy Captain and Skeleton Marshal's Assistant were both worried about whether bunny was pushing herself too hard. This big sis is a little relieved."

"Worried?"

"Yeah. Whether bunny was feeling lonely."

A gentle smile graced her beautiful features, and she leapt onto Gururun, softly stroking Krische's head.

Two humans were nothing — Gururun showed no concern whatsoever and yawned.

She would wake when hungry, but for a nocturnal creature, daytime was when drowsiness ran strongest.

Even having grown somewhat accustomed to life in the capital, the difference from years of established rhythm was not so easily overcome.

"Ehehe, Krische was maybe a little lonely……"

"There, there. You've gotten more honest compared to before. Big sis is happy."

She embraced the slender body and smiled. Krische smiled back — then, raising a finger somewhat proudly, said to Kalua,

"Hmph, Krische is different from before. Krische has grown a little."

"……? Huh, grown……"

Kalua studied the girl in her arms, tilting her head.

*Maybe she got a little taller*, she interpreted offhandedly.

While Krische appeared entirely unchanged both mentally and physically, she was the sort to detect the most infinitesimally small changes.

She would probably insist on calling it growth even if she had grown by a single hair's breadth.

"Oh, is that so? Now that you mention it, bunny does look a teeny bit more impressive."

"Really? Ehehe……"

When Kalua went along with it and offered praise, Krische placed her hands on her cheeks and swayed happily from side to side.

There was absolutely no change in the little princess, but if it made her happy, that was fine.

"Got anything to do?"

"Hmm, not really. Just checking on the Jaragasha and that's it. After that, Krische was thinking of doing some cooking……"

"Ooh, nice. It's been a while since bunny made something. What are you making?"

"Hmm, let's see……"

Krische gazed at the pit where corpses were being thrown in, wearing a girlish expression and lost in thought, then

"How about Krische kneads some ground meat and onion together, and makes hamburger steaks?"

She smiled softly at Kalua.

Kalua's gaze drifted to the 'hamburger steaks' in the pit ahead of Krische's line of sight, and with a twitch of her cheek, she shook her head. "I'll pass on that."

* * *

The two-day ceasefire ended, and nearly 5,000 prisoners were returned to Elsren.

However, most of them had suffered deep psychological wounds.

Having processed the bodies of their comrades and been forced to assist in the dismemberment, they were deeply terrified of Alberinea and had become useless as soldiers.

Bazlar Lukazan no longer considered them combat strength, and decided to assign them to rear support duties — supply escort and cargo transport rather than combat.

Soldiers with broken morale dragged down the morale of their allies.

Forcing them into charges would yield no results.

At least until Wulfenite was captured, they would be of no use.

The Holy Empire forces resumed their assault on Wulfenite the following morning.

However, this was merely pressure — Bazlar was planning, as with the northern section, a nighttime breakthrough raid with his elite troops.

The northern attempt had indeed ended in failure.

That said, the breakthrough itself had not failed.

What the enemy used were explosives utilizing mana crystals — information gathered from soldiers who barely escaped the northern wall and from prisoners, along with the intricate magical inscriptions etched on unexploded mana crystals.

He had not investigated the details due to the danger, but Bazlar judged that this was at least not something suited for mass production.

While the damage from the explosions had been devastating, if they could repeat explosions of that scale at will, the enemy could have afforded to wait longer.

They could have waited not just for the northern force, but until Bazlar's eastern force was also inside.

His side had not foreseen any of it — it would have been the perfect surprise attack.

Yet the enemy had not chosen that — afraid of the risk of luring enemies inside the walls?

No, even accounting for that risk, the benefit of annihilating this main army would have been enormous.

They had not chosen against it — they had been unable to choose it. That interpretation made far more sense.

Therefore, a second breakthrough would succeed.

He was certain of this, and in fact it would have been so.

A forced breakthrough by elites, opening the gates — the plan itself was not bad.

It would be quite difficult to defend against completely. In siege warfare, the attacker holds the initiative.

At the very least, taking advantage of the confusion to funnel a certain amount of forces inside the walls and disrupt the enemy should have been possible.

The other side had bought two days.

He did not think this was meaningless.

The enemy believed that buying time would give them a chance of winning.

Therefore — before that could happen, he would capture Wulfenite.

He was a decisive and valiant general.

He had none of an incompetent commander's indecisiveness.

But then, at dusk.

Listening to the various reports, Bazlar furrowed his brow.

"……The supplies haven't arrived?"

"Correct. Even at this hour, still nothing……"

Linkara answered pensively, and Bazlar narrowed his eyes.

The town they were using for the supply shuttle was two days' journey from here.

Supplies had been sent without issue five days ago, and according to schedule, the wagon train was supposed to return with supplies today.

Two days to return, one day for rest and loading, then two days back again.

"Any messengers?"

"None so far, it seems."

If the cycle had been disrupted, it would be because of some problem with loading the supplies — for instance, the required quantity not being gathered — or worsening weather.

Or else a revolt or riot had broken out in the town.

In either case, however, a mounted messenger would have been sent ahead.

Yet none had come — something was clearly wrong.

"……For now, send someone to the rear to check."

"Yes. ……Alkosars, take a few riders and go confirm."

The man who was called saluted and hurried outside.

Watching him go, Linkara turned his eyes to Bazlar.

"Neither from Arna in the north nor Garkha in the south — there hasn't been so much as a report of an attack. Everything's quiet."

"……The one in charge of the rear was Natrias, wasn't it."

Elsren's holy war was directed by the Papal Court — and when it was, they assembled what could only be called a massive logistical army.

This time, Minuos Natrias, the emperor's second son, had been appointed by the Papal Court to command it.

It was a placement born of political power balancing — ensuring that the Lukazan house could not monopolize the glory of defeating Alberan.

That said, it was merely the logistical army.

The military glory would go to Lukazan.

It was only natural that Natrias would find this disagreeable, and this too could be a form of political scheming.

The Holy Empire was vast, with an abundance of capable people.

No nation could match it head-on — and yet, whenever this great nation suffered a catastrophic defeat, it was invariably due to internal squabbling and politics.

Both Bazlar and Linkara, who knew this well, naturally directed their attention in that direction.

——It was only shortly after that they came to understand the situation.

"Messenger!! Forgive the intrusion during the council, Your Excellency!!"

"……What is it?"

"Sir! The enemy gates have opened!"

"Opened? Did we breach them?"

"No, something seems off — the walls haven't even been taken yet——"

Bazlar ignored the report and rose to his feet, stepping outside the tent.

Linkara and the others followed suit, trailing after Bazlar Lukazan.

——Just as reported, Wulfenite's gates stood open.

What emerged from them was not soldiers — but a black-painted giant.

The monster rumored to have rampaged through the northern section.

At the same time, the reconnaissance cavalry who should have been heading to the rear came galloping back at full speed before Bazlar.

"Your Excellency!! A twenty-thousand-strong enemy force from the east——"

He gazed at the enemy commander who had appeared on the wall, and tore the bread in his hands in half, top from bottom.

Atop the wall, upon a jade tiger, a girl — Krische Christand — was surrounded by soldiers in black-painted armor.

And there too stood Nozan Velraigh, clad in full plate.

"Sandwich the ground meat between bread. Ehehe, General Velraigh, would you like some?"

"No, I'll pass."

He declined with a wry smile, and Krische turned to the opposite side.

She offered one half of the bread to Kalua — "Would you like some?"

"Sure, I'll take it," Kalua said, taking it in hand and turning her gaze toward the horizon.

The walls were thirty shaku tall — one could see quite far from up here.

——Far further than the Holy Empire army could see.

"Everything according to plan, and good timing. ……Ojii-sama and the others have arrived too, so conditions are set."

Dusk — emerging from the indigo-stained distance were dark shapes.

And griffins, taking flight into the sky.

"General Velraigh."

"Sir."

Nozan raised his hand, and what resounded was the blare of trumpets.

Between day and night — beneath a sky of uncertain, mingling hues.

Countless flags bearing wings and a wolf were waved from every corner of Wulfenite.

"……All forces, march. It's over."

——Alberinea had swung the army from the kingdom's north in a wide arc and, just as in the previous campaign against Sarshenka, advanced them through the mountains of Mikuna.

The twenty thousand elite cut off the supply lines behind the Holy Empire's invasion force in a single stroke, seized their provisions, and headed west.

Leveraging the mobility of the griffin cavalry, they intercepted and dealt with enemy messengers while closing in on Wulfenite.

The Holy Empire's downfall was their own greed — many war historians note that the moment Alberinea appeared was the last fork in the road where they might have survived.

Alberinea was, at that time, truly an existence on par with a dragon.

# Chapter 212: Child of God

On the plains southwest of Wulfenite, a battle was underway between the Holy Empire's mounted archers and the Central General, Elzaldo Gokkalus.

From the watchtower of the fort — a blade-scarred face with one eye destroyed, a bristling mustache.

"Soldiers, do not think of this as battle. When they come, shoot. This is a simple task. All you need to do is turn any fool who charges out into a pincushion."

The powerfully built general issued his instructions to the soldiers in a calm — yet deeply resonant — voice.

It was an exceedingly simple battle.

It had been anticipated that the mounted archers would head for the kingdom's center.

Civilians in the kingdom's center had been temporarily evacuated, and their provisions were seized to force the enemy into attrition.

After the mounted archers moved through, countless prepared screens were spread wide across the plains, with ropes strung between them to form defenses against enemy arrows and breakthrough attempts — and with these as the backbone, they fought a thoroughly defensive battle.

Not only the archers, but even the infantry had been equipped with crossbows lent by Arna.

"With conditions this well-prepared, even a boy could handle this job."

"Indeed. One almost feels pity."

His adjutant nodded at Elzaldo's words and surveyed the enemy.

They had suffered heavy casualties in their first charge and had apparently concluded that a breakthrough would be difficult.

They loosed arrows from barely beyond the range of their bows, but that was also within range of Alberan's archers.

The enemy's attacks were forced into a scattered pattern, and half a ri away, the mounted archers were at an impasse. (T/N: "half a ri" here uses the tactical-arc ri ≈ 400m, so roughly 200m.)

In battles on open ground, there was nothing in this world that surpassed mounted archers.

The terror of mounted archers lay in their mobility.

They chose battlefields at will, seized the advantage before striking — their attacks were, in effect, perpetual ambushes.

And they never fell into disadvantage; they were never the ones ambushed.

If they could not win a fight, they simply fled.

They needed only engage in battles they could win. If they could not fight head-on, they severed the enemy's supply lines, or raided them on the march, whittling down the enemy's strength until they held the advantage.

But now, they had no such freedom.

The problem of depleted provisions had stolen their options.

Reduced to this, they were nothing more than archers who happened to be on horseback.

What had ever underpinned their strength was, ultimately, strategic circumstance.

Mounted as they were and presenting larger targets, they could not win a head-on fight against bowmen, nor did they possess the ability to take defensive positions on their own.

The two central armies already stationed at Wulfenite had been heavily allocated with archers.

Precisely in anticipation of this.

Losses were greater on the enemy's side than on theirs, who numbered twenty thousand.

The outcome had already been decided.

They had lost the moment they entered the royal capital's sphere.

"……Truly, the one to be feared is Alberinea."

The defensive war against the three great nations — she had ended it, right on schedule, in just over a month.

Nearly four hundred thousand in total, swept aside with ease.

Two months ago, this 'target practice tournament' that Elzaldo was conducting had already been planned.

At the opening of hostilities, he had thought the projected situation was too dismissive of the enemy — but everything had unfolded exactly as planned, and Alberinea had defeated two nations in half a month.

Honestly, Elzaldo could not begin to imagine what manner of magic she had used.

Ten thousand mounted archers — a formidable foe.

Ordinarily, a force of merely twenty thousand could not have dealt with them, yet Alberinea had shut them down completely.

With a single stratagem, Alberinea had dragged them directly in front of his position.

An easy fight — but not one that was Elzaldo's achievement.

What he was conducting was not annihilation but defense — even if someone other than Elzaldo had held this position, the outcome would not have changed.

"There is no room to use one's head, and as a battle it holds no interest, but…… one should be grateful that there are few dead among our troops. We will be able to send most of them home to their families."

Elzaldo Gokkalus spoke while carefully observing the condition of both the enemy and his own soldiers.

Reduce friendly casualties, increase enemy casualties.

Tactics were simply that — he believed it to be that straightforward.

No clever stratagems, no forcing a decisive victory.

This general's way of war — grinding down and exhausting the enemy to achieve victory — lacked any glamour, but in its place was a stability underpinned by meticulous calculation.

The complexion of friend and foe, the enemy's movements.

By keeping the battlefield undisrupted, he simply poured his energy into cold analysis.

"The horses won't hold out. They should pull back soon, but they'll come again at night. The enemy will target the right wing — have the ambush forces prepare accordingly."

He had observed the enemy commander repeatedly directing his gaze there.

He had thinned the left wing — the mountain side — but the enemy, wary that this blatant positioning concealed an ambush in the forest at the mountain's base, refused to strike there.

That said, there was no ambush there at all.

A bluff, based on the expectation that the enemy would be wary of their own accord.

He was defending a vast plain as it was. There was no reason to waste soldiers on empty deceptions.

The right wing had somewhat greater undulation in the terrain, making horse passage slightly more difficult.

That was why he had reduced the troops there — or so it appeared.

The enemy were light cavalry skilled at handling horses; they would attempt to cross here.

The ambush was on his side.

The enemy was flinching from the rain of arrows.

Having suffered heavy losses in the initial charge, their morale had dropped — and being pressed by hunger, they could not afford the time to recover it.

They would undoubtedly attempt to break through the right wing, prepared to accept some casualties.

What mattered in psychological warfare was letting the opponent think they had predicted your move.

Making them believe they had seen through your intent.

The enemy was wary of the trap suggested by the left wing's blatant positioning, and believed they had seen through it.

And on that basis, they had identified a gap in the right wing.

*I am superior; my opponent is inferior.*

Such thinking was endlessly seductive.

Especially in a desperate situation like this, above all else.

*The ability a general requires is the power to display 'just enough incompetence for the opponent to think they understand it.'*

Elzaldo did not rate his own abilities highly.

With his own strength, he would never achieve a glorious victory — and yet there was a clear reason he had accumulated countless victories without ever courting danger.

War was a battle between soldiers, but simultaneously a psychological contest between commanders.

And the defeat in that psychological contest was the defeat in the war.

There was no need to force a success.

If you could lure the opponent into failure, it amounted to the same thing.

He admired the brilliance of Alberinea, who had made the impossible possible, but he did not possess such capacity himself.

"I will take a nap in preparation for later. I leave the rest to you."

"Sir, leave it to me."

The virtue of Elzaldo Gokkalus lay in the cold-eyed composure with which he regarded his own capacity, more honestly than anyone.

* * *

——What descended upon the Holy Empire forces as night drew near was panic.

Commanders shouted to reorganize the battle lines, but that was the extent of it.

They were exhausted. Psychologically and physically both.

For the past two days they had buried the 'mutilated corpses' of their comrades, and today they had spent the entire day watching their fellows be killed climbing the walls.

Since that Alberinea had appeared, they had known not a single taste of victory or sense of accomplishment.

Perhaps the night raid planned for this evening might have restored some morale.

But that never took place — instead, they found themselves caught between forces to their front and rear.

The soldiers who had only yesterday been released from their 'labor detail' and were working in the encampment behind the lines stood dumbfounded at the sight of the twenty-thousand-strong army emerging from behind them.

They understood.

The reason for their casual release was simple.

Alberinea had freed them in order to kill the northern army's 'stragglers' anew.

They could imagine no other reason.

Every one of them understood the kind of being Alberinea was — that deranged butcher.

The devil who had smiled beside the dismembered bodies of their fellow soldiers, gazing down at them with perfect composure.

The army forming its battle lines behind them flew the kingdom's flag alongside the crest of lightning and hawk.

Krische Alberinea Christand.

What stood there was the Christand main army — Alberinea's own.

Many recognized the crest of the former lightning and hawk — that of Bogan Christand.

The hero of the kingdom who had defeated the Elsren Holy Empire time and again.

They knew that this crest marked the kingdom's strongest army.

If even Nozan Velraigh's corps — merely a single division commander under Christand — had shown such terrifying strength……

Then what must the strength of the Christand main army be?

Understanding the overwhelming power of its commander, Alberinea, the twenty thousand approaching from behind looked like a grand army of a hundred thousand.

Aside from the Black Flag Special Service, there was in truth no great difference in pure fighting strength between Velraigh's army and Alberinea's.

But the soldiers did not know this.

What they knew from rumor and the presence of Alberinea, combined with their psychological state, had robbed them of any capacity for sound judgment.

The Alberan side naturally had to leave troops on the walls and also allocate forces to deal with the army attacking from the south.

Fifteen thousand to the front, twenty thousand behind.

Even being caught between two forces with numbers reduced from forty thousand, the total was roughly equal to the Lukazan main army.

Including the southern force of thirty-five thousand, the Elsren side actually held a numerical advantage.

If every one of them had maintained their morale and executed commands flawlessly, there was a possibility this could have been a fair fight.

However, Alberinea had no intention of conducting a 'fair fight.'

What they first beheld was a blue-eyed giant standing over eight shaku tall, and griffin cavalry soaring into the sky behind them.

Scattering something as they passed over the Imperial battle lines — blue flashes of light.

What echoed were explosions and screams.

It was timed to exploit the chaos of their still-forming lines. The numbers were not great, and casualties amounted to a few hundred at most.

But the soldiers had no way of knowing that.

Having now witnessed up close the source of the blue light inside the northern walls, they stood aghast.

Merely flying overhead and scattering things from the sky.

That alone gave the enemy the means to kill them.

Against such an opponent, there was no hope of fighting.

"Steady yourselves! Casualties are light — form ranks!!"

A commander screamed.

But even that commander could not conceal his own trembling.

Bazlar Lukazan told them the enemy had little ammunition left, but — even if that were true, so long as the enemy could continue this, morale could not hold.

The appearance of the griffin cavalry in this situation was a threat equal to an army of ten thousand.

Following the giant, Velraigh's army too was gradually emerging into the open.

They were beginning to form their battle lines.

From atop the wall, Alberinea surveyed the scene against the backdrop of the sunset, her violet eyes gleaming.

A jade tiger that no ordinary human could even think of confronting — treated like a pet.

She sat upon it and looked down over the battlefield.

Beside her, a griffin cavalry mount descended, bearing a soldier in black-painted armor.

Her elegant silver hair shimmered gold in the sunset's light, stirred by the wind.

Despite her small frame, she seemed endlessly vast.

Perhaps — as a god.

Alberan — the nation where the child of God descended.

That legend was deeply rooted even in the Elsren Holy Empire, which had once been their other half.

A silent infant, born among a weak tribe.

Those once called children of God, the oracle-blessed.

Born with wisdom bestowed upon them, they had shared that power with their people, guided them, and swallowed neighboring tribes — and thus was born the founding king, Bazarishe.

From its very beginning, Alberan's history had always been alongside the silent infants.

And since the last true child of God — Elsreine — had been lost, it was said that the children of God had vanished from Alberan as well.

But what stood before their eyes now was precisely that.

The half-mythical tales of the silent infants.

The modern-day child of God, said to have won the true name of an ancient dragon, stood here now — having demonstrated power that lived up to every legend.

Destroying trebuchets with a single thrown spear, wielding weapons beyond comprehension, slaughtering soldiers like ants.

An evil one, reminiscent of Grabareine — the wicked child of God who had once swept across the continent and brought it to its knees in terror.

She was, without question, Alberan's accursed child.

Those who knew the history could barely think, and simply stared at her figure in a daze.

*They should never have challenged such a being.*

Elsreine, who had no children.

And so, no true child of God existed in Elsren.

Grabareine, who had borne many.

Her wicked bloodline remained deeply rooted in Alberan to this day.

Once more, the griffin cavalry took to the sky.

Alberinea — atop the jade tiger — the Heavenly Sword raised her blade with its sinuous, alluring curve toward the sky.

Fourteen black giants illuminated the dusk with their blue, single eyes.

And once more, explosions and screams engulfed them.

This was not a battle.

——The outcome had already been decided.

# Chapter 213: The Flower-Blooming Girl

——The Jaleia Gashea, or iron war-golems.

Fully armored, unfeeling, unmanned weapons.

A single sweep felled several soldiers at once, and the sight of them swinging both arms was as if mowing grass.

Given the circumstances of his excommunication and expulsion, and the fact that he had fought on the front lines even against his former countrymen in the Elsren campaign, there was no significant opposition.

"Yes. From now on, I shall endeavor all the more to be of service to Krische-sama——"

"——Aleha! That's the princesses' table — get over here!"

From the opposite side, Kokys waved him over with broad gestures, a wine bottle in one hand.

Aleha smiled wryly, gestured back, saluted Krische, excused himself, and departed.

"Nyan-nyan is such a loud person, honestly."

"Oh, let it be. Friends getting along is a fine thing."

Krische puffed her cheeks at Kokys's booming voice, and Selene poked those cheeks.

*Phyuu* — a silly deflating sound. Selene laughed and hugged Krische from behind.

"Finally it's over. I feel like a weight's been lifted from my shoulders, too. ……Krische, anything you want?"

"Want……"

"Yeah. I was thinking I'd give you a present — a reward."

Krische pondered, held in Selene's arms, pressing her index finger against her lips.

Then she looked at Bery.

"Um, ah……?"

She studied her from toes to the top of her head, staring intently, then smiled.

"Then, um…… Krische would like an apron dress like Bery's."

"……You."

Selene said with exasperation at the unambitious Krische.

And poked the forehead of Bery, who had gone red from — whatever she had imagined.

"……What are you imagining?"

"Uuu……"

"You thought she was going to say 'I want Bery as my present,' didn't you? You silly girl. Pervert."

"N-no, that's not……"

Anne watched this exchange with keen interest, saying nothing.

Krische, having heard the conversation, smiled and shook her head.

"Ehehe, Krische belongs to Ber——mmph."

"I feel like I've heard those words dozens of times already, so you don't need to say it."

Selene covered the girl's mouth with her hand.

"……My, how very lively."

And there appeared a princess clad in an elegant white gown.

Kreschenta, accompanied by Elvena.

Her face was plainly inscribed with *displeased*.

Having been left to handle the socializing with the nobility — as if that were Kreschenta's job alone — her cheeks were slightly puffed and her violet eyes glared at the three of them.

"Selene-sama, if you have finished your rounds, you should have come to rescue me first."

"No thank you. I was fed up with it too……"

Kreschenta handled the civil officials; Selene the military officers.

As the nation's first and second in rank, the number of people seeking conversation with them was extraordinary.

Just exchanging greetings with everyone was exhausting when there were over a hundred people present.

Selene was thoroughly sick of the greetings, which seemed to grow impossibly longer with each passing year.

Even during the days of 'Lady Christand,' there had been many — but now it was worse.

Kreschenta's rounds were likely to continue for a while yet, and Selene had used her as a decoy to make her own escape. But seeing the three of them gathered here, Kreschenta must have lost patience.

She appeared to have forced her way over somewhat abruptly.

"I apologize, Your Majesty. If only I had been a bit better at——"

"Never mind that. For someone still getting used to it, you're doing rather well."

Elvena spoke apologetically, and Kreschenta shook her head.

When a mistress cut a conversation short directly, it was somewhat rude.

Most people tried to extend the conversation — and one could not simply cut them off.

The role of a servant at such events was to unobtrusively address the mistress, providing an opening to wind down the conversation.

The mistress had other matters to attend to and was busy — creating that impression was the servant's job.

A single word at the right moment gave the mistress an excuse to conclude. The mistress could redirect the reason — *I would have loved to continue our conversation, but* — and the other party, understanding these social conventions, would respond in kind.

*I apologize for taking up your time* — and so the exchange of pleasantries would proceed.

For the other party, simply having their face and name remembered was sufficient.

Introduce oneself, be seen, leave a small impression — that was enough, and pushing further was considered 'unseemly.'

No one regarded such interruptions as rude.

Elvena's timing was one beat slower than Kreschenta's ideal, but compared to Anne's average of seven beats behind, it was satisfactory — not bad at all.

The truth was simply that Kreschenta was annoyed at being the only one working while the other three lounged on the balcony.

Kreschenta was many times faster than anyone else when it came to paperwork and affairs of state, but these greetings were time-consuming, yielded nothing tangible, and produced no results — she found not a shred of enjoyment in them.

Moreover, for Kreschenta — who had memorized the entire dossier of every person who set foot in the royal palace — they were an entirely unnecessary exercise. A waste.

And there she was, stuck with this waste, while the merry trio carried on.

This was absolutely unforgivable, and Kreschenta's cheeks puffed out irritably ——

"Here, Kreschenta."

But then — a piece of cake was offered to her lips.

Kreschenta froze, cast a quick glance at the surrounding eyes, and accepted the fork.

She was a queen — one of the royal house.

She knew nothing of retreat, a noble existence who met everything head-on, possessed of the capacity to accept all that came.

She chewed, swallowed.

Another piece of cake, offered from her sister's fork.

"Say ahhh."

"Mm…… ehehe, it's delicious……"

Kreschenta, supremely displeased.

And yet her anger was quelled in a mere two cakes.

She was, through and through, a woman helpless before her own desires.

# Chapter 215: Late-Blooming Loveliness

About two months later, the various regions had begun to settle.

The mop-up of remnants was finished, and while villagers and townspeople were being returned to their homes, it would still take another half year or so before the country fully regained its former calm.

Kreschenta dispatched envoys to each nation.

To the Arna Empire, envoys bearing thanks. To the three nations, envoys for negotiation — and to the surrounding smaller nations, envoys encouraging them to commence hostilities.

Against the three nations, weakened as they were, Alberan could attack if it wished.

Carving off portions of territory would be easy, and dismantling an entire nation was not out of the question — but naturally, the other side would resist fiercely.

Even if ultimate victory was assured, the result would be chaos.

A prolonged theater of war would exhaust the people's sentiment and the national coffers.

That said, simply issuing demands for reparations or territory from a position of strength through diplomacy would be ignored.

They were the ones who had raised their banner of righteous cause and invaded.

Having started the fight and lost, they could not bring themselves to utter such pathetically convenient words as *please forgive us*.

They could not cast away the pride that made their nation a nation.

Therefore, the outward diplomacy was one of dialogue and cooperation.

——*There were misunderstandings and miscommunications on both sides, and this war was a tragedy born from that.*

Those were the words Alberan would offer.

*Misunderstandings and miscommunications* — what a convenient phrase.

*We were friendly neighbors, but a small misunderstanding led to a quarrel, and things escalated to this. Now that things have settled down, shall we talk it out and make up?*

An approach phrased this way could draw the other side to the negotiating table while preserving their dignity.

There was, of course, the possibility that they would insist during the talks that there had been no misunderstanding or miscommunication at all — but neither side wanted to prolong the war.

It was all a matter of appearances — and they would almost never refuse this.

Then, demanding compensation for damages incurred from the invasion, reparations for the families of the fallen, and war costs was both reasonable and expected.

What the victor needed in this case was not a heavy hand, but the ability to play the victim convincingly.

Simultaneously, by scattering envoys to the surrounding nations to trumpet the war's end and describing the three nations' losses in appetizing terms, the rest would follow naturally.

No nation could maintain its sovereignty while being attacked by its neighbors and hammered by Alberan at the same time.

Inevitably, they would rush to conclude armistice treaties with Alberan in order to redirect their forces.

Without Alberan needing to press the advantage, the enemy would be forced to negotiate from a position of weakness.

All Alberan had to do was rebuild while subtly brandishing the threat of force.

——The nib was gold.

Moving an exquisite, one-of-a-kind fountain pen with elegant metalwork in fluid, unhesitating strokes, the queen opened her mouth — *ahhh* — and savored a cookie offered by her sister, then swallowed it.

Hair of gold shimmering with red — the beautiful and adorable Queen Kreschenta sat upon her sister's lap, her hand alone moving while she nuzzled her head against her sister's cheek.

The parchment before her was filled with impeccably formal, flowery language, yet she barely glanced at it.

"Here, ahhh."

"Ahhh, nom…… ehehe."

——It was a talent known as automatic writing.

Extraordinary perceptive ability and precision of motion.

The child of God, Kreschenta — behind her youthful appearance, she was nothing less than superhuman. With abilities like hers, composing a letter blindfolded was child's play.

She had letters to write.

But she wanted to be spoiled by her sister.

An ordinary person would have to give up one or the other in such a dilemma — but through sheer talent, she made possible the superhuman feat of 'multitasking' — being spoiled while writing letters.

Sitting sideways on her sister's lap, only her right hand moved across the table, and thus Kreschenta produced letter after letter.

As soon as she finished one and signed it, Anne promptly swapped in the next sheet of parchment. Elvena watched with an expression somewhere between awe and amazement, and once the ink dried, she rolled the parchment and pressed the golden royal seal into the wax.

An elegant sword and scabbard pointing downward, illuminated by the sun and a crescent moon.

The reverse of the upward-thrusting kingdom seal — bearing the meaning of sovereignty over the land — it was a royal seal permitted for use only by the monarch of Alberan. And yet Kreschenta had entrusted it to a servant who was not even a noblewoman.

Elvena, who had initially declined out of reverence, was by now thoroughly accustomed, and the entire process had become an assembly line.

Krische did not scold Kreschenta for her poor manners, but simply spoiled and doted on her endlessly, maintaining the pretense that she was 'helping with work.'

The reason being that slightly off to the side ——

"You! We just did the pale blue dress last time! Your choice! As you wished!!"

"My. That was a fair conclusion — the result of a contest. You lost at rock-paper-scissors, ojou-sama. An equal, good fight——"

"As if!! You cheater!"

The one holding the white-and-black-lined, crisply elegant dress was Bery Argan, her hair grown slightly longer.

And holding the vivid dress of black accented with red was Selene.

There, once again, a fierce battle was being waged.

This was precisely why Krische was clinging to Kreschenta. She did not want to be caught in the crossfire.

Having been dragged into it until the very last minute last time, using Kreschenta's work as a pretext was wonderfully convenient.

Tending to Kreschenta was the perfect excuse to ride out the storm with cookies in hand.

She was Krische Christand (slightly more mature).

"What I wanted was a fifty-fifty contest! If I had said 'let's settle it by the sword,' that would be unfair, wouldn't it?!"

"But you were the one who agreed to rock-paper-scissors, ojou-sama……"

"What you're doing is basically fraud!"

The previous time, as the deadline loomed, Selene had reluctantly accepted the suggestion of settling things fairly with rock-paper-scissors — but it was a trap laid by the witch, Bery Argan.

Using extraordinary visual acuity to read Selene's hand in rock-paper-scissors, Bery Argan had claimed a record of ten wins and two losses.

Having suffered four consecutive defeats, Selene eventually caught on and proposed blindfolded rock-paper-scissors with a referee — but Bery had known Selene since the day she was born.

From there, the psychological warfare began ——

*'I shall play rock next.'*

*'……Hmph, then if I play paper, I win.'*

— and so on. Once exchanges like that started, there was simply no way for Bery Argan to lose.

*'Oh my, why didn't you simply play paper? I declared rock, after all. To play scissors of all things……'*

*'Guh……'*

*'Next, let me see…… I'll go with scissors, perhaps.'*

She trapped the deeply-reading Selene, guided and manipulated her.

*She'll definitely play something different from what she declared next — paper.* Selene deliberated over whether to play scissors, but this was Bery Argan. Surely reading deep within the reading was correct.

Lost in such calculations, she was cleanly cut down — paper to scissors — and stood there, stunned.

*'Now then, next — on three.'*

*'——?!'*

After making Selene overthink twice and fail, she broke the pattern by skipping the declaration, cutting the flow — then declared again.

And yet Bery never lied. It was Selene, overthinking, who destroyed herself — and while Selene managed to eke out two wins, even if the match had been truly even, there was no recovering from a four-game losing streak. She was devastated by the absurd result of two wins and ten losses at rock-paper-scissors.

It felt as though her innermost thoughts were being read — no, Bery Argan had literally read Selene's psychology.

She was fiercely competitive, but by nature a girl who preferred straightforward, head-on contests.

For such a person to start second-guessing her opponent's declarations ——

When she overthought, she did not want a draw.

If she was going to second-guess, she was going to win — that was simply her nature.

And so Bery only needed to declare her hand and play exactly what she declared.

Selene would defeat herself. That was the trick.

——*Oh my, going out of your way to force a draw — what happened to you?*

Being mocked like that was worse than losing after a genuine battle of wits — and Bery understood perfectly that Selene would prefer the latter outcome.

If a tactic was not explicitly against the rules, she would use it without hesitation to seize the advantage, exploiting the opponent's suspicion and then forcing through the win on top of it.

Bery Argan was, fundamentally, an impossible woman.

"Oh…… has Krische-sama's apron dress arrived?"

A bell rang from below the estate, and Bery set her dress on the sofa.

Anne hurried out — "I'll go!" — and left the room.

Krische perked up with an "Ooh" of interest, and Selene, too, smiled.

"——Oh, fine. Let's drop the matter."

"……?"

"Your hair's gotten longer — hehe, it looks lovely."

Bery's red hair, which she had begun growing out since the recent war, now reached past her back.

With her adorable, girlish features, even a modest increase in length added striking beauty.

Selene set her dress aside, leaned in close, lifted a strand of that hair to examine it, and Bery blushed in confusion.

"Um……?"

Bery tilted her head at the sudden remark, and Selene smiled.

A premonition of trouble stirred in Bery's heart.

* * *

——It was a dress of dark indigo.

Cinching her already narrow waist, the back cut open and laced with leather cords.

The sweetheart neckline pushed her ample bosom upward, revealing a distinct valley of cleavage — but a sheer dark-indigo shawl draped demurely from the shoulders concealed the boldness.

The slightly narrow-cut skirt clung to the sensual curves from waist to legs, exuding allure and drawing out a womanly beauty that contrasted with the wearer's youthful features.

Apart from a long white ribbon at the rear waist and some woven lace, the design was relatively simple on the whole.

"I-I really do think this is too bold……"

"You look so childish that this is exactly right. If you wore something with less exposure and a fluttery flared skirt, you'd look like nothing but a young girl."

*Except for the chest*, Selene added, eyeing Bery's cleavage as if to glare.

Bery clutched her shawl to her chest, hiding it shyly.

Dark-indigo opera gloves reaching to the upper arms.

From the front, the only exposed skin was the shoulders and décolletage — not exactly excessive, but the impression of boldness came from her glamorous figure.

The line from her cinched waist to her shoulders, the generous bust, the slight height compensated by the heels of her long boots — the dark-indigo lines threw her feminine beauty into sharp relief.

"Ahhh…… magnificent. To finally have the honor of tailoring a dress for Lady Argan — I am deeply moved. It suits you wonderfully, Lady Argan."

"Th-thank you……"

The visitor was a tailor from Gurgain, one they had relied on for a long time.

White-streaked hair and a monocle.

His gentlemanly appearance made him look like a butler.

Unlike Kreschenta, who used tailors affiliated with the royal house, the Christand household had decided to continue using this Gurgain tailor even after relocating to the capital.

The royal tailors were naturally skilled, but also expensive.

This Gurgain tailor, on the other hand, was reasonably priced and equally skilled, and their long relationship meant the family continued to value his work.

His tastes also aligned with Selene's preference for simple elegance over showiness — a fact that weighed heavily in her fondness for him.

"Ehehe, you look wonderful, Bery-sama!"

Krische had already donned the apron dress that had arrived with the delivery and came running in from the next room.

She called out in a servant-like manner while clinging to Bery's arm, her skirt swishing happily.

Fairy-like to begin with.

Any garment looked as though it had been made for Krische, and there was nothing that did not suit her.

Bery flushed shyly, wearing a troubled expression, and stroked Krische's silver hair.

"……It suits you as well, Krische-sama. Hehe, I should have arranged this sooner."

"That's a loaded remark."

"O-ojou-sama……"

Bery's face went scarlet as her gaze darted between Selene and the tailor.

The tailor tilted his head in mild puzzlement and bowed.

"If the size is satisfactory, I shall take my leave. Should you have further orders, please do come to me again."

"Of course, thank you. Your dresses are always lovely — once things settle down, I may place another order."

"I'm grateful."

"Have you arranged lodging? We can prepare something for you here……"

"No, I plan to return to Gurgain today. With the victory celebrations, I've received many orders…… I confess, I imposed today, insisting that I simply had to see Lady Argan in her dress."

The post-war celebrations meant boom times for merchants.

Public spirits were high and festive; the government was placing orders in every direction for the rebuilding effort, and tailors were caught up in the wave.

Banquets were being held left and right by merchants and nobles for business purposes.

Many were preparing new dresses for the occasion.

He was no doubt genuinely busy.

"Hehe, I see. You've moved a bit farther from Gurgain now, but I wish you all the best in business."

"Thank you, those are most kind words."

"Elvena, please see him out."

She whispered something in Elvena's ear and pressed gold coins into her hand.

This purchase was Selene's personal expense.

Selene disliked wasteful spending, but she was never stingy about spending money when there was reason.

After the tailor left, Bery let out a quiet sigh and examined her reflection in the full-length mirror.

The last time she had worn a dress was as a girl — and not a happy memory.

"You don't look pleased."

After helping Krische change, Kreschenta had remained silent, simply staring at Bery.

Now she seized the arm opposite Krische's, gazing into the mirror.

"Even a packhorse can be dressed up. Even a base, graceless servant can be made presentable with proper attire."

"That's right! You look absolutely beautiful!" Anne chimed in enthusiastically, and Bery gave a wry smile.

"No…… it is, truly, a very fine dress. Beautiful, certainly."

Selene watched her expression, sat down in a chair, and spoke.

"With everything more or less sorted out, and things finally a bit calm…… it seemed like the right time to have a dress made for Bery. I'm not going to force you to wear that to the victory ceremony banquet or anything."

*But everyone deserves a chance to overcome things*, Selene said.

"Wearing a dress is supposed to be fun, I think. Getting dressed up, looking your best, being a flower at a splendid occasion. I've always liked dresses since I was little, and I still do."

She sipped her tea, gazed at the surface, then gave a bewitching smile and said it was perhaps a small indulgence.

"And I like seeing other people looking beautiful, just as much. ……If it's only that you're not fond of them, why not try it just once? You being you, it might wear you out — but if you try attending a banquet like that, I think you'd enjoy it well enough."

"Ojou-sama……"

Bery looked troubled and turned once more to the mirror.

She gazed at her own reflection in the dress, quietly closed her eyes, and nodded.

"……Yes."

Krische watched their exchange and recalled a conversation from some time ago.

She knew that Bery found wearing dresses difficult.

Half-understanding their exchange, she fell into thought, studied Kreschenta's knowing expression, and considered what role was expected of her.

"Then Krische will be Bery's attendant!"

"……What?"

The slightly-more-mature Krische (still quite a child) pinched her skirt to show off the apron dress and declared it.

Selene's expression went flat with bewilderment, but Krische failed to notice and beamed.

"Ehehe, since the apron dress just arrived, let's reverse things — Krische will be the servant, and Bery will be the lady! Krische will support Bery so she can enjoy herself!"

*Bery should enjoy herself wearing a dress.*

*Krische really liked the black-and-white apron dress.*

*This would also end her own dress arguments.*

Truly shooting three birds with a single arrow.

What Krische had arrived at was nothing less than a stroke of genius — the ultimate answer.

"Um, Krische-sama…… Krische-sama is, well, the guest of honor, so……"

The hero of overwhelming achievement who had led Alberan to victory.

For her to show up in an apron dress and spend the evening as Bery's attendant would be a baffling spectacle.

Bery tried to dissuade her, but ——

"……Um, is that a no?"

Forty-five degrees upward — sad, pleading eyes.

A strike that drove straight into the vital point.

——There was no one alive who could stop Krische in this state.

"I-it's not that I don't want it…… I-I'm very, very happy, but……"

"Bery, stop with that tone. If the answer is no, just say no……"

"……Is it no?"

"Ugh……"

Those sorrowful violet eyes were practically a weapon.

Kreschenta watched the two of them with a look of weary exasperation, then fixed her gaze on Bery.

"……Well, it's not as though either of you socializes with anyone outside your usual circle anyway. I see no harm in it. Even if onee-sama shows up in an apron dress, it's a victory ceremony. People more or less know what she's like."

Kreschenta said it as though she could not have cared less.
