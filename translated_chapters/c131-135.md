# Chapter 131: Old Enemies and Postponement

Leaving Karka, they picked up Mia and Kalua.

And so the carriage traveled on through the forest.

Once through this forest they would reach Gurgain; they would likely spend one night camping here.

The Black Flag Special Service soldiers took turns walking through the snow, warming their hands against the cold.

Inside the carriage, as always, there was a girl wrapped in a blanket, snuggled cozily atop her servant's lap, and——

"U-um……Krische-sama?"

She had been staring at her servant's face endlessly.

It wasn't that Bery was particularly bothered by being looked at, but when gazed upon so intensely, as though she were trying to bore a hole through her, Bery couldn't help feeling self-conscious, wondering if something was off about her face.

A faint blush rose to Bery's cheeks, and when she tilted her head in confusion, Krische happily clung to her.

"Ehehe, Bery is so warm!"

"……Is that so? I'm glad to hear it……"

If the girl had a tail, it would surely be wagging furiously from side to side.

Three days since leaving the village, she had been in an exceptionally good mood.

It wasn't that Bery couldn't think of a reason.

Surely part of it was that Krische had found some resolution in her relationship with Galla.

Perhaps it meant that Bery's words had made her that happy.

Whether Krische was different from other people or not was beside the point.

Even if you could guess at another person's thoughts, understanding everything was inherently impossible.

Bery settled on that convenient explanation and lightly kissed the girl who looked up at her expectantly, then embraced her.

Krische pressed herself close, repeating *like you, like you*.

More or less the same as always——the problem was always her defenselessness, which put Bery's self-control to the test. How could anyone be this defenseless? It was almost frightening.

Recalling their recent exchange, Bery thought she was hardly one to talk, yet even so, the weight of this affection and trust made her chest ache.

The twisted love inside Bery, the mischievous impulses, and all the rest.

Sealing all of it away with sheer willpower, Bery let out a quiet groan and turned her gaze to the window.

Outside, sunlight broke through the clouds, and judging by the shadows the trees cast, the hour was just before noon.

It was about time to stop for a break and prepare lunch.

They had declined to take any of the precious meat from the village, so all that remained was dried meat.

Today too, it would have to be soup. Fortunately, they had plenty of vegetables.

*Perhaps I could mince the dried meat finely, mix it with various things, and make dumplings*——just as she was thinking this,

"Ah, what is it?"

Krische suddenly sprang upright, her gaze darting left and right.

Listening intently——*she's rather dog-like in moments like this, just like Kreschenta*, Bery thought, a somewhat rude observation, but Krische's expression was clearly serious.

Those violet eyes were frozen, like a doll's.

Her brow furrowed, and she took the sword beside her and looked at Bery.

"Krische can hear swords clashing, and shouting too. ……A short distance ahead."

"Bandits, perhaps?"

"Maybe. Someone's carriage might be under attack. Bery, um……"

"……Yes. Please be careful."

Krische smiled, nodded, and stepped out of the carriage.

Snow everywhere. Krische's feet sank in.

Outside, Kalua and the others seemed to have noticed as well, their expressions tense, and Kirik called out, "Krische-sama."

"Can you see anything?"

"No, not from here. Most likely someone's carriage is being attacked by bandits. There used to be quite a lot of them around here……"

"Really? I hadn't heard much about that."

Kalua tilted her head, and Kirik explained.

"When I was young, this area was full of bandits. The reason the roads were safe was that the previous General Christand regularly conducted training exercises here that doubled as peacekeeping. It's near Gurgain, after all."

"Ah, right……and the north is pretty much empty right now."

Kalua nodded in understanding.

Krische surveyed the forest on both sides, thinking for a moment.

No enemy figures in sight. Enemy strength unknown.

If the enemy had noticed them——considering the possibility that their numbers might be even larger, it would be better to leave people here.

Under no circumstances could Bery be allowed to come to harm, and if that were the case, Krische alone might not have enough hands.

Krische considered the worst-case scenario and planned her approach.

Even if it was simply a merchant caravan under attack, if she went alone, she wouldn't get hurt.

"……This is troublesome. Krische will go take a look, but since we don't know the enemy's numbers, the rest of you guard Bery. If she gets so much as a scratch, Krische won't forgive you."

"Ahaha……roger that."

"Yes ma'am!"

After saying this, Krische pulled the cheeks of Mia, who was mumbling to herself with a troubled expression.

"……Mia, are you listening?"

"Uu, yes. It's just……I was impressed that everyone can hear sounds like that."

"Having poor hearing is a problem. When we get back, perhaps Krische should make you live blindfolded for a while."

"Ehh……?"

"Anyway, Krische is counting on you."

With that, Krische leaped up into the treetops.

Having her feet caught in the snow was no good at all.

And getting snow inside her boots was absolutely unacceptable.

She searched for bare, leafless dead trees, leaping from one to the next as she made her way through the forest.

The source of the sound was just a short hop through the trees, but——

"?!"

"They're not bandits. What happened here?"

By the time she arrived, everything was already over.

Three sled-carriages, with three merchants and drivers in total.

One of the escorts appeared to have a minor injury and was being treated by another, but the wound didn't look deep. He wouldn't die even if left alone.

Four escorts in total.

Of the other two, there was a handsome young man with golden-brown hair and a one-armed old man.

Both wore sand-colored cloaks and appeared to be mana users.

*Did he have his arm cut off?* She tilted her head for a moment, but he didn't seem to be in pain.

He must have been one-armed to begin with.

Both stared at Krische with looks of utter bewilderment, and Krische thought for a moment before speaking.

"Krische saw what looked like a bandit attack and came from over there to help, but……hmm?"

"Watch ou——"

Before the young man could finish his shout, Krische's hips were already twisting.

A bandit survivor had tried to grab her from behind——without even looking, Krische sent a kick backward, caving in his sternum with the reinforced heel of her boot.

There was no scream, only a dull thud, and the bandit's body was hurled into a tree behind her and crumpled to the ground.

Cardiac rupture. Instant death.

"It looks like things were mostly over already. Is everyone all right?"

The silver-haired girl in a white knit cap pulled down over her ears——the young man who had been about to rush to Krische's aid froze mid-motion.

So did everyone else who had been watching.

The old man alone recovered, slowly opening his mouth.

"You are……"

"Oh, Krische hasn't introduced herself yet. Krische Alberinea Christand. Krische would like to ask about the number of bandits……?"

She stepped forward and recognized the old man's face.

*Who was he again?* She cross-referenced her mental list of things-that-don't-matter, then clapped her hands.

"What a coincidence, Grizlandy-san. It seems you survived after all. That's good."

"Ah……you remembered me."

"Yes, Krische remembers. You're the man whose arm Krische cut off."

The young man beside him looked at Krische with even greater shock, then at the old man——Waltza.

Waltza Del Grizlandy. (E/N: Previously transled as Walter Del Gizlandy. I had a look at the raws ワルツァ --> WaRuTsuaa, Waltza is more accurate, Walter is ウォルタ WaaRuTa for Walter Zargan)

He was the aide to the general of the Holy Elsren Empire, and the old man whose arm Krische had severed.

The memory of exchanging polite greetings on the battlefield was still fresh, and in Krische's mind he was filed away as one of those peculiar old men.

The old man placed his remaining right hand over his chest in a salute.

"Ha ha, to think we'd meet in a place like this……one might call it a trick of the gods. I've wanted to thank you all this time. ……Young master, this girl is——"

"……Krische Christand."

The ambush from a total blind spot, countered without the slightest flicker of alarm or change in expression.

Ability completely at odds with the girl's appearance.

Even amid his surprise and confusion, the handsome golden-brown-haired young man——Aleha——nodded and drew a sharp breath.

"There's no need for thanks. So this man is the 'young master.' That said……"

Krische tilted her head, looking troubled.

"What are soldiers from the Empire doing in the kingdom? Depending on the reason, Krische may have to take you into custody……and this time you wouldn't be prisoners of war, so it wouldn't be house arrest——it'd be a cell, you know?"

Her right hand rested on the hilt of her sword, idly toying with it.

Foreign military personnel present in the kingdom without official justification.

The natural assumption would be infiltration for espionage or sabotage, and in most such cases the individuals would be tortured and disposed of without ever seeing the sun again.

No law existed that permitted soldiers to cross borders without authorization, so officially these people were not supposed to exist.

Since they would not be treated as soldiers, the Sacred Convention did not apply, and any punishment within kingdom law was permissible——the same as dealing with bandits.

Even for private travel, if they had submitted official documentation through proper channels, they would have kingdom officials accompanying them. The absence of such an escort meant they were illegal entrants.

*A bit more troublesome than bandits*, Krische thought, narrowing her eyes.

Whether interrogation or torture, she needed to bring them back in a condition where they could still talk.

She was considering severing the tendons in both their hands when Waltza dropped his sword to demonstrate he meant no harm.

"I am a man who has already renounced his position as an imperial noble. The young master beside me is the same——we are no longer imperial soldiers. We are simply travelers now……the Empire should have issued an official writ of excommunication; you can verify that on your end."

"……Excommunication? You mean the stripping of your titles?"

"Consider it something heavier than the stripping of titles. Reinstatement is not permitted. For the time being, I would ask that you take our word——we are simply travelers. I have discarded my family name, but I swear upon the name that remains: there is no falsehood in this."

"Muu……that makes things even more complicated……"

Krische groaned, then glanced at the driver who was watching them fearfully.

"Could you go back along this road a short distance and tell Krische's carriage to come here? It has the Christand banner, so you'll spot it right away."

"Y-yes! Right away……!"

A silver-haired, beautiful girl——Krische Christand, the one from the rumors.

And the bandit who had tried to grab her, killed instantly.

As if she were swatting an insect.

The girl standing there calmly conversing with the two men as though nothing had happened conjured every rumor about her into vivid reality, and the driver's legs broke into an immediate sprint.

Stumbling and tumbling through the snow, the terrified driver ran off.

Watching him from the corner of her eye, *will he be all right?*, Krische wondered, then——

"Well, never mind. Anyway, Krische's group was planning to have lunch, so let's go a bit further ahead, get set up, and we can talk after we eat."

"I, uh……?"

Krische decided to prioritize the rendezvous and the meal over what promised to be a lengthy conversation.

Burying the corpses in piled-up snow over frozen ground was out of the question.

After they regrouped, they had no choice but to toss the bodies a short way into the forest.

Having a meal in a blood-spattered mess was hardly pleasant.

Krische may have been unusual in most respects, but she possessed perfectly normal sensibilities about dirtiness, so they moved the carriage a little further along before stopping to eat.

The talk with the two men was postponed. They laid out large wooden boards on the snow and packed them down, then set up a campfire.

Digging through the snow every single time was tiresome, so during the day they often used boards as a floor like this.

The flames rose upward. The boards would get scorched at most, and once packed down with snow underneath, the surface was stable enough for a cooking area.

They spread several boards out as a prep station, and Krische and Bery selected their tools and ingredients and began cooking.

Boiling water alone could be done with the magic-crystal stove, but given the temperature, a proper campfire was better for both cooking and warmth.

"Um, is this really all right……?"

Bery cast a troubled glance behind her and asked Krische.

The others had set up their own campfire behind them, and not just the Black Flag soldiers and drivers——even Aleha and Waltza had joined in without complaint.

The Black Flag soldiers had been told by Krische to keep a casual eye on the two, so Kalua and Kirik were nominally on watch duty, but their expressions said they weren't quite sure what to make of this situation.

Only Mia, desperately trying to get her fire started, was her usual self.

Restraining the suspects would have been well within their authority in a case like this, but the two had declared they were no longer imperial soldiers.

The words *I swear upon my name* carried great weight among the nobility.

Krische had briefly explained the situation to Bery and the others, gauging their reactions, and the two men's willingness to hand over their swords and submit to restraint if needed showed no sign of deception.

Everyone agreed there was probably no danger, and Krische followed that consensus.

If Waltza's claims were true, the two were nothing more than innocent travelers.

Confining innocent people went against what Krische considered "bad things," so her conclusion was to hold their weapons, keep them under observation, and otherwise leave them be.

The opponents were two unarmed men, and Krische was at Bery's side——she was confident she could handle it even if they suddenly attacked.

As a result, in Krische's priorities, meal preparation outranked any conversation with them.

The men were former imperial soldiers——and not just any soldiers, but a former general and his former aide.

This was Aleha Sarshenka, who had easily slain the kingdom's eastern general Kalmeda in the previous war.

Though defeated by Bogan, his martial reputation was widely known even within the kingdom, and yet here was Krische, pushing him aside because she was hungry——everyone was exasperated.

"Krische told them she'd prepare lunch first, eat, and then hear them out, so it's fine."

"I-is that so……"

"Yes. Ehehe, and it's nice that we got some meat, isn't it?"

As it happened, one of the merchants had salted pork, which he was happy to share.

Thanks to that, Krische was in high spirits and couldn't care less about the two men.

Bery smiled wryly, not quite sure what to do, but decided it was better than a tense atmosphere during mealtime and tried not to worry about it.

"That's true, it should turn out much tastier than dried meat."

"Yes. Krische will knead the dumplings, so Bery, could you handle the knife?"

"Are you sure? It'll be cold, you know."

Krische was particularly sensitive to cold and chill.

Letting out a little groan, she fidgeted and sidled up to Bery, looking up at her.

"Bery always says that, and then Krische always makes Bery do the cold things……today Krische will handle the cold part."

"Fufu, you needn't worry about that……but, very well, I'll leave it to you this time."

Smiling at the Krische who pressed against her.

Red hair and silver, the two standing so close they seemed to lean into each other——the sight was something beyond words, and the others watched with indescribable expressions.

Always, always, their closeness transcended what could be called simply "getting along," but no one had anything to say about it anymore.

Aleha and Waltza, watching along with the merchants and drivers, found the almost sinfully intimate picture of the two hard to look away from, stealing glances——but were hardly in a position to comment.

Apart from those two, conversation was nonexistent, and an acutely awkward silence hung in the air around the neglected group.

To break that silence, Squad Nineteen leader Kirik considerately spoke up.

"S-still……to encounter the renowned Aleha-dono in a place like this. Coincidence is a remarkable thing. It's an honor."

The handsome young man with golden-brown hair——Aleha——brushed the snow from his head and took up the conversation.

His voice was gentle.

"Honored……I'd assumed people in the kingdom would despise me. And there's no need for such formality——I'm neither a soldier nor a noble any longer."

"Ha ha, even so, one can't help being formal with a former general. If you're concerned about having been an imperial soldier, please set your mind at ease."

Kirik glanced at the other six and continued.

"No one here fought in the previous war. Everyone enlisted during the recent civil war——there are those among us who respect you as a warrior, but none who hold a personal grudge."

"I see. I'll take that gratefully, then. But……since the civil war?"

Hand on his chin, Aleha studied the seven.

His sharp eyes observed their bearing and the mana clinging to their bodies like a faint shimmer, and he furrowed his brow. Then, as though something had clicked, he murmured, "I see."

"A direct-command elite unit under Krische Christand, rivaling Granmeld Varkus's Wolf Pack……I've heard rumors of the Black Century. ……Are you them?"

"That's right. We're serving as Krische-sama's escort at the moment."

Their black-lacquered leather armor and the black cloaks bearing the crescent-skull insignia darkly rendered on the chest.

All seven were mana users——it was immediately clear that these were no ordinary soldiers assigned to guard a dignitary.

But there was something unusual, too.

Normally, nobles would be selected for such duties based on trustworthiness, ability, and various other considerations, but these men didn't look like nobles at all.

That meant they were a private force——and cross-referencing with the rumors he'd heard, the answer came at once.

"……It seems the honor is ours."

The Sword Princess who was said to have led the civil war to victory——Krische Christand.

Mentioned alongside her name was the Black Century, an elite force selected from commoners.

Since they were commoners, the populace celebrated their exploits with great enthusiasm. Aleha had assumed there was some exaggeration in the tales of their feats, but he now realized that was a mistake.

The answer to those seemingly implausible exploits stood right before him.

The Black Century was very likely a unit composed entirely of mana users.

By composing a unit exclusively of mana users, they enabled uniquely advanced coordination and mobile warfare, specializing in creating overwhelming local superiority.

More flexible than cavalry, able to traverse any terrain.

Able to blend into battle lines and conceal themselves in forests.

And with unmatched frontal combat power, this small century-sized elite force could execute headhunting tactics even against corps-sized opponents acting alone.

Aleha immediately grasped the terrifying implications of a unit composed entirely of mana users.

Had they been present at the Battle of Arzlen River, even retreat might have been impossible.

"I've heard of the unit's reputation. You accomplished quite a great deal, I'm told. 'The kingdom's strongest century'——I heard that many times during my travels."

"We're grateful for those words. That said……this isn't false modesty, but our achievements owe more to her power than to us. Krische-sama is, in name and truth, without equal in the world……though you wouldn't think so to look at her."

Kirik directed his gaze toward Krische, happily cooking with Bery.

Her two tail-like lengths of silver hair swayed back and forth, her cute white knit cap pulled down over her ears.

From behind, she was simply a child having fun.

Kirik felt somewhat absurd presenting *that* and explaining that she was the kingdom's strongest warrior, but since that was the norm, there was nothing to be done.

But Aleha showed genuine understanding of his words, and the old soldier Waltza beside him did the same.

The casual display of skill just now was only natural——they both knew that it was she who had trapped his army of thirty thousand, lured them into the mire, and brought about his defeat.

She had built a diabolical fortress across the river, circled around the mountains, and raided his command post. She took the heads of the vice-general and his aide.

When Waltza and his elites stood as the rearguard, she appeared alone, severed his arm, and took him prisoner——they had never forgotten her name.

Aleha's advance——no, the Empire's entire offensive——had been thrown into disarray by this one girl.

He had been defeated on the battlefield.

She was the stronger, and he was the weaker. That was all.

He bore no grudge, but meeting her like this felt like the hand of the gods at work.

He had a keen interest in what kind of person she was.

And regardless of anything else, there was the debt of gratitude for sparing Waltza's life.

"'The Tragedy of Arzlen River,' was it……heh, I've never once forgotten that day. I'd always wanted to meet her and talk, if only once. Coming to the kingdom may have been the best choice of all, wouldn't you say, Waltza?"

"……Indeed. One might call it divine guidance, young master."

Kirik asked, his expression turning slightly serious, with a note of hesitation.

"A journey……but what brings the two of you to the kingdom?"

"We were held responsible for the defeat, you see. Excommunicated by the Pontifical Court——roughly the equivalent of having your titles stripped, on your end. We couldn't remain in the Empire, and when we considered where to go……coming here was something like a pilgrimage, I suppose."

"……A pilgrimage?"

"Yes," Aleha nodded, his eyes drifting to the Christand crest——lightning and falcon——painted on the carriage.

"Yes——a personal pilgrimage."

* * *

Minced meat and vegetables wrapped in cabbage leaves, tossed into a tomato-based soup.

It was just a simple combination of soup and bread for a midday meal, but the aroma of herbs and the richness of melting meat and vegetables rose into the air, stimulating the appetite through the nose.

"It's so good……Ehehe, chopping the vegetables finely was the right idea. Even in such a short time, they melt properly."

"Fufu, no more taste-testing, now. It's mealtime."

"Uu……yes."

Bowls of soup were distributed to everyone.

For something prepared in less than an hour from start to finish, it was remarkably elaborate. The Black Flag soldiers, accustomed to this, took it in stride, but the others accepted their bowls with surprise.

Once she confirmed everyone had been served, Krische announced with barely contained eagerness,

"Well then, shall we eat?"

She was the first to bring the soup to her lips, eating blissfully.

Aleha followed suit, taking a sip and nodding at the flavor——unexpectedly refined for an outdoor meal——then addressed Krische.

"General Christand——should I call you that?"

"Mm……you can call Krische anything, really……oh, but the family name might be confusing since it's the same as Selene's, so Krische might be better. Krische isn't even sure whether she should be called general."

"Then, Krische-sama. ……Regarding the matter from earlier——the reason we have come to the kingdom."

Aleha's expression turned serious as he began.

What would the former general of the Holy Empire, Aleha Sarshenka, say?

Everyone held their breath——but Krische, who had been half-expecting this, was quick to react.

"Wait, please. That sounds like it'll take a while, and the soup will get co——ah, no, let's save it for later, when we can settle down. Krische thinks it's probably a very complicat——*ahem*, a very important and serious matter, so it probably shouldn't be discussed during a meal."

Aleha, and everyone listening, froze at Krische's spectacularly transparent attempt at wrapping her words in niceties.

# Chapter 132: Along the Journey

In the end, Aleha did not get to talk after the meal.

Krische murmured that the sky looked a bit ominous and told him she wanted to get moving early and reach their planned stopping point.

Looking at the sky, it did seem like the atmosphere had changed, and Aleha agreed.

He watched Krische cheerfully cleaning up with Bery.

*Then perhaps during the journey?* But no——the enclosed space of the carriage posed a risk in case of emergency.

Krische would not allow the two inside, and the agony of walking together in the freezing cold while talking was a choice that never entered her considerations to begin with.

*We'll talk once we arrive at the campsite*, she told them, putting it off again——

*'It looks like the wind will pick up tonight, so we should finish eating early.'*

And so they were made to wait even further.

The reasons were clear enough. But by now, both Aleha and Waltza fully understood that the girl had absolutely no interest in them.

They could feel, almost painfully, that she found them a bother, and yet they could hardly leave things as they were. How they would be treated going forward was, for them at least, an extremely serious matter——they needed to at least have that conversation.

Given her casual attitude, it could be read as a signal that she didn't intend to do anything harsh, but clear words were always important.

After the meal, Krische yawned and was about to push things off again with *let's turn in early tonight and continue tomorrow*, but Bery, feeling genuinely sorry for the two, threw them a lifeline.

*'K-Krische-sama, you really should at least discuss the matter of these two gentlemen's future……'*

And so, at last, as night fell, a proper discussion was arranged.

Inside the tent——firmly secured to the trees with ropes and fitted with a large ventilation opening——the campfire blazed brightly. Seated on a crate right beside it, atop Bery's lap, Krische warmed her hands at the fire and regarded the two men.

Since Krische and Bery normally slept in the relatively well-insulated carriage, this tent was meant for everyone else. It had been set up large enough for all the drivers included, but with fourteen people it was inevitably cramped.

Kirik and two others on night watch, plus one of the injured, were asked to step outside.

From beyond the tent flap came the sound of Mia scolding Kalua for hogging the spot by the fire.

"The suspicion against you has not been cleared, for the record, but Krische has determined there's no need for restraints and will not bind you. Krische will hold your swords as a precaution and return them once the matter is resolved. Any questions?"

"None. It's a reasonable measure."

Krische nodded in satisfaction at Aleha's answer and continued.

"Krische happens to be heading back to the capital, so you will accompany us there. You may have your own reasons for traveling, but proving your innocence should take priority. Please accept the inconvenience——until then, you are not permitted to act on your own. Understand that you will be under constant watch."

*That's all*, Krische said, looking up at Bery with satisfaction and rising to leave.

Her tone said the conversation was over——she was heading back to the carriage to sleep.

Bery smiled wryly, wrapped both arms around Krische's waist, and stopped her.

The two men clearly still wanted to say something, and after being pushed back and back and back like this, she couldn't help but feel sorry for them.

She directed her gaze toward Aleha, who had been watching the scene with half-exasperated bemusement. He gave Bery a slight nod of thanks and spoke.

"I've heard the Christand residence is in Gurgain. ……If it would be acceptable, I'd like to lay flowers at the grave of the former General Christand."

Krische, who had been looking puzzled at being held by Bery, considered his words for a moment and replied.

"The Christand grave isn't in Gurgain, it's in the capital. The Christands relocated from the north to the center. Since we're going to the same place, that works out perfectly——Krische can show you when we arrive."

"……I see. Then, I'm grateful."

Seeing Aleha bow his head in thanks, Krische looked up at Bery again with satisfaction.

The talk was finished. It was Krische's bedtime.

She subtly tried to signal Bery that she wanted to go back to the carriage, and Bery, understanding the appeal perfectly, popped a cookie into Krische's mouth to pin her in place.

"Though you were enemies, General Christand was a warrior I aspired to become and deeply respected. Death on the battlefield may be a warrior's fate, but please accept my heartfelt condolences."

"Mugu, um……thank you……?"

"If only I could have met him once and spoken with him directly……but there's no use in regret."

Krische sat on Bery's lap, listening with an uncertain look, unsure how to respond.

Aleha didn't mind, and continued.

"I crossed swords with General Christand several times, even before I became a general. Each time I lost, and driven by that frustration, I repeatedly obtained records of his battles, studied them, and constructed countermeasures and tactics……And then, in my first confrontation as a general——with the advantage in numbers, with all the effort I'd poured into this, I thought I couldn't possibly lose……yet I was defeated again."

He gazed at the silver-haired girl.

Sitting on her servant's lap, wrapped in a knit hat and a properly wound muffler.

Ordinarily, nobles——especially military ones——would be concerned with appearances, but this girl showed no such concern at all, bundled up in her cozy outfit, warming her hands by the fire.

Delicate, childlike.

Showing not a shred of interest in Aleha and his companion, who had been her enemies.

Yet it was precisely this contrast between her true abilities and her appearance and temperament that made her seem a genius, he supposed.

She was nothing like Bogan——a proper soldier seasoned by experience. Her image was the farthest thing from the military ideal.

What this girl possessed was simply a freakish, distorted brilliance, and it was precisely that which had brought him to crushing defeat. So Aleha felt.

Even if he attacked right now, she would cut him down with a single stroke.

Even sitting there, her sword was at her side, without a hint of vulnerability or carelessness.

"I set out on this journey to re-examine my own arrogance. Until now, everything I needed to learn as a soldier was taught to me by the subordinates I lost and by General Christand. That's why the kingdom felt like the right place for this journey."

He had never felt that his abilities were inferior to others.

Despite having cause for concern, he had ultimately trusted his own talent too much and pressed forward——that arrogance was surely the root of his defeat.

And that was precisely why Aleha had lost as he deserved to lose.

To this small girl.

"Meeting you like this, Krische-sama, in the course of that journey, may be the greatest stroke of luck I could ask for. ……First and foremost, I wish to express my gratitude for sparing Waltza's life."

Aleha rose to his feet, and Waltza did the same.

As though by prior agreement, both bowed deeply.

For nobles——even former nobles——to bow their heads like this was a rare thing.

Bery looked on with mild surprise, and Krische tilted her head, thinking it over before replying.

"The fighting at that point was essentially over, and Krische simply acted in accordance with the Sacred Convention. Krische did treat the wound, but Krische was also the one who cut his arm off, and it was Selene who decided to take him prisoner——if you want to give thanks, it seems more proper to direct it at Selene……"

"Even so, there aren't many people who can rescue an opponent they've just been fighting without a moment's hesitation. ……Waltza is like a parent to me, so I am deeply grateful to you for saving his life."

Aleha raised his head.

"Meeting in a place like this may well be the hand of the gods at work……perhaps telling me to repay this debt. I'm a man free of all obligations now——someday, in some form, I will properly return this kindness."

"Hmm……thanks, huh……"

*Being thanked feels somewhat odd to Krische*, she thought, but she shifted her gaze to Waltza.

# Chapter 133: The Endless Sky

A snowfield in the moonlight.

Standing opposite her was a young man with golden-brown hair——once the youngest to earn the holy knight's title, former general Aleha Sarshenka.

And the other, reaching only to his chest, a small silver-haired girl.

Alberinea, the kingdom's strongest——Krische Christand.

The two took their stances, facing each other——or rather, they didn't.

"……Excuse me?"

Aleha was perplexed by the girl who, curved sword in her right hand, was endlessly stamping down the snow at her feet.

She was apparently very insistent on keeping snow out of her boots.

Krische paid no attention to Aleha, absorbed in her task.

"……? Whenever you're ready."

"Easy for you to say, but, well……"

This was terribly awkward.

When Aleha called out, Krische tilted her head quizzically and continued her work.

"Krische doesn't bother with stances, so go ahead whenever you like. Krische wants to get back to the carriage soon, too."

She stifled a small yawn. Not a shred of motivation.

Against Aleha, she behaved as though she were humoring a child.

And in truth, that was probably the extent of her regard for him.

That attitude——even the mild-tempered Aleha felt a faint stir of irritation.

He had trained his sword since childhood, and took considerable pride in it.

He knew that this girl had easily cut down the elites Waltza commanded, but this was complacency taken too far.

"……Then I won't hold back."

He narrowed those keen eyes and raised his sword.

Left foot forward, a left-side lead stance, both hands held high above his head——a high guard. (T/N: 大上段, *jōdan*, the overhead stance in Japanese swordsmanship.)

Average height for a man, neither large nor small, but his limbs were long and elegantly extended, and in this stance he carried a definite aura of menace.

The distance between them was roughly three *ken*. A single sword-stroke's reach. (T/N: 三間 ≈ approximately 6 yards / 5.5 meters.)

The wind stirred his golden-brown hair and sand-colored cloak; powdered snow drifted through the air, and Krische, catching it on her face, frowned and glanced upwind——

——In that instant, Aleha surged forward.

The knee-deep snow meant nothing to him; what he unleashed was a white tempest.

The polar opposite of his usual gentle bearing——a single step of pure, unleashed aggression.

Before she could blink, he was in front of Krische, his blade already descending.

A charge followed by a downward strike from the high guard.

The high guard was an easy stance to read, but by eliminating the need to raise the sword, the advantage was tremendous speed.

A stroke carrying the full force of velocity and body weight could not be blocked.

Any ordinary opponent would be bisected before they could react.

And yet, Aleha was certain this girl would evade it.

Therefore, this was not his killing stroke.

The follow-up upward slash——that was Aleha's true aim.

With this high-low combination, Aleha had cut down many a formidable warrior.

Someone among the spectators let out a cry like a scream, but——

"?!"

What Aleha saw in that instant was Krische's back, spinning.

*Why*——before the thought could form, the sensation of the sword vanished from his hands.

A dull metallic ring sounded, and what he glimpsed was the heel of a boot.

A spinning back kick, twisting at the hips.

She had kicked his fastest stroke aside with her heel from the flank,

"……Honestly, you got snow all over Krische."

And at some point she had transferred the curved sword to her left hand and laid its edge against Aleha's neck.

"M-my sword, that I just bought——!"

"There, there. Sit."

Mia's scream-like wail rang out, but it didn't reach Aleha's ears.

Krische patted the snow off her cloak, brushed it from her muffler and hat, and glanced at Aleha.

In those violet eyes there was no surprise, only a cold gleam, devoid of emotion.

"You're reasonably fast, but the high guard is easy to read, so that won't do. You were planning to finish with the rising slash or a horizontal sweep, but you should consider the possibility of someone matching you from the side. Not many people could manage it with their foot like Krische, but there should be……hmm, the occasional person who could at least deflect with a sword."

Aleha stood stunned, then quickly recovered from his daze.

"……I see. You're beyond anything I imagined."

"Obviously. You lost because you failed to gauge your opponent's ability."

Krische stated this as a matter of course and adjusted her hat.

Her movements hadn't been blindingly fast.

If anything, they looked leisurely——the bare minimum.

She had simply read Aleha's sword and timed her response to match.

The precision of her movements, and her insight.

She could match a heel to a swung blade with ease——she existed on a plane far above him.

"Against someone as small as Krische, you'd be better off using tight, precise strokes at the very edge of your reach. Hooking with the tip……you stepped in too far, too eager to finish."

"Hmm, then it would go like this."

She slipped past his left side.

The blade pressed flush against his neck.

"Left foot forward, left-side lead into a high guard——the descending stroke necessarily travels from your upper right to lower left. So before that, Krische slips past your left side and takes your neck. Reverse if you switch feet. With that much forward momentum, you can't kill your speed or stop your body."

Krische pressed a fingertip to her lips, thinking.

"Even a mana user with excellent physical ability can't defy the laws of physics or the structure of the human body, starting with the joints. If you assess your opponent's maximum speed, maximum acceleration, and the range of their blade, the answer of what to do follows necessarily."

Her words were simple and direct, but they described the ideal of swordsmanship——what might be called the ultimate art.

To perceive everything in a fraction of a second and seize the initiative.

Easy enough to understand in words, but impossibly difficult to execute.

And yet she clearly stood at that very summit.

Krische glanced obviously at the carriage, clearly wanting to leave, but Aleha paid no mind and retrieved his sword.

Feeling overwhelmed by such a vast gap in ability——outside of early childhood, this was perhaps the first time.

He wasn't being stubborn about it, but something was welling up inside him.

"Please."

He raised his sword and fixed his gaze on the girl.

* * *

So he believed it was only a matter of time.

Kalua, too, looked at him with exasperation and leaned back on her hands.

"Thirty years, huh……sounds like there's a long road ahead."

"Twenty……three or four."

"It may not go smoothly, but……I've made up my mind. If this sword is needed, I'll take it up for the kingdom. Is that all right with you, Waltza?"

As though pouring all his feeling into what he had found sooner than he ever expected.

# Chapter 134: Den of Evil

The White City——the royal capital, Albernaria.

In the audience chamber of its royal castle, a session was underway.

"With all due respect, I feel this is excessively heavy-handed, Your Majesty."

Rows of pillars supported a ceiling so high one had to crane one's neck to see it.

Blood-red carpet with gold trim.

The nobles arrayed on either side were all great houses of renown, and at the far center——atop a small flight of steps, seated upon the throne, was a small girl.

Golden hair tinged red in the streaming sunlight, and keen violet eyes.

Clad in a white dress, her figure was nothing short of beautiful.

Queen Kreschenta tilted her head at the words of her subject——Duke Arcasakos——and made a face of gentle concern.

"But, ojii-sama, restoring the kingdom's finances after the ruin left by my uncle requires everyone's cooperation. Military reorganization is an urgent matter of national defense. Surely you understand that?"

"But even so, reducing the stipend——"

Among the nobility, certain individuals were entrusted with administrative territories.

However, this was merely administration——in principle, all land belonged to the king, and the taxes collected from it were likewise the king's. The "stipend" was a portion of that tax revenue granted to nobles who managed administrative territories, under those very pretenses.

Kreschenta intended to reduce these stipends to put the nation's finances in order, but naturally, enthusiastic agreement from the nobles was out of the question.

Nobody was happy about a pay cut.

The thin, long-bearded old man——Duke Arcasakos——said exactly what she had expected.

"Your Majesty has invested in various new undertakings, beginning with the military academy……if we are to look at the finances, surely that is where we should begin. If the stipend is reduced on a mere whim, our livelihoods will suffer."

"The military academy has gratifyingly received generous donations from many nobles, so the expenditure from the royal treasury is practically nil……and as for the civic schools, we are essentially only establishing the framework. The subsidies involved are trivial little things."

Civic schools——a structure that could barely be called a proper school.

Most towns already possessed government-owned assembly halls or similar facilities.

By repurposing these rarely-used buildings as educational spaces once a month and having publicly recruited teachers instruct in reading and arithmetic, the system would take shape.

It was not compulsory; merely encouraged.

Teachers would receive a living wage, and children who attended classes would be given at least a piece of bread——a small savings on food costs for the common people.

She expected a reasonable number of children to come.

Nobles with administrative territories would be ordered to implement this system, the costs coming from their own coffers.

It could be framed as charity for children who lacked learning opportunities, aimed at long-term national strength——no one could refuse such a premise outright.

A full school would strain the national budget, but expenditures were minimized.

Donations would surely come for this too, and once a modest initial investment set things in motion, the system would develop on its own.

Once it took shape, proper schools could be established.

Raise the intellectual level of the populace, and eventually use examinations to select officials who could replace the current crop of incompetent nobles.

A plan spanning decades——this was merely the beginning.

She had no intention of allowing complaints, and Kreschenta flicked her gaze toward one of the nobles.

"D-Duke Arcasakos, you go too far in addressing Her Majesty."

"……You."

"We all agreed to these measures previously. I, for one, believe the reduction in stipends is unavoidable, as Her Majesty states. Relations with our three neighboring nations remain precarious——is the kingdom not the foundation upon which we all stand?"

The young margrave who spoke up was pale and visibly sweating.

Arcasakos glared at him as though he wished to kill him on the spot, but the man deliberately avoided meeting his eyes.

And following his lead, several others voiced their agreement.

Every one of them had been aligned with Arcasakos, and the old man's face flushed crimson.

*You've betrayed me*, he breathed, his knuckles going white.

Kreschenta laughed inwardly.

The secret ledger left behind by the great merchant her sister had eliminated——Roland Seba.

The legacy of a man who had been entrenched in the kingdom's center was proving enormously useful.

The names of many powerful nobles were recorded in those pages.

From slave trading to the fencing of stolen goods, and even illicit drugs——the ledger was the perfect weapon for chipping away at the Arcasakos faction's strength.

"It pains me as well. Of course, I promise to restore the stipends once the kingdom has recovered. Ten years at most——won't you cooperate for that long?"

"……It seems I am the only one making a fuss. Forgive me, Your Majesty."

Arcasakos trembled as he bowed his head.

Kreschenta regarded his anguished face with a smile.

"Not at all——it is I who am making unreasonable demands. Please, raise your head."

"……Yes."

Kreschenta wore the face of a queen as she gazed at that expression of bitter resignation.

"Someday, I swear I will repay your loyalty with something worthy. ……For now, please accept that."

*——Though by that time, you will no longer be here.*

She appended the thought mockingly in her mind, and turned her attention to what lay ahead.

An array of curios adorned the shelves and walls, and every chair and desk was of the finest quality.

On that desk——

"Damn it all! Those two-faced curs, every last one, bewitched by that cursed child!!"

The long-bearded old man——Arcasakos——slammed his favorite wine goblet onto the desk in a fury.

Fragments flew, and a servant let out a cry, but Arcasakos ignored it.

Fahre Arcasakos——a lean man who appeared middle-aged——furrowed his brow at his father's display, then sank onto the central sofa with a sigh.

He spat the words and thrust the empty goblet toward the servant.

*Mine too*, Fahre said, and the servant hurried to oblige.

The young man was a member of a branch family——it wouldn't matter much if he overheard.

His expression had reversed entirely from moments ago——he wore a genial smile.

# Chapter 135: Black Wings over the Capital

Blue-white in the moonlight.

The royal capital, Albernaria——the White City was held in a cold stillness, the very air hard and crystalline, silence filling every space.

In the first-class quarter within the city walls, on its most prestigious lot, stood a great mansion with a red roof.

The grand estate of the ducal house of Arcasakos.

Looking down upon it from a height of one hundred and fifty shaku——from the Great Bell Tower——was a single shadow. (T/N: 百五十尺 ≈ approximately 150 feet / 45 meters.)

Wrapped head to toe in a black cloak, peeking out from the hood were silver hair and a face of white beauty.

And jewel-like violet eyes.

"……It's cold."

Burying her nose in her black muffler, Krische studied the layout of the mansion below.

A U-shaped, two-story building. Arcasakos's bedchamber was apparently on the west side, opposite from where she stood on the east.

There was an orchard in the garden to the front, but nothing else that offered concealment.

They had likely cleared sightlines deliberately. Around ten armed guards with torches patrolled in pairs.

Several windows had been left open despite the cold, with figures visible inside. Perimeter surveillance, no doubt.

An unusual level of security for the well-policed first-class quarter.

Whether it was self-awareness of the enemies he'd made, or simply his nature.

Either way, it was completely irrelevant.

"Let's get this over with."

One yawn.

She threw herself lightly from the bell tower, then kicked powerfully off the wall at roughly mid-height.

Converting the downward momentum into horizontal velocity, she caught the wind in her cloak by gripping its hem with both hands and glided——landing with a roll on the mansion's red roof.

Having infiltrated without being noticed, she walked across the roof to the west side.

This was the tallest building in the area, with a spacious garden.

Even a mana user couldn't jump from a neighboring rooftop, so no one watched the sky, and it seemed no one had anticipated someone leaping from the bell tower either.

She pattered along, watching the serious-faced men below as they chatted and scanned the perimeter, and rendered the harsh verdict that they were competent guards but lacking in imagination.

Perhaps the Black Flag Special Service should be put through more advanced, more imaginative training drills.

Mentally composing a grueling training schedule, she dropped down onto the balcony of the bedchamber.

Here, too, four men stood guard on the outside of the iron railings, diligently patrolling.

She watched the backs of their heads from above, decided there was no issue, and looked at the door leading inside.

A white, double-leaf door, barred from the inside with a crossbar lock.

It was of fine quality, with no gap to exploit. *Can't open it normally*, she thought, placing her hand against it.

She manipulated the metal fittings with magic, released the lock, and slipped inside as though nothing had happened, drawing her knife.

There lay the figure of an old man, breathing softly in his sleep——Duke Arcasakos.

Kreschenta squeezed Krische's arm tight and said,

Selene sighed again and pinched the cheek of the happily-clinging Kreschenta.

*What are you doing!*, Kreschenta swatted her hand away and glared at Selene.

Puffing out her cheeks.

"A military upbringing like yours may not teach you this, Selene-sama, but when there's anxiety left unchecked in the palace, you have to suspect every trivial thing like that. I'd prefer to use political means to remove him, of course, but that would take time and be tedious. This way is faster."

*It's quicker to just kill him*——Kreschenta turned around on Krische's lap and clung to her.

Neither offering advice nor admonishing.
