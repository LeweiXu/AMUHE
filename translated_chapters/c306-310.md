# Chapter 306: Reine — VII

Riding one horse after another to reach the royal castle.

Without properly eating or sleeping, making straight for Fiiri's room.

Fiiri's room was empty, and a thin film of dust had settled over the shelves and paintings.

She owned many things, but Fiiri had been fastidious. The scene was enough, at least, to announce her absence.

"……Elsreine-sama."

"……Where is Fiiri?"

The one peering through the wide-open door was the lady-in-waiting Libinia. She quietly shook her chestnut-colored hair from side to side.

Her face was dark.

"About two weeks ago, while she was tending the flower bed……a knife, to the chest."

"The culprit?"

"A servant of the royal estate, Nasel. However, he took his own life……the Riselnia family he belonged to has been disbanded, and a thorough investigation was conducted, but the reason……"

She knew his face. They had never spoken.

"……Why Fiiri, of all people?"

"I don't know. ……Why Fiiri-sama had to be killed, I……"

She covered her face to hide her tears and sank to her knees.

It made no sense.

Fiiri was a servant, and had nothing to do with politics. She was not the sort of person who would make enemies, and not the sort of person capable of it. If it had been a random act perhaps it could be explained away, but given what Gorzarius had told her and the way Fiiri had spoken, it was clearly aimed at her.

Reine passed the weeping woman and climbed the stairs.

The servants looked at Reine with something like fear, or pity, or grief.

She knocked at the office door and a voice said, *come in*.

Her mother was working at her desk, and looked up as Reine entered.

"……You're here rather quickly."

"Kaa-sama — did you kill her?"

Her mother sighed, wearily, as if troubled, and then smiled. Then she stood and came closer.

"What would I gain from doing something like that? I have no desire for you to hate me."

"Kaa-sama hated Fiiri."

"That, certainly, is true."

Reine stepped back from her mother's reaching hand. Then she glared at her.

Her mother's face took on a troubled expression, her hand hovering uncertainly.

"Reine did as kaa-sama asked. I disposed of all of Rougran-niisama and the others, and put down the rebellion, and until now I've been a good girl about everything. So why — why did you kill Fiiri?"

"Why would you assume it was me?"

"……Who else would kill Fiiri, and for what reason?"

Her mother sighed again.

*Calm down,* she said.

"Someone has been filling your head with nonsense. ……Look at the situation — perhaps the goal is to use this to make us fall out with each other? It's true, I did try to find some way to remove that girl from your life. I considered killing her, but if that were my intention I would have done it long ago, and I wouldn't do it so carelessly."

"……Recently, Seil-niisama told me he had spoken with Fiiri. Wasn't that what displeased you as well, kaa-sama?"

"……You suspect me relentlessly."

"Then who would do such a thing?"

*We're going in circles,* her mother sighed.

"Suppose, hypothetically, that I did kill her — what would you do? Would you kill me in revenge for her?"

"That's……"

"……Rest for a while, Reine. You're exhausted and your head isn't working properly. You look dreadful. Let us speak calmly again after that."

Always that gentle voice.

Her mother was always gentle with Reine.

Reine had never doubted the words that said she was loved, and even now she did not doubt them.

*——Yet toward everyone else, she was not.*

Her mother hated everything except Reine.

Perhaps this too was love, in her mother's eyes.

Perhaps she hadn't wanted to grieve Reine — yet even so.

"……Reine, Reine doesn't really understand kaa-sama."

For just a moment, her mother's face stiffened.

"Reine has heard that before you were born, it was very hard for a long time. That you couldn't trust anyone anymore……"

*It is the King's Sickness,* Gorzarius had said.

The retainers who had remonstrated with her mother and been executed had said much the same thing, and most likely Fiiri too had wanted to do something about it.

Whenever Reine said she wanted her mother to change, Fiiri had always looked pleased.

"But there are good people like Fiiri in the world, and Reine only wanted to build that small paradise kaa-sama once spoke of, with such people……someday, with kaa-sama, and Fiiri……for everyone to live happily together. That was all……"

That was all it had ever been.

She loved both her mother and Fiiri, and she had only wanted them to live happily together.

She had only wanted to tell her mother, to make her realize what was good about Fiiri.

Her vision blurred and a sob escaped her.

Her chest was tight and she could not breathe properly.

Why had it not reached her?

The only thing she had wanted to convey to her mother — why had that not reached her?

"Did kaa-sama find it unpleasant that Reine thought that way? Is it because Reine is foolish, because Reine can't think the same way kaa-sama does, that……"

"Reine, that's not it, I——"

"It's all right. ……I don't want to talk to kaa-sama anymore."

"……!"

Reine ignored her mother's call and left the office.

She ran straight out, mounted her horse, left the royal estate, and rode out through the city gates.

*Why,* she kept repeating.

*——I don't understand. Why Fiiri?*

*……Regrettably, it can be no one other than Her Majesty the Queen. A murder within the royal estate……and what is more, while targeting Her Majesty herself would be understandable, deliberately targeting a servant to kill her — it is difficult to imagine anyone doing something so pointless and senseless.*

The exchange spun over and over in her head.

*From what Your Highness has told me, Her Majesty appears to have found Fiiri an irritant. The quarrels between the two of you were always over that servant……could it be that Her Majesty felt threatened by Fiiri, the one Your Highness held dear?*

*Threatened……*

*The fear that Your Highness might drift away from her. To Her Majesty, Your Highness is an irreplaceable singular existence — it is inevitable that she would fear losing you.*

*But Fiiri never……and Reine wouldn't——*

*Yes, but……Her Majesty is ill. Deeply so, to the point of being unable to trust anyone but Your Highness. She may even, at the bottom of her heart, doubt Your Highness herself.*

Before she had left for the capital, while preparing her horse, Gorzarius had spoken to her with a sorrowful expression, as though the words were difficult to say.

*What a grievous state of affairs,* he had sighed, and yet he laid out many things for the stunned Reine to hear.

It was too certain to dismiss as unbelievable, too weighty to simply cut away.

As he said — her mother had hated Fiiri, and had sighed every time she saw Reine being influenced by her.

They had only just had another quarrel about it — just as she had removed Fiiri from Reine's immediate service, Reine had always feared her mother would punish Fiiri, and had tried to be careful.

But that anxiety had always existed in the back of her mind, and so she could not deny his words.

*……Could it be a false report? That it wasn't, that it wasn't Fiiri, but someone else……*

*……I have only heard this secondhand rather than confirmed it myself, but……please leave this with me. You should confirm it yourself — that is probably for the best.*

Then he knelt, and Gorzarius continued.

*……I am your subject, and I will wait here. ——Elsreine-sama.*

Her head swam, her body was heavy.

She wondered what she would do when she returned, but she had wanted, at least, to get away from the capital.

She rode and rode, changing horses, heading simply east.

She slept and ate in the saddle, and days passed like that.

"Reine……"

"……Seil-niisama."

The one who appeared on horseback with a small escort was Seil.

He looked at Reine with a stern, furrowed brow.

"……Where are you going?"

"That is no concern of Seil-niisama's."

"It is very much my concern. ……Are you intending to betray Mother?"

When Reine did not answer and tried to ride around him, the escort cavalry moved with equally stern faces to block her path.

"……Please move aside."

"Calm down. You are only confused. Did Gorzarius say something to you? Don't trust that man. He won't follow my orders, he uses the army for his own ends, and he's trying to use you for some scheme."

Seil kept repeating, *calm down.*

"If you move, the kingdom will split in two. It will become a drawn-out war. ……That is not, at the least, what Fiiri would have wanted——"

"……What did Seil-niisama speak to Fiiri about?"

"……?"

"Benuure said it was for Seil-niisama's sake……didn't you fill Fiiri's head with worthless things as well? Isn't that why it came to this?"

Seil froze, and Reine leapt from her horse to his.

"……Ugh……!?"

Seized by the collar and slammed back-first to the ground, a naked blade was at his throat.

Reine's purple eyes, gazing down from just above, were cold and full of hatred.

"……Don't ever let Reine see your face again. If you get in my way any further, Reine will kill every one of you here and now."

"……Reine……"

She said her piece and remounted.

The eyes of the girl who looked so young were frozen cold, and even the battle-hardened cavalrymen who had been blocking the road trembled like boys and found themselves clearing the way without thinking.

Reine rode off again, and Seil did nothing but slam his fist into the earth.

A tyrant who enforced oppressive rule, executed many upright people, and sought to grip the world through fear alone — Grabareine.

By her side, the one who had tried alone to correct that broken heart — Elsreine — was forced into a war she had not wanted against the brothers who had risen for the people's sake, lamented it, and was in the end subjected to the cruelty of having even her beloved servant killed.

Queen Grabareine had no heart left.

And so Princess Elsreine rose up and raised the flag of rebellion against Queen Grabareine.

That tale spread in an instant, and when it reached the ears of those who had been devastated by the princes' crushing defeat, great numbers of common people flooded in from all over to the eastern provinces.

The only existence capable of standing against the ever-victorious Grabareine was Elsreine alone.

She had demonstrated her brilliance in the war against the fierce and courageous princes, and she had shown lenient treatment toward the soldiers who had participated in the rebellion.

Soldiers who could have faced immediate execution — she declared that the guilt of those who had been incited was not heavy and released the majority of them, and it was said that her reason for personally charging into the enemy headquarters and cutting down her brothers in four duels had been precisely that.

The reduction in taxes following the rebellion's suppression — lenient and humane treatment that had originally been planned to stabilize the country — had been the carrot following the whip, but those who had followed Elsreine spoke of it as the princess's warmth and compassion, spreading the narrative of good and evil.

In barely a single month, even those who had not participated in the princes' rebellion had begun to rise up.

"Elsreine-sama, how does this find you? Perhaps some fresh air outside would——"

"Rukazan, please leave me alone."

"……That won't do. The common people are gathering as well. Many in the eastern provinces have begun to show signs of submission. It is time for Elsreine-sama to look forward."

Alberan rebel forces, headquarters Renaria.

The largest city in the eastern provinces, and the former royal capital of a kingdom Grabareine had destroyed.

In its castle, Gorzarius came to see the Elsreine who lay in bed in her room day after day.

"Look forward — and do what? ……Reine doesn't want to do anything."

"If you remain like this, Fiiri too will grieve."

"……Fiiri is already gone."

"Indeed, she may no longer be alive."

Elsreine frowned and sat up.

Gorzarius looked out the window and spoke.

"Yet……her words are living in your heart, Elsreine-sama. Were the days she spent alive meaningless? Would you say that all the things she was born and taught you carried no meaning?"

"That's……"

"I understand how you feel, grieving Fiiri's death. She too died on the way……surely with much left undone. But for you to pass your time in idleness like this is tantamount to denying the life she lived."

Reine's eyes went wide, and Gorzarius closed his.

"Why do you suppose soldiers like us risk our lives in battle?"

"……Ah, um……"

"Most are not as strong as you. That I am alive at all is luck……a gamble that even at those odds doesn't balance out, that probably looks foolish from your perspective, Elsreine-sama."

She thought for a moment and nodded.

"From your point of view, everyone is an extremely fragile creature. And yet they choose to fight. ……They might become merchants or craftsmen, work fields — there are so many paths to live, yet what is the reason to risk one's life in battle?"

Gorzarius turned to face her and looked at her straight.

"……It is a wish, Elsreine-sama."

"A wish……"

"That even if they die, what they were protecting will live in peace. A wish like that……that is precisely why they can sometimes become stepping stones, can even accept death. Because they believe that those who survive will surely fulfill their wish for them."

Then he smiled gently.

"A wish more precious than one's own life……Fiiri surely had one as well. She was surely thinking of it in her final moments……would you, without fulfilling it, spend the rest of your life simply lamenting Fiiri's death like this?"

"Reine……"

"From where I stand, that is far more grievous. Because the many words Fiiri spun for Elsreine-sama would have no meaning whatsoever, and would simply vanish."

That was a grievous thing, certainly.

Fiiri would surely not be pleased that Reine was like this.

"……What do you think, Elsreine-sama? Won't you fulfill her wish together with us?"

"With you and Rukazan……?"

"A peaceful world without war……a small paradise, I believe you called it."

"A small……paradise……"

What floated before her was something Fiiri had once drawn.

Everyone smiling and happy, a place where swords and armor lay discarded.

"Certainly it will be a difficult path. To unite many hearts as one and build such a world……yet it cannot be a wish that will never be fulfilled. At least if one continues to wish for it, I believe one can approach it little by little."

"…………"

"……In name, we are now rebels against Her Majesty the Queen. But you still cannot bring yourself to hate Her Majesty completely, can you, Elsreine-sama?"

She nodded.

Soldiers had gathered in more than sufficient numbers, and the kingdom's side was unstable. Many voices had come calling for her to lead this force without delay, attack the capital, and destroy it — but Reine had been shaking her head.

*Why did you kill Fiiri,* she kept thinking, yet she could not bring herself to wish her mother dead.

She knew her mother loved her, and Reine loved her mother too, and respected her.

She was simply sad.

That was all Reine had. It was simply sad — irrevocably, unforgivably sad — that her mother had done something that could never be undone.

"From the moment you returned from the capital, I sensed that was so. Had that been your intention, you would have acted on it then. ……You are a kind person, Elsreine-sama; even now you love Her Majesty."

She nodded, and *all is well*, Gorzarius smiled.

"You need not listen to the words of fools who act on emotion. They will settle down in time. And for that reason too — we need a new name, not as the Alberan rebel forces."

"A name……"

"Yes, as a new place Elsreine-sama will build — as a new nation."

"……Build……a nation?"

"Yes. That would surely be best."

Gorzarius turned his eyes back to the window.

In the sunlight, the voices of birds trilled.

"……We wielded force, proclaimed Her Majesty the Queen an evil tyrant, gathered many common people and nobles……but I too do not think Her Majesty is truly such a person. She was simply subjected to much malice, and came to trust no one……a pitiable person."

"……That……"

"It is not my wish to drag Her Majesty from the throne, nor to raise a rebellion. Only……I wish Her Majesty to understand our feelings, and yours, Elsreine-sama……that is all."

*Words alone will not be enough,* Gorzarius said.

Squinting in the dazzling light.

"……But even if words cannot reach her, if a nation of the kind Elsreine-sama wishes for could be built……if we could show her a place where everyone lives happily and in peace, perhaps that could clear the cloud from Her Majesty's heart. ……I believe there are things that come through, as in Elsreine-sama's paintings."

Reine watched such a figure, and cast her eyes down.

After a moment, she asked.

"……Do you truly think so?"

"Yes. At the least I believe it, and I will lend what little strength I have in cooperation. ……If Elsreine-sama has need of me."

Reine rose from the bed and stepped from it onto the carpet.

Gorzarius went down on one knee and bowed his head.

"……Elsuren, please."

"Elsuren……"

"That……the name just now."

*Is it strange?* Reine asked, and Gorzarius gave a wry smile.

"A small paradise……as a name for a nation, it seems a little incongruous."

"Ah, if it won't do……"

"However……it is a fine name with a wish in it."

Gorzarius removed his sword from his belt and raised it before Reine like an offering.

"……Would you accept this Gorzarius Rukazan as the first subject of that Elsuren?"

Reine looked slightly taken aback, but quickly smiled.

She took it, said *yes*, and returned the sword with a bow of her head to his waiting hands.

*Elsuren* — the word Fiiri had spoken, with a smile, came to mind.

*——He still did not look like a loyal retainer.*

# Chapter 307: Reine — VIII

——The year 203 of the Alberan Kingdom calendar.

The fracturing of the continental superpower Alberan, and the birth of the Elsren Republic.

The eastern Alberan territories that had only recently submitted to Grabareine — the various powers in the continental center rejoiced at the founding of a nation opposed to Alberan. Before official establishment, it had taken the form of an anti-Alberan coalition, a web of mutual defense agreements, but Elsreine established a central parliament to strengthen her ties with them.

She incorporated powerful figures from across the region as delegates, and in the time Alberan spent grappling with internal problems born of its fracturing, she assembled the shape of a state with astonishing speed.

This was largely due to Elsreine's overwhelming display of military talent in the recent Alberan civil war, and the near-divine skill with which she resolved the various practical problems involved in founding a nation.

It was said she possessed a magical eye that saw the future; using the gathered funds as capital, she turned enormous profits in a brief span, and built a financial foundation for the otherwise resourceless rebel army.

*Swallow one month and you have a nation, swallow two and you have a great power.*

In an extremely brief span, Elsreine came to control the economy of the continental center, and the great merchants of each region, astounded by her *alchemy*, rushed one after another to the capital Renaria.

The reason the two great powers — later the three Grand Ducal houses of the Elsren Holy Empire, the Natrias and Liberslebe families — declared their participation is said to lie there as well.

"The Chairman's sentiments are admirable……however, given the current situation we should be stabilizing the country internally and preparing for the Alberan invasion that is certain to come. The establishment of orphanages should be deferred — time and budget should be directed elsewhere."

"That's certainly true, and yet……"

The first conference chamber in the heart of Renaria.

Men gathered around a great round table — the most powerful nobles in Elsren.

The golden-haired girl sitting at the head of the table against the large surrounding map — Reine — wore a troubled face, considering how to phrase it.

Elsren was territory that had only recently been incorporated into Alberan.

There were many war orphans from Alberan's invasion, the public order was in disorder, and stability was a distant prospect.

Originally, after the rebellion, the plan had been to provide generous care to address such problems and restore the peace — and becoming the Elsren Republic had not changed that plan.

The difference in the situation, however, was the presence of Alberan as a powerful hostile nation.

The delegates prioritized military expansion and wanted to direct funds there.

That reasoning was understandable, but as far as a defensive war went, the current capacity was more than sufficient.

They seemed to have conquest of Alberan as their goal, and disagreements had arisen.

Reine had no thought of killing her mother or conquering Alberan.

She only wanted to make this country a good one where everyone could be happy.

Simply that.

But that was only Reine's reasoning. Most of these delegates had been on the side that was conquered by Alberan — she could understand their reasons for hating her mother, could not flatly deny them, and she had no right to do so either.

Reine was the chairperson; the nation's affairs had to be deliberated together.

"Well, let's wait. What the Chairperson is concerned about is ten, twenty years hence — increasing the military must be kept to a minimum or expenditure mounts. The Chairperson believes the current defensive capability is sufficient……am I right?"

"Yes, Liberslebe has it exactly. Reine believes that in a purely defensive war, we currently have the strength to hold against — kaa……against Alberan."

"I too share some of the others' concerns……yet at least in military matters the Chairperson surpasses everyone here, and listening to her, I see no great error in her assessment. Is there anyone who disputes this?"

The man seated beside her was Kirias Liberslebe, lean and willowy.

His face appeared gentle at first glance, but his eyes were sharp, and they seemed to appraise the delegates.

"You all seem to be overly swayed by emotion. Alberan is formidable however one looks at it……defeating it with a makeshift force is difficult. Even assuming everything went perfectly, the best we could achieve would be a stalemate. ……And if that happened, we would ultimately be beaten by Alberan's national strength. We do not have the foundation for a prolonged war."

Reine nodded at having her thoughts spoken for her.

Kirias was the most influential voice in the parliament.

He was quite sharp and eloquent, and understood her intentions well.

"This country is overflowing with orphans. Ten years from now they will sink into lawlessness and severely disrupt the public order. They are like a debt — let them compound and they will bankrupt the nation. Refuse one gold coin that must be paid now and in ten years it will demand ten, twenty……perhaps a hundred gold coins. By then it will be too late."

*What we must think about is this nation's future,* Kirias continued.

"Now is the time to shore up this nation's foundations. A nation is what allows you, gentlemen, to fulfill the Alberan defeat you desire. The Chairperson is not proposing orphanages out of simple sympathy. Understand that. ……I share the Chairperson's view, and cast my vote in favor."

Reine bowed her head lightly in thanks, and he gave a wry smile and shook his head.

The one who spoke next was a heavyset bearded giant, Oruros Natrias.

"My vote's the same. Though, well, I can understand the concern about military strength too. How about sending a bit more force from various regions to the western front line that'll take the brunt of things? At least no fool wants internal friction right now. I think the western folks'll feel a bit easier about it too."

He was not a particularly adept figure in internal governance, but he had a commanding presence and held the many nobles together.

He had originally possessed the largest territory in this region, which no doubt contributed.

There were some problems, but nothing particularly serious.

Elsren's start was going smoothly.

She left the first conference chamber and headed to her office.

The vice-chairman — Gorzarius Rukazan — spoke.

"A tiring session, I imagine. Having become used to it, each of them says exactly what they like."

"It is a little tiring, certainly……but this is fine. Reine wants to hear all sorts of opinions too, and if Reine were a monarch, people probably couldn't say what they wanted after all."

Gorzarius had envisioned a nation where Reine held absolute power, but what Reine wanted was a parliamentary republic.

In Alberan, her mother thought of everything and decided everything.

She suppressed dissenting views, or executed those who voiced them.

Dictatorship was not a poor system in itself, and she could understand her mother's reasoning.

It was undeniably her mother's achievement that she had unified and expanded the nearly-fractured Alberan so greatly, and it was precisely because her mother had strengthened her own power and freely wielded it.

Her mother had expanded the nation and grown its population.

If a nation that was large and stable was a good nation, then her mother was a good queen.

But what Reine wanted to build was a place where everyone could be happy.

*……What society is, precisely, is a difficult question……let me think. Perhaps it is something that individuals build by making up for what others lack?*

*Making up for what they lack……*

*For example, just as Elsreine-sama wishes me to teach you something when she sees my paintings, I too am taught what I am missing from Elsreine-sama's paintings. The relationship of giving and receiving to and from each other is perhaps one form of a small society.*

It's a small society, but, Fiiri had said.

*One uses what one is good at for others, and is helped where one is not good……this world is one where everyone helps each other that way, living within their role in it. Warrior or craftsman, even a noble is in the end just one role in a great society. In any age, what is important for an individual is — within the role assigned to them in this great society — what will they fulfill?*

*……That's a little difficult to understand.*

When she said so, Fiiri had looked pleased.

*Heh, yet Elsreine-sama surely already knows, I think.*

She said such things and laughed.

Reine recalled that smile and closed her eyes for a brief moment.

"Reine wants to hear many opinions, to be taught many things, to understand many ways of thinking. The only thing Reine is good at is calculations……if Reine struggled alone, what would be built would probably be an Alberan just like kaa-sama's. ……So Reine wants to exchange opinions with many people, and build a country and a society where many people can live happily."

*——The heart that tries to understand others is the seed of a better society.*

Fiiri's words were always a little difficult.

But that was probably what they meant.

What one liked and disliked, what one wished for and wanted to do.

Not whether one's mind was sharp or dull — what mattered was things like that.

If something was good, a foolish proposal was still good.

If wisdom was lacking, Reine could supplement it.

By listening to all sorts of opinions that way, by trying to understand everyone, by fulfilling their wishes — surely in time it would connect to a better society.

Others were a little difficult for Reine.

But they were all human — there was surely somewhere they connected, and like Fiiri and Reine, she believed they could understand each other someday.

And she wanted to show the place built that way to her mother as well.

"……If such a place came to be, kaa-sama would surely……"

"Please do not say such things in public. Many hold grudges against Her Majesty the Queen……"

"Ah……yes. I'm sorry……I let it slip."

Entering the office, Gorzarius gave a wry smile and shook his head.

"Your sentiments are of course understandable……I hope that even a little of Elsreine-sama's feelings might be conveyed. Though the dead cannot return to life……"

"……Yes."

"But Fiiri would surely be proud of an Elsreine-sama such as this. She was probably thinking, more than anyone, about how to heal Her Majesty's ailment."

Reine nodded, and Gorzarius continued.

"From what I've heard……it seems that after Elsreine-sama departed to battle, Fiiri was summoned by Her Majesty."

"……Summoned."

"The details are unclear……but thinking it over, it is probably most reasonable to conclude that Elsreine-sama's dispatch to battle was in order to do away with Fiiri. Perhaps she wanted to distance you and ensure the matter did not reach your ears."

Reine cast her eyes down, and Gorzarius sighed.

"……The day we spoke of having Reine go to suppress the rebellion happened to be the day……I had another quarrel with kaa-sama over Fiiri."

"You mentioned that previously. I cannot say with certainty that it was the final trigger……but it seems the one who took her life was indeed Her Majesty. I had people I had placed covertly looking into it as much as possible……"

"……Thank you. I'm grateful for the thought……but I don't think kaa-sama would leave spies undiscovered. I think it would be better to withdraw them."

"……As you say."

*——Mother said it wasn't her.*

It was true that Reine had been out of her right mind, and the thought had flickered that maybe it really had not been, but listening to Gorzarius, who apparently continued investigating even after that, it seemed her mother had killed Fiiri after all.

"In any case — what matters is what comes next."

"……Yes."

"Given the heavy taxation they've suffered, orphanages would be a good idea. Charitable works are necessary to gather the people's support. Establishing the Queen as evil and deepening the antagonism — you may resist that method a little, Reine-sama, but……"

"……No. If it is necessary, it cannot be helped."

The tyrant Grabareine — a wickedly cruel great monarch.

By presenting her mother as evil and Reine as the good who resisted, to spread it widely among the common people, to make the justification clear and win the people's support.

She had some resistance to Gorzarius's proposal, but building a nation naturally required winning the broad support of the common people.

The opposition of good and evil was easy for even the unlearned to understand, and when it came to good and evil, most people wanted to be on the side of good.

His thinking was rational, and setting aside matters of sentiment, she had no objection.

Her mother was a distinguished queen — Reine did not doubt that even now.

Nor did she think her mother was evil.

Her mother had been a kind parent to Reine, was wise, and had at least been trying to build a good nation as she conceived of it.

If nothing like this had happened, if her mother had accepted Fiiri — perhaps in ten or twenty years' time they could all have been living happily together.

But Reine had Reine's thoughts and position, and her mother had her mother's thoughts and position.

That was the only difference, she thought.

*……Good and evil are very frightening words, Reine-sama.*

*Frightening……*

*The question of what perspective makes something look right and what perspective makes it look wrong. To label someone evil is to stop thinking and stop trying to understand them……to label oneself good is to lose the heart that corrects one's own errors. ……People can become endlessly foolish by making up their minds about things.*

Speaking of good and evil lightly was wrong — Fiiri had said so.

Put it into words and you become bound by the words, and eventually it becomes a fixed idea.

You start looking only for reasons to call the other person evil, and looking only for reasons to call yourself good.

"……Only, that approach does not seem a very good one. Once the country has stabilized, I would like everyone to be able to live happily, day by day."

The core of the Elsren military — Oruros Natrias.

The sharp-minded man and parliament moderator — Kirias Liberslebe.

As if everything were going wrong — that sort of feeling.

"I'm trying to make a good country, and I think I'm doing my best. But no matter what I say there are only problems, and recently I angered the nobles again, and nothing goes well……I find myself thinking my paintings might come out like that too……"

The problems of the territories they had been given were problems for them to resolve and process on their own, and with taxes being paid to the nation, the nation intervening there was a gross overreach — they showed their anger.

And moreover, that such words came out at all was synonymous with Reine's distrust of them.

This country was an empire.

Multiple lords with territories, existences like the kings of small nations, gathered and formed it.

Reine sat at the top as the emperor, but Reine merely bound them together — she had no right to directly meddle in their territories.

So long as a fixed amount of taxes was paid to the empire, whether they governed however they liked, Reine's side could not interfere, nor could she take away their territories.

Their argument was correct in a sense.

With the post-war confusion still present, taxes to the empire were kept light, but in the negotiations toward raising them to what should properly be collected, they said *the effects of the previous war are still strong and disorder in the territories makes it difficult.*

If corruption were the cause, Reine had only to look briefly at their territory's finances to find who was pocketing what and how, and she could resolve it.

If they lacked ability, Reine could supplement it.

Was she intending to strengthen her power as empress and force a dictatorship like her mother's — when asked that, no words of denial came either.

Gorzarius and the others too said Reine was too hasty, and that society was not made of purely rational things alone — that in troubled nations necessary evils also existed.

Punish a person doing ten evil things and a person doing twelve evil things emerges, making it pointless, and for that reason too things should be handled cautiously, and in that regard Reine had been reckless.

Of late, every time Reine opened her mouth in parliament, the session fell into disorder.

Reine's words as empress were weighty, and if they provoked backlash the country would fracture.

To prevent that, she was repeatedly cautioned to be careful in her speech, and in fact the words Reine spoke always angered the nobles.

As it repeated, she became frightened of forming words, and leaving everything to Gorzarius and the others, sitting in discomfort and falling silent had become the norm.

Fenera looked at a Reine who had said that, considered, and —

"Ah, um……no, Fenera fully understands that even as a complaint-listener she's inadequate……"

"……That's not true at all. Heh heh — it made me a little happy that you'd say such a thing……it made me think I have to try harder."

She smiled at Fenera's relieved face and lightly slapped her own cheeks.

Fenera was looking at her with a puzzled face.

"No quarrels and everyone smiling and living happily……Fiiri was so very clever, yet she dreamed of something so foolish, which I thought was strange. But Fiiri understood that too, properly……"

When she saw the smiling faces of the orphanage children she thought so.

They quarreled a little but made up right away, and the next time she came they would be playing together happily, and everything was simple.

Perhaps that was, in truth, the natural state of things.

Growing older just made everything more complicated and tangled, making something go wrong.

"If the children of the orphanage grow up staying good children……if a society where there's no need to fight and everyone can live happily could be built, someday it could draw closer to that kind of world. Of course, building an Elsren just as it is in the dream would be very difficult, I think……but, that such a place truly exists……"

Fiiri's drawn Elsren.

The happiness of a paradise-like utopia.

That painting had not left her mind even now.

"……Fiiri is no longer here, but — I want to build a place where Fiiri would have rejoiced if she were alive, and simply……that's all. That all the things Fiiri taught Reine truly had meaning……Reine wants to prove that. ……Because Fiiri was there, there is this happy place — I want that."

Fenera smiled at those words.

"At least as far as Fenera is concerned, she is already so to speak a resident of the Elsren Elsreine-sama speaks of……the present place here is already, for Fenera, something like Elsren."

Words she had heard before.

Reine quietly opened her eyes wide, and Fenera, seeing that, colored slightly in embarrassment.

Fiiri's happy face, not quite understanding.

The sound of happy laughter that had filled the room.

"Ah, um……"

Confused, Reine nodded and clung to her.

Gradually, she could feel the tears soaking through the apron dress, and wondering why she was crying, she entrusted herself to it.

At least in doing so, the tumultuous feeling in her chest grew quieter.

"Please don't worry, Fenera is here……please take as long as you need."

Fenera's worried words.

There is a church in Alberan worshipping the twelve gods.

The royal family are descendants of these gods, and therefore their blood is something special.

Alberan nobility too receives the protection of the royal blood — they are special existences, and therefore the common people must be protected by them and must serve them.

Reverence directed at the royal family and nobility is equivalent to faith in the gods, and such a structure is a justification for controlling foolish common people.

Her mother said it was a relic from when Alberan was still a barbarian tribe.

Religion, she said, was skilled at manipulating foolish groups, and *convenient* for instilling a favorable moral code in the name of a god's teachings — apparently a system used in most nations.

Depending on the land it was strongly believed and had practically become law, but Alberan's faith in the twelve gods was relatively loose — primarily used to underwrite the royal family's prestige.

It was also tolerant of the gods worshipped in other lands, under the form of the great twelve gods — recognizing the existence of other gods and at times accepting them.

Even when invading undeveloped lands, without denying the gods they worshipped, they drew them under the umbrella, and introduced the great twelve gods who stood above those gods, achieving cultural submission.

For example, when conquering a land that worshipped a god of valor, that was not denied.

Without question praising the god as one of the divine pillars, and lauding their courage and faith, one simply showed that before the great twelve gods, the greatest god of valor, Koreis, this existence had been inferior.

*Admittedly a great god of valor — but one that simply could not overcome Koreis, the supreme god of valor.*

Faith, in lands where it equalled law.

Smashing it all would naturally produce the chaos of lawlessness.

But if that faith was recognized, and one simply said they had merely not known of Koreis — then their faith, their law and order, would for the time being be preserved, and one could instill the teaching of the most noble god of valor, Koreis, in a form convenient to oneself.

Her mother spoke of religion as a very convenient *tool* and taught Reine to handle religion *as a tool.*

She felt neither particularly good nor bad about that.

A tool was a tool and a means was a means — it was fine if it worked toward a good outcome, and Reine thought of it as a sort of legal order.

The teaching of the twelve gods was not extreme, and she had not paid it particular attention.

It fell under the Papal Court's jurisdiction, but she had not paid it much importance — and it was some time before she noticed.

"……I simply wish for people to know of Elsreine-sama's wish — the paradise that will one day arrive — and so I have been spreading the teaching in her stead. I have obtained permission from Rukazan-sama and the others, but……"

"Yes — they say the common people need something to cling to……"

Only, there was an indescribable unease.

"But, the Elsren Reine speaks of……isn't a place you are guided to after death……"

First, people learn peaceful teachings, and as a result of that, may a paradise come to be in this land — that is the ideal. ……The interpretation and process differ somewhat, but what is taught here is by no means something bad."

"That's……"

*Love your neighbor, help one another, set aside strife and have a peaceful heart.*

"Elsreine-sama's distinguished deeds go without saying, and all who lay eyes on that painting are moved by the magnificence of that painting — surely not drawn by human hands — and believe that Elsreine-sama is a divine-like existence descended from heaven to this land."

Something different from what Fiiri had wished for.
