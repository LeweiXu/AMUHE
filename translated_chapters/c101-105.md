# Chapter 101: A Troubled Young Lady

*——A twisted aberration.*

*That was what she was, socially speaking. She had known for a long time.*

*At the very least, she was different from normal.*

*She could not understand their jokes or their humor.*

*If others were sad, angry, or in pain, she did not care as long as it had nothing to do with her, and she felt nothing when a human being turned into a lump of meat.*

*The obvious things that 'normal people' felt simply did not make sense to her.*

*In truth, she had thought that was fine.*

*Emotions impaired clear thinking.*

*Emotional people were the polar opposite of what Krische considered excellent, and she saw no particular advantage in drawing closer to that sort of 'normalcy.'*

*Still, it was an important factor for maintaining good social relationships——and she thought she would like to learn how to act 'normal' convincingly if she could, but if she could not, then she could not. That was that.*

*There was no question that she was more capable than anyone, and if she gave up on blending into society, she could ultimately manage one way or another.*

*What had begun to feel strange was——*

*'……I don't think the unusual parts of Krische-sama are strange, nor do I find them unsettling. Rather, I think they are a charming individuality that no one else possesses.'*

*——from that day onward.*

*Ever since then, Krische had been strange.*

"…………"

She cracked her eyes open beneath the blanket and pressed her face into Elvena's chest, seeking warmth.

Warm. Comfortable. Calming. Reassuring——so many feelings that she hardly knew what to do with them all.

She thought things had been much simpler before.

Body temperature regulation, a cushion. That had been the extent of it.

But now she was dependent on being held like this.

It made her happy, and conversely, sleeping alone left her restless, unable to sleep soundly.

In the past, that would not have bothered her much.

All she needed to endure was the cold.

——She felt as though she had grown weaker, steadily.

Things that had not bothered her now did, and emotions swayed over matters she had never cared about before.

On the other hand, her joy had grown so great that she could not imagine being without it.

What she had now was a helpless instability.

"……Bery."

Krische was hopelessly dependent on Bery.

She had been taught many words——like, love.

But what Krische felt was so much more than those words could contain, so far beyond expression, that she thought her head might burst from the weight of it.

Bery asked nothing of Krische. She simply told her she was fine as she was.

But Krische wanted to do anything and everything for Bery.

The unreturned 'repayments' kept piling up, leaving her at a loss.

So——to make Bery like her even a little more, to not be disliked, to not make her look sad, to make her happy.

Not merely pretending to be 'normal,' but wanting to truly become 'normal' someday.

If it were possible, she wanted to become the best version of Krische that Bery could wish for.

*'……You are a beast. Merely hiding among people.'*

She did not need to be told. She already knew.

Bery called it an individuality.

Bery said she liked Krische. That she loved her.

But the anxiety always followed close behind.

*'Hah, everyone you hold dear will leave once they realize it. Selene, that servant of yours——they are all simply mistaken.'*

She could say with certainty that things were fine now.

But perhaps next year, perhaps ten years from now——

Krische was an aberration.

There was no guarantee anywhere that anyone would keep loving such a Krische forever.

So she had to try even harder to be liked——ah, see, she really had become strange.

This was a battlefield. This had nothing to do with anything right now.

What she should be thinking about was today's battle, and yet the small anxiety crawled through her chest regardless, indifferent to relevance.

It was simply unpleasant.

She recalled Gildanstein's face and pressed her own deeper into that chest.

She breathed out.

Then she felt a hand gently stroke her head, and when she looked up, Elvena was gazing at her with a worried expression.

"Can't sleep?"

"……Krische just sort of woke up."

She had eaten and gone to bed early.

That was the best thing to do when she felt restless.

But her sleep had been shallow, and she had woken several times.

The sun had not yet risen.

"……It's the middle of a war, after all. It can't be helped."

Elvena said this, then touched the eternal magic lamp.

The faint mana at her fingertips——fed to the lamp, it cast a soft, warm light.

An eternal magic lamp with internally stored mana could be lit by anyone, using the trace amount of mana that every person possessed.

But the one Krische had brought was not the internally stored type.

That Elvena could light it so easily came as a small surprise.

Feeding mana into a magic crystal was easy for anyone who had properly studied the technique, but difficult for those who had not.

"You're quite skilled."

"……Hm?"

Elvena tilted her head, then noticed Krische's gaze and smiled.

"Oh……I was made to maintain these sorts of things."

Eternal magic lamps without internal storage were not mainstream——in a sense, they were a kind of testing instrument.

In the days when the quality of eternal magic lamps was poor, they had apparently been used among nobles to gauge one another's abilities. Lamps deliberately made difficult to illuminate had even been crafted for this purpose.

Not too much, not too little.

If one did not feed mana steadily at the proper level, the lamp would flicker.

If the mana required for illumination was not delivered in ideal proportions, it took time.

Nowadays the quality of the lamps themselves had improved greatly, and their role as testing instruments had become entirely vestigial——but they still functioned well enough to gauge one's ability in this way.

In that regard, Elvena's performance was careful and reasonably refined.

As though steering her mind away from unpleasant thoughts, Krische turned her attention toward this.

"……After the war ends, if you can't find good work, Krische might have you help with things. Elvena is attentive, and you seem like a quick learner."

After saying it, Krische nodded to herself——that was true.

Thinking about it, Elvena was Kalua's sister, and Kalua's handling of mana was 'reasonably skilled,' so it might be better to have her do this rather than simply serve as an attendant.

Between Bery and Krische, the household was more than sufficiently managed.

"……Help?"

"Yes. Once the country settles down, Krische wants to research various things, so you'd help with that. Working with magic crystals."

It was the sort of thing she had thought about when she saw Bogan, or when Dagra was injured.

If she did it well, she might be able to create mechanisms that could properly heal such wounds.

She had managed that much on a whim, so if she studied properly——

"Battlefields are inconvenient in many ways, and injuries and such problems occur often. Krische was thinking it would be nice to be able to do something about those."

"……With all due respect, I have no education, so I may not be of much use."

"Krische hasn't studied at all either, so please don't worry about it. It'll be trial and error."

Really, Bery would be the best.

Intelligent, the most skilled at handling mana after Kreschenta, and an unmatched partner——but Krische did not want to steal Bery's precious cooking time.

With that in mind, Bery was out of the question, and Selene and Kreschenta were both busy.

If she needed someone suitable, Elvena was the obvious choice.

"Krische intends to finish today's battle today, but the war might continue for a while yet. Krische wants to make sure no one around Krische dies."

"……If that is the purpose, I have no objections. Whatever I can do to be useful."

Elvena smiled softly.

A face like Kalua's, with a smile like Bery's.

She sat Krische up and placed her on her lap, holding her gently.

"……Your eyes seem wide awake, so shall I make some tea?"

"Ehehe……yes. Isn't Elvena sleepy?"

"No. I've always been the sort who manages fine on little sleep."

Had something happened on the battlefield?

Elvena could tell that Krische had been restless.

Since returning, she had seemed somehow irritable, and even after getting into bed, she clearly could not settle.

But since Krische said nothing, Elvena could not ask——and once Krische had clearly given up on sleeping, Elvena decided to try steering her thoughts away from the battlefield, even if just a little.

What topic would be good——what came to mind was that servant named Bery.

Krische mentioned her name more than anything else, and she always seemed happy when talking about her, so Elvena thought that would do.

"Bery is, you see——"

Perhaps that was the trigger.

What followed was an unstoppable torrent of boasting that left Elvena speechless.

Apparently, Bery was incomparably better at cooking than Krische, a perfect servant who handled every matter of the household entirely on her own.

She was first-rate at everything, and Krische went on and on in absolute praise, dragging out countless episodes from memory about how kind and clever and amazing she was.

Krische was, at the very least, the most skilled cook Elvena had ever seen.

Swift and dexterous, she juggled multiple tasks simultaneously with a brilliance that even professionals would struggle to match.

Someone incomparably better at cooking than her——and the claim of handling all the household work alone was something Elvena found hard to believe, knowing the amount of work involved, and yet if Krische said so, it was likely true.

What Elvena pictured was a superhuman who made Krische look modest by comparison.

What kind of person could Krische, herself already superhuman, praise so unreservedly——in Elvena's imagination, Bery was Krische (Large).

"Bery told Krische she loves her, ehehe, and then……"

And so the talk shifted into guileless lovesick rambling that made Elvena blush to hear.

By this point Elvena felt the tiniest twinge of regret at having raised the topic, but once Krische started talking, there was no stopping her.

Watching Krische shyly go on about like, like, love, love you——it tickled Elvena's mischievous side, but she held herself in check and limited her contributions to the occasional murmured agreement.

As she listened, Elvena unknowingly hit upon the truth: Krische's lack of reaction to that kiss the other day was not simply due to her innocence, but more likely because such gestures were part of her daily life.

Krische's stories were truly those of a child.

She did not organize them logically or weave in humor.

She simply poured out what had happened, feelings leading the way.

The only things that came through were that she loved Bery, and that life at 'the estate' had been very happy.

Entertaining? Not particularly. Enjoyable? If anything, it might have been somewhat tiresome.

Her storytelling was exceedingly poor——in a sense, Roland's boasting might have been more entertaining.

And yet——however amazing Bery was, however much Krische treasured her.

The way she spoke of it all so happily, so joyfully, was endearing and heartwarming.

Simply listening made a warm feeling rise in Elvena's chest, and she stroked Krische's head while murmuring the occasional agreement.

She had been whispered words of love by clients before.

She remembered the feeling of letting them pass with a cold heart.

But what would it feel like to have love whispered to you like this——without any pretense, by a girl this terrifyingly adorable?

"Bery-sama must be very happy, to have such affection directed at her by Krische-sama."

——That would surely be the happiest thing of all.

It was simply what Elvena thought, without any deep consideration.

"……Do you think so?"

Krische reacted to that, and Elvena tilted her head.

"Something the matter?"

"……No. Krische hopes so."

Even as she said it, Krische fell silent, as though a flame had been extinguished.

After all that lovesick talk, was there still some anxiety?

Elvena thought for a moment, then spoke.

"Fufu, no one could have such affection directed at them by Krische-sama and not be happy."

That was not necessarily true in all cases.

Still, very few people would find it bothersome to be told they were loved by someone like her.

And given the kind of woman this Bery must be, to be loved so dearly by Krische——there was no room for doubt.

"Let me put it this way……if Krische-sama were to tell me she liked me that much, I'm confident I could live happily for the rest of my life on that alone. I'm sure Bery-sama feels the same way."

"……Elvena still seems like she exaggerates things, somehow."

"That's because Krische-sama underestimates herself."

Elvena chuckled and stroked Krische's cheek.

"……Once the battle is over, you can ask her directly and you'll see. Surely——no, absolutely, Bery-sama feels that way about Krische-sama."

"……Will it still be that way, always?"

"Yes, of course."

She smiled, thinking, *You really do lack confidence in yourself,* as she gazed at that flawless face.

So beautiful and capable at everything, yet so hopelessly clumsy and mismatched.

It was funny, and it was a strange feeling.

"Krische is, well……not normal."

And hearing those words, Elvena understood. Ah——so that was it.

The rumors that painted only one side of her——Elvena was aware of them to some extent.

Perhaps something had been said to her on the battlefield.

Opposing sides would surely exchange heartless words.

How to put it——Elvena looked into those violet eyes and spoke.

"Everyone has good qualities and bad qualities. Just as Krische-sama sees being not normal as a flaw, I have those sorts of flaws too, and so does nee-san, and surely Bery-sama as well."

Krische's eyes widened.

*Let me think,* Elvena mused, and took a lock of her own hair, trimmed at the shoulders.

It curled only slightly inward——not noticeable when kept this short, but when she grew it out, the curl grew stronger and tangled.

"For example, I really dislike how my hair curls like this, and I consider it one of my flaws. How does Krische-sama feel about it?"

"Eh? No, it's not strange at all——"

"Fufu, you see? Other people care far less about our flaws than we think. Even more so if they love us……of course, there are bigger flaws and smaller ones, and ignoring your own flaws entirely is a problem, but."

*The same is true for what Krische-sama worries about,* Elvena said.

"According to a book I once read, finding someone's good qualities is what it means to like them, and accepting their bad qualities is what love is. ……A long time ago, nee-san told me she thought this hair I hate so much was cute."

*I was so happy,* she said with a smile, gently stroking Krische's head.

Smooth and silky——hair so beautiful it made her envious.

"It's a little different, but it's the same kind of thing. ……I think Bery-sama finds the not-normal parts of Krische-sama truly dear. I think she loves Krische-sama having accepted all of that."

Krische blinked several times, and there was a small silence.

Then she lowered her eyes and pressed her face against Elvena with a happy expression.

"……Ehehe, Elvena really is similar to Bery after all. The things you say are somehow just like her. Krische has a feeling……Bery would say something like that."

"When you think about those sorts of things alone, they tend to spiral toward the worst conclusions. If you just talked about it, it would turn out to be nothing at all."

Elvena said it with a self-deprecating tone.

"……I was the same way. I decided on my own that nee-san must have forgotten about a fool like me, alone with those thoughts, convincing myself, giving up……but nee-san had been desperately searching for me the whole time."

She held Krische close and pressed her face into that silver hair.

"It was a foolish, disrespectful thing to do to nee-san, who had been trying so hard. Deciding on my own, thinking like that on my own——I think the fact that Krische-sama feels that anxiety, that you doubt her, is far more painful and sad for Bery-sama. Going toward the worst, always toward the worst……Krische-sama, every time you're alone, are you going to doubt the Bery-sama you love so much?"

"……That's——"

"That is my advice, as someone who was foolish enough to do the same thing."

She smiled mischievously, then continued.

"……If something worries you, talk to me, or to anyone nearby. There may be people who dislike Krische-sama, but there are plenty who admire you. They would all say the same thing I just did, in unison. It's that sort of trivial, blow-away-in-the-wind kind of worry."

"……Yes. Thank you."

"Since Bery-sama is someone Krische-sama loves that much, all that anxiety will vanish the moment you see her again. So, please rest easy."

Krische nodded, and clung to her.

She savored the words she had heard, laughing softly.

Then, after a while, she looked up at Elvena with a troubled expression.

"Um, Elvena……Krische has sort of calmed down, and, well……"

"Fufu, yes. Then let's go back to bed, shall we? I'll wake you when morning comes, so rest well until then."

"……Yes."

Elvena smiled wryly at the way Krische clung to her like a spoiled child, and lifted that small body into her arms.

"Krische will finish this quickly and go see Bery."

"Yes, indeed. ……And to do that, you need to rest properly."

In Elvena's arms, Krische smiled as though a thought had just struck her.

"Once the war is over, Krische will give Elvena some time off for a while too. Krische plans to give the same to Kalua and the others, so you can take it easy together. Krische is going to have the century come to the capital with a stipend, so you should look for a house or whatever in the meantime."

"……The capital."

"The capital. Kreschenta is there, so Krische will have to move out of the Christand estate……"

At the word *Kreschenta,* Elvena tilted her head for a moment, then recalled the princess's name.

And she belatedly grasped the fact that the girl she served had originally been royalty.

From now on, she would be serving a girl who addressed the princess without honorifics.

Quite the promotion from a slave——it was so much that it did not feel real.

"There's a lot to do, and it'll be busy. Let's finish today quickly, and then……"

With a small yawn, her slender body grew warm with drowsiness.

Elvena smiled softly and stroked her head.

"Yes. ……And for that, I pray you return safely."

"Krische doesn't get hurt, so it'll be fine……"

Watching Krische fall asleep almost immediately, Elvena stayed as she was.

Until the sun rose, Elvena simply gazed at Krische.

# Chapter 102: The Storm and the Beast

The morning sun rose, and with it the second day of battle——before the soldiers guarding the Royal Prince Gildanstein stood the Velraigh army's First Corps, led by the Great Wolf Granmeld Varkus, whose name echoed across every battlefield.

His fame and the banner of the howling wolf carried a presence that intimidated those who faced them by their mere existence.

Many of those opposing them were soldiers scraped together from various commands.

However, the ground directly before the main camp was naturally packed with veterans and seasoned soldiers, and even against the fearsome Granmeld, they held their formation without yielding a step.

Gildanstein held the left wing; Keesliton the right.

They had deployed in a wide arc around the Velraigh army, which was positioned atop a gentle hill.

Gildanstein had divided his nine corps into two——five under his own command, four under Keesliton——each functioning as an independent army.

This was likely out of concern that commanding from a single headquarters would cause delays in reaching the distant wings.

Their aim was the Velraigh army's center.

Two corps each were arranged in depth along Gildanstein's right wing and Keesliton's left wing, giving those sectors double the thickness against the enemy.

They intended a central breakthrough through sheer numerical superiority.

A deployment that practically announced its intent——and this was deliberate.

Because the Velraigh soldiers were positioned on a hill with excellent visibility, every one of them could clearly see the enemy's disposition.

No matter how much the Velraigh soldiers surpassed them in quality, battle fatigue wore down even Krische.

The prospect of an enemy onslaught that continued without end even as they killed——the effect this had on the soldiers' morale was enormous.

——*Those who hold the high ground have the advantage.*

Many believed this blindly, but in truth, holding the high ground did not always confer superiority.

When observing a battle, people tended to focus on the violent physical struggle——the raw physical energy——but the true essence was far more psychological, far more a matter of the mind.

This was not a simple killing match, but a battle involving tens of thousands.

What decided victory and defeat was morale and discipline, the capacity for organized combat——not the number of dead.

Having deployed on the hill and surveyed the enemy's formation from above, the morale of the Velraigh center had dropped due to the enemy's blatant concentration.

The power of visual impression was not to be underestimated.

In a fight where their own lives were at stake, no soldier wished to find himself in dire straits.

What everyone desired was a glorious life, not an honorable death.

Knowing you held the advantage raised morale; knowing you were outmatched lowered it.

That was a natural principle, and a commander had to carefully manage the information conveyed to his soldiers.

In that sense, the excellent visibility afforded by the hill——the information it gave the soldiers, and the resulting negative effect——was too significant to ignore in this situation of numerical inferiority.

The Gildanstein army had deliberately concentrated its forces with this in mind.

To break their fighting spirit before the battle even began.

The Velraigh army on the hill had the advantage of potential energy——the strength of high ground.

But considering the psychological pressure on the center, the situation could not be called advantageous, even if it was not quite disadvantageous.

In terms of deployment, the two sides were at a stalemate.

Gildanstein's center and left wing only needed to prevent the enemy right wing from flanking or breaking through.

Gildanstein's right wing, and Keesliton's left wing.

Their role was to hold until those forces completed the central breakthrough.

The soldiers facing the Great Wolf Granmeld Varkus and his famous 'Wolf Pack'——the reason they could maintain their composure despite that was their confidence in this arrangement.

——But a single girl who appeared before them changed that atmosphere entirely.

Parting the ranks like a wave, what emerged behind Granmeld was a wolf-helmed figure bearing an iron bludgeon taller than a grown man.

And behind him appeared a girl who had no place on a battlefield.

Silver hair, a cloak, and armfuls of white infantry spears.

From a distance, that was all they could see——and yet,

"……The cursed child."

Someone whispered in fear, and even those who did not know who she was recalled the rumors.

The Head-Hunter of Bernaich, the monster of Christand.

Seasoned soldiers, renowned commanders——none of it mattered to her.

That aberration had swung her cleaver and torn through the throats of celebrated warriors as though she were cutting grass.

Yes, throats——she was said to have a pathological fixation on slitting throats, collecting the heads of those she killed and laughing.

*Silence!* barked the centurions.

The girl, in response, hurled the spears she carried.

The spears arced skyward.

Tracing parabolas, as if marking the path forward.

What appeared to be a casual throw——the spears formed a line aimed directly at their front ranks, and every last one of them struck the earth simultaneously.

No one understood what she intended——not until that moment.

From a distance beyond even arrow range, the girl surged forward alone.

Her initial speed exceeded a galloping horse, and with a spear in hand, her charge appeared faster than a flying arrow.

And then her body spun——

"Hii——"

——The spear loosed from her hand split the air with a thunderous roar and shredded through countless bodies of flesh.

From atop the hill, gazing at the enemy headquarters——at a man clad in black armor——Granmeld, standing beside her, asked:

"Spears, is it?"

"Yes, they're good for creating an entry point. Krische wants to disrupt the wall of heavy infantry at their front line."

In her arms, held as one might cradle a bundle, were eight infantry spears, each six shaku long. (T/N: ~6 feet / ~180 cm)

Granmeld reached out a hand as if exasperated, but Krische said "Krische is fine" and kept walking.

For the diminutive Krische, eight spears were far too much baggage, and no matter what she said, the sight of it was anything but "fine."

Her arm strength far surpassed a normal person's, but there were limits imposed by physique.

Fine as she might claim, in truth they were quite a load for her.

"Keith, Krische is leaving things behind Woof-woof to you."

"Yes, ma'am! Leave it to me!"

She gave the order to Third Battalion Commander Keith, and moved to the front line.

Slowly, she descended the hill.

The soldiers turned when they saw Krische's load, wondering what was going on, but Krische paid no mind.

If she said it was fine, then it was fine——Granmeld led the way ahead of Krische, clearing a path through the ranks so her spears would not catch on other soldiers.

A mere eight spears——what could those possibly accomplish? But if Krische said it would work, he was willing to trust her.

Thrown spears only gained meaning through massed volleys launched on command just before a charge.

From a commander's perspective, this seemed pointless——but as an individual warrior, Granmeld placed deep trust in Krische's words.

Against a formed battle line.

Against an army——he wanted to see her thrown spears, which she declared would be effective.

He had missed them during the battle against Hilkintos.

"Right, make way. Our princess is coming through."

Once they reached the very front, Granmeld stood alongside Krische at the foremost line.

Archers deployed ahead, and beyond them the massed ranks of enemy soldiers forming a seamless wall.

A shiver down his spine——but it was exhilaration.

The fear of death was what always ignited Granmeld's fighting spirit.

"So you're really going in alone?"

"Yes. Krische will disrupt them a bit, so please follow along and pry them open."

Untying the cord that bound the spears, Krische showed not a shred of fear.

The front rank against ten thousand——and she intended to be the very first to charge in.

*She's insane,* Granmeld thought, the corner of his mouth twisting upward.

A self-professed born battle-maniac, and yet even Granmeld felt impure beside this girl.

As casually as if she were walking through camp, her heart unmoved by this situation——completely as usual.

Absolute confidence——she possessed something that could not be measured by Granmeld's standards.

He had seen many formidable warriors, but not one had possessed confidence like hers.

"Woof-woof opens the path, Krische takes the Royal Prince's head, and it's over."

"Hah! Simple and clear. I like it."

"Yes, that way Krische can focus without worrying. Simple is best."

With that, Krische began hurling the spears.

From far away, then gradually closer.

The spears traced parabolas and struck the earth simultaneously, as if by some strange design.

"Well then."

Krische shot forward as though cleaving the wind.

One step with her hips dropped low, and by the second she was already at full speed.

Her acceleration was staggering——but that was not what one should have been watching.

That accelerating, excessively forward-leaning body.

As though slamming on the brakes, she arched her upper body back and drove her heel into the earth before her.

Her knees absorbed the impact that would have sent her entire body flying, supple and soft, and her raging upper body was redirected forward——the readied spear was launched from far behind her with a thunderous boom.

Not even an arrow shot from a war bow could match the speed of this spear.

A velocity that threatened to leave sound itself behind.

The bullet of white spear that captured every eye struck, a beat later, piercing through the line of archers from beyond arrow range.

And continuing further back, it shattered the heavy infantry along with their raised great shields——and effortlessly punched a hole through that thick front rank.

Granmeld, watching from close by, was dumbfounded.

Given how much she talked it up, he had expected she could throw harder than he could.

But that was all he had thought——and yet Krische's thrown spears were, just as she had said, more than sufficient against an army. Her words were literal truth: effective against an army.

It was a perfected individual feat far beyond anything he had imagined.

Flowing with her broken balance and inertia rather than fighting it, Krische planted a hand on the ground and rolled like a cat.

She reached for the next spear, accelerated again, slammed to a halt——and casually loosed another earth-shattering spear.

Slightly to the left of the first point——smashing through that wall of flesh.

——What rang across the battlefield was the enemy's anguished screams shaking the air, and a thunderous cheer from atop the hill.

From the overall perspective, each throw killed somewhere between ten-odd and twenty or thirty.

Even if she threw them all, the casualties would barely reach two hundred.

Against a total force of 45,000, the damage inflicted was trivial.

But for that much to be accomplished by a single person——for one individual to put it on display before every soldier watching——

The morale boost was on par with an army of ten thousand.

Even the soldiers in the center deployment who had been quailing before the vast enemy force watched the spectacle on the right wing from atop the hill, eyes blazing, and screamed their euphoria with every fiber of their being.

And conversely, what it inflicted upon the enemy was the terror of an inhuman transcendent.

The dismay of having their ironclad vanguard crumbled so effortlessly by a single girl.

Without any command, as though by unspoken agreement.

What was directed at the dancing girl was a torrent of arrows.

The massed bows that should have been aimed at the army were directed at the lone, exposed Krische, and loosed.

The storm of arrows that blackened the sky targeted the girl——yet she pressed forward as though nothing had happened. She accelerated through the rain of arrows as a matter of course. Threading through the gaps with the barest minimum of motion.

To the archers loosing at her, it looked as though the arrows themselves feared her——as if they swerved away of their own accord.

When she grasped the third spear, their terror only deepened.

——Before anyone realized it, the atmosphere of the battlefield had been transformed by a single individual.

The corps commanders and battalion commanders watching from the hill, and even Nozan Velraigh himself, could not contain their laughter at this individual martial prowess that existed outside the bounds of common sense.

The ancient, savage valor of the warrior's archetype——the reckless courage that should have died before discipline and battle lines and organization——embodied by one girl.

The peerless hero of a bygone age, now nothing more than a fairy tale, was there before them——

"——There is nothing left to command here!"

And Granmeld, who had been shown this at the closest range, roared as the frenzy surged up his spine.

"I'm going! Follow me!! Show them who it is that stands behind our mad princess!!"

As the third thunderclap sounded and Krische reached for the fourth spear, Granmeld charged forward.

A full sprint with no concern for keeping pace with his men——Granmeld became a beast.

But no one cared.

The 'Wolf Pack'——all they had ever known was to chase the back of their leader and to tear into the enemy's flesh.

The veteran mana-users with the fastest legs followed Granmeld's charge.

Each note scattered a human life like petals, marking the end of an existence.

What was to be honored was not life.

The madness-drenched battlefield moved toward one thing alone——victory.

Here, his own life was a trivial thing.

Face his father's corps head-on and be overwhelmed.

That was the kind of pressure it possessed——and yet even that corps was a small world within the larger battlefield.

What Gran needed to seize was a small victory within that small world.

——His mind settled.

The fear that had been inside him drained away, and the blood that had seemed ready to boil in his skull finally began circulating through his whole body.

Emerging ahead were enemy cavalry and reserve pikemen. A force to prevent flanking.

He wheeled his horse to the left, presenting his back to them.

Then, with his spear, he pointed toward the infantry following behind.

"——A path to victory has opened! The target is there!!"

By leading the cavalry along the enemy's far right, he had forced the pikemen into a defensive stance——creating a narrow gap between them and the advancing front line.

That was why Gran had charged ahead.

The light infantry behind him changed direction.

They raised their great shields and rushed the enemy's exposed flank.

They hurled javelins, then slammed bodily into the disrupted pikemen.

Led by the light infantry, roughly 4,000 followed——nearly Gran's entire force.

Assuming Kokys's breakthrough, they would strike the Second Corps from the side with everything they had, splitting it front to back.

That was the all-or-nothing stratagem Gran had devised.

He could never defeat Kokys's Second Corps in quality.

A head-on fight would end in defeat.

Because that corps was the strongest force in the kingdom, built by the father Gran respected more than anyone.

He could not match them in soldier quality.

But even the strongest soldiers were vulnerable to a flank attack.

A saturating assault to completely neutralize the pikemen, then cut deep into the formation.

Literally all-or-nothing——fail, and there was no recovery.

But he was certain of success.

Because Kokys had bypassed Gran, driving into the inner seam.

As a result, the Second Corps' front line had missed its swing against Gran's force, leaving itself completely exposed to a flank and rear attack.

Gran allowed himself a smile of triumph.

But it was not over yet.

Enemy cavalry were closing in.

They were charging desperately to prevent his troops from penetrating.

If they were hit from the side at this stage, everything would be ruined.

Of course, Kokys had placed his best veterans on the inner side of his rightmost pikes.

Breaking through completely would take time, and the cavalry had to be stopped to buy it.

Gran reversed course and faced the oncoming enemy cavalry.

And——spear leveled, he charged.

"All units——follow me!!"

——A head-on collision.

The enemy noticed and showed a momentary flinch. Gran bared his teeth.

Riding at full gallop, Gran reared back his great spear.

Shorter and thinner than his father's.

But a steel-cored lance——more than enough power.

He wound his entire body, twisted his torso, and brought the flat of the great spear crashing into the lead cavalryman.

The man tried to receive it, but the difference was too great.

The enemy's lance snapped; the steel of his cuirass buckled; Gran's spear crushed bone through armor and killed the rider.

The flung body slammed into the ground and disrupted the horses behind.

That single blow destroyed the enemy's momentum. Gran pressed on, picking off the disordered cavalry.

Halted horsemen were nothing.

He split skulls, pierced hearts and throats——nothing on that field could stop Gran.

And the soldiers behind him roared and followed.

"The rest is yours! Bonnets, with me!"

"Sir!"

In a single pass he had half-wrecked the enemy cavalry. Leaving the cleanup to others, he led a hundred horsemen toward the enemy battle line.

In the meantime, the enemy pikemen had been thrown into thorough disarray——no longer capable of repelling a cavalry charge.

"Nobody here is dumb enough to be skewered by a pike. Let's go!"

"Sir! All units——chaaarge!!"

——He spurred his horse.

The mount was in fine form. Eager but steady.

He watched the enemy before him.

Pikes were not leveled.

Those that were held by soldiers whose faces showed only fear.

A spear thrust without conviction could not take a horse's life.

The distance closed with terrifying speed, and then came——impact.

Since the invention of the saddle and stirrup, cavalry were weapons in themselves, even unarmed.

Nearly ten times a man's mass, sheathed head to hoof in powerful muscle.

At full gallop, converting all that kinetic energy into impact——a human body crumpled like a twig.

A hundred riders in succession spread that shock across a thousand soldiers, paralyzing their cohesion for a single, critical instant.

The dull thud of countless collisions.

He crushed a soldier who had tried to brace but could not, and spared a glance at his mount.

His horse bore countless wounds——he had pushed it too hard. This battlefield would likely be its last.

Yet even now, it charged on. Scattering enemy soldiers with its hooves.

As if answering its master's will to press forward.

It was a good horse.

The best he had ever known.

By temperament, the horse was gentle——yet here, where its master demanded it, the animal delivered everything a warhorse could give.

Gran answered the horse's voice by swinging his spear.

He battered aside the enemies clinging to him and pushed forward.

If this horse survived, even if it could never run again, he would care for it until the day it died.

He looked behind.

The cavalry following were fighting desperately to keep up.

To his right——the soldiers who had charged in.

His father's——the strongest Second Corps had been split nearly to its middle.

——After severing the corps, he would press on to the isolated forward group and take Kokys.

It had been a magnificent, unstoppable advance.

The Second Corps' soldiers were all formidable.

Even torn apart this badly, they did not break; scattered and separated, they still came.

Not a single one faltered——every man was a warrior.

What rose within him was awe for the father who had forged soldiers to this degree.

Gran alternated his position several times but stayed at the front as much as he could.

His cavalry had already been halved.

Yet they followed Gran.

A commander always at the vanguard——they looked upon the courageous Gran with respect, driving him forward.

Eyes that had not been there before the battle.

The contemptuous gazes directed at Gran, who had betrayed his lord——at least in this moment, they did not exist.

Leading the infantry, carving a path like a blade——

Gran split the enemy formation diagonally, and——

"——!?"

What waited there was not his own allies.

——It was a spear, aimed at him.

He threw himself from the saddle and rolled on instinct.

His horse's neck was broken; it collapsed with a scream.

Tiger-crested armor, magnificent and imposing.

A muscular frame that seemed about to burst its plates from within. The spear in hand was eight shaku, three sun long——solid steel, a heavy lance. (T/N: approximately 250 cm / ~8'2")

Its tip was like a thick short-sword grafted on, and the butt-spike could shatter stone with ease.

The weighty armor was not for defense.

It was ballast——a counterweight to wield that monstrous spear.

Each step carved deep gouges into the earth.

In name and in truth, likely the kingdom's greatest spearman.

Kokys Nakutra Rinea Agrand stood before Gran.

As an opponent beyond all reason.

"Good spirit. It seems I underestimated you, Gran. ……Quite the impressive charge."

"……Father."

Gran looked to the right——toward the allied corps that should have been deployed on his left flank.

It was already in complete disarray.

And then he understood.

After breaking through the left wing, Kokys had read Gran's intent from the flanking strike——and had immediately decided to turn back. In the process, he had torn through what remained of the left wing from behind, crushing it as a parting gift.

Confident that Gran could not interfere.

It had been the finest possible counterstrike, within the limits of Gran's imagination.

Flawless, without deviation, accomplished in a trivially short span of time.

——*There was no way Father could have come back this quickly. Not while also annihilating an entire corps.*

That was what reason told him——and yet reality stood right before his eyes.

"Hey."

"……Sir."

At Kokys's signal, a soldier beside him tossed something.

What rolled to Gran's feet was a head.

It belonged to Tobal, the corps commander who had been leading the left flank alongside him.

"But allowing the breakthrough was your undoing. Did you think that with these rabble, with your little tricks, you could manage something against my corps? Did you honestly believe I'd have trouble with the ones you left behind me, Gran?"

One step closer.

Kokys's foot sank into the earth.

To Gran, his father's figure might as well have been a giant.

"And now, you've stopped. What begins from here is not a contest of tactics——it's a simple contest of strength. ……Gran, please don't make your father any sadder."

"——!?"

He barely dodged the spear.

What erupted was a sound no spear should make.

Not *cutting* the wind——

It was a blow that seemed to crush and warp the very space around it.

Before either of them noticed, the surrounding soldiers had backed away, forming a circle.

Father and son——Kokys and Gran.

There were only two.

The soldiers Gran had brought had lost their will to fight at the sight of Kokys.

They knew defeat was absolute; the spirit that had blazed moments ago was gone.

As though they knew what was coming.

"Can you afford to look around?"

"Ugh——!?"

——A thrust that seemed to pierce all the way through to the horizon.

A single strike that would punch through armor, flesh, and bone with ease.

A blow beyond reason, and yet it was merely a feint.

Every thrust in the relentless barrage——each one deserved to be called fatal and more.

That monstrous lance, wielded as lightly as a hand-spear.

His center of gravity was stable, his core perpetually balanced.

Only the spear moved with a fluidity that defied gravity, shaving away at Gran's life and spirit.

The blade would bisect Gran.

The steel shaft would shatter his bones through his armor.

The butt-spike would pulverize whatever it grazed.

Even Gran's own steel spear——a weapon no ordinary man could wield——seemed like a twig before Kokys's lance.

The onlookers could not utter a word.

The roar of Kokys's spear——the sheer wind pressure.

How many thrusts were delivered in the space between blinks?

That Gran continued to dodge them was proof he was no ordinary fighter——but the outcome was clear.

Because even in this fight, Kokys was visibly holding back.

With an upward sweep of the shaft, Kokys shattered the spear Gran had been forced to block.

Sent flying, Gran scrambled to his feet, drew his sword, and charged again.

"Not yet——!"

Gran screamed.

His lunge was a predator's leap for the throat.

And still, Kokys's spear was faster.

Gritting his teeth, without mercy, Kokys shattered that sword.

——The sword Kokys had once given Gran for his first campaign.

Gran's body was hurled away, tumbling across the ground.

The sword had absorbed some of the force, but his arm and his ribs were broken.

This time he could not rise. He could only lift his face to look at Kokys.

Kokys's face was a tangle of emotions, and——

"……Forgive me for being a failure of a father. Regardless……I grieve."

"Ah……"

He drove his spear through Gran's torso, armor and all, and pulled it free.

Kokys stood frozen before the body for a moment.

Remembering the feeling of lifting that infant in his arms.

"——Your commanders, Leeba Tobal and Gran Agrand, have been slain by Kokys Agrand!"

And then he roared, as though bellowing to the sky.

"If any among you wish to die on this spear, step forward! Whoever you are, I will tear you to pieces——!!"

His voice shook the air and thundered across the earth.

Many lowered their swords at the sound.

But then——from within the ranks, a voice rang out.

"——Corps Commander! The main camp's flags!"

Kokys turned to look.

Approaching from the right were six yellow flags, and at the far left, red-black-red.

Only a handful within the army knew what those signals meant——generals, battalion commanders, flag-bearers, and selected messengers.

This was deliberate; the system was designed to prevent harm to soldier morale.

"……Corps Commander Melkikos has——"

Kokys's eyes widened. He clenched his fist.

It was the flag announcing the loss of the left-wing corps commander——the death of Terrius Melkikos.
