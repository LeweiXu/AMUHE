# Chapter 96: Bunny

A room in Roland's estate.

It was likely one of several reception rooms.

A table sat in the center with sofas facing each other, and a shelf lined with liquor.

Inside the room were Kalua, Mia, and Elvena.

The other three had stepped outside.

Some time after the raid, the city's patrol corps had been alerted by the commotion and dispatched to the scene.

Since Elrouga's agents had already spoken with Baron Kornal, the city administrator, it did not escalate into a major incident.

Baron Kornal was a noble, but merely a city governor — an opportunist who bent with the prevailing wind.

This man, steeped up to his shoulders in the corruption of Kirzalan, proclaimed political neutrality — in short, he was a fool who simply went along with whoever held the most power.

Obsessed with nothing but money and status, Kornal discarded his long associate Roland without a moment's hesitation and, the instant he was shown Roland's letters, began wagging his tail at the Christands.

The patrol corps under his influence conducted a perfunctory scene investigation — which was really just corpse removal in the garden — and did not set a single foot inside the estate.

Some time had passed since Krische had taken Roland away.

The estate was quiet.

The other three had told Mia's group they would check on the patrol corps and head back first, then left the room.

That said, this was merely a pretext.

They had entrusted the situation to Mia, who was particularly close to Kalua.

And in case anything happened, they remained nearby rather than actually leaving.

The purpose was to make sure Kalua didn't act rashly — in other words, to prevent her from doing something like taking Elvena and running.

Regardless of anything else, Elvena had a debt, and something had to be done about it.

But the amount was far beyond what any single individual could pay, and the possibility of Kalua fleeing with Elvena was entirely plausible.

Her long-lost younger sister — having heard the story from Mia, the others understood the circumstances.

Such stories were not so rare as to be unheard of.

Even if not within their own families, there were those around them who had been abducted in similar fashion, and Bagu, who had formerly worked in the underworld, was especially familiar with the dark underbelly of the kingdom.

Like Mia, they sympathized with Kalua and worried about their comrade.

In this kingdom, no distinction based on sex was made regarding work or any other matter.

While men were given priority in matters of hierarchy, this was influenced by a political system in which a queen could come to power depending on circumstances.

It was not unheard of for a noble house to be headed by a woman, and female soldiers were not uncommon either.

That said, for a woman alone, searching the entire country for her sister with nothing but herself — that was certainly a harder, more perilous road than it would have been for a man.

Especially for someone as striking as Kalua, the dangers she would face were incomparable to a man's.

The extraordinary swordsmanship Kalua possessed — skill that no ordinary man could match — when one considered how it had been forged, the harshness of her journey was easy to imagine.

And by sheer chance, she had found her sister.

If only it could end as a happy ending — but the situation did not allow for that.

They, too, were torn over what should be done.

*— Comrades who had fought back to back, shared the same meals, and risked their lives together.*

Precisely because they were in the same squad, their feelings for her ran deep.

Many had come to admire her strength, and many had come to admire her character.

If it came down to it, they would help her escape — they held enough affection for Kalua to consider that. But at the same time, there were also their feelings toward Krische.

There was no denying that she was warped, that she was abnormal.

But everyone in the century knew her purity, her kindness.

They had heard her speak about such things before, and just recently, when Dagra had made his difficult request, she had answered with unadorned goodwill and compassion.

She was extremely earnest, placing great importance on rules and regulations, and yet — there was also the feeling that she might still help Kalua.

That said, it was still a difficult decision.

The moment Krische returned and said no — and the moment Krische set her mind to it — Kalua would no longer be able to escape.

That was precisely why they, too, were agonizing over whether they should help Kalua flee now.

"……I know bunny is a good kid too. But that amount is what it is. Do you really think anyone can just toss out that kind of money for a single soldier in the middle of a war?"

"But……"

"And even if she did help, it would still cause trouble for bunny. If you get far away now, bunny probably won't come after you, so……I still think that's the best thing to do."

Mia fell into thought at Kalua's words.

The price for Elvena was an amount that could sustain tens of thousands of soldiers for a day.

It was something that could, however slightly, affect military operations — not the kind of sum where one could rest easy thinking Krische could easily cover it.

It exceeded the bounds of goodwill to spend that on a single soldier.

Mia told Kalua to wait for Krische, and Kalua rejected the idea.

The one who spoke up was Elvena, who had been watching her sister and Mia with an uneasy expression.

"……The gentleman, Daglis-sama, said he could arrange work."

"Elvena, stop."

Kalua lowered her gaze and placed a hand on Elvena's head.

What Elvena wore was a serving outfit with a great deal of exposed skin.

That was the only decent garment she had been given, and with practiced ease, she had simply wrapped herself in it.

"……Just being able to see you again like this, nee-san — that alone makes me feel saved. That's how it's always been, and I'm……already used to things like that."

Elvena said this with a smile.

It was a beautiful smile — one that had accepted many things.

A suggestive outfit that revealed the curve of her chest. A short skirt — she had become utterly accustomed to such tasteless clothing, wearing it as naturally as everyday wear.

During the long years apart, Elvena had spent her time in such a place, and grown into an adult.

The kind of adult who gradually accepted resignation.

Kalua bit her lip and shook her head.

"I understand what kind of person Krische-sama is. From everything everyone's told me, from the way she was when I met her the other day — even though it was only a brief moment. And I understand how everyone here worries about you, nee-san."

Elvena leaned into Kalua and smiled.

"I'm fine. Things may continue as they have been, but — from now on, I properly have something to hope for. If I could be so selfish as to say I'll wait for nee-san to come for me someday……that alone is enough. Rather than throwing everything away and running, spending a few years like that and then living with nee-san without any lingering worries — that's so much more wonderful."

"Stop, Elvena. ……I don't want you doing that anymore."

Kalua spoke with an expression full of anguish, and Elvena stroked her cheek.

"I don't want nee-san to suffer either. I can tell — that you never forgot me, that you've been trying so hard all this time."

Kalua's skin — faintly traced with old scars.

Kalua's once-beautiful body was covered with countless such marks, and her once-soft palms were now those of a warrior who gripped a sword.

Calluses broken and hardened over and over, speaking of the years and how Kalua had spent them.

She had never so much as held a sword before.

If Kalua truly wanted to take her and run, that would have been fine.

Of course she didn't want her sister going off to war.

She wished they could live somewhere quiet, far removed from such things.

When she thought back on everything that had happened until today, that seemed like the most wonderful life imaginable.

But having heard everything until now, her answer was different.

Kalua had feelings about her current place, and deep down, she valued it.

She had an easygoing personality, an air of magnanimous openness at first glance — but she was really someone warm-hearted and kind.

That was precisely why Elvena admired her sister more than anyone — and precisely why she spoke.

"This was all originally caused by my foolish……reckless behavior. I don't want to drag nee-san around any further because of my own mess. The nee-san I admire is the freest, most wonderful person in the world."

Kalua could not answer, and clenched her fist. Elvena placed her hand over it.

Having heard all this, Mia looked at Kalua and spoke.

"……No matter what happens, when the time comes, I'll help however I can. Just like I said before. So, let's wait for the Corps Commander and then decide? Even if the debt can't be resolved, the Corps Commander might be able to find other places for her to work."

"Mia……"

"The Corps Commander is, um……she's got no common sense in a lot of ways, and she's, well, not just a little but quite a big dummy, so that's one thing, but……still, she's reaaally ki——"

"……Who exactly is a dummy with no common sense?"

"Hyeek!?"

Hearing the voice, they turned to the entrance — and there stood Krische.

She had returned remarkably quickly.

Krische had decided to wrap up Roland's interrogation when Elrouga's agents arrived, leaving the rest to them and coming back.

Roland had become cooperative and was voluntarily divulging all manner of information, so further threats or torture were unnecessary.

For Krische, Kalua's matter was more important than Roland, who was already a dead man in her mind.

But having hurried back, the very first thing she heard upon arrival were Mia's words.

Looking displeased, she furrowed her brows and placed both hands on her hips.

Then she walked up to Mia without ceremony and pinched her cheeks.

"So Mia thinks of Krische that way."

"Ngh, th-that's not……it was just a figure of speech……"

"Thinking it is one thing, but saying it out loud constitutes disrespect toward a superior officer. You do understand that, don't you? Now Krische understands perfectly well what Vulture meant when he said your bad habit is speaking your mind too freely. Good grief……"

"Tha, that's not what I meant, Corps Commaaander……"

*Honestly*, she placed both hands on her hips, sighing just the way Selene would.

Saying things like, *Krische has ended up with quite the troublesome subordinate*, she turned her gaze to Kalua and Elvena.

The more she looked, the more alike they were.

Being told they were sisters, one might think *well, perhaps* — but then again, coincidences do happen.

There was absolutely no reason for Krische to be treated like such a dummy, she thought, nodding to herself.

"Krische gave it some thought. As a result, it is not possible for the Christands to repay Elvena's debt. It's a large sum, after all."

"……I see."

Kalua didn't raise her voice; she simply smiled with an air of resignation.

Krische continued without pause.

"That said, Kalua has been working hard, and has been very good to Krische. For Krische to do nothing in return — considering what's owed for everything up to now, the ledger simply doesn't balance. So, Krische will purchase Elvena's debt for the time being."

"……Huh?"

Krische smiled.

Then, raising a finger, she spoke cheerfully.

"The problem with repaying money is the interest, so Krische will do something about that. If Krische purchases the debt, the current ten percent interest can be reduced quite significantly."

Kalua and Elvena stared blankly.

Noticing they didn't seem to understand, Krische thought for a moment.

"Umm, specifically, Krische would reduce the current interest to one-tenth — about one percent."

*It's not charity, after all*, Krische said.

"To put it simply……the interest amounts to roughly one pumpkin per day. If you repay about eleven pumpkins per day, you could pay it all off in less than ten years. Considering the bonuses on top of a soldier's salary, Kalua earns reasonably well, so with effort, it's within reach."

Krische recalled her life in the village.

"Krische lived in a family of three in the village, and our daily expenses were roughly one pumpkin. As long as you're careful about luxuries, you can live perfectly well on that much. Well, if you're living in a city, you might need two or three pumpkins for rent and such, but even so, Kalua earns reasonably well, so it should be fine."

The two were still staring blankly.

Krische tilted her head. *Nn?* She continued further.

"Umm, and also, from what Krische saw the other day, Elvena is quite capable as a servant. Considering that Anne, who lives in at the Christand estate, earns about two pumpkins a day, Elvena could probably manage three or four pumpkins without doing anything too difficult. So if the two of you economize, and Kalua works really hard, you might be able to pay it all off comfortably in two or three years."

No matter how much she continued, there was no reaction.

Krische looked at Mia, at a loss.

Mia noticed her gaze, smiled happily, and bowed her head.

"……Is that really okay?"

The voice was Kalua's.

Krische nodded.

"Yes. If Krische handles it through written documents and defers the payments, then Selene probably……won't be too angry. By then, the war will be over too. There seem to be creditors in this city as well, so Krische was thinking of visiting the debt holder after tomorrow's execution."

*If Krische goes, they'll agree to those terms*, she continued.

From what she had heard from Roland — she knew the creditor was involved in various illegal dealings, so she was confident they wouldn't try to gouge her on the debt purchase.

"But the debt hasn't disappeared — the holder has just changed to Krische. Krische will be strict about collection, you know; falling behind on payments is absolutely not permitted. Krische is using the Christands' precious money, so if you try to default on——woah."

Kalua stood and embraced Krische.

Krische looked at Kalua with surprise and tilted her head.

"……Thank you."

"Um, the debt hasn't gone away, you know? Just so you're aware, the minimum interest will be deducted from Kalua's salary."

"Hehe, that's not what I'm talking about. ……But, I like that about you, bunny."

Kalua said it frankly, and seeing that she was apparently happy, Krische smiled.

Selene might scold her a little, but since it would ultimately generate some profit as interest, she probably wouldn't be too angry.

Even if she was scolded, it surely wouldn't be a *terrible* scolding, Krische thought.

Kalua was a reasonably capable subordinate, and it was worth maintaining a good relationship with her. If their relationship soured and Kalua left the century, that would be a problem for Krische too.

In the end, maintaining a good relationship with the reasonably capable Kalua would ultimately benefit Selene — and so the theoretical justification was fully assembled in Krische's mind.

*It's fine, it'll be fine*, Krische nodded to herself.

In truth, she would have preferred to wait for Selene and consult her before deciding.

But if, by some chance, Selene said no, Krische would have no choice but to obey.

What Krische prioritized above all was Selene's interests.

To Krische, Selene's wishes were things to be fulfilled without question, to be obeyed.

Going against that to independently do something for someone else was something Krische could not do.

The reason she had decided to take the form of after-the-fact approval was precisely this.

In Krische's mind it was a gray area, but Selene would probably forgive her — and on that uncertain judgment, Krische acted.

That said, having required this much deliberation from Krische, Kalua would have to work even harder than before to justify things to Selene.

"Listen well? Krische is prepared to apologize to Selene for this. From now on, Kalua will have to work much, much more. As long as the debt remains, you will work hard under Krische's command."

"……Yeah."

It went without saying for Kalua. With a wry smile, she separated from Krische, knelt, and took Krische's hand, pressing her lips to it.

"As you command. ……I swear on this name and this sword."

"Ehehe, then it's a promise."

Krische smiled and approached Elvena.

Elvena, too, bowed her head with tears welling in the corners of her eyes, saying, *Thank you so much.*

"Nn……now then, what to do about Elvena, who's now out of a job. Do you have anywhere to go?"

"No……"

"From what Krische has heard, the job Daglis mentioned comes with lodging. If Elvena doesn't mind, that could work, but……"

"Huh? U-um……"

"C-Corps Commander……that's no good……"

"……?"

The "considerate establishment" Daglis had mentioned was, in short, merely a considerate brothel — but Krische still did not properly understand this.

Given the flow of the conversation, this was only natural.

Mia, her emotions swept clean of the prior moment's moved feelings, was now holding her head in exasperation at Krische, who was unknowingly about to send Elvena back to selling her body.

"Uh, usa-chan……? Umm, that job is, how do I put it……"

From there, Kalua went through considerable trouble trying to make Krische understand that the nature of this work was not at all appropriate.

While Krische had knowledge of animal mating, her values were profoundly different from those of a normal person — and on top of that, she was a girl of such spiritual innocence that she might believe it if told storks delivered babies.

Avoiding direct expressions and wrapping things in euphemism, communicating that this was "not good work" required quite some time.

And so, half a quarter-hour later.

As the hour grew late and Krische began to drowse, the gathering came to a close.

Roland's estate had many rooms for entertaining distinguished guests.

They had secured rooms at a city inn, but it seemed foolish to go all the way there at such a late hour, so Krische's group decided to use the estate's spare rooms and retire for the night.

Mia took the sleepy Krische away, and in the room assigned to Elvena, Kalua lay gazing up at the ceiling.

"Hehe, she really is a strange person……I thought so before, too."

"Bunny is a little unusual. ……But she's a really good kid."

She stroked the head of the softly giggling Elvena and smiled.

"……Usa-chan."

"Ah, I wonder who started it. She built the current century, you know, but the training was inhumanly harsh. On top of that, she never smiled, and all she ever said was things like *you lack motivation*, *you lack grit*, *your effort is insufficient* — a real demon instructor."

The purpose had been to drill proficiency in handling mana — at least in name — but the training had been truly grueling, she thought.

Even Kalua had been so exhausted she couldn't eat properly for days.

Literally from dawn to dusk, pushed to the limit — some collapsed.

After that, a brief rest, and then, as if it weren't enough, hauling massive barrels loaded with stones.

Those like Kalua, who had unconsciously been using mana already, had it somewhat easier, but for those who hadn't, it must have been nothing short of torture.

When Krische came to visit the soldiers who could barely eat, she was leisurely munching on a cookie as she said:

*"Dagra, isn't the push not enough? They're still relying on their muscles. Krische thinks they should be made a bit more tired."*

Saying such things without a care — back then, she had been bitterly resented.

"She looks that adorable, yet she orders training that makes people vomit blood without batting an eye — and then someone started calling her *that crazy rabbit*."

But that was only at first.

The general's daughter Selene and the servant Bery were highly popular among the soldiers.

When those two were at her side, Krische would nestle up to them like a child, laughing — from their perspective, she was astonishingly cheerful.

Seeing and hearing that, everyone gradually realized she was a girl even younger in spirit than she appeared.

That the grueling training was simply Krische commanding what was normal to her — not something done out of malice.

Bagu, from their own squad, had once been among them.

Before anyone knew it, even those men had been won over by Krische's manner——

"Eventually, everyone came to understand she's just a serious, childlike girl, and it turned into more of a pet name — though I'm about the only one who calls her that openly. Hehe, isn't it cute? Bunny."

"I was thinking — you don't have to be so formal with me, you know? You can talk to me the way you talk to Krische-sama and Mia-sama."

"Nn……somehow, that's a bit embarrassing."

"But compared to how you talk to them, it feels like you're being distant with me. I'd much rather you speak casually — it makes me feel so much closer."

"Oh,……you're a strange one."

*Either way is fine*, Kalua said, and in the same fashion, she reached out to confirm, touching Elvena's cheek.

Elvena pressed even closer, burying her face in Kalua's chest.

"……From now on, can I always stay with you?"

"If nee-san is going back……I'll follow."

"I can't go back. I was disowned, you know."

*I'll do my best at bunny's place for a while*, she said, and held Elvena tight.

"Nee-sa——"

"Don't worry about it too much. I can't say there weren't times I regretted it, but finding you like this is because of all that. All's well that ends well, or however it goes."

Clinging to Kalua's shoulders, she peered into her eyes.

"Even if I went home, I'd surely just be treated like something fragile and unwanted. There'd be no place for me. ……If nee-san isn't going back, then I won't either."

"……If that's what Elvena wants, I promise."

"Yes……"

"And also……"

Kalua let her gaze waver as if hesitating, and slowly closed her long lashes.

When she opened her eyes again, she spoke with faintly glistening eyes.

"……I'm sorry. This time, I'll take you with me properly."

Elvena's eyes narrowed for a moment as if looking at something far away, and she smiled.

"I'm sorry too. I was a hopelessly selfish little child. ……But I haven't really changed much, have I."

She giggled and gazed at Kalua.

"……I feel like I've been waiting all this time. To hear those words."

"……I see. I made you wait."

"……Yes. I was waiting."

*— So I'm never letting go again.*

She said this, clung tight, whispered *good night*, and closed her eyes.

In her smiling sister's arms, the girl fell into the first true sleep she'd had in years.

"Yeah. ……Good night, Elvena."

# Chapter 97: Light and Dark Spiral

It was the public square of Kirzalan.

People had gathered in a wide ring with distance between them, and at the center stood five soldiers in black-lacquered armor and a silver-haired girl.

They held aloft the banner of the crescent-moon skull as a show of force, standing atop a hastily erected platform.

Baron Kornal, the thin-framed governor of Kirzalan with his neatly trimmed figure-eight mustache, was present at the scene, and before him, the pale-faced old man — the somewhat haggard Chief Judicial Officer Elze — brandished a letter and delivered a speech with enough force to practically spray spittle.

What he held was the letter Roland had sent to the Hilkintos army.

Commerce was to be held paramount, and merchants — who revered profit over honor — must not hold power; they were to exist as neutral parties who cooperated with those in authority only through contracts. At minimum, that was how merchants were treated under kingdom law.

To pose as a collaborator while secretly colluding with the enemy was unforgivable, and therefore this base merchant Roland would be executed — so Elze desperately shouted the words he had stayed up all night composing, putting on the appearance of "a righteous guardian of the law."

What he glanced at sidelong from time to time was Krische.

Cold, looking bored.

That beautiful face did not so much as twitch, and the gaze of inorganic violet that watched Elze revealed nothing.

Whether she was satisfied with his words or not was impossible to tell.

The witness — Elze, who had been receiving bribes from Roland, understood that the prior night had been a demonstration aimed squarely at him.

It was his role to convey to the public that this execution was carried out through legitimate means, not as an act of violence by the Christand army — and he feared that depending on how well his speech went, he might share the same fate.

And Baron Kornal, who observed his desperation up close, also feared the Christand's daughter standing beside him — the cursed child, Krische.

He was aware that Elze had been present at the torture as a witness, but the man's state was far beyond normal.

It was easy to understand that a terrible fear must have befallen him.

Kornal had long governed Kirzalan by virtue of his position.

His dealings with the city's merchants ran deep, and Roland was naturally no exception.

He could not claim there was nothing shady about any of it.

Slave trading, smuggling, and all manner of other "commerce" conducted by such corrupt merchants — he had known about it, and had looked the other way while accepting bribes. His position was no different from Elze's.

If the Christands achieved victory and Princess Kreschenta took the throne, his head could fly on nothing more than the cursed child's impression of him.

He had intended to proclaim neutrality and remain as uninvolved in this civil war as possible unless asked — but now he reconsidered.

He turned his thoughts to his assets, calculating how much he could afford to give up for their sake.

"— Therefore, in the name of the blood and heroic spirits connected to the kingdom, Chief Judicial Officer of Kirzalan, Falkus Elze, shall carry out the execution!"

The square was filled with fervor.

The majority of people there had come simply to watch an execution.

They were half-listening to Elze's words — people who just wanted to see a head roll.

Some among them bore grudges against Roland, but they were few; most were simply looking forward to this execution as entertainment.

*— What is enjoyable about an execution, Krische wonders.*

Krische, who had been listening to Elze's long and noisy speech, tilted her head and stifled a yawn.

Having been woken by a headbutt, she wanted nothing more than to go back and take a nap.

She moved her lips absently, narrowed her eyes sleepily.

At last it seemed to be ending — she scrunched her brows, fighting off the drowsiness with a displeased expression.

Catching this from the corner of his eye, Kornal sensed danger and stepped forward.

"I am Fanaktoa Elbekiir Kornal, entrusted with the governance of Kirzalan. Allow me to explain the circumstances of this affair once more, in my own words——"

Just when she thought it was finally over, Kornal suddenly launched into a speech.

What Krische felt was sheer astonishment.

*It's not over yet?* — she glared at Mia, and Mia answered with a troubled expression, whispering, *Probably just a little more.*

Everyone except Krische had more or less gauged the sentiments of Elze and Kornal from their behavior.

Krische was the only one annoyed by the length of the speeches.

And so, after Kornal's speech — larding praise upon the Christands and Krische for the better part of a quarter-hour — finally concluded, the cage that had been placed behind them was at last unveiled, its covering removed, and a single man was brought out.

It was Roland, screaming as he was half-carried, half-dragged by two masked men on either side.

Krische had not injured him in any place visible above the clothing.

Outwardly, dressed in slacks and a jacket, Roland appeared unharmed — yet in several places, blood was seeping through even the thick fabric.

Both arms and legs had been tightly bound at the roots to stop the bleeding, and his gouged torso was also firmly wrapped in tourniquets and bandages.

Even so, he was barely alive — sweat glistened on his face, his eyes were vacant.

His complexion was ashen, and there was something wrong with his breathing.

He prayed to the gods that her blade would never turn on him.

Roland's eyes — clouded with fear, regret, and despair.

The inorganic violet narrowed ever so slightly, as if in pleasure.

Then they passed.

Laid face-down on the block, Roland screamed and let out porcine gasps.

The stench of blood, feces, and urine wafted through the air.

During an execution, that space belonged only to the condemned and the executioner.

Roland's estate — the kitchen.

She did not want to remain here a moment longer.

He, too, had apparently been thinking the same — he answered her look with a nod.

"……I arranged this meeting as a basic courtesy, but it was wasted time. General Keesliton, I wish you fortune in battle. As for you, Your Highness — I would ask that you refrain from further disgraceful displays."

Nozan spoke as though spitting, then turned on his heel.

Krische gave Gildanstein one last glare.

Gildanstein simply laughed with amusement.

Little time remained until nightfall, and her mind was in turmoil.

Concerned both by Krische's state of mind and the risk of the battle dragging into the night, Nozan decided to let the day pass — and Gildanstein, too, maintained his defensive posture.

Neither side made a significant move, and the day ended.

And so, the curtain on the decisive battle would rise the following day.
