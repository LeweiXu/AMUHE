# Chapter 246: Shadows and Splendor

The war against Elsren.

It was when she had petitioned her father to accompany him to the battlefield.

*"Generally speaking, one might say that effort should be valued. There is worth in the act of trying itself. ……Even if the results don't follow, that effort becomes fuel for the next attempt. And so, if one has a goal, rather than deliberate, one ought to first strive toward its fulfillment. In time the results will become clear, and the act of continued effort holds value in itself."*

However, this is the logic of commoners, her father said.

*"Once you stand on the battlefield as a soldier, effort has no value whatsoever. ……All that is needed is results — and nothing more than the single achievement of victory. Because a soldier who fails to bring back results is never given the chance to think of a 'next time.' Setting aside if the fool's mistake kills only himself, one's own defeat endangers the lives of one's comrades, and the lives of the very people one is meant to protect."*

His eyes could have killed.

He was a kind father, but before being a father he was a warrior, and a leader who commanded warriors.

When facing her as a fellow soldier, he always showed Selene a stern, unyielding figure.

*"Effort is expected as a given — it does not merit evaluation. The battlefield has no need for soldiers who cannot produce results, and naturally, no need for such commanders either. Between a hardworking fool and a lazy genius, the latter wins without room for debate. A person who cannot show results has no place in my army. ……And that applies to you as well."*

*"……!"*

*"You are a daughter I am proud of. You have talent, a sharp mind. You have not let that make you arrogant; you have built yourself up through effort. For your age, you are exceptional — a rare gem the likes of which are seldom seen. — However, compared to my corps commanders, you are far too weak, far too immature, far too unsteady, and in overall ability including experience, you do not even match my battalion commanders. If one sets aside your age and coolly assesses your abilities, you are, at the end of the day, only that."*

His words were delivered like a verdict, imperious, brooking no dissent.

Her father was adored by his subordinates, revered, and feared.

The strongest in the surrounding world — Christand the Thunderbolt and the forces under his command were indisputably the heroes of the kingdom, the sword and shield of the realm that could never be permitted defeat.

And precisely because of that, her father tolerated not a shred of laxity in his army.

Even if it were Selene — had he judged her useless, he would have discarded Selene Christand the soldier without a moment's hesitation.

She knew that her father was a man who valued a soldier's duty above all else.

That was precisely why she could feel prouder of him than anyone.

*"Once you step onto the battlefield, once you choose that path, there is no turning back. I will never permit excuses or complaints. Nor will I grant you leniency simply because you are my daughter. Should you expose your incompetence on the battlefield, I will deem you unfit as a soldier and permanently remove you from my army. ……With that said, I ask once more. Do you still wish to request accompaniment, understanding all of this?"*

Selene looked Bogan straight in the eyes and placed her right palm over her left breast.

She straightened her posture as though clicking her heels together, and saluted.

*"Yes, Otou-sama. ……I swear upon my name, I will serve you without fail. Please permit me to accompany you."*

The hero — Bogan Christand — fixed her with a hawk's piercing gaze, and nodded.

*"……Understood. I assign you as the General's adjutant, Christand Army. Fulfill duties beyond your station."*

He pressed his right thumb against his left breast as though driving it in, and returned her salute.

How to express what she felt in that moment, she did not know.

It was the first salute returned to Selene as a soldier.

It was the moment she felt, for the first time in her life, that her father had acknowledged her.

*"Stubborn girl. Between Lazura, Bery, and you — it must be the Argan blood."*

With an exasperated sigh, her father settled into the sofa.

*"However, since you have sworn upon your name as a noble, I have no intention of permitting you to take that back. If you wish to be a soldier, show results at every turn. If they whisper behind your back that you are only there because you are my daughter, then silence them by demonstrating abilities that leave no room for argument. At the very least, I saw in you the capacity for that, and that is why I gave my approval. Prove through your actions that my eyes were not mistaken."*

*"……Yes. I will deliver results without fail. I will never bring you shame, Otou-sama."*

For a soldier, results are everything.

That was the creed her father held dearest.

It was the creed Selene too must hold dearest, and so long as she did, even after her father was gone, Selene would remain her father's daughter.

*"— Then I'll protect you from anything terrible, forever."*

Swinging her blade for someone's sake, protecting all of them.

So long as she did that, so long as she was that — Selene felt she could take pride in being her father's daughter.

She had stepped, of her own volition, into a world where results were everything, not effort.

Complaints were not permitted to Selene Christand.

The soft feel of the bed — what am I doing, she thought, cracking her eyes open.

Small-scale fighting should still be continuing in the west.

And yet Selene was not commanding it; she was in the estate, indolently sprawled on a bed.

*"That's a terrible face. ……If I may speak as a fellow soldier, and with all due disrespect — you are in a dangerous state right now, Selene-sama. Please do not push yourself."*

Because she had been sent back, unable to accomplish a single thing.

It was not Kokys's fault. He was simply being a proper soldier.

Selene was the one at fault.

— What am I doing, she repeated.

She had not been able to help Krische or Kreschenta in any way.

She could not even properly fulfill the role assigned to her as Marshal.

And while all of that was happening, Bery had died.

As a sister, as a soldier, as the head of the household — Selene had not produced a single result.

Far from it; she had only gotten in the way.

There was no value in such a person.

*"Bery is here, so you can rest easy now. Krische will prepare from here, and create a world where Bery and everyone can live in peace. This way, from now on, forever——"*

She had tried to stop Krische, but Krische had brought Bery back regardless.

Krische had never once broken a promise.

When she said she would do something, she always delivered results.

All Selene had done was fail to trust her and try to get in her way.

Results are everything — if Selene had believed in her, she might have been able to suggest measures to maximize safety.

Even if it was an unknown domain like souls, even if common sense said such a thing was impossible, that was no excuse.

If she had not been emotional in that moment, if she could have thought logically, she might at least have been able to detain Krische until Kreschenta arrived.

At the very least, Krische had acted because she had some kind of conviction, and that was precisely why she had been able to bring back results.

Selene did not think Krische had been absolutely certain of success, but choices always carry risk.

Even given the risk, she could have made time to weigh it against the outcome, and if the risk had been made clear, even if she could not stop Krische, she might at least have gotten her to listen.

— Or perhaps, she thought, that had been what she truly wanted all along.

The result Selene had wanted was nothing more than for Krische to accept Bery's death.

In Selene's mind, Bery was already dead.

Even supposing she could have guided everything to the optimal outcome — even supposing Krische had said she wanted to save Bery despite the danger — it was doubtful whether Selene could truly have sent her off fully prepared.

She would have stopped her after all, afraid of losing Krische too.

At the very least, she would have been unable to accept the risk of losing Krische.

And for that reason, she would have decided that Bery could remain dead.

"……Why did it have to be you?"

What if Bery had been in Selene's place?

The more she thought about it, the filthier she looked to herself.

Even if it were Selene and not Bery, Krische would surely have tried to save her.

But if it were Bery, she surely could have calmed Krische down even in that state.

*"— It is a trifling matter. ……As I have told you before, Krische-sama is not that sort of person…… and that is precisely what I love so dearly about her."*

If Krische had found the solution, Bery would have weighed the risk — and in the end, no matter how she felt, for Selene's sake, for the sake of what Krische wanted, she would have sent her off.

*"If anything should happen to me, please take care of Krische-sama. ……I don't need an answer."*

Selene had watched Bery since the day she was born.

She had admired her all along.

And so she could imagine it with conviction.

*"— Fumbling, clumsy, and yet more earnest than anyone…… I wished to devote my whole life to such a Krische-sama. ……Please forgive me."*

Surely from that moment, Bery had already resolved to accept all of Krische's choices, to accept everything about her, and to love her.

There was not a trace of muddiness in Bery's love.

A hidden name was just a word.

But to offer one's hidden name — one's true name — was to do precisely that.

And it was because this was the true name of such a woman that meaning dwelt within it.

That Krische loved Selene was a fact beyond doubt.

If Selene were in danger, just as Bery had said, Krische would try to save her even knowing the risk.

Selene loved her too.

But if asked whether her love was as clear, as unmuddied as Bery's — it was not.

What Selene had was merely 'affection,' not the kind of unquestionable 'love' that Krische and Bery possessed.

The hopeless inferiority she had felt toward Bery must have sprung from that.

She had been jealous, yet accepted it as inevitable, because she had unconsciously understood.

The reason Bery had been able to open the hearts of Krische and Kreschenta, to give them so many things, to earn their trust — was surely because she was who she was.

*— Please take care of Krische-sama.*

Selene had no right to nod to those words.

The radiance dwelling inside the mana crystal was endlessly beautiful, and when told that it was Bery's soul, she found it convincing.

If souls existed, then Bery's soul would surely be a thing of boundless beauty — Selene felt that way, and Krische's and Kreschenta's must be the same.

But surely Selene's soul was not.

"……I'm not like the rest of you."

Krische had said she would create eternity.

A paradise that no one could threaten — a childish, ideal world.

And yet she was certain that Krische and the others would see it through.

Krische's absolute was absolute in the truest sense.

Even death, that inescapable fate, had been overcome by her absolute.

And so Krische and the others would obtain their paradise.

Beyond doubt, absolutely.

Selene had no confidence she could keep up.

The paradise born of those pure souls would be an unending hell for Selene.

To spend all of eternity ahead as a tainted foreign body — to have it shown to her, again and again, that she was different from them, through all of eternity.

Even so, they would love her.

With an endlessly pure, gentle love.

Slowly, softly, like cotton around her throat — until someday, Selene could bear it no longer.

"……Why am I like this?"

The words escaped her, and the wretched feeling blurred her vision; she pressed her face into the pillow and stained it.

She felt herself to be an endlessly pathetic, weak person.

If it were Bery, she would have said without hesitation: I will follow you anywhere.

If it were the wish of the one she loved, she would surely not have faltered.

Why was she not that kind of person, she thought.

Why had she not been able to become that kind of person, she thought.

All that left her mouth were hopeless, miserable complaints.

She felt so pathetic she wanted to die right then and there, and she could only resent herself — a self that had nothing to hold her head high about.

* * *

Since she had been absent from the castle for a day, Kreschenta had expected some minor commotion, but the quick-witted Elvena had apparently informed everyone that Kreschenta had taken ill and was resting.

The pillar of light from the spatial transference could lead some to suspect that Kreschenta had done something, but it had been in the dead of night.

Only guards had witnessed it, and while reports had been filed, few would connect it to Kreschenta.

The rumor of the half-attendant servant's death was known, and the previous day's absence had not caused anything resembling a stir.

She attended to her duties as usual, returned to the estate, and —

"……Still sulking?"

"……Kreschenta, please don't say it like that. Krische is the one at fault."

Seeing that her sister was not in her room, she headed to the kitchen.

There was Krische in her apron dress, staring fixedly at the meal on the tray.

The meal was probably for Selene. It had not been touched.

Kreschenta walked over, picked up the bowl of soup, leaned against the table, and began eating.

Krische gave her a slightly reproachful look, but said nothing and fell silent.

At the very least, Selene would not be eating today — that much was certain.

"It's been a while since Krische had Onee-sama's cooking, hasn't it. It's delicious."

"……Shall Krische warm it up?"

"It's fine. Krische is used to cold food."

She said it, finished the pumpkin soup, set the bowl on the table, and stroked Krische's cheek.

Eating warm meals on a regular basis had only begun after she came to Christand.

The food was cold, but royal meals were always like that; it did not particularly bother her.

Warm food certainly tasted better, but she was not inclined to demand it in this situation.

"……Does Kreschenta understand?"

"More or less. ……Krische isn't angry at Onee-sama or anything."

"Then why——"

"Selene-sama is a troublesome person."

Kreschenta said it, and drew her sister close.

Then she settled into a chair and placed Krische on her lap.

"People like that almost always have rules."

"……Rules?"

"Yes. In the same way as a noble's duty, or matters of principle — they have their own internal rules."

She took the mana crystal hanging from Krische's neck and gazed at it.

"If no one found out, there would be nothing wrong with killing someone. If you thought it best, you should simply do it. The law's punishment is void if you are never discovered. ……And yet most people do not. Setting aside the cowards who are afraid to kill, afraid to break the law — there are many who would not make that choice even without such fear."

It looked as though she were toying with it, and also cherishing it.

Krische watched her sister's hands in silence.

"Such people invariably have rules within themselves. Regardless of what society thinks, personal decisions they have resolved to uphold no matter what. ……'I swear upon my name' is one such phrase. It is merely a verbal promise — there should be no issue with breaking it — yet no matter how much they stand to lose, most nobles will honor it."

"……Swearing upon one's name."

"Yes. In some cases, they would even choose death — or perhaps, no matter how much suffering they must endure, there is something they have decided they will absolutely uphold."

How foolish, Kreschenta said, and enclosed the mana crystal in both palms.

Krische could not see her expression.

"Of course, how far they hold to their rules varies from person to person, and the contents differ greatly. The reason Selene-sama is acting like that is surely because of such a thing. ……From where Krische stands, it's absurd. She's fixating on something pointless."

"……Kreschenta."

Krische spoke the name with a note of reproach, and Kreschenta said, It's a fact.

"……It is a pointless fixation. It is nothing more than stubbornness."

With that, Kreschenta slipped the mana crystal, cord and all, from her sister's neck and placed it around her own.

When Krische tilted her head and looked back, Kreschenta continued.

"Nothing good will come of Onee-sama sitting here thinking. Why not go to Selene-sama and talk to her directly?"

"Selene's room was locked…… And why did you take Bery?"

"It's probably better if you're not wearing it. And can't you just open a lock on your own?"

"That would be……"

"She won't be angry. Selene-sama isn't angry at Onee-sama."

Krische thought for a moment, then nodded.

Then she hopped down from Kreschenta's lap.

"……Whether or not Krische really understands what Kreschenta is saying, Krische understands a little bit, at least. ……Krische will try talking to Selene again."

"Yes, off you go."

Kreschenta smiled as she said it, and Krische smiled in return.

"Please look after Bery, Kreschenta."

Repeating words she had said countless times before, Krische left the kitchen at a trot.

Left alone, Kreschenta watched the door for a while, then lifted the mana crystal slightly and gazed at it.

She stared and stared at the radiance held within, doing nothing else.

# Chapter 247: Longing and Innocence

"……Sorry for coming in without permission. But Krische wanted to talk to Selene."

There was no answer to her knock, so she manipulated her mana to undo the lock.

Krische entered Selene's room, saw that Selene had not raised her face or turned to look, understood she was not asleep, and approached.

"I told you to leave me alone."

"……Yes. Sorry."

"I'll hear you out tomorrow. ……So please, not today."

Krische hesitated briefly, then said nothing and sat on the edge of the bed.

She let her gaze wander the room.

Come to think of it, it had been quite a long time since she had visited Selene's room like this.

Usually it was Selene who came to Krische's room.

Krische stroked the head of Selene, who lay facing away.

"……Kreschenta told you to come, didn't she?"

"……Yes. She said rather than Krische sitting alone thinking, Krische ought to talk to Selene properly."

"About what?"

When asked, Krische thought about it, then smiled as though fondly remembering, and spoke.

"It kind of reminds Krische of when Krische first came to Christand. ……Back then, the Head of House asked Krische to keep Selene company, and Krische just went to Selene's room without thinking about anything. ……Without thinking at all about what to talk about."

She stroked Selene's smooth, long hair, twining it around her fingers.

"Krische wants to know the reason. Why Selene doesn't want this. ……If Krische knows the reason, then Krische would like to think about all sorts of things to make Selene not feel that way anymore."

"……Please, just make it tomorrow. I told you, didn't I? I'm tired."

"If Selene promises to tell Krische the reason tomorrow, Krische will go. But Krische doesn't think that's what will happen."

She continued.

"……Maybe Selene really isn't angry. But maybe Selene is fed up and disappointed with Krische. ……Is that why you don't even want to talk?"

"……Stop."

"Krische knows Selene said Krische wouldn't understand. It's true that Krische is a fool. There are things Krische can't understand that a normal person could. But Krische wants to properly understand Selene. So——"

"——Stop it!!"

The voice rang out, and for an instant Krische froze; Selene pressed her face hard into the pillow she was clutching.

"……Really, stop. I don't want to feel any more miserable. I'm sick of it. ……Please, just leave me alone for now."

"Selene……"

She was trembling, crying.

Selene cried often. But usually it was from relief, or from joy — today was clearly different.

Krische wrapped her arms around Selene from behind.

"……Sorry."

It was not that she understood the reason.

It was just that Selene was crying, and that was surely Krische's fault.

At the very least, Krische had hurt her enough to make her cry like this.

The trembling back was like a child's.

The stubborn, willful elder sister she always knew was not there.

— What is the cause, she simply wondered.

Thinking back, Selene had not seemed herself even when Krische last saw her at the battlefield.

Selene had been crying when Krische left, too — that was all she could think of.

Krische was aware she had been terrible, practically ignoring Selene, but she had assumed Selene would understand the circumstances.

Of course, that might have been nothing more than Krische's selfish assumption, and given the present result, it seemed it was not so.

Thinking did not help.

And yet asking what was so miserable felt like it would only hurt her more.

After thinking for a while, she set the thought aside for now.

"Krische always thought that all Krische needed was one house."

Krische said.

"It didn't even have to be a proper estate — even a tiny cabin would have been fine. ……A place like that where everyone could live together forever, and that would be enough. No danger, no wars, no bad people…… Even during the civil war, if Selene had nodded, Krische wanted to abandon everything, flee deep into the mountains, and live quietly."

That is what they call a dream, isn't it, Krische said, smiling.

"Krische thought that once the civil war ended, once the wars with the neighboring countries ended, there would be peace for a while — but peace is never eternal. Even without war, there are problems, all sorts of things. Krische has Krische's circumstances, but other people naturally have theirs too, and there's no guarantee they'll want the same things."

She buried her face in Selene's neck and narrowed her eyes.

"Krische was already fed up with it. Krische even thought it might be better to just destroy everything…… But it's much more peaceful for Krische and everyone to simply move away, so Krische thought that was best, and so——"

That man from Elderant had come to avenge someone Krische had killed.

In the end, no matter what Krische did, sparks of war would always ignite somewhere.

Those with malice would always appear.

Including indirect deaths, Krische had killed well over a hundred thousand.

How many people bore grudges against her, she could not know.

Krische did not care how many or how deeply they hated her.

But such people might reach not just for Krische, but for those around her.

Going forward, and further still — the longer it continued, the more their numbers would grow.

Hundreds, thousands, past a hundred million.

*"— Thinking only of your small world, punishing all who cross you according to your whims — you must be a terrifying existence to many who lack power. At the very least, many of the weak — the nobles — must have felt their security threatened by you."*

She remembered her grandfather's words.

*"And fear will always turn to enmity. For now, they fear you and will obey. But that will fester, and ten years, twenty years from now, it may threaten you and those around you once more."*

*— Are you going to repeat the same thing, every single time?*

How foolish, she thought, closing her eyes.

She could see where this path led.

Risk would only accumulate, and the eternity Krische wished for would never, ever come.

The karmic cycle would someday threaten Krische's peace.

It had to be severed somewhere.

And so — but still.

"……Is it wrong for Krische to wish for eternity?"

Bery had promised forever.

And yet she had seemed to accept the death that would one day come.

Gallen had been satisfied with his death, and Elrouga the same.

This is enough, they said. They said it with smiles that matched the words.

*"— Yet man is not an individual, but a being who weaves as a collective, building countless bonds to live. As our ancestors before us, I wish to entrust myself to that current. ……I find beauty not in the eternal great blossom, but in the form of the hundred common flowers that wither and scatter their seeds, and I wish to live my life to its fullest."*

All Krische wanted was eternal happiness.

She did not even think it was strange.

Simply put, when Krische thought things through normally, the conclusion inevitably arrived there.

But for most people, she supposed, it was not a normal thing to wish for.

"Selene, Krische selfishly believes that Selene loves Krische. And Krische loves Selene. ……So Krische just thinks it would be nice if that kind of relationship could go on forever — is that wrong?"

*— Should people die?*

At those appended words, Selene's body went rigid.

She clutched the pillow tighter, pressing her face into it.

No words came to her that could deny her beloved sister's question.

When her mother had died, how bitterly she had mourned.

She had not forgotten how that felt.

If there had been a way to save her, she would have done anything.

Back then, Selene too had wished for eternity with her parents and Bery.

She had accepted it because there was no other choice.

People die someday, and the only difference is sooner or later — because she knew it was the natural order of the living.

Growing up was nothing more than a convenient fiction.

A fiction for accepting the world's unreasonable cruelties — and those who meekly swallowed those cruelties were, no doubt, the ones who came to be called adults.

Krische was pure as a child.

And surely she would remain that way forever, keeping a child's innocence intact.

She was the one being in this world capable of making even eternal happiness a reality.

She had the power to bend any cruelty, to fulfill every wish of her own.

— She would not give up, would not accept.

She refused everything she did not wish for, and could go on refusing it.

She had no need for that convenient fiction called growing up.

She was not the one who accepted cruelty but the one who created it — a being whose soul would remain unsullied for all eternity.

"……If I said you should die, would you give up?"

What a terrible question, she thought.

It was not a question she had asked after deep thought.

"……If Krische's wish is selfish, and Selene says so, then surely Bery too would——"

"It always comes back to Bery."

Selene said it, mocking herself.

She could tell without looking that Krische was gazing at her with a puzzled expression.

"Why do you ask me? Don't worry about me — just do whatever you want. ……You don't need me. Even without me, you'll all carry on just fine."

"Selene……?"

"I'm deadweight. You know that, don't you? Why did you come to the battlefield? Because you were worried, weren't you? ……Because you couldn't trust me with it — that's why you left Bery behind and came to the battlefield."

She knew that was wrong.

Regardless, Krische had come because she was worried about Selene.

Leaving Bery behind to help Selene.

"……I'm ordinary. A nobody. I'm hopelessly tiny compared to you and Kreschenta, and I can't even give you a single moment of peace the way Bery can."

She clenched her fists until it hurt.

"Tell me. In this paradise of yours, what am I supposed to do? I'm stupid, I'm powerless — what can such deadweight possibly do? ……Should I just sit there like a decoration, watching all of you and smiling?"

And she let out a breath, as though laughing at herself.

"……Please, just stop it. I've had enough."

She said it and felt her own disappointment, and yet a small sense of relief, too.

The temptation to give up had always looked sweet.

It had always been right there beside her, something she had pretended not to see.

Something she had refused to acknowledge.

The hollow pretenses had been weighing on her more than she realized, and she laughed at the discovery.

In her disappointment with herself lay a dark pleasure and relief.

"Bery alone would be enough, wouldn't she? Unlike me, Bery would stay with you forever in this paradise you want. Once I'm gone you'll get used to it before long, and someday you'll stop thinking about me."

*— I would like…… to stay with you like this, always.*

That exchange, overheard on the way to the capital.

*— ……Krische wants to be with Bery always, too.*

She had probably understood even then.

That she was standing on the outside.

She had only pretended not to notice.

Their relationship was complete, so perfectly finished there was no room for anyone else to enter.

Each held what the other needed, fitting together like the halves of a tally.

Selene had simply been receiving the overflow, the surplus of love that spilled out from them.

"……You never needed me in the first place. With just Bery, you could have been perfectly happy. So——"

"Selene."

Suddenly her shoulder was pulled, the pillow taken.

Krische straddled her hips and pressed her lips against hers.

Selene felt the faintest surprise, then watched with detachment.

Artless affection — a child's innocence.

The usual kiss.

She simply watched the purple of the girl's eyes as their lips met, and eventually looked away.

Not because she was embarrassed, even now.

Those eyes had simply been a little too beautiful to look at.

"Krische always thought that Krische had no value unless Krische was excellent."

Krische said as she drew back.

Her purple eyes did not waver, and she turned their perfect circles toward Selene.

"……But Selene told Krische it was okay for Krische to cook with Bery forever, and that Selene would make sure Krische could."

It's the same thing, Krische said.

"If you're thinking purely about the food, Bery alone would be more than enough for the estate's cooking — Krische serves no purpose at all. It's a huge waste…… and yet Selene said that was fine."

Have you forgotten? Krische asked.

Selene did not answer.

There was no way she had forgotten.

"It's true that Selene isn't clever, and Selene might be pretty weak. Really clumsy and sloppy, and bad at everything…… but Krische doesn't think any of that matters. ……After all, the ones who taught Krische that are Bery and Selene."

Krische smiled and stroked Selene's cheek.

Her cool hand was strangely warm.

"……Krische always thought the ideal Krische was one who could do everything. But because Selene and Bery said it was okay for Krische to be a fool, because they accepted Krische and loved Krische…… Krische started thinking that maybe being a fool was okay after all……"

She traced her fingertip along her lips as though searching for words, and kissed her once more.

"At the very least, Krische feels the same way about Selene. It's true — honestly, Krische has never once thought Selene's abilities were really amazing, but…… that is a completely separate matter, isn't it?"

She seemed to look down on Selene.

And yet, lovingly.

"……Krische is pretty sure that Krische loves the incompetent Selene that Selene talks about. Short-tempered, stubborn, full of all sorts of strange hang-ups…… but kind, and always trying her hardest — Krische loves that Selene."

She did not adorn her words.

She was always sincere, head-on to the point of naivety.

The words sounded like insults, but there was not a shred of such intent.

"The way Selene loves the foolish Krische."

In those purple eyes shone an emotion beyond description.

The smile was like that of a girl her age, and color lived in those doll-like features.

She had changed, little by little, at some point, and looking back at the memory it was as though she were a different person.

Who had changed her — Selene was not so foolish as to think she was not among them.

But, simply.

# Chapter 250: The Unsullied Garden

Drawing inexhaustible mana from the vast energy known as 'the source,' cycling it into the atmosphere.

An increase in magical beast sightings, a rise in the proportion of mana wielders, and continuing bountiful harvests.

"So Krische came to hear the answer. How about it — Kalua and the others too?"

A girl's voice rang like a bell, overlapping with the distant cries of boys at sword practice.

— The sounds came from a training hall named the Black Flag Swordsmanship Academy.

Once the unit hailed as the strongest in the kingdom — the Black Flag Special Service Company.

This hall, where its members personally taught swordsmanship, had prospered well enough to become one of the capital's foremost academies; but the masters had gradually been replaced due to age, and now the majority were former students who had never seen a battlefield.

Listening to the voices from the hall, which seemed reborn into something new —

"Hehe, Bunny never changes."

The old woman sitting up in bed in the dojo master's quarters said it with a rueful smile.

Her beautiful black hair still bore traces of its former glory, though most of it had gone white.

Decades on, still beautiful — she gazed at the silver-haired girl as though looking at something too bright, then looked at her own palms and closed her eyes.

Aged though she was, her profile remained lovely.

"When Krische asked before, Kalua said to ask again when you were about to die."

Three elderly women were in the room.

One with black hair like hers, one with chestnut hair — both streaked with white, both having aged beautifully.

Among them, only the girl with the bell-clear voice remained untouched by time, standing there with a small creature's charm.

Wearing her apron dress, as always.

"That was a long time ago, but yeah, I said that."

"Selene told Krische not to force anyone, so it's not an absolute or anything…… but Krische thinks it would be wonderful to stay like this forever. Kalua would be with Mia and Elvena always, and it's so peaceful. There's nothing to worry about at all."

So please come along, Krische said, smiling.

A modest smile, unchanged from decades past.

Harboring no doubt, no trace of calculation — simply a smile that believed in happiness.

Surely it would remain unchanged forever.

As though she had seen the future, Kalua felt a strange sense of acceptance.

"Bunny."

Kalua beckoned with a wave, and Krische approached curiously, sat on the edge of the bed, and had her head stroked.

The silky hair held a youth long since lost to Kalua.

"I still don't really know how to explain it to you, Bunny."

"……?"

"Mia, you talk her through it."

"You know I'm bad at that sort of thing."

The old woman with the long chestnut hair glared as she spoke.

Aged though she was, the dynamic between them had not changed.

As ever, Mia was displeased, eyeing Kalua with exasperation.

Kalua laughed softly and gently hugged the girl.

"……It's not that I'm tired of living. There are still things I want to do, things I'd enjoy. ……But right now, I feel pretty satisfied."

Krische took hold of Kalua's arm as it wrapped around her.

Arms that had grown thinner year by year. Wrinkled hands.

"……Satisfied."

"Yeah. If there's a right time to stop in life, I kind of feel like now is it. ……If I went on with you from here, I'm sure it'd be fun in its own way…… but I don't think there'd be another chance to end my life feeling as content as I do now — that's what big sis thinks."

"……You're not 'big sis' anymore — you're very much an old lady by now."

"Mia, don't interrupt."

Kalua laughed and continued.

"I met you as Kalua and fought, found Elvena, was saved…… and then I opened this training hall, and I found more than a few successors I can trust it to. ……If I'm going to close the book on Kalua Belyus's story, I think letting nature take its course is the most beautiful way to end it."

She held the slight, small body tenderly.

"Of course, if there's still more I can do and you tell me you need me going forward, I'm not against it. I'd stick with you as long as it takes…… but I don't think that's why you're asking, is it?"

Krische nodded quietly.

It was not as though war had vanished entirely.

But the battlefield was now ruled by iron war-golems and magical weaponry, and the Black Flag Special Service had been disbanded long ago.

Krische no longer took the field herself; each of them lived out their days in peace, far from battle, and though she visited them, she had long since stopped asking them for anything.

Needed or not — at the very least, not needed.

*"If it is required of me, I shall give you my eternity as Krische-sama's blade. ……But I do not believe you seek the Vulture of a soldier any longer."*

She had approached only a select few, and the replies were always similar.

They all said much the same thing as Kalua.

Each time, she remembered Gallen's and Elrouga's smiles.

"……Kalua, do endings have to exist?"

"Beats me — I don't know. Difference in outlook, maybe."

A soft laugh, and Kalua spoke.

"I've been thinking of living as dying for a pretty long while now."

"……Dying?"

"Yeah. You live your life to the fullest so you can reach a better death, a better ending. ……Because an end will come someday, you enjoy everything along the way with all you've got, and that's how you arrive at the present. I've had more than enough fun for a life that was always going to end, so more of it doesn't particularly call to me, and I have no regrets, no unfinished business…… something like that."

Krische wrinkled her brow and muttered a pondering *muuu*.

Kalua giggled.

"Just a different angle, I guess. ……For you, Bunny, living in the present is the goal itself."

"……Are you calling Krische a fool again?"

"Not at all. Big sis has known since way back that you're incredibly smart, Bunny."

"……Krische still feels like she's being called a fool."

Krische puffed out her lips and inflated her cheeks.

Watching her, Kalua's refined, wrinkled face creased deeper with amusement.

Observing from the side, Mia spoke.

"She means it. It's not as though the idea doesn't appeal to me…… Your paradise sounds like it could be fun, Krische-sama. But if I can think 'this is a good ending,' then a good ending it is. ……I feel the same way as Kalua, I suppose."

She narrowed her eyes, looking toward the training hall.

The voices of children and instructors, the hard clack of wooden swords.

"Part of me wants to watch what comes next forever, and part of me feels I needn't bother. ……If I were told I could start over from scratch in a new life, the idea of lying down to sleep imagining that sounds rather nice."

She smiled, wrinkles deepening.

"You know how, when night comes, you naturally grow sleepy — it's like that. ……I think I want a fun day to last forever, but no matter how fun the day, eventually you just want to rest."

Mia too settled beside Kalua, leaning against her.

"……There's no greater happiness than falling asleep wondering what tomorrow might bring. What kind of life would I lead if I were born again — thinking about things like that…… I find it more than enough. I'm happy."

Krische watched them, thought for a while, and nodded.

"……If that's what you both say, Krische won't push any further. What would Elvena like to do?"

She turned to Elvena.

Elvena, who had been watching quietly, gazed at the two of them, gave a slightly wistful smile, and shook her head.

"As I have already told you, Krische-sama, I will go where you go. ……If it's possible, I would like to see Bery-sama once more……"

At Elvena's words, Kalua looked at her.

Meeting Kalua's gaze, Elvena gave a small smile.

Aged, they had come to look even more alike — and yet a difference in temperament, or perhaps in the things they had accumulated, lent each a distinct air.

"I suppose I am on Krische-sama's side, as it were. I would like to experience a gentle time where the sun never sets. There is so much to learn and to think about — no amount of time would ever be enough."

Kalua weighed her sister's words.

Then she nodded.

"If that's what you've decided, Elvena, I've got nothing more to say."

"Yes."

Elvena nodded too, and bowed her head.

Then Kalua turned to Krische.

"……If you've got time, I'd love to eat your cooking again, Bunny. It's been a while."

"Ehehe, okay. What would you like?"

"Pumpkin soup, maybe…… something easy to eat."

"……You're probably the only person in the world who can say that to Krische-sama so casually."

Mia said, sounding exasperated, and Kalua laughed.

There, only a gentle, unhurried time passed.

* * *

In the carriage on the way home after lunch.

Seated on Elvena's lap, Krische let out a soft, thoughtful *muuu*.

"Is Krische really just a weirdo after all?"

"Ufu, yes — Krische-sama may be quite the eccentric."

Elvena laughed happily and nodded, cradling the small body in both arms.

Her wrinkled face looked upon the girl with boundless tenderness.

"Unlike Krische-sama, everyone has lived expecting to die within a hundred years at most. The way Mia-san thinks is probably closer to normal."

"……Is that how it is?"

She puffed out her lips, slightly displeased, and Elvena giggled.

Looking up at her, Krische asked.

"Why is Elvena willing to come along?"

"……'Why,' you ask — that's a difficult question."

A pause, as though thinking it over.

Her eyes narrowed, gazing at something far away.

"Perhaps it is what I would call stubbornness."

"……Stubbornness?"

"Like Krische-sama, I wish to prove that eternal love exists."

How childish, she said with a wry smile.

To the child who had stayed a child.

"A vow of undying love is merely words. I want to prove that my love is true, that it matches the words I speak…… that may be why."

Elvena said it and reached toward Krische's chest.

She stroked the mana crystal hanging there.

"……What if, by some mistake, for hundreds of years, thousands of years — or even for all eternity — you could never meet Bery-sama again? Even then, Krische-sama could surely offer her an unchanged love?"

"……? Yes."

"Ufu, just so. But what Krische-sama can affirm without a second thought — for someone like me, it is not so easy."

That is why, Elvena said.

"Now that I've said it aloud, it really is just stubbornness. That said, well……"

She laughed, running her fingers through Krische's hair like a comb.

"……Tea never grows tiresome no matter how many times you drink it. Wanting to spend more quiet, ordinary days with Krische-sama and the others — that reason is, unexpectedly, quite a large one."

"……Ehehe."

Krische reached both hands behind her head and toyed with Elvena's hair.

The shoulder-length hair had a slight wave to it.

The white-streaked strands pleasantly tickled her fingertips.

"It's all very complicated, but at least Krische is relieved to hear that. Krische was worried that maybe Elvena still felt bad about not repaying Krische for saving her, or something like that."

She looked up at Elvena, leaning against her, and held up a finger with a serious expression.

"Elvena worked hard and paid off every last bit of the debt, even with the strict collection. That matter is finished, okay? You're not allowed to worry about it."

"……My. Ufu, it is one of the reasons, certainly."

Elvena smiled with quiet grace.

"To Krische-sama, the debt was settled long ago. But the freedom and happiness I gained through it are still right here."

She placed a hand over her own heart.

"Please don't give it another thought. It has nothing to do with debts…… The gratitude for kindness received — simply remembering it makes me happy."

"Mmm…… If Elvena says so, then fine."

Elvena laughed softly, then stroked Krische's hair aside and touched her forehead.

"More importantly…… what were you doing with the two of them?"

She changed the subject.

*"……That was delicious. I've got nothing left to worry about."*

*"Don't say things like that. ……Thank you for the meal, Krische-sama."*

*"Ehehe, Krische was worried when she heard you'd collapsed, but you look pretty well — that's a relief. ……Right, then."*

Just before leaving, Krische had pressed her fingertip against each of their chests.

A thin blue arc of light traced itself.

*"……?"*

*"In Krische's village, there was a prayer so that a close couple could be together again in the next life. ……Krische isn't sure it works, but it's something like that."*

*"Hehe, a charm. Hear that, Mia? Old ladies blushing isn't cute."*

*"Kalua, shut up."*

A small thing at the moment of parting.

That was what Elvena had been asking about.

"Kalua and Mia have been together forever and they seem so happy, so Krische thought it would be nice if they could be together in the next life too. ……That said, Krische doesn't really understand the mechanics of rebirth, so it's really just a little charm."

"……I see. Ufu, if we live long enough, perhaps we might meet those two reborn."

"Yes."

Elvena smiled ruefully, then lifted Krische up and held her tight against her chest.

"……Are you lonely?"

"A little. ……Krische still can't get used to it."

Krische answered quietly, pressing her face against Elvena.

Like a child who would never change, in a soft, restrained voice.

Kalua could no longer even control her mana properly.

For a mana wielder, that was the sign that the end was near.

She had seemed well, but she was only putting on a front.

That was the sort of person Kalua was, and Krische knew it well.

"I feel the same. ……But I think a smile makes for a better parting memory than a sad face. Nee-san was very pleased."

"……Yes."

Even if it was what they wished, eternity was a gentle and cruel word.

Because this pure girl would go on watching, one by one, as those around her fell away.

"And so, that is as it should be."

Not many wished for the paradise she desired.

A paradise was a cage, and a bond was a chain.

People change, and an endless eternity was terrifying precisely because of its beauty.

They had not wanted to cloud her, no doubt.

And so they had phrased their refusals in a way that would not wound her.

Those words of refusal were, in truth, an expression of love.

The vows they could have spoken would have been stronger than any wedding vow — but no one could believe in the kind of innocence she possessed, not within themselves.

And so, if only she herself could be a small help in clearing the clouds that gathered over this beautiful girl.

Now, and in the days to come — so that she might gaze forever upon the radiance the girl had brought to her world.

The mingled hesitation and emotion within Elvena.

One conclusion among them was something like that — something she held dear, something she cherished — and she quietly traced the silver in her arms with a fingertip.
