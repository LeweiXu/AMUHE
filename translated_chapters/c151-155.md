# Chapter 151: Light That Splits the Heavens

(T/N: Author's note: 1 ken ≈ 1.8 meters.)

*——An intruder in the forbidden domain.*

Before those words had even reached him, Vinsreel had already taken flight toward the forbidden domain.

Having lost the trail partway through, Vinsreel had concluded from the tracks thus far that the intruder had headed toward the river, and the traces he found there convinced him of Lila's presence.

A jade tiger split clean in two and strung up, and below it, fallen griffin feathers.

The feathers were not those of an adult — they were unmistakably Larnel's, the juvenile he had entrusted to Lila.

The intruder had, at the very least, spent time there with Lila.

And in all likelihood, the intruder's objective was the divine being.

There were no signs of torture, but had the intruder been a person of good will, they would have returned to the village by now. It was reasonable to assume the intruder had extracted information by some means and headed toward the divine being.

After ordering one of his men to relay a report, Vinsreel immediately set off toward the forbidden domain, and along the way——

"Fast as ever, Vinsreel!"

"Chieftain!"

He encountered his father — Chieftain Alkirens — astride a griffin and heading for the sacred domain.

Alkirens had some twenty-odd warriors with him, and Vinsreel could see several more griffins lifting off from the distant village.

The chieftain himself carried a spear.

He clearly intended to mobilize every warrior able to fight.

That alone was enough to grasp the severity of the situation, and Vinsreel fell into formation beside him.

"Lila is most likely with the intruder."

"……So that's how it is. I'm told a whistle sounded from the forbidden domain."

The worst possible situation, Alkirens declared.

The sacred domain's whistle was sounded only when a magical beast appeared or when an intruder was detected.

The opponent was the sort of lunatic who hunted magical beasts as game.

He knew Guardian Velvas's abilities, but he had to admit that the guardians alone would be at a disadvantage.

It was likely best to assume the entrance had already been breached.

"——This is no ordinary opponent. They have been hunting magical beasts like rabbits. The gate to the forbidden domain has surely been broken through."

"I have been on guard since the first report. Even if we are too late, we will do everything in our power. There is no other course of action. We can only pray we bring them down as quickly as possible and do not incur Yagelnaus-sama's wrath."

——Otherwise, it would not be strange for this entire mountain to vanish, literally.

Vinsreel nodded at the words that followed.

The question of what would happen to Lila had already passed beyond consideration.

At the entrance to the sacred domain, two men were being treated for their injuries.

One of them was Velvas.

Upon recognizing them, he pushed aside the warrior tending to him, mounted his griffin, and rose into the air.

His wounds did not appear severe.

He must have judged solo pursuit impossible and waited for their arrival.

Velvas was a composed warrior who did not confuse courage with recklessness.

"……My apologies, Chieftain. Full responsibility lies——"

"Save your excuses. The enemy?"

"A girl……with a horse and a jade tiger. Lila-sama is with her as well."

"A girl……leading a jade tiger?"

Velvas nodded.

"She appeared quite young. But I did not underestimate her on account of appearance. I fought with everything I had, and was defeated without even posing a proper challenge. ……Even were I to fight her ten times, the result would be the same."

His expression was deadly serious.

Alkirens and Vinsreel furrowed their brows deeply.

The words of Velvas, who held the title of Guardian, carried the greatest weight of all.

"That I remain alive is solely because my opponent showed me mercy. ……I am well aware of the shame of showing my face before you after such a disgraceful defeat……but please, allow me to accompany you."

"Granted. Whatever the case, we need every fighter we can get right now. All judgment on these matters will wait until everything is over — show me the pride of a warrior."

"Sir."

Vinsreel thought for a moment, then asked.

"What is the enemy's intent, Velvas? I am glad you are alive, but it is exceedingly puzzling."

"……I cannot say. Lila-sama, who was accompanying that girl, said it was an audience. That if we did not interfere, the girl bore no ill will toward Kreisharana."

"That amounts to the same thing. What is Lila thinking?"

Vinsreel closed his eyes for an instant.

Priestess though she may be, this was an unforgivable mortal sin.

Lila should have known that.

Lila was a kind girl.

Perhaps she had been swayed by sentiment — yet given the gravity of the crime, it was hard to believe this was a rational decision.

If the divine being's wrath was incurred, everything would be lost.

"……Lila-sama is not the sort to do such a thing out of self-preservation. Given her words and demeanor, it is possible she was coerced, with the Kreisharana people used as leverage. ……The opponent possesses the strength to threaten an entire village."

"……Foolish."

No matter how many Kreisharana died, even if they were destroyed for it.

Compared to incurring the wrath of the divine being, that would still be the lesser outcome.

Vinsreel shook his head, and Alkirens spoke.

"What's done is done, Vinsreel. Focus only on what must be done now."

"……Sir. My apologies."

On their way to the divine being's basin, they rendezvoused with Velvas's group and several other warriors.

None had deep wounds; all had survived.

What was astonishing was not so much that the enemy had spared their lives, but that the enemy had had the room to hold back.

In battle, keeping someone alive is far more difficult than killing them.

Against the trained warriors of Kreisharana — and against griffin cavalry that ruled the sky, no less — to strip them of their ability to resist without taking their lives was something possible only with an overwhelming gap in ability.

He beat his griffin's wings onward, wondering just what kind of monster this was.

And then, pressing forward, they reached their destination.

Before them loomed the divine being's hill, and what met their eyes was air swirling in a vortex.

——It was already too late.

The monster was most likely already before the divine being.

"Vinsreel!"

"Sir! Everyone, battle formation! The fight has already begun! The enemy is no ordinary opponent — do not close in. Given the circumstances, we strike with a simultaneous lance volley and end this at once!"

He gripped his spear tight and pulled the reins.

His griffin — Littgund — craned his neck upward and beat his wings powerfully, soaring up and over the hill's slope in one burst.

Beyond the hill lay the basin — and rising up with its back to the magic-crystal cliff face was the divine being, its form like a fortress.

A single sweep of that tail could obliterate a village.

Before those claws, even a towering castle would be sheared away and collapse with a single stroke.

At the end of the long, serpentine neck was a jaw massive enough to swallow a house whole — and those freezing red-purple eyes.

Vinsreel had visited the divine being's presence seven times before, but never once had he seen it reared up like this.

The overwhelming mana and pressure — everything about the air in this place was different.

Even with two ri of distance between them, Vinsreel felt fear and trembled. (T/N: "Two ri" here uses the tactical-arc scale, ≈800 meters.)

Even the valiant Littgund was cowed by the pressure the divine being emitted, wanting nothing more than to flee this place.

——A dragon was, to humans, a being that could only be called a god.

Comparing the two was akin to comparing a human and an insect — so vast was the gulf between dragon and man.

On a whim, it could annihilate a city, a nation itself.

Dragon and human were simply different orders of existence entirely.

To point a blade at such a being was the height of folly.

The gale those wings stirred up was a localized storm.

Whether caught in it — he spotted Lila, tumbling toward them, and Larnel chasing after her. In the corner of the basin, he noted the jade tiger, cowering in terror before the divine being.

And then, alone amid all of it in that basin——

"What is she……?"

——The one who stood against a god was an insect with silver hair.

Her black cloak billowing.

Clutching a spear that was no more than a twig before a dragon, defying the storm.

From an extreme forward lean, she reversed in an instant, arching her upper body back and setting her spear — a fleeting motion that was almost beautiful.

But what was unleashed from that motion was a torrent of force no longer recognizable as a spear throw.

With a thunderous roar, the hurled spear pierced through the sandstorm before her.

It pried open the wall of wind before her, reducing the distance of one ri to nothing — and piercing the scales and flesh at the base of the dragon's wing, the spear did not stop. (T/N: "One ri" here uses the tactical-arc scale, ≈400 meters.)

With a violent shattering sound like breaking glass.

The cliff face behind the dragon crumbled away in a cascade of blue radiance.

Mingling with the dragon's blood, what emerged was purple.

——What this insect-like girl unleashed was a strike capable of slaying even a god.

Vinsreel, Velvas, Alkirens.

Everyone present forgot to breathe and think for an instant, staring at the girl.

They had expected no ordinary opponent.

They had braced themselves.

But what they saw surpassed their imagination by far.

The girl righted herself as if tumbling, and seized another spear in her hand.

Only then did Vinsreel, who had been frozen still, manage to come back to himself.

"Kh……don't let her throw another spear! Target her from behind!"

And, shouting, he worked the reins and forced the terrified Littgund into flight.

——The claim that the scales were like ore was no lie.

She had intended to tear the wing clean off in a single strike, but the scales were harder than she had imagined.

Sturdier than mere rock — but without the toughness of metal.

If so, thrown spears could inflict sufficient damage.

It was not as though the dragon could not be killed.

Rather than fighting the wind, she seized a spear and leaped backward.

Even for Krische, pushing forward through all this gale was impossible.

It was a nuisance. As expected, dealing with those wings first was the better approach.

There was also the danger of it fleeing into the sky.

She had surmised from watching the griffins fly, but whether consciously or not, the dragon manipulated mana and controlled the wind without relying on magic crystals.

With that much mana, lifting that enormous body would be effortless.

She needed to maneuver so as not to give it an opening. If it escaped to the far sky at this juncture, even Krische would have no chance of winning.

Forward again.

Noticing, the dragon moved its arm to shield the wounded base of its wing.

It clearly understood her aim.

She updated her assessment — reaction time faster than expected given its bulk — but threw the spear regardless.

That massive body — she had known from the start that a single throw could not finish it.

The spear pierced the dragon's scales, gouged flesh, and embedded itself in its arm.

It did not penetrate through. The flesh itself must also be formidable.

"Kh……"

Rolling, righting herself, Krische immediately leaped backward.

The dragon had its back turned — and what swung was that enormous tail.

Simple and straightforward; the motion was readable.

But its tip moved faster than sound, cracking the air as it hurtled toward her.

Before that force, a human body would be scraped away along with the very earth.

Her leap put roughly three ken between her and the tail's tip. (T/N: ≈5.4 meters.)

Yet the shattered earth became projectiles flying toward Krische.

Any single stone, mishandled, would be fatal. Each carried enough force to burst a human body even through armor.

From the countless bullets she selected only those that would hit her, kicked them away, and rolled to right herself.

The dragon was a body of absurd proportions, yet faster and sharper than she had imagined.

Its speed was unbelievable given its apparent mass.

"Take aim!"

From behind Krische rang out words in the Kreisharana tongue.

They had caught up, it seemed.

What was hurled toward her were spears — the sound of wind they carved, twelve in number.

But the gale the dragon had stirred still lingered.

The spears' trajectories warped and skewed; only two would strike her.

——As though she had eyes in the back of her head.

Without turning, Krische caught those two alone, and tossed one forward.

What she needed to do was simple — there was only one thing.

Grab a spear and throw it. Repeat.

Even if each wound was no more than a pinprick to the dragon, eventually those wounds would accumulate until they could no longer be ignored.

From a forward-leaning acceleration, she slammed her heel into the earth ahead and braked hard.

She seized the rampaging force and channeled every bit of it into the spear in her hand.

A throw at her absolute full power — the extended muscles and joints ached, but she paid it no mind.

So long as nothing tore free, pain was the same as nothing.

The hurled spear embedded itself in the base of the long neck.

From under the jaw to the underside of the tail, scales seemed to be absent in that stretch alone.

It was thick, but as she had guessed, the strength of the outer hide was at best that of tanned leather.

The spear split flesh, and blood laden with immense mana sprayed out.

She had hoped it might punch clean through, but fortune did not favor her that far.

She pressed forward, reaching for the second spear, and——

"——I won't allow it!!"

Descending from above was a single shadow.

Blocking her path with a shout was a young man astride an enormous griffin.

The living hero of Kreisharana — Warrior Leader Vinsreel.

The spear strike he swung was the same that had felled countless jade tigers.

But the girl evaded with ease, leaping backward to open the distance.

"I cannot permit any further outrage. ……I will have your life!"

A surprise strike from the blind spot above. Yet even that was insufficient.

Reaction speed, physical ability, breadth of vision.

In every regard, Vinsreel understood anew that she existed on a plane far beyond his reach.

Silver hair that shone like the moon.

The beautiful girl's eyes seemed to look at nothing, utterly detached from reality.

Yet they captured everything — purple.

——The eyes of an absolute being, akin to the dragon's own.

The mere direction of her gaze froze his spine.

He understood instinctively — this was an opponent he could never defeat.

Madness-steeped arrogance, fearing not even to point her blade at the dragon — at the heavens themselves.

Human yet standing outside human reason — that was the kind of being she was.

The wind died, and for an instant, silence.

At Chieftain Alkirens's command, countless spears rained down upon her with the sound of splitting wind.

Through that storm of spears — any one of which could easily reduce a jade tiger to scraps of flesh — the girl danced.

Just as before — without even turning.

By sound alone, she perceived everything around her.

She twisted her body, slid across the earth in an arc.

As if performing a dance, she effortlessly caught one of the spears in her hand.

Without so much as grazing her cloak — a transcendent, immortal's dance beyond comprehension.

There was a beauty in it that made one forget this was battle, that threatened to enthrall.

"Littgund!!"

Littgund dropped low, beat his wings once.

He lunged as if hurling his entire body, attacking the girl.

Filling her field of vision, and as he did, Vinsreel leaped down and circled around from the side.

A single instant of opening, of borrowed time.

He did not think of winning. Stealing her time mattered more than landing a blow.

If he could wound her, if he could rob her of the time to throw spears, eventually even this girl would reach her limit.

Using Littgund as a wall, he slipped past the flank — and there stood a forest of spears embedded in the earth.

Within it, he spotted the girl leaping backward and lunged.

What he thrust was a strike powerful enough to pierce stone, every ounce of his strength behind it.

"Kh……!"

But the girl no longer even looked at him.

She twisted at the waist, dodging Vinsreel's spear behind her back, catching the shaft under her left arm.

And without killing that momentum, she spun, pulling Vinsreel toward her——

"Ugh, gkh!?"

——The impact was instantaneous.

The girl's right heel drove into the accelerating Vinsreel's side.

A beat later, searing pain shot through him and thought ceased.

Vinsreel's body was lifted by the impact, slammed into Littgund.

The agony severed his thoughts, color draining from the edges of his vision.

Helplessly, he watched the girl break into a run, and could not hold back — Vinsreel retched.

"That such a warrior could exist in this world……"

The thunderous roar that continued was the sound of spears shattering the scales and piercing the flesh of the divine being — Yagelnaus.

Unfazed by countless thrown spears, effortlessly dodging Vinsreel's all-or-nothing strike.

And the spear she hurled as a matter of course possessed force beyond all reason, piercing even the divine being.

No one was there to reproach Alkirens for his murmur, which sounded almost like a confession of despair.

Even witnessing it firsthand, it did not seem real.

It was precisely because they were warriors of Kreisharana, who had devoted their lives to training, that they understood the limits of the human body.

The girl's physical capabilities did not, in truth, exceed their own.

And yet she toyed with dozens of warriors and bared her fangs at the divine being itself.

She was an aberration that no common word like "monster" could describe.

Against an opponent of this caliber, thrown spears were meaningless.

If anything, they served her purposes more than theirs.

"Kh — all further lance throws are forbidden! We offer our flesh and blood as a shield for Yagelnaus-sama——"

He began to speak but stopped — not of his own will.

The air had changed in an instant to something alien.

——Yagelnaus beat its wings once, mightily.

A renewed gale erupted, and the girl, who had been closing in, was lifted into the air.

What differed from before was that the dragon tilted its jaw upward, opening it wide.

And that a radiance like the sun had appeared before those jaws.

"——Everyone!! Clear the front and evacuate skyward!!"

He knew it only from legend.

He could never have witnessed it himself.

Yet what was before him — he understood instantly what it was.

It splits mountains apart and reshapes the land.

A roar of destruction that could bring down a nation.

——It was what was called the dragon's roar.

That violence was directed, without a shadow of doubt, at the lone girl still floating in the air.

* * *

At the training grounds of the royal capital Albernaria, Selene and Elrouga could be found.

"Please don't overdo it. If you collapse too, imagine what Krische-sama would think when she returns."

"Working myself just tired enough is better for my nerves, Faren. When I have free time, I can't stop thinking the worst."

Selene let out a sigh as she spoke.

Outside the city walls — what was being built adjacent to this training ground was the second campus of the military academy.

A crown-owned building in the first-class district would serve as the main campus, but that could only accommodate classroom instruction.

Practical exercises were better conducted with actual soldiers, so a simple building would be erected beside the training grounds to serve as a venue for hands-on training.

Tabletop war games.

Tactical education as knowledge.

All of that only took shape with practical operational experience.

In the war against the Holy Empire, and in the civil war too.

Selene had felt her own powerlessness more painfully than she could bear, and she understood that better than anyone.

With proper training facilities, thorough instruction could at least reduce the chances of another fool like herself leading soldiers on the battlefield.

Among the tasks entrusted to her, Selene placed particular weight on the military academy.

"Kreschenta has been worrying about me too. Honestly, how pathetic."

Kreschenta kept finding reasons to spend time with Selene.

With neither Krische nor Bery around as things were.

The desire for someone to be affectionate with was surely one reason, but there was also an intent to keep an eye on Selene's condition.

Normally, she rarely showed her face before Selene without business, yet lately she had been turning up under one pretext or another.

"Heh heh, Her Majesty the Queen may have a rather different air from Krische-sama, but they are quite alike in their kindness."

Selene gave Elrouga's ghastly visage a look of exasperation, then smiled wryly.

Kreschenta paid no particular mind to other people's appearances. Naturally, the same applied to Elrouga's face.

*——Could it be that this old man simply placed everyone who didn't flinch at his face into the "kind" category?*

Such a thought occurred to Selene, though saying it would be too rude.

As the Marshal's advisor, Elrouga had ample occasion to interact with Kreschenta.

He knew how Kreschenta had dealt with the noble massacre Krische had carried out.

He surely did not labor under the delusion that she was merely the gentle, adorable young queen she appeared to be.

"……For the time being, I have spoken with Corps Commander Agrand on my end and sent a letter to General Velraigh as well. Those two should pose no problems."

Elrouga said abruptly.

"General Garhka is, well, a pure battle-lover — an innocent child of sorts. He hardly seems the devoutly reverent type. I believe we can rest easy on that front as well."

"And General Keesliton?"

"I spoke with him briefly before he headed west, and he seemed quite fond of Krische-sama. He said 'so long as she doesn't go too far astray'……but the recent noble massacre concerns me somewhat. I hope it hasn't changed his mind."

The massacre of great nobles — though not openly discussed, no one among the central nobility was unaware of it.

The culprit had killed seventeen people without leaving a single trace of evidence.

The crime was too abnormal — impossible to prosecute through law.

Yet precisely because of its abnormality, rumors spread.

And the name that came up was, naturally, Krische — and the Black Flag Special Service under her command.

Of course, Krische's true capabilities were known only to those within the military.

For the common people, the circulating rumors were merely rumors.

Given how utterly abnormal the incident was, the prevailing view at present was that only a few had actually been killed, and the claim that seventeen people had been torn limb from limb in a single night was an embellishment.

Kreschenta had Daglis spread a counter-narrative:

*'In the wake of an assassination attempt on the queen, malicious nobles fought among themselves in the shadows, and several died.'*

She was working to deceive the public eye. That version of events was far more plausible than the truth of seventeen people dismembered in a single night.

Kreschenta's operation was, for now, succeeding.

However, if Krische were to kill a dragon and return, it would undeniably have further negative effects on the current situation.

Because anyone capable of slaying a dragon would be capable of anything.

If the populace came to fear Krische and public order deteriorated, it would inevitably ripple outward.

The possibility of people like Felwors making a move was very real.

"What we need to watch is the north and the west……and internally as well. No matter what happens, we're in a difficult spot."

This was not a power struggle between royals like the previous civil war — between Princess Kreschenta and Prince Gildanstein.

Whatever the reason, marching an army on the capital under current circumstances would be indefensible treason.

Raising the flag of rebellion would not easily rally soldiers, but discord at a time when neighboring nations were collectively preparing to crush the kingdom was quite possibly fatal.

"If I dare imagine the worst, I cannot see a path to victory. I intend to do everything within my means, but should it come to that, I'm ashamed to say I too would be largely relying on Krische-sama."

"……Half of this situation is that girl's fault. You have no reason to feel responsible."

Selene said with exasperation.

Whether dragon-slaying would become infamy or glory.

That remained unknown until the outcome was decided.

If one thought optimistically, if one thought pessimistically — possibilities were endless.

——Regardless, at the very least, she prayed only that Krische would come home safely.

War and daily life alike.

Everything in Selene's future began from there, after all.

"Mm……what's that?"

"What is it?"

Elrouga turned his eyes skyward and squinted.

Selene tilted her head and followed his gaze.

A blue sky with sparse clouds.

Roughly north-northwest from here.

Something like a luminous line was faintly rising from far beyond the horizon, reaching toward the heavens.

As she wondered what it could be, it suddenly moved, and——

"Huh……?"

——It sliced through the clouds above the capital in an instant.

From far beyond the horizon, crossing even the mountain range.

A light passing over the capital — Selene realized, a beat late, that it was a torrent of force produced by an unimaginably vast quantity of mana.

"……This is……"

She knew the legends of dragons.

So she tried to comprehend it, but her heart refused understanding.

Even those who had been training around them stopped and stared at it in a daze.

Selene did the same — and then swayed, and Elrouga caught her reflexively.

A vortex of mana slicing through the clouds from over a thousand ri away. (T/N: Here "thousand ri" uses the standard geographic scale, indicating an enormous distance.)

It was an abnormality one wanted to refuse to comprehend.

Yet the two of them alone in that place realized what manner of existence had caused this phenomenon.

And also, the existence that had most likely triggered it.

"……Krische."

Still held in Elrouga's arms, Selene called the name of the girl she loved, her voice trembling.

# Chapter 152: The Paradox of the Heavenly Sovereign

The light faded and the haze cleared.

What lingered around them was heat like a volcanic crater.

The hill they had crossed over no longer retained its original form; in its place, a river of searing heat now flowed, connected to the basin.

The earth where the girl had been running about moments ago was beyond recognition.

It had been transformed into an extreme environment that did not permit human survival.

And the abnormality was not confined to this place alone.

The mountain that should have been visible far behind them had been carved away, projecting a strange silhouette like a crescent moon.

None of them could believe that absurd sight, yet those present here were forced to comprehend it.

If one looked at the dragon standing at the end of the magma flow, if one felt the heat still scorching the air.

——If one recalled the light that had been unleashed from the dragon's jaws, what had happened was self-evident.

"……So this is the dragon's roar."

Alkirens murmured in a daze.

Before the warriors could pull the reins, the griffins had chosen evasion on instinct.

Thanks to that, it appeared no warriors had been caught in it — a small mercy.

Some had lost control of their griffins and fallen to the ground; some had been buffeted by the blast of heat the roar produced; some had been unable to rein in panicked griffins and turned tail — but the losses were light.

Even if some had been killed, it would have been one or two at most.

Vinsreel, too, was airborne alongside Lila, pressing a hand to his side.

A stroke of fortune amid the disaster, one might say.

But that did not mean Kreisharana had escaped danger.

The lives and deaths of those present no longer mattered.

If offering the lives of everyone here would suffice to earn forgiveness for this transgression, that would be a thing to celebrate above all.

——A single roar.

That alone had rewritten a landscape that must have existed since the distant past.

Reshaping terrain, capable of destroying nations — they could only be called natural disasters.

No matter how great the hero, no matter how vast the army — before a dragon, they were no better than insects.

Not an opponent humans could match, nor one they were permitted to raise a blade against.

——A dragon was an absolute being.

Countless legends repeatedly proclaimed them equal to gods.

One might describe a person as "like a storm."

It might be recorded that their strikes could shatter mountains, that they were such a monster.

Most of that was exaggeration — but where dragons were concerned, there was not a single word of it.

If one were bent on destruction, one could destroy the entire continent.

Turn a capital into a lake, flatten a mountain — if their power were wielded in full, the word "impossible" simply did not exist.

As effortlessly as a child crushing an anthill, as casually as kicking over a sandcastle.

For them, it would surely be a trifling thing.

Human endeavor continued only at their whim.

Everyone present understood this anew, and feared it.

Yet this statement required a caveat——

"Kh……stay on guard! She's still alive!!"

——with one single exception.

With Vinsreel's shout, what echoed through the mountain was a thunderous roar.

The sound of a spear, beyond all reason, piercing and gouging the absolute being.

"……Honestly, Gururun is quite a silly thing. It's dangerous, you know."

For any ordinary human, it would have been inescapable.

Lifted bodily into the air, and what was unleashed was a dragon's roar that could pierce a mountain.

Krische had considered manipulating wind through mana.

However, whether she could dodge with that was a gamble — at best fifty-fifty.

Generating wind sufficient to lift and freely maneuver a single body was extraordinarily difficult.

Fundamentally, Krische lacked enough mana, and whether she could produce the thrust needed for evasion was doubtful.

If she drew upon the vast mana surrounding her, she could say with certainty it was possible — but seizing control of it would take time.

Unlike freely drifting mana, the mana here originated from the dragon.

Converting dragon-colored mana into something neutral and making it her own involved too great a time lag, and the dragon, which had already accumulated sufficient mana, would have unleashed its roar a fraction sooner.

Mana was like paint.

Each individual's color was different, and each could paint with only their own single color.

If it were colorless and transparent mana like that in the ambient air, one could simply dye it in one's own color and be done with it — but to use paint of another color, one first needed to go through the step of rendering it colorless and transparent.

When she was focused solely on it, as when she had drained mana from Kreschenta, it was still manageable — but while operating her simulated muscles and manipulating wind with mana, even Krische found it difficult.

Mana released from the body naturally tended to revert to a colorless state.

The greatest difficulty in wielding magic drawn in the air — her unique technique — was how to maintain one's mana as one's own while operating it.

Creating a canvas of one's own mana to substitute for a magic crystal, then inscribing the desired formula upon it.

The technique called magic that Krische had created required such a process, and therefore even something trivial consumed an enormous amount of mana.

Griffins, possessing mana far exceeding that of humans, devoted it to a single purpose — flight — manipulating wind to fly.

The dragon likely worked the same way.

With that much mana, lifting that massive body would be effortless.

Krische's precision of mana manipulation far surpassed theirs.

Given the same amount of mana as they had, she could undoubtedly fly more efficiently even without wings.

The problem was simply that Krische lacked the mana for such brute force.

She had been considering whether the principles behind griffin flight might inspire a new approach, but griffins did not use mana for physical augmentation the way jade tigers did.

Then, seeing Krische hop down from its back, she exhaled in relief.

"Thank goodness……you're safe. Your injuries, though……"

She noted the torn dress and bandages peeking from beneath the fur cloak, and her brow creased with worry.

Seeing that, Krische stood on tiptoe and gently patted Elvena's head.

"Krische is fine. Krische is sorry for worrying you too, Elvena. ……But this solves everything, so please don't worry."

"Y-yes, no……I-I don't have any reason to be apologized to, at all……"

Even as she spoke, tears welled, and she hastily dabbed them away with her fingers.

Krische watched her intently, then smiled.

"Thank you for taking care of the house."

"……Yes."

Elvena smiled too, and nodded.

"But, the rest will have to wait a bit……Krische needs to go to Bery. Could you watch this one for a moment?"

"Th-this one……?"

"Gururun. He saved Krische in a tight spot……Krische's debt-cat, you might say." (T/N: Krische uses 恩猫 — a play on 恩人 "benefactor," swapping "person" for "cat." Rendered as "debt-cat" to preserve the wordplay.)

"I……I see."

On all fours, the jade tiger still towered well above Elvena.

The muscle from its shoulder to its foreleg was probably as wide as Elvena's waist.

Unmistakably a fearsome predator at a glance. What she wanted was not that sort of explanation.

But faced with the reality of being abandoned before this predator, she could only nod, fear and all.

No matter how she explained it, her sense of terror would never get through to this girl called Krische — and Krische already wore the expression of someone who considered the explanation finished.

"Gururun, you're not allowed to eat Elvena, okay? Stay put."

The beast — this "Gururun" — responded with a low rumble, as if it understood or didn't.

Krische wagged a finger at the creature and issued her command, which only heightened Elvena's terror — but of course, Krische did not notice.

Leaving Elvena abandoned before the jade tiger, she leaped straight to the second floor.

Her destination was the balcony of her own room, where Bery slept.

Using mana to unlock the latch from outside, she found Anne inside, standing before the hallway-side door, holding a frying pan at the ready.

Anne hadn't noticed Krische's entrance at all, both hands gripping the frying pan tightly.

"……What are you doing?"

"Eek!?"

Anne's shoulders jumped. She spun around, saw Krische, and sank to the floor right there.

*Krische-sama*, she whimpered pathetically, and answered in a daze.

"I-I heard a dragon had come, and j-just in case, I thought I should protect Argan-sama……"

What she planned to do about a dragon with that frying pan.

What standing guard at the hallway was supposed to accomplish.

And why a frying pan in the first place.

"……Go put the frying pan back. That's Bery's favorite……honestly. If you handle it roughly, Krische will be upset."

There was much she could have said, but she condensed it to a single remark and approached the bed.

Red hair, a refined face.

Bery, frozen in time, was exactly as she had been when Krische left.

She had never imagined anything would change in this state, yet seeing her brought a sigh of relief.

She extended her palm and traced the geometric pattern.

Compared to creating it, the mana needed to dispel it was small.

The invisible wall separating worlds vanished, and Bery's slightly ragged breathing filled the room.

She immediately pulled down the sheets and lifted the woman — in her negligée — into her arms.

Bery's brow was faintly creased, as if in pain, and her body burned with terrible fever.

Krische furrowed her brow and pressed her cheek to Bery's head.

"Anne, the door."

"Y-yes!"

Anne scrambled to open the door and dashed to the entrance, Krische following behind.

She moved through the hallway and down the stairs, taking care not to jostle Bery.

Stepping outside, she told them both:

"Well then, Krische will be off. ……Anne, put the frying pan away."

Without waiting for a reply, Krische ran.

The jade tiger chased after her in puzzlement, overtaking her, glancing back several times at its oddly slow master——

"Gururun is too rough, so no."

——told off by Krische, the tiger let out a low rumble and matched her pace.

A dragon in the capital.

And a jade tiger strolling through the streets.

The commotion was just as bad as before — a nuisance.

She could not leap across rooftops while carrying Bery in this condition.

She considered what to do, but the chaos eased as soon as she left the first-class district.

"Krische-sama!"

There stood men in black-lacquered armor.

The Black Flag Special Service, lining the central avenue and calming the panicking citizens to clear a path.

Running alongside her at the front was Dagra.

"As expected of Vulture, always sharp."

A little ahead, she could see Mia, Aleha, Tagel, Korintz, and others directing crowd control.

Krische was genuinely impressed as she ran along the cleared path.

"My apologies, but this was undertaken as a special operation — I would ask for your retroactive approval."

"……Very well, Krische will allow it. But aside from this time, don't make a habit of it. You'll get in trouble."

"Yes. Of course. It would set a poor example for the men."

Dagra saluted on the run — improper form, but the situation warranted it.

Mobilizing a unit for non-training purposes without authorization was not permitted.

Given the circumstances, the excuse of suppressing civilian unrest could be invoked, and retroactive approval would smooth things over — but it was still not ideal conduct.

It was a rare action for the by-the-book Dagra — but right now, he saw her not as a military officer, but as the master he would serve for life.

He knew that her servant Bery had been stabbed.

He surmised that her disappearance was connected, and having heard the dragon's words, the broad picture came together.

If so, supporting her was his duty.

That Dagra had independently ordered the unit to follow her was for that reason.

"I must say, though — disappearing without warning and returning on a dragon's back. I am astonished, though I daresay I have grown accustomed to such surprises."

Dagra glanced at the girl-like woman in her arms, then at the jade tiger, and frowned.

That was the primary cause of the surrounding commotion.

Attending to the things Krische did not notice was his role.

"Take the alley to the left. I'll lead the way — we've already cleared the route."

"Nn, Krische leaves it to you."

Guided by Dagra, she linked up with Kalua, who had cleared the area before the gates.

"Heh, you always keep us on our toes, bunny."

Offered the usual quip, Krische emerged through the crowd and pressed on.

——And so, once more, before the dragon.

Dagra and Kalua halted a short distance ahead.

Krische stepped past Selene and Kreschenta and held Bery up for Rigaleiwe to see.

"Rigaleiwe-san, this is the person Krische wants you to help."

Rigaleiwe drew its face close and studied Bery for a while, then slowly extended a single finger of its left hand.

Everyone held their breath, fearing she might be crushed — yet the dragon's movement was unexpectedly gentle.

*'Open her mouth.'*

"Err……yes."

Krische set Bery down gently, supporting her neck.

Then, pressing lightly on her chin, she parted those faintly pale lips.

The dragon used its right hand to dig a claw into its own left arm.

Mana glinted blue; the red blood turned purple.

It seeped and trickled, running down the finger and along the steel-like claw to its tip.

A single drop fell toward the sleeping princess's lips.

Strangely, the blood seemed to move of its own accord, drawn in as if by will, vanishing past her lips.

Two drops, three — and the dragon withdrew its arm.

The blood had already stopped, and the remaining drops became a blue mist that dissolved into nothing.

When Krische looked up at the dragon curiously, what echoed was the voice of mana.

*'I told you — it is too much for your kind.'*

The dragon indicated itself with a claw.

*'Those small ones who once gorged themselves on our remains out of greed — many died, unable to endure the blood. Any more would be excessive.'*

"……Like medicine that's too strong?"

Krische pondered, and the dragon narrowed its eyes.

*'……Even with generations of interbreeding that have acclimated the body to the blood, the fundamental physiology is different. If you wish to experiment on that one's body, I could give more.'*

"……That's enough. You're being mean."

Mana rippled in amusement.

Krische pouted — and then the body in her arms moved.

"Ah — Bery?"

Bery clutched her chest in pain, and a blue glow of mana rose around her body, enveloping her.

Krische immediately hugged her tight and stabilized the overflowing mana.

The spasm-like movements gradually subsided. Krische stroked Bery's back and pressed her own cheek to Bery's.

No one spoke.

Before the dragon, they simply watched the two.

——After a while, what they heard was——

"Krische……sama……?"

For Krische, it was the most familiar voice in the world.

"Bery! Thank goodness, properly, you're……"

She pulled back slightly to look at her.

Those large eyes, somewhere dreamlike, opened just barely — but opened with certainty — and regarded Krische with wonder.

Gazing at nothing, drifting between sleep and waking.

Yet she smiled for certain, and reached her hands around Krische's back.

"……That even in the afterlife……I could see Krische-sama."

"Bery isn't dead! You're properly, properly alive."

Bery tilted her head in puzzlement, looking left and right.

Her hazy eyes still seemed caught in a dream, her mind not yet working.

Yet she had undeniably opened her eyes and spoken to Krische.

"Is that so……?"

"Yes, it is! Ehehe, huh……?"

She should have been happy, and yet her vision suddenly blurred.

Krische wiped at it in confusion.

She wiped the welling drops again and again, staring at them in puzzlement.

Bery cupped Krische's cheeks in both hands and, with her thumbs, carefully wiped them away in Krische's stead.

"But, either way……"

And just like that, she touched their noses together.

Slowly, their lips met.

Softly, gently.

The same feeling as every kiss they had ever shared.

They stayed that way for a while, and then Bery smiled happily, pulled away, and traced Krische's lips with her thumb.

"If I could see Krische-sama again."

She held Krische tight and let her long lashes sway as she closed her eyes.

"Then I am——"

And there, her words trailed off.

In their place, only calm, quiet breathing, and Krische smiled in relief and held her gently.

"……Ehehe, sleep well."

Cradling the one she loved, she stroked that red hair, pressed her cheek to it, and wiped her eyes.

The soldiers watching from the side must have understood what had just happened.

Those standing just behind Selene and Kreschenta were the same.

Selene and Kreschenta had been watching the touching scene — and then.

Selene openly, and Kreschenta with only a faint twitch of her cheek, stiffened their expressions. (T/N: The implication is that the public kiss between two women drew an awkward reaction, though the narrative does not dwell on it.)

*'Your wish is now fulfilled. If there is nothing more, I shall return.'*

"Ehehe, yes! Thank you so much, Rigaleiwe-san. ……Krische really wishes she could give you something in return."

*'There is no need. After centuries of tedium, this entertainment — the price for it is trifling.'*

"Nn……but……"

*'I have told you — you and I perceive things differently, on different scales. There is nothing for you to concern yourself with.'*

Krische tilted her head, troubled, thought about it, and nodded as if struck by an idea.

"Oh, then Krische could give Rigaleiwe-san a lov——mmph."

"……Stop that."

Selene's hand clamped over Krische's mouth, and she sighed.

Krische glared at Selene, slightly displeased.

"Mmm……"

"Don't 'mmm' me. He just told you not to worry about it, didn't he? That's called forcing your goodwill on someone. If someone gave you ten or twenty pumpkins when you said you didn't want them, you'd be troubled too, wouldn't you?"

Krische furrowed her brow at Selene's words and fell into thought. Selene sighed once more in exasperation.

She looked up at the dragon and spoke.

"……Thank you, Yagelnaus-sama. This debt of gratitude is one I shall never forget in my lifetime. May this bond with Krische endure."

*'So long as that one's feelings do not change, it will continue of its own accord. There is nothing to worry about.'*

Krische, seemingly persuaded by the pumpkin analogy, looked up at the dragon and smiled.

"Ehehe, Rigaleiwe-san aside, Krische is incredibly grateful. Thank you so very much."

*'I too shall rest my body. ……If the mood strikes you, come visit sometime.'*

"Yes."

With that, the dragon turned its back and began walking north.

The tremors continued and snow danced. When it took flight, a distant blizzard swirled in its wake.

Its wingbeats — powerful even through the torn wings — were somehow graceful, and the swirling snow scattered the sunlight, and the melting droplets painted a rainbow.

Krische watched the dragon depart, her cheek pressed against the sleeping Bery.

She simply watched the dragon's receding form, looking utterly content.
