# Chapter 216: Your Name

Krische had wanted to wear her apron dress to the investiture ceremony as well, but Selene and Kreschenta's joint objection forced her to concede on that front, at least.

She had grown exceptionally fond of the apron dress, it seemed.

Krische's displeasure mounted, but when Selene relented and agreed to let her wear it at the banquet, she showed a grudging acceptance.

Naturally, the dress-selection dispute resumed.

On the morning of the day itself, it was all but inevitable that Krische's breakfast would be skipped.

In the end, they reached the conclusion that a blindfolded Anne would choose the dress, and the one selected was black and red――a rare victory for Selene, yet there was no winner's joy in her heart.

Having won, she realized: the dress selection had only been a pretext.

At some point, Selene had come to want to defeat the woman named Bery head-on.

Those sophistic arguments, that needlessly formidable ability she always had to spare.

Wasn't it running away――pitting the chaos of Anne against the genius of Bery Argan, leaving everything to a coin-flip of fortune?

Hadn't she fled from the contest?

Wasn't this a victory born from fearing a head-on match?

Hard on herself and fiercely competitive to an extreme――Selene Christand was, through and through, a troublesome woman.

The victory ceremony was splendid.

The only one wearing a dark expression was a hungry Krische.

Kreschenta, caught in the crossfire, felt much the same inside, but when it came to keeping up appearances, she was unrivaled in all the land.

She would never let hunger-born irritation show on her face.

Among the various promotions and commendations, the Central General Elzaldo Gokkalus was appointed Northern General.

The Northern General, by convention, did not directly receive enemy assaults head-on.

However, when the west and east were attacked, providing swift reinforcements to both flanks and creating openings for victory had been the north's traditional role since antiquity.

It was a critical position demanding adaptability and a high-level strategic mind.

He was the most experienced in the Central Army and possessed the strategic acumen to match, making his appointment a sensible conclusion.

His restraint in not overextending for battlefield glory and his commendable handling of the horse archers also weighed heavily in his favor.

Teknrea Remin and Kokys Agrand were raised as alternative candidates, but Teknrea lacked experience and, moreover, was unmarried with no legitimate heir――meaning if she fell on the battlefield, House Remin itself could be endangered.

Such 'considerations' were a significant factor as well.

As for Kokys, while his tactical ability was exceptionally strong, he was somewhat lacking in strategic thinking.

And for personal reasons, he was a man who had sworn his life to Christand.

He had privately told Selene that he would prefer to remain as a corps commander in the Alberinea Direct Army.

Though he never said it aloud, Selene understood that he was concerned about Krische in his own way, and she agreed.

Thus the conclusion: Elzaldo Gokkalus as the Northern General.

The promotion of Aleha, formerly a general of the Holy Elsren Empire, to corps commander of the Alberinea Direct Army did cause some murmuring among those unfamiliar with the circumstances, but he had taken the heads of at least three corps-commander-level enemies while serving as the Black Flag Special Service's adjutant during the recent war.

Having personally slain his own brother served as his purification rite, and the fact that his posting fell under Alberinea's direct command meant the matter concluded without major incident.

And so the victory ceremony proceeded without particular issue.

――The problem was the banquet.

Having repelled the invasion of three great nations, with a casualty ratio of one to twenty.

Single-handedly dealing devastating blows to the elite forces of three nations, claiming five generals' heads.

The hero who saved the nation with unprecedented, unparalleled military achievements――Krische Alberinea Christand.

"Ehehe, it's been a little while, Velraigh General, Woof-woof and Baldy Four-Eyes too."

"Y-yes……"

The girl of fairy-like beauty had her long silver hair swept up and gathered, her slender body wrapped in a black-and-white apron dress, looking for all the world like a small servant.

She spent the evening clinging to the arm of the red-haired, baby-faced beauty beside her, who wore a dark indigo dress.

Why was she dressed as a servant?

Who was the person beside her?

For those who did not know her, it was an utterly mystifying sight.

Those who had attended previous banquets recognized the red-haired servant who was always with Krische, but the situation still defied comprehension.

As with the previous civil war, some approached to pay their respects to the woman who had earned the greatest military distinction, but they could not even bring themselves to ask: why the apron dress?

She was simply too nonchalant about wearing it.

"……Um, may I ask why you are wearing an apron dress?"

Thus, Nozan was the first to pose the question.

The beautiful young man in military dress wore his confusion plainly, as did his wife, his subordinates, and their wives.

"Heheh, Krische is Bery's attendant today!"

"K-Krische-sama……"

Looking troubled, her cheeks flushing, Bery bowed her head apologetically.

She was a woman who looked girlish――endlessly young in appearance.

Without the deep valley of cleavage glimpsed from the dark indigo dress, and without the mature bearing and timbre of a lady, one might have taken her for a girl of Krische's own age.

Seemingly uneasy in the unfamiliar dress, her bashful manner colored her expressions more richly than usual, and in appearance alone she could have passed for a young lady attending her first banquet at her parents' behest.

Her hair, normally kept at shoulder length, had been grown out and swept up, her refined beauty accented with light cosmetics.

With the air of a sheltered young noblewoman unused to banquets, what stood before them was, no matter how one looked, a beautiful girl who stirred every protective instinct.

Nozan, who had known her since she came to Christand, recalled the memory of her playing at being a servant nearly twenty years ago, and felt a distortion in the fabric of time.

Those who possessed mana aged slowly from their physical prime onward.

Nozan was no exception and understood this well.

But the sight of her――her aging having halted remarkably early――was bewitching in a way hard to put into words.

She was certainly past thirty, yet depending on how one looked, she appeared even younger than Selene.

Normally, her bearing as a servant――a step removed, deferential――lent her the air of an adult, but in her current appearance, even that was absent.

At first glance, the impression was simply that of a slightly young lady and her servant.

"I-I see…… Still, a dress truly does suit you well. As a matter of fact, both Bogan-sama and Lazura-sama very much wished to see you in a dress. Perhaps the two of them are rejoicing right now."

"Thank you…… But please, don't call me 'sama,' Margrave Velraigh. I am a servant, after all……"

"Haha, saying that while dressed like this rather undermines the argument."

Nozan had always addressed Bery in that way.

Lazura's younger sister――his lord's sister-in-law.

Nozan had always shown nothing but courtesy toward Bogan, and the same extended to all those connected to him.

For Bery, it was quite unsettling.

She had grown somewhat accustomed to it through Anne and Elvena, but being addressed so by Nozan, who was clearly her superior in standing since long ago, had never sat right with her.

"Um…… Does Bery know anyone besides Velraigh General and Woof-woof?"

"Ah…… who is 'Woof-woof'……?"

She knew about Vulture, Skeleton, and Nyan-nyan, at least, but Krische never talked about the battlefield once she was home at the estate.

When she did, it was usually with Selene.

She had known that someone was called Woof-woof, but she hadn't been sure who it referred to.

"It's me, Bery-sama."

Looking up at the giant who stepped forward with a resigned air at those words, Bery was perplexed.

She had met him several times, but the appellation had not connected in her mind.

"C-Count Varkus, you're……"

"Well, I received that pet name from Krische-sama during the civil war, you see……"

A fierce face with a scar on his cheek.

Woof-woof――the cute ring of a dog's bark was the polar opposite of this brawny warrior of solid muscle and bone.

The man scratched his cheek with a look of resignation, then looked down at Bery and nodded.

"Indeed, as the general says. A dress suits you splendidly. I'd love to recruit you over to my household, truth be told……"

"You can't, Woof-woof. Bery and Krische are going to be together fore――ah."

As if struck by an idea, Krische clapped her hands, tugged Bery's arm, and addressed her: "Bery-sama."

Nozan and Granmeld tilted their heads, and only Bery understood, smiling wryly.

It was a prompt to cut short the greetings――the same sort of cue Bery always gave Krische, playing the role of the attendant.

Bery usually did this for Krische, but she seemed fully immersed in her servant role now. Naturally, the execution was anything but smooth.

"It is time we moved along," she whispered, and Krische bowed her head to Granmeld.

The others seemed to finally understand as well, and laughter rippled through the group.

"Honestly, Krische-sama. This is only in appearance, you know――the star of the evening is you, Krische-sama. Everyone has come especially to speak with you."

"Is Krische not allowed to be your attendant?"

"Fufu, it makes me happy, but your feelings alone are more than enough. Just your thoughtfulness is making this thoroughly enjoyable for me."

She leaned in happily, stroking Krische's cheek.

As always, the distance between the two was remarkably close.

Among the wives in attendance, those well-versed in rumor knew about the talk surrounding Alberinea and her servant.

The rumor that Alberinea had no interest in men had spread fairly widely.

Seeing this scene, they could well believe it, and they exchanged glances.

Most assumed it was simply that she was a child unsuited to her abilities, but the intimate bond between this eccentric fairy-like princess and her servant――

There was a certain aesthetic quality to it, well-crafted enough that the spread of such rumors seemed only natural.

Though by now, the rumors were anything but baseless.

"Ehehe, and this one is Baldy Four-Eyes……"

"Baldy Four-Eyes……"

Bery's expression stiffened ever so slightly as she turned to look.

The bald, bespectacled, visibly fastidious thin man likewise stiffened his cheeks and bowed.

His wife beside him burst out laughing, ducking her face and shaking her shoulders.

"Saldan Rinea Galkalon. The other day, I was given that pet name……"

"I-I see…… Um."

*Shall I do something about this?* Bery asked with her eyes, and Saldan silently shook his head in resignation.

*I appreciate the concern*, his gaze said.

Bery had heard Selene grumbling about wanting to do something about 'that girl's nicknames,' and indeed, it was quite something.

At this point, they were simple insults.

Skeleton at least seemed to have pleased Elrouga in a way, but thinking that the number of victims would only continue to grow was rather disheartening.

The greetings continued from there.

She herself wondered why she, a mere servant, was receiving greetings, but a certain atmosphere had taken shape in the hall.

Krische――this adorable creature――was unable to read the room, yet excelled at creating these sorts of inexplicable atmospheres.

Before long, Elrouga and Kokys appeared as well, and Bery found herself, together with Krische, at the center of the circle without quite knowing how it had happened.

It was likely because Krische had a clear purpose: making Bery enjoy herself.

Normally she would have already retreated to the balcony to eat by this point, but she seemed quite enthusiastic today, occupying a position more befitting her actual rank than usual.

As Alberinea――second only to the Marshal――and as the Princess Royal, standing at the center of this gathering was entirely proper.

――Aside from the fact that she was dressed as a servant, in an apron dress.

"Hmm…… Seeing everyone gathered like this, Velraigh General does look like the odd one out."

*Come to think of it, Krische forgot to give one*, she said, looking up at the ceiling and tracing her lower lip with her index finger.

The mood in the room shifted in an instant, turning to something taut.

"Ooh, that's right. I was just thinking it seemed a shame that Velraigh's the only one without a pet name."

"Right, isn't it…… hmm……"

Kokys was thoroughly enjoying himself.

His grin all but screamed *get saddled with something terrible*, and Nozan regarded Kokys with a look of exasperation.

"Quite pleased with yourself, aren't you, Nyan-nyan."

"Why you……"

"I care not what name I am given. To receive a pet name from Krische-sama is an honor."

His handsome face wore a smile, and Nozan struck a pose of perfect composure.

Grrr――Kokys glared with visible frustration, but Nozan was unfazed.

Cool, collected, calm.

He affected an air of *no use worrying over the whims of a child*.

But Granmeld, who happened to know that this man was in fact extremely anxious about what his pet name would be, hunched over and shook with suppressed laughter.

Even Nozan could not quite ignore that, and he shot a sideways glare.

Sensing the strange atmosphere, Bery looked at Krische with a troubled expression.

――*To receive a pet name from Krische-sama is an honor.*

The words thoroughly satisfied Krische's heart (which was, in large part, that of a very small child), and she gazed at Nozan with emotion.

Starting with Vulture, Krische had not been especially confident in her nicknaming ability at first.

Given her lack of confidence in her own conversational skills, she had wondered whether she truly had the knack for such things――but every single person had unanimously praised the pet names she bestowed as an honor.

The joy of having one's efforts recognized exists in Krische no differently than in anyone else.

The delight of being praised in an area she was unsure about, where she felt she struggled, was immense, and its accumulation had grown into great confidence within her.

The only one who had ever been negative about it was Selene.

*Surely it was Selene who lacked the sense after all.*

Because it was Selene――whom she respected and loved――she would not openly ridicule or deny her, but she was now convinced that when it came to this, the 'silly' one was Selene.

"……Everyone, a moment of quiet."

Krische spoke a single line, and those around her exchanged glances and fell silent.

The girl in the apron dress interlaced the fingers of both hands and, with a gesture like prayer, gazed at Nozan, then closed her eyes and bowed her head.

The voices ceased abruptly, and even those unrelated to this peculiar circle hushed at the silence, peering from a distance toward Krische and Bery at the center.

The musicians, too, exchanged glances, and at the conductor's signal――having read the room――they stilled their hands.

From the stage, they could see the Princess Royal closing her eyes as if in prayer at the heart of the gathering.

This was surely something momentous, they concluded.

At the very least, music seemed ill-suited to this atmosphere――silence enveloped the great hall as the light of sunset streamed in.

Selene and Kreschenta, who had been conversing elsewhere, could not help but look around at the sudden hush, and when they spotted Krische at the center of it all, they furrowed their brows.

――*Krische is about to do something absurd again.*

Kreschenta was exasperated with her sister, and Selene murmured a quick "Excuse me" and quietly made her way over.

It was, if nothing else, a fantastical scene.

The girl in the apron dress, like a holy maiden offering prayer, stood before Nozan with her eyes gently closed.

As though she were about to solemnly deliver some divine revelation.

"……It is decided."

"……!"

Everyone held their breath in suspense.

The voice that rang through the hall was sweet amid the silence, clear as a rolling bell.

Even those who had no idea what the circle had been discussing exchanged wide-eyed glances, swallowing hard, waiting to hear what had been decided.

*Pata-pata, fuwa-fuwa, hane-hane, yon-yon, pippi――*

"――Let's go with Piyo-piyo."

"P-Piyo……"

"That's right, from now on it won't be Velraigh General――it'll be Piyo-piyo!"

A clear, radiant voice.

Krische beamed with a face full of smiles, her voice ringing through the hall.

"Ehehe, that's amazing, what a wonderful nickna――owww!?"

"What kind of nonsense are you doing, you silly girl."

Her stepmother Selene had pinched those loosened cheeks.

# Chapter 217: The Flower of the Banquet

"I half thought some kind of performance was about to start."

Kalua laughed merrily and popped a morsel of steak into her mouth.

She wore a borrowed crimson dress with ease, her hair swept up and her face composed with cosmetics, and there was something almost noblewoman-like about her――a lady of aristocratic bearing.

The hall falling suddenly silent, and then the girl in the apron dress at its center.

Surely Krische was about to do something――only for it to be a naming ceremony.

Kalua was thoroughly amused.

"U-um, listen……"

The one who had started all of this was Kalua herself.

Mia, wrapped in a white dress that hid her slight chest behind a red rose, rubbed the corners of her eyes and glared at Kalua.

"……Who do you think is responsible for this?"

"Mia?"

"Wrong!"

Those summoned from the Black Flag Special Service were Dagra and his adjutant Mia.

Aleha had been invited on account of his promotion to corps commander, and Kalua for her recognized valor in slaying the jade tiger.

As a rule, few people were invited to a victory banquet of this caliber.

Either one held the rank of corps commander or higher, or one had achieved some truly exceptional feat of arms.

It was a lavish affair that even battalion commanders were rarely called to. Mia was like a cat dragged somewhere unfamiliar, while Kalua was entirely her usual self.

"Well, never mind the details. This might be a once-in-a-lifetime thing, so we should enjoy it."

"Listen……"

"……Well, she's got a point. It's too late to fret."

Dagra responded to Kalua's words with a look of resignation.

Their chaperone for the two comely women――Dagra himself was also fidgeting in an unfamiliar black military dress uniform with silver embroidery, quietly bringing wine to his lips.

Back when he was a centurion, there had been no occasion to wear formal military attire, and it had been no different since his promotion to company commander.

Dagra, whose everyday wear was leather armor and a deep indigo shirt and trousers, felt distinctly out of place in this stiff, impeccable uniform.

"……You really don't suit it, Captain."

"Be silent. I'm well aware."

Since becoming company commander, he had purchased one formal uniform on the principle that he ought to have at least one――his wife had been delighted, saying he looked wonderful, but his child had delivered the blunt verdict that it didn't suit him at all.

And he himself had to agree.

On men like Nozan or Aleha, such formal attire looked perfectly natural, but military dress uniforms were, as a rule, things adorned with flowing, elegant embroidery.

No military dress uniform exists that flatters a fierce-looking man.

A shaved head and a stern face――not ugly, certainly, and one might even call it a manly face, but it was decidedly not a face suited to military finery.

For most occasions, armor would suffice, but a banquet was another matter, and Dagra found himself in a rather indescribable state.

They had already finished their greetings to Selene, Krische, Dagreen, and Kokys, and all the business to be done here was concluded.

Being summoned was an honor, but the discomfort was undeniable.

He drank his wine and surveyed the hall, wishing the banquet would end soon.

"Speaking of which, where's Corps Commander Aleha?"

"Ah, he went out for some night air a while ago."

Crossing to another country and rising through the ranks――it sounds impressive.

But in truth, he had fought his former countrymen and killed his own brother.

There had been something distant in his manner, as though his heart were elsewhere.

"……He must have a great deal on his mind."

"General Remin is wearing her uniform, isn't she."

"Y-yes…… well."

And yet Alberinea was in an apron dress.

For Teknrea, a soldier even if a woman, wearing her uniform was perfectly normal.

If anything, the fact that Alberinea――second only to the Marshal――was dressed as a servant was far stranger.

Alberinea tilted her head in apparent puzzlement, blissfully shelving her own situation――she was Alberinea through and through.

"Dresses don't suit me."

Tall for a woman.

An expression with a crease perpetually lodged between her brows.

Her looks were not bad. If she were cast as the male lead in an opera, the role would suit her well.

But as for feminine loveliness――that was simply not hers to claim.

"Hmm, so General Remin doesn't do well with dresses either."

Alberinea traced her lovely lips with a fingertip, and turned her gaze to the person beside her.

It was a servant Teknrea had seen a number of times.

Alberinea's personal attendant――she knew only the name Bery.

Though in many senses, one could call her a famous servant.

Not merely because of the infatuation men held for her loveliness, but because this servant had been deeply involved in the recent assassination incident and the visit of the ancient dragon.

Alberinea had challenged the ancient dragon solely to save this servant.

At least, everyone who had been present knew that much.

For a noble like Teknrea, the fact that Alberinea had subsequently put seventeen great nobles to the sword in retaliation was also known.

Officially, it was framed as an audience with the queen――along with the queen's directive not to spread needless rumor and disturb the nation――and by now it was fading from people's memories.

Even so, there were still a fair number who understood the full circumstances, as Teknrea did.

She had dressed her servant in a gown and stood beside her in an apron dress.

Twining her left arm around the servant's as if snuggling――surely, that was the whole truth of Alberinea.

She did not boast of her extraordinary feats on the battlefield, nor put on airs.

She was simply, utterly, a child.

*――Let it go*, Teknrea silently closed her eyes.

Regardless of all that, the war would surely have happened.

And that victory could not have been achieved without her.

That was all there was to it.

The 'young' servant wrapped in a dark indigo dress――Bery was beautiful.

The way she seemed embarrassed under Alberinea's gaze, the troubled gestures and expressions, were lovely and endearing.

Like Alberinea, she was slender and small-framed, with large eyes and ample bosom.

She was close to Teknrea's ideal of a woman.

If pressed, Teknrea might have wished for just another inch of height, but perhaps it was precisely that deficit that gave her a charming imperfection.

That she could still feel such allure even with Alberinea――a crystallization of beauty――standing right beside her was surely for that reason――perhaps it was because imperfection, an element of something lacking, created a sense of familiarity that made one look all the more favorably compared to something flawless.

"Does wearing a dress bother you as well?"

"……Yes, Duke Remin. I haven't had many occasions to wear one……"

The red-haired servant smiled with an uncertain, slightly troubled air.

She drew her shawl closer and flushed faintly, and the sight was indescribable.

"……It suits you very well. If even you say you find dresses difficult, then I certainly cannot wear one."

"Ehehe, that's right! That dress looks wonderful on you."

"Th-thank you very much……"

Alberinea happily clung to Bery's arm.

Bery only grew more flustered, and her endearing manner stirred something protective.

It was not only her appearance but her nature as well.

What she was born with must simply have been different.

If something about Teknrea had been different, too――if, for instance, this body of five shaku seven sun were three sun shorter, things might have been a little different.

If she had not been the eldest daughter but the younger sister, perhaps.

She had devoted herself to the way of the sword from a young age, and it had been twenty years since she last wore a dress.

If she had worn one even once in all that time, would things be a little different now?

"With how well it suits you, you should enjoy dressing up while you're still young. Naturally, I understand your position as a servant makes it difficult, but……"

"Young……"

"Hm?"

"N-no, it's nothing. Thank you very much."

Bery bowed her head, looking as though she found it difficult to respond, and Teknrea tilted her head.

*Well, never mind*, she thought, and turned her gaze back to Krische, offering a salute.

"I came to offer my greetings, partly to express my gratitude for the battlefield. I apologize for taking your time on an occasion like this."

Having completed her greetings, she took her leave, and the elderly adjutant who had been standing silently behind her, Milkaruz, spoke.

"My lady."

"……I've told you to stop calling me that."

"Forgive me…… If my memory serves, the Lady Argan is thirty this year. If you would call that young, then surely it is still well within the time for you to wear a dress as well, my lady."

Teknrea froze.

* * *

On the balcony, the light of stars and moon.

She had never been especially fond of glamorous gatherings.

Aleha cooled himself in the night breeze, gazing absently at the sky with wine in hand.

His tolerance for alcohol was neither weak nor strong.

Perhaps because he had been drinking without eating much, he felt somewhat tipsy.

"You there."

"……?"

He turned at the voice, and there stood a woman in military dress.

He had glimpsed her face briefly at the encampment.

Teknrea Remin――a general of the Central Army.

"Not accustomed to Alberan's banquets?"

"That isn't quite it…… I've simply never been comfortable at events like these."

Despite the darkness of night, her eyes glistened as if wet.

"What a coincidence. Neither am I. I'd rather be swinging a sword."

Watching her drain her cup, he took the wine bottle from the table and poured for her.

Then held it out for her.

"A general of Elsren crossing over to Alberan――I was curious what sort of person you might be. I've heard the name Aleha Sarshenka."

"No, I apologize. A sharp tongue is a bad habit of mine. ……The Four Generals of north, south, east, and west――in every era, they are the kingdom's finest warriors, held in the highest esteem by all of Alberan's fighting men. To have bested one of them is something you may rightly be proud of."

"Her strategies appear unorthodox but are fundamentally sound――a kind of orthodox approach. However, she can introduce new perspectives and new techniques into that framework. So long as Krische-sama exists, no nation and no strategist will be able to keep pace. ……Like the moon overhead, seemingly close yet untouchable."

He gazed at the crescent moon, reached a hand toward it, and laughed.

The further one stretched, the more distant the moon's outline seemed.

Every nation would surely attempt to uncover the secret of Alberan's great victory.

But even if they grasped at it, all they would find was the shadow of a moon far, far away.

"My current goal is to see how close I can come through the years of training ahead. A few years ago, all I wished for was advancement, but now I've found something greater."

"……Oh?"

Teknrea leaned her back against the railing and asked.

"Given the way Alberinea is…… I'd rather not intrude."

She indicated with the wine in her hand――the apron-dressed Alberinea walking through the hall.

And beside her, the beautiful servant in a dress who looked young and lovely.

Alberinea seemed far happier than the face she had worn on the battlefield.

"If you would be so kind, I'd like to hear about Alberinea's battles from you. Curiosity about you was only half the reason I came――the other half lies there."

"Of course. I'll admit, I was a little nervous――wondering why the general had sought me out."

"A natural assumption. Though, if you'll forgive me, I'm relieved to learn we share a common interest."

Aleha raised his cup, and Teknrea lightly touched hers to it.

"A warrior's tastes are ever the same, in any age. I'm sorry I'm a woman who doesn't even wear a dress, but I'd appreciate it if you'd keep me company for a while."

"Haha, a beauty who carries a military uniform so well is an honor surpassing any lovely flower."

Teknrea froze briefly, and Aleha tilted his head slightly.

"……?"

"……Nothing. A gnat, just now."

Teknrea slapped her own cheek and looked away.

Finding it odd but deciding not to press the matter, Aleha opened his mouth.

"Then let us begin in order, starting with the Battle of Galshan――"

The banquet continued in splendor for some time after.

Their conversation lasted throughout, and it is said that Milkaruz, observing the scene from inside the hall, was dabbing at his eyes with a handkerchief.

# Chapter 218: Facilities

One year after the Five-Nations War――three facilities had been completed in the capital during that time.

The Alberan Academy of Magical Studies, the Albernaria Military Academy, and the Albernaria Arsenal.

The Academy of Magical Studies conducted collaborative research on mana crystals, which had previously been a matter of individual study.

The Albernaria Military Academy provided instruction by retired soldiers, along with research into tactics and strategy.

The Albernaria Arsenal focused primarily on mass-producing Alberinea-model magical constructs.

None of these were entirely without precedent, but nothing of this scale and clarity had existed before.

Krische Christand served as the director of the Alberan Academy of Magical Studies and the head of the Albernaria Arsenal, while the inaugural headmaster of the Albernaria Military Academy was Elrouga Faren, Advisor to the Marshal.

"……So, going forward, research will be done all together, cooperatively. I've read the documents that were submitted in advance. I will now ask for a presentation of your individual research, doubling as an introduction."

In the first-class district of the capital, there 'happened to be' several vacant mansions.

They were the estates of great nobles who had been done away with by assassination.

Clutching Grace's sleeve, hiding behind her body, watching him with an air of observation.

Three or four years old, perhaps――about the same age as Selene over there.

But her bearing was still, almost as though she were a thing crafted rather than born, with an ephemeral quality, and her skin was snow-white as if unused to sunlight.

A girl who did not belong in a village.

*'……You even named her?'*

*'Yes. ……And Father, I want you to know――no matter what anyone says, I won't give her up.'*

Grace held Krische protectively and glared at Gallen.

Krische's gaze moved back and forth between Grace and Gallen.

*'I'll say nothing. You and Gorka decided this together as husband and wife. I've no right to interfere. ……And as if a stubborn thing like you would listen anyway.'*

Grace had grown into a fine woman, yet had not been blessed with children.

The one child she had finally conceived was lost, yet now she seemed like a mother――and she looked as though she had regained a little of her brightness.

That he had permitted her to raise this clearly unusual child was not out of pity for the child itself, but simply for that reason.

He crouched before the girl and smiled.

*'I'm Gallen. How do you do.'*

*'…………'*

The girl called Krische said nothing, only gazing steadily up at him.

She did not seem frightened.

It was more like she was observing the being called Gallen, with something faintly inorganic in her gaze.

*Perhaps she doesn't know how to speak――*

*'Krische, greet him properly. Remember what I told you? When you meet someone for the first time, you say: how do you do.'*

But that was not the case. The girl looked up at Grace, nodded, then turned her eyes back to Gallen and lowered her head.

*'……How do you do.'*

A voice clear as a rolling bell.

*That's right, good girl*, Grace said, stroking her head, and the girl softened her expression, showing a quiet smile.

Like a fairy from a children's tale, beautiful――that small emotion colored her face.

*'……A well-behaved child. I look forward to our time together.'*

Gallen, too, reached out a hand.

Krische seemed to watch the movement of that hand intently.

When it settled on her head, she quietly narrowed those violet eyes.

* * *

"――Ehehe, when Selene saw the spider on her shoulder, she was so startled, and she kept going kyaa, kyaa until Krische caught it."

"Is that so. Well, many people dislike spiders. I've been somewhat wary of them myself ever since one bit me as a child."

"……Ojii-sama has things he doesn't like?"

"Of course. I merely pretend otherwise."

Gallen smiled ruefully and stroked Krische's head.

They were walking outside the capital――the military training grounds.

Tents were pitched here and there, with encampment setup and teardown drills underway, and soldiers were hurrying about.

"Ojii-sama too…… hmm, Bery and Anne and the others say it's all those skittery legs that bother them."

"I feel the same. I'm no fond of spiders or centipedes."

"Is that really how it is……?"

Krische pressed a finger to her lips and tilted her head.

"Well, to give a dramatic example…… you wouldn't want to eat a meal in a room with spiders and centipedes crawling all over the place, would you?"

She closed her eyes for a moment at the words――perhaps picturing it――and furrowed her brow.

"……That would be a little unpleasant."

"It's a matter of degree and circumstance. Selene is fine spotting a spider in the forest, but she can't bear one landing on her shoulder indoors. You're fine with one or two spiders, but a great many of them would be unpleasant for you. ……It's a question of where your threshold lies."

Krische nodded with an air of admiration, *I see, I see*, and then smiled, seemingly pleased.

"Then Krische might dislike spiders a little too."

"Is that something to be happy about?"

"Ehehe, Krische is the same as Selene and ojii-sama."

Krische clung to Gallen's arm, her cheeks softening, and Gallen smiled wryly in that troubled way of his.

*――This morning's dream. She recalled their first meeting, from those early days.*

Compared to back then, even compared to when they had lived in the village, Krische had become much brighter.

No――she had not been dark to begin with.

When she was with Gorka, Grace, Galla, or Gallen――with certain people, Krische had shown all manner of expressions and been lively enough for her age.

The number of those she trusted had grown, and her world had expanded.

In the few years since coming to Christand, Krische had grown more vivid still.

The civil war and the great war that followed――she had been swept up in many things, and the road had not been smooth.

Even so, the changes that had come to her were, he was sure, good ones.

He felt nothing but gratitude toward the many people who watched over her, starting with Selene and Bery.

At the very least, this granddaughter of his――who could hardly be called normal――could smile this happily because of those people.

"Mm, it's Aleha and General Remin."

"Ah……"

Inside the small forest where the Black Flag Special Service's training ground lay, on the way there, they heard the clash of steel.

Sparring with blunted training swords were a young man with tea-gold hair and a woman tall for her sex, with golden hair.

Teknrea did not notice them, but Aleha, who had apparently been instructing her in swordsmanship, spotted them first and lowered his sword to salute.

"Krische-sama, Gallen-sama."

"Good afternoon. You too, General Remin."

"Ah, yes! Forgive the unsightly display……"

Teknrea noticed them at once and pressed a hand to her left breast in salute.

She had been at it for quite some time, it seemed, and a fine sheen of sweat glistened on her skin.

Krische surveyed her surroundings, returned the salute, and tilted her head.

"……Why are you training out here?"

"If we train in the open, we end up drawing a crowd…… It's a private session, so we wanted somewhere with fewer people."

"Y-yes, it was I who requested it, Krische-sama. It would be unbecoming for a general to be seen taking sword lessons――it could affect morale."

"I see……"

Krische nodded in apparent acceptance, and Gallen regarded the two with a wry smile.

He had been seeing this particular pairing quite often of late.

"Ehehe, practice is a wonderful thing. General Remin, Aleha loves sparring, so please keep him company as much as you like."

"Y-yes……"

Krische was in high spirits――come to think of it, the number of times she had been asked to spar had dropped considerably of late, and now she saw the likely reason.

Now that she thought about it,

*'Aleha, about that tactical matter you mentioned before――'*

*'Oh, what about it?'*

Or,

*'A warrior of your caliber shouldn't still be wearing a mass-produced cuirass. I happen to be bringing a skilled armorer to the estate soon to have my armor adjusted. How about coming along?'*

*'……Armor, you say. I was just thinking I should find somewhere to commission a set――'*

Such things――she felt as though she had been seeing the two of them together rather often.

They were probably doing sword training regularly as well.

Krische thought it a thoroughly good thing.

The subtle shifts in Teknrea's slightly flushed expression were entirely beyond Krische's perception.

She simply concluded that the redness in her cheeks must be from exerting herself so hard in practice.

"Also, Krische thinks your forward step is a little too forceful. You're tall, so it might be better to keep your center of gravity a bit further back."

Teknrea's eyes widened in mild surprise, and Aleha smiled wryly.

"That was exactly what I was just telling her…… How about it? Perhaps a demonstration――"

"……Krische and ojii-sama have business with the Kuro-Fuyo, so."

Krische glared at Aleha, and Teknrea let out a soft laugh at the sight of the two.

Krische tilted her head and stared hard at Teknrea's face.

"Is something the matter?"

"No, it's just……"

Something about her bearing had changed, somehow――as though the edges had softened, a gentler quality than before.

But Krische could not quite put it into words, so she shook her head. *Never mind.*

"It's very good that you and Aleha are getting along. Selene and Skeleton were both worried about whether Aleha would be able to make proper friends with people in the kingdom…… Knowing he has someone besides Nyan-nyan and the others to talk to, Krische feels as though a weight has been lifted."

As for Aleha, having been installed as corps commander and then more or less left to his own devices, the detailed groundwork had mostly been handled by Selene, Elrouga, Gallen, and Kokys.

And yet she assumed the air of a guardian――Krische nodded again and again, as though she had been looking after him all this time.

Aleha laughed at the sight of the little girl's self-important manner, and Gallen smiled wryly.

Teknrea, her face reddening further, said:

"A-as a warrior, when one meets someone deserving of respect, birth and background are irrelevant…… If anything, it is I who have been learning a great deal, and I am the one in his debt."

"You flatter me too much. In truth, I've been helped enormously by General Remin in many ways."

"Ehehe, it's very good when two people acknowledge each other like this. General Remin, please take care of Aleha."

"Y-yes!"

*If Teknrea stays friends with him, my burden should lighten* ――Krische said with innocent simplicity, and Teknrea saluted with her flushed face, her mind turning over several possible meanings.

Aleha, too, smiled wryly at the sight and bowed his head. "I'll be in your care."

"Krische, we shouldn't keep them. Let us be on our way."

"Yes, ojii-sama!"

Krische clung to Gallen's arm, and with the two seeing them off, they headed toward the Black Flag Special Service's area.

――But partway there, a presence she had been sensing drew her attention, and she tilted her head, pulling Gallen toward it.

"……What are you doing?"

A little distance from the pair――in a spot where the gap between the trees afforded a clear view of them.

There stood the one-armed veteran Waltza, with his prosthetic arm, and the equally aged Milkaruz, Teknrea's adjutant.

Gallen regarded the two with a look of mild exasperation.

"J-just keeping an eye on them, you see……"

Waltza saluted hastily, and Milkaruz followed suit.

"……I understand the impulse, but nothing good comes of old men meddling in such things. Two fine warriors, and here you are peeping……"

"……H-how terribly embarrassing."

"Our apologies……"

Krische tilted her head in solitary bewilderment, and Gallen smiled wryly before adding:

"That said, wanting to see how the young ones fare…… I suppose that's the nature of old age."

"……?"

Gallen stroked Krische's small head with tender affection.

Krische could hardly be called normal.

A kind girl――yet in many ways, different from other people.

What he ought to be grateful for was that she had been blessed with the people around her.

Not only Selene and Bery, but many who understood her.

Some offered respect in the truest sense; others offered love.

"Bunny, Mia says she's the only one getting scolded and it isn't fair."

"I didn't say that!"

Kalua laughed brightly, hugging Krische from behind while teasing her friend.

"……Mia, an officer must sometimes take responsibility. Running from responsibility like that is not a good――"

"Th-this is obviously Kalua's――!"

"Mia-adjutant, it's really not good to interrupt a superior officer."

"Kalua! You……!"

As he watched the scene, the one who sidled up to him was Dagra, wearing a troubled expression.

Gallen smiled wryly and said:

"……This place is always lively. That's a fine thing."

"I apologize. It's really quite shameful……"

"No, it's better for Krische that they're this close. I'm grateful to you, Company Commander."

It was an honest sentiment.

In daily life, she was kind and hardworking.

But on the battlefield, she was far too much so.

When she had killed those bandits, it had been the same――Krische felt no resistance to taking human life.

No matter how cruel the act, if it was deemed necessary, she would carry it out without hesitation.

Krische was too strong, and she possessed a ruthless side.

That she was not isolated despite all of this was thanks to Selene and the others, and to these people, too.

They were part of the reason Krische could live within the circle of others.

"……There won't be a war for a while. The last one was my final battle."

"Gallen-sama……"

"But I can feel at ease because this unit exists. ……I'd like to entrust that girl to you, going forward as well."

Dagra looked at Gallen and quietly saluted.

Gallen returned the salute, and smiled.

He had returned to the battlefield for Krische's sake.

To shield her from whatever sparks might fall upon her.

But whether he had actually been of any use, he could not say.

The people who protected Krische were far more numerous than Gallen had ever imagined.

Even without him, her future looked bright.

He could not recall being so grateful for his own powerlessness.

"Upon our swords and our names, we swear. Rest assured――all of us――"

The strength left his body, all at once,

"Gallen-sama!"

and his body was caught in an instant.

His vision swam and blurred, and then,

"……Ojii-sama?"

it met the violet eyes turning back toward him.
