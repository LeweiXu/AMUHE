# Chapter 51: Unease

Krische stood and traced the central defile of the Dragon's Maw.

"In tackling the Dragon's Maw, a breakthrough through this central passage would be the most desirable victory. However, this central defile is precisely the problem. Concentrating forces is difficult, and if we were to force our way through, there would need to be a clear difference in the quality of the soldiers themselves. That said, given that we have failed to break through after nearly two months, the Second Corps simply lacks the strength. This situation is therefore meaningless."

"……Krische."

"No, Selene-sama. What Krische-sama says is correct — it is none other than the inadequacy of our Second Corps. Please, do not concern yourself. She is merely stating facts as facts."

Selene started to interject at Krische's blunt manner of speaking, but Kokys checked her.

When Krische looked at the two, Selene let out a small sigh and Kokys nodded.

"If a central breakthrough is impossible, then a breakthrough on either flank. Or an envelopment. But envelopment is not a viable option. Due to the central defile, the enemy likely has a large number of idle reserve units. Even if we were to envelop over that great distance and cross the mountains, they would simply redirect a detachment to meet us, and that would be the end. Annihilation in an isolated state is plainly foreseeable. Therefore, a flank breakthrough is the appropriate choice. We must be prepared for some bloodshed."

A flank breakthrough meant, as the words suggested, breaking through the enemy's flank positions and swinging around to their rear.

An envelopment meant an even wider swing, bypassing combat to reach the enemy's rear.

While a flank breakthrough was a purely tactical maneuver, an envelopment — though also a tactical means of targeting the enemy's rear — approached a strategic development that included threatening their rear lines of communication.

Depending on the circumstances, one might attach the qualifier "tactical envelopment" or "strategic envelopment," but when speaking simply of "envelopment" in a tactical-level discussion like this, it referred to a tactical envelopment.

In the kingdom's usage, "strategy" referred to the movement of armies, including the selection of battlefields, while "tactics" referred to the means of attacking after contact with the enemy.

Since the strategy demanded the capture of the Dragon's Maw to advance into the capital region, battle here was unavoidable, and therefore the discussion here concerned tactics.

In that respect, it differed from the previous war, where they had been able to choose their battlefield.

The only one who looked puzzled by Krische's words was Anne.

Bery, who had spent years listening alongside Selene to Bogan's instruction, also had a reasonable understanding of basic military terminology.

When dealing with a superior enemy, avoiding battle and targeting the rear through envelopment was an excellent means — but naturally, isolation was unavoidable.

The mountains grew higher as one moved east or west toward their peaks, requiring an enormous distance to achieve a strategic envelopment.

For that reason, Bogan and the others had discarded that option from the start.

The conclusion was the same after all: a mountain breakthrough on one of the two flanks.

What Krische had just laid out was the shared understanding of everyone present.

"So then, whether a flank breakthrough is currently possible. Given that no breakthrough has been achieved during this period, the Third and Fourth Corps also lack the strength. It must be said that an independent breakthrough by either would be difficult. If we fought on for a full year, perhaps a result might emerge, but the longer we take, the more war expenditures pile up, and the situation may worsen. As the lord has said, this greatly benefits the enemy, and the uncertainty of how the west, south, and north will move is considerable — swift victory is required."

She slid her fingertip from Mitskronia in the west to Bernaich in the east.

"We will breakthrough the enemy deployed at Bernaich on the left flank with the full army excluding the Second Corps. The First, Third, and Fourth Corps will launch a general assault to shatter them, then attack the enemy deployed in the center from behind. This will assist the Second Corps' central breakthrough. The Second Corps can be said to be the corps most capable of frontal combat in the Christand army at present, so this takes first priority."

Then she pointed again to Mitskronia in the west.

"Naturally, the enemy will attempt an attack from Mitskronia. However, once a sufficient number of them have gathered, we burn them alive along with the mountain. We spread oil beforehand and set it ablaze. Whether it burns well depends on the weather, but at this time of year dead leaves are prominent, so it should burn quite nicely. Roasted to a crisp. It would be nice if His Highness the Prince were to burn to death as a bonus."

Krische smiled and, her explanation finished, took her seat.

The assembled faces fell silent, frowning, and looked to Bogan.

"……However, burning the mountain is——"

"It will grow back eventually. We can think about compensation for nearby villages afterward, can we not? The objective is to win."

Krische drained her cloyingly sweet tea and continued.

"Eight thousand five hundred reinforcements. Our forces are unlikely to grow beyond this. Therefore, now, this very moment, is the time to attack. The harsh mountain operations for three corps will produce stragglers, and casualties from stray fires on Mitskronia may be considerable. But we can certainly inflict even greater losses on the enemy."

Bery said nothing and poured fresh tea.

She added generous amounts of honey and milk, stirred it, and Krische thanked her — thank you very much. Bery said nothing, smiled softly, straightened her posture again, and lowered her eyes.

"Overwhelm them from a position of superiority, plunge them into chaos, and deliver the killing blow while they cannot resist. What is demanded of tactics is victory. ……Krische and the others are not here to protect a mountain — we are here to win the war and make the princess a queen, are we not?"

Krische answered Bogan, who had expressed concern about burning the mountain.

It was something she could not have cared less about.

Krische wanted to get this over with quickly.

"……I'm in favor, General. With support, a breakthrough is possible. And it's bold — I like that. I haven't got the head that Velraigh has, so something this straightforward is to my taste."

It was Kokys who answered.

"Knowing you, General, you must be thinking about postwar matters, but let us prioritize winning first. Once we win, anything can be managed. After all, the future queen herself is right here."

"However, burning the mountain is far too excessive an act."

It was Salva, one of the Third Corps Commander's adjutants.

He made a sour face and glared at Krische.

"Burning a mountain is sacrilege. Mountains bring forth bounty and sustain the people. To burn Mitskronia, which bears a hero's name, would engrave infamy as a stain upon history. ……I am aware that Krische-sama is talented, but she is still young, and I must say her thinking is too immature. In the first place, what do you intend to do if the wildfire spreads without limit?"

This man had harbored resistance toward Krische for some time.

He was not incompetent, and the words he spoke now were perfectly reasonable, but mingled within them was a distrust of Krische.

Krische answered expressionlessly.

"It will be put out by rain eventually. Many texts indicate that great fires generate clouds and bring rainfall. Besides, 'without limit' is impossible. Given the terrain, at most one or two mountains and some forest will burn, and that will be the end of it. If we win, the nation can simply provide compensation, can it not?"

"But what if we don't win——"

"Are we currently having a discussion premised on defeat? This is a plan for winning, and there are no certainties on the battlefield, are there?"

Krische answered flatly, and Fourth Corps Commander Elrouga spoke up.

"Indeed. Rather than carrying on an endless war of attrition here, accepting the stain of burning the mountain is not a bad trade. And I consider the chances of victory to be very high. The issue is that a swift mountain breakthrough by a large force must be achieved."

"Let us begin moving troops under the pretext of reorganization starting today, and shift some of them upward. The highest priority is that the enemy does not notice, but——"

Selene said.

Her mind was already made up.

"Complete concealment will be impossible. These are soldiers of the same kingdom; there will be spies among them. The fatigue will be considerable, but if we are to do it……let me see. Beginning at dusk tomorrow, we have the First and Third Corps climb the mountain, then a light rest. The Fourth Corps, with less fatigue, moves first, followed by the First Corps, and lastly the Third Corps, which has the longest march distance. We simply need to deny the spies any opportunity to run off with the information."

"Shall we send the First Corps' light infantry for the breakthrough role and the heavier infantry up ahead? Krische, your preference?"

"Yes, Krische thinks that would be best. It would be good to have the Fourth Corps create a weak point."

The Third Corps Commander smiled at the words of the two beautiful young ladies and looked to Bogan.

"It seems the corps commanders are unanimous. Salva's concerns are valid, but as Corps Commander Agrand says — we have Her Highness the Princess with us."

Bogan made a sour face and looked to Kreschenta, deep in thought.

Burning a mountain was not as simple as the words made it sound.

The kingdom worshipped the twelve gods, but was also tolerant of indigenous faiths.

Many villages held mountains as central objects of worship, and he harbored concerns about burning such objects of veneration.

Kreschenta, receiving that gaze, was also deep in thought.

Though she understood only the rudiments of tactics, she possessed a mind not so different from Krische's.

If Krische had judged and concluded this, then it was without doubt the best plan — so Kreschenta thought.

Krische did not doubt her own superiority, and Kreschenta was the same in that regard.

She already considered postwar matters a trifle.

If she herself were to become queen, then it was of no consequence.

Kokys's words that anything could be managed were exactly right, and even if some minor rebellion arose, that could simply be dealt with as it came.

What Kreschenta desired was her own security and happiness, and for that, Krische's existence was indispensable.

So long as Krische and the companions in her birdcage were happy, everything else was of no matter to Kreschenta.

That said, Kreschenta clearly recognized that her own perspective and Krische's differed.

Krische's proposal — she was searching for whether it contained any problems.

"Burning the mountain……it is indeed a fearsome thing to do. It may earn us resentment hereafter."

Kreschenta slowly opened her mouth.

The tent fell silent, and all eyes turned to Kreschenta.

"But if the war drags on, even greater tragedies will follow. Many soldiers will die, and the land will be exhausted. And that will create new sparks of conflict. Those who stand above others must look to future happiness, even if they are resented in the moment — so my father, His Majesty the King, told me."

Selene and Bery showed the faintest reaction to the words "His Majesty the King," but kept it from their expressions.

Kreschenta fashioned a sorrowful expression. It was precisely the emotion everyone wished to see from a princess — filled with a fragile, lovely poignancy that stirred the desire to protect.

Kreschenta understood the effect her voice, her expressions — her own beauty — had upon others.

And she had learned to wield it.

"Those words were meant for precisely a time like this. That is what I believe."

Gravely, yet clearly.

She performed, shaping her words to align with Krische's wishes.

Selene and Bery were reminded anew of the being called Kreschenta, yet Krische, who should have known the same truth, showed no reaction whatsoever.

*Kreschenta really is good at talking*, she thought with idle admiration, gazing at her.

"Salva-sama's words are most apt. That is surely where the Margrave's concern lies as well. However, I believe that above all else, ending this war is what matters most for the kingdom."

She turned her eyes to the displeased-looking Salva.

Salva, finding her gaze upon him, hastily straightened his posture.

Watching Salva, Kreschenta came to understand anew what the flickering unease she had felt stemmed from.

That Krische was feared, and that there were those who disliked her.

Selene's concern was evident, but Krische — particularly within the military — had too great a tendency to disregard such relationships with others.

As social skill, it was far too immature, and precarious.

"There may be those who cannot truly accept this in their hearts. I share the pain of that feeling. But if it is to prevent even greater harm, then I wish for it."

That said, what to do about such people was difficult.

Kreschenta suspected everyone.

All who might harm her, all who had the ability to do so.

All the more so because Kreschenta lacked Krische's strength.

That was precisely why trusting others and entrusting anything to them — even within an army — felt endlessly perilous.

The only reason she had come here was because Krische, who could protect her, was here; back at that fortress, Krische had not been. That was the sole reason.

But compared to Kreschenta, Krische was less vigilant.

Likely because she was confident in her own abilities — but her lack of caution against schemes directed at herself was troubling.

That was what clung to her as unease, Kreschenta thought.

The Christand army was excellent, more disciplined than any other.

But that did not mean it was perfect.

It would be better if matters of concern were eliminated, but having someone dispose of people like Salva one by one was impossible in this situation, and if one were to consider it, one would need to investigate how much loyalty each soldier directed toward not only Krische but Bogan, Selene, and each corps commander.

There was no way to do such a thing in this situation, and from her position as princess, she could do nothing but affirm Krische's words.

Bogan listened to those words, closed his eyes, and made his decision.

"Burning the mountain. Setting sentiment aside, it is indeed a good plan. At times we are compelled to burn cities and villages in order to defend the realm. Today is no different — we have no choice but to think of it that way. Salva, accept it."

"……Yes, sir."

* * *

"The battlefield really is full of too many anxieties. I simply cannot fathom why onee-sama sits there so carefree."

Four people in the room.

Kreschenta and Bery sat on a field cot, and Krische was sitting sideways on Bery's lap, held in her arms.

Selene had apparently given up on voicing complaints about this and sat in a chair, while Kreschenta, as always, pressed against Krische with a faintly displeased look.

Anne had remained in the other tent to greet and attend to Bogan.

This was half the result of being treated as a nuisance by Krische.

The course of action was set, the personnel assignments decided.

Selene would go up to Bernaich, and Krische to Mitskronia to observe the situation once.

Selene's purpose was reorganization, while Krische's was to issue instructions regarding the fire-setting.

The meeting had continued for a while after that, and on Third Corps Commander Terious's proposal, word was passed to the troops that the attack would come from Bernaich tomorrow.

Information leaking was certain, so it was better to instead lure the enemy into attacking from Mitskronia.

The general assault would run from deep night to dawn the day after tomorrow, but tomorrow they would make a show of initiating action, draw the enemy's attack, and then withdraw.

The numerically inferior Third Corps would naturally be pushed back, and under the guise of being pressed by the enemy's offensive, they would withdraw and set fire in coordination with the action on the Bernaich side.

The enemy would be on guard tomorrow morning and afternoon, expecting an assault from Bernaich, and would naturally strengthen their defenses — but if that extended into the night, their concentration would falter.

If it were merely a defensive posture, that would be one thing, but a defensive posture against a general assault would leave the other side no room to breathe either. And a defensive posture without breathing room could not be maintained for long.

Terious's proposal — that it was better to target a lapse in vigilance rather than aim for a complete surprise — was sound, and the meeting settled on that as the plan of action.

Since they would leave the encampment and ascend Bernaich tomorrow, today was the last day Krische could rest. Naturally, Krische was indulging in Bery's affections.

"I understand how you feel, but if you suspect everything, you can't wage a war."

"Perhaps not."

Krische reached out to pat the displeased Kreschenta's head and told her.

"Kreschenta, Krische is leaving Bery in your care while Krische is away. No matter what happens, Krische will get Selene out, and if something goes wrong, Krische will deal with it then."

"……I am not as skilled with a sword as onee-sama."

"You don't seem any weaker than the average soldier, Kreschenta, and you're good at using the floofy thing, aren't you? Bery is good at it too, so at least running away should be possible."

"That……calling it 'the floofy thing' sounds foolish, so please say 'mana.'"

Kreschenta told Krische with an exasperated look and glanced at Bery.

Bery smiled wryly and answered, I am not all that confident, however.

"I have at least received some instruction in the sword from the lord, so I should not be a burden——"

"Bery is not allowed to do anything dangerous."

Krische wriggled herself upright and brought her face close to Bery's.

Through the gaps in her silky silver hair, jewel-like violet eyes peered at Bery.

"Bery's hands are for cooking, so it would be terrible if you got hurt."

"……Krische-sama."

"You must not think of anything strange, understand? Even if something happens, that is a last resort only. Anything else is absolutely forbidden."

Bery swayed her red hair and smiled happily, then kissed Krische.

She answered yes — clearly, but in a quiet voice.

"Ah……"

And immediately after answering, Bery noticed the gazes of the other two and froze, and Selene opened her mouth in a tired-sounding voice, saying, *you*.

"I'd been a bit concerned, but really, Bery. Have you finally gone completely Krische in the head?"

"……Um, well, that is."

Her cheeks reddened like apples, and her eyes darted left and right.

Ah, I knew it, something was suspicious! Kreschenta continued.

"I just knew Argan-sama was doing it on purpose! You took advantage of onee-sama's ignorance to teach her only things convenient for yourself, didn't you!"

"Th-that is not, well……"

"If it wasn't, then what! K-kissing onee-sama as if to show off to us……what kind of person are you……!"

Bery's face reddened further, and Krische, tilting her head at the scene before her, kissed the thoroughly red Bery and declared.

"What are you angry about? It is an expression of love. Bery loves Krische, which is why she kisses Krische. And Krische loves Bery too, so it is mutual love."

"O-onee-sama, Argan-sama's love and your pure love are definitely different things……"

"Fufu, Kreschenta, the thing about love is, you see, it comes in all sorts of forms for different people. You might not understand yet, Kreschenta, but that is how it is."

Krische raised a finger, wearing a face as though she understood perfectly.

Since coming to the estate, Krische had been in the position of being spoiled, but when it came to children younger than herself, she had been known in the village as a dependable big sister who looked after them well.

Selene aside, Kreschenta was a child younger than herself, so Krische held a peculiar confidence that she was the one in the right.

"Ah, Kreschenta, you're sulking, aren't you. It's all right. Kreschenta, come here."

She beckoned Kreschenta over.

Kreschenta could not resist the temptation, and when she brought her face close, she received a kiss, her cheeks coloring.

"Uuu……"

"Selene too, come."

"L-look here……"

Selene looked exasperated, but she read the room.

In any case, this was simply how Krische was, and there was no helping it.

Resignedly she stood and drew near, and Krische gave her a kiss.

It was not entirely without a hint of unconscious rivalry toward Bery, and she plopped roughly back into her chair and pressed her lips to her black soy tea, hiding her embarrassment.

Krische looked happy as she nuzzled against Bery's cheek, and Bery, her own cheeks red, stroked Krische's head, then gave an exaggeratedly deliberate cough.

"W-we were in the middle of a serious discussion, so please, do continue……"

"……What are you trying to cover up, honestly."

"Um, well, that is……"

"……Fine, I'll hear about it later."

Selene said so, and told Kreschenta — *the truth is, the battlefield is nothing but anxieties*.

"How the enemy will move, how our allies will move. You win great victories in ways you never intended, and suffer great defeats where you never expected them. The battlefield is nothing but uncertain elements. The only thing known is each side's total troop strength……no, sometimes even that is unclear."

Selene poured fresh black soy tea and stretched.

Her long golden hair swayed, her supple body arched, tracing an elegant curve.

"The battlefield is a chaotic world where everything is obscure. You fill it in with plausible information and use tactics to transform the chance events that may occur into certainties, to the greatest extent possible — to anticipate and control them. Whether Krische's plan is perfect, we cannot say. But the odds are favorable given the current situation, so we decided to go with it. You don't seem to like that kind of uncertain conclusion."

"……That is so."

Kreschenta thought it over and studied Selene searchingly.

"Are you reproaching me?"

"If you understand what you've set in motion, that's enough. I have no hobby of saying the same thing twice — it's merely instruction."

Selene said, and tipped her cup.

"The Christand army is strong. Krische is here too. But we are by no means invincible, and anxiety is ever-present. Please understand that there is no certainty in war. Krische may look carefree, but she properly understands that."

Prompted, Krische blinked but nodded.

"Well, yes. If Krische knew everything about both sides, there's no way Krische could lose, but it's as Selene said. Krische is just sort of working with the information available and winging it, and the last war was the same."

Selene furrowed her brow at words that contained nothing but confidence that losing was impossible, yet said nothing.

She was simply stating facts.

At the very least, in sand-table war games under equal conditions, Krische was undefeated, and neither Bogan nor former First Corps Commander Nozan nor the tactician Elrouga could match her.

Selene, naturally, had lost every time as well.

What she said was, within her own framework, simply the truth.

Even so, it was a bit irritating to Selene.

"Even if Krische lost, there's no way Krische would die, so Krische has no anxiety about that. But Krische is still worried about Selene, and worried about Bery, so Kreschenta, please properly protect Bery. When we're far apart, Krische can't protect her."

"……I understand."

"Good. Since we can't know what will happen until it happens, the best thing is to make our preparations and then rest our bodies and do something enjoyable."

Krische said so and clung to Bery with a squeeze, indulging in her with a kiss.

Since she would not be able to come back tomorrow, stockpiling indulgence and kisses from Bery to cover tomorrow's share was only natural.

Bery, the redness not yet faded from her face, held her firmly and stroked her.

Selene cast a dubious eye at the melting Krische, and Kreschenta pursed her lips.

And so, on the eve of the decisive battle for the Dragon's Maw, the four spent a peaceful night together.

# Chapter 52: A Peaceful Time

They climbed the mountain from morning.

It was a grueling journey for the soldiers, but for someone like Selene who could consciously wield mana, even wearing full armor posed no difficulty.

Elegantly engraved silver plate armor. A crimson cloak.

Her golden hair was tied and gathered at the back, and the hawk-crested helm she carried tucked under her arm.

Krische was in her usual one-piece dress.

She wore a black cloak, but the only thing that could be called protective gear was her steel gauntlets.

Her boot toes and heels were reinforced with metal fittings, and the gauntlets had solid reinforcement over the knuckles — practical items — but overall she was so lightly equipped it was unsettling to watch.

Her long hair was tied in two braids with a flower ornament Bery had made for her, the braid-tails swishing left and right.

Selene looked at Krische beside her and recalled the previous night.

"——I……offered my name to her."

The extra sweetness in Bery's demeanor lately had been nagging at her.

*Could it be that things have gone as far as they can possibly go*——she had refrained from asking despite such suspicions, but they were on the eve of battle.

She wanted to clear up things that bothered her at times like this.

*'I suppose I should tell you……'* Bery, too, had seemed as though she wanted to discuss something, so Selene took her outside the tent for a private conversation.

*'So, did something happen?'* When she asked, Bery's cheeks reddened, her gaze wavered, and the very first thing she said was that.

Selene was dumbfounded by words beyond what she had expected, and lost hers.

"Th-that's a joke……"

"It is not. It is the truth. ……I made a vow."

If it had been an impulsive act of laying hands on Krische — well, she could maybe, barely understand that.

Certainly, that innocence and purity written all over her face, that defenselessness, was an unbearable temptation.

But offering one's name was an entirely rational act.

Selene had been perhaps seven years old when she was taught about it.

*'Selene, a hidden name is a magic name from the age of the gods. In your whole life, you teach it only to that one person to whom you give everything — a magic name. You must never tell anyone. When you find someone wonderful like Papa and can be together like I am, only then do you offer that name.'*

In the era when what was now called the hidden name was known as the true name, it held absolute significance.

The soul was sealed within the true name, and to the one who learned it, one must offer everything and submit. So absolute was the true name that it was said a nation had once fallen because a true name was discovered.

Of course, that was an age long past, and in truth no such power existed.

Even if it were to become known, nothing would actually happen.

But the act of a noble offering it to another still carried profound meaning even now.

The difference between commoner and noble was faith in one's own name.

A word sworn upon one's name could never be broken by a noble.

To break it, one would choose even death.

Such was the faith nobles held, and noble society existed upon that premise. It was for that reason that nobles were taught the ways of nobility repeatedly from earliest childhood.

The family name was one such thing, and the hidden name was its ultimate expression.

House Argan was a minor noble family of baron rank, yet it possessed a history older than the Christands'.

Even her mother, bold and unrestrained as she was, had conveyed the importance of names to a noble with solemnity, repeating it many times to Selene.

That was precisely why the weight of Bery's telling her hidden name was extraordinary.

Bery, who never put on noble airs.

And yet, when it came to noble etiquette and conduct, she had studied them more deeply than anyone and wore them as second nature — a true scion of noble tradition.

There was no way such a Bery would lightly speak her hidden name, and the weight of its meaning froze Selene in place.

"……I am a wretched, unpleasant person."

Quietly, Bery began to speak.

"I have not told you much about the old days, have I, ojou-sama?"

"Well, you never would say no matter how I asked."

"Fufu……I was ashamed. I was never a good enough child to be giving you lessons in morality, after all."

Bery continued.

"Spending all day in bed looking out the window, reading books. When I was small, it never even occurred to me to go outside like this, so I just turned things over and over in my head, constructing arguments, and convinced myself I knew the world. Everyone around me looked stupid, and oh, I would think, how unfortunate I am."

She spoke as though ashamed, and smiled bitterly.

She drew lines on the ground with her foot, unable to stay still, constantly moving.

"The servants back then disliked me. Naturally. ……Nee-sama came to check on me every day, but honestly, I envied her brightness and kindness, and resented her for it. She had everything I lacked. I said many cruel things."

*It's a matter of temperament*, she said with a troubled expression, and stopped.

"……I am twisted. Somewhere my mind grew cold, and when I saw something good I envied it, and I saw only the worst in people, and then all I could see was the worst in myself. At the core, I disliked myself. So I took to disliking others, to disguise it."

Reminiscing.

Bery spoke of such things with a slightly distant look in her eyes.

Selene could say nothing, and simply listened in silence.

"Deep down, I was aware of it, so I told myself that when the time came I would devote my whole life to nee-sama. I wanted to at least be beautiful in some way. ……When House Argan fell into debt, I made that vow, and yet in the end, I was protected by nee-sama and rescued by the lord. The height of foolishness. ……I could not do a single thing properly."

Dark, sunken eyes swayed with sadness.

"I searched for meaning the whole time. Living without understanding, I thought I would at least try to fulfill whatever role was asked of me……that was about the extent of my feelings. Putting on a pleasant face, but drawing a clear line, so I wouldn't show the ugly parts, wouldn't see them."

Bery said, and looked up at the sky.

A slightly waned, imperfect moon shone in the cloudless sky.

"But even to someone like me……Krische-sama was simply, so very beautiful."

Her red hair shimmered and swayed in the gentle wind, and the moonlight illuminated her well-formed profile.

It was a profile so beautiful that one could not help but be captivated.

"……So very beautiful. Pure, without lies or pretense or impurity — such a Krische-sama may find this world very difficult to live in. There may be those who hate her, those who fear her. But to me, she is still beautiful, and——"

*——Hopelessly dear to me.*

She lowered her eyes, and placed both hands upon her chest, as though embracing something invisible.

It closely resembled an expression her mother had sometimes shown when speaking of her father.

A face filled with something beyond description, beyond words.

"——To be at Krische-sama's side. I realized that my meaning was there. And if so, then my name belongs to Krische-sama. That was what I wished."

That was why she must have looked so beautiful.

At the very least, as she spoke those words, Bery looked more beautiful than any face Selene had ever seen.

"She told me she understood loneliness, and fufu, such a small thing is a great discovery for Krische-sama. Fumbling, clumsy, but more earnest than anyone……I wished to devote my life to such a Krische-sama. ……Please, forgive me."

Bery bowed deeply, and Selene sighed.

"It's not for me to forgive or not forgive, honestly. ……What kind of servant are you? I told you that you didn't have to look after me anymore, and instead you're making me look after you."

"……I am sorry. But my mind is made up."

The face that showed a faint smile was, again, so very beautiful, and Selene looked up at the sky with a soft, ah.

This stubborn servant's feelings would not change.

Confronted with a result she had more or less predicted, Selene rubbed the bridge of her nose.

"How am I supposed to explain this to Father? He was worried it was truly a waste for you to spend your whole life as a servant. I can already picture his stunned face."

"Please don't make too much of it……it is merely my self-satisfaction."

"Knowing you, I'm sure you'll be saying you want to hold a ceremony before long."

He had been rather fond of King Sherbaza, and was aware that the fault was his own.

He harbored no resentment at being spoken of harshly; he accepted it as natural.

He was more aware than anyone that he was mad.

"But the flaw both shared was being too much the idealist, too soft. Both ideal nobles — and precisely for that, they were exploited by a monster. ……Kreschenta desired the throne. To make herself the ruler. My brother's illness was also Kreschenta's doing, but my tenderhearted brother could not bring himself to suspect his beloved daughter……and so he was killed."

Salva said nothing, seemingly deep in thought.

But it was clear he was quietly weighing Gildanstein's words and reflecting.

"At the recent victory ceremony, I encountered Krische — the cursed child who was supposed to have been killed — now Bogan's adopted daughter. And that is what prompted her to act. I do not know what happened, but the facts as they stand lead to no other conclusion. Do you know anything?"

"……No. But I have certainly heard rumors that the two are close."

Salva's heart was already on his side.

Gildanstein narrowed his eyes.

From this man's acquaintances, he had heard that Salva had repeatedly voiced complaints about Bogan's adopted daughter, Krische.

They apparently did not get along at all, and there were a fair number within the army who disliked Krische.

"I don't know much about this Krische. I've seen the face. ……But I've heard the rumors. Including that you don't think well of her."

Salva lowered his eyes and was silent for a time.

Then he spoke slowly.

"Krische-sama is……without doubt a genius of war. That is entirely true, and she is like a small child."

"……A child?"

"The general and Selene-sama love such a Krische-sama. She is a bit unusual, but pure and kindhearted — toward those close to her, at least, she is goodness itself. She is exactly as she appears, and so I understand their feelings."

Quietly, surely, Salva continued.

"I do not think the general or Selene-sama are wrong in that regard. ……But I am afraid of Krische-sama. That is how I feel."

"Hmm — afraid."

"……She does not understand the principles of things. Like a child crushing an insect, she kills and hurts people without any hesitation whatsoever — she is fundamentally different from other human beings. She knows neither good nor evil, nor right and wrong, and yet she is stronger and sharper than anyone. She could turn any direction. An uncontrollable monster — when I learned the rumor that she was of the royal house……a cursed child, I found it easy to believe."

Salva shook his head and looked at Gildanstein.

"Forgive me for saying so, but I hold no good feelings toward Your Highness. However, if Her Highness the Princess is the same sort of person as Krische-sama, then I too feel there is reason in Your Highness's words."

"That will do. I am not asking you to swear personal loyalty to me. I simply want you to fulfill your duty as a noble of the kingdom. To prevent Kreschenta's usurpation of the throne."

Salva nodded, but after a pause, spoke.

"……Two things — if you would swear upon your name."

"Let me hear them."

"Yes. ……The general's life is beyond saving at this point, I suppose. But I ask that you spare his daughter, Selene-sama. She is honest and hardworking — I have watched her closely since she was a child."

"I see — the hero's daughter inherits his qualities. Well-beloved, indeed."

He told him with a laugh.

"Rest easy — I had no intention of doing anything to her regardless. As I said, Bogan is a loyal retainer of the kingdom, and those words are no lie. I will not destroy the house, either. ……What is the other?"

Salva looked away, as though considering, eyes downcast.

"……If it could be granted — Krische-sama as well."

"The cursed child, too?"

That was unexpected.

He had believed as fact that this man disliked Krische.

"I do not like her. I think her a frightening person. But I also know she does not relish war or killing. She is simply pure, and does what she does for the general and Selene-sama. And the two of them love such a Krische-sama from the bottom of their hearts. ……I cannot think that one whom the two people I revere love so dearly deserves to die."

He bowed his head once more.

He was saying this prepared to be cut down — Gildanstein saw resolve in it.

"I have found reason in Your Highness's words, that is true. And the general's rank is the highest of honors. But the stain of betraying the general to whom I owe so much will follow me for life. ……That is why I want to be satisfied. That I did what I could, at least, and that this was a decision I need not be ashamed of."

A mere small-timer.

He had thought so dismissively, but perhaps the man was not so easily dismissed after all.

*Not bad at all*, he thought.

"I cannot guarantee it. But I will do everything in my power, and I swear it upon this name of Alberan. ……You made me swear, of all people. In return, fulfill your own duty."

"……Most gracious words. I swear upon this name — without fail."

Gildanstein continued.

"When the reinforcements arrive, I will launch a major offensive from Mitskronia. Let my unit through and lure Bogan out. That is your job. Use my name freely — promise fitting compensation to anyone within the ranks you can exploit."

"Yes, sir."

"You are not a bad man. Live up to my expectations."

Watching Salva walk away, Gildanstein murmured.

"Don't call me a coward, Bogan. As you well know — war is fought for nothing but victory."
