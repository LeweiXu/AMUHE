# Chapter 156: Dragon's Blood

The catalyst for the Seventeenth Christand Internal War was Bery, who woke the following day.

Krische had immediately tried to throw herself at Bery, but since Bery's body had been frozen in time, only two days had passed from her perspective since she was stabbed. Even with the blessings of the great dragon, the wound in her abdomen had naturally not yet closed.

Krische's movements were halted by the head of House Christand, Selene Christand, and she was forced to abandon her attempt to infiltrate the bed and cling to Bery.

So what to do next——stepping forward here was the Queen of Alberan and honorary Christand, Kreschenta Alberan. She indicated a chair, suggesting that Onee-sama should sit here, scheming to establish her own position atop her sister.

But the one who objected to this was, once again, Selene Christand. She invoked the just cause that Krische was also an injured party, declaring that her own lap was the most fitting place for Krische.

The situation descended into utter chaos, and ultimately resulted in the construction of a peculiar object on the bed: Selene sitting at the head of the bed beside Bery with Krische on her lap, and Kreschenta clinging on from directly in front.

"Have you more or less got the picture?"

At Selene's words, spoken as she held Krische and stroked her head, Bery nodded.

What had happened while Bery was asleep——the content was relatively serious business, but the gap between it and the scene visible from Bery's perspective was stark.

Wearing an awkward, ambiguous smile, Bery said, still lying on her side, "I'm sorry."

"……It was my carelessness that——"

"It really was. Your carelessness nearly destroyed the kingdom, after all. As if an apology could make up for——oww……"

"Kreschenta, if you say something like that again, I'm going to get angry."

Krische pinched Kreschenta's lips between her fingers.

Selene reached out and lightly flicked Bery's forehead.

"Don't go moping like that. ……You were simply a victim. If there's anyone out there who would hold a woman accountable after she was stabbed in the stomach and nearly died, I'd love to see them. You should be openly glad your life was saved and thank this little fool and the holy spirit."

Bery pressed a hand to her flicked forehead and stared blankly at Selene, then let out an amused laugh.

She smiled like a young girl and clasped her hands together in an exaggerated gesture.

"……Oh my, Nee-sama, Ojou-sama is saying things that sound just like you."

"Come on……"

Selene, still with Krische on her lap, pinched Bery's soft cheek and leaned in close.

"You seem quite energetic. Maybe being considerate was a mistake."

"Fufu, my stomach hurts a little, but……my condition itself isn't bad."

"I see. Well, I wasn't worried. ……You did kiss Krische right in public, bold as anything, the moment you woke up, after all."

"U-um……"

Her face turned bright red at once, and Bery's eyes darted around.

Then she looked up at Selene as if checking her reaction.

"A-about that, you see, th-that memory is, how should I put it, hazy……"

"You don't remember? Krische, Bery says she's forgotten that she kissed you——"

"Th-that's a lie, I remember……b-but I thought it was a dream……"

Her eyes glistened with embarrassment.

Selene chuckled wryly and lightly patted her head.

"Honestly. So, is your body really all right?"

"Yes, it's just……I feel a little flushed. ……Could I have some water?"

"Oh, yes!"

Krische reached for the water pitcher and poured water into a cup.

Bery tried to sit up and winced, and Selene lent her a hand.

Her skin was slightly feverish, flushed.

The curves of her breasts and waist peeking through her thin negligee carried an indescribable allure, and Selene stared at her with an exasperated look.

"When you're like that, so much about you is really wasted, you know."

"……?"

Even the way she tilted her head was alluring.

Selene sighed, thinking she must have no shortage of suitors, and shook her head.

"No, never mind. You've been drinking nothing but water for a while now——how are you feeling? Think you could eat something?"

"Um……yes, I've calmed down quite a bit."

Krische dissolved honey into the cup, stirred it, and handed it to Bery.

Bery took it and gazed at it for a while, then slowly brought it to her lips.

She lowered her eyes as if savoring it slowly.

Then she smiled at Krische.

"Thank you, Krische-sama."

"Ehehe, then maybe Krische should go prepare a meal early——"

It was just then that the door was knocked and Anne came in.

Gazing with bewilderment at the three-tiered beautiful-girl object, what Anne carried in on a silver tray was rakura preserved in honey.

Ooh, Krische looked at Anne with a touch of admiration.

The timing was as if she had been watching for the right moment——and in fact, Anne had been standing in front of the door holding the tray, waiting for the right moment.

"Anne, you're almost like a proper servant."

"Th-thank you very much……"

The phrasing was as if to say she was normally not a proper servant.

Anne was slightly hurt, but——

"Don't say it like that. Anne worked hard while you were away."

"——!"

Tears welled up at those words, and she stared at Selene with a look of deep emotion.

Kreschenta eyed Anne dubiously, but showed interest in what was on the tray——the honey-preserved rakura.

Kreschenta had a sweet tooth.

"Y-yes! I prepared the honey-preserved rakura I was taught before……how does it look? I thought even without an appetite, this would be easy to eat."

Anne had been there when Bery woke up. Naturally she had tried to fuss over her, but Krische had said she would do it and shooed Anne out of the room——yet Anne was a woman of fortitude. Something like that would not break her, and she had a secret weapon.

This honey-preserved rakura, which she had been steeping since the night before.

The pale peach-colored fruit was enveloped in amber honey, showing a beautiful luster.

Simply peeling and slicing sweet rakura and soaking it in honey——a preparation so simple it could hardly be called cooking, but the result was not bad.

Krische stared intently at the honey-preserved fruit, then turned to Bery.

"Bery, how about it?"

"Yes, since you've gone to the trouble, I'll have some."

"Wonderful. ……Hmph, it's made just as Argan-sama taught me. Today's honey-preserved rakura is quite a masterwork, I must say."

"Fufu, thank you."

Krische took a plate from Anne, placed several pieces of the honey-preserved rakura on it, and stared at them intently.

The fruit had incisions cut into it, and this was Bery's technique.

If one were steeping them for several days it would be one thing, but with an overnight soak the honey's sweetness would not fully penetrate to the center. So by making cuts with a knife, the honey permeated the entire fruit——well done, Krische nodded, and looked at Anne.

"Not bad for you, Anne. It seems you properly remember what Bery taught you."

"Y-yes! I tasted it, and today's should be a truly outstanding batch……I'm sure everyone will be satisfied."

Selene chuckled wryly at the imperious Krische and the delighted Anne.

It was an extremely simple sick-person's dish. Not just clumsy Selene, even a child could make it, and she felt slightly uneasy about what kind of evaluation Anne held in Krische's mind.

Setting aside her extreme propensity for blunders, Anne was a servant who could handle most things reasonably well, but Krische and Kreschenta's assessments of her were exceedingly harsh.

Krische smiled with satisfaction, cut a piece with a spoon, and brought it to Bery's mouth.

"Here, say ahh."

"Oh, ahaha……if you'll excuse me."

Bery opened her mouth with an awkward look, tasted the rakura——and froze for just a moment.

When Krische tilted her head, Bery shook her head and smiled, addressing Anne.

"It's very delicious, Anne-sama."

"——!"

"Ehehe……then Krische will have some too."

Krische said this, popped a piece of rakura into her mouth, and her cheeks softened.

"The rakura is a bit tart. It's delicious, though."

"Um, well, I thought Argan-sama might prefer this kind……ones that aren't too ripe. It's already sweet enough being preserved in honey."

"Thank you. It's a little crispy, with a nice texture."

"……While Krische was away, Anne actually learned to select ingredients properly……"

Krische looked at Anne in surprise, and Anne laughed shyly.

"Fufu, while you two are resting, please leave the cooking to me. During the time you were both away from the kitchen, I was secretly training."

"……That's unexpected. So you actually were capable of making a proper effort."

"Huh……?"

Anne froze, and Bery, with a wry smile, gently admonished Krische, saying, "That's not nice."

Kreschenta, who had been watching these exchanges with something like envy, gazed up at Krische and opened her mouth.

"Kreschenta is a little spoiled baby, isn't she. Here, say ahh."

"Mm……"

Kreschenta savored the rakura she was given, her cheeks relaxing, then as if noticing something, she fixed Anne with a serious stare.

"Passable. Not bad."

"Y-yes! Thank you very much!"

Selene chuckled wryly, took a plate from Anne, and picked up a piece of rakura.

Not that there were no problems——but at least, she felt relieved that daily life as they knew it was continuing, and smiled.

The rakura fruit was almost cloyingly sweet, delighting Selene's palate.

"What would you like for lunch? Would soup be better?"

"Yes, I think so. Would you mind?"

"Of course! Bery needs to get better soon……Krische will make a soup with lots of nutrition."

"……Yes."

Krische grabbed Kreschenta by the waist, set her down on the bed, and stood up.

"Then Krische will go prepare lunch. Krische is a little worried whether Anne kept the kitchen clean……"

"I-I did keep it more or less clean……"

Anne's eyes darted, and Bery looked troubled.

Kreschenta, also set down on the bed, stood up.

"I'll come too——"

"You have a meeting, don't you? Aren't you keeping people waiting?"

She tried to follow Krische out, but Selene grabbed the scruff of her neck.

Kreschenta pouted and glared at Selene.

"……A queen should arrive a little late."

"Honestly, no. We have a meeting with this side later too. ……Krische, I'll come back at lunch, so please make enough for us as well."

"Okay."

Krische climbed onto the bed, kissed Bery, said "I'm off," and left the room.

Watching Krische, who seemed quite energetic for someone covered in injuries, Selene chuckled wryly and stroked Bery's hair.

"……I can't say there aren't problems, but for now the worst has been avoided. So don't worry about it, and take it easy for once. I'm glad you're well."

"……Yes, I'm sorry for worrying you, Ojou-sama."

"I'm always the one causing worry, so we're even."

Selene smiled, and Bery returned her smile with a nod.

"Once you're better, will you cook for me again?"

"……Yes, of course."

Bery's gaze wavered slightly, and Selene tilted her head.

Bery pressed her side and smiled wryly, then quietly laughed, looking troubled.

"No, I tried to lie down and my stomach twinged a little."

"……Honestly."

Selene placed a hand on Bery's back and gently lowered her down.

Bery said, looking embarrassed, "I'm sorry," and Selene glared at her with exasperation.

"I'll have a doctor called again later. Shall we get you a painkiller or something?"

"Yes, I'm sorry for the trou——"

"None of that. Let yourself be pampered for once, and put us to the trouble. ……You stubbornly cling to your position as a servant, but before that, we're family."

Selene pressed Bery's cheeks between both hands as if squishing them.

Bery stared at Selene like that, then lowered her eyes.

"Um……you see."

"……What?"

"Honestly, Selene-sama, you're the one who brought it up, how long are you going to keep her waiting?"

The voice came from the door.

Kreschenta was puffing her cheeks, glaring at Selene and Bery.

"I'm coming already. ……Bery?"

"No, it's nothing. Have a good day."

"……If there's something, tell me. It'll bother me."

Selene furrowed her brows and placed both hands on her hips.

Bery shook her head and smiled.

"I'll tell you another time, when things have settled down. Please don't worry about it. It's nothing serious."

"……Hah. Fine. ……Listen, all right? I don't care what it is, but brooding alone about things is not allowed."

"Yes. I'll be careful."

And so Selene left the room too, and the one remaining, Anne, tilted her head and looked at Bery, wondering what had happened.

Bery shook her head and smiled.

"Anne-sama, I'm sorry, but……could you bring me some fruit?"

"Yes, of course! What kind would you like?"

"Something refreshing……fufu, after eating a little, I think my appetite has come back."

"Oh, that's wonderful……please wait just a moment!"

Bery watched Anne bustle out of the room, smiled wryly.

Alone in the room, she quietly sat up.

She scooped honey from the jar on the side table with a spoon, dripped it onto the back of her hand, and gazed at it.

Feeling its faint aroma, she slowly pressed her lips to it.

She rolled the thick honey on her tongue for a while, as if confirming something.

Then she lowered her eyes.

# Chapter 157: The Kingdom's Heroine

The next day was a work day for Krische as well.

Aside from naturally needing to check on the Jaleia Gashea, which she had left unattended, she also needed to attend meetings she had been unable to participate in for some time.

It was not as though she attended every single one, of course, but given the circumstances, this time was different.

Krische obediently agreed with the advice of Kreschenta, Selene, and Elrouga that she should at least show her face as a matter of form.

Of course, she would have preferred nothing more than to spend time with Bery, but even to save her, Krische had acted quite selfishly.

Even Krische felt a degree of remorse toward Selene and Elrouga, whom she had dragged around considerably, and on the day she returned, she had been thrown into the bath where she endured an endless lecture from Selene.

She could not refuse.

Kreschenta, observing the world-rare sight of Krische showing genuine willingness to participate in governance, was pleased beyond what her expression revealed.

She appeared at that day's Privy Council internally overjoyed, alongside Krische.

The assembled nobles saw Krische in her usual attire——shirt, skirt, and cloak——exchanged glances, and dropped to one knee.

Quite a few of them had seen the light that split the sky above the capital, or had glimpsed the dragon itself from a distance.

Having brutally killed seventeen nobles without leaving evidence, challenged a dragon and returned alive, and been granted its true name——once they understood that she was the queen's trusted blade, a monster beyond question, they could only stiffen and beg forgiveness.

Kreschenta walked the blood-red carpet to the throne and sat. Krische stood at her side.

The queen was pleased by their demeanor.

Had Krische merely assassinated nobles, that would have been one thing, but now she had also forged a pact with a dragon.

That "flying lizard" was a thoroughly unpleasant creature to Kreschenta, but its prestige was more than useful.

"Good morning, everyone. Please make yourselves comfortable."

At Kreschenta's words, those who had been kneeling rose and came to attention.

Unprecedentedly proper, their manners were precise. Kreschenta had simplified such formalities on the grounds that they were tiresome, but if they were performed voluntarily, she nodded that this was not bad either.

"I'm sure you've heard by now, but due to certain circumstances, my dear sister was away from the capital for a time. I apologize for not communicating the reasons, and for the unnecessary confusion it caused."

She felt not a shred of remorse, of course, but words cost nothing. Appearances had always been important.

"However, as you know, Onee-sama was granted the honorable name of the holy spirit Yagelnaus-sama, and furthermore has returned having forged ties of friendship with the Kreisharana, who had been in a state of severance from Alberan for centuries since a past war. In time, relations between our nations shall surely flourish."

A murmur spread through the chamber.

Every face showed surprise.

Any noble present would know the history with the Kreisharana. From the victorious Alberan's perspective, it was a matter of the distant past——but the hatred harbored by the Kreisharana, who had been driven into the mountains, had never faded, and for that reason a non-aggression pact and severance had continued.

For this to change so suddenly——their surprise was understandable.

"As Alberinea——Onee-sama originally journeyed alone to Albyagel to investigate Kreisharana's movements, which she found concerning in light of the unsettled situation surrounding the kingdom. But looking at what she accomplished beyond that, it is truly a heroic deed. I think it is something the kingdom can be proud of."

An envoy mission to the Kreisharana, doubling as an inspection.

Kreschenta had decided to present this incident publicly in that light.

The story was that the dragon audience had been conducted incidentally, resulting in a bond of friendship.

Of course, given the dragon's own appearance and its statements, the fact that Krische had turned her blade against it could not be entirely concealed——but those were mere rumors in the end.

Truth was nothing but a trick of words.

The populace did not care about truth; they simply drifted with plausible-sounding words and believed whatever they wished. In reality, they had no means to verify any of it, and those who lacked the capacity for thought would take the most plausible story as fact.

Compared to the idea that a dragon would carry on its back someone who had drawn a sword on it, the story that friendship had been formed through an audience was far easier for them to understand.

"Onee-sama is the symbol of the kingdom's martial might, Alberinea, and the Kreisharana are a culture that values martial prowess above all else——despite centuries of separation, surely there were things that resonated between them. It so happened that they were in the midst of a festival, and there Onee-sama demonstrated her swordsmanship, and they competed together."

It just happened that the Kreisharana were holding a festival. Krische had popped in, and though she was from Alberan, a hostile nation, it was an auspicious celebration, so they invited her as an individual. There she displayed her swordsmanship——oh my, what a strong person, we had no idea Alberan had someone like this——and they became friendly, and she performed a sword dance before the dragon, and so on. Such a fictional tale had already been prepared, and Kreschenta casually turned to Krische.

"Um……yes, that's right. Swords……or rather, it was more like spears and legs……"

"Fufu, oh, Onee-sama. It's all the same."

Kreschenta nodded with satisfaction.

"Afterward, she performed a sword dance before the holy spirit Yagelnaus-sama——and what followed, you all know. I hope this satisfies your curiosity."

She surveyed the room.

Confusion was on every face, but at least no one showed any sign of defiance.

That was natural, since Krische had disposed of everyone who would have.

"That said, regardless of the reasons, considering the Sacred Convention, Onee-sama's actions cannot be praised without reservation. I'm sure some of you have such concerns. Onee-sama herself expressed a wish to take responsibility for this disturbance, and though her accomplishments represent an immense contribution to the kingdom, after balancing merit and demerit, there shall be no reward——that shall serve as the punishment for this incident."

Under normal circumstances this would have warranted a parade, but the kingdom had been in utter turmoil.

Kreschenta had wielded her authority heavily, and after the purge.

Restraint, and at least a token punishment, was the appropriate course.

Kreschenta desired to hold absolute power, but she believed that such power must not rest on fear alone.

Her ideal was a system where everyone would gladly and willingly offer their heads.

A world where they obeyed her words from the heart, pledging loyalty and prostrating themselves.

What she sought was that form, and what shaped it was reward, punishment, and discipline.

To create such a world, she must at least make them believe these things functioned properly——that things were fair.

The breeding ground of enmity and rebellion was the unfairness of dictatorship and rule by terror.

The gesture of Kreschenta herself punishing Krische, whom they viewed as the queen's puppet, was important. Neglect it, and the same thing would happen again.

Structural inequality of power and simple unfairness were similar but fundamentally different things.

Kreschenta did not confuse one for the other.

Her sister beside her was the same as always.

Standing there with a face as if she understood perfectly that punishment was natural for doing something wrong——Kreschenta looked at her foolish-looking sister and smiled with satisfaction.

For the moment, freed from various worries, Krische was at her side.

For her, that was enough.

"That is all from me for now. If there is nothing else, Duke Larkos, please proceed with your reports."

"Yes. Your Majesty. Then, from my end——"

That day, Kreschenta was in an exceptionally fine mood.

Beside the estate——next to its stable, carpenters were working busily.

Moving with brisk efficiency as they drove stakes, what they were building was a shed slightly too large for a stable, and what they kept stealing glances at was the beast yawning in front of the estate.

The jade tiger, left half at liberty.

The estate used as the Christand residence had been enclosed by a fence to some degree from the start, but the carpenters were working inside that fence.

And the beast inside that fence was no mere tiger or lion.

Its massive frame, standing eight shaku at the shoulder. A magical beast, a jade tiger, whose jaws could bite off a man's entire upper body in one snap.

Working near such a creature had them on edge, and the work——including fence expansion——was proceeding at a very brisk pace.

Standing before them were two beautiful girls, one gold and one silver.

And a craggy-faced man of imposing build, and a gaunt old man with a face like a skull.

"Ehehe, a nyan-nyan friend for Gururun. This one's a two-legged nyan-nyan."

Grrrr, the jade tiger rumbled, being stroked on the head, looking drowsily at Krische, then at the two-legged nyan-nyan——Kokys.

Selene and Elrouga exchanged glances at this newly discovered victim, and Kokys scratched his cheek with an indescribable expression.

"S-so that nickname really is settled, then……"

"……?"

"No, never mind. Let's just give up."

Kokys's shoulders slumped, true to his words.

Selene looked at this rock-like man with pity. Elrouga's cheeks lifted in wicked amusement.

"Heh, wise decision. No warrior, however mighty, stands a chance against Krische-sama."

"……If you ask me, I'm the one getting the worst of it."

Vulture and Skeleton, with an extremely generous interpretation, could perhaps pass as war names of a sort. Granmeld was Woof-woof——similar to Kokys's situation, but between woof-woof and nyan-nyan, the latter was clearly more pathetic-sounding.

Why me, he thought, but his opponent was Krische, who transcended all common sense.

He had no choice but to accept it.

The meeting with Kreschenta had ended, and next was the military meeting.

Before that, Kokys had mentioned wanting to see the jade tiger Krische had brought back, which was how the four of them had gathered.

Kokys sighed, and, careful not to provoke the jade tiger, crouched down before it and observed.

The jade tiger, being stroked by Krische, was as docile as a cat.

Setting aside its extraordinary physique, it was even somewhat charming.

"You come back riding a dragon, and now you've tamed a jade tiger like this……honestly, what can I say. I've hunted three of them myself, but they never seemed like the kind of beast you could tame."

"Mm……Krische had a big deer she couldn't finish eating, so she tried feeding it, and it was surprisingly easy……this one is quite gentle, you know?"

"Gentle……would it be all right to get a bit closer?"

"Yes. Elvena just gave it its meal a little while ago."

"Hah……poor thing."

Selene sighed, remembering.

When she had returned a little earlier, she had happened to see Elvena's feeding, and the girl had been so frightened she was practically in tears.

With her hips pulled back, tremulously placing a lump of meat——

*"G-Gururun-sama, h-here's your meal? I-it's not me, okay……?"*

——saying things like that as she set it down before the jade tiger.

Her smiling cheeks were twitching, and when the jade tiger pounced on the meat, she was so startled she fell on her backside.

Selene had been so worried by the sight that she proposed letting Krische handle the care, but——

*"N-no! This is a child that will be living here from now on, and as Krische-sama's servant, when I think of Bery-sama who is convalescing, I cannot give up over something like this……"*

Elvena had been truly admirable.

Trembling, she clenched her fists, her eyes filled with determination.

So admirably that it was pitiable. She, too, was a victim of Krische.

"Oh……it really is gentle. When you look at it like this, it's almost cute. Quite a different impression from seeing one in the forest."

Kokys smiled like a child and nodded.

"Is Nyan-nyan a fan of animals?"

"Hm, ah……I don't look the part, I know, but I do like animals. I keep several dogs at home. My wife's hobby, partly."

"Dogs. Ehehe, Krische also likes dogs, or rather, playing puppy is——mmph."

"……Please stop saying silly things."

Selene clapped her hand over Krische's mouth just in time and sighed.

Kokys and Elrouga tilted their heads, and Selene waved them off——don't mind that.

"Oh, Bery!"

It was then that Bery appeared on the second-floor balcony.

Not in her apron dress, but in an unusual one-piece outfit.

Supported by Elvena, with a blanket draped over her shoulders, she showed her face.

"Excuse me from up here. Agrand-sama, Faren-sama, good morning."

"Oh, Bery. Glad to see you well. How are you feeling?"

"Yes, much better, thank you."

Kokys smiled, and Bery returned his smile with a polite bow.

Selene placed both hands on her hips and glared at Bery with exasperation.

"Bery, honestly……"

Whether she truly understood or not.

"I heard lively voices, so I got a bit curious. See, staying cooped up all the time isn't good for the body either, is it? Especially when the weather is this lovely."

"Yes. ……Would that be all right, Ojou-sama?"

"……I suppose there's nothing for it."

Supported by Krische and Elvena, Bery looked slightly troubled at being treated as an invalid.

Saying "I'm fine" with a wry smile, she came out to the garden and approached the jade tiger.

"My……Bururun was quite a large creature, but this one is even more so. The difference is striking up close."

"Yes. Ehehe, it's a suuuper big nyan-nyan. They're called jade tigers, apparently. It uses mana, so it runs faster than Krische."

She looked surprised, but folded her skirt behind her knees and crouched down.

"Come here, Gururun."

Then she beckoned with her fingers, as one would call a cat.

Gururun glanced at Krische beside it, yawned as it stood, and slowly approached Bery.

When petite Bery crouched like that, the jade tiger's great maw was exactly at the height of her head.

If it casually opened its mouth and closed it, Bery's upper body would promptly find its way into the jade tiger's stomach.

Elvena's face went pale and she stepped back.

But the jade tiger brought its face close to Bery's head, as if sniffing her scent.

It explored here and there on Bery's body in this manner, then stared at her for a while and settled onto its belly.

As Bery slowly reached a hand toward its head, it yawned, closed its eyes, and obediently let her stroke it.

"Fufu, it's big, but it really is like a cat. How adorable."

"Yes, it's a well-behaved good child."

What Elvena felt was sheer astonishment.

Selene watched the apparently distraught girl with pity, then looked at Bery with exasperation.

Utterly unguarded before a beast that seasoned warriors feared——Bery was exactly the same as always.

"Oh, but you shouldn't pet against the grain of its fur. It's quite prickly."

"So it is. Would a stiff brush be best for grooming……"

Krische crouched down beside her and idly began stroking Gururun.

Side by side like that, they looked like sisters, and the redheaded woman looked every bit a young girl from any angle.

In her apron dress she carried a somewhat mature air, but in a one-piece outfit, she looked exactly her apparent age.

More than a dozen years since they first met, and still the unchanging appearance of a beautiful girl——a woman. Three of the four, excluding Elvena, looked half-exasperated, and Kokys scratched his cheek and approached Gururun.

"Still, Bery, you really are fearless. A woman who calls a jade tiger she's seeing for the first time 'cute'——in the whole world, that would be just you and Krische-sama. Even I wouldn't dare touch one that boldly."

As he spoke, Kokys gingerly reached out and stroked Gururun's back.

Stroking a living jade tiger's back was a first even for Kokys, and he let out an impressed "oh."

Bery pressed her fingertips to her lips and tilted her head.

So she could not bring herself to say it, putting it off like this.

A hopelessly pathetic, weak person.

"……Bery?"

The voice made her flinch, and she turned.

In her negligee, Krische stood at the entrance, a little sleepy, looking at her curiously.

"Krische-sama, what brings you here?"

"……Well, um, Kreschenta was sleeping funny and woke Krische up, and then Bery wasn't there. What are you doing?"

She was relieved that she had already put the seasonings away, and smiled.

"I got a little thirsty. I was thinking of making some warm milk."

Krische padded over and leaned against her.

Warm from sleeping, her body radiated heat.

Her violet eyes shone even in the dark, catching the moonlight from the window.

Those gemlike eyes, glittering, were turned to her, and she asked:

"Bery, um, are you all right?"

"Hm?"

"It's……the mana in your body has changed, and Krische was worried that you might actually be in pain somewhere, or feeling something strange. ……She heard that the blood from Rigaleiwe-san was very potent, so she was worried."

Krische said, and lowered her eyes.

"As I've said, my body feels even better than before. There is a slight sense of unfamiliarity when I control my body, but……I've gotten used to it, and it's actually easier than before."

Saying it, she wanted to sigh at her own wretchedness.

Even now, she was afraid to say it.

Perhaps she should have confided in Selene when she had the chance.

Day by day her heart grew more timid, and as time passed her courage dwindled further.

"Everything is just fine."

She stroked Krische's head, and Krische nodded quietly.

Guilt gnawed at her for the lie, yet she could not bring herself to speak.

*I'm the worst,* she thought of herself.

"Um, if anything is wrong, please tell Krische right away. Krische will fix it somehow, she's sure."

"Yes. ……Would you like some as well, Krische-sama?"

"Ehehe, yes."

Krische nodded and smiled.

Bery smiled back and went to the cold box to fetch the chilled milk.

Krische reached for the honey, glancing at the nearby seasonings.

Bery, her back turned, did not notice that Krische had gone still.
