# Chapter 186: The Eye of God

"More than you promised, Krische! Even I was caught completely off guard."

Clad in lion-emblemed armor drenched in copious blood, great war-axe slung over his shoulder.

Dagreen Gahka emerged from the battle lines, laughing cheerfully as he reached out to clap Krische on the shoulder――but.

Krische, disliking the thought of getting stained with blood, reflexively leapt away and dodged it.

"Krische believes she showed Gahka-shogun beforehand, though……"

"Even so, seeing it on an actual battlefield is entirely different. ……Why did you dodge?"

"……Gahka-shogun is covered in blood."

"Bah, what a fastidious little thing you are."

Spreading both hands in exasperation, Dagreen looked behind him.

There was no longer any group in the area capable of organized resistance.

Galshan soldiers were turning their backs and fleeing.

The enemy's far left wing was still alive, but they were clearly unable to conceal their confusion at the breakthrough of their center.

Naturally so.

Galshan had lost both its generals.

Soldiers could only function as soldiers because they had commanders.

Losing them in rapid succession made it nearly impossible to restore the troops' composure.

Because the tide of battle was something anyone could read.

All the more so since these were soldiers who had been riding high on the euphoria of imminent victory――they could not easily accept such a reversal.

Victory was now all but certain.

"I'd like you to explain that thing later――Bonjara, was it? But that can wait. I'll take these fifteen thousand and head to Olgan's position as planned."

"Yes, please do."

"……Are you really sure? Having had everything set up for me this nicely, I feel guilty stealing the best part."

Krische swayed her silver hair, tilting her head slightly.

"It's not stealing. This was the arrangement from the start. Krische wants to minimize army losses, so that takes priority."

"What a selfless creature. ……Well, if you say it's fine, I'll move first. Your part comes after. Do as you please."

"Yes. Vinsreel."

"Sir!"

Vinsreel, who had been waiting behind, brought Littgund flapping forward before Krische.

Five shaku at the shoulder, the body of a lion and the face of a raptor.

Each beat of the elegant blue wings unleashed gale-force winds――Dagreen caught a glimpse of Krische pressing down her skirt from the corner of his eye, but his interest was on the griffin's rider.

Gripping a blood-slicked spear, clad in furs.

The warrior's equipment was light by any measure, yet an unmistakable martial presence radiated from him at a glance.

"Vinsreel-dono, if I may?"

"Yes, Gahka-shogun. I don't mind what you call me."

Vinsreel tilted his head, wondering what this was about.

"I see. It can wait until after the battle, but……would you mind letting me ride behind you?"

He immediately understood the intent.

Dagreen's eyes were shining like a child's, gazing at the griffin with unmistakable envy.

"I'd love to experience the view from the sky just once……"

The rugged man confessed with visible embarrassment, and Vinsreel smiled wryly, thought for a moment, then nodded.

"So long as the griffin isn't too tired, I have no objections."

"Hahaha, that's something to look forward to. In return, tonight I'll treat you to my finest hidden stock. So that you don't die following this princess's reckless schemes."

"Geez……Gahka-shogun, Krische doesn't do anything reckless."

Krische puffed out her cheeks and leapt onto the back of Littgund.

"Just as Krische said, the civil war and Rigaleiwe-san were more than enough. From now on, Krische has decided to only fight battles she can win one-sidedly."

――Gripping a spear in his right hand.

"……Alberinea."

A short while earlier――Zalvarg was leading three thousand elite, pressing forward at the head to attempt a flanking maneuver around the Alberinea army's left wing.

With the Alberinea army directly ahead, Olgan's main force had drawn up its battle lines.

On the left wing, the curved flank of Kilrea's right wing overlapped.

The only route for a flanking move was from their own right wing, and when Zalvarg had led the reserves in a dash forward, thirty flying cavalry had launched from Nakria.

Olgan had moved the reserves――Zalvarg――to assault the Alberinea headquarters.

They must have been aiming for precisely that timing.

It was as if every action had been read.

No――in fact, it had been.

From the opening of the battle to this point, Alberinea had calculated everything.

Otherwise, this response would be impossible.

Olgan, whose army had been rendered capable of immediate decisive engagement by the instant destruction of Elkaarl's forces, had taken a stance for a headquarters battle, and from the right wing had Zalvarg lead the elite in a flanking sweep around the wing's edge.

This had not been decided in advance. It had been decided just moments ago.

Yet as if she could see the future itself, Alberinea had read every move.

"Zalvarg-sama!"

"Ignore them for now."

Just past the enemy's left wing.

In the distance, near Kilrea's position, incomprehensible explosions were echoing.

He did not understand exactly what they signified.

What he did understand was that the enemy's aim was not a small-force raid on their headquarters.

The annihilation of the left wing led by Kilrea――a frontal breakthrough and headquarters assault by Gahka's forces was the true objective.

Zalvarg understood this no longer through logic, but through pure instinct.

What Alberinea sought was not merely to win.

She sought a complete victory over Galshan's full military might.

To overwhelm, to crush, to ensure they could never rise again.

――He recalled the girl's emotionless purple gaze.

Beautiful as jewels, and deeply colored.

The absolute confidence dwelling within them, and the arrogance.

It was like a child looking down at insects.

He had seen her in the capital, and again at Nakria, and again when she had slain Alkazaris.

Only now did Zalvarg finally understand the existence called Alberinea.

For her, this was nothing more than a game.

The battlefield where warriors staked their lives was, to her, no more than a war between ants.

And she possessed the power to make it so.

Galshan would be defeated by nothing more than the individual called Alberinea.

There was no victory to be had; the best they could hope for was to turn this into a mere defeat.

If they could crush the Alberinea headquarters immediately, they could at least disrupt the enemy's coordination and buy time for a retreat.

"……Agrand, was it?"

Part of the enemy left wing――and the enemy headquarters had already taken a formation to counter the flanking move.

The enemy strength facing Zalvarg was around two thousand.

However, at their very forefront was a set of armor he recognized.

At the center of the enemy line――a tiger-styled helm and massive, imposing plate armor.

The corps commander who had been at Alberinea's side in the capital.

Likely one of Alberinea's close retainers.

Bold of him to take the very front――yet a correct choice.

He was without doubt a warrior chosen specifically to stop Zalvarg.

And emerging from both flanks of the enemy front line were two black-steel giants each.

"……Leave the center to us."

"Forgive me, Ehryuke. ……I leave it to you. ――The banner!"

The banner behind him unfurled wide.

The trumpet sounded, and the entire force assumed charge formation.

At the same moment, from the other side came the simultaneous twang of bowstrings.

What the enemy loosed was a rain of arrows.

Simultaneously, Zalvarg broke not toward the tiger-armored warrior directly ahead, but toward the enemy's left flank.

His targets were the two black-steel giants charging toward their battle line.

――The giants' weakness is in the head.

Elkaarl's final words had been relayed through his soldiers.

Cutting diagonally between the lines, Zalvarg readied the spear in his hand.

The giant――from the eye in its head, blue light flickered, and it reacted instantly.

The two on the left wing moved toward him.

At close range, the eight-shaku frame was unmistakable.

The enormous curved blades mounted at the elbows, an utterly alien monstrosity.

Likely one of Alberinea's magical weapons.

The speed visible at a glance, the weight and armor――it was no wonder the soldiers feared them.

Each massive footstep carved deep gouges into the earth.

Ordinary soldiers would be incapable of even engaging one.

Even a skilled mana-wielder could easily be killed in the attempt.

If you feared them and kept your distance, if you failed to land a killing blow, you died.

Zalvarg glanced to his left for an instant――the enemy corps commander was already running toward him.

Take too long and he would be killed regardless.

It was a gamble.

But Zalvarg pressed forward, trusting the words Elkaarl had left with his life.

*"――Hahaha, so the man who racked up all those kills was a former cobbler? I was originally a carpenter myself……Truly, this army has all sorts. Let's fight hard together, Zalvarg the Cobbler. Together with Elkaarl the Carpenter, for the sake of that madman of a hero, Olgan."*

Words left at the cost of Elkaarl's life.

There could be no lie in them.

The giant swung its right blade in a wide arc.

In that instant, Zalvarg stepped in and drove the spear he held into the iron grate at the center of the head's eye.

Releasing it, he leapt simultaneously, using the giant's shoulder as a foothold to launch forward.

――Zalvarg of the Twin Blades.

It was not that he had particularly favored dual-wielding.

When he plunged into the enemy to kill as many as possible, he was surrounded on all sides.

Simply put, two swords were more practical than one against multiple opponents――a straightforward reason.

If one sword could kill five, then two could kill ten.

It was that crude a notion; at first, he had merely picked up a fallen sword.

Anything that could kill enemies was fine, and Zalvarg had no attachment to any particular weapon.

The second giant had already raised its left blade.

There was no hesitation whatsoever at its companion's destruction; its reactions were razor-sharp.

That alone made it a threat beyond any ordinary veteran.

Zalvarg slipped past the giant's flank as if rolling.

He drew a curved blade and severed the joint between head and torso――its neck――in a single stroke.

As expected, what came back through the blade was the sensation of metal.

Zalvarg's swordsmanship could cleave plate armor with ease, yet the joint had some thickness.

He felt the curved blade that had severed the neck take a chip, and frowned.

Minor, but this weapon would likely not last beyond this battle.

Having disabled both giants in the blink of an eye.

Zalvarg heard the enormous cheer behind him――voices praising his valor――and remained calm.

All he understood was the fact that, for the moment, they would hold.

And that was all their role required.

Of the three thousand, twenty-five hundred were merely decoys, and they understood that themselves.

From the farthest right flank of the elite he had brought, five hundred camel cavalry surged ahead in a further breakout.

Those five hundred were what Zalvarg ultimately commanded.

The enemy corps commander wore heavy armor. He could not catch the lightly equipped Zalvarg.

Zalvarg had not the slightest intention of fighting Kokys Agrand.

He had no interest in single combat between warriors; only the result mattered.

Zalvarg of the Twin Blades was not a warrior, but a soldier.

――Zalvarg let a piercing whistle ring across the battlefield.

At that signal, soldiers emerged from the right wing, accompanied by dogs.

What they led were trained medium-sized hunting dogs――normally used to harry light skirmishers.

They would not be lethal against an enemy centered on heavy infantry, but for buying time, that sufficed.

All he needed was to pin down the enemy corps commander.

"Rogerius!"

"Sir!"

Curved blades to sheathes.

He leapt onto the camel brought by the cavalry captain, and received a spear from a nearby cavalryman.

Ahead lay the enemy headquarters.

At its center were archers.

Beyond them, dozens of Alberinea's hunters in black-lacquered armor.

"Arrows incoming! Spread out, three-arrow formation!"

――As expected of the headquarters guard.

The volley came swiftly after the breakthrough.

No――they had read the breakthrough and had their formation ready from the start.

They had realized the true objective was a further flanking breakthrough by the camel cavalry.

At the very least, the enemy headquarters' commander had not been fooled by Zalvarg leading from the front.

Cavalry had no means to block arrows.

Split the five hundred into three groups, widen the intervals, thin the arrow density, and pray nothing hit.

He had been prepared for that as well.

The point was simply that enough men survived to reach the enemy commander.

Zalvarg always thought in the simplest possible terms.

Not how much damage they would take――but whether or not they could take the enemy general's head.

To the left――from the Alberinea line facing Olgan's.

Soldiers in black-lacquered armor were seeping out like water.

Each one sprinting like cavalry――mana-wielders.

Of the three cavalry groups, the left would be killed by them.

From behind the enemy headquarters, some three hundred cavalry had burst forth.

Of the three cavalry groups, the right would be pinned down by them.

Horses disliked the stench of camels――but even so, breaking through would take some time.

The kingdom's cavalry were few in number compared to Galshan or other nations, but many were mana-wielding nobles and thus elite.

Still, all of this was expected.

From the start, the true force was the one hundred and fifty with Zalvarg at the center.

By the time they closed to fifty ken, three volleys had come.

Elite archers. Against the camel cavalry charge, they showed no fear; their rate of fire was tight.

Their aim was accurate as well.

Zalvarg discerned among the thousands of approaching arrows only the handful aimed at him, and deflected them with his spear.

And seeping from the archer line came black-lacquered armor――fifty of them.

A third of the one hundred and fifty had already fallen.

Their spears――likely, this would bring casualties to half.

What they held were not throwing javelins but close-combat spears.

Yet they hefted them with ease, throwing them as casually as javelins.

And a mana-wielder's thrown spear was not something that could be caught easily, even if seen.

They stepped forward, set their stances.

Of the spears thrown, ten were aimed at Zalvarg as their commander.

He leapt instantly, watching the camel beneath him get skewered by countless spears, and pressed forward.

Appearing from ahead was a single old soldier――a man clad in unadorned armor.

The headquarters' adjutant, or perhaps a guard.

In his left hand was a great steel spear, and in his right, something spherical.

Zalvarg reversed his grip on his spear.

And the man also released whatever he had gripped in his right hand.

It sailed over Zalvarg's head, blinking blue.

――What was that?

An unknown object. He did not recognize it. The flickering blue light――the glow of mana crystal.

Why at this timing――there was some meaning.

What had happened on this battlefield――the explosions at Kilrea's line.

"――Everyone, use the camels as shields!!"

He shouted and lunged forward.

Hearing the explosion behind him, he gripped the reverse-held spear.

His step drove into the earth――he hurled the spear.

Concentrating all his body's force into his hand, rolling with the recoil and rising――

But the man had deflected Zalvarg's thrown spear.

A formidable expert――blocking Zalvarg's thrown spear from this distance alone proved this was no ordinary opponent.

He instantly drew the right curved blade. Never mind what was behind. Likely dozens had died. They had lost their charge momentum. But not all of them were dead.

The man, in turn, had readied his great spear and stepped in.

A familiar style. The spear technique used by Empire nobility.

But whether from injury or some aftereffect.

The movement of his left hand was dull, and his center of gravity tilted slightly off-axis.

――The thrust was sharp.

Zalvarg dodged it with a half-turn and moved to the man's right, toward his left flank.

Twisting his entire body, he released a sweeping arc of a strike.

The man also twisted, trying to shield the strike aimed at his neck with his left arm.

The experience behind his judgment――undoubtedly a warrior of renown, but that much was clear.

Zalvarg intended to sever both the left arm and the neck together.

"――!?"

But what rang out was a dull metallic sound.

Zalvarg's curved blade had torn through the steel gauntlet, but could not cut through the bone beneath.

He pulled back before the blade snapped, before it could bite deeper.

What lay inside the gauntlet was not flesh and bone.

It was the same material as the giants from before.

The old soldier thrust again with a sharp spearpoint; Zalvarg dodged and twisted his body.

A single arrow grazed the outer edge of his left thigh, cutting shallow.

The one who had fired it was a white-haired old soldier on horseback, nocking another arrow on a short bow――a face he recognized.

The old man he had seen at the Nakria meeting, one who likely could not even use mana.

And yet from thirty ken, he was accurately tracking and targeting Zalvarg's movements.

"Zalvarg-sama!!"

At the cavalry captain's scream-like cry, he pulled back for an instant.

The spot where Zalvarg had been was pierced by a close-combat spear that roared through the air.

"Waltza, I told you not to do anything reckless."

"Sir, he appeared to be an adversary who warranted caution."

Black-lacquered armor stained with copious blood.

Visible even from atop an elephant, a fighter rivaling Agrand.

He carried his longsword shouldered, and he alone had broken through the camel cavalry on the left.

He apparently judged that sufficient.

And it was true――the cavalry that had split left had taken javelins from the flank and were already half-destroyed.

"Given the situation……I suggest you surrender. I have been told you understand the western common tongue."

His opponent had advanced alone.

Zalvarg considered whether he could kill him, and dismissed it as pointless.

He had half-understood already.

This was nothing more than a desperate last stand.

That charge moments ago――had he achieved the best possible result, it might have succeeded.

But it had been absorbed, and on top of that, Zalvarg had failed to finish the enemy's expert.

The moment it had devolved into a melee, Zalvarg's plan had failed.

No――perhaps it had been hopeless from the very start.

From above the man's head, the gale of a griffin's wings.

A single shadow descended.

Perhaps so that her skirt wouldn't flip up, she was holding the hem with both hands, and there seemed to be no weight to her landing; it made no sound at all.

Silver hair flowed like water, and through it, purple eyes glittered in the sunlight.

"Ehehe, griffins are nice and fast."

An innocent voice and a breathtaking smile.

Descending from the sky was Alberinea――Krische Christand.

"You've arrived rather quickly, Krische-sama. I thought I'd get to play a bit longer."

Flanking maneuver upon flanking maneuver to raid the headquarters.

The black-lacquered armor had been stationed behind the battle lines in anticipation of exactly this, and the headquarters had been furnished with just enough strength to absorb Zalvarg's charge for an instant.

"Krische told you, didn't she? Krische said she'd be right back. Honestly, Nyan-nyan let them escape, so two Jaragasha got destroyed. Krische will have to scold him later."

"Hahaha, well, it's unavoidable. Considering Corps Commander Agrand's performance today, the Jare――"

"It's Jaragasha!"

――In that brief time, she had easily disposed of Kilrea.

Without a single drop of blood on her, what she held was Kilrea's beloved sword.

For her, everything that had happened on today's battlefield was a foregone conclusion――all of it a game within the palm of her hand.

Her appearing here at this very moment had also been built into her 'schedule.'

The girl looked up at the man in black-lacquered armor, hands on her hips, cheeks puffed.

Seeing that, Zalvarg lunged forward almost unconsciously.

He drew both curved blades from his waist and swung at the girl.

The girl, full of openings, spared a glance.

Wearing a slightly troubled expression, she precisely caught the tiny chip remaining on Zalvarg's fastest right blade and 'bisected' the curved sword with her own.

Zalvarg felt no shock anymore.

The girl passed behind his right side――Zalvarg twisted his entire body and swung the left blade as he spun.

"――!?"

But the girl had released her curved blade and planted her hands on the ground; she was already twisting her hips.

Her left heel shattered the unarmored upper arm.

Then she seized Zalvarg's remaining right arm and slammed his tall frame into the earth.

She stomped on his torso and right arm, and pressed the curved sword at her waist against Zalvarg's neck.

"Ugh, gah……"

"This situation is hopeless, so you should stop doing pointless things. Krische is much stronger, and Zalvarg-san is much, much weaker than Krische."

In his consciousness fading from the impact.

She raised a gauntleted finger.

As if admonishing a child, the girl spoke.

"You are merely a witness to the outcome."

As if stating something beyond all doubt, as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

As if a god were demonstrating a law of nature, those purple eyes shone.

"――The match is Krische's victory."

So said Krische Christand.

# Chapter 187: A Strong Rain

*"……Onii-chan, please don't do anything foolish. Don't worry about me."*

He had been a notorious nobleman.

Unlike Zalvarg, his younger sister――still a child then――was striking and beautiful, and that was precisely why she had been singled out, ordered to serve as a household attendant, and sent to the estate.

That was what nobles had been like in the days of the Galshan Kingdom.

Absolute powers――beasts who could do anything they pleased with commoners.

His pale-faced sister, rather than choosing to flee with Zalvarg, accepted her fate and went to the nobleman's estate.

A few years later, she was killed, accused of stealing from the estate.

His sister would never have done such a thing; when he investigated, he learned she had become pregnant, and when she refused to abort the child, she was killed.

Caught while trying to escape.

A common story――a conclusion he should have known from the very start.

Even so, he had told himself that his clever, beautiful sister would surely adapt to life in the estate, that perhaps she might even live a decent life.

Her letters, written in clumsy characters, always said that despite hardships, she was living reasonably well――and he had tried to believe them.

For the first time in his life, he took up a sword and went to the estate.

And when Zalvarg was effortlessly captured, he still remembered well the words that man had said.

*"I regret the waste, truly. Heheh, your sister had the loveliest voice when she cried."*

It was fortunate that Olgan's rebellion had coincided.

Rescued from execution at the last moment by the rebel forces, Zalvarg decided to fight alongside them.

It was not from some noble sense of mission.

It was nothing but revenge.

He decided to devote his life solely to killing that man, and for that reason alone, he swung his sword as if his life depended on it.

If he killed more than anyone else, he could rise.

If he earned distinction, he would be placed in important positions.

He charged into enemy lines targeting the commanders' heads, and claimed many a head.

That said, he had few memories of those days.

*"……Zalvarg, was it? I've heard the stories. I'm sorry."*

What he remembered was breaking into the besieged enemy's chamber.

And finding there, all too easily, the body of his enemy, dead by his own hand from poison.

*"……What has my life been, then?"*

*"Your enemy is dead. That ends your revenge."*

Even driving his sword into the corpse's heart returned nothing.

Dead.

The man who had toyed with and killed his sister had died without any real suffering.

Without tasting even a fraction of the agony his sister must have endured.

*"If you wish to desecrate the corpse, do as you please. If it would make you feel better."*

Zalvarg said nothing, sank to his knees, pulled the blade free and turned it on himself.

*"But I will not allow you to die."*

The man caught the blade with his bare hand.

*"……Why?"*

*"You used the revolutionary army for your revenge. And from what I hear, it was this revolutionary army――my revolutionary army――that saved your life. Don't you think you owe me a debt?"*

*"……A debt?"*

The man――Olgan laughed and seized Zalvarg by the collar.

*"Regardless, your revenge is done. This is the beginning of your new life, Zalvarg. If you're going to sell that life at a pittance, I'll buy it for now."*

*"I don't consider it for sale."*

*"Well, for someone suicidal, you seem rather clear-headed."*

And then, pressing his forehead against Zalvarg's as if in challenge, he declared:

*"As the last of the royal line, I must bring about a world of equality for all. Lend me your strength for that purpose."*

*"……I――"*

*"――That said, I have no intention of spouting pretty words. Rest easy."*

Olgan laughed cheerfully and released his grip.

*"A lofty cause, but just a pretense for fun. It's not as though I felt sorry for you or the commoners――I simply didn't like my brother and the scum around him, so I picked a fight."*

*"A fight……?"*

*"Yes, I do love war. ……I've heard of your exploits many times. I've always thought you were a man fit to swing a sword at my side. Your revenge is done, you must be at loose ends――come help me."*

It was an absurd proposition.

*"Tragedies like yours are a dime a dozen. And they will continue to happen. But if you aid me, I will remake this nation so that what happened to your sister never happens again. ……As a side benefit of enjoying the war."*

*"……You are mad."*

*"Hah, how rude. But do you think any member of royalty would relinquish power without being a little mad?"*

And yet, his eyes were magnetic, overpowering.

*"Zalvarg, dying is something you can do anytime. If you're still set on it, then rather than die here, die on the battlefield. I'll give you a better death than this one. And if you killed yourself here, having failed even to finish off your enemy, you couldn't very well face your sister with your head held high, could you?"*

He wore an unkempt stubble, and his eyes were deeply shadowed――he likely hadn't been sleeping properly.

Yet those eyes were youthful as a boy's, brimming with light.

And he extended a thick, gauntleted hand.

*"……I, Olgan Dikel Shahnia, last king of Galshan, swear upon the rain: I shall grant your life of nothing but regret a true harvest."*

Why he had taken that hand, Zalvarg still did not know to this day.

"Ugh……"

――When he awoke, he was lying on the grass of the plains.

His broken left arm had been splinted and left as it was, but both legs were bound, and his right arm was tied to his torso.

"You're awake, Zalvarg. You were out for quite a while."

He looked toward the voice and saw his lord, hands bound behind his back, sitting cross-legged.

His armor bore countless gashes, his face sported bruises, and blood still dripped from his nose, yet he sat with absolute composure.

Around them, Kingdom soldiers were rushing about.

The Galshan banners had been lowered; what flew was the arrogant kingdom flag, its sword-tip thrust toward the heavens.

"……We lost, then."

"In that situation, what victory could there be? Did I look to you like a man strong enough to slay Gahka and then slay Alberinea on top of it?"

"You did not. Against an opponent I couldn't even fight, I wouldn't expect the Vice Chairman to last a single exchange."

"Hah, rude as ever."

Olgan looked amused.

"A thorough, utter defeat, Zalvarg. Hahaha, magnificent, a rout so complete it's almost refreshing. ……I've just earned my place in history as an incompetent."

"So it would seem. You look remarkably pleased about it."

"It's not a bad feeling, you know――being overwhelmed after going all out."

Olgan simply laughed.

As always, and more cheerful than ever.

――What echoed across the field was nothing but thunderous noise.

Against Dagreen Gahka, who had broken through Kilrea's line, Olgan chose not to defend but stepped forward alone.

In answer, Dagreen too stepped forward.

The outcome was already decided.

In the form of Alberan's victory.

This was merely the confirmation――the settling of a rivalry that had spanned decades.

"Gahka! I'll concede victory to Alberan, but you I will kill!!"

"Hahhahhaah! That's a loser's howl, Olgan!! Be grateful for my kindness in humoring someone who can do nothing but bark!!"

What Dagreen Gahka sent thundering was the roar of his great war-axe's swing.

Compressing the air, gouging the earth and still not stopping, the dull blade could shear through steel itself.

What Olgan swung was a thick-spined great curved blade.

A five-shaku blade that was practically an axe in its own right; aligned edge-on, it would bisect anything.

No ringing of steel upon steel sounded here.

If either struck their weapons together head-on, the one receiving would surely lose their weapon.

And so both avoided that while targeting the other's weapon――and beyond that, sought any opening to cleave their opponent's torso or neck.

The result was a storm of countless whistling arcs through the air.

The earth bore countless scars from the gouging blades.

A closely matched contest; it was Olgan who seized the initiative.

He created a slight opening, invited the other's overhead swing, and dodged.

Then he brought his blade crashing down onto the steel shaft of the great war-axe that Dagreen had driven into the ground.

His excitement threw the angle off――shallow, unable to cut through, but sufficient to render it useless as a weapon.

The great war-axe's haft buckled and bent at the midpoint.

A scream-like murmur spread through the Alberan soldiers, but naturally, Dagreen Gahka was not done.

He drew the longsword at his waist and, with unerring precision, targeted the nick left in Olgan's great curved blade from its failure to sever the haft.

He snapped the great curved blade at the midpoint.

Now a scream-like murmur from the Galshan side.

Without his great curved blade, Olgan had nothing but a short knife.

"You won't!!"

Yet Olgan shouted and hurled the broken great curved blade at Dagreen.

A mana-wielder armored in muscle from head to toe. His throw was nothing like a desperate last resort.

A lethal weapon that could pierce steel plate on impact.

Instinctively, Dagreen deflected the incoming great curved blade with his longsword.

"What's delicious is delicious, and that's fine. Work hard so you can go home sooner, and say 'it's delicious' properly. Then fix Bery-san's tongue. Just sitting there suffering over Bery-san's tongue and denying yourself isn't healthy, and it's not what Bery-san wants either. Take things one at a time――and Bery-san surely wants that for you too."

"……Yes."

"You look scary when you're being serious, so come on, smile. It's delicious, right?"

Krische nodded, and Kalua laughed, pushing those soft cheeks upward to force a smile.

The new recruits, now aware of what she was like on the battlefield, watched in barely contained terror; the veterans looked at Kalua with weary resignation.

"Ugh……I'm hungry……"

"……You, honestly. Krische-sama, the briefing is finished."

Just then, returning with Dagra was Mia.

"All right. The Vultures can rest now too."

"Sir."

Casualty reports, liaising with the various corps, briefing for tomorrow's march.

*"The bothersome stuff, the Vultures can handle,"* Krische had said, pushing her own duties onto them, and Mia had been running around everywhere.

Dagra eyed the exhausted, slumping Mia, but today he did not scold her, only smiled wryly.

She hadn't had a proper meal since noon and had been on her feet the whole time.

Given the victory, he would cut her some slack today, and without a word, Dagra took his meal from Beltz and parted ways.

He headed to where Waltza was, surrounded by Neigal's engineering team, checking the condition of the prosthetic arm that had caught the curved blade.

Aleha had been with Dagra's group too but had been intercepted by Kokys along the way.

Mia also took her meal from Beltz, made a face of displeasure, and.

Settled down next to Krische and Kalua.

"Haah……beez beans again today……"

"……Mia, being picky is bad."

"Ugh……yes."

She rolled a green bean in her soup with her spoon――beez beans, cultivated mainly in the southern kingdom through Galshan――and sighed miserably.

They were simply sweet, not bad-tasting at all, but Mia did not care for them.

On top of that, every single meal for the past few days had featured beez bean soup.

*Being picky on the battlefield is spoiled*, Mia had been telling herself, enduring it, but facing beez beans on top of exhaustion and hunger was the final straw.

Mia was utterly sick of them.

"……I think they're tasty, actually, beez beans. Sweet and nice. Though I get that it's a bit much when it's every single meal."

"……It's the sweetness I can't stand. I like sweetness in fruit, but sweetness in actual food just, ugh."

Mia glared grumpily at Kalua, who was still holding Krische.

"Haah……if only, just for this moment, the sense of sweetness would disappear. I never asked beans to be sweet."

"M-Mia……you idiot!"

"……?"

This came right after the story about Bery losing her sense of taste.

Even Kalua panicked and tightened her hold on Krische.

"……Did I say something weird?"

"Y-you see……Mia, your timing is really, how do I put it……"

Kalua nervously checked on Krische in her arms, peering at her face.

Mia looked at Krische too, tilting her head.

Krische's eyes had gone slightly wide, staring at Mia.

"……Just the sweetness."

"Is everything all right, Krische-sama?"

Krische thought for a moment, then looked up.

"Beltz, get Krische some fruit. Anything."

"Huh? Oh……yes."

"Mia, a beez bean."

"Uh……?"

She held her hand out to Mia.

Mia tilted her head but scooped up a single beez bean on her spoon and placed it on that small hand――and immediately Krische wrapped it in mana, staring at it with those purple eyes.

"Krische-sama……is this all right?"

Beltz, having fetched several kinds of fruit, looked at Krische curiously and held out the basket.

Kalua, also puzzled, took it instead and placed it on Krische's lap.

Krische swallowed the candy, put the beez bean in her mouth, tasted it.

Then she tried an apple.

Similarly wrapping it in mana, staring intently, taking a bite, tasting it, swallowing.

Then she took a candy from the pouch around her neck and repeated the process.

"……It was a simple thing. Same as the poison-detecting Piririn."

Krische sprang to her feet and approached Mia, stroking her head.

"Ehehe, Mia is a dummy, but Krische is glad she made her the Kuro-Fuyo adjutant. This is your greatest achievement ever. Good job."

"Huh? Oh, y-yes……th-thank you……?"

Krische smiled happily and trotted off.

"Neigal, could Krische have some spare mana crystals?"

"Y-yes, sir!"

She tugged the sleeve of Neigal, who was near Waltza, and the two ran off toward the tent serving as the Jaleia Gashea storehouse.

Mia and Beltz stared after Krische, dumbfounded.

Kalua thought for a moment, and smiled.

"……Kalua, what just happened?"

"Hmm, probably a good flash of inspiration. ……Fufu, thanks to Mia's little bit of foolishness."

"……Are you making fun of me?"

"Nope. Maybe what someone as clever as the bunny needs is a touch of Mia's foolishness. I'm complimenting you――it's a good kind of foolish."

Kalua laughed cheerfully, stood, and stroked Mia's head.

"For the bunny, Mia might be the one who earned the most merit in this whole campaign."

"……? Huh?"

As Mia's brow creased further, Kalua watched where Krische had gone.

"……If everything's solved, then nothing better."

Kalua narrowed her eyes gently.

* * *

"Bunny, it's late. You should sleep. There's marching tomorrow too."

"Just a little more……"

"No. Come on, let go of the mana crystal……you can continue tomorrow. You always get sick right after a battle, so resting is part of your job."

"Uuu……"

That night, even under the blankets, Krische would not release the mana crystal.

* * *

"Koza, Krische's meat portion was a little small……"

"M-my sincerest apologies. I'll bring seconds right away."

"……Bread, too."

"Yes. Hey, bring out bread."

――The next morning, Krische's appetite was larger than it had been in a long time.
